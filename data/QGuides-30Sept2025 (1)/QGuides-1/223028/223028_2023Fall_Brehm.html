What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course is an adventure. It is a box of chocolates, and we don't know what we're going to get –– in part because we didn't go out and buy a box of chocolates. We took a bunch of ingredients we had in our kitchen and made the chocolates from scratch without a recipe and with only our instincts, inspiration, and feedback from the group to guide us. Really, it's more like a Willy Wonka kind of chocolate factory. And some of what we create isn't even chocolate!

If you have the space in your schedule, you should make time for this course because there are very few courses at Harvard where you will have such complete freedom in creating something that you get to share with people at the end of the semester. Such freedom is scary because you have to figure out what rules to create to guide you, and you will learn by failing and discovering what elements are working and what ones aren't.
Take this class if you are interested in exploring the movement and devising aspect of theater. You don't need to be actually physically good at it, but be mentally prepared and engaged. The time spent in this class is definitely not short, 2h45 min in class then another 75min studio lab. Plus dinner, had to stay from 3–745 at the quad for this class. But you get to really bond with your ensemble in the class, and all contribute to a final piece of performance and get to know yourself better in the process as well, very rewarding! Professor brehm is a great professor in teaching style. She's very enthusiastic, gives you a lot of hands–on experience and is very approachable for support and feedback outside of the classroom.
