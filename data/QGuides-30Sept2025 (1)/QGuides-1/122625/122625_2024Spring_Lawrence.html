This was one of the most fun courses I've taken this semester, and I feel that I got a great introduction to the broader themes within HEB. I've taken two HEB courses before this, and I could see recurring themes and the relationships between them from this course. I formed great relationships with my classmates. I will say, though, that the weekly summary points were kind of a pain as I often found myself writing 5+ pages for each, and other students in my section seemed to be doing the same. A bit much for an HEB survey course, in my opinion. From talking to previous students, I do think the class became more time–consuming this year, mainly due to the length of the summary points assignments. Still, I learned a lot and would highly recommend. If you plan on declaring HEB, I think you will find this course enjoyable.
Participation is necessary every week and there are also a lot of interesting topics & discussions. There is a lot of readings each week so be prepared for that. Otherwise it's pretty chill. Just stay engaged & work on the essays in advance.
it's chill. do your work and keep up with your readings and you'll be fine
Take this course!!! Dr. Desmond genuinely cares about her students and the subject matter. The community within class is great as well and the material is great. If you take this course you will not regret it.

There are four essays due throughout the semester. The grading was pretty generous but don't expect to get feedback on anything.

You can do well in this class easily, especially with some background in HEB.

A double–edged sword.
1: One of my favorite classes so far at Harvard in terms of course topics and readings. Found myself more engaged that others I have taken.

2: Weight is placed more heavily on not being at all late to the 2 hour section than your actually contributions in section – something that (although not stated in the syllabus) can absolutely devastate your grade.

3: no flexibility from the course instructor regarding unfair grading. So few assignments that getting 80s and 90s on all of your papers will almost certainly prevent you from an A or A–.
This is a great class where you will learn a lot about how to think HEB and the class component is great. It is more of a laid back vibe where you can read the readings at home and then go to class and express what you think and what you learned in a super nice way. However, I would say be very careful with this class because a very big portion of the grade is writing assignments and they could be tough if you have never written in a scientific writing kind of way. It is also tough to get feedback and I found it super hard to improve my essay scores throughout the semester.
Honestly really enjoyed this course more than I expected! I feel like I learned so much and created a strong foundation of Human Evolutionary Biology knowledge. The grading was fair and I always really enjoyed the in class discussions.
