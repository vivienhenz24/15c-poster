What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
A wonderful course! For all incoming HEB (or HBBE) concentrators – the course is very reasonable in workload. Odds are, if you're premed, you'll be taking it along with Chem27 –– you will be MORE than fine. The readings for this course are genuinely fascinating more often than not, and if the course is structured in the same manner that Cammie did, you'll come out of it having learned a ton!

The essays can be quite challenging and grading might be a bit harsh. Make sure you attend office hours, know EXACTLY what they want (because sometimes there are unspecified requirements), try your hardest, and you'll probably be okay.
Sophomore Tutorial is definitely time consuming, but you'll learn a lot! I feel much more prepared to enter other HEB courses after completing Sophomore Tutorial. The readings are occasionally difficult, but it's cool to see yourself having an easier time with them over the course of the semester. I will say that your experience of the course is likely to vary quite a bit depending on who your instructor is, but they are all good just different from one another. The essays can be a bit daunting at first (or, at least, were for me because I wasn't quite sure what was expected) but you get very strong feedback on each of the papers and they become easier. Also: I highly recommend taking HEB97 and GenEd1027 in conjunction with one another. The two courses complement each other quite well and information from one carries over to the other.
This course exposed you to all the different fields of HEB and allowed you to broaden your knowledge and maybe find new interests within the human evolutionary biology department.
The skills that you acquire after this course are very important: critical thinking, analysis of a scientific paper, writing skills, communication skills, etc.
