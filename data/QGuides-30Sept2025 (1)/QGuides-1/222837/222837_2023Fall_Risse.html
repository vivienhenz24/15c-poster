This course was my first introduction to philosophy. It was definitely an uphill battle trying to understand the nuances of all of the thinkers, and it was a LOT of weekly reading and assignments (discussion posts, participation, etc). The papers are fairly short, which I appreciated. The second half of the course, for me, was much much more interesting when we actually began talking about technologies and applying some of this thinking to modern tech such as AI, biotech, etc. Sections were kind of underwhelming, but the prof and course staff were great.
Almost every aspect of life is in some way impacted by or involves technology. The course captures attention and changes the way one sees the world around them. I highly recommend Dr. Risse and this class.
You will cover a lot of material very quickly, but the readings are always interesting and Risse does an impeccable job breaking difficult philosophical positions down into understandable concepts in lecture! Pariroo, the Head TF, is also amazing and section is very helpful if you need further support. The assignments are not too frequent, nor too difficult. You should try to start the papers early, but they arenâ€™t overly challenging! Overall, this has been one of the better courses I have taken at Harvard. The cross–school nature of the class also definitely plays to its strengths. Design students may struggle a bit more with the philosophical readings, but soon will explore concepts more design–related in the third part of the course. Undergrads, do NOT be afraid to take this class !
