Incredible course. So interesting, balanced workload, and Professor Brekus and Carleigh are incredible teachers and people! Could not recommend this course more.
The content is incredibly interesting. This was my first religion course, and I learned so much. Classmates were very engaged, and our discussions were always fruitful. And Prof Brekus and Carleigh are absolutely fantastic in every way. Definitely recommend taking this course.
Professor Brekus and the course assistant, Carleigh Beriont, are both absolutely amazing, kind, intelligent, and helpful people. They are both wonderful to discuss anything with– both topics related and not related to the course. This course helped me understand a lot about the current state of America, both politically and generally. It also helped me understand the ever–present repercussions of the civil war. It's a pretty chill class– you will get the most out of it if you read the readings and contribute to class discussion– just don't forget about the midterm paper until a few days before it's due! and start your final paper on the earlier side as well– it helps if you can get guidance with your ideas or first draft. The only thing I will say is that the course does not delve as much into modern religion and politics as I would have enjoyed because there is so much time spent on the build up to it.
This class is awesome due to its amazing teaching staff. Catherine and Carleigh are so informed about religion and nationalism and do an amazing job at providing both sides of controversial debates as well as not historicizing these perspectives (i.e. connecting them to modern politics). They are also both so kind and welcoming. As a CS major who was raised agnostic, this class was very accessible to me. That said, I did have to do a little extra to remain engaged every week, either by participating when I wanted to stay silent or looking up background I wasn't familiar in. I came in to the class with a very secular view of American ideals and left realizing that religion is very deeply embedded in our country's history. DO THE READINGS, they are fascinating! If you are at all curious about American exceptionalism or white Christian nationalism or the many conflicts our conflict has weathered or the misdeeds that were given religious justification, this class is for you.
I highly recommend! Prof Brekus is incredible – go to office hours and get to know her. She is so friendly and approachable. The workload is extremely manageable and I learned SO much. Zero religious background needed.

Catherine is good but Carleigh was great and loved her input. I wish it was a little more controversial or we could truly debate opposing opinions because it would have made class more interesting. Papers are okay just long but nothing insane
Take it! It's designed to be easy and open to people without a history/social sciences background, so that is not a concern. The course materials are very interesting as the topic is deeply current, and so understanding the historical parallels is an experience that really resonates. It's also a good light course if you do have history background, and the materials are great. Prof. Brekus is just a DELIGHT, and a wonderful and brilliant person to learn from.
This a great course for people with lots of knowledge about the subject or very little at all. The material is fascinating and class is enjoyable. Lack of a section, while good for my schedule, made keeping up with the readings a little difficult, but I get that the size of the class wasnt anticipated. Assignments were straight forward and generally enjoyable.
This course is amazing. Professor Brekus is SO knowledgeable and intelligent, while also being very personable and caring with students. The class is such a delight and also important in especially in today's day and age with a lot of religion in politics (this course will help you to see that this is nothing new, but alas)!
This is a great class. Work is very manageable (although the weekly reading responses can be a lot just make sure you factor them into your schedule) and Professor Brekus and Carleigh are fantastic. I highly recommend this class especially to those who might want a more American history type course.
I LOVED this class. It is very much a choose your own adventure, you get what you put in type of class, which I think is so much fun. You have a lot of choice in the assignments that you do, and both Carleigh and Professor Brekus are so encouraging of pursuing your own passions within the class. Professor Brekus and Carleigh were both incredible, and I would highly recommend this class to anyone interested in American history.
