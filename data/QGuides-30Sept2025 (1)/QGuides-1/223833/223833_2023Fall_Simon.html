What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class discusses many important, relevant social movements and places them in dialogue in a very interesting way. Dr. Simon is a passionate professor and tries her best throughout the course to create a learning environment conducive to growth. If you are a student of color, be aware that realistically, some discussions are not facilitated the best and can result in harm. These topics are sensitive and require quite a delicate yet brave approach, and this approach was not always successful in my experience in this course.
If you're passionate about engaging in final projects, assignments, class discussions, learning lab work, and insightful speaker sessions, this course could be an exciting opportunity. There's also the unique chance to visit a high school and gain some fascinating insights. However, it's important to note that navigating the Lecturer's extreme disorganization, prolonged delays in feedback, frequent shifts in meeting locations, ambiguous assignment expectations, and fluctuating due dates might pose considerable challenges.
This is arguably one of the best classes I've taken at Harvard. Dr Simon is always so passionate and the course really draws you in to share your own thoughts and listen to your peers.
I highly recommend this class if you are interested in the issues and looking for an alternative kind of learning experience that isn't typical in most classrooms. While these are really heavy and pressing social issues, the classroom community is really loving and welcoming to diverse perspectives, which Nicole makes a big effort to create. I always felt welcome in that class, I looked forward to hearing the perspectives of my classmates every week, we eat lunch together –– for me, it felt less like a class and more like a group study break with good conversation. Nicole is also someone with a wealth of knowledge about teaching and education, and I picked up a lot of great teaching pedagogy just from observing her teach.
TAKE THIS COURSE!!
Take this class if you have any interest in education policy, teaching, or anything education–related! As a senior, I came into this class wanting to explore my interest in education, and I left the course with a newfound appreciation and passion for education policy and the craft of teaching and learning. Nicole Simon is a terrific professor who I recommend wholeheartedly.
I think this course would fit a very specific type of student, and that student just happened to not be me. While this course was very lenient in terms of deadlines, the course felt very disorganized, and I think, because of it, it was not the most enjoyable experience. The professor did not seem to know what she was doing for very important aspects of the class like the end of the semester final project showcase and field trip, until the last minute. It was not very comforting to meet with the professor about what to expect for the final project showcase, just to find out that she herself did not fully know what was going to happen. The professor would ask students for help on many different parts of the showcase (organizing it, emceeing for it, creating the programs for it), which I felt was a big contrast to other courses I was taking where important events were already planned out, booked, and established way in advance by the professor and the teaching staff, with no student planning/participation needed. I understood that she was busy as a resident dean as well, but it felt that the professor was not as prepared nor present for our course as she could have been. One particularly negative experience I had was at the final project showcase, when the student emcees were welcoming everyone to the showcase, and everyone clapped for the event. The applause was quiet. Because of this, the professor got up on stage, ordered the audience, saying, "That was pathetic. Try again," regarding the audience's applause. This kind of comment just did not sit well with me, considering the audience had taken time out of their day to come to our showcase. All in all, this may have just been my own not–so–pleasant experience with this course, but please just be aware of it!
Go into this class with authenticity, passion, and the willingness to learn from others while also learning more about yourself. This class is really what you make it so provide feedback, apply yourself, challenge your classmates, and explore the amazing subjects of education and social movements!
