What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This course is good if you are interested in health insurance and how countries structure their healthcare policy systems (particularly the economic side of healthcare, emphasis on health insurance models). It definitely emphasizes the economic side of healthcare rather than the healthcare itself. It is not a super heavy lift, and is a chill environment to learn about healthcare with other pre–med freshmen. The professor is kind and makes class a comfortable learning environment.
Definitely take this course! It's great for someone who is interested in learning about healthcare from a different, not–purely–scientific perspective. You learn a lot about global health systems. Don't worry about the workload in this class– it's not a lot. I think it's a good low–stress class to complement other classes in your schedule.

The professor is also great– he's good at teaching these topics and does a good job overall.
It had a much more economic focus than I had expected, and if you're a pre–med, he'll definitely remind you of some of the challenges and downsides of becoming a doctor. You also have to set aside time to devote to the readings because you will get much more out of the class if you are consistent about reading them and prepared to ask thoughtful questions to the guest speakers, who all have very cool jobs and lots of valuable insight and advice to give you. This class isn't exactly what I expected it to be, but you definitely get out of it what you put in.
I really enjoyed learning about the economics behind healthcare. Dr. Saini is very knowledgable and kind. Take this course!!
overall a good, easy course with great guest speakers. It feels more so like a lecture kind of class than a discussion based class at times, but I think you learn a good amount of information on healthcare systems.
Definitely take this class if you're interested in what the course title and description describes!
This course is great for anyone intending to work in healthcare, healthcare administration, or public health. You will hear from a wide variety of perspectives and learn a great deal about the challenges being faced in healthcare around the world.
While the title of the course may be a little misleading (It is very America and Health Economics Focused), Professor Saini is super sweet and brings in so many guest speakers throughout the semester to help you learn how money impacts health systems and learn from different perspectives. It is a pretty cool course overall with a manageable work load that really allows you to explore the content through pure exploration of content online on your own.
Whether or not you are interested in learning about how healthcare systems operate, I think this is a great seminar regardless. The workload for this class is super manageable, and the content is intriguing. Dr Saini is a great professor as well, laid back and engaging, and is easy to approach. I would definitely recommend this seminar.
