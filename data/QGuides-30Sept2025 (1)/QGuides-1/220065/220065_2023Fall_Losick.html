To preface, the professor is lovely and the material is quite engaging, and I would definitely recommend it. It's a rather new class though, so I do have some minor gripes with some of the other parts of the course. The professor's in the process of writing the textbook, and the textbook is very helpful. But it also makes lecture a little redundant, because the content is roughly the same as the lecture slides. Since the textbook is still a work in progress though, it's understandable. Secondly, the only assignments (other than the final paper) are reading quizzes due before every lecture, and some of the quiz questions felt poorly worded – like I would misunderstand what the question was asking, and that was why I would get it wrong, not because I didn't understand the material or hadn't done the reading. Given how much these quiz questions end up counting for your grade, it's a little frustrating when that happens. In–class polling questions are also graded based on accuracy, not participation, which I feel like might undermine the discussion, because you're more stressed about getting the right answer before time is up than you are thinking about the question. I don't think accuracy–based grading is needed to motivate people to think hard about the question and try to get it right most people will do that anyway, especially since they're already sitting there in class. The final paper is more than a little stressful and it's also worth 50% of your entire grade I think it might've been helpful to break it up into smaller assignments so we have checkpoints throughout the semester and can get more systematic feedback about it, like submissions for a proposal, outline, bibliography, etc. As it is, right now I have no idea how 50% of my grade is going to shake out.

Professor Losick is a nice lecturer, kind person, and great conversationalist. The lectures are interesting and are focused more on following the story of molecular bio than on memorizing content, and you get to meet some incredible guest lecturers whose work was foundational to the field. However, be careful with the assignments–– course staff were very unclear about grading, and set confusing guidelines regarding the importance of the (pretty difficult) homework quizzes and in–class polls. Students were told that our performance in these do not matter, however our grades were still impacted by the scores we got. We also received very little guidance on the final essays, not even a rubric on which we would be graded. We were consistently told not to worry about grading, but you actually do need to worry. Make sure to review your writing directly with the prof if you want to succeed–– other course staff were not the most accessible in the week following up to the deadline. Also, do not expect course staff to be incredibly generous with final grading–– many people had borderline A's, like 91.9%, but were not rounded up.

If you care more about learning than you care about your grade, then this is a good class for you. However, for people who need a good GPA for med school, etc., be careful and at least stay in communication with course staff frequently.
