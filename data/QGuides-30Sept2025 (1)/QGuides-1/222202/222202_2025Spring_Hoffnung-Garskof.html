What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Definitely take this class. One of the few seminars at Harvard where you really do practical work and not just academic. Understand how to make a legal argument with the tools of a historian. Jesse and the rest of the teaching team are always super available, and it was great getting to know them throughout the semester.
TAKE THIS!! This has been one of the most transformative courses I have taken. It was so cool to work with immigration attorneys and help them in such tangible ways. The discussions about the case were a bit hard sometimes, but the instructors really supported us and created such an intimate environment in the classroom. I really loved getting to work in our groups and feel like I made such great friends from the course, despite the work we were doing being so high–stakes and stressful at times. We went beyond the asylum system and really got to know the histories and cultures of the countries we were researching.
This course is a unique experience that you probably can't get at any other undergraduate institution in the country; that alone is reason enough to want to take it. However, despite its incredible premise and intention, the course is highly disorganized. There are weeks at a time when there will be no assignments, followed by us being notified that we have a few days to finish a major deliverable. There are always issues with Canvas, and assignments are somehow simultaneously overly specific and vague. For a course that has been taught for over a decade, it should be in much better shape!

That being said, the world has been crazy this past semester, and immigration in particular has been a sensitive issue for many. For that reason, I believe that the course and teaching team should be given some grace. It's hard to teach a class on a topic like this in the current political and social climate!
Take this course if you want to do immigration advocacy. This course is very laid back. But sometimes the instructions for assignments are a little unclear.
