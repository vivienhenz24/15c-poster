What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If you want to see what researching for and asylum case I recommend that you choose this course. HOWEVER, please ONLY do so if there is a syllabus and the class seems fairly organized. Otherwise it is very difficult to focus on the case since you will be more worried about deadlines and assignments. If you believe that the class is very scattered after the 1st week I recommend you consider whether you can balance this class since assignments and dates may change quickly. It is challenging to conduct research in collaboration with others but it is good experience in the field.
This course teaches you a lot about the research and academic work which accompanies the legal work in the asylum application process. Very community–engaged and teamwork–centered, and I learned a lot from this experience!
This was a demanding but really wonderful and rewarding course! I learned a ton about asylum law and the nitty–gritty of what it takes to build a case. The assignments and work revolve largely around your group, so I would work to establish a healthy and supportive dynamic with one another. If you are at all interested in what legal research looks like and don't mind spending upwards of 10 hours a week on work outside of class alongside your team, I highly recommend it.
Take this class! You get the opportunity to complete meaningful work and you get to help real people.
I cannot stress enough how much Professor Hoffnung–Garskof's History 1016 (History of US Immigration) and History 16 (Immigrant Justice Lab) have taught me. I've learned so much about a central part of my background of being an immigrant, about about both history and the present moment, and developed crucial writing and research skills. Life–changing, to be honest. I'm so grateful to have taken these courses with Prof. HG, and I cannot recommend enough to everyone at Harvard.
This class is probably one of the only opportunities to learn skills that will be applicable to the workforce (if you desire to be an attorney). Most other classes here focus on theory and teach you how to be good at research, while this class uses those skills to actually impact the world (you work on a real case).
Such an amazing experience. This course was truly great. It does require you to have a lot of flexibility towards assignments and just an open mind. We work with actual attorneys on real asylum cases so the work load is based on that, and what they need. This means that assignments and plans are not always concrete and are changing. But it was a class I would take again, even though it can be frustrating at times, not because it was a bad class but because of the topics we deal with. It can be hard but I felt it was very fulfilling to be doing something that could help change someone's life.
Take this course! You're taking it after History 1016, and this will give you a taste of real–world applications in immigration law. The actual content may change, since this was the first year of the IJL at Harvard and we had to shift partners early on in the semester. However, Jesse was really kind and informative, helping you have a good experience. You'll be surrounded by compassionate and intelligent people who take an interest in immigration–related issues. I loved working with my partners for the semester–long research project, and you get to be connected with actual lawyers working on real cases. Overall, a case study on what a Harvard course should look like, and I would recommend enrolling!
