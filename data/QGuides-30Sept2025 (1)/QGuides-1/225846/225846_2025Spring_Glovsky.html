Amazing Course! If you want an understadning about the broad–strokes events in African history, this is that course. One of the best courses I've taken at Harvard. Doesn't shy away from the complicated past, but in a way that seeks to understand why things happen
Take this class! Prof. Glovsky is great, reasonable, and an expert on Africa (an official African scholar). If you want to go from beginner to intermediate in terms of your knowledge and skill in reasoning about Africa, this is the class you must take!
Take this class you will learn SO SO much the professor is so passionate and really cares about what he is lecturing. The class covers a lot of material so prepare to spend time recapping the material each week. It is super worthwhile to take this course even if it feels unfamiliar.
Definitely a good class to take if you want to learn more about the African continent in colonial and post–colonial times. Regardless of how much you know about the continent and its history, this class will give you a much broader perspective than you had before. Professor Glovsky is very fun and a super engaging teacher who welcomes hard questions and makes discussions interesting
Take this class!!!!! Professor Glovsky is wondertastic and you will learn a lot. If you're someone (like me) who is scared of speaking up in class or scared of your professors, you should take this class –– it really helped me learn that it's okay to speak up in lecture. Also, I learned a lot and had fun doing it. If you want to learn more about Modern Africa history and read some great novels and primary sources, you should take this class.
I also think for all ye non–history people out there, this is a very accessible class.
There is a lot of material packed into a short time, because obviously you can't cover the entire modern history of such a diverse continent in a single semester. Not a lot of work outside of class, but definitely pay attention in class and take notes if you care about the topic because it's very easy to miss things. Overall just an interesting survey where you will learn (and get some hands–on experience with really cool museum visits!)
This class manages to cover a broad array of topics related to African history over many centuries. There's obviously more history than can be covered in any one semester class, but you get a good grasp of many developments, countries, etc. The readings were also very interesting since we got to read novels, graphic novels, primary sources, and watch documentaries.
The course gives you what it says it will: a survey of modern African history. Reading is very light and the materials are interesting with trips to the Peabody Museum and MFA built in which is nice and makes it more dynamic than the average history class. That being said, lectures were not very engaging unfortunately as they could use more multimedia materials and primary sources in the presentations, but Dana was an excellent section leader!
This is a great class to take to learn about African history. You will undoubtedly leave the class curious for more history to dive into.
Highly recommend, Prof. Glovsky is great. For sure do the readings, they aren't that long but you will get so much more out of the course. The novels we have to read are also incredibly interesting.
I highly recommend this course for anyone interested in learning about the history of Africa. I was a bit skeptical about joining a new course without a q report, but I am so glad I did. Professor Glovsky does a great job of keeping us engaged, and Dana is great at facilitating conversations in the section.
