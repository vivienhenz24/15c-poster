Alex is amazing! His lectures are extremely clear and well thought out. Psets are a nice blend of theoretical questions and programming. Tests are overall fair––make sure to study hard for them! Final project is pretty straightforward. Take this course if you want to learn more about the math behind unsupervised learning (there was a lot of lin alg involved so be ready for that)! Alex is incredibly kind, smart, and caring and this was definitely one of my favorite classes I've taken!
This was such a fun and interesting class! I highly recommend it. Alex is a great teacher and I genuinely looked forward to each of his lectures. He does a great job explaining mathematically why a bunch of ML algorithms work so that each algorithm ends up feeling like a natural thing to do, rather than something you just learned works but don't know why. He is helpful and will answer any questions you have along the way. The class also helped me greatly sharpen my linear algebra skills, which is essential for ML.
I would definitely recommend this course to anyone interested in data science. In just this intro course, you will learn many key UL algorithms and additionally build the framework to teach yourself state–of–the–art techniques in this rapidly advancing field.
Difficult course that is worthwhile to take. Alex is an amazing lecturer and you learn a lot from every single lecture. The course moves quickly and can be fairly difficult at times, but that ends up helping with your learning. Also a great course if you want to polish up your linear algebra skills as matrices appear a lot throughout the class.
It's a lot of math and can be challenging if your linear algebra background is not very solid, but the teaching team was wonderful and were always very kind and helpful when I had any questions or concerns.
Don't sleep on the linear algebra pre–requisite, make sure you know your eigeneverythings and matrix properties and you'll avoid a lot of early headache.

That said, great course. The professor is very insightful into any problems that arise and accessible as well. Probably one of the best–organized courses I've taken, and what you're learning is really applicable to the world of statistics today.
This is a good class! It has fun math and fun demos.
This course is very fair, pretty interest, and I found it hard but I think that's because I don't have the strongest math background. Many of the students in the class were either taking stat 210 or had more advanced math background like 25 or 55 which seems to have helped a lot. I definitely still learned a lot in this class. Alex is a great teacher and will always make time to help students. Overall, I'm grateful I took the class.
I would highly recommend this course. Alex is a great professor and the course gives a great foundation for unsupervised learning. I really think that I will be able to easily built upon this knowledge and grasp an understanding of most unsupervised learning techniques that I will be introduced to in the future. The midterms are tough, so definitely prepare well for them. However, I thought the final project was actually quite fun.
This is an amazing stats course ! I enthusiastically recommend! Alex is an excellent lecturer and professor. The format of the course is such that it is not stressful but very instructive (biweekly checkins, two midterms, final project instead of a final exam). It may be difficult in the beginning of the course to adjust to some of the matrix algebra if that is not something you use a lot but honestly I think one gets the hang of it by Midterm I. If you are at all interested in unsupervised learning or other data analysis techniques, I think this is a great class to take. It's a good mix of theory and applications, but has an applied bent, and overall super interesting. Highly recommend if you've taken 110/111 and looking for another stats class.
