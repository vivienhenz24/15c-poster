It is tough but a great learning opportunity
This course is challenging but, if you put in the work, reasonable and manageable. This is a course you need to review for consistently as the semester progresses. You cannot (and will not) learn an exam's worth of material in a week, let alone a weekend. Set aside 90 minutes to two hours per day for content review, and by the time the exam rolls around, you will feel prepared and unstressed. When studying, I recommended using flashcards on Anki (I actually made my decks as I was listening to lecture) and looking for patterns in the material. It is impossible to memorize everything unless you have a photographic memory, so making mnemonics (e.g., "the nerves in the branchial plexus spell "MARMU"") or other memorization pathways ("all muscles in the posterior calf are innervated by the tibial nerve") is really helpful and will keep you sane. This course can also be pretty dry: lectures are really just an introduction to the material, and you need to do most of the learning on your own, and the learning that you do is all muscle names, organ placement and function, etc. It's the anatomy version of a physics or math "plug–and–chug" for a whole semester. Make sure you find the material inherently interesting, otherwise it'll be difficult to motivate yourself to learn all the material. That being said, as challenging as I felt the course was, I felt really rewarded by working so hard and learning things that felt so applicable and important to know about. Every flashcard I learned felt useful –– there was just a lot of them!

HOWEVER!! but however––––– you have to. HAVE to be consistent with learning the content throughout the course. They restructured it this year and I think its much better. But don't be caught up in 'its just memorization'. It takes time, effort and self discipline to do this course. All in all, I emerged to love this course, it was not stressful like other courses have been in the past.
