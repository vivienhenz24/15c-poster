This is a lovely class and I would highly recommend it. Prof Liu splits "neuroengineering" into a few categories: observing the brain, probing the brain, and integrated approaches combining these technologies. You can expect to spend about 1/3 of the semester on each one of these.

Prof Liu is a very effective lecture –– it feels like he's put a lot of thought into what he is going to present and teaches in a very clear and understandable manner. He is always happy to stop for questions anytime.

The lab is super interesting. This year it was tissue clearing of mouse brain sections and then laser confocal microscopy to perform 3D connectivity mapping (basically 3D imaging and reconstruction of the neural structure of the mouse brain). Very cool experience, which was a highlight of the course.

Assignments were short prelab/postlab reports, and then 'journal club' midterm presentations where you will present with a group on one of about seven papers provided by Prof Liu. He emphasized time and time again that if you wanted to get anything out of the course, it is vital to read the papers. It is through this exercise that you come to truly understand where the field is now. It is quite easy to just read your own paper and present on it, but I would stand with the professor on this account –– make the extra effort to read as many papers as you can. Your grade does not depend on this, but your understanding will definitely deepen.

I found the presentations to be good assignments, however because everyone must present on their papers, it did end up taking up a significant portion of class time, which was a little unfortunate. Also, *do not go over the time limit in your presentations*. Prof Liu is very strict about them and likes to keep class on time.

Final projects were fun, I liked the idea of having us write NIH–style grant proposals for novel research. Talk to Prof Liu about your project ideas –– he is a wonderful resource and was very helpful and thoughtful.

Prof Liu really is a leader in this field, and knows his stuff quite well. This class was a pleasure, and I'd highly recommend it!
