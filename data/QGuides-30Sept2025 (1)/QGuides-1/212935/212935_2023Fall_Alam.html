Familiarity with theoretical texts would be helpful for the first couple of weeks, otherwise might be tough to participate in class in the first couple of weeks. Other than that small note, this course is fantastic and brings together some of the smartest and most thoughtful people. The "case studies" in class are great, there is some discussion around more current events as well. The class is long but honestly conversation was so great it didn't feel like 3 hours. If you are interested in topics of health and borders, highly recommend. If you are thinking of becoming a doctor, even more highly recommend!
This course was truly excellent. If you have the opportunity to take it, register immediately. Not only did the course really critically engage with crucial issues of our time, it did so while centering humanity and fostering open discussion and inquiry. Thank you Prof. Alam for a once–in–a–lifetime class.
This course is one of the three best courses I've taken throughout my time at Harvard and, hands down, the best HistSci course I've taken at Harvard (as a HistSci concentrator). Professor Alam is an exceptional professor, and this syllabus stretches your mind and allows you to interrogate current issues in migration with a fresh approach.
This class in incredible! Professor Alam is a great professor and the topics we covered in this class are super important. Some of the theoretical content at the beginning of the course was difficult at first, but Professor Alam explained it a really effective way that helped me understand it much better. I definitely recommend taking this class!
This course is wonderful! We learned about borders, hospitality, biopolitics, and interrelation between migratory flows and health/healthcare. 100% one of the most thought–provoking classes I have taken here at Harvard. Dr. Alam does an excellent job facilitating discussion smoothly and breaking down complex theory in a manner that is understandable and applicable to various historical contexts we discuss in the latter half of the semester. The workload is manageable and the material is so interesting––make sure to do the readings so you can fully engage during seminar because you truly get what you put in. Very rewarding. I can't recommend it enough!
This is SUCH a good class. It can feel very long, but Professor Alam is so smart and the discussions always felt really meaningful. I feel like I learned so much, and there was a lot of creativity and flexibility allowing us to focus on the issues we really cared about.
This was a great course to take as a non histsci concentrator. The material was very interesting and I always enjoyed our discussions, which is notable given that it was a nearly 3 hour course. Professor Alam is fantastic and does a great job of directing discussion while also generating excitement for the topics we covered.
Professor Alam is truly a wonderful teacher, and it is such a joy to take a class with her. Discussions were very engaging, and for the most part it did not feel like the 2 hour 45 min class dragged on too long. everyone is engaged and supported, and participation is rewarding. The readings can sometimes feel overly–technical but they all hold deep insights once you get past the mud.
A must take at Harvard!
