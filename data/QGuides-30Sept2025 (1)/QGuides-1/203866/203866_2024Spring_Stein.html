Stein is an amazing teacher. Participate in class. Do the readings seriously. This was really a great experience. I learned so much.
This class is great for engaging in a broad range of academic finance papers. Professor Stein is great at teaching and running the class; he's also very accessible outside of class, super knowledgeable about everything finance, and is overall very nice and helpful. The papers themselves are definitely nontrivial and it will take a committed number of hours each week to read them (even just the required ones) and to have relevant thoughts/questions to bring to class. If you are willing to put in the time, you'll get a lot out of the discussion. The term paper is also quite fun but you'll realize that finance research is actually quite challenging in that it's actually quite hard to find significant results.
This is a great junior tutorial if you are interested in the stock market and arbitrage. It is not a behavioral econ class but covers some of those principles through a different lens, less than I was expecting though. You have so much freedom with the final project and the papers you read in class give a lot of great ideas. Professor stein is a really interesting lecturer, even about topics that are less engaging. I enjoyed coming to class each week and enjoyed the weekly readings. It is easy to stay on top of weekly tasks but make sure you are staying ahead of the final paper or it sneaks up on you.
One of my favorite classes at Harvard!
Awesome class. Prof. Stein is a very engaged professor, and he significantly emphasizes authentic discussion during class time. The class content prepares you well to undertake a finance term paper, which is valuable for graduate school or starting a senior thesis.
ECON 980Z is an amazing course! I came in with very little finance background and still learned a ton. Professor Stein is one of the best lecturers in the Econ department that I have ever learned from and he is genuinely invested in students' learning and success. I think this class is particularly informative if you are interested in graduate school in economics as you have the chance to work on a paper and experience the research process first–hand. Unlike other areas of economics, financial economics has a lot of great datasets available and with some experience with STATA/R, you should be able to find interesting results worth writing a paper on. 100% recommend for anyone looking to learn more about economics research and/or finance.
This class was a great experience. Professor Stein is so humble and passionate about teaching. It was great opportunity to learn from him. Definitely try hard to understand the material before the class in order to fully understand the lectures. It can sometimes be difficult to follow along. However, with some effort, anyone can take a significant amount from this class. The final paper was such an amazing experience. Stein was very encouraging. Your hard effort will be rewarded.
From what I've heard from friends, this is by far the best Junior tutorial. Stein is incredible (friendly and very smart) and class discussions add a lot to your understanding. Start thinking about your research paper early and do all of the readings. Also, if you don't have a finance background, don't shy away. However, this is not a behavioral economics class, it's mostly about markets.
If you're not a finance bro, it would be nice to familiarize yourself with certain terms before you take the course. Stuff starts right off the bat, so be prepared!
Take this course! While it may seem a bit challenging at first (or a lot) in its complexity of topics and models, just try to remain focused and grasp as many and as much of the concepts presented in class as possible, and do the same with the associated papers we read prior to each class. You will learn a lot more this way! Also, make sure to start early on the final project – you will thank yourself if you do!
I came into this class knowing nearly nothing about finance, but the class does a great job of being accessible regardless of background. The main thing is to be open to reading lots of research papers; the class is built on reading research papers and learning about different ways the behavioral finance literature has explored particular phenomena theoretically and empirically before its your turn to answer your own question with the final project. I really liked this class even though it was on a subject I had no prior background with, and professor Stein is so so nice, funny, and supportive. Unless you are strongly opposed to the idea of reading academic papers about finance for an entire semester, you should consider taking this!
Great class if you're interested in learning more about behavioral finance readings/weekly responses are very doable, and one of the best parts of the class is getting to see everyone's presentations on their paper in the last two weeks.
Super awesome and fun class, would highly recommend to anyone who wants to understand more about financial markets and investing!
