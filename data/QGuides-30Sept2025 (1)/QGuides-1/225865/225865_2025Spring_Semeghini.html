What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class gets you to the forefront of quantum computing platforms and focuses on trapped ion, superconducting qubits, neutral atom platforms. The homework assignments are challenging, but you'll learn a good amount if you go to class and pay attention
I highly recommend this class for anyone who wants to learn about state–of–the art qubit platforms. And as compared to 260, this is more relaxed and takes a hardware–focused approach.

The workload is quite manageable. You get to learn from a professor who is extremely up–to–date on the field as well as an impressive set of guest lecturers who work on different platforms.

The lectures are fast–paced confusing, though because the class tries to cover an immense amount of material, and it doesn't help that Giulia generally doesn't provide lecture notes or any supporting material.
Very fast paced, covers a wide breadth of material. expect to read many recent publications.
