One of the best courses I have ever taken at Harvard. Balances well with other classes, and in general is very forgiving in terms of course difficulty and content. You WILL learn in this class, and you will enjoy it. I definitely recommend this if you're on the fence between 12b, 15b, or 50b.

PROFESSOR: (4/5)
(5/5) | PERSONALITY
(4/5) | KNOWLEDGEABILITY
(4/5) | COMMUNICATION
Louis, Kat, and Steve are a great team, and are great in lecture. Louis has great humor and is very engaging, and Kat and Steve are great resources to ask for help or clarification.

LECTURE: (5/5)
(5/5) | ENGAGEMENT
(5/5) | CONTENT
Great demonstrations and lectures that make it worthwhile to come to class, but in the case that you can't attend, they are recorded and slides are uploaded to Canvas. Lecture slides are easy to look back on and a great resource for homework.

SECTION: (5/5)
(5/5) | USEFULNESS
Never had a bad experience with office hours. Staff is always so helpful and willing to help. Have no fear, if you seek help, your plight will be solved.

HOMEWORK: (5/5)
(3/5) | DIFFICULTY (MEDIUM)
(3/5) | TIME EXPECTATION (MEDIUM)
(5/5) | LECTURE PREPARATION
(5/5) | FEEDBACK
Homework reinforces concepts and is analytically–focused. PSets are meant to show that your conceptual understanding translates mathematically. A lot of them can be done purely based on formulas and equations found in slides, but the kicker is, you have to know how to use them, so pay attention in class! Definitely a great idea to go to office hours, but most of the problems on the PSets can be done without going to office hours. Some coding–based problems which are fairly interesting. Gradescope feedback is IMMACULATE.

EXAMS: (5/5)
(3/5) | DIFFICULTY (MEDIUM)
(4/5) | LENIENCY
(5/5) | FEEDBACK
You get 2 reassessments per exam, of which you only have to do questions you got wrong. This is an incredible system that prioritizes learning and diminishes student stress over grades. Questions are on the same difficulty as homework and are pretty manageable in general; you don't have to study an absurd amount to do well on the tests. You get study guides beforehand too which is pretty awesome. Gradescope feedback hits your errors right on the head which is pretty important for conceptual understanding.

LABS: (4/5)
(3/5) | DIFFICULTY (MEDIUM)
(5/5) | ENGAGEMENT
(4/5) | COURSE RELEVANCE
Labs were based in JupyterLab and were data–analysis–based than conceptual reinforcing, which I wasn't a super fan of, but I had great TAs that really broke down the labs into easy–to–digest components and made the overall lab experience pretty streamlined.
This is a good physics course, especially when taught by Professor Louis. He does a great job explaining concepts in lecture and office hours, and is very understanding and willing to answer questions. The problem sets and assessments are quite manageable. The Python coding aspects of the course can be a bit difficult at times; Dr. Ledbetter and Dr. Adams' office hours are most helpful in this regard. Having taken Physics 15a, I appreciated how the pace and structure of PS12b allowed for much more complete (albeit less in–depth) understanding of the course material.

Demos during lecture are really cool, and instructors are really nice, so you can definitely get through it.
