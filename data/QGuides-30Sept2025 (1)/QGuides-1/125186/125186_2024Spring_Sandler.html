A good class if you are interested in poetry. There is a community engagement section for 3 weeks which takes longer than the alotted section time, so keep that in mind, although I found that the community engagement was a very welcome addition to the class.
DO NOT take this gened for a grade, there are a bunch of others that will take less of your time and will guarantee a better outcome. The grading is incredibly subjective and highly corresponds to whether you hit the points that your TF wanted you to talk about with very little room for the freedom of your own argumentation; you will also be harshly punished for the most minor mistakes and the feedback is rarely constructive enough for you to understand what were the exact reasons why you got a low grade. Meaning that so often you just don't know how to improve your writing or what in the grand scheme of things (not just a particular spot in the paragraph) can you change to become a better writer. The assignments are a pain, you might pour hours into them and it will still be a hit or miss. Also, at the end of the semester, we had both the final paper (called "portfolio") and the take–home exam which together amounted to over 20 pages of work – this just seemed really excessive for a gened. On another note, Professor Sandler is a lovely human being and a great professor. Wednesday's classes have been one of my favorite parts of the week (NOT the sections). She also puts enormous effort into bringing guest speakers to a few lectures which were all very enriching experiences. That doesn't compensate, however, for what felt like really unfair treatment in terms of grading or how much I improved over the semester.
If you'd like to take this course be prepared and put some work into the writing assignments because most of your grade depends on them so those grades will likely reflect your final grade. If you're a good writer this class will likely be a breeze but if not you'll likely have to put more work in to get the same result.

This course will really teach you a TON about poetry. This was not an easy class, but I really enjoyed it. While I've taken several English classes at Harvard, I haven't taken a poetry class before. I never really liked poetry compared with novels: it often seemed unapproachable and opaque. But after taking this class, I think I have more practice reading and analyzing poetry, and I feel more confident that I will be able to enjoy poems that I encounter in the future. The readings are all greatit has become a goal of mine to revisit some of the poems which we didn't discuss as much. Overall, I think this class is a great GENED if you're interested at all in exploring contemporary poetry.

This course is 100% accessible to those who don't study the humanities if you buy in and want to learn more. This course is the definition of the more you put in, the more you get out. If you treat this course like oh I just need to get the assignments in or I won't do any readings you're doing yourself a serious disservice because the poems are fire, and the level of care and detail the teaching staff shows in each assignment is done because it allows you to grow – don't waste it! When we took the course you should read the syllabus and see but it's not all creative and instead you spend time responding to poems through writing assignments, making a podcast or writing a slide on a poem – don't worry you do get the chance to be creative in the portfolio but this isn't that kind of course where everything is creative.
