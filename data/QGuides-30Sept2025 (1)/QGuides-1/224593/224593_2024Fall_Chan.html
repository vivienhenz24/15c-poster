What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Super interesting course and amazing prof! He's thought through the concepts enough that he definitely is confident in his own opinion, which he will emphasize a lot sometimes, including strongly questioning your dissenting opinions. However, he also fostered a lot of open conversation just among the whole class. He encouraged everyone to speak up and tried to teach life skills in addition to "Performing the Orient" knowledge. The content covering racism in ballet, music, ballet history, Asian history, and more was fascinating. It seemed to really connect with everyone. He also gave us the opportunity to see live Boston Ballet performances, which was very fun. It is a decent amount of work, because some weeks he'll assign a whole book or a 2hr ballet and substantial readings. Also, you must write a 10 and 20 page paper.
This course was more work than I expected – readings were a lot longer than I expected and writing the papers took a lot of effort. However, it was deeply rewarding to dive so deeply into the niche of the ballet world.
I hope this class comes back to Harvard. It's so relevant– if the college truly cares about "productive discourse", they should be turning to courses like these.
Overall really loved it!! Was a little more intense than I was expecting at times but learned a lot!
Phil is really great. I came into the class with no experience with ballet and he always made sure the material was accessible for people with or without ballet experience. Many of the topics that we discuss are about bigger topics that relate to society including race and capitalism. Overall, really great discussions and the class ended up being quite close–knit.
Definitely no ballet background needed––but experience in writing longer research papers and finding obscure material to cite is important. If Phil is teaching this class again, you'll find him really knowledgeable. He tends to leave very encouraging comments and feedback and you really get a sense of your own growth over the course of the class. Generally, it is very thought–provoking for anyone interested in matters of "the West" versus "the East", as well as chronological history or how historical events like wars directly impact art. If you have room in your schedule, I couldn't recommend it more (colorful, fun, lots of music and dance–video–watching in class!). The workload isn't heavy week–to–week but if you don't start your papers at least a week in advance, you won't have a good time (as good sources are hard to find and WILL take you a day or two at minimum).
Chan is truly phenomenal. best tdm class at harvard hands down. the readings and performances we watch every week are so interesting, eye–opening, and fun. LOVE LOVE LOVED this class. learned and grew so much–––also healed my relationship with ballet!
