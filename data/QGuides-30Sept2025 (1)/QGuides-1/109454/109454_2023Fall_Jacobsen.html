This class is great. The material is very interesting, and the assignments test your knowledge and thinking abilities. The course staff are great.
Chem 105 is perhaps the most difficult course in the chemistry department, but it is SO worth it if you love organic chemistry. Professor Jacobsen is the BEST lecturer I have had in the chem department, and he is also very accessible for questions during his weekly office hours. His lecture slides are second to none and an incredible resource to have access to, both for studying and for a future career in chemistry research. Section is very rewarding and each week introduces a new classic paper that widens your understanding of the history of the field and your ability to read papers quickly. Yes the psets and exams are very difficult, but studying for them together with a group made for a great bonding experience. If you are considering pursuing further research in organic chemistry, TAKE THIS CLASS!
Really great course! Tough, but you will learn so much and I can't imagine being an organic chemistry graduate student without having this foundation. Even if you don't plan on being a chemistry graduate student, if you're interested in getting a greater understanding of mechanisms in chemistry this class will be enlightening.
Take this class! It really deepened my appreciation for organic chemistry–– the teaching staff is so incredible, Professor Jacobsen is a fantastic professor, and they really, really care about your success and your learning! It's intense but you'll learn so much. This was one of my favorite classes I've taken at Harvard.
TAKE IT! Especially if you took Chem 17/27!

It is a truly eye–opening class that goes above and beyond anything I have taken before. If you enjoyed organic chemistry, you were wrong because you need to take a class like this to fully immerse yourself in the subject to fall in love.

The one caveat for 17/27 kids is that there are select topics that would be helpful to review beforehand (others are discussed in more detail in lecture, so you would not be behind)

1. Pericyclic reactions (~3 lectures)
2. Organometallic (Just review the fundamentals of inorganic chemistry – the class breezes through them)

Lectures are fast–paced. Personally, annotating the lecture slides briefly while paying attention and then rewriting your notes over an hour or two later is the way to go about it in my opinion.

Prof. Jacobsen is a wonderful professor who genuinely cares so very much for the material and his students, so much so that he even shows up to section (the most enthusiastic professor I have ever had!)

TAKE IT!
GREAT COURSE! DEFINITELY TAKE IT IF YOU LIKE ORGO! This course gives you a way more fundamental understanding of organic chemistry than arrow pushing, and it tries its best to approach this fundamentally empirical science from a deeper level and draws concepts from physical chemistry to build a first–principle driven understanding. and Prof. Jacobsen is the most approachable and caring professor I have ever met, and he really cares about his students!
The class is immensely rewarding in terms of the skills you acquire throughout the semester. However, there is so much material that it sometimes feels overwhelming, and in a sense a bit useless, because it is clear that you won't be able to retain quite a large portion of it in the long run.
If you like organic chemistry and are open to a challenge, take this class!! I am a pre–med, non–chem concentrator who took Chem 17 and 27, but found that I really enjoyed those classes and wanted to learn more about organic chemistry. I was intimidated by this course because there are a lot of chem concentrators and graduate students, but I felt motivated to put in the work because it truly is so interesting and rewarding when you find that you are able to understand things. I can say with absolute certainty that this is my favorite course I have taken at Harvard, and I found every lecture genuinely so fascinating. You can definitely take this course if Chem 17/27 is your only chem background, you just need to be willing to put in the work. Professor Jacobsen cares very deeply about his students and wants everyone to succeed, so take advantage of his office hours and the TF office hours to aid your understanding. This class has a reputation for being very challenging, and it definitely is, but it is so worth it if it's a topic you are interested in.
Difficult and hard but useful
It's incredibly difficult, but well worth it. Professor Jacobsen is a great teacher and cares a lot for the course and the TFs are very supportive. Although I felt the course to be very challenging throughout, I left it with a much greater understanding and passion for chemistry. That's the best type of class in my mindone that is challenging, while simultaneously deepening your understanding and vigor for the subject matter.
This is the hardest class I have ever taken at Harvard. That being said, I highly recommend this course to anyone who wants a more in–depth understanding of organic chemistry and "why" certain reactions happen the way they do. You will learn so much in so little time, which is partially why I found the course quite difficult. I am so glad that I pushed myself to take such a challenging class, especially since I came in with a Chem 17/27 background, and it feels so rewarding to reflect back on what I've learned now that I'm at the end of the semester.

If you do decide to take this class, and I recommend that you do, then make sure to form a study group with the other undergrads taking the class and attend as many office hours as you can. Use the annotated notes to solidify concepts and maybe use Clayden's organic chemistry textbook if you're still struggling with the fundamentals of a concept. The course staff is so smart and willing to help you – they want to see you succeed! And if you put in the time and effort, you will be fine.
This is an amazing class for anyone looking to go into the field of organic chemistry, or simply looking to further their understanding. It is also a lot of fun to take!
Hardest but most worthwhile class you can take
A good course for a solid foundation in research focused on synthetic organic chemistry.
Try to get ahead on starting psets so that you can go to office hours and ask questions. The instructor goes through material very quickly so be sure to review the lectures and understand each slide before the exam.
This class was the most difficult class I have ever taken, but I loved it. It may have had an impossible midterm that I did cry about after (not even half) finishing, 85 slide lectures that we got through 40 slides of, psets that I would never have finished without office hours, but through it all I had an amazingly supportive community in my friends in this class. We spent most Fridays talking to Professor Jacobsen in his office hours, sometimes about class content but sometimes about random other things. Throughout the entire class, I always felt that everything I was learning was so relevant, so engaging, that everything we were learning had a reason, and a thread to follow into the world of research today. Truly the best class I've taken at Harvard, but certainly not to be undertaken lightly.
