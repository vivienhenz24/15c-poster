What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
The premise of this class is tough to explain, but just trust it. Valeria is a wonderful professor, and the course is interdisciplinary in the best way: we read academic articles, essays, fiction, and poetry from all over the world, listened to audio, watched documentaries, and thought about sound from all kinds of different perspectives. The atmosphere of the class was friendly and collaborative, and it drew a fascinating group of students from around the university. If you're interested in a course that will stretch your mind in addition to giving you the chance to express yourself creatively, this is a great choice.
This class pushed us to have a truly interesting engagement with sound and listening in the world. You will not think about sound or reading the same way again, and that's a beautiful and exciting thing.
This course, or any taught by Professor Luiselli, is what an education in the Humanities is about. Don’t go into it expecting a workshop that’s focused on the production of writing. Think of it like the fuel or the nutrients for your writing. You will come out of this course a better scholar, with an understanding of diverse ideas and texts, which you have explored in an environment which cares about your individual scholarship and the lens you bring as an individual. This is a class for serious writers; more than that, this is a class for thinkers. I cannot recommend this class, or Professor Luiselli, highly enough!
Sound is expressed through patterns. This course explores all elements of this through writing and audio experiences. You’ll grow as a human and a writer through the tooling and explorations in the course
