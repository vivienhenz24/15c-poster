Without question, GOV 10 was one of the best courses I have taken while at Harvard.

While readings were sometimes dense, theory isn't something that you should just skim over. It should be digested meticulously and internally debated. Therefore, this sometimes made doing the weekly readings/Perusall assignments take longer than some may like, but it's ultimately 100% worth it and will pay off when you exit the course with a rich, newfound understanding of the world.

In my personal opinion, theory is one of those topics where some just "get" it while others may have to work a little harder. I absolutely fell into the latter, particularly due to not having any prior experience with theory or philosophy. However, the course is set up in a way that allows everyone to grow, fulfill their true potential, and eventually exceed the standards.

Both section leaders were absolutely amazing, and a specific shoutout to Savannah for hosting sections that always propagated unique debates (side note: taking this class during the onset of AI is a once–in–a–lifetime opportunity).

I left every single lecture and section thinking to myself, "Wow. I'm surrounded by some of the most amazing minds in the world." There are several classes I've taken at Harvard that I believe truly feel changed my life, but this one takes the cake, by far.
Gov 10 is a 10/10 course. You will find yourself relating class materials to other classes or real–world conversations. Must–take class!
This class I think would be better defined as Democracy: All the Political Thought Behind it. I think I went into this course thinking that we would cover a variety of political theory that wasn't just about democracy. This class was super good thought. Eric is so sweet and very responsive inside and outside class.

PARTICIPATE! He just wants to hear your thoughts and chat about them. The readings are really long and the once a week lecture may see like you have time to dilly dally and push off the readings to later, but get ahead of the curve and read after class is over.

My section leader Savannah was such a baddie, but I think she's done TF this course. I'm not sure if Hadley is not TFing next time this course is offered, but also super baddie. She was super real. I forgot to do the back half of my exam and she let me come back into the exam room to finish my test.

The assignments are very reasonable. The assignments where we have to write papers, there is a ton of support from the teaching staff that allows you to no just write a rough draft but also get feedback to improve it for a final draft; I find that to be pretty rare in other classes here.

Definitely take this class!
Absolutely take this class! Professor Beerbohm is truly one of the best professors on this campus, and he will not be teaching this course for a couple years. Despite this, the course will still make you fall in love with theory and thinking about the most basic aspects of our society. So long as you do the readings in a timely manner, attend lecture and section, and come into everything with an open mind, this course will be transformative. The readings are not particularly difficult, they can be dense at times, which makes it slightly daunting to complete, but that's what comes with any theory course. Some of these readings truly changed my perspective on the world around me, and it would be a disservice to not only the professor, but also to yourself to not read every assigned text.
At first, the class may seem challenging, and you may feel out of place if you have never been introduced to political theory. However, I promise you will learn, and Professor Beerbohm will be there to guide you if necessary. This class is really rewarding if you make an effort to engage with readings and ask questions in lecture.
