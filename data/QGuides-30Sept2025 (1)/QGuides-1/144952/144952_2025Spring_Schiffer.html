This was a fair class overall. Professor Schiffer was a kind and accommodating instructor. He genuinely cared about student well–being and was flexible with deadlines, which was greatly appreciated.

However, the course materials left much to be desired. The published lecture notes were often unclear, and the study resources for exams, including answer keys, were difficult to follow. Practice problems frequently did not reflect the style or difficulty of the actual exams. For example, on the second exam, we were expected to solve a Navier–Stokes problem as a function of two variables, despite only having practiced problems involving a single variable. This disconnect made exam preparation frustrating and unnecessarily challenging.

Better alignment between practice problems and exams, along with clearer notes and solutions, would significantly improve the course.
