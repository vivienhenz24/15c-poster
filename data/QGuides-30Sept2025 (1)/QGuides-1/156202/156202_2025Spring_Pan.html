Choose your group carefully, as your experience will largely depend on them. The professor frequently canceled class and literally didnt respond to anything and left the states for the month before the capstone presentations, which made it challenging to stay on track. I was required to take this course, and unfortunately, some group members didnt contribute, with little accountability enforced. That said, if youre consistent with your own submissions and stay in touch with your TF, its very manageable and can be an easy A.
Great class where you can apply a lot of machine learning techniques to real life problems
This class has so much potential, and I was so excited to take it. Unfortunately, as it is being currently run under Weiwei, it will be a massive, massive disappointment unless something changes. A few years ago, the projects in this class would be exciting and from a number of different partners, including companies like Microsoft. This semester, unfortunately, almost every single project was healthcare related, because that is Weiwei's work. It was quite clear that many of these partners had no interest in the class, and simply did not care about the projects. Many project teams that I spoke to did not receive data and necessary information from the partners until almost the end of the semester; this was also true for my team's project. There was a general sense of complete disengagement from the partners. The class was also poorly organized – there was hardly any context about assignments, and TFs / project mentors themselves did not know anything about the assignments and deadlines. We were supposed to have presentations and exciting guest lectures from industry partners, but not even a single one ever happened. Weiwei is also just so unresponsive – she simply didn't show up to our group's meetings twice (and didn't even tell us that she wouldn't be coming). There was simply no communication about most things – we were not even told about the exact timings and details of the poster session until the day of the session. Ironically enough, Weiwei and the TFs did not even show up to our poster session – we stood there for 3 hours presenting to random people passing by. In our weekly check–ins with her, it was clear that Weiwei had no idea what our project was about and where it was headed, which meant that we never got any concrete advice or sense of direction from her. I know, of course, that the capstone is meant to be a class where you own a lot of the decisions regarding the project and are expected to do things independently. But the problem was that we had a completely unresponsive partner and a completely unresponsive instructor in Weiwei, who simply didn't do anything. Not to mention that we didn't even have data to work with for most of the semester.

Overall, I think that this class under Weiwei is a complete mess. The CSE department really needs to figure this stuff out and make sure that this doesn't happen in the future. MIT has a similar capstone project, and I've heard from their students that that class is one of the highlights of their MIT experience. It is a shame that the Harvard version is anything but that. To be fair to Weiwei, she is extremely smart and a wonderful lecturer. And I'm sure that she is also really busy with other things. But that doesn't mean that the capstone project (which is described as a 'mini thesis' for our program) can be relegated to being an afterthought by the instructor. I really hope that the department finds a way to make this course better.
