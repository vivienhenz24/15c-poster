What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Your experience is almost entirely based on your group and project partner.
This is a great class for actually practising the skills you learn in the rest of your degree
The capstone course this semester showed some room for improvement in its organization. There were moments when our group's project seemed to lean more towards academic research rather than the anticipated industry–focused capstone experience. Progress initially felt sluggish, with the first three weeks dedicated exclusively to literature review. However, the course became increasingly engaging towards the end, especially when we delved into actual coding. This is somewhat expected when the project veers towards academic research. Nevertheless, I had hoped for a greater involvement of industry partners in contrast to the academic research groups.
If you have the option, take this class! It's incredible.
Experience varies greatly depending on your project, partner, project mentor, TF and teammates. The project might not be well–defined or technically impressive but that's real world for ya. You likely gain more presentation, communication, project management and collaboration skills than technical skills, but again it varies.
Most students are in this course because of the master or the secondary field.
I would encourage students taking this course to reach out to the instructors before the course starts and try and propose a partner and project that is aligned with the students interests while meeting the capstone requirements.
