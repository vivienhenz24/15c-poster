What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Take this class! This is one of (if not the best) political theory subfield classes you can take. Don't be daunted by being taught also at HLS and HKS, it's truly amazing. Professor Unger arrives at the class, sits down and speaks in an unparalleled stream of consciousness for 2 hours every week. The students make interesting questions at the end and there can be really productive discussions. Unger's ideas are revolutionary and they can really broaden your horizons. Take advantage of the section to discuss the papers in advance and it will be fine. The long final paper can seem scary but you can reuse some of the ideas you wrote in the midterms and the TF this year (Tim) also helped us understand how viable our ideas are.
This is a great class to learn about progressivism/progressive ideas beyond the currently imagined progressive reforms. Lecture can be tedious as it is Unger speaking from memory for 2 hrs, but his thoughts are always interesting. Very light on assignments, just do the readings.
It is probably not worth taking this class unless you are a diehard Unger fan? It's entirely centered on Unger's thoughts and so I found it to ultimately not be that useful of a course.
Hands down the best way to fulfill the requirement. No doubt about it!
Pay attention to lecture! And be prepared to not receive the most prescriptive, concrete examples of change, but rather small examples of them to engage and debate with. It is a start to alternative thinking, and a decent course to fulfill the political theory subfield requirement with.
