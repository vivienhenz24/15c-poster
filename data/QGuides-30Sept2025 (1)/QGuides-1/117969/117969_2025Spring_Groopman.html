I would recommend this class very highly.
This was my favorite course I've taken at Harvard, and one of my favorite educational experiences ever it's a perfect blend of medicine, ethics/philosophy, and literary/journalistic analysis, so there's something for everyone and you get to meet all types in this class. Dr. G is so kind and knowledgable, and is an incredible guide, mentor, and resource. I would 100% recommend this course to anyone regardless of if you're interested in medicine, you'll get something really meaningful out of it.
If you are at all interested in medical writing, TAKE THIS CLASS! Seriously. Dr. G is an amazing instructor, he's very personable, and he gets to know everyone really well. The readings can be long, and there's a fair amount of writing to do, but if you're passionate about the topic you will never dread an assignment. This class introduced me to a great breadth of medical literature, and I often found myself doing more reading than required just because of how much I loved it. I learned SO MUCH about medicine, cancer, AIDS, psychology, STEM cell research, and how to write effectively.

If you're reading this, you're probablyyyyy pre–med and the rest of your course schedule is packed with science courses like LS1b and PS11 and PS12. I ask you to take a step back from your psets and remember *why* you want to study medicine – for the people. This class will teach you so much about the human condition, about health and illness, love and hate, life and death. Take it, run with it, don't be afraid to ask questions or say you don't understand something.
This class is super chill, not much work (just weekly reading and writing, and the writing stops towards the end), and Dr. G is an incredible professor. He teaches with sincere wisdom, kindness, generosity, and dedication. He picks genuinely interesting stories for us to read, and it's an enjoyable experience on Thursday after lunch to wind down the day reading with him. The class isn't just about illness, but about the social, historical, and personal difficulties that come along with it. Dr. G does an excellent job making the class a more broader, applicable picture.
Oh, and he also brings a treat to eat every class!!!! (Hope he keeps doing that)
This was the best class I have ever taken, and quite possibly the best I ever will take. If you have the opportunity to take this class, 100% do it.
It is a wonderful class– very engaging and the professor is amazing.
