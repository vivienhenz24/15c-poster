It's a great class and I'd definitely recommend it, AS LONG AS you have 10+ hours each week to work on paintings; I took it with 3 pset classes, which I would not recommend doing. These will be a very chill 10 hours since you're probably just listening to music and applying paint on a canvas (so very different from working on a strenuous 10–hour pset) but you do need to set aside that time, ideally across at least two separate sessions, to be able to finish the weekly painting homeworks. You'll be fine if you, like me, have zero painting experience since the class is graded on how much effort you put in and how much you improve (not how good your paintings look). That said, most people who take the class probably do have lots of experience so it's easy to feel intimidated, especially because the class is taught "throw you in the deep end"–style and doesn't really teach painting technique. But I felt much more comfortable with oil painting (even painting human figures relatively accurately) by the end of the class! Also, both Matt and the section TFs (Maria especially) are wonderful people and very understanding of all kinds of circumstances. Overall: easy class, great instructors, but a LOT of work.
