What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
You're probably taking this course because you're an astrophysics concentrator so you have to. I recommend working with an advisor who has a lot of time to work with you (not a postdoctoral student or professor). Having a good/bad advisor is what makes/breaks the experience.
Very significant time investment, but definitely worth it. You should walk away from this class with an impressive paper, a crucial recommendation letter (from your research advisor), and a general confidence that you are capable of completing astrophysics research.
Depending on the research you choose, it will be a very time consuming class. There might be weeks where you easily spend 14+ hours to work on your research. If you take this class make sure you get results as soon as possible, it will really safe you in the long term. Overall, AY98 was an incredible introduction to research and the weekly guest lectures were super inspiring and interesting. This has been one of my favorite courses at Harvard.
This class was a wonderful way to gain exposure to the many subfields of astrophysics. This course really allowed me to gain exposure to the methods, the ways of investigating and thinking, and the many sub–fields of modern astrophysics, which is a huge field. I really appreciated the chance to speak with and ask questions of professors each week, that was truly special. I feel that I learned a lot from the class, not only about research fields but also about career paths in astronomy. Genuinely a useful and valuable course, especially if you're (probably) aiming to do research in astrophysics for the semester.
Think hard about which project you want to take on and WHO you want to work with at least one month before school starts. Getting to work one–on–one with some of the world's best astrophysicists is an opportunity few ever get and is what makes the Harvard Astronomy Department so special. Building a relationship with a notable professor will be your biggest takeaway. Also, produce as many results as possible in September/October because that first draft really sneaks up on you!
This is a good course, but you definitely get out what you put in. Finding a good research mentor early on is key, and staying on top of this very fast paced class. Prof. Chen is great, and it's very cool to hear from a new scientist every week.
