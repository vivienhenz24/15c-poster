What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This course is the stock–standard first grad algebraic geometry course, based on commutative algebra (so based on scheme theory and what you would see if you read Hartshorne Chapter II or Vakil) instead of complex analysis (where by this I mean building from complex manifolds following Griffiths–Harris as Professor Popa did last year). It was done very well. Ming Hao paced the material and lectures in the best possible way (taking into account the background of the students coming in), and the assignments were of a good length and comprehensive.

One gripe I had was that the assignments would change quite a lot since its initial release (so I'd submit my writeups, and then a day later questions get modified, added, removed, or typos get fixed). However, apparently this is an issue unique to me (everyone else was less than punctual with turning in homework, from what I heard), so this shouldn't be a demerit to the course at all. Overall, I really enjoyed getting the chance to revise this deeply important and fundamental material by working through the exercises and lecture notes Ming Hao prepared (having learned it before but only informally via my own reading).
Ming Hao is a wonderful teacher! Very clear lectures and helpful office hours. Would recommend taking 221 (commutative algebra) before taking this course, even though it isn't listed as a strict pre–req.
this is a good class. ming hao is very nice and very helpful in office hours. the psets and homework are good.

if you do want to take this class i would encourage you to stay on top of the homework. some aspects of this class were made quite frustrating because many students just wouldn't show up to class and/or wouldn't do the homework. this puts a lot of burden on the instructor, and frankly it was also just annoying not being able to discuss the course with anyone because everyone was behind. so please take this class only if you know you have the time for it.
Scheme theory is very interesting and has a rather different flavor from a lot of math you may have done before. The course provides a solid foundation in the topic. I took the course with Ming Hao, and he is a phenomenal instructor who cares a lot about his students' success. The workload was very reasonable.
