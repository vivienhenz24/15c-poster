What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Professor Popa is an excellent instructor who gives great intuition about everything he covers in is courses. Take this class.
Prof. Popa is a very effective lecturer and is able to strike a balance between rigor and intuition. Learning algebraic geometry from the complex–geometric viewpoint is very interesting and you get to see a lot of different subfields of math combined to produce amazing results.
This course is an excellent introduction to the analytic side of algebraic geometry, and pairs well with an introduction to scheme theory.
This class is very instructor dependent as there are many ways to approach the subject. You absolutely must take it, however! Algebraic geometry is the greatest thing ever no matter the perspective.
This is the best version of Math 232 by far (for at least the last five years). Usually it's a number theorist who does abstract scheme theory, leaving everyone with zero geometric intuition or grounding in the matter, but Popa's version really gets into the heart of the geometry. We start with complex analysis in several variables, and then move on to complex manifolds, and then eventually to compact Kahler manifolds. In the process, we develop lots of beautiful geometry, and we also gain an intuition about why we even care about schemes and sheaves and cohomology (i.e. we see why these are very useful things to consider by starting from the geometry, not the algebra). Popa is also the best lecturer I've had throughout Harvard, so if he is teaching this version of the class again, definitely take it!!!!!
