This class had more work than I was expecting, especially with the code since I felt like I didn't have a lot of CS background. But I would say the class is manageable, especially with friends in the class. I would say it was very engaging and overall a class that has a lot of help/office hours. The staff makes sure that the students are understanding the material and are able to help you out whether it's in the class or other worries you have.
A cool class to take if you are interested in the intersection of neuroscience and math/coding. We also got a deeper understanding of the workings of neural networks, which I found really interesting.
This is a great course. Kristina is an amazing person and professor and you will feel supported to succeed.
TAKE THIS COURSE! Dr. Penikis is great, and the material is super interesting and well–paced. You'll meet great people and get to work on fascinating self–led projects!
NEURO120 is a fantastic course that is more of a dip into computational neuroscience than a full immersion into it. The course covers a lot of material – some of which, if you have background in CS or courses like NEURO140 will already be known to you (such as neural networks) – sacrificing depth at the cost of breadth and thus leaving a lot up to the student. Don't get me wrong – you'll have basic understanding about every type of mathematical model and its application after every class, and that understanding together with the great python libraries will be in most cases sufficient to complete the homeworks, but expect to spend at least another couple of hours at home if you want to gain a full understanding of all the mathematics and intricacies behind the models, which often are assumed to be very quickly understood during class. If you are computational neuro student – you have to take this class. If you're a normal neuroscience student – I actually don't think it would be a bad idea for you either, as it would be more like an accelerated course in statistics and CS useful to your future research. If you're a CS student – might be fun to do what you've likely discussed much in class in an applied context.
Neuro 120 was an awesome , seminar–like class! Every lecture, students had opportunities to jump in the conversation and ask questions. We also had two very interesting guest lectures. For me, the final project was my favorite part of this course. Not only did my partner and I absolutely crush the final project but it was super fun discussing our ideas and thoughts with Dr. Penikis, the section leaders, and our classmates! I feel like we were able to contribute significant analysis with our final project while utilizing a lot of the concepts that we learned throughout the semester. The PSETs and assignments were difficult at–times but I felt that this difficulty was necessary for adequate retention of knowledge. Also, I'd say Dr. Penekis is a great lecturer and is really geared towards students (unlike other busy professors). The content of the class was amazing as well: got a very holistic, comprehensive views on specific parts of computational neuroscience (neural networks, UMAP, PCA, spectral analysis, LIF, functional connectivity analysis, etc).
