This class does a good job of introducing non–Swifties to Taylor Swift while keeping Swifties engaged in the content. I learnt a lot about music analysis, songs that she sang pre–Debut, other artists and their influence on her, novels and also the law and fashion of Taylor Swift. That being said, I have a few comments if you are thinking about this class.

We had the option of taking the class pass–fail and I chose to do it for a letter grade. Worst decision I made! Stephanie and the TFs expect that you write all your papers and assignments like if you are an English concentrator. I understand that it's part of the English Department but keep in mind most students who took this class are really just Taylor Swift fans studying various other subjects. It was also the first time around 90% of the class ventured into the English Department. So of course writing the papers were really difficult. It was very difficult to get an A on a paper in the class unless you know exactly what Stephanie wants in the paper (most likely if you took one of her classes before or if you are part of the English department). I let many of my friends read my paper before I submitted, and the Writing Center, all who said it was perfect and they thought the argument was clear, only to hear otherwise from this class. It was as if nothing could please the Teaching team. So if it is offered pass–fail, TAKE IT PASS–FAIL.

Secondly, lectures themselves were chaotic. Stephanie is a very entertaining lecturer but we often took a while to start and settle and lot of the content felt rushed. I think we tried to cram too many things into one class. Talking about an entire album and then an entire book in 75 minutes was almost impossible. I also think some of the extra readings and references didn't connect as strongly to Taylor or the class than we expected. In Stephanie's mind it made sense, but a lot of the TFs and even students could only losely see it. Once Stephanie started talking about a book or anything non–Taylor, the class started to lose focus and become distracted.

Guest lecturers were a lot of fun. I particularly enjoyed the week on fashion, and so did the class. Everyone was fully engaged. The issue was that often times when guest lecturers were invited to the class, they were only given 10–15 minutes to speak. Sometimes, I think that Stephanie should let them speak for longer and she herself speak more concisely. Students often had more questions and more interest in the niche topic the guest lecturers were speaking about.

We had a weekly assignment based on the particular album studies that week which was okay. It didn't take that long and was often fun. I think for sections, we could have done more fun activities. Not once did we make friendship bracelets, bake cookies from Taylor's 1989 era, or even take polaroids. I think section should have been dedicated to doing more fun activities while we discussed the content.

Overall, I do think the class was enjoyable but definitely needed more structure. I think grading should have been less harsh so look out for that if you plan to take the class. I think making the class into a Gen Ed is the best option for something of this nature.

Also, sadly Taylor herself didn't show.

I know the class seems weird but it is worth taking. I did not enter the course as a Taylor Swift fan, but I would consider myself one now. Aside from that, this class was great at focusing on topics other than Taylor Swift. We talked about music and how it's composed, how it comes off to a listener, what makes a song popular, how it brings people together, etc. We also did a lot of work with poetry and analyzing that in comparisons to music. So even though this class is called Taylor Swift, it is really more of a cross between an English and Music class that uses Taylor Swift to get the conversation started.
The enthusiasm that Stephanie has for the subject is infectious. Her lectures are also really well done – she is constantly making connections between Swift's work and other artists or literary figures. The course also had amazing guest speakers that added a lot of value. For example, we had a person from the Harvard Law School speak about the legal side of Taylor Swift's re–recordings. The TFs also brought incredibly interesting perspectives. For example, there were TFs who focused on musicology, culture around music, performance art, etc. – it made it extremely interdisciplinary. Stephanie also worked to make it an extremely welcoming environment. I recommend that you be somewhat of a fan of Taylor Swift's music if you are going to take this course. Although it is an interesting course in regards to examining a super fascinating pop culture phenomenon, you do spend a lot of time talking about Taylor and if you hate her it probably would not be a very enjoyable course. I also recommend you somewhat enjoy literature as well. People who expect to just talk about Taylor Swift for a few hours every week will be disappointed at the amount of literature you examine and the essay requirements. I also want to say this course taught me about so much more than Taylor Swift's connection to literary figures. I learned about the formula for creating a catchy pop song (which was one of the best lectures I've ever been to at Harvard), the cultural significance of Beyonce's album Cowboy Carter, and was introduced to so many musical movements that I had completely not been exposed to before. This class also genuinely made me a fan of poetry. I now write it on my own for fun where previously I had dismissed the subject entirely. It is a really enjoyable class!
