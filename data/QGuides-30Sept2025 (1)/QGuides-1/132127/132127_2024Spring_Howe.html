This class (at least if taught by Professor Howe) is harder than you think. I took (and got an A) in Stat 110, and this class still required a fair amount of effort. I was spending 6–7 hours a week on the weekly pset to get things correct and if often required going to office hours to check answers. This is also frustrating since all 10 of the psets add up to only 25% of your grade, which is the same as the midterm. The midterm (and final as well to some extent) was much harder than anticipated with barely any partial credit given at all. Thus even after doing well on all of the psets (and putting a lot of time into the class), my grade was in jeopardy after the midterm when I made a few fairly small mistakes. Psets can have a very fast turn around time, with some of them being released in class on Tuesday and due that same Friday afternoon. There is also both a final project and a final exam. Thus, budget more time for this course than you expect even if you have a strong math background, and don't expect to necessarily cruise to an A.

That being said, the curriculum was great and the topics covered are super useful if you have never seen them before. If you are planning on doing anything STEM (literally any field at all), statistics is incredibly important even just to read and understand papers. All the hypothesis testing, ANOVA , regression, etc. are super useful topics and necessary for research, industry work, etc. Even if you have seen these topics before, they are covered fairly in depth. You could potentially just read the textbook instead of talking this class if you are motivated enough, but that's not me. Overall decent class, very useful topics, but be prepared for bad grading on exams and to potentially be worry about your grade for no reason.
