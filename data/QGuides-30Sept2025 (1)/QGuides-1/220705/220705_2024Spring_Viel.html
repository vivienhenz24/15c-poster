What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course takes an idea and grows to a company, teaches you how many resources at the disposal we have as Harvard student with an idea. This class is perfect for anyone who has an idea of being an entrepreneur and creating a company.
If you have a seedling of an idea and even the slightest interest in entrepreneurship, I highly recommend this class. It is best for those people who either want to get something started off from the ground or want to go through the thought experiment of entrepreneurship. For those who already have a business started and need more specific advice or guidance, there are other classes that could potentially be a better fit (unless this class changes its structure). There are opportunities to pitch, write your own business plan, and talk to many impressive entrepreneurs. Also, the people made this class. All of my peers were working on incredible ideas and it was privilege to learn among them. The environment is fun, thoughtful, and non–judgmental. One of my best experiences at Harvard!
This is a great class with a minimal workload if you come in with even a tiny bit of progress on your venture, even if it's simply a very clear idea on what venture you'd want to pursue if you actually did build one. The guest speakers are engaging. The process of actually launching a venture can seem highly opaque, but this class really sheds a light on how you actually can get your company or organization off the ground.
If you have a venture you are serious about, I would recommend taking this course to think critically about its development and gain access to a good network of student founders and both Harvard and external advisers.
