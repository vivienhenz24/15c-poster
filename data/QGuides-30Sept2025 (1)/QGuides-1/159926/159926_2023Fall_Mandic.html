What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I would tell future students that there is a lot of discussion in class, so you should not take it if you are nervous to participate. I would also tell them that the final paper was challenging, for me at least, and there is quite a bit of reading.
This is definitely one of my favorite sociology courses that I have taken at Harvard. Professor Mandic is a great teacher and you can tell that he is very knowledgable/cares a lot about this topic. I loved learning about political sociology and would recommend to anyone interested in studying aspects of war or revolution.
This course was originally in the format of a lecture, but it was offered in the format of a seminar this semester. I wish there would have been more structure to the seminar, such as a lecture given by the professor at the beginning of the class or end of the previous class to inform our readings and discussion. I had no previous background in the course content/topics, so it felt a little difficult to actively participate in discussions where I didn't have much to say despite the readings given. Also, this class is only graded on participation, discussion posts, and a final paper. which is a little scary, especially since the final paper is free range.
Absolutely take this course! Professor Mandic is super knowledgeable, encourages the whole class to speak up and do our best, and has such great enthusiasm for all the class material. This class will definitley take some time to do the readings and at times the subjects can be hard to read due to emotional responses it may or may not elicit. But, it is a wonderful class that you will learn a lot from and I high recommend you try it out.
I would recommend this course to people interested in history. However, note that the final paper is one of the main assignments and is basically only discussed in the last class so I recommend going to office hours and getting an earlier start on your final paper ideas.
