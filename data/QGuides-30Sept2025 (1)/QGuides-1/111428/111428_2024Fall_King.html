Gov2020 is a great class to learn about the craft of research, both in terms of producing and disseminating it. Classes are very engaging, and it is a great cohort–building experience, as you get to know the research interests of other students. Gary and Noah were extremely generous with their time, and always interested in raising them to the next level. Take this course to think bigger and do research.
As a graduate student in political science at Harvard, this is a must–take course. How can you miss the opportunity to learn from one of the most successful political scientists alive and hear how to be successful in academia?
This course offers a rare curriculum focused around socialization to the social sciences (and academia) and I would recommend it to any G1. I really appreciated how it covers topics that students are expected to otherwise learn through osmosis. This includes research, data, and replication principles; how to choose a research topic; how to think about skills development over the course of your education (and career); presentation skills; and more. Moreover, it designed to help you learn and not overwhelm you, with a perfectly manageable workload. Takes this course!
Please take Gov 2020! Even though I took it as an AM–Gov undergrad, it was very relevant to whatever I have studied before and truly taught me a "hidden curriculum" that will help me beyond undergrad. I learned how to write a journal–publishable paper, and working with graduate students in all the class assignments opened my mind and now I strongly want to go to graduate school. Prof. King is also just the kindest soul you'll ever meet and he was always punctual in everything, available to give feedback and to chat, and this is a course that I really really enjoyed that I wish I could take again.
Please see my comments earlier. I would recommend this more as a G2 course, or a G1 course for those with some prior training in statistical inference and use of R, though it is definitely still helpful and accessible for those without. Having some projects you are already working on would be great as well, as you can then apply the lessons of the course to something concrete outside of a class assignment.
This is a great first class to grad school to learn both short–term skills and long–term wisdom in the field. It's good to enter the class with some research project / direction in mind + some methods training to get the most out of it since it proceeds at a fast pace.
