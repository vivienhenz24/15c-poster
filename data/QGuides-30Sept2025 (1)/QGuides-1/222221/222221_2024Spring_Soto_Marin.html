Take it! The material is really interesting and Prof. Soto Marin is super knowledgeable and a very good lecturer. The readings are also good but very, very long. The assignments are pretty time consuming and not that worthwhile IMO, but there are only 4 of them so it's fine.
Professor Soto Marin is awesome, and I learned a lot in the class! Be prepared to do a lot of readings though.
This was an interesting course and I enjoyed taking it. The topic was interesting and the readings were well selected. If I had to critique the class, I would say that the slideshows could have been more organized. I would recommend this course. Professor is very nice, thoughtful, and clearly is engaged with the material.
You'll get something out of the class if you do the readings. I learned basically everything from the readings and nothing from lecture.
This class is an interesting and light workload introduction to classical economies. Professor Soto is incredibly kind and passionate about the material. However, it's a pretty birds eye view discussion and it feels a bit cursory at times.
Read, enjoy, don't overthink, ask questions.
Do not take this class if you can avoid it. The course readings are dense at best and wearisome at worst and lectures are incredibly dull. Professor Soto Marin's conduct in the class also fell short of expectation for a course that is marketed as open to all departments when she distinctly chose favorites in the class while also berating multiple students in class as if they were children. Her ego made her inaccessible to many students. An ego that, for her teaching ability, is frankly without reason.
Know that there isn't a lot we know about ancient economies due to lack of data, so you wouldn't really be learning about economic history in the same way compared to a more modern economic history class. You look a lot at research related to crop yields, or analyzing papyri fragments, so just think about whether that is something interesting to you before taking this class. I personally thought we would learn more about broad economic history and didn't realize the field of ancient economic history is like that. Nothing wrong with the course, but just keep this in mind if you're expecting a classic economic history course going in. That being said, this course is quite a gem. I haven't taken the final yet, but the other 60% of your grade is four essays, all of which are graded fairly leniently and only two pages each. Super chill class.
If this topic is of any interest to you, take this class! Prof. Soto Marin is amazing and this class will give you unique insights into a topic not many people talk about. As an econ concentrator, I had no classics background and I loved this class.
This class is great. Professor Soto Marin is a great lecturer and makes the topics engaging. There is minimal homework (4 essays) and feedback was always helpful. There are quite a few readings though.
Great class! Workload isn't bad at all and it is also a very interesting course.
