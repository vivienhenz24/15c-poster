This is a great Gen Ed. The course staff has been amazing, and both professors truly care about the students. The class felt like a strong community throughout the semester. The projects and smaller assignments are engaging, and the section component is also a valuable part of the course. Overall, this would be an excellent science Gen Ed choice.

The course was very disorganized, although the teaching staff is absolutely amazing. If it were to be better structured – take it! Keep in mind, this is not a light workload – 3 exams, 3 projects, and an in–person final. Many seniors said it was the most work in a gened that they had ever done.
This course is a really great GenEd, especially for anyone who is interested in scientific inquiry, asking philosophical questions when it comes to science, and just having a general desire to better evaluate scientific claims. No background is necessary for this course. My advice to do well:
– Go to all lectures and sections, it may seem as if attendance is not mandatory, but it really is and will affect your final grade
– Put effort into the midterms and the assignments. They give you plenty of time to go through the steps of each assignment (roughly 3 weeks per assignment and 30 hours for each of the two take–home midterms). You will need that time, so please use it. Go to office hours and present your work, the sections leaders will tell you exactly what you can do to change/improve
– Do the readings / discussion comments. It's literally one comment and will get you points for your final grades
– It's really easy to do well in this course, as long as you put in the work for it! Be sure to stay engaged in lecture and group discussions
– The final project is a really fun one, you get to be really creative with it!

The teaching team is so supportive and they embrace your mistakes and confusions. The only thing that I will flag is that this gened has a more busy workload than most. As long as you keep up, youll be fine!

I think that the content of this course is truly wonderful – I have become a much more critical thinker thanks to this class, and have genuinely learned so much. Ned and Doug are also great professors, and clearly very passionate about the subject matter.

That said, I think that the course, as it was run this semester, was not as organized as it could have been. The workload was also very intense – we would have more or less weekly reading assignments, and quizzes at the beginning of the semester (and these quizzes, at least in my opinion, were HARD – I would spend hours on them). We then had two midterms, three projects, and an in–person final exam – this semester, the deadlines for the second and third projects were within two days of each other (and these projects were substantial), we had the second midterm as these projects were being run, and we had the final immediately after. For the final itself, we were not told what what was going to be on the exam until four days before we had to take it; and feedback for past assignments was not released until very late.

All in all, I think that the content of this course is really great, and that it would be a wonderful GenEd if the organization of it were to be better tailored.
The professors and TFs are great and will be a great source of support when you need it in the course. I highly recommend going to office hours and not waiting until things start to pile up or you really get confused about a concept before scheduling office hours. Unless you feel very confident about statistics and probability, expect to do more work than you might expect from a GENED, or even a little bit more than what it says in the syllabus. The content is fun and the assignments typically are not that long, but again, if you don't understand, that can make them take a lot longer. Additionally, even though the grading is laid out in the syllabus, there were changes made throughout the semester that kind of complicated for me determining what my grade would be at the end of the semester. Also note that the cutoff for an A is a 95 (meaning you have to have 95 points, meaning losing only 5 points from all the assignments combined). The professors did let us know this could be lowered, but throughout the semester, there was not much discussion of this. If you are deciding to take this class, I would weigh your interest in the content with mainly your ability in statistics, and how much work you will realistically put into this class.
If you've taken any prior statistics courses at Harvard, this class will feel like a breeze. Both professors do a good job of lecturing and engaging the class. Fun, light–hearted, and a great class to take.

This class was a journey! I will definitely say, first and foremost, that this class is not easy. That being said, you will learn so much and grow so much as a student by taking this course (which is what its all about). If you are looking for a gem, look elsewhere! I learned so much from this class, and in my opinion, the first unit was definitely the most difficult. The first midterm had a lot of probability on it, and it got into some really complex stuffsame with some of the take–home quizzes that we had. However, after that, it was pretty much smooth sailing.
