What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If you are interested in political theory/philosophy and want to improve your skills at independent work, take this class! You will be surrounded by a small number of other similarly–inclined students and be exposed to a core set of topics in contemporary political thought. Prof. Rosen is a truly wonderful teacher who cares about his students' success. With his long career in the field, he brings anecdotes and insights that bring the course materials and thinkers to life and provides insights you would never get from the texts alone.
This course is not an entry–level political theory course. I recommend taking at your junior year when you have perhaps dabbled in philosophy and political theory already. A great prerequisite is Eric Nelsons political theory course, 1060. Another great prerequisite is the philosophy of law, offered in the Phil department. Overall, the course is not difficult necessarily in the assignments or the strictness of grading, but the caliber of discussion and debate that is encouraged by the high–level of reading in the course. If you are going to write a political theory thesis or even considering it, absolutely take this course.
If you're at all interested in political theory, you MUST take this class! Professor Rosen is such a wonderful, supportive, and thoughtful instructor and the course readings are super engaging. One of my favorite aspects of this course is that Professor Rosen really gives the students control of the subjects we study in the latter half of the semester. In terms of workload, you will have a healthy amount of readings to complete each week, two presentations on those readings, and one (long) final paper –– it's very manageable (and enjoyable too). The class environment was also super welcoming –– it was not a high–pressure or overly intense environment at all. Overall, I came out of this class with such an enriched understanding of contemporary political theory and with the proper tools to better approach writing political theory. I cannot stress this enough –– take this class!!
This is a great class. Its main pro is that if you're interested in political theory, you gain exposure to a ton of readings on a variety of issues. (But the reading load is still manageable.)
