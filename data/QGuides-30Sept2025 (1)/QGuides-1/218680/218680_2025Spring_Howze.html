What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If you are debating on whether to take this class or not, give it a shot! It's a really fun class that will probably change your perspective on art. You will get to do a lot of very unusual assignments which are actually really fun. I suggest you read the syllabus thoroughly to get an idea. Professor Howze is amazing (although he will probably be a couple minutes late to class more often than you'd expect) and he will teach you how to think like an artist and how to appreciate art.
I'd recommend this class with reservations. If you think this is going to be a writing class or that you'll learn a lot about writing, don't take it.
It's a very silly unserious class that takes itself very seriously. During class exercises we at one point ended up drawing a family tree for an assignment, we ran around and played tag, we made our own games and we drew comics. Honestly not super related to writing, more creating in general. This is more of a theatre class, so if you're into that, I WOULD recommend it!
The class ended up being 6 students, and I now feel kinda trauma–bonded to all of them. I say this lightly because the class WAS fun, but it was wacky at times.
One warning that I hope is changed in the future is that the homework is a little intense. Well – maybe not really, but it feels like it compared to the class. During class we'd sit and talk and draw and laugh and maybe run around and share snacks, but then get assigned 200–pages of reading with a 1–hour podcast to listen to that might not even get brought up the next week. There are weekly writing assignments that do not get brought up during class, and I don't think I ever really received feedback for them. I DID receive awesome feedback for stuff I did DURING class, or assignments that will be specifically relevant to class, but it felt like I was writing into a void sometimes.
So a lot to consider – this class was definitely a lot. But overall, as much as I may have complained, I am actually glad I took it. It was VERY fun. Even the many many readings were super interesting, and we got to meet one of the authors we were assigned to read, too! We had little field trips throughout, which were awesome (going to museums, exhibitions, etc). The class environment was really fun and a few times I found myself red in the face laughing incredulous. Just great in general.
So yeah, keep all this in mind and enjoy the class if you decide to take it! It will definitely create core memories
