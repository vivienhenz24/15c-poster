Comments
If you are newer to environmental spaces, or wanting to explore different fields and careers in more social–facing environmental roles, this is a great course. The guest speakers were great.
This class is really fascinating for anyone interested in climate change issues, especially from a social sciences perspective! The readings are fascinating and Professor Frumhoff is a true expert in the arena of climate action. Section discussions are really interesting too.
This is a great course if you want to learn about what climate responsibilities of different people are. Professor Frumhoff is genuinely amazing, and (at least while I was taking this course) the students tend to be engaged and excited as well, which makes it a great environment. The only issue is that, if you are new to this topic, and if you think you might have a hard time immediately contributing to the conversation, I must say that the participation grade is slightly harsh and not super understanding. Regardless, know they will be realistic and literal about how much you participate or how many times you speak. Other than acknowledging this, I truly recommend the course! It was life–changing for me and made me realize I might want to consider a career path relating to environmental policy. Great class!
This class is an amazing opportunity to learn from experts about climate responsibility in an engaging, discussion–based format. You'll also learn so much from the other people in the seminar, who have a wide range of academic and personal experiences relating to climate change. The reading is manageable and interesting (this is coming from a STEM person who is usually overwhelmed by how much reading there is). The final paper is very open–ended and lots of guidance and support is available. All–around 10/10 class
This class was so special to me: the sheer diversity in the class and the important discussions you get to talk about are so interesting. The readings each week prepare you very well for discussions and professor Frumhoff is so knowledgable, and personable, and you can tell he genuinely cares for the class. This is a must–take class!
I highly recommend taking this class if you are interested in climate justice and how we are going to implement all the great things we have come up with to solve climate change. This class focuses a lot on who is responsible for climate change (nations, governments, fossil fuel companies, individuals) and if they are responsible, how/how much should they pay for their role in climate change. The professors are fantastic and encourage group discussion and multiple perspectives.
Take the class! Professor Frumhoff is so supportive and encouraging. I learned so much in this class.
If you get the chance, take this class!!!! Especially if you intend to work in a climate–related field. I know I'll still be thinking about this experience long after I've graduated.
This course was the best course I have taken so far at Harvard. The weekly sessions are so engaging and the time flies by so quickly because the discussions are so interesting. You receive great feedback and you can tell that Prof. Frumhoff and Dr. Morabito give so much thought to making this class as insightful as possible. 10/10 would recommend
The content of this course is very interesting and Professor Frumhoff structures and teaches it well. The grading is not the easiest though as the grades can be misleading at the beginning since you only have your weekly reflection grades until the last month of the course.
