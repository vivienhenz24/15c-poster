Prof. Rogoff is an engaging lecturer and very down–to–earth. The project gives you an opportunity to talk about macro in a group of 3–4 with him in his office. Him and his wife also host the class at their house at the end of term, which was one of the many highlights! Really recommend this course for anyone interested in macro policy and cool macro models, and the course is very manageable if you keep up.
The material you learn in this class is very well taught and beyond what you'll find in a standard undergraduate course. Professor Rogoff is exceptional. It is a must take if youre interested in macroeconomics.
Take this class if you have any interest in global macro policy issues. Professor Rogoff is incredibly generous with his wisdom, and is very accessible to his students. Almost every lecture is a case study, and you will get great exposure to a variety of models.
Superbe class, you will learn extremely, but it is also very hard
This was the best lecture–based econ elective I took. Prof. Rogoff is a well of insight and knowledge on international financial and macro policy. The class will help you learn how to think through these global problems using theoretical and empirical approaches, and will give you lots of opportunity to ask questions and deepen your understanding.
There is a fair bit of math knowledge required for the PSets (1b level is enough, 21a would be better). Other than that, the class is not overly difficult and is set up for you to do well.
We spent majority of the time especially outside of class in exams and psets on math and models. I wish we focused more on empirical evidence and best practices.
This class was a highlight of my semester. Professor Rogoff is amazing, and you should definitely take this course.
