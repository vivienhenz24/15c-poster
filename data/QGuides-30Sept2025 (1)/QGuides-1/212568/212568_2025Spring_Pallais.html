This was a wonderful course! The workload was very manageable, the lectures were engaging and enjoyable, and the teaching staff was incredibly kind and supportive. Designing a field experimentwhether for my own paper or during class discussionsturned out to be more interesting and challenging than I expected. The course was mostly lecture–based, though there were some in–class group activities. I would highly recommend this course!
TAKE THIS CLASS. Even if you think you know the basics of how to set up an experiment, you will learn new things. Professor Pallais and Wilbur are amazing and incredibly available to answer questions. Though the lectures can be a bit dry at times, the course discusses many creative methods of setting up experiments given the practical constraints of studying human behavior. This is all while having a very manageable workload

Even as someone who is not entering academia, I've gained skills that I can use in my future career –– the ability to effectively evaluate products or programs is critical in many fields.
This was one of the most fun and interesting courses I have taken at Harvard and highly highly recommend to any ec concentrators or anyone interested in research! In core economics classes, they always throw around the term "RCT" but rarely ever look at experiments in practice. This class is super interactive because you actually get to design those experiments and think through all the logistics as if you were going to conduct them. If you love problem–solving, this course is definitely a great fit for you. You also get to know your peers as a lot of work in class is in groups, and I have made friends through that. Finally, a significant portion of this class is presentations. People get really creative with them, and they are a ton of fun to listen to! Professor Pallais and Wilbur were amazing and always made themselves available through email and office hours, so you have a ton of support too.
