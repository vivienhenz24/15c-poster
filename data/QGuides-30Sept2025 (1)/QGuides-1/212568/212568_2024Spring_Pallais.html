Such a great seminar, Mandy is amazing and Wilbur is such a great TF. Felt like a small class in high school (in the best way) but with really interesting discussions and great support.
One of my favorite classes that I've taken at Harvard –– Professor Pallais and Wilbur are wonderful
If you're looking for a way to fulfill your Econ writing requirement with an interesting but not back–breaking course, take this one! Prof. Pallais is a wonderful lecturer, class sessions are enjoyable and informative, and assignments are straight–forward and reasonable. You learn how to think about experiments in new ways and be better able to critically evaluate claims people make about experimental designs. You might even discover a topic you're interested in pursuing for a senior thesis or just otherwise (on the flip side, the midterm and final papers are so open that you can continue to explore existing interests/organizations you're already part of). Make sure to go to the TF and Prof. Pallais for feedback on your midterm and final paper ideas so they can make them even stronger. Also, don't be fooled by the first topic listed on the syllabus: we skate by regression discontinuity and diff–in–diff designs –– the course talks about them just to say what we're ~not~ focusing on. So don't take this class thinking you're going to investigate them. It's really just about experimental designs, but those are very interesting in their own right.
Prof. Pallais is great!
loved this course!! super fun and refreshing compared to most econ electives and courses. designing experiments is super interesting, and Professor Pallais really cares about her students. I enjoyed the topics/studies that she went over. The class environment is super chill and engaged. There's not a lot of instructions/structure to assignments.
<ECON ELECTIVE GEM> Class meets once a week (though two hours) and weekly written assignments (2 pages double spaced) are graded on a check plus/check basis. There is a 25–page paper due at the end of the semester, but no midterms/quizzes throughout. Would definitely recommend to anyone who prefers writing one cumulative paper over taking multiple exams. All academics aside, Mandy is a lovely instructor with very fascinating ideas when it comes to designing experiments – work in this course could help for senior thesis research.
This was a fantastic course – I would highly recommend to all Econ concentrators. Professor Pallais is a great lecturer, and the topics she covers are very interesting.
Highly recommend this course. Experiment design is not something discussed in a lot of economics courses so this can be something very useful to learn. For a senior thesis if you're thinking of running an experiment potentially, this is a good introduction. Professor Pallais is very kind and very helpful in meetings about your paper.
I highly recommend taking this course. Not only does it prepare you for writing a prospective thesis but listening to every case study provides very valuable insights on many different disciplines of economics.
This class is probably the most chill ec junior seminar out there, and you get to explore a very interesting topic (field experiments) while getting to know a really nice professor in a smaller setting. Know that the papers for this class are not traditional economics research papers, as in other junior seminars, since you're just designing experiments in them and not actually analyzing any data. Don't hesitate to reach out to Professor Pallais or Wilbur for help at any point because they're both great resources.
