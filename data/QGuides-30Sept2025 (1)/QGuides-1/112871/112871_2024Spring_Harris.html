Joe is a wonderful lecturer, and his passion for algebra is evident in how he teaches the course. Content wise, point–set topology is generally straight–forward; algebraic topology is often baffling if not incomprehensible (in the end I have gained an appreciation to the lifting lemmas, but I suspect that is just Stockholm syndrome); and complex analysis is weird but elegant. Joe likes to teach complex analysis with a more algebraic flavor, so he tends to hand–wave the more analytical parts and focus on understanding the high–level concepts from an algebraic topological standpoint. There is elegance in viewing complex analysis through algebraic topology, though I would've preferred if we had a stronger emphasis on the "analysis" part of complex analysis.
