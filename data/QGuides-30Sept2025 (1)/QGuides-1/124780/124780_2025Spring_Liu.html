What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Naijia is a very kind and accommodating instructor; you can tell that she really cares about the course and her students! It was a joy to learn from her. The material was difficult especially towards the second half of the class because it became very technical. But I think it was nice that the exams were take home, and it tested the concepts fairly, so I didn't feel too much stress. There were also only 4 psets staggered biweekly which ended up being very well balanced for me, as I was taking 2 other challenging pset classes simultaneously.
I took this class to fulfill the grad–level stat requirement for AM/Gov undergrads. If that's you, this is a good choice!
Absolutely take it if you are looking for a stats 110 substitute that will lean more on the side of practical applicability and linear models.
This is a good option for AM concentrators on the government track trying to fulfill their application requirements. The psets are fair, and so are the exams. Some experience with probability would be nice, especially in the first half, but not necessary.
