If you're even remotely interested in southern history, contemporary southern discussion, or the South in general this course is very interesting and covers a wide range of topics related to the South. The readings can be hefty and disturbing sometimes due to the nature of southern history but everything is approached in a sensitive manner. There aren't many assignments and Dr. Kirby is a great resource at all times.
This is quite literally the only class about The South at Harvard. Dr. Kriby is awesome and I'm so grateful she created this class, at a school that doesn't really have anything like it. Lowkey this made me wish I would have considered Hist–Lit as a concentration lol. Great class! Highly recommend any class with Dr.Kirby she is brilliant!
This is a great class, particularly if you have a connection to the South. Dr. Kirby is incredibly passionate, very engaged with the class, and always willing to be flexible if a student needs that. It is probably not a course for STEM concentrators or those without prior experience with historical/cultural analysis, but for social sciences/humanities concentrators, this is a fantastic class that is really helpful in thinking about the South and the US more broadly.
Dr. Kirby is a great professor, and I would highly recommend this class. She is respectful of students' time and busy lives outside of class, while making the most of the time in class. The assignments are thought–provoking but not incredibly difficult nor terribly time consuming.
Please, please, please take this class! This should be a requirement for all interested in American Studies or regional studies. There is so much abundance in this course, and Rachel is truly the best educator you will find at this institution. I cannot recommend it enough! The community and conversation was wonderful! And truly, without the dedication and passion of Rachel, the experience would not have been as special as it was.
