What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Take the class. I study STEM and decided to take this class as an elective my senior spring, and I must say it might be the most interesting class I've taken at Harvard –– and one of my favorites. I learned so much about poverty and wealth and how that impacted different social strata. There are a lot of readings but they are very interesting. The entire 1h15m is discussing the readings, so coming prepared to each class is key. There aren't that many assignments (a few 1 page papers, and a 5–7 midterm paper) which makes this class very manageable, even as someone who does not study humanities. Prof. FitzGerald is also an amazing, kind teacher, and looks out for his students. I will really miss these class discussions.
TAKE THIS COURSE!! COULD NOT RECOMMEND MORE!!

1) Prof. FitzGerald is so kind, and his knowledge on the subject is so incredibly deep. Each class we would pepper him with random questions on the middle ages, and he always had an informed answer
2) the material is super interesting, I KNOW IT SEEMS NICHE, but I feel like I have a totally new understanding of not only the Catholic church but religious history in general and how it interacted with the economy. You could almost call this course "The Economy and Religion in the Middle Ages" because you learn so much about how money and religion were intertwined.
3) It expanded by world view in a huge way...I am not religious at all, but many of my classmates were, and I think I grew a lot as a person and students listening and engaging with students who have very different approaches to the world than I do
4) You should expect to do 85%+ of the reading, but if you're someone who enjoys seminar courses, this is a great class for you. The entire time is spent debating topics related to the readings, and I think Prof. FitzGerald does an amazing job facilitating interesting discussion
5) Prof. FitzGerald is SUPER supportive and engaging and interested, and I feel very lucky to have gotten to take a course with him


TLDR: take this course, i know it seems random, but it is so interesting and I promise you will not regret it
