What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Do the readings they're essential for in–class discussion and honestly just extremely interesting. Primary sources are very well chosen and will get you thinking even after you leave the classroom. The topic is very, very heavy, and discussions can get sensitive and emotional but it was all part of trying to understand the history of what we were studying. It's a great course to take if you're interested in Latin America and especially if you're eager to put in the work. Very, very rewarding. Prof. Weld is awesome too.
If you have any interest in Latin America it is definitely worth taking this class! Prof. Weld is one of the coolest and best instructors I've had at Harvard. The course is by no means easy, but you really get so much out of it.
I am a sucker for survey courses, so I may just not have loved how specific this course felt at times. I guess my academic interests are much more about the Southern Cone (Argentina and Chile part of the course) than Central America (Guatemala and El Salvador part of the course), so I found the parts about the latter less engaging. However, if you know that this is a topic that interests you, you will love this class. Weld knows how to run a classroom well. She structures discussion without being overbearing. She makes you think and ask interesting questions.
Prof Weld is so incredibly intelligent and extremely open to different views. It's refreshing to have that at Harvard. This had to have been one of my favorite courses I have taken over the years. I learned so much and it was all incredibly thought provoking.
The subject material of this course is incredibly difficult and can have a cumulative effect on your experience in the course.
Take this course if you are very, very invested in the course content. Be prepared for a lot of reading.
It is a great opportunity to critically approach the dynamics of the American continent, both Latin America and United States and its foreign politics. It requires a good amount of reading and analysis.
