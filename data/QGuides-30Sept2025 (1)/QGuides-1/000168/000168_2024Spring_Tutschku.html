What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If accepted, treat the opportunity seriously –– it's a once in a lifetime chance to work with this equipment, these facilities, and faculty.
If you are interested in music production and making music personally, then take this class. !WARNING! It is not what you would expect. This is a course that is interested in making non beat based music (very ambient and evolving synthesized sounds). However, it will break you from a typical mold that much of western theory and even modern production will automatically try to put you in. This is genuinely such a unique opportunity if you are willing to explore this field.
One of the best classes I've ever taken. You learn so much not only from Hans but also your classmates.
This course is a great way to change your relationship with sound and composition via an instrument you may not be familiar with. The lectures are a bit dry and dense (and, in all honesty, somewhat ineffective as I have not retained 90% of the information from them), but most learning comes from composing and experimenting with the synthesizers themselves.
If you can, you should absolutely take this class. It changed the way I hear music, and has made me a much more informed composer. To a lot of people, the type of music that you will hear and create in class sounds like a lot of somewhat–unpleasant beeping, but these is a great deal to learn from patching synthesizers as a composer or songwriter of any style. This class breaks music and sound down to its most basic components and teaches you to work with them, and I am very grateful that I got this experience. Also, the final concert is incredibly well organized by the department and the staff, which is a very cool experience as an undergrad. Take this class with an open mind and I think you will learn a great deal!
