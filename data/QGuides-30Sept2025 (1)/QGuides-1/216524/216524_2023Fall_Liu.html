Overall, a great class. David is SERIOUSLY talented as a scientist, mentor, and lecturer –– I have no idea how he hasn't won a Nobel prize yet. He's extremely knowledgable about all things gene editing, but also an entertaining lecturer. The class dives into a *lot* of different aspects of gene editing –– mechanisms, pharmaceutical development, policy, ethics, etc. I honestly didn't think it would be able to cover everything, but we went in depth on basically everything they mentioned in the syllabus! It's totally focused on the science elements though, so I'd recommend a different class (SCRB 120?) if you're interested in the ethical debates of gene editing.

It's tough to make a 3 hour class on Friday evening entertaining, but David does a pretty good job. If you're not used to long classes, though, the lecture can be really brutal. As engaging as David is as a lecturer, the class isn't very interactive (it's 2 hours of content–based lecture, and 45 minutes of student presentations on individual papers related to that week's topic). The final projects were really fun, and we had a lot of creative liberty. The TFs are also a shining part of this course experience. Nick and Colin are AMAZING. Absolutely phenomenal!!! They can break down the most complex papers into picture books! They are really great teachers, and very knowledgable about their fields.
This class is good if you want to learn about genome editing, how it has been developed since its conception, how it works on the molecular level, and its ethical and societal implications.
TAKE IT!
You get out of this course what you put into it.
This course is a phenomenal overview of different methods of gene editing, taught by one of the academic leaders of the field. It also provides insight into some of the ethical and business considerations of developing therapies from gene editing. The workload is fairly light, with one paper presentation and a final project that gives you an opportunity to dive deeper into an area of interest in any format that you would like to use. Having basic technical background is useful to understanding course concepts, but no prior experience with gene editing or therapeutic development is needed.
This course is a relatively low–effort and high–reward class, which is rare especially in the sciences. Each of the lectures are well–curated and you learn a lot from Dr. Liu over the course of the semester. The weeks where you present require a bit of preparation.
This course is a very interesting and thorough course on topics related to gene editing. Dr. Liu is a very enthusiastic lecturer with a sense of humour. Both TFs are extremely helpful to guide students through their assignments and presentations. I don't think anyone will regret taking this course.
Definitely take this class. It might be confusing if your biochemistry background is not that high, but the teaching staff wants to help you learn and you get what you put into the class.
Very interesting course that is a great way to learn about genome editing from the leaders in the field without being stressed out by problem sets and exams!
