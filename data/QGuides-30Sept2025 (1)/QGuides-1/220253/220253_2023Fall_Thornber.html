What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
*** TAKE THIS CLASS *** This was such an amazing class and I don't think I'll ever have a similar experience at Harvard – the small classroom setting, the supportiveness and immense knowledge of the Professor, the kindness and humor of everyone in the class, the topics we discussed, it was all incredible. I would consider myself relatively educated on feminism, but the readings we had in this class opened my eyes so much that I essentially realized my understanding of feminism had been reductionist. This class is honestly an amazing way to learn more about the feminist movement, learn about incredible women whose memoirs you'll read, get really close with a bunch of other people who have similar passions as you, learn from an incredible Professor who's not only extremely knowledgeable but also funny and extremely interesting with tons of stories to share!

I can't stress this enough, though it doesn't fulfill any requirements, freshman seminars in general are a great way to get close to a bunch of other people and to professors, and I can't think of a way to better fulfill it than this!
If you are considering taking a first–year seminar, I 100% recommend this class. Also, I strongly encourage any man who may be considering taking the course to PLEASE give it a chance and join! The material we read is so unique and redefined my meaning of gender justice entirely. The professor is so understanding and she is so incredibly passionate about the course. She is always looking for ways to make the course better and really values her students' opinions. You will not regret taking this course one bit!! Also, our class only had 10 students and we grew very close throughout the semester. Discussions felt more like fun conversation rather than a chore/assignment!!
I would highly recommend this course to anyone. The professor is amazing and incredibly knowledgeable on the course content, and the readings and corresponding class discussions were powerful, engaging, and invigorating.
I will tell future students that this is a great opportunity for them to learn about gender and justice further and see if they are potentially interested in continuing this study at Harvard in the future. It is a. great introductory course and the material is not difficult, but sometimes the classes can be very long and slightly boring.
Even if you think you can get by without doing the readings, do them. They will shed so much light on the themes at hand, and even for me and my already well equipped background in feminism, change perspective.
This class was one of the best classes I've ever taken. The class readings are very enlightening and make you see the world in a different way, and the class discussions are always one of the highlights of my week. If you want to learn/talk about gender injustice, stereotypes, or cultural differences in the world, this is the course for you. In case you are hesitant because of the course description or readings, this course is what you and your classmates make of it, so don't be intimidated.
TAKE. THIS. CLASS.
