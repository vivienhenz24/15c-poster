What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Do NOT enroll in this course or any course that Professor Croft teaches. If you are enrolled in her course currently, I strongly recommend that you choose an alternate class! This has undoubtedly been **WORST** experience of my undergrad experience at Harvard.

The onboarding experience for visiting Professors needs to be improved as it seemed most of the class gave her grace for being newer, coming to class unprepared discussing personal challenges to adapting to Cambridge. While obviously this is understandable, Professor Croft offered no grace at all to students! Her disorganization was so ironic considering the harshness of her grading to students' work.

Now reflecting on this course it was extremely poor. For example:

– Readings would go up like 3–4 hours before class with the expectation that students should be well versed in the material. Canvas pages and modules were constantly locked and she would insist that they were not. She would cite that resources were on canvas when they were not. For example, she constantly referred students to a glossary that did not exist on the canvas page until just a few days before final papers were due (this was after she was reminded by a student).
– Navigating the canvas page was a hellish maze until a student had suggested Professor Croft organize a bit
– Harsh and picky grading, extremely defensive to student feedback
– Professor Croft refused to explain what she wanted to see in assignments. She just graded arbitrarily without clearly explaining why she choose to grade the way she did and what exactly her expectations were.
– Even though this was not an English course she would take off significantly for minor grammar errors.
– She would sent SO many emails that I became overwhelmed (it was more emails that all my other courses for the semester combined) and yet none of these emails actually communicated important aspects of the class material or grading expectations
– She would make up her citation system (and extension of Chicago) and asked students to submit an annotated bibliography but said it was not annotated (confusing, right!)
– She was not open to office hours and only had a few times to meet. The week of the final paper she didn't hold office hours for the five days leading to the submission deadline.
– She pushed off all research questions to the Fine Arts Librarians, who were ALSO confused by her unclear rubric and quantity of emails.
– Lectures were wayyy to long. For example, on the last day of class she lectured for an hour and 45 minutes straight without leaving room for students to ask questions. She often also interrupted students, especially during in class presentations.
Meet with Dr. Croft to better prepare for assignments and to better understand what is expected.
Just dont. The workload is enormous, and changes constantly. Never depend on the syllabus for either the readings (more get added up to 2 hours before class, and what once said pick one changes to read all without warning) or due dates (things get moved up to a week earlier than planned without warning.) the grading is really hard to figure out, since the professor gives no rubrics and changes the directions constantly (like how the last paper was supposed to be an expansion of our first, and then got changed to a whole new paper on the same subject last minute). The professor is reasonable about some emergencies, but not great about accommodations even through the DAO and is not very responsive to feedback. The content is interesting and I recommend you do learn about Aboriginal Australian art and politics (which was a much bigger part of the course than originally assumed. There were reasons for this but it wasnt what I expected), but not through this class or with this professor.
