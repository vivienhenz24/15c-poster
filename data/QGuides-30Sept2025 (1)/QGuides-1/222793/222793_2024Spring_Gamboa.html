This course presents a series of challenges that may not always feel rewarding. Class is conducted in an intense environment, which might be daunting for those who are less confident in their drawing skills. There is a noticeable atmosphere of comparison among students which added to the pressure. A lot of times the feedback is harsh and sometimes it's even "redo it." By the end of the course, many of us felt burnt out and the final project's intensity + quantity of homework did not help that.

I think the professor is a really wonderful person and incredible teacher. I can't deny that this class pushed me out of my comfort zone and improved my overall drawing skills. I also developed friendships in this class and I think it's a great way to bond with others if you're hoping to seek that in a class.

The class should be an intermediate–level course. This class should also transition from 1–homework a week to 1 homework every 2 weeks halfway through the semester. But I do recommend it to those who wish to really push themselves and recommend it for the professor as well.
Take this class!! Maria is incredible, so patient and willing to guide you but also push you to do your best work. The class was challenging because she expects a lot from you, but worth it because you get so much out of it. I learned so much from this class and my art great a lot. Being able to work with live models was also an amazing opportunity. I would definitely recommend taking this class.
The class is fun, having live models is a very unique experience that is worth the work of taking a 4 hour studio course. It can be tiring, but you will get used to it. The work quality improves over time, so just stick it out even if you dont like your work at the start. Maria is very kind and supportive with giving tips on work and making you feel secure in your art work.

a good class overall; however, class is extremely long (and on Friday afternoon) so you may get burnt out drawing for so long; it's also not organized very well and a good amount of time is wasted; you learn a decent amount about anatomy of the skeleton at the beginning from the first homework assignment but then you're expected to know it all for later and may forget some of it which may make your drawings not as realistic, but you're never tested on it which is fine; people slacked a lot on homework because there were not repercussions for submitting stuff late, but the instructors do get kind of mad so do your best; i think i definitely improved at drawing the human body but this is not an exhaustive anatomy class so don't expect to become a master; a lot of the people in the class are already great artists but don't be put off if your skills are on the worse side, that's why you're there, to learn; the instructors are sometimes extremely helpful and other times hard to understand and not great at explaining things, but more good than bad i think
