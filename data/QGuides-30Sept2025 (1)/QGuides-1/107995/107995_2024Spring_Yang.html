This is hands–down one of the best courses I've ever taken at Harvard. Professor Yang and Dr. Park are an incredible duo, and the amount of depth we covered in such a short time regarding the incredibly complex semiconductor industry was incredible. I always felt like walking into every class I was leaving with more than entire semester's worth of technical knowledge and political/economic perspective on the different issues in the semiconductor industry. They also genuinely care about students' learning and put a lot of effort into making sure that the course is actively molded by the interests of the students. They are also always willing to meet outside of class, and their commitment and dedication to the course really showed with how good it was. I wish more Harvard classes were like this.
This class is pretty useful to learn about the semiconductor supply chain. It doesn't go a lot into the technical detail, and instead focuses on politics, economics, and spends a lot of time on analysis frameworks like AGTO, TOWS, etc. Most of the homeworks only take a few hours but one of them took like 15 hours out of nowhere.
Prof. Yang and Prof. Park were excellent. They brought a lot of useful knowledge and insight into the class. The class composition itself was very compelling. I was an undergrad, and I feel like I learned a lot from the grad students that were taking the class as well. If you are a stem concentrator, I would especially recommend this class as I think you'll learn something that is hard to convey in normal stem classes. I feel like I had to do a lot of difficult conceptual thinking in this class hunting for original takes on topics that I could defend well. This is a non–trivial task!
This is a broad class that covers a lot of ground over the expertise involved in both the production and regulation of semiconductors.
Good class.
This is a great class to take if interested in becoming more fluent in semiconductor technology and geopolitical tensions between US and China. However, this is not an easy class as the professors expect dedicated effort on assignments from the start and active participation in class. If you have experience with the related topics, that is a big bonus as you will get more out of this class than otherwise.
This is honestly a great class––I like the professors a lot. Couple kinks being worked out and some of the analysis stuff can get a bit annoying (applying random frameworks) but semiconductors are super cool and the industry is fascinating and Yang/Park definitely inspire interest in the content. One of the more impactful classes I've taken here.
