Comments
The logistics of the class weren't the best, but it was a great course at the end of the day. Hopefully the course has a set TF and section schedule in a timely fashion the next time it's offered, and that the information about big assignments is more transparent. We didn't even know about the final project until the deliverables were due, which we had no idea about. Thus we had 2 days to think about and draft a proposal. Also, the information about the final exam didn't come until a few weeks before, rather than when everyone gets their exam assignments (location was not known until pretty late, so don't get your tickets to go home until they confirm if the exam is on Zoom like the entirety of the class, or in person).
This is a great course for those who need to fulfill their divisional distribution requirement, and for those who are interested in science, as a whole! Irwin is a real treasure, in terms of the enthusiasm and experience that he brings to this course. It's very rare that you have an opportunity to meet and learn from someone who helped prove General Relativity and plate tectonics!
Irwin is so great and so kind! The course material is interesting but it is tough having both a final project and final exam. However, Irwin is super understanding and accommodating!
Irwin is a highly accomplished scientistgo look at his Wikipedia page. It has been the greatest honor of my time at Harvard to be taught by Professor Irwin Shapiro in what very well may be his final year teaching. Thank you, Irwin, for your passion for science and kindness towards students.
Irwin is the cutest professor on the planet, no cap. This course is probably one of the most accessible ways to complete SEAS or QRD requirements, and this is coming from someone who is completely STEM–illiterate and didn't do a p–set until her very last semester at school. The instructors are very understanding, graceful, and excited to hear from students.
