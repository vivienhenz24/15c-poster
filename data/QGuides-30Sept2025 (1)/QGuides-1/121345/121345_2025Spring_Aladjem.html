What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Great, chill class, with brilliant peers. Unstructured, but still interesting. The readings are all over the place and you have a random second 20 page paper unlike any other SS junior tutorial, but it somehow wasn't terrible for me.
If you're interested in learning more about the law and its operations very broadly, this is a great course to take! Terry is a wonderful instructor who spends a lot of time and effort curating a reading list that covers a broad range of topics and perspectives. The midterm and final papers aren't too bad, having very broad prompts that allow students to pursue whatever topics interest them most. At times, discussion could get a bit challenging because of a lack of structure within course sessions, but most of the time, discussions were great! This is a very good junior tutorial to take, I recommend it strongly.
This is a great course for students hoping to explore law–related interests in a structured setting. The two papers in this course are very open–ended and give you the opportunity to investigate problems of your choice, which is great for junior spring and thesis prep! There's a lot of reading, but I really recommend doing as much of it as possible to weave the many threads of this course together.
