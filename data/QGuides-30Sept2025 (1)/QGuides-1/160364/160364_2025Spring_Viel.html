What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

This class is amazing and fulfills research requirements for MCB, etc. It is structured as once a week whole group meetings, with the rest of the class operating through planning within the research groups. You get to work around your group's own availability and delegate your work (and how much time you need to spend in the lab to complete your experiments) on your own and it's great. Please do not take this course if you are going to not participate in your group, because your grade really is just dependent on showing up, learning, and a final grant proposal paper. You will not get an A if you cannot even do that and show up, plus it drags your team down! Overall, very light load, and very great introduction to research. My TF was lovely and is a very accomplished researcher, and Alain is also a sweetheart and very passionate about his students. the projects were very interesting and I learned so much about lab work, given I had zero lab exposure prior. Must take!!
You should have prior lab experience. Be wary that you will not receive as much guidance as you expect and you really have to advocate for yourself if you need help.
This was a great class! I enjoyed my team and the experiments we did. I would say your experiences vary depending on which experiment you are put on, but I would say overall this class was very interesting.
This class is great! I took it as a first year and I really enjoyed it. However, if you have a busy schedule, do not take this class because it is really hard to find time outside of class to go into lab. This should not be a fifth class because then it becomes nearly impossible to go into lab (i was always running in and out during lunch time and it got really stressful). This class is what you put into it, and the more you dedicate the experience the more you will get out of it.
def recommend this class!!
The strengths of this course is that you get the opportunity to work in the lab and gain some hands–on experience. If you don't know certain lab techniques, you will be instructed on how to do so by your group leader. So, don't be scared if you have never stepped into a lab before. You are asked about which research topics you want to do for the whole semester, and then you get sorted into the teams. I think most people got their first or second choice. I recommend this class with reservations because your experience is highly dependent on the students you are with for your group. In a large group, there can be a handful of people who can get off with doing essentially nothing for the whole semester. If you work more, you will just get more out of the class.
Try to show up to lab whenever you can to work with your project leader and learn as much as you can! This really is a course where you get whatever you put into it. Divide up presentation work with your peers, and it really isn't so bad. Also make sure you start brainstorming for your final paper early and ask for feedback as you write.
Writing the proposal at the end of the course was pretty difficult due to the lack of reading materials and writing assignments throughout the course documenting the kinds of knowledge that can be valuably applied to potential future experiments we need to come up with for the proposal (though the head of your lab may provide you with more guidance). Otherwise, this is a great way to begin research in the life sciences.
This class was great in obtaining research skills in an environment where extra effort is made to teach you in a way that you may not always get in a lab. For instance, my project leader David was very patient in answering the many questions I had on the various experimental procedures as well as in helping me understand the background behind what we were doing. Additionally, while I think being assigned your preferred project (you are assigned to 1 out of 3) would likely benefit your enthusiasm for and experience in the course, I believe that valuable skills can be gained from any of the projects offered by the course.
