Professor Popa is an excellent lecturer. The homeworks were quite long but often illuminating.
Prof. Popa is kind, fair, and works hard to deliver engaging and effective lectures. This course requires a substantial amount of algebra understanding, so be prepared to brush up on ideas from field theory and commutative algebra. The content, by nature, is difficult and abstract. Don't let this stop you – the theory is beautiful and the connections within it deserve to be understood.
The content is super cool if quite difficult. Sometimes it gets so abstract that it feels almost like it lacks rigor which can be a little frustrating.
This class is not easy in general, anything Popa teaches is not easy. But if you power through it, you will learn a lot both from his wonderful lectures and from the problem sets. For 137 specifically, definitely make sure to go through Hartshorne (and sometimes Harris, and sometimes Eisenbud) when doing the psets they will serve you well, both with understanding the material and with making the problem sets more straightforward.
Great with Popa! Good but quite hard.
This is a great math class and I would recommend it. Popa is a clear lecturer and he does a good job motivating the fascinating field of algebraic geometry. Do be warned though that it is a lot of work. There were also not very many office hours times to choose from, but the CA's were very responsive to their emails. Overall one of my favorite math classes in college.
The psets will be hard, work together. Also, try to find textbooks online to read.
When taking this class one must come with the expectation of refining their intuition concerning the topic of algebraic geometry. Try to understand what it means intuitively speaking to have a certain dimension, to map a variety to another, to look at a "general fiber" or a "general hyperplane." It is one of those classes were focusing on the formalisms may make you miss the richest part.
Math 137 was one of my all–time favorite classes! Professor Popa's lectures are very clear and well–motivated, and the material itself is really exciting.
Good lectures, good lecture notes, generally good problem sets. However, keep in mind that you will find problems in the homework which are unreasonable to complete without an external source so if you get stuck, look in Harris or ask for help from a CA sooner rather than later. In this iteration of the course, the 3/4 CAs were remarkably incompetent (while, to his credit, Jarell really knew what he was doing) in that they were unable to help with homework and were not prepared to lead meaningful sections I hope CAs will be held more accountable in future iterations of the course.
Great class, especially with professor Popa! One of the best lecturers at Harvard. Content from math 123 and 131 are important; having taken other geometry classes (ex. 132, 136, 230, ...) helps with taking home geometric intuition of the subject.
This was maybe my favorite course of college so far. The material is absolutely beautiful. The beginning of algebraic geometry is a lovely and fairly cohesive theory, making for a very nice class. Throughout the course, the work feels very motivated and reasonable. If you are looking for a challenging, interesting, abstract course, this is a great option!
This is one of the most difficult courses I have taken in the math department, but it was also a lot of fun, because I felt like I had the appropriate background and was able to dedicate a decent amount of time to learning the material. You should NOT take this course if you have not taken 123 –– I would personally advise not taking them at the same time either. Popa is a great lecturer but moves fast, and expects you to put in the work outside of class to cement your understanding. Please take this course with the mindset of being ready to learn and work a little outside of class, and you will learn a lot!!
