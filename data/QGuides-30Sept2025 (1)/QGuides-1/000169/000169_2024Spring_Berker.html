The professor is great. The book leaves a lot to be desired in my eyes. But it still contains some really interesting/novel stuff.
Such an incredible class–– reading through PE is difficult, but definitely worth it. Professor Berker is so enthusiastic, warm, and supportive. He renders the incomprehensible parts of the book comprehensible –– no small feat!
This is a fantastic course! Nozick's book is fascinating to read and I loved focusing on just one big work. Prof. Berker is excellent at leading the discussion.
You likely aren't familiar with Nozick or his book, but that doesn't matter. This is a unique opportunity to take an undergraduate philosophy class with a seminar format, which I believe is the ideal format for learning philosophy. It also majorly reduces the weekly workload (until the final paper, that is). Furthermore, the highlight of the class is Professor Berker. He's arguably a better philosopher than Nozick, and I learned so much about how to do philosophy by just listening to him speak. So even if you aren't sure about taking a class that only examines one textwhich shouldn't be a concern because of the wide variety of topics it coversyou should absolutely still enroll.
If you get the chance to take a seminar with Prof. Berker, you should: he's one of the best instructors I've encountered at Harvard.
It's a fun and worthwhile class if you really like philosophy.
Especially if you are interested in more analytic philoosphy, this is a great class. Berker is an extremely engaging lecturer. It was really special reading Nozick's book with him, especially because he hits on all of the most pressing philosophical topics, like do we have free will, what is the meaning of life, and how do we exist over time?
This is an excellent phil course! The text itself is very clever and easy to engage with and lectures are great at breaking down each chapter of the book. Prof Berker and Malcolm are great at balancing engagement with explaining the text. I would highly recommend to anyone interested in phil!
This class is AMAZING! One of my favorite at Harvard. It will push you to think critically and creatively. Professor Berker and Malcolm are amazing, the handouts each week turn a very difficult book into an actually understandable philosophical discussion every time. You definitely get out what you put in in terms of weekly meetings, if you do the reading and stay engaged in class you learn a ton and the time flies by. I'm so grateful I got to take this class!
