ES50 is a super fun intro to electrical engineering course! I have a lot to say about ES50, but I'll try to keep it relatively brief:

One PSet a week, but it will make you regret registering for this course. The level of rigor is unmatched and not reflected at all in the lectures. In fact, for the last two months of class, I didn't attend a single lecture because I knew that anything that I was taught wasn't going to help me on the homework or lab.
Marko is a brilliant man and is very knowledgeable in EE, however I've found this to be counterproductive with him teaching an intro–level course. I constantly find him deviating off of a simple topic and get way too deep into a concept that isn't taught until a much higher level.
The two take–home quizzes/midterms are the only somewhat reasonable reflections of what I have learned. They are reasonably difficult and reflect much of what is taught in the course.
The final project is fun, only because you have the creative freedom to do anything you want EE related in a $100 budget. If you have no EE experience, this can be kind of intimidating, but finding a new idea is very doable.
Overall, I would NOT recommend this course unless you a) have to take it, obviously; b) absolutely love EE and have a moderate level of experience; or c) you're up for the challenge and have an extra dozen hours per week in your schedule.
