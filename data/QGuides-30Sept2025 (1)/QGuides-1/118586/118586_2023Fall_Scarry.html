What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Make time to do the readings! There is a lot of content to get through, and in order to participate most fully, it is necessary for students to have done the readings.
If you love to read, you will absolutely love this course.
– This class takes you on a tour of all of Hardy's major works which is an incredible, once–in–a–lifetime experience. If you're not ready for such a journey, then it's perhaps best to be realistic because most weeks you really are trying to finish a 350+ page novel, but if you do have the time, you will find the class more than worth it. Also, the readings are novels, good novels, so it's not like reading through Kant's Pure Reason (as good as that is).

– You would be greatly remiss to pass over a chance to study under Professor Scarry; I can not write enough good things in her praise. Everything she says is so articulate and insightful and intelligent and relevant that I'm often in disbelief that what's she's saying is extemporaneous. She pulls from a wealth of knowledge from history and philosophy and of course literature, having read, it seems, every great book in the english language. Most importantly, she is kind, and very willing to meet outside of class.

I am really sad that this class is over, which is probably the best testament I can give to the seminar's worth.
Try your best!
