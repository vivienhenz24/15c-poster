What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class is my favorite class I've ever taken at Harvard (as a rising senior). The combination of practical assignments with theoretical discussions and readings about several prominent theatre directors was honestly wonderful to me because I feel like I really expanded my worldview of directing while honing my own skills. Shira is also an amazingly thoughtful and supportive professor, and our class community is one of my favorites at Harvard.
As a non–TDM concentrator who wanted to explore this space, Shira's class was possibly one of the best places to do that and I'm very thankful I got to enrol in this course! Granted, at times the discussion felt a bit harder to follow because fellow TDM concentrators had a much more vast arsenal of knowledge regarding shows but the course was crafted in a way that is non–exclusionary, and Shira is very skilled and inclusive in engaging and facilitating sharing. It's an incredible safe space to explore your artistic visions, but definitely be ready to commit some time to figuring out rehearsals and getting your actors + props + venues sorted out earlier. That was usually quite difficult, so seek help from your friends and fellow coursemates, and get creative!
I absolutely LOVED this class!!! Not a TDM concentrator, but the community that was created here was so so so so so important to me, and I really learned from all of these strong, amazing, talented women. 100% recommend!!
