Thinking about structural injustice is not only valuable philosophically, but also in our roles as global citizens. Gina is brilliant and an engaging instructor, and I enjoyed diving critically and constructively into the literature she selected.
Structural injustice is an interesting and intuitively important question in political philosophy, and though it can get quite muddied, this seminar works hard to make things precise and useful. Prof. Schouten is one of the most thoughtful people –– she is constantly trying to improve and tailor pedagogy to best work for students. The discussions were illuminating and students were great. Enjoyed every class.
That this class is a masterclass itself of thoughtful pedagogy. Gina is a great teacher, mentor and feedback giver, and everyone gets to contribute during the class in a way that is convivial and fun. The content is often dense and difficult but the classroom vibes make up for it!
This class was great! Almost all the texts were extremely interesting, Gina is just an incredible facilitator, and the other students were absolutely terrific! If you're interested in political philosophy, I'd really just recommend taking any class with Gina. Truly felt like such a privilege to get to participate in this seminar each week. Many thanks to Gina and to my peers!
TAKE THIS COURSE!! My praise here will undoubtedly be insufficient. Much can be said about the rich/expertly–crafted reading list, but I owe much more of the experience to Gina's excellent teaching and my classmate's excellent contributions. You are in very competent hands. TRULY: Gina is a gem in this department and you would be doing yourself a disservice if you did not take the chance to engage with her brilliance. There were many times when I would haphazardly present a half–baked idea and she would immediately disassemble it to see the most important elements/build it back up with precise language.
