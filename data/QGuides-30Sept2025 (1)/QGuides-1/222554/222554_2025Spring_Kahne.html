TAKE THIS SEMINAR!!! Despite how scary the end–of–semester research chalk talk may seem, it is not. The entire semester works you up to this moment. TAKE THIS SEMINAR! Not only will you become comfortable with the research process and reading research articles, but you'll get a fully funded research position over the summer! Dan and Sien are amazing people and I am so so glad I was able to take this class :)
From all the classes I took as a first–year at Harvard, this has been my favorite. Truly, Dan and Sien are such amazing teachers. Not only do they have so much wisdom and expertise in the sciences, but they are also such a joy to be around. Class was never boring, and at times, I wished we met more than once a week. Although I haven't participated in the summer research program associated with this seminar yet, I'm super excited because I know I'll learn more. Dan and Sien went above and beyond when it came to preparing readings and materials for class and facilitating class discussions, and even bringing great scientists as guest speakers to talk to us in class. The final project of giving a chalk talk sounds daunting at first, but the course is structured so well that you take little steps at a time, and you will be prepared for it before you even realize it. This class was an absolute gem, so please consider taking it if you're interested in research and have not done it before!!!!
Take this seminar! The students are from a wide range of experiences with scientific literature and research, and the discussions are super engaging. Dan and Sean have the funniest dynamic and really keep you engaged. They also bring in multiple esteemed professors across different departments to give lectures, which is a rare opportunity. Upon completion of the course, you also get the opportunity to participate in FUEL, a ten week summer fellowship that will ultimately pair you with a lab!
I definitely recommend taking this course. Some of the classes definitely felt long and a bit boring because some weeks did not incorporate a lot of group discussion and consisted almost entirely to one of the professors or a guest speaker lecturing to us. However, I think the chalk talk final presentation helps you develop useful skills and enhance your ability to read and understand scientific papers, which is important if you want to go into scientific research. If you're trying to decide if pursuing research is something you might want to do, this course will definitely help you gain more exposure to it and start figuring out if it's right for you. Dan and Sien are also both very kind and enthusiastic and encouraging and help to create a really fun and inclusive environment within the group.
