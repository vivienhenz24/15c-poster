Not the best course. Readings can be tough, and the format of the class is a presentation by a student of the week's reading for the first half of class and then a discussion of the Rhino assignment we did for that week. Instruction on how to use Rhino was minimal, which made assignments tougher to do.
This seminar is good if you are interested in architecture and want a low stakes way to learn more about the field. Some of the assignments are difficult to do with a limited knowledge of how to use Rhino but overall they are very doable. If you take this class, know that it is much more work than you regular freshman seminar, and there were some homework assignments that took 8+ hours, but I still recommend taking this class if architecture is something that interests you.
Be prepared to think critically about the buildings you inhabit. Do not limit your architectural designs to conventional thinking.
You should take this class if your interested in architecture but don't know much about it– its not that hard its just effort and passion.
This was certainly an interesting class, and it made me think about architecture, as well as the physical spaces and materials around me, in a new way. However, a lot of the discussions we had in the seminar were very abstract and theoretical, to the point where most of it would go over my head and I would wonder what the point of the discussion even was. We referenced quite a few interesting pieces of architecture, but I wish we spent more time dissecting the projects. In addition, a lot of the conversations and readings we had on architecture were western–oriented, and so it was disappointing to not see examples and references to non–western cultures. So unfortunately, in this aspect, the seminar didn't live up to my expectations.

For the design portion of the course, Rhino definitely has a good amount of a learning curve, and it can often be frustrating to work with. But with each design iteration, it does become easier to work with, and after many hours and lots of sweat, blood, and tears, it's rewarding to see an idea of a house become an actual, created object. Even though the final project was difficult, I really enjoyed the freedom and creativity associated with it.

The highlight of the class though was Professor Holder –– he's such an intelligent person and he always has something poetic and deep to say about virtually everything. Every conversation I have had with him has been so intellectually stimulating, and he is also just such a supportive and nice perso
Very fun class! Lowkey, casual class seminar style that lets you discuss and break down various architecture styles and thought processes.
