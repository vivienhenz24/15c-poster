This is an amazing class! I imagine it'll be very hard if you don't have a science background. I leaned heavily on my general science and chemistry background. Also, you'll have to do coding, so coding experience in R or python is helpful. Or just any coding experience and the confidence that you can learn. Bryan is an amazing lecturer that really engages the class and also teaches with humor. He knows a lot and has made the class very engaging and hands–on. One of my favorite science classes at Harvard:)
Honestly, this class was great and I would highly recommend taking it. The content is interesting and incredibly broad in its scope – even if it doesn't have the time to go extremely deep into certain more tangential areas as a result, there is truly something for everyone to gain here regardless of if you're more policy–focused, engineering–focused, or somewhere in between (e.g., don't take this course expecting to become an expert on fluid dynamics or hydrology or public policy or anything, but you will be very well–versed on the core cycles/flows/processes underpinning the natural world by the time you're done; take this course to gain a strong fundamental sense of how the environment works overall and how to approach and model the science/cause–and–effect relationships of key issues). Labs are particularly fun (and you'll get to use some awesome equipment along the way, expect to look forward to this for sure!), and Prof. Yoon does a great job lecturing and putting together the HW. The workload isn't too bad either, provided you start early on those lab reports.
Take it! Bryan is lovely and you getting a great understanding of chemical and physical environmental concepts around Cambridge and in water/forest environments generally.
Definitely worth taking. best upper level ESE class.
This is a great class! It covers a really cool range of topics surrounding environmental science, and the course load is not too much so that it overwhelms the actual content. Bryan is a fantastic lecturer also, and makes sure that the labs/assignments are interesting and relevant.
