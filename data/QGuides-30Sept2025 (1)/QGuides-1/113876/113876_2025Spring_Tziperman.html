Pros:
Eli Tziperman: I believe Eli Tziperman is one of the kindest men on the planet –– I am so glad to have met and learned from him.

Linear Algebra Confidence: I had previously taken AM22a and not learned much on the subject. Taking AM120 reinvigorated my understanding of linear algebra and truly made me feel confident in my ability to grasp and understand topics in this crucial field of math.

Community: AM120 has a fantastic community as a function of the sheer amount of support offered. The Monday 5–7 HW helps and the numerous office hours offered throughout the week provide basis for connection and collaboration that form a strong sense of community. I enjoyed this a lot.

AM120 Textbook: Follow the textbook, seriously. Contains literally all the material covered in the course and on the problem sets. No need to read it front and back before every lecture; it suffices to just read the relevant section for the pset as you are doing the pset.

Cons:
Hand calculations: These are the one thing I would certainly change about the psets. Repetitive hand calculations (for example, doing convolutions by hand) do not help with 1) learning the material 2) the enjoyment of working o the problem. The first hand calculation is fine, but to do hand calculations for one problem that can take up to two hours hurts, especially when you know a computer program can do it in seconds. This truly hurt because we could save so much time doing the problem the modern way. Or maybe this requires changing the problem to match current techniques (perhaps, instead of doing a convolution by hand, we could build our own CNN to do an image recognition task, etc). I truly believe that the course would benefit from adding some more up–to–date application on the problem sets/course material.

Coding Problems: Usually, we just copy and paste code from the AM120 index, instead of writing pseudocode, thoughtfully approaching the problem, etc. I was coding in two other classes this semester so I was fine not really doing it for this class, and I had already learned neural networks, CNNs, etc and coded them, yet if I had not, I certainly would have appreciated more coding hands on exercises in class and some walk–throughs for coding pset problems.

AM 120 was an interesting course. Before continuing, I will say I took this course as a last–minute req–filler as a senior, so consider what I say with a grain of salt (since a lot of the material was repeat from previous courses).

The course, in my opinion, did a great job at providing an overview of computational methods. Eli is an extremely enthusiastic professor and helps to foster such a great community in the course.

If there is one thing I want to say, however, is that the course focuses so much on computational and programming knowledge for the psets, but then the exams are all paper exams with access to only scientific calculators (+ cheatsheets). This I dont mind as much, but the issue is that because most psets are coding focused, the exams can be a really quick punch, since you spend most of the semester honing your programming skills and higher level abstraction of the subject, then the exam asks you to perform 4x4 matrix multiplication 5 times by hand for question 1 part a under time pressure. It can be a bit discouraging when studying, since you may correctly understand how a neural network works and exactly how to program one and what operations to do, but you get the question wrong because you accidentally miscalculated the first element of the second out of fourth activation vector and your answer was 0 instead of 1.

tldr; material is good, professor is great, exams were tedious
