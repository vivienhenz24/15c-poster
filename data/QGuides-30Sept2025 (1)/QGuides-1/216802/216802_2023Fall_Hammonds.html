Comments
This course covers incredibly interesting, pertinent subject matter. As a STEM concentrator taking this class to fulfill a divisional distribution, I expected to be in over my head. I certainly did not feel as if I was. The class moved fairly slowly, probably because it met only one time a week (I'm not sure if this is helpful, just given the fact that I'm not super familiar with how seminars are supposed to work), and I found that the workload was also pretty insubstantial. That being said, the readings were always intriguing and made me more familiar with parts of history previous courses of mine had overlooked. I wish we could have discussed more in class, but all in all, an interesting class.
Professor Hammonds is extremely knowledgeable and informed on this matter. Please run and take this class.
this is a wonderful course. While the workload is low, students take away a lot from the course!
This class was confusingly easy. the assignments were very simple– they were basically reading comprehension questions. Despite this class being a seminar, there was almost no discussion component of the class and it was almost all lecture. Prof. Hammonds was extremely knowledgeable about the subject and it was amazing to hear her talk, but I feel like I didn't get much from the class because there were almost no opportunities for me to engage. However, the class was almost no work and not challenging, and even the final project was not stressful for me to complete.
