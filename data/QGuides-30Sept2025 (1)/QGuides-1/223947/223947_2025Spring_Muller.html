This class is genuinely one of the best I've ever taken at Harvard. I've learned so much about not only the criminal justice system, but thinking about how we see punishment and humanity in society. Professor Muller is great and being able to talk through these issues deeply has given me so much insight into this topic. Take this class!!!
If you have an interest in prison TAKE THIS COURSE!! The professor is great and the materials are amazing.
This class is the best class Ive taken at at Harvard. If you get the chance, do not miss an opportunity to be taught by Professor Muller.
This is an excellent introduction to the incarceration system in the U.S., and the discussions were my favorite part of the week. Don't be afraid to be critical of the texts you are reading and be vocal about it during discussions, because chances are other people also share your feelings. Be prepared for your beliefs to be challenged and changed. My one critique is that I wish we had more papers exploring minority populations within the prison like women and transgender individuals, especially with how much they're coming up in current politics, and I hope some more readings and discussions on these subjects can be had in future iterations.
Also, please dedicate time to this class. I had a packed schedule and took this class because I wanted an engaging class without a lot of work outside of class, and I found myself really struggling with the final essay because I hadn't been able to build it up sufficiently outside of class or give it the time it deserved. The final paper is a lot more work than you expect and my essay definitely suffered because I couldn't give it the time it needed. I still learned a lot from this class and enjoyed it so so much, but make sure you're able to make the most of it.
Also also, schedule office hours ahead of time and learn how the system works. I really struggled to get a time slot and you will probably have to schedule several weeks out. Just something to keep in mind when figuring out your paper.
