a good gen ed!!
I would recommend this course to students looking to fulfill their A&C GenEd requirement. The workload is very reasonable overall and lectures are well–organized. Professor Pirozhenko can come off as a bit intense upon first meeting her, but she is really very funny and attentive. Don't be afraid!

Pros: This course has a good balance of readings between scholarly literature and fairytales. This definitely makes it more fun than the average GenEd (the stories are interesting and there are always adaptations, or versions you haven't heard of). Professor Pirozhenko clearly puts a huge amount of effort into her presentations and materials and cares deeply about the course content. I really enjoyed the visual art and movies she shared during class! Professor Pirozhenko was also very receptive to feedback from the course staff and moved the date of the midterm when she realized it fell on Housing Day and altered the requirements of the final paper to be more reasonable.

Cons: The workload is sometimes unevenly distributed between the weeks. There were some weeks when we were expected to read just two fairytales and others when it was necessary to read 10+. Additionally, the midterm felt a little bit frustrating. There were T/F questions that spanned the scope of all the fairytales we had read and text IDs for all of the secondary literature. This was a fair amount of work to study for and it felt like there were details being emphasized in the "study guide" that were not a focus of the course. The actual exam, however, was fair and the short response were very reasonable, but it felt unreasonable that we should be expected to remember details about the 50+ fairytales we had read at that point.

The class is a great way to close the aesthetics gened. Reading the fairy tales is super fun, and they are very useful when you can put them in a cultural, historical, and social context in class. Professor Pirozhenko's lectures can seem a little bit dry as she mainly just reads off the script, but she puts a lot of effort into engaging students with the material in the classroom and facilitates a lot of discussion. She also very clearly cares about the class and its content, making her a great professor for this class overall. Bastian is a gem of a TF; you will be so lucky if you get into his section. I unironically was running to his section every week as it was super engaging and fun. Every section hour flew by really quickly. Overall, the class is very interesting, especially if you have time to put into it. The assignments are super introspective, and you will get a lot out of them if you decide to put effort into them.

The scholarly readings contained lots of Marxism, feminism, and critical theory, which I didn't love, but there were other perspectives as well, and I appreciated that Dr. Pirozhenko seemed to genuinely respect the work of the Grimms, rather than just dismissing it as the work of "dead white men" that shouldn't be worth anything to us now. Section was a lot of fun, as were the response papers and final project. Overall, I would recommend this course.
