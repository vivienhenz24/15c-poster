I would tell them to make sure they know exactly why they want to take this course. That it is mostly theoretical and requires a strong math background.
This is a fantastic course! Dr. Wakeley is an amazing professor. He is incredibly knowledgable and makes the class material very accessible despite the concepts being difficult. I highly recommend this course.
This is an excellent class if you are interested in gaining familiarity with the mathematical language of evolutionary biology (i.e., theoretical population genetics). It is not a replacement for OEB 242, and I would probably recommend taking 242 before this one (although I didn't, and I did fine). Having comfort with basic probability theory (at the level of Stat 110) is essential to get anything useful out of the class; familiarity with stochastic processes (Stat 171) would have been helpful for the second half of the course, although I didn't have it.

Problem sets/assignments are fairly straightforward; you can usually finish them in section. The final project is an interesting original research question in which you use msprime to simulate some coalescent scenario, and can be a lot of fun if you start early. For undergrads: this is a grad class, which means that the purpose of problem sets is not to punish you for not properly learning the material – you can skate by without learning very much, so it's incumbent on you to read the book and ruminate on the lecture material outside of the class. You won't get very much out of the class if you don't.
Take this class if you are interested in population genetics or evolution. Also make sure you are familar with probability, statistics and linear alegbra.
This class was fantastic! Great teaching staff, super interesting content, and very reasonable workload. John is an extremely clear and knowledgeable lecturer as well as just a really nice person. I came into this course with more of a stats background and developed a lot of quantitative intuition about evolution.
Very interesting class however lectures can be fast–paced and difficult to keep up with sometimes. Start class homeworks early.
