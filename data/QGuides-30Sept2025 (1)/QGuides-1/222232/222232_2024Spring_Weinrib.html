I cannot recommend this course highly enough. First and foremost Prof. Weinrib is just the best. I have not had many, if any, better professors during my time at Harvard. She is brilliant, razor–sharp, one of the most engaging lecturers I've ever heard, and also extremely warm, kind, and funny. She alone is a reason to take this course. Additionally, you are better off as a historian when you take this course since you really get at the heart of what it means to sift through competing narratives and make historical arguments. I didn't realize this until I was done with the course, but it is remarkable. The material is of course very interesting too and the readings are not lengthy at all, which makes it easier to engage. You will also be cold–called! That can be scary as an FAS student, but honestly it helped me focus more and I got more out of the classes where I was prepared to be cold–called than the ones I wasn't.

HIST 1219 was an overall delight. Cannot recommend it highly enough, even if it requires putting in some work.
TAKE THIS CLASS! Professor Weinrib is phenomenal and you will learn so much. If you're planning on applying to HLS as an undergrad it's approachable but difficult, so you'll be rewarded for working hard but never feel like you're drowning. Cannot recommend highly enough.
This class is challenging, but it is so worth it. Professor Weinrib is a phenomenal lecturer, she is fair just, and kind, yet will still hold you to high expectations. The content is eye–opening and the readings, albeit hefty, are engaging. If you are someone who enjoys learning for learning sake and who finds history interesting, I would highly recommend this course.
Spend as much time with the readings as you can before class! I stupidly took this class while writing a thesis which led me to often fall behind with the readings. Don't let that happen! you lose out on a lot when you show up to lecture not having already wrestled with the readings, in my opinion.
This is a fantastic class if you're willing to challenge your understanding of how law functions in society and history, and Professor Weinrib has fantastic lectures and really helps stitch everything together.
This is a fantastic class! I look at both history and the law with a new perspective now and Professor Weinrib is a wonderful lecturer. If you have ever wanted to take a legal history course, this is an amazing option and you will cover so much.
