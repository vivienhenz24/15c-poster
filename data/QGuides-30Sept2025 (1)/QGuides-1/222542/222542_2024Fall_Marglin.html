This is a thought–provoking class, where you learn and build knowledge on socialist theories as a comparison to the status quo. Even if you are not into economics or philosophy this is still one of the only classes from which you actually take a way a lot of interesting information. Professor Marglin is such a sweetheart, he brought us apples from his orchard every single class, and was always attentive to what we had to say. He offers awesome feedback and is a unique resource in the economics department. Every class we had interesting perspectives on socialism, and Professor Marglin didn't shy away from listening to contrarian arguments. He also did well in selecting the members of the seminar, and if you want a course where you get to know your classmates really well on an intellectual level this is such a good pick. The readings were moderate in workload, but it is a seminar afterall and their all relevant to studying economics. I think the best thing about this class is the liberty to think and understand the readings, something you don't often see in economics courses that just assume an ideal market.
I would recommend this seminar only if you are strongly interested in the topic
TAKE THIS CLASS!! Professor Marglin is easily the best professor in Harvard I have met so far! He is an incredible thinker and teacher! Through the duration of the course, I felt like I was invited to openly participate in a dialogue with a great value to the academic community. Every single idea I had after doing the readings felt important and like a potential contribution to the academic community. Professor Marglin brings that feeling! ENROL!
TAKE THIS SEMINAR! Professor Marglin is an amazing professor who always has office hours open to discuss different topics, and you will come out of the class with a whole new perspective on the world. The readings are important economic works that will help you understand historical events, and it has easily been my favorite class this semester. Can't recommend it highly enough!
This course definitely has much more extensive readings and essay assignments than other first–year seminars, but it is a very interesting topic and it is clear that Professor Marglin is passionate about exploring the subjects at hand and cares a lot about students.
