It's a good class. The readings are interesting, but you can get by only reading some of them and reading summaries of the longer ones online. Sections are a lot of great discussions. The assignments are just 3 2–page papers on any of the readings or images from class and then the final project at the end (which, to be fair, is a little long: 10–15 page essay or an equivalent creative project). All of the answers for the midterm and final you can get from the review session before each one.
This is genuinely such an amazing GENED. It is pretty easy in terms of workloads and readings, and the readings that you actually have to do are very interesting. Depending on who you get as your TF, the grading can change a lot but generally they are very lenient and care about helping you be better. If you are interested in modern politics this is not the class for you as it almost all history, but it is very applicable to most social science and humanities concentrations.
Stay on top of the readings.
This is an absolutely fantastic course. Prof. Stauffer is easily one of the best professors I have had at Harvard, no question. It is of course a lot of work, but the final project/paper can be whatever you want it to be. The fact that there is a midterm exam and final exam is not actually a substantial addition to your workload, and the readings are all relevant and extremely interesting if you have an interest in the subject matter. Stauffer is one of the best lecturers I have ever had. If you have any interest in race relations throughout American history, America leading up to the Civil War, or any related topics, I strongly recommend.

We've had entire class periods dedicated to the use of photography in the war, the song "Battle Hymn of the Republic", and the film "Birth of A Nation". Additionally, you'll discover so many thought–provoking facts that will stay with you for a while.

Professor John Stauffer is also great, and is clearly an expert in his field. Section is very engaging as well. Take the class!
