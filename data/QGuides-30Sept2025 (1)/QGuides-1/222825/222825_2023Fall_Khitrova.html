Take the class! It's so fun and Professor Khitrova is a really amazing lecturer.
This course provides an excellent historical survey of silent film, carefully analyzing and animatedly discussing some of the most formative and groundbreaking films of the era.
This class is a lot of fun. Daria is extremely nice and funny, she knows a massive amount about the era, you probably don't, the films are all fantastic (rare for a film course!) as well as extremely influential and educational (you'll understand far more about movies after taking this course), and the course load is light (other than watching movies, you just have to do a small amount of usually easy reading and do a couple pretty easy projects).

Take it as a fifth class if you can. It's not much work, you'll learn a lot, the professor's great, and it's fun. What more could you want?
This is a great course for anyone who likes to watch silent films or is simply interested in learning more about cinema in general. The workload is reasonable, and the discussions were always very insightful. Professor Khitrova knows a lot about the history of film, and that is reflected well in her lectures and work feedback.
this professor will be one of the BEST you ever have at Harvard –– super interesting, engaging lecturer and super knowledgeable about the topic. i truly enjoyed learning about silent film and watching a variety of them, and discussions were interesting!
There aren't a lot of assignments in the course, so put effort into the ones that exist. Take the course –– it's super fun and you learn a lot about film.
