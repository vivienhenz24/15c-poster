At times, the written assignments felt monotonous, repetitive, and overwhelming, but Professor Hummel's lectures were extremely effective at teaching the material. It is the TF Zoe Butler that has given me reservations. She graded harshly, but only because she had a specific style in mind and took away points when the writing style did not conform to her preferences. Though I would not hesitate to take another class with Professor Hummel, I would avoid this TF.
This is an amazing course. As far as gov classes are concerned, this has been one of my favorites so far! Professor Hummel has done a beautiful job of breaking each week down into manageable chunks, with a good mix of interesting readings that connect well with what is discussed in lecture. Keeping up with each of the readings is more than possible, and she uses fun reading competitions on Poll everywhere to check for understanding of those readings. The class has roughly six response memos, due every other week and 3–4 pages each, asking you to connect the memoirs you read to the internal or external metric that the memoirs are associated with. Additionally, it has a final paper, 12–15 pages, where you evaluate the success of a chosen movement based on the semester's worth of learning. However, there are pre–submissions built into the course to give you feedback and make sure you are on the right track for the big paper at the end of the semester. for those who need any kind of accommodations, the teaching staff are incredible at making sure you have what you need, and as long as you communicate ahead of time, professor Hummel is extremely proactive in making sure that you are taken care of. Finally, the course staff is super understanding and flexible, providing a 48 hour extension bank that you can distribute as you please throughout the semester for every assignment except the final one. The lectures are engaging, the content is timely, the readings are a lot of fun, and the teaching staff are super kind. Take this course!
Super timely and applicable course during the Israel–Palestine protests. The class gives you lots to think about and does a good job of attacking protests as rigorously as possible. Granted, like all Gov classes, things can feel a bit hand wavy at times, but thats just how courses like this inevitably have to be because of the subject matter. Professor Hummel is a very knowledgeable instructor and clearly knows what she's talking about. The class is extremely reasonable in terms of grading and work load. Would recommend for the comparative requirement for Gov, or just as a general elective!
really loved this class! if you engage with the materials, then you will definitely enjoy and take a lot from this course.
This course is pretty chill, and fluctuates between interesting and repetitive. Prof Hummel gives great lectures and is so warm and kind, and if Zoe's the TF she leads awesome fun sections (but can grade randomly pretty harshly on the reading responses). It does get repetitive because you're basically just learning theories of protest every class and the reading responses get tiresome even if they're short and straightforward. That said, the readings are really interesting and pretty short, the memoirs are cool, and you will see protest movements differently after taking this class.
I hope you don't have Zoe as your TF. She's a great person, but not exactly a great TF. Try to engage with Professor Hummel herself instead of seeking help from the TF; it will serve you well.
Amazing class. Professor Hummel is a great lecturer and I loved the small group setting of this class. It really allows you to form your own opinion on protest movements, even though we all learn the same content. The TF Zoe was literally the best TF I've ever had. She was so so funny and made everyone feel super comfortable in section. One thing I would love to see in this class is a rubric for assignments because sometimes the grading felt a bit unpredictable. Other than that, loved ittt!!!
Topic and professor–wise, this class is incredible. The course material is wonderfully curated by Professor Hummel, who must be one of the most diligent and impressive professors in the gov department today. Her slides are clear, she is accessible outside of class, and is always more than happy to clarify any questions. One of my favourite professors at Harvard so far.

The sections led by the TF are another matter. This years TF, while well–meaning, hosted chaotic sections that felt like a waste of time and completely unrelated to the course material. She was a wonderful person with a great sense of humour but rather unprofessional as a TF – vague feedback, unclear assignment instructions, and not always accessible outside of class.

That being said, with a different TF, I would highly recommend the course as it was incredibly engaging and interesting. The assignments were manageable and set you up very well for the final paper.
I highly recommend this course for students of government or those with any interest in politics. It's well–designed, organized, and usually a good size which allows for lots of interaction.
