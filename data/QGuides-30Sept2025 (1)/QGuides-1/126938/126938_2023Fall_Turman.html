What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I greatly enjoyed this course and I feel like I have made lots of progress in my speaking and listening because of this course. The course is helpful for learning lots of French grammar as well. It pushes you and helps you grow without being so challenging that it is burdensome or "drowning." Professor Reynes specifically was great. I also enjoyed the fact that we focus on French culture outside of the country France, looking at other parts of the world.
If you are debating taking this course or dropping into a lower level, I would honestly say to take French 20 versus French 10/11. I doubted myself before the course, but found that all of the immersive assignments really helped my speaking and will definitely make a difference for when I take French again.
Taking French 20 will immensely help with fluency; I learned more in this semester than most of my previous years of middle and high school French combined.
Take this class! I learned a lot about the francophone world and the global influence of the language as well as many interesting things from a variety of cultures. You have the opportunity to practice all the new grammatical concepts you will learn as well as learn about relevant topics. I feel like I can speak the language significantly better now than following the conclusion of 11 the prior semester, so you can make substantial progress in terms of comprehension, speaking, and writing ability.
