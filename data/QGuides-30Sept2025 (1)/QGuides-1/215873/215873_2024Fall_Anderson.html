Last year, some of the students might've been disgruntled from how the course was taught because it was being managed differently, but I think Prof. Anderson has got it figured out for this year, so your experience of the course will be likely the same as mine: the workload is not high at all (except for the final project at the end of the semester that you are continually encouraged to work on throughout the whole semester in many ways). Prof. Anderson is kind, engaging, and his lectures, while science–heavy, also introduce a lot of current events stuff, so it's not too STEM–heavy or humanities–heavy. Would recommend.
TAKE THIS CLASS! Professor Anderson and Miguel are the best, and you learn a lot. One of the best parts of this class was how open it was to discussion and dialogue, so if there is an instance where you disagree with what someone said (even the professor), speak up! Also, pick a project idea that interfaces with climate change in a way that is interesting to you. There were a wide variety of projects that myself and my classmates did and all of them were both interesting and important.
This is a great class all around: time, classwork, community.
Professor Anderson is so knowledgeable, and he's so sweet. I love his lectures. This class made me change my mind about geneds; I want more like this, where I'll learn so much while having a lot of fun!
Wonderful content but disorganized – last–minute assignment changes, no grades posted during semester, etc. I don't regret taking this course at all but wish it had been more structured.
Professor Anderson is amazing and delivers on his promise to get all students to the "99%" percentile in terms of understanding climate change. Through this class, you'll get a holistic understanding of every aspect of climate, from the factors inducing climate change, to the range of disastrous effects, to what we can actually do to solve this problem. Particularly interesting is Professor Anderson's unique perspective on how the most promising thing we can do is to produce accurate and scientifically–backed quantitative forecasts of various climate risks using extremely high quality data collected from balloons / airplanes / spaceships. Indeed, he pioneered a similar approach back in the day and is part of the reason why humanity has essentially solved the ozone crisis.

Our class was unique in the sense that there were only ~12 people (because of early registration mishaps), so lectures were full of very illuminating discussions among Professor Anderson and all the students. Sections were also very homey and fun to go to. In terms of work, there were no exams, but instead a few essay / short calculation homeworks plus a big final project. I liked this a lot since the final project gave me a chance to experience what it's really like to do research on an aspect of climate. Overall, this is an amazing gened to fulfill the science requirement and at the same time learn almost all that you can learn about one of the most challenging issues of our time.
