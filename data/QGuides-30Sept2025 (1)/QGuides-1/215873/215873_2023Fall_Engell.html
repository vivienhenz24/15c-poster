This is a good overview of climate change and it spans many different topics. The readings and guest lectures are very interesting. However, I will say there are many assignments and biweekly quizzes so you have to at least do some of the readings and review the content. This course helped me understand the complexities of climate change and inspired me to be more conscious about the climate crisis. However, the way the class was taught could have been improved, as the lectures often didn’t have much structures and they often were repetitive..The connection between concepts (especially scientific ones) covered in lecture and climate change was often nonexistent, so it was sometimes confusing to understand the applications of the science. As a result, I would say you probably need some scientific background to understand the scientific topic lectures because it can get confusing really fast.

As for the assignments, the class doesn’t require too much work, but I found the assignments to be specific but not really specific at the same time, because they would ask you a bunch of questions but would not really give you clear guidelines on how to approach the assignment. And in my opinion, the essay part of the assignment was usually graded not completely fairly. The exams are take–home, but they do take a long time to do.

That being said, this class is definitely not horrible. I liked the section portion because it (at least for my section) allowed for a more free exchange of dialogue about climate change and solutions. I also do like the professors — they really have done a lot for climate response and advancement (although Engell is retiring after this semester I believe). I would definitely recommend everyone take a climate change–related course at least at some point because it is such an important issue, but maybe just not this one.
