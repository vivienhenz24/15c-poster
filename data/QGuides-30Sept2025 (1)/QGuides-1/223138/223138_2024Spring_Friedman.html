This is likely one of the hardest gov classes one can take. The professor is awesome and the material is interesting, but the grading structure means A LOT rides on the final essay and test. They are literally all that matters. The final was insane –– it's ~50 questions pulling from random lectures and information you're supposed to know (like asking for examples of concepts from outside of class). It's graded very nicely, but it is still much more difficult than the typical essay exam that accompanies a majority of classes in the department. Additionally, there are only two large writing assignments, which makes it hard to gauge whether or not your final essay is good enough to get the grade you desire because you're operating with limited feedback.
That being said, I feel like I gained a lot from this class, and my understanding of US Foreign Policy has dramatically changed. Professor Friedman was great, nice, and incredibly insightful, and I thoroughly enjoyed my experience. Just know that if you only want the credit, there's probably easier classes that get you the same thing.
This course is awesome, highly recommend. Prof. Friedman is excellent but unfortunately he was visiting from Dartmouth. Harvard Government department should make a big effort to bring him to Cambridge, the department needs more scholars like him. Assignments were fair, not overwhelming, and very practical. I came away with strong knowledge in insurgency and modern civil conflicts
Take this course if you can dedicate a lot of time to research
Please take this class. It is unfortunate that he is a visiting professor from Dartmouth –– I would've jumped at the opportunity to take another course with him if he was at Harvard for longer. This class is not only fascinating, but it is taught incredibly well, both lecture and assignment–wise.
I really enjoyed this class! Prof. Friedman is a great lecturer and very knowledgable about the topic at hand.
Do not take this course if you are interested in learning about civil wars. Take this course if you are interested in learning about US counterinsurgency strategy. The course provides only a topical treatment of civil wars broadly in recent years and focuses a lot on the US response, especially in Iraq and Afghanistan. Both these cases are not fully appropriate to the course.
If that is what you want, the professor is an excellent instructor and is very helpful.
This course is more difficult than I think it appears. I thought the final was slightly unpredictable, and if you are not used to writing in the particular format this class follows it will be more difficult to get an A on the papers than you think.
Professor Friedman is great. Great lecturer. Interesting. Engaging.
The course is a comprehensive yet thorough overview on contemporary strategy in international civil wars & insurgency from a US perspective. Lectures are engaging, combines a lecture style class with a seminar/discussion section thoughtfully, and the assignments are well–thought–out and really teach how to write. Challenging yet manageable. Learned not only course content but how to write more effectively through well thought out and helpfully guided (but not over guided) assignments. Truly a great class to take for IR and on how to write effectively for gov overall.
TAKE THIS COURSE!!! Professor Friedman will make sure you learn a lot in a low–stress environment. if you are interested in intrastate wars across the world, you will be so glad that you took this course.
