It is conceptually challening but you will learn so much so its worth it!
The class is built on a solid foundation. The lecture slide decks are detailed enough to stand on their own, and the mix of optional and required readings helps bridge course concepts with real research. If you skim the slides before class and read at least one paper each week, you will walk into lecture prepared and find the exams which closely follow lecture material and practice questionsfair and manageable. Adding to this, the teaching fellows are genuinely invested in student success, so scheduling office–hour time early pays dividends.

That said, a few components can feel rough around the edges. The News & Views assignment often seems forced when treated as a standalone essay, but tying it to your final–project topic makes the reading serve double duty. The final project itself drops you into open–ended research quickly; a brief scope/sanity check with a TF before submitting your proposal can prevent hours of rework. Section labs introduce new software each week without much context, but these softwares are what you have to use in your final project, so do try to pay attention during section.

If the course evolves, three tweaks would tighten things further: quick solve–along checkpoints in lecture to break up dense slide runs, a section redesign built around one overarching problem tackled with different software each week, and a short workshop on turning a primary paper into a polished News & Views commentary.

Until then, future students should skim slides ahead of time, annotate during lecture, and review immediately afterward; treat every section as a puzzle session focused on tool choice; leverage office hours earlyespecially for the final project; and pick a News & Views topic that dovetails with the final project so the workload compounds instead of doubling.

Chem 171 is a great class overall, but it does have its quirks. Here are some notable pros and cons:

Pros:
– Professor Balskus is great! She is incredibly knowledgeable and friendly, and genuinely wants everyone to learn
– The TFs are also quite good– they gave very useful feedback, held frequent office hours, and genuinely wanted everyone to succeed
– The course material is quite interesting– the first 1/2 of the course is pretty much a speedrun of Chem 27 (how organisms make all kinds of biomolecules) but more in–depth and with some added categories, and the second 1/2 of the course is how to apply your knowledge of how biomolecules are made to do interesting science (such as identifying the genes responsible for making the molecule, identifying the molecule made by a set of genes, and using biocatalysis to engineer new ways to make molecules)
– The class is a good mix of Undergrads and Grad students, so you will definitely meet new people
– The class takes ~7 hours per week, which is pretty standard
– There are a few guest lectures from people in Biochem / Biocatalysis who come and visit, so you will see industrial applications of the topics and maybe even build useful connections

Cons:
– The sections are not very engaging. The sections are quite interesting in theory– each section, you learn about and use a new Bioinformatic tool, and in your final project, you use 3 of those tools you learned about. However, the sections do not have enough structure in my opinion, as most of the time was spent just playing around with the bioinformatic tool yourself
– The grading on psets was incredibly harsh– multiple points are taken off for simple / non–understanding mistakes, such as not writing water or acid/base co–products. This detracted from finding where my understanding was lacking
– The psets, tests, and projects took a while to be returned (but the feedback was good, so this is not the biggest deal)
– No information was provided about the grading curve at any point in the semester

TL;DR: Chem 171 is a good class and I would recommend it to anyone interested in biochemistry. It has some quirks, such as the grading being harsh and the sections not having enough structure, but overall, I learned a lot and had a great time in the class!
