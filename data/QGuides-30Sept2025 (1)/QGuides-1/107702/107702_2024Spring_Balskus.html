Highly recommend if you want to learn biochemistry and workload is very reasonable!
This class is pretty good if you want to learn biochem. The grading was arbitrary at time. My peers and I lost so many points for things that were not that important. For instance, we filled out a grid with check marks, and we lost 33% of the question for basically not putting the row and column in the box we checked, even though it was obvious which row and column we were filling out since it was literally a grid. We also lost 25% of a question for not putting one oxidation state on one transition metal, which would be the same amount of points as if we didn't put an oxidation state on all the transition metals. Surely there is a more fair way to grade, especially if there will be a cutoff for the final grades because at this point I might be a few points below what is necessary for an A, and it would be frustrating to not get that given that I understand the material and just lost points due to a strange grading system. Beyond that, the material covered is good and relevant to the field. The final project is fun. So if they fix the grading, then this class is pretty good since you learn a lot in an effective way.
This course teaches a wide scope of reactions and really opens your mind to the world of biosynthesis. It certainly feels connected to Chem 27 in the first half but then broadens even more to experimental techniques in the second half which I enjoy. I imagine one reason why there weren't a *ton* of enrollees was because of the new quantum upper level taught but also because Chem 170 has take home exams and only two problem sets. However, I do think because the first half of the class really has mechanisms that the exam format makes sense. The teaching staff is knowledgeable and there to help you succeed. Sure, they can be unnecessarily picky with small protonation steps/other trivial things, but you adjust. Overall, recommend to anyone who wants to have an understanding of natural products and all the techniques to study them.
It's a good classyou learn a lot about biosynthesis and the flow of the class is coherent, starting with background knowledge and moving into methods. Some parts are rough around the edges, like certain parts of the final proposal, but there's a good amount of support too.
I like the harsh grading in this class because it allows me to learn more effectively.
This course is great if you enjoyed Chem27, either the broader concepts covered or the mechanisms. It provides greater detail about both of these things and really helped me to contextualize the important science involved.
This has been, without a doubt, my favorite course I have taken in my past 3 years at Harvard. Yes, it is a challenging course, but it was incredibly fulfilling, and I found the rigor to be at a reasonable level. The lectures are always very engaging and relevant to the latest research and advancements, and the assignments also always taught me so much. I truly enjoyed taking this class and have found my interest in biological synthesis to have deepened tremendously.
