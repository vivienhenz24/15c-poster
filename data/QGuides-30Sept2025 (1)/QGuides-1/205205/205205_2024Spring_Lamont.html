What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Take it, especially if you want to write a thesis! Don't be afraid of the time commitment.
Do not take this class simultaneously with your junior tutorial, but it would be a great class to take in preparation for your thesis. It can be a large time commitment, but you get out of the class what you put into it. It isn't often you get the opportunity to work so closely with an esteemed faculty such as Prof. Lamont (i.e. discussions and direct feedback from her on your writing/ideas) so take advantage of it! A great course overall.
Highly recommended!! As long as you are interested in conducting qualitative research, take this course! This also would be a good practice for the thesis.
If you are considering writing a thesis, I would highly recommend this class. It would be best to come in with a potential project in mind, as you do start working on the project quite early in the semester. Professor Lamont is brilliant, and her passion for qualitative methods was evident. If you are studying sociology or the social sciences in general, it would be a shame to pass up on the opportunity to take this course.
This class is not for the faint of heart. Prof. Lamont is wonderful and it is such a privilege to learn from someone with that much experience and renown in their discipline. Nevertheless, from the very beginning, this class requires many hours per week as you go through each stage of the research process. Conducting a good research project over the course of a semester is really really hard. It is even harder, I learned the hard way, if you take it with another course with a big research paper. So, plan your classes and activities wisely and be prepared to put in the work.
