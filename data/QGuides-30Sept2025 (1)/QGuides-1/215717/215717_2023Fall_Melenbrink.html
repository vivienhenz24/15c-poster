This is an amazing class. It is very unique and you should take it before you graduate Harvard if you can. You will learn how to build things from scratch, 3D print, laser cut, make machines, use Arduino and so much more. You need literally no experience or knowledge about anything to take this class. Nathan and the teaching team are all super helpful and are always around in the lab to help with assignments and projects. It is time consuming but I never felt like I was doing work when I was doing my assignments. It also was incredibly good for my mental and physical health to take a class that was not just staring at my screen and reading or writing or doing psets. All in all, a super awesome class that will teach you so much stuff that you would not learn otherwise and make you a much cooler handier and useful person in case of an apocalypse or something. Take it!!!
Take this course if you feel like you want to learn more about how to build things from scratch in a laid back classroom setting with supportive peers.
An incredibly interesting, fun, and informative overview of a bunch of different fabrication techniques. Professor Melenbrink does an awesome job of introducing all the topics in an approachable and beginner–friendly way, while also going into enough depth that we are able to implement the concepts in a meaningful and deep way. Lectures and the live demos are very engaging. The hands–on component of the course is a huge plus––one of the best opportunities at Harvard in my opinion to actually practice and implement engineering techniques (rather than just studying them conceptually on paper). I started this class not knowing anything about any sort of fabrication, and I learned so much and had so much fun in this class. I HIGHLY recommend this class for anyone who feels that Harvard's engineering/science courses are too theoretical––this is a great way to learn how to actually build things. It is quite time consuming, so you need to be prepared to dedicate significant chunks of time to your projects to get the most out of them. HUGE shoutout to the TFs, they are so helpful and patient.
This was the first course I've taken that's extremely hands on and has absolutely nothing to do with my concentration. Nathan is amazing, sometimes the lectures are hard to follow with no background but overall the most fulfilling course I've taken at Harvard. You definitely get what you put in. Highly recommend!
