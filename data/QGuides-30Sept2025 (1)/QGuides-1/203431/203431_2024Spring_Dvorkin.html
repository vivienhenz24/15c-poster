If you are interested in cosmology you should absolutely take this course. The course offers you a lot and gives you opportunity to choose what to focus on with your final project. Cora is a great professor that has expertise in any area related to the class which means that there will always be enough support and guidance on every topic slightly related to the course.
A must–take if you want to do research in cosmology.
The material of this class is really cool. Within a couple weeks you'll be talking about dark matter and energy, and calculating things like the age of the universe.

But you need to maintain a real interest throughout the semester, because you can coast through this class without really knowing what's going on. The problem sets aren't easy, but for me most of the challenge was in recalling the right definitions and scouring the lecture notes; I didn't have a great picture of what was going on but still was able to do the PSets. And then halfway through the year, the problem sets just stop (and there are no tests, so really no one is checking that you learned a single thing after Spring break).

If you don't take this class, you'll probably never see or do any cosmology, so I think it's worth taking. But actually try – no one will make you. Only the first PSet required a real knowledge of GR, and that was just computing some Riemann tensor components.
This course is great if you have some knowledge of cosmology or general interest. If you don't it's still interesting but might not be worth it to take if you have a lot of other challenging courses. Please start the project early. I did not and it was almost my academic downfall. Chandrika's section and office hours are very helpful and I would definitely recommend
Take Cosmology with Cora this class is amazing! Youll learn about so many fascinating topics, from inflation to dark matter. Coras lectures are captivating and her excitement is contagious. The workload is pretty manageable, and there is a lot of help available. Chandrika was an amazing TF! The final projects are heavily emphasized and a rewarding way to explore whatever interests you!
