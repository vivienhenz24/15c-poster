Overall, this is a pretty good neuro elective, but it hasn't been my favorite neuro course offered. I thought that the material itself was very interesting, and I enjoyed learning about different pathogen mechanisms for bypassing the blood–brain barrier. However, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, there is a lot of content to memorize. The exams are definitely fair, but you won't do well if you don't prepare at least a few days in advance, ideally throughout the semester as notes are posted. Second, the writing assignments aren't difficult, but the grading for them (at least this year) felt arbitrary. I didn't know why I got 8/10 versus 10/10 since feedback wasn't very clear. Additionally, the in–person presentations can get boring at times and are really dependent on how well the two students presenting prepared. You don't get to pick your partner, either, since the presentation schedule is just based on what dates would be convenient for you. I learned a lot, but I wish I would've gotten to interact with my peers more beyond just the person I was scheduled to present with.
The content of the course is incredibly interesting. You learn about such a variety of microorganisms, how they infect the body, and the symptoms that show up in the body/brain. Highly recommend this course! Make sure to be on top of the assignments and exams (flashcards and memorization is probably the best way to memorize because there is a lot of info).
Great neuro class with clinical focus
I really loved this course and thought the content was interesting! Loved Laura and the small seminar format. Great Neuro class to end on as a senior!

While there was a ton of content and it was at times difficult, they were so supportive and helped us to learn so much. Have to study well for the exams, but the lecture slides are really clear and cover what you need to know. The content was really interesting. I loved the applications and case studies we explored. I definitely learned a lot. The exams can be a little difficult, so make sure to study well for them. It's a ton of memorization, don't wait till last minute. I felt like the write–ups and presentation were graded pretty harshly. There weren't many instructions on the write–ups and the grading was mainly subjective. I didn't like that aspect of the course and wish they were more transparent/had a set standardized rubric. That being said, I really enjoyed the discussion aspect of the course and learning more from my peers about the papers they presented. The studies were really fascinating. I also gained a lot of critical thinking skills and research design/methodology skills (which isn't necessarily a huge emphasis of the course) through reading the papers and critically analyzing them. I recommend taking this class although it is not a gem or an easy A. Laura and Brendan were great instructors and really helpful!

Hi! If youre reading this, you're probably a pre–med neuroscience concentrator trying to fulfill your upper–level course requirements. If that's the case, I highly recommend considering other options.

Past q–report comments would glaze this course but I need to warn you that this is NOT A GEM. Again, NOT A GEM. Youll be expected to recall extremely fine details from the slides, down to the smallest specifics. There is a large volume of content and the lectures are not easy to follow. Grading in this course is neither transparent nor generous. Expect points to be taken off for minute details on your presentations, write ups, and final written assignments. Try to ask for a rubric beforehand because they do not provide any of this during class!

There are so much better options to take to fufill your requirements!!
