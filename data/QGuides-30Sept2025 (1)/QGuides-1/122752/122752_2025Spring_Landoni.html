Landoni is fantastic, would definitely take the class just for him. Grading can be difficult but the ec department gives him/all the instructors specific cutoffs to follow.
Mattia is a tough grader. He is pretty fair, but I think you will find him harsher than expected, and you have to work pretty hard to improve grades. I was able to, and it did improve the quality of my work, but I think it should be a fair warning that this class IS hard. You have to spend time on your submissions and carefully think through your reasoning on what you wrote. You must do the readings and you have to think of things to say to get participation credit. Just asking questions is not enough think of something to contribute. Mattia makes you start the paper way earlier than most sections and that is the best because you can just put all your past assignements together (with some editing and expansion of course) and then your final is already done. But, be mindful that you have to as a result pick a topic early and plan ahead when you submit proposals. Simple proposals are better.
Dr. Landoni is an incredible instructor, and the readings are amazing! Take this class.
Mattia is an engaging discussion facilitator in section, and though it was a very unfortunately timed section (not a fan of a post dinner–time class), I generally walked away with a decent amount of interesting knowledge related to the papers' behavioral finance theories + how to do academic research, which is the end goal of the class.

By the nature of all EC sophomore tutorials, there are many assignments: one due nearly every class. My main frustration with the class is that––though I don't know my end grade––the grading seems to edge on deflationary. Getting a single point off on a problem set makes your letter grade an A– for that assignment, two points off gets you to a B+. Granted, the psets are pretty easy (especially if you've taken metrics & stat 100), but I'd rather them make the assignments harder so that there's room to flub on a minor coding error here and there. This is the fault of the course structurenot Mattia.

However, Mattia is also a bit harsh in grading, although his feedback is pretty constructively helpful. This is because you need a 96.25%+ to end with an A in his class, and all his assignments get placed on a letter grade scale, so while your As all help you with some blanket 100s, the A–'s are plentiful and drag that average down quite a bit. This is just something to be mindful of, you can still do well and it seems to be very much a 'you get rewarded with time spent' kind of class. One negative feedback I have about Mattia's grading is that the research proposals will get docked to A– or B+ land due to having empiric methodological imperfections, such as a not quite accurate regression proposal. However, the class also only has STAT100 as a pre–requisite, instead of econometrics; but it seems like having econometrics would be extremely helpful to avoid misunderstandings. I think it is much more challenging to navigate the class if you're seeing these econometrics methodologies for the first time and applying them while barely understanding what they do. It's not like the readings that use a certain regression methodology give a step by step lecture how to apply them to your own variables. I think more guidance by Mattia or the rest of the course team would be apt here.
