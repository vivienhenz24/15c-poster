What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This tutorial may not be suitable for students who have no interest in financial / business economics. But if you find topics in finance remotely interesting, you will have a blast. Professor Landoni is a great teacher who is not only caring but also extremely knowledgeable. In his tutorial, you will explore the intricacies and the beauties of corporate finance, capital markets and accounting. In the process, you will learn how to think about any problem like an economist. To get the most out of this tutorial, spend time doing your readings and take econometrics before this tutorial. Or at the very least, enroll in econometrics concurrently.
Professor Landoni is an absolutely fantastic instructor. "The Taxation of Capital Income" could have very easily been incredibly boring and confusing, but instead Professor Landoni made it the most fun and interesting out of any of my classes. He not only generates enthusiasm for the course content, but makes complicated topics approachable and breaks them down for the class. In this class, I was never afraid to ask a question that might seem stupid and I learned so much. Professor Landoni is also very helpful in office hours, which makes the paper–writing process a lot less intimidating. I would recommend ranking Professor Landoni first in the lottery for sophomore tutorials when picking your classes.
Mr. Landoni was one of the most kind, intelligent, and engaging professors we have ever had. And I do not say that lightly. He genuinely cared about his students. He actively prepared for every session and we all could tell that he put in hours of preparation for every lecture. He was confident about the material he taught, and he had answers to every question we asked. He engaged us and encouraged debate about the topics we discussed, fostering some of the most intellectually rigorous conversations we have had in our college careers thus far; with his guidance and pointers, we not only better understood the points we were making, but we also understood opposing perspectives too. He also took the time to meet with so many of us individually to ensure that we not only understood all the theories we discussed, but also that we understood the econometric process necessary to undertake different research projects. He spent hours beyond the scope of Office Hours and Class to meet and make sure we all understood the material being taught, even though he has another job at the Fed and has a family to care for. That is only the tip of the iceberg as to what makes Mr. Landoni an amazing teacher and mentor.

There are not many teachers who are as passionate and as caring as Mr. Landoni is, yet are also as effective. I learned more in Mr. Landoni's class than we did in some other classes taught by well–seasoned Professors. And that is a testament to his genuine passion for his students and the material at hand.

Mr. Landoni is not a traditional teacher; he is one of the best teachers I have had.
