Take Economics and Mental Health with Matt Basilico! It was amazing and Prof. Basilico was great at facilitating discussions and interacting with students in a way that made us feel supported as students and just as people interested in this topic. Truly a class that taught me a lot but was also not as stressful as other sections of this course just because of the way it was taught and the guidance that the professor provided.
Take this class with Dr. Basilico if you can! This was hands down the most thought provoking, interesting, and enjoyable economics class I have taken at Harvard so far. The readings are curated very well as they give you an opportunity to see how we can apply very common economics concepts and models to mental health and health policy in general, which is something not usually thought in other econ classes. Dr. Basilico himself is very kind, flexible and definitely cares a lot about his students and, specifically, their well–being. His feedback was really helpful for improving my writing skills, and his way of viewing economics/psychopathology gave me a new perspective in the field as well. Just a fantastic section!
take basilico's section
Take this course with Professor Basilico, he's an excellent instructor and the economics of mental health is super interesting. You will learn a lot both in writing good final papers as well as background knowledge.
The Economics of Mental Health course section is phenomenal! The readings and discussions about different types of mental health disorders and the societal presence of mental health will shape how you think about mental health. At times in the course, we think about mental health developing from an evolutionary standpoint, and that was very interesting as well. I think the final paper is the most interesting assignment because you get to run your analysis and regressions on any variables that you want as long as they are related to mental health. Anyone who takes the slightest interest in mental health will love this course.
Favorite course I've taken at Harvard. As long as you engage with the readings (honestly there are a lot of readings and it will take a lot of time), you will do well. If you are interested in mental health and comfortable talking about it, I strongly recommend taking this course.
