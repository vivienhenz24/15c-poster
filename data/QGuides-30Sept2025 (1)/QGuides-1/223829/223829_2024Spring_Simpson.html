TAKE THIS CLASS! If you have any sort of interest in improv, acting, yoga, or fine arts TAKE IT. Pete is one of the most understanding and entertaining people I have ever met. This class was honestly a lot of fun. You get to learn about so many different aspects of movement and performance as it covers a wide range of these topics. At times, it felt a bit disorganized. Some of the assignments had so many parameters that remembering all of the things you had to include in a performance was almost more difficult than the performance itself but this class is a baby and I think Pete is well on his way to smoothing out all the wrinkles. If you come to this class with an open mind, a willingness to move and try new things, and a bit of adaptability, you will do well. Pete is genuinely such a cool instructor with an interesting background. If you have any opportunity to probe his mind about his past experiences with performance, definitely take advantage of it.
Professor Pete made every class so exciting. It was a true honor being able to take his course he often talked about his experiences and the class always had something dynamic and new to show us. The performances in the course were really meaningful and really allowed the class to showcase their emotions and talents through movement.
This class is really fun, and the professor is pretty cool and chill.
This course was simply FUN.
Here's the best way I can summarize what it's about: the instructor is a legendary Blue Man. You know, from the Blue Man Group. Look it up.
Take it for a different sort of challenge. Not the sort of challenge you get from psets or essays, but a challenge nonetheless. You'll have to tap into your creativity (which, if you're like me, you haven't done in a while). You'll have to create and memorize movements and lines and cues as part of performances. You'll have to share those performances with an audience of your peers. None of that is easy. But it is a different sort of challenge. One that will refresh you. One that will stretch your mind in a different sort of way, creating more room for the psets and essays and the like. You'll meet genuine people who are attracted to this sort of space; the sort where you can be comfortable. Comfortable being weird and silly. Comfortable stepping outside your comfort zone. A space where judgment is suspended for the sake of exploring, investigating, pursuing, striving for art.
I'm glad I took this course. It was anxiety–inducing and nerve–wracking at times. Performance is always that way, at least at first. But I gained more confidence and new insights into how my body can move and communicate. This is a great way to balance out the schedule in a way that maintains intellectual engagement.
This is probably the most fun class I've taken at Harvard. It's super chill, there's pretty much no workload outside of the 2.5 hours of class each week, and Pete is a great teacher. Just be attentive for class each week and put effort into your solo and ensemble performances and you'll be fine.
It's important to have more body and movement consciousness and this class delves into that precisely if your concern about the body and how to understand it to build narratives you should take this class.

I am so happy that I had the opportunity to take this course. Not only do I feel more knowledgable in the subject area, but I feel like a healthier person. Professor Simpson is clearly knowledgable and an incredible resource!
