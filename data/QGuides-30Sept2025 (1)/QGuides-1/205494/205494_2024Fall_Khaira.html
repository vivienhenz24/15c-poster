Of course, this tutorial is a required course for the anthropology concentration, but I think it was an almost exclusively enjoyable experience! It's probably one of the least arduous junior tutorials, and it's definitely much less taxing than sophomore tutorial. This course is critical for expanding your view of anthropologists' roles and for planning your senior thesis (whether or not that's something you're sure you'd like to do). It's also a really enjoyable class due to the strong support system, including both course staff and the rest of your cohort.
amazing tutorial. all of the staff are super supportive (I had Mohit), and our teacher:student ratio meant a lot of room to talk with them. super thesis–focused, but they mention/make room for non–thesis–writers too! the weekly "lecture" part is usually guest lectures or workshops, so you get an idea for logistics of research and what the field looks like in practice. the syllabus is super flexible for you to choose which readings work for you.
The teaching staff for this class really make it. They're all really helpful in determining a course of study for upcoming thesis research (if you decide to pursue it).
Damina is really wonderful. Tutorial can be a bit stressful if you are taking this course to prepare for your thesis, since the expectation is generally that you should have an idea of what you want to pursue quite early on (in first few assignments), but it is also very helpful. All the TFs are knowledgable and great resources.
This course is very helpful in beginning to design a senior thesis project. It provides a useful progression toward making ideas more concrete, and facilitates connections with resources, faculty, and others who can be helpful in the process. The teaching team is very supportive in providing individualized feedback. While I still have much to refine, I feel the course provided me with a good foundation as I continue to develop my project.
Anthro 98A is an AMAZING course –– all Anthro concentrators should be excited about getting to take it. Whether you plan to write a thesis or not, you will learn so much and feel much more prepared to engage in Anthro research. Damina is utterly PHENOMENAL, and so are the TFs (special shoutout to my TF Mohit)! They clearly care about students and about Anthro research, and it is a privilege to learn from them.
