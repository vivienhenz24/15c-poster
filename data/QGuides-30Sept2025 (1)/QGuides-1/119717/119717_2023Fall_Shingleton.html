Dr. Shingleton is a fantastic teacher and this is a fantastic course! Truly such an important subject to learn about – I didn't realize how much I didn't know until taking this course. The readings and classes are interesting and engaging.
Take this class! If you have any interest in psychopathology broadly, though especially if you have an interest in EDs, this class is great. Dr. Shingleton is a fantastic professor, and really frames the class to best fit the students. The class is filled with a variety of videos, discussions, and guest speakers. The projects are all very manageable (try not to procrastinate! though even if you do you'll be ok) and they are really interesting! The class really allows you to intersect eating disorders with your other psych related interests.
I really liked this class! Dr. Shingleton is one of the most empathetic and engaging professors I've had at Harvard, and I loved having a small discussion–based class with her. I learned a lot about eating disorders that I didn't know before – I feel like there are a lot of misconceptions about EDs, but this class cleared things up! I enjoyed the structure of the class – part lecture, part discussion. I also enjoyed our assignments. They weren't too challenging, but they allowed me to convey what I had learned in class.
It was a great community!
Alright class. Pretty interesting readings. One of the more structured seminar classes I’ve been in. I was stressed about the capstone project but it wasn’t that bad . I would start early if you can to get it over with
This is an awesome class if everyone in the class prepares and comes with an open and non–judgemental way of thinking
Fantastic class! If you are at least slightly interested in eating disorders, you must take this course. Dr. Shingleton is an incredibly kind and caring instructor, and she is also an expert in the field of EDs, so you will definitely learn a lot from her. This class is especially helpful for breaking some of the taboos around EDs and understanding how these disorders can actually look like in real life. It was also great that there was a lot of flexibility around assignments (i.e., no set deadlines for the response papers), which gave me the opportunity to balance the assignments with my schedule. With that being said, make sure you are not leaving those papers to last week because then it gets really busy around the end of the semester. Overall, phenomenal course — changed my perspective & learned so much, 10/10!
