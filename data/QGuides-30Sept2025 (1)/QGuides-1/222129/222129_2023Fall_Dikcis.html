Run, do not walk, to enroll in this course. Professor Dikcis is an incredible facilitator of learning, and the material is so interesting on its own, but she makes it even more enriching and engaging. The assignments are manageable and interesting, and at no point did I feel like I had busy work. As a super senior who needed to get a divisional distribution credit, I was weary about enrolling and this class became one of my favorites by a lot!
Professor Dikcis is amazing and any class with her will be a fantastic experience. Not only does she teach really well, but she very clearly puts a lot of care and effort into making sure that her students get the most out of her courses. The kinds of assignments you will do and the feedback you will receive will definitely make you a better writer. Also, a lot of this course is applicable outside of English which is a huge bonus.
This is a great course! Professor Dikcis is a fantastic professor, she's really engaging and accessible for office hours, and the assignments are all very interesting (using AI in an academic context and thinking about how AI mimics "thinking" is really rewarding), and the range of readings are really well chosen and worth exploring. Could not recommend enough!
** This is a must–take class. I believe it's absolutely a diamond in the rough, not because it's necessarily easy, but because it's fun, and you really build a bond with your classmates over two hours. If you like socratic seminar style discussions, social justice, AI, or general futurism then this is a must. The playing around with AI that many want to do in their spare time is encouraged here and there is a lot of flexibility in assignments. Don't half–do anything, Prof Dikcis appreciates effort, passion, and creativity, and is more than willing to support her students in exploring new ideas. It was fascinating and I interact with AI and my technology in a new way. Also, I was the smartest kid at my Thanksgiving dinner because I yapped on about stuff we learned here, so there's that.
TAKE THIS CLASS. Great prof, great syllabus, actually fun and interesting assignments + really good feedback. I literally never leave course feedback but I'm doing the people a favor here. If you feel like you haven't written a paper since high school and don't want to take an English class with a bunch of people who write them all the time, don't worry because the lit kids are going to be in the same boat as you in this classthe syllabus is super unusual for Harvard's English department with a huge range of mixed media (graphic novels, movies, music videos, websites, art installations, short stories, magazine articles) and the assignments are way more accessible/unusual/open–ended for English: one close reading paper, which you rewrite with AI and examine the differences; one creative writing assignment (imagine waking up in hundred yearswhat does AI look like in this world? the best one in my opinion) and then illustrate your story with image generation AI and then examine how the AI interpreted your story; final project is either traditional research paper about AI and social justice issues or designing an AI prototype in pretty much any form (almost everyone did the second one). Professor Dikcis is so good. I loved this class, it was honestly the highlight of my semester. Even for classes I really like the readings can be such a drag sometimes but because every week there were so many different media forms, this class provided such a nice break from endless reading.
THIS WAS MY FAVORITE CLASS OF THE SEMESTER (I'm a first–year). The professor is AMAZING and kind and so knowledgable on the topics. If you are interested in the field of Science, Technology, and Society (STS) at all, this is a great introductory class to take. You learn so much information relevant to the world we live, but more importantly, where we're going. It will definitely make you more conscious than your peers when it comes to how you interact with and interpret new technologies.

The most important thing I have to say to you, prospective student: take the class, Take The Class, TAKE THE CLASS!!!
Highly recommend taking this course–––one I was super excited to go to each week! I thought the material was extremely interesting / relevant and the in–class discussions were always fun to be a part of / listen to. Professor Dikcis was so engaging and brilliant, and facilitated such great discussion. She also provided really helpful and timely feedback for each assignment in class, which I found very helpful.
