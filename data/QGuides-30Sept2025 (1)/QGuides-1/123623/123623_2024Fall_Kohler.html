You're probably taking this class because you're forced to, so I'll give some advice for future class–takers below:

1. The time commitment is no joke. If you're intent on learning the material and completing the psets diligently, you will definitely need to start early, go to office hours often, and reserve multiple hours for debugging your code. My advice is to complete the psets in the most efficient way possible: don't get stuck on one phase or test case for more than a day, use the Ed board for help, and go to office hours the week before the deadline before the TFs get inundated with students.
2. In my year, the class was taught by Eddie. With his teaching style, the psets and the tests are very different. The psets are all about implementing the system structures taught in class, while the tests focus on having a broad understanding of why systems are designed a certain way, which Eddie does well going over in lecture. However, lecture is often not enough to perform well on the tests, because he doesn't usually have time to go over all the small details. Make sure to read the posted lecture notes on the website in addition to going to lecture! The test questions also have a certain style that it will be useful to get familiar with. Make sure to do as many practice problems before each exam.
