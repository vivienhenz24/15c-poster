Comments
Take this course! Professor Olupona will be one of the best professors of your Harvard career.
This class is essentially a Nigerian grandfather telling stories about his past experiences for 3 hours. It can be enjoyable if 1. that sounds like a style you can learn from, and 2. if you are actively engaged in the discussions. If one of those aspects is missing, then at least half of each class will feel uninteresting.
this class is such a gem. there isn't any section, but the class duration is 2hrs and 45 min. but you only meet once a week and you have two assignments. showing up is 30% of your grade. i highly recommend if you need an elective for AAAS
Make sure to take a course with Professor Olupona if you can. He is one of the best (and hilarious) professors here, and this course will also open your eyes into the complex situation present in the Nigerian context. This course is not intensive due to busywork, but it is definitely a good idea to stay on top of the readings so as not to fall behind in the class discussion, though each lecture is relatively separate from the others. All in all it is a great course and if you are interested in Nigeria, whether or not you are Nigerian yourself, you should take the course.
This class wasn't the most exhilarating class I've taken so far, but its content was interesing, and besides the weekly readings, the graded portions are quite limited, allowing you to take in what's being taught without the pressure associated with grades such as other heavier courses. Professor Olupona is a storyteller and conveys the backgrounds behind each of the topics covered in class in an amusing way. If you really care about the culture, you'll love this class!
