I think it's rare to find someone who is highly fluent in both physics and philosophy. Professor Barandes is one of those people, and being able to take a class with him about the subject matter that he has such deep expertise and unique perspective on (that's not gatekept by prior experience by the way) is an incredible opportunity that I would recommend not passing up. I thought the pacing of the class could be improved, but all things considered it was such a unique learning opportunity and the psets+readings were really effective, so I consider it a win overall. Coming from a Phil concentrator, the quantum theory stuff really took me out of my depth and I sometimes felt like there was more for me to catch up on than the physics people, but Professor Barandes is quite intentional about making it accessible, and I think it's a cool opportunity to wade a little outside your comfort zone.

Any time someone asked a question, it was clear he had already thought about it while preparing and he had a perfect answer ready. He's also so enthusiastic and it's impossible to not get sucked in once the material gets cool.

If you have a chance, TAKE THIS CLASS. I can not tell you exactly what you will learn, since the course is improved upon each semester, but I can tell you that it will be fundamentally fascinating. We spent the first half of the semester essentially rederiving linear algebra, and the second rederiving all of the axioms of quantum mechanics, all from base principles with very little prior math knowledge needed. Readings included many paper from the original proposal/formulations of quantum mechanics, and section/OH helped a lot with interpretation, so that prior philosophy knowledge requirements were also minimal. Though I took the course as Phil 151, there was a fairly even distribution of people who took it as a physics course, and one could consistently rely on others who had more experience in either physics or philosophy for perspective. The class is small (30ish people), competitive to get into (because the subject and professor are both well liked by students), and takes all years/levels of experience (everything is self contained/taught within the course). This is also a wonderful class for a more logical/formulaic philosophy experience and a more questions/interpretation–based experience physics. I would definitely recommend attending section/OH if possible, the material is fascinating and teaching staff are very willing to discuss related topics. The course generally follows the professors own formulation of quantum mechanics, has no exams (only a final essay), and has only one pset a week (with a reading section and a very straightforward math section).

Overall, I had a wonderful time in this class and would absolutely choose to take it again. I seriously can not overstate how interesting and welcoming this class is!

Great class! Jacob is a truly gifted lecturer and is obviously extraordinarily intelligent. This was not a major issue by any means but thought it was worth noting.
