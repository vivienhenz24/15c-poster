Take this class for Ed the TF. He’s amazing and so caring / knowledgable. Not a great seminar if you aren’t interested in health ec policy
Would absolutely recommend (especially if Ed is a TF again!). If you have any sort of interest in industrial organization or health policy, both Professor Pakes and Ed are incredibly knowledgable on the topic and are great in making sure you learn a lot about healthcare economics as well. The class is presentation heavy (a student presents on one of the papers that was assigned for that week), and then you write a paper on what you read for your presentation – these don't have to be super high–stress though, as Ed will guide you through your presentations and papers if you ask. Both Professor Pakes and Ed are amazing, the support you get from Ed throughout the semester is unmatchable :).
great professor and especially TF, Ed. if you want to learn more about health economics, this is the class for you. minimal assignments – only 2 prezos and 2 essays, but if you really engage with the material, you will learn a lot. take it !
Ariel and Ed are both incredibly knowledgeable about healthcare economics. I have learnt so much about the healthcare system from an economic perspective. The class covers diverse topics ranging from how the way in which physicians are paid affects patient outcomes to the impacts of direct–to–consumer pharmaceutical advertising.
I think you'll engage most with this class if a) you're interested in healthcare markets & policy; and b) you're comfortable with statistics/math/econometrics so you can really grasp and critique the empirical methods used in the assigned readings. The class itself is pretty low stress and no tedious assignments beyond two presentations and critical papers with a lot of flexibility on how you space them out through the semester. Both Professor Pakes and Ed are phenomenal – they have so much knowledge to share if you tap into the support they're willing to provide.
