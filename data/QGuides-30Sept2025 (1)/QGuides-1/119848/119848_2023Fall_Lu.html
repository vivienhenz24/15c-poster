This was a great intro into a lot of methods used for high dimensional data. Junwei's notes and lectures were informative and clearly presented. Overall, I think I was able to learn a lot
This maybe a course that is better to take once you have a dissertation idea.
This class ia a great coverage of a diversity of topics relevant in modern statistics (concentration inequalities, penalized/sparse regression, optimization,). It is not a "Methods 2" for Biostatistics PhD students per se, but covers topics both "recently established" (~90's–2010's) and more modern/cutting edge statistical/machine learning applications. A very useful overview if you are all interested in a breadth of topics, such as high–dimensional statistics, more learning/ML focused work, graphical modelling, Also very useful for any students with less of a computer science/optimization background for its review of optimization methods, both in implementation and in proofs of convergence for convex optimization.
Primary focus of the course is LASSO and its variations
This is a very good class if you're looking to get in contact with relatively advanced material in modern high–dimensional statistics and statistical learning.
