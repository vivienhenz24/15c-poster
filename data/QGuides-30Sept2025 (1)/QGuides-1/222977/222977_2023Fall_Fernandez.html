Take this course! Dr. Fernandez is truly such an amazing lecturer and really values the opinions/personal experiences of students. His thoughtful leading of discussions really crafted an environment that encouraged Asian American students to share their stories, which really enriched the course. This course genuinely changed the way I view Asian American appearances in pop culture, and I absolutely loved going to class every week!!
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! It is absolutely wonderful and was the highlight of my week. Professor Fernandez is so kind and really cares about the subject and you as a student. Assignments are very managable and the readings are quite interesting and enjoyable. You can make your final project about anything you want, really, so there is tons of room for academic exploration. Seriously there is no reason NOT to take this clas.
EMR 159 is a really good introduction to Asian American media studies, and I am really happy I took this course because 1) it's very low key and 2) it's very interesting if you like the intersection of Asian Americana and movies/music/literature/etc. Leslie is a fantastic instructor and genuinely cares about their students. They were also really flexible, which was a godsend because I was taking way too many courses. I learned a lot about my own ethnic background in relation to the Western world, and I feel more confident in my ability to both analyze and create media that tackles really difficult issues that are fundamental to the Asian American/immigrant experience.

Best senior fall course and honestly best course at Harvard ever.
THIS CLASS IS GREAT! Harvard doesn't have an Ethnic Studies department, but I've been on a mission to take interesting ethnic studies courses throughout my time here; EMR 159 certainly did not disappoint. Dr. Fernandez is engaging, generates so much enthusiasm for the subject matter, and is SO intelligent about Asian American studies, specifically Techno–orientalism, which is the main focus of their research. I loved how the class covered different medium, including film, television, food documentaries, music, contemporary media like YouTube, and literature. I liked that Dr. Fernandez paired a piece of "pop culture" with an academic text every class –– kept a good balance. I learned so much, and I'm looking forward to taking more of Dr. Fernandez's classes going forward.
Take this class! You will learn so much about the breadth of Asian American topics in pop culture today. Professor is super helpful and open to talk to students outside of class. You will talk about important Asian American pop culture concepts with a critical and theoretical lens. This is definitely a gem.
