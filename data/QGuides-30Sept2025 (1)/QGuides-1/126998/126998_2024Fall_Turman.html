What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I thought this class was excellent both thematically and with respect to how much French you actually learn. Prof. Turman is a fair grader and a very flexible teacher – we always go over homework in class before turning it in so as long as you've done it on time you won't be marked harshly. The writing projects can be a bit tough (I felt like there was a quick turnaround from one project to the next, though looking back this improved my writing dramatically over the course of a semester) but I appreciated that we always got feedback on drafts before turning in the final, and Prof. Turman was always available during office hours if I had questions.
Make sure you take the time to prepare each week before class so you can really speak confidently on the subject matter,
This is my fifth course in French at Harvard and is one of my favorites compared to previous ones. It's important to consider the amount of work before committing to it. You can do well in the course with a solid foundation of conjugations and basic grammar. Even though you would not encounter assignments extremely complicated, they tend to be too many, and it is easy to get behind if you do not do them on time. Take the course if you like languages, want a citation, or simply because you are interested in it, but keep in mind that there will be multiple small assignments that should not be underestimated just because they do not seem too complicated. For example, homework for a week might be five pages of exercises (reading and grammar), a recording of a short video (3 minutes), and an in–class writing composition of 400 words.

I took the course because I am doing a citation in French but I enjoyed it! It is also a good way to balance your schedule because all the assignments are done by the last day of classes so you don't have to worry about this class during reading period. You will be done with it!
Definitely take this class! Try to be in Dr. Turman's section if you can, she's brilliant! But keep in mind that it's like double the workload of Fr40, you will always have an assignment to due – though there's 4 essays/exam/projects throughout the semester so it isn't a very stressful midterm/final class. Also the small classes means there's a lot of participation, so be prepared to be speaking a lot!
