Some background in architecture, general architects and a passion for the history of art and architecture is honestly what will keep you driven during this course!
Great introduction to the subject of art and architectural history! It doesn't do the best job in easing you in in the beginning but you'll get there quickly if you do the readings and review them regularly. The lectures can be a bit tedious and the slides confusing to look back on as they have no context, but professor hamburger is very knowledgeable and sections are great (Time the tf is very friendly and enthusiastic)!
Absolutely incredible class! Great lectures, super informative and interesting. Professor Hamburger is legendary, and it's very easy to see why. His lectures never get boring or repetitive, and he is able to teach a beginner–friendly class like this one without making it uninteresting for those with more background knowledge. I don't know whether Tim will be TFing this course again, but you should hope that he is. He's super kind and makes every section fun and informative. He's always happy to discuss course material and catch you up if you miss anything. The only thing is you should make sure to attend lecture or find a classmate to send you their notes if you can't, because there is very little information available on Canvas about lecture content other than the slides, which are all images.
TAKE THIS CLASS!! Do it! It's not an insane amount of work– just make sure to stay on top of your readings, it honestly is a gem. Even if you fall behind...like I may have, it is really easy to catch up. All you need to do is take notes with the weekly reading questions in mind and bam you're guaranteed to ace the midterm exam and have a really solid foundation to write your paper. Professor Hamburger is also always available to answer any and all questions you may have. Overall the course really gives you a well fleshed out view of the architectural history of Notre Dame, but also of Paris. You come out seeing things in a new light and its really fun to have all these new insights that you don't have to put an insane amount of effort to learn. Even just showing up to lecture teaches you a lot.
Enrolled in this course curious about Notre–Dame and its history, came out with a newfound interest in 19th–century art and criticism. Professor Hamburgers exhaustive knowledge of Gothic architecture and its afterlives makes this such an exciting courseyou cant learn this anywhere else. Would absolutely recommend. Id never taken art history before and found this interesting but also accessible.
Despite being called "Notre–Dame," you only spend maybe half the class talking about Gothic architecture (and even less about the cathedral itself), and the rest about Gothic reception and then just 19th century Parisian history (the class takes a weird turn). Grading is fair/easy, so it's friendly to people who aren't from HAA.
Take this class! It is beginner friendly for those who have never encountered HAA before. Prepare to do a decent bit of reading and thinking but you really get out of the course what you put in. The sections are super cool with direct engagement with certain objects in the museum. The paper is light and the exams are essay based.
This class is really cool and engaging, with a dense amount of content that you get each lecture (but do not need nearly all of it for exams). Prof. Hamburger is a knowledgeable, opinionated(!!!), dense, if rambling, lecturer, and I got a lot from every class. Lecture for this class was by far my favorite hour of my Tuesdays and Thursdays!!!!! Our TF Tim also made this course great with helping us unpack everything that Prof. Hamburger would discuss. Midterm was fine. I thought I did worse than I did; you truly just need some good examples from lectures and readings to back up your points on short answer questions. Our final paper was fun. Overall, great course whether you are an art history person or not, but do not expect to grasp everything that you hear.
This class is awesome and the professor is great. TAKE IT. TAKE IT. TAKE IT.
