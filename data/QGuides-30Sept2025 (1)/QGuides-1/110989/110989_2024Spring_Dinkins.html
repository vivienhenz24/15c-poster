Be prepared to spend 3–4 hrs consistently on the 3 psets per week study at least 1 week before midterm and finals, always ask questions right after class if confused about the topic. Multiple Choice 20 questions for each exam – are very logic focused. The course all ties together and it teaches a different kind of math unlike what you have done before with calculus. It's very logic and reason focused and you need to think about concepts in a new way
A complete time drain. This is course is not well structured. The office hours are always full, and the problem sets feel like busy–work at lot of the time.
It felt like a lot of work compared to the difficulty of the content – section is taught well, most of your learning comes from the worksheets which are a little disorganized. It's just a lot of content, especially in studying for the final, as the last couple of chapters/worksheets felt irrelevant to what we had learned prior and were just memorization
21b was a great experience. The content is slightly more challenging than 21a, so I strongly recommend taking that first to both learn its content and adapt to the 3 PSETs a week routine. Assignments are quite fair and there are a plethora of available resources (MCQ, Course Videos, Office Hours) to succeed. Make time for this course, and you will enjoy it!
This class is not a joke. If you want to take this course because "linear algebra is useful to learn" (which is true), be prepared for A LOT of long hours doing problem sets, at MQC, or office hours. The midterms and final are not easy either. The hard part about Linear Algebra is there isn't really one way they can quiz your knowledge. They have true–false questions at the beginning of every exam that can absolutely destroy your test score but aren't super intuitive and are easy to mess up. Problem sets take a long time so do them with people in office hours or MQC OR find people who have taken a linear algebra course before. The section leaders are super caring and will try their best but this subject is just a lot of material. Good luck future soldiers.
The best thing you can do for yourself in this class is to pay a ton of attention in section, and then actually do the psets by yourself or in a small group (not just copying your friend who took it last semester). The class builds very heavily on the stuff you do on the psets, and like you're probably used to in Harvard math at this point, you often have to discover things yourself on the psets. This isn't too bad if you're able to pick up on the subtleties of what you've learned, but if you throw that aside and don't understand certain little things, you'll feel completely lost, and think it's impossible. I would say do whatever you can to become passionate about this stuff. Really engage with it from day one, and get to know everyone in your section, including the TF, CA's, and other students. If you get off to a bad start with this class, it's going to be really hard to break out of that later on, since everything builds ontop of itself. I will say the class is hard to get a high grade in. The test questions are often based on little things you have to pick up on, and if you happen to miss it for some reason, you lose a pretty large proportion of the test then and there. It's hard to succeed on the exams unless you're clued into every little thing. Treat the psets like the place for you to prove yourself, and I think the tests will follow suit.
The PSets are longer and more challenging than those in 21a, but the material was more interesting and interconnected in my opinion. Use MQC to check PSet work because that can offset harshly graded exams. Definitely attend quiz sessions and review the quizzes before the exams for extra practice.
This class is fantastic and I would highly recommend it. There are three psets a week, but as long as you stay on top of it, it's easy enough to manage. MQC will be your best friend, so definitely go there for help.
Math 21b is like most other intro math classes at Harvard – 3 psets a week. Some math 21b psets though are extremely long, some are twice as long as others. This class will really suck your time during the week and be aware of 10–12 hour workload required for this class. It feels like you are constantly doing math with 3 classes a week and then 3 hours psets 3 times a week.

I will say some of the practical applications of linear algebra are cool. Erica is a really great preceptor and section leader, kind and very knowledgeable. Exams are fair with Matt Demers as course head. I heard previous scary statements about exam difficulty but the exams this semester were very fair and Psets prepared you well.
