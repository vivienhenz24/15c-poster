Super fun class! You definitely need to do the readings if you want to be prepared for the papers, but they are really interesting/fun and typically not long. If you are even remotely interested in Irish folklore you should totally take the class!

This is such a great course! I came in wanted to learn more about my Irish heritage and feel as though I gained so much insight into the culture and history of Ireland. Graham and Professor Sumner are really engaged in the material which made the course extra enjoyable.

Do–able workload, really great class if you're used to writing papers. Lectures help summarize stories overall, section didn't always feel necessary. I had a folklore background but would have found this course helpful if I didn't because it gives a good overview of folklore that I would have found helpful.
The readings for this course were really fun and a very easy addition to other semester workloads. I was surprised at how the grading went for papers. It seemed like the grading was more serious than anything else in the course? I was surprised my the reasons for the grade I received occasionally (more than I was surprised by the grade itself). Compared to another course in the department I had taken before, this course was more difficult to achieve an A in. If you are genuinely interested in reading Celtic folklore, it's not a bad addition to your semester. However, I would be careful if you're not used to writing humanities papers/would only be okay receiving an A in this class.
You should absolutely take this course!! The teaching staff is fantastic and very supportive, and the content is super interesting!!
