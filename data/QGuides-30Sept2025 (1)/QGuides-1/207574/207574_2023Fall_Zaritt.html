What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
TAKE THIS CLASS! You will come away learning a lot more than you could have ever expected! Saul is also a fantastic professor and such an engaging lecturer!
This is an excellent class! The workload is manageable, and I found the discussions to be super interesting and engaging. Professor Zaritt is amazing! He is super thoughtful about the class, and even brought in a feast on the last day. I looked forward to this class so much every week, and found the assigned readings genuinely fascinating. The oral history project was also a super cool chance to interview an expert in Yiddish culture/studies, which no other Harvard class has ever facilitated for me. If you didn't do the readings, you would probably find this class super chill, but I'd recommend working a bit harder because you will be rewarded.
TAKE THIS CLASS! It will challenge many of your pre–conceived notions about what Yiddish is, and all with a very relaxed workload and supportive seminar environment. Prof. Zaritt is phenomenal, and the oral history project was unlike anything I'd encountered in any class before.
Definitely take this class, it is mainly discussion based and you learn so much about not only Yiddish, but culture in general. It is not stressful at all and it is very fun.
This is a great class. It's very much about Yiddish and Jewishness, but it's also about broader themes: nostalgia, nationalisms, queerness, humor, translation, and race in America. Professor Zaritt is a committed teacher, much more interested in facilitating successful learning experiences than he is in arbitrary deadlines or grades, and I hope to take another course with him in the future. I'm very happy this class exists at Harvard, and I'd highly recommend it.
TAKE THIS COURSE. I went into it with no background in Yiddish, and it was incredibly interesting the professor does a great job at choosing the readings and topics for each week, and I felt that I learned about Yiddish equally from academic, historical, literary, and pop–culture lenses. The seminar environment was very welcoming and inclusive, and had a wide range of students (many of whom do not know Yiddish). Take it!
