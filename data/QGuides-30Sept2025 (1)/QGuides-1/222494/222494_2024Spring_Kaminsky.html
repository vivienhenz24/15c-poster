This course is amazing – we worked with a variety of cool collections in the Schlesinger archives in a low–stakes format and had very rewarding assignments like revising a finding aid to account for ignored queer voices and our own final creative project. The readings that Professor Kaminsky assigned were diverse and very helpful for even beyond this class. She also did an great job of being flexible and accommodating to her students' needs and navigated difficult conversations and situations very well.

This is an incredible class! You will learn a ton about archives and queer history and bond with your classmates! Lauren and Jenny are great instructors and I am so glad they are teaching this class again.
If you want to engage deeply with questions of what it means to identify, study, and archive "queerness," this class cannot be recommended enough. Never have I felt so deeply engaged with questions of how we navigate between the personal and the intellectual within academic spaces, and the work I did in this course was some of the most rewarding I've done my entire time at Harvard. I truly cannot recommend this course with enough enthusiasm, excitement, and passion to truly explain how much this course meant to me while completing it.
