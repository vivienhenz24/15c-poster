I'm gonna be honest, I expected this to be my favorite class this semester and it ended up being my least favorite. It wasn't that bad – the work was manageable and Mariano is very nice – but class discussions honestly sucked and our analysis did not really seem to pry at the true substance of the readings. If I could do it over again, I definitely would've taken a different seminar. This class did introduce me to Borges though and I love it! Mariano is obviously super smart (maybe a bit too smart) but sometimes rambles on and his analysis just feels like a reach sometimes. So often it felt like we were arguing over trivialities as opposed to the actual main ideas of a text.
This is a very interesting and chill first year seminar! Great to balance courses with a heavier load (think of it as a two–hour break per week to just sit and talk about books).
This was one of my favorite classes. Professor Siskind is great: he is passionate, understanding, and friendly. It usually felt more like a book club than a class. You definitely do not need to be Latin American to take the class–– you just need a genuine interest in the material. Listen to the advice on the website: investigate how you are going to get the books sooner rather than later, because some of them are difficult to find.
TAKE THIS CLASS! It was a lot of fun and the conversations we had about the books were always interesting. If you take the time to read the books you can gain a lot from this class. Mariano is amazing, very smart, and he is always eager to hear what his students have to say. You could not ask for a better professor.
