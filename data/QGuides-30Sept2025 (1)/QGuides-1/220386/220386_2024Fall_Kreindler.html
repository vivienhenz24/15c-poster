Take this class!!! This is my favorite economics course I have taken at Harvard by far. The course workload can get a bit heavy around the weeks when problem sets are due (4 in the entire semester) but if you plan ahead the workload is super reasonable. The course topic itself is also super interesting, and Gabriel and Kartik do such a wonderful job of teaching the material in a way that is fun and easy to understand. The midterm and final are also very reasonable there's a lot of support from the teaching team leading up to the exams which is super helpful.
Don't be afraid of the theory, Prof Kreindler approaches it really accessibly! I concur with previous reviews that this was a good elective for completing the theory req
This is a great course overall – I loved Professor Kreindler and Kartik. They were both very engaged with the course material and are very nice people. All of the PSETs really did contribute to your understanding of the course material and this is one of those few classes where I felt like doing the PSETs actually helped to internalize some of the material. Professor Kreindler does a lot to make this class a great experience for the students, from giving helpful in–person feedback with our final papers in 30–minute sessions, to helping me out with my thesis, to organizing guest lectures for us. I also just learned an incredible wealth of information about immigration and its ties to Economics with all of the course material being very interesting. I'd highly recommend this class for any economics concentrator or anyone just interested in immigration in general.
Pretty easy and interesting elective if you like questions around migration, urban economics and development economics. The examinable material is a lot easier than the content and papers covered in lectures, which can make the lectures feel unengaging at times. Prof. Kreindler is very nice and Kartik was a great TA for the course.
TAKE THIS CLASS!! Gabriel and Kartik are the absolute best and make learning about migration super accessible and engaging for students. The course is structured very well, with sections reinforcing the concepts we cover in lecture, and psets reinforcing both lecture and section by reviewing in depth the models we cover. Gabriel is so kind and approachable, which goes a long way in making students feel like they belong in the class. Kartik is a genius and is so good at reviewing the content. I don't get how he words everything so well in real–time, but he does, and it's honestly amazing. The way he explains complex models and breaks them down step by step makes everything so much clearer. Both Gabriel and Kartik are incredibly supportive, and they create such a welcoming environment where everyone feels comfortable asking questions and engaging with the material. This has definitely been my favorite economics class so far, and I can't recommend it enough!!
