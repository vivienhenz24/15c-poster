Dr. Becker is an amazing professor with so much life and career experience! Her stories from Fiji and working with Dr. Paul Farmer are fascinating. It was truly a pleasure and honor learning from such an accomplished person. Beyond her accomplishments, she is a genuinely sweet and supportive person.

I initially went into this class worried that the scope would be too narrow: weight ideals in Fiji. But this course does a great job of incorporating various topics, so it is good for anyone who is interested in medicine, public health, and also government, policy, and more. I really enjoyed this class!
I would highly recommend this class. Professor Becker is very genuine and makes a low–stakes, stress–free environment during class. This is definitely not a class where you learn technical, scientific things: rather, you'll learn science in a context of social norms and society. The focus of this seminar is definitely more on the latter: body image, social media use, and how different societies vary in terms of eating culture are main topics. Overall, I'd highly recommend because this seminar was so unique for me. I definitely hadn't ever taken a course like it before.
THIS COURSE IS AMAZING, BEST SEMINAR EVER!!!! Prof. Becker is genuinely one of the most passionate and interesting profs at this college. If. you are in any capacity interested in public health/obesity medicine/eating disorders, take this course, you will not regret it. There is a lot of reading but don't let it deter you–it is interesting and you will find yourself looking forward to completing it. Also it is not 100% necessary for course discussions and she understands if students have heavier weeks and are not able to read before hand. This is literally the best FYS out there, trust me TAKE IT!!!!!
professor is SUPER knowledgable and so nice. topic is interesting but sometimes student presentations can be boring. good for a heavy schedule!!
Take this class! Very eye opening to weight as a public health issue. Discusses concepts like socioeconomic status impacting healthcare as well as weight as well as many other factors. There is lots of room for discussion in this course. The readings were also engaging and interesting. Prof. Becker is extremely helpful and knowledgeable about the topic, and her insights always added to the discussion. The final paper is a good chance to explore anything within the topic that interests you and makes for a great final project. Definitely recommend to anyone even mildly interested in the topic, you will see it is more expansive than you think!
This is an amazing seminar and taught me so much about how body standards intersect with race, culture, wealth, and economics. Would definitely recommend!
