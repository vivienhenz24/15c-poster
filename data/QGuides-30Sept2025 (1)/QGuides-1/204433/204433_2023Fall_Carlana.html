professor carlana is great, but grading is a little wonky – you get what you put into this class, go to lecture and participate and you will learn a lot. definitely helps to have some econometrics knowledge, though
Overall, I think this class was an excellent way to build on from my Econometric skills that I had learned in class (ECON 1123). I would highly recommend this course for anyone who is interested in Economics research as it relates to the Education field. I thought that the grading, overall, was very fair and it was curved to accommodate for holistic performance in the class. The professor is incredibly knowledgable in the area, and you will certainly become a much better researcher once coming out of this class.
Great course to learn about education policy in a very quantitative lens.
This is a cool class! I was nervous coming in as an undergrad when it's mostly grad students and I didn't have a super solid background in econometrics (just AP stats really), but Dr. Carlana explains everything very clearly and as long as you're comfortable with linear regressions the background part shouldn't be too much of an issue. I took this class because I was thinking about an ed secondary, though the class focuses much less on substantive education issues than it does on (more broadly) how education/econometric research works and how to interpret it. Which is also a cool skill to have!

The workload is not too bad – there are 3 psets in the first half (not too hard), then a midterm (hard, but not graded too harshly), then a research memo (2 pages summarizing 6 papers but you do it with a group), and a final project which is a decent amount of work. The material can feel a bit dry at times, I think it depends a lot on how much you are interested both in the statistics/econometrics and the education aspect of the course.
