DO NOT TAKE THIS COURSE (unless you are super interested in real–world material design and willing to put up with by far the worst course structure I have ever experienced)

The lecture part of the class focuses on real–world approaches to materials which can be useful if you're a mechanical engineer. But, the problem sets focused on math and material science that was not explained in class at all. So, you had to go to office hours where you and the TFs would try to figure out the problem and come up with a half–answer (the TFs were amazing, they just didn't have an answer sheet). It was just overall incredible annoying.

And the professor shuts down any answer that isn't exactly what he wants. And he laughs at you for asking about practice exams, what percentage the exam will be worth, or any basic thing which is the bare minimum for a professor to share with the class.
Be prepared for things to change in this class, its entirely independent with very little written guidance for the exam and project but you learn alot and its cool how much freedom you have for the project because you can investigate literally anything.
This is a fun class. Professor David Clarke has decades of experience and is extremely passionate about material science & engineering. The lecturers were never boring and explored a large array of different materials and their applications across industries. I feel like I learned a lot, but was never too stressed out about this class.
For some reason, the professor gave us an in–person midterm for the FIRST time in the history of this class. It seemed highly unnecessary, but after locking in to study it did help for me to actually internalize the material. A fair class, make sure to go to lecture 90% of the time so he knows that you're not taking the class too unseriously.
This class covers interesting material, but can be frustrating at times. Psets were often not similar to content taught in class and required skills in programs like COMSUL that are not taught during the course. I think the class is quite doable though, and will hopefully be structured differently in the future.
Prepare and organize early for this class in order to not struggle later on. Pick your group mates wisely as it could be easier to work by yourself if you have group mates that don't communicate or participate. I had a great group but it is something I saw other classmates struggling with. I really enjoyed the course content.
It's a fun class and I liked it but read the textbook to help you grasp more of the content! Loved working on the project part of this course!
Don't take this class! Professor Clark's grading and attitude towards this course is extremely subjective. He adds assignments and midterms when he feels like it simply because he can. The lectures were not engaging and none of the variables or equations were explained. If you asked a question the response you were met with was basically to read the textbook but the "reading assignments" were hardly talked about. It was hard to know what we were supposed to be doing for the course. Understanding the content is a lot easier when you use it for the final project, otherwise at times it feels unnecessary. The class will be much better with a lab component so if there is a new professor and a lab component then you might consider taking this course.
I would strongly advise other students NOT to take this course. It is not worth the damage to your mental health. Unless the course it taught by a different professor, I would not recommend that they take this class.
