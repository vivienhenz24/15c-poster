The lectures for this course are not very technical, but they are genuinely interesting and fun. The problem sets are infrequent, but be prepared to reference the textbook for the problem sets and midterm as there will be some calculations and concepts that are more challenging, especially without experience from courses like ES120 or ES128. The course capstone project is a great opportunity to do something that you care about and if you budget your time well throughout the semester, you will easily find the time to do everything needed for the scope of the project. Overall, I would highly recommend the class as it is fun and not a lot of work.

The lectures for this class are not designed with the aim of teaching you anything meaningful. I strongly believe this could be a great class – the content is not absurdly difficult at all, but it is not taught in an approachable way. If you are thinking about taking this class, it's probably because you heard it is a low hour requirement engineering elective to fulfill the requirement. This is true, however I would seriously ask yourself if you would rather spend 4 hours a week scratching your head slowly cobbling together acceptable pset answers every week, or spend 6–8 hours a week engaging with another class's course material, and leaving with any new knowledge. That being said, I don't know how much better the engineering department really gets.

On one hand, I feel a little bad talking so poorly of this class, because honestly I put very little effort into it as the semester went on. Pretty soon, I stopped showing up to class entirely, and didn't really feel like I could levy much criticism after that point. Then I would show up to see what it's like, and it would remind me why I stopped going in the first place. I don't understand why anyone thinks that the best way to teach a class which ostensibly wishes to teach students how to interact and engage with various formulas, ideas, etc, is in this fashion. Ideas are briefly thrown up on a slide deck before being skipped over for ramblings about stuff which never show up in the assignments. I can't seriously imagine that anyone believes this is the best way to impart a group of students with any knowledge and/or skills. Clearly they had something else in mind, which they might think is good! I think it is a shame.
This course is probably the easiest in the ES department. Several times, the TFs had to step in and clarify, correct, and rework the professor's homework problems. This course should also be taken after ES 190. So if you already took that course and want to learn more about materials, than ES192 may be worthwhile. Otherwise, I would consider ES190 before this class.
