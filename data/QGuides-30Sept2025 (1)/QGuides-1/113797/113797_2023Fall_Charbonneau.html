As a humanities concentrator with absolutely zero experience / skills in STEM this was very approachable and the professor / teaching staff are so enthusiastic about the subject and genuine about their interest! The labs are also really cool because you literally get to use the telescope to see planets and stars and supernovae that I feel like you only read about. One of my favorite classes and really helps situate you in our expansive universe in a powerful way.
If you are interested in space and astronomy, it is a very interesting class. The professor does a good job of talking about things enthusiastically and will welcome discussion of random things you see on the news or any questions you might have. The TFs are very knowledgeable and are great too. The homework assignments are rather hard. They start out very easy, and over time progress to very difficult assignments that are only somewhat related to the course material. That being said, the TFs are happy to help and they do a great job of making it doable. The chapter readings are definitely necessary for exams. If you don't do the readings, you probably won't do too well. If you take this class for a letter grade, I would say that it is rather difficult and stressful. If you take it pass fail I imagine it's a lot more fun as you can relax and learn all the cool stuff without worrying about acing the homework.
this was an amazing course! prof charbonneau is passionate and great at lecturing, sections are super fun because we use the clay telescope, and the content is just extremely fascinating!!
If you are interested in space you should absolutely take this class. No background is necessary –– I am not a STEM concentrator and was still able to track and understand everything going on. Lectures were fun and engaging and the textbook made everything digestible. Weekly homeworks take some time, but if you go to office hours you should be fine.
Take this class!! It is such an interesting way to fulfill your science distribution requirement (coming from an upperclassman Social Science Concentrator). Professor Charbonneau is the best. Lectures are engaging, entertaining, and informative. The labs are so fun – dress warmly for stargazing on the roof of the science center!
The lectures are amazing! Professor Charbonneau does a great job in keeping the class engaging and has the most amazing demonstrations that really help in understanding the conceptual part of the class. He also talks about the women and other figures who advanced the field of astronomy, which was fascinating especially as many were part of Harvard. The psets can be challenging but if you attend the weekly homework sessions offered the sets will be much more manageable. The readings are quite easy to understand and not too long.
I would tell them – no questions asked – take this course. This is the most interesting and fun class that I have ever had the privilege of taking at Harvard. Every lecture is packed with enthusiasm and demos, and there wasn't a day that I hadn't enjoyed my time in this class. The homework assignments are rather thought–provoking, and you are best served going through them with a friend. They too are rather fun, but they can be a bit math–heavy (as one should expect). The math is not terribly difficult (no calculus required, physics knowledge is helpful), but I would recommend taking notes and jotting down the formulas presented in lecture. You will be seeing them again.
Above all else, enjoy your time with Professor Charbonneau. He is a radiant and infinitely–insightful professor, and he is genuinely fascinated by the material. Ask him any and all questions you may have, and listen as he guides you to a new understanding of a topic you hadn't previously considered. Never before have I encountered a professor that can spend 75 minutes highlighting how miniscule we are in the grand scheme of things, and still make us feel so important.
Take this class.
