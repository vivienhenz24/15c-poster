take this course if you're interested in it! I was reluctant to take it as if didn't fulfill any requirements for me at the time, but it has been such a treasured elective and I look forward to it every day. It is such a treat to get such a comprehensive review of three art forms, and I feel I have so many new references to carry me through the world of art, literature, and architecture.
This class is a gem! It is a very quick introduction to German modernism, and the pace is rather fast as it moves quickly across architects and their works (I think there were over 200 works we needed to know for the midterm). However, I never felt too overwhelmed as the pace was great, lectures were engaging enough to remember a lot about the artworks just by attending lecture, and section brought lively discussions that helped internalize the content even more. The assignments are not overwhelming, but they are engaging and helpful. I would strongly recommend this class if you want to learn more about architecture, modern art, or modern literature.
Phenomenal course! If you're vaguely interested in art and/or literature, this is a treat. I can't think of many courses where the assignments include looking at art and almost half of the section meetings are trips to the Art Museums.

I knew very little about German modernist art and literature going in and it was a very fun way to explore it – and learn how to analyze, interpret, and critique art in general.

In short: very fun, very engaging, not a ton of work outside of class.
This course is a rapid–fire introduction to the modernist period, which is a fascinating time of upheaval and questioning. Prof. Burgard and Therese led our small group thoughtfully, and it is, as in all courses, genuinely a lot of fun if you sincerely engage with the topics. It is not particularly rigorous, but you get exposure to a lot of different areas.
You will learn a lot about how to read art and literature, and be challenged to defend your interpretations. These skills are very useful!
