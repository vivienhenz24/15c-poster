This class was a great experience, the discussions and materials were all really inspiring. It is perhaps my favorite course I've taken at Harvard, I couldn't recommend it enough!
TAKE THIS COURSE!!! If you are reading this, you might be deciding between some other courses related to Soviet or post–Soviet culture, mostly focusing on Russian thought. But this course is your chance to learn more about works that survived heavy censorship and are in the process of being rediscovered (I mean, your final project might turn out to be a great contribution to restoring one of such works). Professor Tokarskyi is one of the most approachable professors I have ever had!! If not for his office hours, I don't know how I would survive the course (I'm a STEM concentrator), and here I am considering a secondary in Slavic Studies.
Prof. Tokarskyi is one of the best professors I have every had. He is incredibly knowledgeable and accommodating to the needs and interests of the class, and this course is an excellent opportunity to do a deep dive into the dialogue between Ukrainian literature and global literature between the late 1800s and the 1970s.
This is a fantastic class. TAKE IT!!! Bohdan is such a profoundly intelligent instructor that is so knowledgeable, kind, and warm.
