This class teaches you a variety of math models and ideas and presents opportunities to apply this in throughout the projects.
Take this course! Great way to satisfy your honors requirement, and Prof. Kuang is so amazing: he gives reassuring Dad in the best way possible! Do note that there are quite a few assignments – more so than I had anticipated, but they're all easily done with a good group (would recommend taking the class with at least 2–3 other people you know you'd work well together) and some of them are optional / count for extra credit. There are 3 major projects, two of which are group–based (with the same group) and the last is individual. In the moment, they were a little stressful, but it's actually really fun to create these modeling projects and take ownership over a small research study that you apply the concepts learned in class to. I definitely spent a reasonable amount of time on this class each week (around 7 hours, more or less depending on the week), especially for an APMTH class.
This is a great class if you put in the work to understand each model! The course goes through many (many) models and how they are applicable in real life. Professor Kuang and the TFs are all amazing and very willing to help. Most of the work and assignments are done in groups, so make sure there are people in the class that you work well with and are responsible – this will save you as things pile up. With that being said, you do need to put in some time after class to really understand what the code and/or equations are saying. Overall I would recommend taking if you want to apply what you've been learning so far!
This is a great course to take for senior year, because it is a chance to apply what is covered and learned in other applied math courses like ODEs (AM 105/108) and optimization (AM 121). There is also a lot of freedom in this course to explore personal topics of interest!
This class is a great way to combine all of your past AM course work. You will also review/ learn AM concepts from all sorts of topics, which I find really interesting. Super important is to enter the class with 3–4 people to do the two group projects with. There is also an individual final project. Professor Kuang is amazing and so kind. Definitely worth taking!
Not a bad AM course! I enjoyed the topics covered and was never overwhelmingly confused.
AM115 is a pretty monotone class, you essentially learn how to derive certain equations that can then be applicable to some sort of mathematical model used in real life. Most assignments are extensions of an example used in class and the group projects give you the freedom to build on or use a similar model for your own topic of choice. I recommend taking the class with a reliable friend because whoever is in your group can make or break how stressful the group projects are,
This is great class. I enjoyed it and had fun working on my final project. If you are going to take this class, take it with people you know and people who you know you will work well with on group projects. There are a lot of group assignments in this class, and if your group is pretty bad then you're not going to have a good time. A good group will make this class a breeze. The staff were amazing though and do a great job of making the material interesting by applying it to all sorts of problems in the real world.
Professor Kuang and Sarah really made this version of AM115 amazing. Definitely make sure you come in with group members in mind because most of the class is just group projects. But compared to past iterations of this course, I found Kuang's version to be at a very steady pace, with fair grading and understandable teaching.
The course is interesting and fairly chill for the honors AM requirement. Lectures are not super engaging, as Zhimming likes to get very deep into niche topics. I didn't really walk away learning anything dramatically new, more just an extension of how to use models learned in previous AM classes. Projects are chill but psets are graded pretty harshly.
