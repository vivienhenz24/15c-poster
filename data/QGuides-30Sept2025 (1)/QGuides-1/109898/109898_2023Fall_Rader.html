This class is a lot of work. After each lecture, you have a quiz, and sometimes during class you will also have lab–like exercises.

Psets are sometimes weekly, sometimes every two weeks. They will take you a lot of time. They are partner psets, but the questions will feel redundant and will be worded without a lot of clarity on what the teaching staff wants from you. They will ask you to interpret graphs and then will not tell you what graph or what you really should be interpreting. They will ask you to choose your own value of depth and then the autograder will only accept a depth of 20, so you will run until you find that max_depth=20. Your psets will be graded slowly and you will only receive those grades until AFTER the next one is due, so no, you won't be able to fix any mistakes from last week.

They will give you a midterm where the teaching staff will be solicited to generate questions, and the questions will be worded imprecisely (what exactly is a "worthwhile predictor" and is that language precise?) I'm not convinced that it is.

The teaching staff will not honor the concept of a Thanksgiving break. You will have a project deadline due the Monday after break, and then a pset due that Wednesday. You will be facing multiple deadlines per week.

And lastly, the teaching staff will not respond generously to your questions on EdStem. They will resolve queries that are unanswered. They will leave EdStem questions unanswered for the 3–4 days leading up to the second midterm. They will respond to your homework questions where you say "I don't understand what you mean by this _____" with a response that says "What might those values suggest?", you will respond back and say "Well, I don't know what they suggest, it's why I don't understand" and they will respond back with another question that rephrases your initial question.

In short, this class is undeniably useful. Data science techniques that you learn in this class will help you with modeling, they will help you with understanding research papers (now you know what matching is) and now you know what beautiful soup is (she is very beautiful to me) but this class has a couple major flaws that you should be aware of:

1. It is not necessarily student friendly (organization wise and teaching staff wise)
2. It is a consistent amount of relatively repetitive and annoying work where you will feel that your time is not valued
3. It is a class where you are aided by having not just a Stat 110 background but also a pretty rigorous math and CS background, as they will ask you to interpret and assume you understand stat theory well enough to tell them what is "good".
This class will teach you some very useful pandas, numpy, and sklearn functionality. It also will give you core knowledge and intuition for statistical learning. If youre a masters student, youll also learn some cool theory about GLMs, PCA, etc. at an advanced undergrad/first year masters level. Tests are not too hard, all multiple choice/fill in the blank drawn from the lectures and readings.

But, the workload is high. Homework takes many hours since it largely consists of looking up a lot of documentation to accomplish one or two dataset analyses per homework. The final project runs parallel to class, and youre expected to complete substantial chunks of it during holidays. Many assignments/tests had typos and in a couple cases errors, but these were patched quickly.

My biggest warning is that you will receive virtually no feedback except through 1–2 channels. In our year, it was unless you post on Ed. Even emails to the course inbox took weeks to be answered. Homework assignments were returned after 3+ weeks (meaning that 1–2 other homework assignments were due in the interim), and comments were sparser and less helpful than automated grading/language models would have provided, probably due to the immense teaching workload involved and the test–driven nature of the Python assignments. The TFs heroically kept up with Ed, and the instructors/TFs were all very nice and helpful people who genuinely seemed to care about making the lives of students better. It just is a very big and high–contact class that requires mostly independent work and diligence (and a light course schedule other than it!) to succeed.

In short, Id recommend this class as you are guided through learning a ton of useful skills yourself. Consider whether your academic goals involve learning ML skills, and whether you can sacrifice productivity in other areas, especially if youre a grad student.
