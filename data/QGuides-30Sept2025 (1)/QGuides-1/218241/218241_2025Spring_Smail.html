Take it

The assignments are great and there is a great variety of them. The class allows you to work with actual historical artefacts and helps to see how issues about the medieval times interact with contemporary ones.
In terms of engagement/interactivity, this GENED is well above average!! I'd definitely recommend taking it if you'd like to work with others and do many hands–on projects/attend section field trips (to the Houghton Library, Harvard Art Museum, etc.) Even if the subject matter feels trite, or like something you've never considered before, you will get SO MUCH intellectual satisfaction out of this course if you treat it seriously (and it is).
Great class, Dan is an awesome professor and is incredibly engaging. The workload is medium for a GENED, not a gem but not ridiculously hard. Overall just a cool class that I'd recommend taking.
I would highly recommend this Gened for the arts aesthetics and culture. Professor Smail is incredibly knowledgeable and an incredible professor. It wasn't too challenging, but also wasn't so easy that the grading was unfair or not based on effort/assignments in the course. I'd wholeheartedly recommend!

fun class, Dan is a super engaging lecturer and builds in a lot of group discussions/projects to the class so its not just listening to lectures. certainly worth taking, especially if you're into history, no prior background in medieval history required
This is generally a great course! The professor is super passionate about the subject and really just wants his students to be passionate about the subject as well. This is a pro because lectures and activities are designed around this objective, but also a con because it's hard to skate by just by grudgingly completing the assignments. Although there aren't many true assignments, the ones that exist are quite labor intensive.
This class is a great introduction to the art and culture of the middle ages! It's not a course on medieval history, although that is touched on heavily throughout, but rather focuses on the materiality and literature of the time. As a result, the readings are compelling, the assignments are interesting, and the lectures are wonderfully detailed and enthusiastic.

In general Prof. Smail is an amazing lecturer, and he really encourages student participation and engagement. The course TFs as well Andy Chen and Angela Zhang are awesome, and in talking with them, you get the feeling that they care deeply about their students.

This class isn't a "gem" or whatever people call it, but it's not overwhelming, and is a great A&C gened imo!
I would highly recommend this course to anyone who is interested in learning about medieval history. Though the course does perhaps come with more work than your average GENED, it's extremely interactive and you will learn so much about history and how to engage with historical objects.
Professor Smail is truly one of the best professors here! He makes an effort to memorize everyones name, and actually does it! He is kind, genuine, and just nice to be around. Not only this, but he is also extremely knowledgable in his field. In regards to the class itself, it is important to understand some art background, and how to write and critically analyze pieces.
This is an amazing course for interactively learning about the Medieval Ages. The content and lectures are really interesting, however, the assignments can be really tedious and take up an unnecessary amount of time. The grading can also be extremely vague and sometimes harsh, which I find to be the biggest downfall of the course. If you take this class pass/fail it will be a lot better though.
Even though there's more work than what I understand to be typical in a gened, I did enjoy this class and I feel I've learned a lot. It's not super intense or anything, but I think I got a pretty good bang for my buck in terms of work put in and content learned.
Love this class! Assignments are fun and engaging, and the class dynamic is also very engaging. My favorite gened I have taken as a now graduating senior.
Take this class if you like medieval history, or even just history. The teaching staff has a true passion for the material and it makes the class wonderfully playful. They are technically readings, but they are long and honestly not that necessary for the class, especially if you already have background medieval knowledge.
Not a light courseload (readings/paper assignments require a substantial level of detailed work), and is a good overview of topics in the Medieval World. Though this is an arts gened (assignments are aesthetics–focused), there's a lot of history content as well. You get what you put into this course, especially re: a lot of flexibility for the topics of the final projects/papers, so choose something you're interested in. Going to the archives/university collections is encouraged! Teaching staff care a lot about the course and the material and this really makes the experience special :)
