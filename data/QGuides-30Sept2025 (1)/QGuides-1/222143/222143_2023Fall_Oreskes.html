Take this course, but be prepared for discussion! Although this course is listed as a lecture course, it is primarily discussion based. Some weeks there will be a LOT of reading, but the readings are always extremely interesting and/or historically significant, leading to a deep understanding of the history of knowledge and ignorance. The number of readings that you'll do each week can fluctuate pretty drastically, so make sure to look ahead in the syllabus so you aren't left with 300 pages of reading to do in one night. You will probably get cold called, so also make sure to actually do those 300 pages of reading! Overall, an amazing and important course with an amazing professor!
This was a great class! Prof. Oreskes is really cool and a great person to get to learn from. The course was a little disorganized since it was the first time organized, but the material was very interesting and I would definitely recommend the class. Even though she is very busy, Prof. Oreskes clearly cared a lot about getting to know students and was always willing to talk outside of class, which was really great.
Professor Oreskes is amazing, although kind of intimidating. This material is absolutely relevant for understanding modern relationships to science, climate change denial, misinformation, and whole host of other pressing problems.
TAKE THIS CLASS – it's the best class I have taken at Harvard. Prof Oreskes is hilarious, smart, and engaging, and she will push you to think differently about democracy, science, and culture. The work is not bad at all, and is super interesting. You won't regret it
This class is so interesting! Professor Oreskes is a really great lecturer and classes are very interactive and a lot of fun. This class will change the way you think about American politics and a lot of really relevant issues like climate change. The class kind of shifts topics every couple weeks or so to the kind of interests/projects Professor Oreskes has worked on. The class does not focus that much on book banning to be honest. But you come away with a really good understanding of the kind of motivating factors and causes of a lot of political issues. The readings for this class are a bit heavy though, but to be honest you can get a lot out of the class without having done all of them. (Though before the midterm you do need to do all the readings because you'll be expected to know them for the midterm).
While I am not necessarily interested in climate change, taking this course was a great choice. it is interesting and the information is taught in a way that anyone can understand despite not being familiar with climate sciences. the course was super cool and I would take it again if i could.
It was overall interesting, and I would take it again. However, while I was always interested in the overarching premise of the course: misinformation, censorship, etc. the class jumped around many many topics and some of them were pretty boring. Prof. Oreskes is a GREAT lecturer, and her lecture were always SUPER engaging (probably my most engaging lecturer so far), so I would really recommend this class. I think I just didn't love the class more because of my personal opinion/interests. If you read the title and find it somewhat interesting, please take it. You won't regret it.
If you have the opportunity to take a class taught by Professor Oreskes, enroll immediately. She is one of the most inspiring academics and scholars of the History of Science at Harvard. She also uses her work in an activist capacity to draw awareness to critical issues we face in the world today like censorship, climate change denial, and the influence of money and politics in science. The reading list is so enjoyable and I always felt compelled to come to class prepared because of how engaging our discussions moderated by Prof. Oreskes were. At times, her lectures can go off topic and assignments are not returned in a timely manner, but her presence and the subject matter itself are reason enough to look past that. Bottom line: if you're at all interested in climate change, academic scholarship actually making a difference in the world, or understanding the most vexing political and social issues facing science throughout history, take this class!
This has been, by far, the best class I took at Harvard. Prof. Oreskes is an amazing, amazing lecturer and incredibly knowledgeable about the class subject. Although there is a lot of reading to do and the class might feel a bit disorganized, she does a great job at going through it all and engaging the students. The content is remarkable and a must–know for everyone.
This class was fantastic. It is by no means a gem– you need to work, and work hard, to really benefit from the course. It is definitely possible to sit in the back and try to disengage (though you may get called on!), but it would only be a disservice to yourself. Overall, it is worth the effort one hundred times over.
