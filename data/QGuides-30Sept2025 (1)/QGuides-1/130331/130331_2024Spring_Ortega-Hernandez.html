I absolutely loved this course and could not recommend it more. I learned a lot on the field trip to Utah, and made lasting friendships and memories. This course is a fantastic deep dive into the history of the world, and fossils and geology. Both lectures and labs are interesting and engaging.
EPS/OEB 56 is fantastic! The course content is fascinating throughout, and the fieldtrip serves as a highlight of my Harvard experience so far. It provides a wonderful context to the world we live in today, and Nadja and Javier are interesting, kind, and extremely knowledgeable professors. Be prepared to study a ton for exams, as there is a lot of content along with no real review of that content (the course lacks psets or other homework other than post–labs), meaning that you have to go back and relearn a lot of the course if you don't study throughout.
TAKE THIS CLASS! This field trip was legitimately one of the best things I've done at Harvard. So many cool places, and I came home with like, not an exaggeration, 20 fossils including 5 trilobites. Even if you don't get to go on the trip (which, boo), Javier and Nadja are really passionate and quite good at teaching the material, and you get to see cool stuff in labs. The exams aren't bad if you study, and the lab assignments are reasonable. This was a top 3 class I've taken at Harvard. Take it.

Take this class if it has a field trip! A lot of the class grade is made up of the midterm and final, and there isn't a lot of study material, but if you pay attention in class you should be chilling. Weekly workload is very low too.
TAKE THIS CLASS! Both profs + TFs invest a lot of time into making the course a great experience, the workload is super manageable (basically just have to finish your lab from the week + midterm/final), and the field trip is loads of fun & a great way to get a way better understanding of the course material.
If you're thinking about taking this class, do it!! The content is very interesting and well–taught, the workload is manageable, and the field trip was genuinely one of my favorite experiences in college. The EPS concepts will be more challenging if you're an OEB concentrator (and vice versa), but the course staff are all extremely friendly and willing to help out. The course content is quite dense leading up to the midterms and final, but as long as you pay attention in class, you will do great and enjoy the experience!
This is a really fun class for students interested in biology and earth sciences! I was already a little familiar with a lot of the topics because of my previous experience with bio/eps classes, but I feel like I'm leaving with a lot more in–depth knowledge about a lot of interesting stuff. The two professors (both incredibly fun and engaging) alternate with EPS and Bio lectures, but they do so in a really cohesive way, such that you get a good understanding of how the two disciplines are interconnected. The weekly labs are hands–on, fun, and teach you a lot, and the mid–term and final are incredibly fair. I over all think this is a great class that everyone in the life/physical sciences should consider taking before graduating.
