Comments
Take it!!!!!!!!!! This course perfectly exemplifies what we came to Harvard for. Each seminar is brilliantly crafted with meaningful discussions and plethoras of relevant centerpiece objects that change every week. In few other contexts does one have the opportunity to interact with so many priceless artifacts of Mesopotamia, ancient Greece and Egypt, and pre–Columbian indigenous civilizations. Professor Georganteli is EXCELLENT. Her expertise is astounding and her passion for the subject unquestionable, yet she is also humble enough to take genuine interest in each of her students and their passions. She welcomed us to lengthy office hours, where she would always greet us with treats and lively discussion. I literally went to her office hours to discuss personal problems on several occasions. And did I mention that she took the class out for croissants and a guided MFA trip?????
Amazing introduction to art history even if you aren't a concentrator in the humanities!
Definitely apply for this class! Prof. Georganteli is the most amazing, caring, funny person you will ever meet. I would take it for her alone, but luckily, the class is really interesting too. Prof. Georganteli fostered such a great community, and we got to handle real artefacts every class. It was so cool!
ABSOLUTELY TAKE THIS CLASS–– cool experience (getting to handle very very old and expensive objects), great field trips (MFA, art making), and lovely professor. I remember reading the Qreports before and someone saying Eurydice is a goddess but she literally is. Incredibly kind and funny and just a joy. Also, go to office hours!! She'll make you a cup of coffee/hot chocolate and it's just a wonderful time.

Some of the readings are a bit of a grind (~60 pages of readings on a bad week), and the response papers can be a bit tiring, but don't let this scare you. TAKE THIS CLASS YOU WON'T REGRET IT
This class was a wonderful first–year seminar. Professor Georganteli is amazing, and the content is engaging and interesting.
This seminar is a wonderful experience – whether you're a humanities lover or are more STEM–oriented, this course has something for everyone. It adds in a bit of history, archaeology, science (in terms of the environmental and material processes behind objects), and economics. Looking at the various objects and exploring the many museum locations and workshops is something you won't be able to get elsewhere in a single semester.
