I enthusiastically recommend this Seminar. Dean Brown–Nagin has been studying social movements for a long time, and she is one of the most formidable faculty here at Harvard. It was a pleasure to learn from her.
I highly recommend taking this course. Dean Brown–Nagin is a phenomenal instructor. She cultivates very insightful and thought–provoking conversations that challenge your thought process as a student and encourage you to be more curious about the function of the law and social movements beyond the simplistic narrative that has been expressed. The course material is very engaging, and I learned much more about the ways to approach change in a manner that is more effective.
This class is a really interesting class for learning more about social movements in our countrys history and how social movements leverage and employ the law. The readings are very palatable, and its easy to engage in class discussions. The class did not really change/impact me on a profound level, but the topics were interesting and I enjoyed Professor Brown–Nagins teaching style.
If you are in any way interested in politics or law this is the course to take. You get to learn about social movements and its deep, but nuanced relationship with law from the wonderful Dean Brown–Nagin, who one of the handful of scholars currently leading this field nationally and internationally. The seminar was both a privilege and a pleasure. The readings, the environment, and the people all combined to offer a great experience that extended well beyond being simply academic, but grew me also as a thinker and student. Wonderful course, amazing professor, excellent experience all around, definitely recommend.

I would definitely take the course if you are interested in social movements or social change. Dean Brown Nagin is definitely an amazing professor and has a lot to offer intellectually and within her own experiences.
