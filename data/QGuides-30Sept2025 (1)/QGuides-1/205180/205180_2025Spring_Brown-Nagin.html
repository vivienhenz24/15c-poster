Do the readings and don't be afraid to contribute in class. Keep an open–mind.
I think this course is really interesting and Professor Brown–Nagin is incredibly sharp and astute! I do think though that this class's title is a bit misleading about what the course's actual content will be –– we spent most of our time focusing on social movements and their strategies as a whole (e.g., logos, organizing techniques, coalitions they built, etc.) with law being one of the potential strategies, instead of the specific intersection between the movements and the law. I don't think that's a bad thing in any way –– and it actually ended up being good for me to explore community organizing and the other side of how we define social movements! –– but if you're coming into this course expecting to analyze specific legal cases every week and analyzing five legal cases in reference to each other, then that isn't what this course is built for. Still truly so worth it –– I liked taking it as a fifth course during my spring semester to offset a majority STEM workload!
Take this course!!!! Brown–Nagin is a one–of–a–kind professor. Her professionalism and demeanor are so intimidating, but if you have useful insights and do the readings, she will love you. The coursework is light, so its a great class if you have a more intensive schedule.
This is a great introduction to seminar style classes, but the conversation may not go as deep as intended. Oftentimes, class became a regurgitation of general history about different movements and then a roundabout conversation on personal experiences or analyses of the movement without much direction. That being siad, it was interesting, required very little work, and the class community was great! My only reservation would be taking this class in tandem with other law–related classes, as you may get tired of the overlap.
Super fascinating class. Great first–year seminar to take. Professor Brown–Nagin may seem intimidating at firstshe will push back constructivelybut I really enjoyed learning from her.
This class is a fantastic way to learn about how law affects public opinion and social movements. You learn about many movements, and there is barely any work other than the readings.
