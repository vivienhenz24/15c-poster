What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Would highly recommend this course. Dr. Carmody is so knowledgeable, and the discussions each week are always extremely interesting. This was a great way to learn about the microbiome from an objective, science–based perspective. Definitely make sure you can devote some time to this class, as doing the readings is important to be able to understand the context of the discussion. The readings probably took between 1–3 hours a week. Additionally, the final project is time intensive, and requires you to analyze microbiome data in R and then write a 2000–2500 word paper about your findings with scientific figures. Although experience in R is not required, it definitely makes the final project more manageable, and the TF Claire was always very available to work through code–related issues. Overall, I would definitely recommend this course, but definitely make sure you have room in your schedule to devote enough time to this class, to get the most out of it.
BEST CLASS AT HARVARD. I couldn't recommend this class enough. This was probably the most revolutionary class I have taken at Harvard that made me completely rethink the human body and inspired me so much! Rachel is an engaging lecturer, very insightful, clever, a visionary, but also such a kind–hearted, open, and honest person. I promise you won't regret taking this class! Plus, you get to analyze your own microbiome, how cool is that?!
If you have never coded before enter this course with reservations. The lab section is normal bio lab for the first half of the term and then turns into all coding for the second half of the term. You do not need to know how to code to join this class, but I found it very overwhelming and frustrating at times because almost everyone else did know so it wasn't always taught in a super beginner friendly way. That said, Claire (TF) was super helpful and made the code–learning process so much more manageable and eventually enjoyable (to a degree). I think the class overall is very interesting and worth taking. I think I learned a lot, especially during the final paper. I would just be hesitant if you have never coded or if you aren't super comfortable with dense scientific papers, because the HW is always to read challenging scientific papers and then class is mostly discussing them.
Definitely have some coding experience, it will be useful for the final paper.
TAKE THIS CLASS!
Professor Carmody is truly at the cutting edge of biology. The topics covered in this class have a really wide range so there is something for everyone, from immunology, reproduction, neurobiology, evolution, food, etc. Each seminar is so engaging, and she is very encouraging of every student regardless of academic background. Lab felt practical for the first time ever, and there was a great community in the class.

This is what every science class should be like!
