What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

I think the course is overall amazing and the teaching team is fantastic (especially Monique and Liam who are great)! The course can be a lot of work though and the LPS's always take more time than expected, so make sure to attempt most of the pset before monday section (OH are usually right after) so that you can get your work checked (especially the pymol questions which can be tricky and confusing since the answer isn't always obvious/you can't always find it on your own). The biggest piece of advice I can give for this course is to stay on top of the work and complete assignments once they are made available rather than waiting until the last minute. If you're interested in biology and/or chemistry, the material will be super interesting and the course will be really rewarding. An additional plus is that the class size is relatively small, so you get to know and bond with your MCB 65 cohort!
I don't know, the content would be interesting if it wasn't already all things I had learned previously in an assortment of other, previous classes. This is more a fault of MCB/CPB requiring a MCB 60–level that can't be replaced with upper level courses than of MCB 65 itself. The staff are committed and the design/organization of the course makes sense. I did really enjoy the labs, and I think the fact that labs were completed individually and we wrote up our own lab reports was great, because it reflects what research and senior thesis writing is like and is useful preparation for it. The pop questions weren't that much work, but just because they were little unplanned assignments, they were easy to let slip through the cracks and it is personally kind of annoying to have to remember them on top of everything else that is going on during the semester. Psets and exams were fine. If I had to do a 60–level this would probably be the one I would enjoy the most.
Use the office hours and all the office hours. The psets can be weird since they are asking you questions 'you'd likely have to answer when doing science' but they are somewhat interesting. The exams are HARD. This is not a class where the exams ar easier than the practice exams. Make sure you know the equations inside and out, Ince they hardly asked you to solve for stuff you have never solved for before :)


I consider this class to be a semi–grad class. You need a background in biology, some chem (not that much), physics, and math (at the level of 1b). These classes are not requirements but will go a long way in your success in the course. The good thing is that you have 3 teachers and a TF and not that many students. My advice would be to make the most out of office hours. It is easy to get lost in one of the concepts but office hours are always there to help.

This course will have to be the center of your universe during the semester if you want a chance of getting and A. Very useful for structural biology though. You finished the class feeling like a pro a pymol. If you are doing any type of research involving proteins, this is the course to take.
If you have to take an intermediate MCB class, this is probably the best one to take, it's not super hard, but it's still pretty time consuming. Labs are interesting since you're actually working toward a question and not just randomly learning lab techniques. If you took LS50 you'll find a lot of content overlap (stat mech, diffusion, protein structure stuff, michaelis menton, etc), but you'll still learn from stuff that's more specialized toward protein structure. Psets are kinda strange but go to office hours and you'll figure them out. Small class was fun and I love the course staff!! They are the absolute sweetest, really understanding, and very knowledgable!
TAKE THIS CLASS! (if you really love biochem) This class was definitely a time commitment with weekly labs and relatively hard p–sets, but it was also such a fun topic. Everyone is so supportive and you will genuinely learn so much about applicable research techniques. A must–take if you are contemplating any type of biophysics or structural biology. It is by far my favorite course I've taken in college so far.
