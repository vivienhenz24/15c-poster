one of my favorite classes at harvard, please take!
Take this class! As a Stats major on the bioinformatics and computational bio track, I wanted a nice introductory course to genetics and OEB 50 was a great alternative to the more lab–heavy LS1b course. So I waited until my sophomore year to take this instead of LS1B and it did not disappoint! The course instructors are so genuinely happy to teach the material and always take questions during lectures!
If you are considering the course for your intro bio requirement, don't take this course. It's dull, most assignments feel like busywork, and the professors show inconsistent care at best and are patronizing of students at worst.

Furthermore, genetics is a topic that is rich, complex, and sprawingnot something that can be necessarily best visualized in chalk drawings or projector transparencies, which are what are used to teach this course. The instructors try to bring in more of a social fact to the study of genetics, but the section discussions are dull, surface–level, and it seems like most students haven't actually done the section reading or don't really want to engage in discussion in section. If you want a relatively easy course to float through, take it, but otherwise you could be learning in a deeper and more fun way in another course.
This is a great class. The true strength is the course staff, they are so so kind. It is not without flaws as the week to week schedule is a bit weird, the content of section feels a bit removed from the lectures and the exams and psets are difficult. One thing that I wish I had known is that when they say "if you can do the psets you can do the exams *wink wink nudge nudge* they DO NOT mean that the exams will be just like the psets. Yes they will be similar, but it is essential that you understand how to apply concepts, and not just know how to do specific problems in order to do well on the exams. Office hours were a must for me but I totally enjoyed the time I spent learning with the course staff in a smaller environment. Also take good notes!! Lecture content is done on paper on an overhead projector or blackboard so it is not made available afterwards. A major plus is the course structure is very forgiving so despite the content being difficult at points, it is still possible to do well!
Take this class!!! Great teaching staff who are truly there to help you learn. The final project is a creative group podcast on a topic of your choice
TAKE THIS CLASS, a lot of overlap with LS1b but the teaching staff is AMAZING and you really learn the material
This class is amazing. There is not a thing I would change about it. I loved the material, the teaching staff, everything. Three take–home exams, nine completion graded PSets and a very fun Final Project (podcast) is all you must do. The class isn't easy as content is very challenging, but the teaching staff makes it extremely enjoyable with their approach and attitude. They really just want you to learn and enjoy their class. The best biology class you could take at Harvard by far if you ask me!
The course description says this is an intro genetics course, but it isn't. You do need LS1B. That being said, it is possible to get through this class without prior genetic experience, but you'll be lost a lot of the time. If you have taken LS1B, this class is low–stakes and a good OEB option to meet requirements. That being said, if given the chance to go back and take the class again, I would not. I would probably have taken a different OEB class.
The psets are very important for the exams, make sure you understand how to complete each type of question.
