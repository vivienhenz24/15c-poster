Most likely you are STEM/pre–med student deciding whether to take LS1a or LPS A. I was recommended to take LS1 a based on my placement scores. But I chose LPS A because it class provided a solid foundation in understanding chemistry and biology, with a stronger emphasis on Chem. The first half of the class covers chemistry (ranging from atomic structure to molecular geometry, polarity), while the second half covers biology (intermolecular forces and cells to mutations and cell signaling).
The class is very structured and the Canvas is easy the navigate in terms of locating the necessary materials for learning chem and bio.

Lecture – You'll have three lecturers: Tucci (Chemistry), Boselli and Ostapovich (Bio). In my opinion, Dr. Tucci was the most engaging lecturer out of all three of them.
Before lecture, a pre–lecture handout is available on Canvas for you to preview the content that will be presented in class. During lecture, we use a platform called PollEverywhere that involved answering multiple questions. They are only graded on completion and they're meant to give you an opportunity to discuss the content with your peers. Oh, and they count for attendance. The lecture notes can be downloaded on Canvas to your GoodNotes and the TFs provide paper copies of lecture handouts as well. Tucci fills out the handout as he lectures so you can fill it out as well. And of course, the annotated lecture notes are posted immediately after lecture finishes.

Section (Discussion + Lab) –
I really appreciated the discussion sections since they review the content you saw in class at most a week before. Just like lecture, the TF provides a handout for you to complete during section as a class.

Completing lab assignments were not difficult, just tedious because there were multiple steps to take. Be sure to ask your lab TF any questions or concerns you have when carrying out a procedure to make sure you are doing it correctly. I think the annoying part about the labs were completing the pre–labs and lab reports. There weren't lengthy, but there were a few questions that were pretty hard to wrap your head around. Luckily, there are help rooms to finish your assignments with the help of Lab TFs.

Help Room – These are essentially office hours. You'll be able to complete your pset, pre–lab, or lab report with the help of PSLs, TFs, and occasionally the lecturers. They held almost every weekday immediately after class, the evenings, and almost every Sunday evening. My only complaint is that the Lab help rooms are held only 1–2 times a week: a weekday and Sunday evening.

Exams – To succeed in your midterms and finals, DO ALL THE PRACTICE TESTS. More often than not, the practice tests are similar, if not, harder than the actual tests. In my experience, I saw this commonly for chem and sometimes bio. For bio, you'll have to review the pre–lecture handouts and complete the practice tests to prepare. From my experience, to study bio, it is helpful to redraw the diagrams and figures from the lecture, study the vocabulary and all the biological processes. The midterms in general were not cumulative, but the final exam was, with a stronger emphasis on biology.

Overall, the class is well–organized and offers many opportunities to get help with succeeding. This truly was a stellar introductory course to prepare for taking future STEM courses at Harvard.

Structure:
A course google calendar is provided and is extremely helpful because it includes all assignment due dates, class times, and office hours.
The Canvas is very organized and everything you need is in one place. I have never once spent more than five seconds looking for a material (except for maybe the beginning when getting used to everything).
Lecture handouts are provided for each lecturer, but make sure to write down additional explanations, more than what they write on the notes.
The Canvas also recommends reading the textbook chapters and pre–readings, but these are also not necessary as I have never read one. Even Tucci said the textbook chapters are not needed.

Assignments:
1 P–Set a week. Chemistry p–sets were easier to follow but checking with friends and in help room is a must during the biology portion; grading is often very picky. I spent 3–6 hours on these.
Pre lab due before lab section. Often hard to understand and complete when just seeing the lab on paper, especially since they expect you to be able to devise your own instructions to follow and already know how to complete the calculations for what you are testing. Can be done in 1–2 hours in help room.
Lab Report due after the lab that is usually more writing and calculations. Can also be done in 1–2 hours in help room.
Optional practice problems. Not necessary to complete. I have never done a practice problem.

Midterms:
Looking back, the first exam (chemistry) is EXTREMELY easy. Please study. Do the practice exams. This is non–negotiable. I scored pretty low (for over–achiever standards) because I did not study enough, and without the first exam score I would have easily gotten an A in this course. The second exam (chemistry) went the best for me, and the last exam was shaky because it was biology and poorly taught, but oh well.

Final Exam:
Start in advance. Just do it. Even if you look at your notes for an hour a day starting reading period, or do one problem a day, I promise it makes all the difference. For reference this final was on the last day of the examination period (Wednesday) and I started the weekend before (Saturday) but did not take it seriously until Monday which resulted in me not being able to take both practice tests and losing out on sleep as I packed my suitcase and studied the night before. Another tip, I know you want to leave, but if you are not proactive and tend to procrastinate, I do not recommend flying out right after the exam because the extra stress is not worth it (though it is a sweet, sweet victory).

Conclusion:
Overall, it is a great course that does what its supposed to; I definitely have a strong foundation in chemistry and biology. You can definitely get an A if you work like you are supposed to and since this class is only offered in the fall semester, if you are even considering it just take it. For reference I am a potential physics concentrator but postponed physics in favor of this course. I hope I didnt forget anything, and if you read all this, I hope it helped!
