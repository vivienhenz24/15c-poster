What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Be proactive, give ample time for doing problems, discussing them, and learning the material
This was, without doubt, one of my favorite classes at HSPH. It was uncomfortable, fast–paced, and I needed to work (very slowly) through the calculus and linear algebra to understand what Professor Wang was teaching. Doing the homework and exams was a truly humbling experience, but I came out of the course with a newfound sense of confidence in understanding advanced mathematical and statistical concepts, and with an insatiable desire to learn more. I loved that every morsel of knowledge in this class was earned – there was no other way to learn asymptotic theory without, for example, sitting down and understanding how Taylor series are involved. If you are in search of a class that is taught by a wonderful (and very funny!) professor and TFs, with ample support, and in a manner that will change the way you see models and estimation (the bread and butter of many public health disciplines), please do this class. It is so incredibly rewarding.
This class is difficult – so be prepared! BUT I think it's very worth it, especially if you are interested in statistics. I highly recommend going through the notes on your own so that you can keep up with the material as it comes in class because it can be quite quick.
For outside biostats people, this course may not be an easy one, but it truly worths investing your time to become less allergic to formula/distribution!!
This was a fantastic class, and Rui Wang is a great professor. This class requires a lot of work and time but ultimately I have found it very rewarding and it has greatly broadened and deepened my understanding of statistical theory. Know that the exams are very challenging, but don't worry too much about your number grade and try to focus on how well you understand the material and things will work out in the end.
