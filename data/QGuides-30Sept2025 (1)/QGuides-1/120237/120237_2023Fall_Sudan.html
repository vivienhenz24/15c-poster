I'd guess that CS 229Rs vary a good amount, but this one was really awesome! Madhu is a great lecturer and very approachable outside of class, and coding theory is a fascinating topic. Having biweekly psets and no final exam was also very nice for work balance.
Madhu is great, but I think the course was too abstract for anyone but the premier theoretical CS postdocs to fully appreciate. I understand this is a 22X graduate theory class, but it was quite a step beyond what I am used to in CS theory. When you are reading this in 3 or 4 years when Madhu teaches coding theory again, but think if you would like applications in your class.
CS229r on coding theory is a great class that provides a broad survey of the area while also spending sufficient time on all the subjects to gain enough depth to inspire future research into interested topics. There were 6 bi–weekly problem sets, a project that could replace 1 or 2 of the problem sets, and no exams. Significant mathematical maturity and preparation is required, but advanced undergraduates can do well in the class too. Madhu was an amazing professor, and you could tell he was very knowledgeable about the subject and cared about his students.
This course is excellent, very fair and the instructor (Madhu Sudan) was amazing. Madhu is so enthusiastic about the content that it rubs off on you and he is always willing to make time for you and help you. He truly cares about his students and makes the course adaptable to students currently taking. The topic is also very interesting and relevant across many areas of research and you will find the history of the field to be very interesting. I strongly recommend to take this class or any class by Madhu. I really enjoyed the experience!
This class certainly delivers on its name. It talks about a number of different error correcting codes and is very theoretical. The class is doable if you struggle with PSET 0, but be prepared for a very difficult course. It sounds like this was not a universal experience, but for my relatively weak math background this course was quite tough. It was rewarding, though, and I found the topic very interesting.
I strongly suggest students with similar background (quantum information and quantum complexity theory) to take this course.
This is a great class that really gets into the history of coding theory. You will learn a lot.
Madhu is a phenomenal lecturer. Each topic was presented with complete clarity, and you truly feel that you could've created (most of) these ideas on your own, which is a testament to the strength of Madhu's pedagogy. The problem sets don't take up very much time, and you have plenty of time (plus late days!) to complete them.
