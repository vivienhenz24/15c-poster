I really enjoyed this course. The teaching staff is nice and they work so hard to make classes engaging. The movie assignments are actually interesting and I honestly had a lot of fun doing them. If you desperately want an A make sure to reach out to either the professor or TF to ask for advice about your work, they will be so willing to give it and it will really help because sometimes the assignment requirements may not be completely clear. They're also really receptive to feedback and just want you to succeed in general. Take the class!!!
Take notes in class. This class is a gem so long as you take those notes, as you'll get to access them for the exams. The course does have both a final paper and final exam, but that's really the only super stressful thing. No section either, so not a ton of classtime required.
Interesting lectures and subject material, but the grading expectations on the assignments weren't uniform across students and seemed really subjective and/or harsh at times. Professor is open and fairly accessible, TF seems friendly but didn't have much interaction with him other than his grading (again, which seemed unclear as to what was expected in our work).
Great class, very interesting and not much work
To future students who would like to learn about prehistory in Europe, I would highly recommend this course. The classes are stimulating, the readings are very interesting and, in addition, the professor uses a lot of media, videos, films, documentaries, to complement the topics. t is also possible to access archaeological materials, which is a unique opportunity.
SO HAPPY I TOOK THIS CLASS! Dr. Clark is the best and most knowledgeable. You can really tell how curious she is about the subject matter she teaches! I am so happy I decided to take this class. It is something, not the most engaging, but I've found it to be one of my best here. Since the class is at the Peabody Museum, you can get access to some of their impressive artifacts.
TAKE THIS COURSE!!! Dr. Clark is so kind and knowledgeable about the subject, and provides lectures and discussions that make anthropology/archaeology accessible to anyone (even those without prior knowledge). The assignments are fair and interesting (e.g. watching relevant movies/documentaries and fact–checking them). The exams (midterm and final) are also very fair as long as you paid attention during lecture, you will do well. The final essay provides a lot of freedom to more deeply explore any archaeological site you are interested in, and the checkpoint assignments (annotated bibliography, outline) ensure you don't procrastinate and receive feedback before the full essay is due. Overall, I thought the course material was super interesting it really changed my perspective on prehistoric life and how societal structure has changed over time! As a senior, this was one of my favorite classes I have taken at Harvard.
Game of Stones is just as fun as it sounds! You learn a lot about prehistoric Europe and how societies changed over time. I really enjoyed the assignments, textbook, and classes. You also learn a good amount about methods used in anthropology and how we know what life was like thousands of years ago.
The tests are lengthy and specific and difficult to get through in such a short time frame.
Prof. Clark is great and the material is interesting. Low workload that is relatively rewarding. Would recommend.
