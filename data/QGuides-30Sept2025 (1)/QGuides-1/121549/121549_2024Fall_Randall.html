I would recommend this course to anyone! I am an Economics concentrator and don't plan on concentrating in physics at all, however I've had a fascination with astrophysics for a while. This is a great way to learn more about astrophysics without feeling the pressure of grading. I also really enjoyed the community that formed around this course – I was even able to attend talks by famous theoretical physicists which I wouldn't have if I didn't attend this course. Totally recommend!!
This seminar is a very enjoyable look into more advanced astrophysics topics. Though some background might be helpful, the readings and presentations are also pretty thorough and do a good job explaining the material. The class is incredibly student driven, which means each session can go in a completely different direction. Some of our best classes were spent discussing random tangents and theories. If you love talking about astrophysics, take this class!
Take this course! Prof. Randall is super nice and it was truly an honor to learn from her. It's essentially a crash course on the world of standard model physics and cosmology. I would highly recommend that anyone interested in STEM apply for this course.
If you want a deeper than the average person's understanding of the most important modern physics theories and discoveries this is a worthwhile seminar. Be prepared however to present at least twice in the class and write summaries each week. But they are quite relaxed assignments that you can take as far as you want. Professor Randall is helpful during presentations as she will clarify and emphasize certain things that she believes are important in your presentation. She often interrupts presentations but it is not disruptive, rather it helps to further people's points. Really take advantage of being in a seminar setting with such an accomplished professor as she will answer all questions. In addition, due to the seminar, we were invited to meet with Kip Thorne!
Take this class if you are interested in understanding all about small physics. Don't let the first week's workload discourage you, and MAKE SURE NOT TO SCHEDULE YOUR PRESENTATIONS DURING THE MIDDLE OF THE SEMESTER. Try to be in the first month so you aren't stuck with one during the midterm week. I loved the course, and you will too. Professor Randall is great!
I really didn't like this class at first: I found the material confusing, the instructor unengaged, and the workload over–the–top. But as the class went on, the students became closer together (trauma–bonding over the vast amount of reading), the topics grew more interesting, and Professor Randall became more and more engaged until she was regularly participating in our presentations. We ended the class with a museum trip and a pizza party, which was very fun :)
The readings may seem a lot at first, and the presentations every week may seem daunting, but I promise that everything is so much more fun if you become friends with the people in your class and also really embrace the topics you are learning each week. The workload is very much manageable––it is maybe just one or two weeks that you have to put in a little more work to create and give a presentation.

Prof Randall's research is also SO interesting, and the whole class became more fun and conversational throughout the semester because the entire class consistently showed our interest and the fact that we were putting in the work. You'll get so much more out of this class if you do that–for example we got to have a conversation with Kip Thorne and also took a field trip to the cosmograph gallery in the MIT Museum!!
