What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
ES105hfr is systemically unfair. Not only does this course satisfy the junior engineering design requirement for only electrical engineering students, but it also imposes a strict grading system and enrollment process.

Firstly, the grading system is harsh. While you will most likely leave the course with an A, the professor is just one of those teachers who refuse to give out a 100. It all boils down to the instructor's pedagogy, which I do not agree with. On the syllabus, it says that "Everyone cant be doing a great job with everything, all the time. Its just not possible." I find this comment to be a bit pessimistic.

The course also has an enrollment cap that is very unfortunate – EWB chapters all around the world allow any student to contribute to ongoing projects. Even though our chapter is structured as a class, I firmly believe that this course should be enrollment–cap free. Let students who would like to support humanitarian causes participate.

ES105hfr counting as a junior design course only for electrical engineers is unnecessarily selective. Why EE majors in particular? If anything, the skills learned in ES105hfr are more aligned with civil/mechanical than electrical engineers. This course should satisfy the junior design requirement for all engineering students.

I find myself discussing the structure around ES105hfr more than the content of the course itself – the work you do is great, there's no doubt about it. The students are incredible, and they are the reason why this course works in the end. But I cannot seem to remain quiet about the structure around the course, which I hope to see improve in the future.
This course is a rare opportunity to work on a project with real stakes. If you're EE and need to do your junior design requirement, I would strongly recommend taking this as opposed to ES 96.
This is a really fascinating class that allows you to do real–world engineering work for an amazing cause –– it is really a breath of fresh air from the rest of the engineering departments. Los Sanchez will not be continuing so those on LS in the fall will be working on project research.
Take this class! It's no walk in the park, so be prepared to dedicate a good amount of time per week. That being said, it becomes more meaningful if you step back and treat this as actual project work that impacts real lives, so definitely not something to lose track of behind everything.
best course you can take at harvard!
Take this course! It teaches you amazing problem solving skills and you get to use skills and techniques you learned in the classroom on real world problems! The course environment is very fun and welcoming and you might get to go on a trip which is an incredible experience!!!
