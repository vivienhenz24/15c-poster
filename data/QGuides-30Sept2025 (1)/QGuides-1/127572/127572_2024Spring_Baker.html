I learned a lot
This class teaches students how to view the transition to clean electricity, and how to understand the policies that both enable and slow that transition. Additionally, it teaches basic skills in evaluating the economic feasibility of projects, which is useful in many areas of business.
Baker has a ton of experience in the field and his lectures are super engaging. I learned a lot in this class while still being light workload. The readings are mostly worth it if you choose to do them in full. Would highly recommend if you can sit through a weekly 3 hour lecture!
Goes pretty in–depth for a lot of renewable energies. Workload is super manageable throughout the semester just 1 short assignment every week and then a group final paper. However, it's pretty hard to sit through 3 hours of lecturing.
Excellent coverage of every type of renewable energy source to include past projects and future projections. Economic viability, societal challenges to deployment, and current use cases for each was very insightful.

**If you are curious about energy (no pre–reqs required) or climate change, this is a can't–miss seminar!
This class is super interesting! If you have any interest in renewables, take it! You will learn a ton about a bunch of different technologies and what the world might look like in the future. Professor baker is a great lecturer, even if the classes are a bit long. Overall, great class.
Take this class! Baker is an incredible professor who has so much passion for this topic. It is very inspiring and makes for a very engaging learning environment.
Super easy. Readings are good and not pointless. Take if you REALLY want to meet someone with real life experience implementing renewable energy. Baker super great and approachable and super smart.
Really cool course, with a professor that is extremely qualified to teach it. Prof. Baker has started his own energy venture, is a Harvard economist, and extremly engaging
when lecturing. Light workload, so you really have to take initiative yourself to learn what you want out of it. PAY ATTENTION in class, use the readings to do backround research on the things presented in readings and form your own opinion.
Take it. Great class, great content, great professor. The lectures are very long but its worth paying attention to and the assignments are very doable and even fun. The Professor is brutally honest about the prospects of different renewables, but the realism is refreshing and he still highlights potentials and hope. If you want to be able to answer the question of why the we don't invest more in hydrogen batteries, what challenges the grid faces, and what the future of energy will look like – this class is for you.
If you are planning to pursue a career in climate policy or technology then this class is a must. I would highly recommend this course to anyone with a technical background, interesting in learning more about the policy considerations in the renewable energy transition. Very little work every week and lectures are very engaging and interesting.
I have learned more in this class than any other class at Harvard. George Baker is an excellent lecturer, he makes the class. The class meeting time is kind of long, but he keeps everyone engaged the entire time–I feel like I am on the edge of my seat during class. George Baker brings in such an interesting perspective on the energy transition. BEST CLASS EVER IF YOU ARE INTERESTED IN LEARNING MORE ABOUT ENERGY SYSTEMS.
Great overview of all things renewables. Professor Baker is super knowledgeable and an engaging speaker and is fun to listen. Highly recommend for ESPP concentrators but also to anyone interested.
Overall, this is a great course! Professor Baker gives really great, engaging lectures, and you will learn a lot about renewable energy. My one concern with the course is the final project: the project is quite open–ended, which is both fun because you can explore the topics that are most interesting to you, but the requirements for the project felt unclear at times as well. Since the project accounts for a significant portion of your grade, it was stressful at times because sometimes I didn't know if I was doing the right thing or if I was missing anything. If there was anything I would improve about this course, I would say adding more structure to the project could be really helpful. But otherwise, course readings, lectures, and other homework assignments were great! I would definitely recommend taking this course.
This is a great junior tutorial if you like energy and economics and policy. You learn about a new type of energy each week and dive into how it is used, its limitations, the science behind it etc. if you pay attention and do the readings, you get a lot out of this class. the final project is very interesting a great capstone to how much you have learned throughout the semester
Take it if you want essentially a study hall with minimal structure and confusing final assignment
I came into this class not knowing how a hydroelectric dam worked and I left with a comprehensive understanding of nearly every form of renewable energy and well–informed opinions on energy policy. If you're looking for a gem where you still learn, I could not recommend this course more. This class is especially good for non–ESPP concentrators.
