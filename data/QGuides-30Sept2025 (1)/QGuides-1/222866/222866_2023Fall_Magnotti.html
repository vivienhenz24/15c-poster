What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If you are interested in pain you should take this class. Work load is relatively light, which makes sense because it’s 2 credits. Class once a week. A homework assignment once a week– answer about two questions in paragraph format. Group presentation on a research paper–15 minutes. It wasn’t too bad. He was flexible and allowed us to pick our groups is we wanted and those who didn’t have someone got paired up by him. Then final paper. Overall very interesting.
I highly recommend. Dr. Powell is an effective and passionate lecturer. His classroom environment is incredible relaxed, which lends itself to understanding the material better and approaching him with questions. I also think the topic of pain biology is incredibly fascinating and uses a lot of techinques that are common across other fields in science.
I really enjoyed this course, especially the mix of lecture and discussion that entailed our weekly meetings. I appreciated the opportunity to gain relevant background knowledge on the neuropharmacology of pain and to apply that knowledge to reading and analyzing papers and exploring a topic of interest in the field in the form of a final paper. Definitely recommend taking this class!
This is a great introduction into the field of pain––Dr. Powell is a wonderful instructor, and you will learn a lot.
Dr. Powell is the best! He really cares a lot about the course material and the students, and he's a really chill guy. The course material can be a bit dense at times, especially if certain topics don't interest you, but I would definitely recommend for a broad overview of pain and the community.
Take this course if you have a genuine interest in learning about the neurobiology of pain. You will be exploring both at a high level and in the weeds. Dr. Powell is a hip teacher –– young, energetic–– but also tends to be formal in teaching style outside of in–person classes (his Canvas messages and course policies are quite strict). Overall, packed with content, and it will deliver what it promises, so just make sure you actually are interested in pain as a physical phenomena (as opposed to emotional or psychological –– this course is focused on the NEURO side of things).
