I learned how performances work to make the categories of race, gender, and sexuality real. The theme of David Henry Hwang's M. Butterfly––– that looking ( this has to do with the main character being so wrapped up in a racist, sexist fantasy of the other main character) can disable your ability to see what is right in front of you––– was truly mind–expanding. This course was indeed mind–expanding, taking things I already knew, but framing them in a different way. You should approach this course with an open mind. Professor Bernstein gives engaging lectures. I have observed that even those who were initially skeptical about certain topics gradually began to open their minds to the class content. I owe this to Professor Bernstein's lecturing style. The Teaching Fellows, specifically my Teaching Fellow, provided important information for the midterm, and were dedicated to providing useful feedback on assignments. My TF was happy to read each of my drafts for each of my assignments, and she provided me with feedback that helped to improve my grade. Professor Bernstein and the Teaching Fellows work to create an environment that is positive and inclusive. This class did not feel at all pretentious or like we were being talked down to. This class felt like a community of people learning from an expert in the field committed to making the material understandable, and engaging, to those who are not experts. I highly recommend this class to all who are interested in taking it.
