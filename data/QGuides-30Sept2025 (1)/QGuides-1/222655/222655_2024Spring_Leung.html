This is Dr. Leung's first class and I'm cautiously hopeful future versions of this class might be betterbut I definitely wouldn't recommend the class without significant changes. Every aspect of the course had major issues, and it was not well–run or organized at all. The lecture component of the course was not engaging and Dr. Leung did very little teaching. Instead, 2–3 students would present on the week's readings and introduce a new paper, and then those students would generate discussion questions and lead the discussion. Essentially, the students taught all of the lectures. This structure did not work well at allthe presentations were very long and discussions were not engaging. Most of the time, it felt like the sections would have been the same with or without Dr. Leung present. The class would be much more enjoyable and engaging if Dr. Leung presented the readings/the week's topic and led the discussions herself. Or perhaps, minimize the role of student lectures, so they only present for 10 minutes on a supplemental study and then Dr. Leung can take over. Because of these issues, class was an absolute waste of time.

Grading was a huge issue in the course. On the last day of finals, we still only knew 5% of our grade (a research proposal assignment). It was incredibly frustratingwe had no idea how we were doing in the course at all. Grading was incredibly slow or did not happen at all. It would have been less stressful if she updated the grades throughout the semester, or even eliminated something to incorporate a short paper in the first half of the semester, just to distribute grade updates a bit more. We didn't have our grades from leading the discussion, and the two major assignments outside of that were presenting and writing a research paper. I didn't get the order wrongshe had us present our fake studies first and then turn in the paper later. Which brings me to my next point...

The structure of the final presentation and paper made absolutely no sense. It would be far more sensible to write the paper first or at least submit a draft or outline or something (which might have also distributed the grading a bit more and give students some time to revise). Because the paper was due on the last day of finals, most people had not even begun writing. The presentations would have been much better if there was some sort of incentive to write the paper first. Orswitching the order might help. Change it so the paper is due first and the presentation is after that.

Overall, the course was incredibly poorly run, unengaging, and overall, not a good experience. I'm hopeful future iterations might be betterbut as it stands right now, I wouldn't recommend it to anyone.
