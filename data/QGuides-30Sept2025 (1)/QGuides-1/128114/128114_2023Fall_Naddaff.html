Even if you are not interested in Comparative Literature, I would still recommend this course.
I love Professor Naddaff and I love this course. Easily one of my favorite courses at Harvard. I would say it is one of the most rewarding college experiences, since everyone was so personally connected to the material, and Professor Naddaff is the sweetest and is a very knowledgable person. The assignments were open–ended, and you could exercise your freedom of creativity. The content was very substantial and the discussions were rich. There is quite some reading in this course especially on some weeks more than others, but it is so worth it: the books are very good. TAKE IT!!!!!
Everything in this course is amazing. I loved how interactive and immersive it was. The conversations spoken in class were extremely insightful and enlightening. As an Arab, the class felt like a great way to grasp Middle Eastern literature and other art mediums and discover new perspectives from authors I have not thought of. The course was my favorite of the semester and it felt like a privilege to attend class weekly. While the readings do take up time, sometimes ranging from 6–7 hours a week, I found them very enjoyable. Professor Naddaff is an amazing professor who is incredibly understanding, engaging, and enthusiastic about the course topics. I would highly recommend this course to everyone.
Don't hesitate to take the course!!! My only recommendation is to make sure your courseload isn't too heavy with other classes so you have time to do all the readings (especially if you're not in a concentration that usually reads a lot). Professor Naddaff is the best and an amazing person to get to know, so talk to her! This is the best course ever so I can't stress how much you should take it!
If you're debating taking this course, please do! It serves as a wonderful introduction to the field of comparative literature, and exposes you to Arab American literature, which does not get enough attention.
