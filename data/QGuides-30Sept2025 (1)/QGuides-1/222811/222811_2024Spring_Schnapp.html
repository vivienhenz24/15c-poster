This class was simply phenomenal. Professor Schnapp is one of the most intelligent, engaging, and fascinating lecturers I have EVER had in my academic career. Every week left me in absolute awe. Even though I did not have any background in comparative literature, everyone was so incredibly open and supportiveI did not feel like an outsider in any sense of the word. The readings were also truly wonderful – they were just the right balance between challenging/thought–provoking and genuinely entertaining. If you have the opportunity, definitely take this class!! You will not regret it.
This was just a really cool class. Courses like these are what make Harvard the institution that it is. It was a true privilege being able to explore something on the very frontier of literature right now: the impact that AI will have on stories. This course exposed me to a whole new set of authors and schools of thought in literature that was incredibly illuminating and really exciting to learn about. Professor Schnapp is also just one of the most brilliant literary historians ever. Every time he spoke, he had such amazing insights to add to our discussion, so it was awesome learning from him.
Cool concept for a course, but did not live up to expectations for me.
this is a great class to take, even if you know pretty much nothing about COMPLIT or AI (as I did). Some of the readings get fairly theoretical and hard to understand, but the only thing we did with them was discuss in class so it was really fine. Otherwise all of the assignments were super fun and some weeks you literally just have to read folktales. The project at the end is also really enjoyable and you can honestly complete a fair amount of it in class time.
Any class with Schnapp is going to be great, and this one isn't an exception. He's extremely knowledgeable about the topic, the readings and discussions are interesting, and the workload is light. The final group component can really depend on the group you're in which is assigned randomly (the entire class was split into 3 groups,) but the final product was amazing and getting to use the Learning Lab made things much more fun. However, especially since this was the first time the course was offered, it was a bit difficult to follow along with how each topic relates to each other, and sometimes the work can feel a bit like busywork. If this course is offered again, I think that it would be great to expand further into having more generative storytelling components to share with the class and allowing more hands–on activities, since when we got to do those, they were very fun.
