The quality of your section will greatly depend on the faculty instructors. Some approach this journal discussion as simply 'grading for participation' or disinterest, while some are great at conveying what they want us to take away from a scientific paper.
Who you get as section faculty matters a lot to your experience in this course.
It's useful if you're not very familiar with paper reading or reviews. If you are, it's only particularly useful for the "how to academia" lectures on grants.
The course is good depending on how much your assigned faculty is willing to be open–minded and help you to break down what's going on in the papers. You'll have to do all of the readings on your own, but it felt like some faculty only wanted to drill you on the figures and not give an overview on how to interpret the papers and fully understand what is being discussed.
Faculty discussion can be intense sometimes, but it does help you!
Grading depends on the faculty in your session where some groups are graded more lenietly than the others.
Use your time effectively with this class
It is safe space to freely exchange ideas and opinions while critically dissecting a paper
My main takeaway is that the course was a great way to get closer to my peers, and I learned many new things in the section meetings. However, for the paper discussions, the way the session went and my understanding of the paper (if I wasn't too familiar with certain parts after reading it alone) was mostly dependent on how the faculty members ran the session. The discussion/willingness of students to engage and participate actively also seemed to depend on how the welcoming faculty member(s) made the session feel, and unfortunately there were cases in which I felt that the students did not feel as welcome to participate.
Really diving into these papers is awesome, really helps to ask people who might have expertise in a particular paper to get some necessary context
The faculty sections can be useful to gain experience reading primary literature in a diversity of fields. The writing assignments towards the end of the course are a somewhat helpful introduction to writing project proposals and applying for grants. Some of the TA sections and workshops are a waste of time.
Please participate in faculty discussion, it makes it so much more painful when no one wants to talk.
Be wary if you have a discussion section with Steve Elledge! Make sure you know EVERYTHING about the paper. Otherwise you're probably fine.
Quality of the course is highly dependent on assigned faculty for the section
This class is a great introduction to grad school and my section built a good sense of community during the semester. It is quite an easy class, as long as you can understand scientific literature and feel comfortable participating in small group discussions.
This class is not a lot of work, but it's also not particularly enriching
It was a great class and I took a lot away from it! I also had a great group of faulty throughout which made the class even better. It helped expose me to various fields of scientific literature, while challenging my critical thinking skills in the context of using what I have learned/ read practically within research. It helped expose me to the process of peer review, while helping me to understand how to go about formating an aims pages. These were all valuable assets to learn in the start of my PhD journey.
This class provides an overview of and experience with the peer review process, reading and discussing scientific literature, and experimental design.
I know it's annoying but really do try to read the papers thoroughly ahead of time. The more you understand the papers before section the more useful the discussions will be for you to broaden/deepen your approach to reading scientific literature.
