Comments
The lecturer is a really enthusiastic teacher. They are very invested in the material and could hold engaging conversations about the topic. This is a relatively new course and already worked out some of the kinks. They will definitely improve this class in the future and very receptive to feedback. Take this class if you want some historical insight into the development of medicine/science in the 18th century.
Carly is an excellent professor –– they come to class prepared and do a great job of guiding discussion and providing relevant background. They are also very receptive to student feedback. My favorite aspect of the course was the flexibility in writing assignments –– while there is a solid amount of writing in the course (as to be expected of an English class), it is very reasonable and goes beyond the traditional essay format. As a non–humanities concentrator, this course was very accessible to students of all backgrounds. I will say that the readings, especially at the beginning of the course, tend to be quite dry (as to be expected of 18th century literature) –– but they get much more readable by the end of the course.
This is an interesting course without a huge workload. The reading is from the 18th century so can be difficult at times to comprehend but there are only a few fairly short assignments. Dr. Yingst is very open to feedback and is super knowledgeable about the subject. Would recommend for a pre–med english requirement!
Professor Yingst is great and really committed to student learning and feedback! Assignments were thought–provoking and interesting. Great for students of any major, whether it be from science or humanities or anything in between
This class was a lot of fun! It was my first time studying the 18th century, but I've been persuaded it's a really interesting period. I was honestly a bit apprehensive at first, but thinking about science, in general, turns out to shed some very interesting light on how artists were thinking about how/what they were creating. The assignments were very flexible and fair. Reading could be a lot at times (looking at you Roderick Random) but for the most part, it was pretty enjoyable.
It's a very interesting and fun class to take if you're interested in the topic. It doesn't feel very formal or strict so it feels like you're always progressing at a pace you're comfortable with. The assignments are also a blast.
Carly is a brilliant, brilliant teacher. Intellectually rigorous yet encouraging and disarming, they set a high standard for teaching. A must take especially if you haven't taken an English course before.
