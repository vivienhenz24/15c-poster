If you are at all interested in medieval literature, take this course. Professor Donoghue is great and fosters discussion in the weekly course meetings. I learned so much from the works we read, and even with little prior experience with these types of readings was able to engage in discussion and explore topics more deeply in the midterm paper and final project. While this was not a Tolkien class per–se, we did touch on ways more contemporary authors and artists have been able to draw on medieval tropes, and why this literature remains relevant in our world today.
This is an excellent course. Professor Donoghue is an excellent instructor and the material is deeply fascinating. The reading loads can be a bit heavy, so be careful in balancing your schedule, but otherwise it's a wonderful course. It's not a Tolkien class!
This was hands down my favorite class of all time. If you love medieval literature, discussing fascinating ideas with interesting people, and perhaps learning a bit of Old English, this is the class for you! Professor Donoghue is a classic academic and truly delightful to talk to. The readings are dense, difficult, and WONDERFUL. I never would have found many of these books on my own.

It's a difficult class with a lot of readings, but it will be worth it. HIGHLY recommend.
Warning: This is not a Tolkien class! Tolkien was barely ever mentioned. But if you're interested in reading a bunch of cool sagas and poems, this is the class for you. Professor Donoghue is extremely knowledgeable and helpful outside of class, though he doesn't add much structure to class discussions. It's easy to get a bit lost, so it's important to stay on top of things. Really the type of class where you get out what you put in.
Unless this class has changed considerably, the fact of the matter is that you should not take it if you are looking for something about Tolkien. If JRR Tolkien is the primary reason you want to take this course, it's not for you. He's not involved. Other than that, there is a bit of a Hum 10 vibe with a book, or several readings, a week. Professor Donoghue is really sweet. I learned a lot, but my primary goal of nerding about Tolkien was frustrated entirely by the complete lack of Tolkien in the classroom.
