This is a basic course in differential geometry and doesn't cover any particularly specialized material, so there isn't much benefit to taking it if you've had exposure to a significant fraction of basic geometry. That being said, for someone without such background, it's a good way to quickly build a solid foundation.
This class is very good for anyone looking for a solid foundation in differential geometry, especially from a more abstract perspective.
This course covers a wide range of essential material for any advanced mathematics undergraduate. The assignments and exams for the course are well–designed and manageable, strongly reinforcing the understanding of the course material. I would recommend this course, but be wary if the Taubes textbook is used in the future, as it is difficult to follow.
This course will provide learners with the necessary material to begin exploring low–dimensional topology.
Very important material if you want to do geometry (for instance, algebraic geometry or complex geometry). The lectures are very mid / boring, but the TF much more than makes up for it with amazing teaching and exposition / help during OH. Even with subpar lectures (and a subpar textbook), I would recommend the course simply because it is so important.
