Take this class!!!!! This is a great introduction to the economic and political aspects of climate change. The class is not quantitatively heavy which makes it a great class for all backgrounds. The assignments are also great ways to engage with Harvard and the outside world on climate issues. They pull somewhat from the readings but each assignment is so unique and customizable for what you want to do. The TFs are always available to talk and they give great advice on what they expect – just go to office hours. The final study guide perfectly encapsulates the final, and they don't have any huge curveballs. If Rachel is TFing again, this class is a must take. She made a 9:00am Friday section somehow the most lively and cool. Seriously this class is awesome.

This class is very disorganized and unconventional. I learned from the readings, but I struggled to learn in lecture. We did class activities almost every lecture (which was about 80 people). During these activities the directions were usually pretty unclear and we would waste about 20 minutes of lecture trying to do the activity. The lectures were never really coherent and did not always connect to the rest of course material, in my opinion. As for the section component, it was not bad but none of the TF's were on the same page. I don't think that was the TF's fault, but people in other sections were learning material that was very different from what was covered in my section. Lastly, the assignments were a bit arbitrary. There are three big ones throughout the semester, and students were either given very concrete and direct instructions to follow, or very flimsy guidelines. For example, our final project (worth 25% of our grade) was a paper with no page minimum or maximum, and extremely vague guidelines of what information should be included. Overall, I was unsatisfied with the class that had so much potential to be really interesting and engaging.
