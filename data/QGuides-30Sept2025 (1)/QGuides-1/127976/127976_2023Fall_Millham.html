What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If you are at all interested in the history of surgery, this is a great course to take. Dr. Millham is great at presenting information and there are so many memorable parts and activities in the course!
1. Take this class for sure!! It is not a hard class, but if you put even a bit of effort into the material you will come out knowing so much interesting history about medicine that your perspective on surgery will be changed forever. Pre–meds and history buffs, this is an amazing class for you.
2. The depth and variety of the readings is awesome. Dr. Millham provides many interesting primary sources and historical analyses for us to read that really were not difficult to get through as long as you set some time aside to do them. Or, if you really are pressed for time, skim to find the main ideas so you aren't just sitting there in silence when he asks a question in class (the goal is to learn, and even knowing a little bit to jumpstart a conversation is better than not knowing anything). I also really appreciated how many different perspectives were provided/considered in the course.
3. The hands–on experiences of this class are not to miss. I absolutely LOVED all the field trips (Countway Library, Etherdome, Anatomy Lab**). I thought they were incredibly insightful and only reinforced my desire to go into medicine in the future. Dr. Millham has established such a good relationship with all the professionals at these locations, and I could really tell they love what they do. Also, who else can say they've held a human heart in their hands!
4. Also, Dr. Millham cares greatly about his students. Especially during some of the rougher weeks of the semester, he is an awesome source of kindness and food to his students when he was not required to be. Definitely one of the most conscientious teachers I've ever had.
1000% take this class. The material and course content is wonderful but most importantly (and what distinctly sets this class apart) is the professor. Dr.Millham provides a sense of community like no other. Our class quickly grew into a comfortable, friendly, and happy space. As a first–year, it was an amazing support system to have. The field trips are also AMAZING. This class is low–stress, high–reward.
This class is so much more than the history you learn. It's a community and safe space to come to once a week and just learn for the love of learning.
Take this class! Dr. Millham is so knowledgable and has a great sense of humor, and classes were interesting and enlightening!
This class was the transformative experience that Harvard claims to give. I will never forget all of the hands–on activities, vivid stories, and the incredible care that Dr. Millham showed us. Whether it was traveling to the Countway Library to see 500–year–old books, the Ether Dome where we were transported back in time, or holding a physical human heart that once had a warm heartbeat and harbored decades of stories within, I will never forget the memories we made together as a community. From the stories by Dr. Aldridge of his time in the military to Dr. Millham's stories of the surgeries he's done and the people he's saved, I have fallen in love with the beauty that is medicine. And even as the campus was in turmoil from the Israeli–Palestinian conflict overseas, he provided us with a safe space to share our concerns and to speak about our feelings. This freshman seminar did magnificently at both things a freshman seminar is supposed to do––it was an eye–opening look at an incredible topic and it really helped adjust me to freshman year at college.
Absolutely take it.
