This was such a growth–inducing class. It was hard for sure, but Professor Malik is so knowledgeable and knows how to facilitate a class discussion so well that I walked away learning something interesting with every class. I became a highly critical thinker, understand political science research design ten times better, and now, racial and ethnic politics will be the subject of my thesis.
Take this class if you are interested in any of the topics in the title. The readings are very interesting and cover political theory, international relations, research experiments, and thought pieces.
Prepare to read deeply and know what the authors are saying!
Take this class knowing that you will learn a lot but also that it will be quite challenging. The class assignments especially require a lot more time and effort than an average Harvard class. The expectation of the professor is also quite high from the students' work. Go to office hours and start your papers weeks earlier than the deadline. If you are genuinely interested in the topic, then it would be a wonderful class and the work will be worth it, but also if you are not, it will be a quite demanding class and you might feel lost at times.
This is a great class. Prof Malik challenges students to be thorough in their arguments and understanding of different concepts. I loved that this class touched on ethnic & racial politics in the US context as well as in many global contexts. I also loved that Prof. Malik centered students' thoughts and didn't heave her ideas on to us but rather helped us be more accurate in our analyses. She may come off a bit intimidating/overwhelming but that's only because she's a top–tier expert and wants us to be the best social scientists possible!
The course is great. I recommend taking it! Professor Malik is great –– She wants her students to better understand literature, how to read and critique/dissect literature and learn about research methods. The course has a decent amount of readings but nothing too overwhelming as long as you read them each week. The amount of assignments given is manageable. Had a few smaller papers, a final research paper, and two discussion posts per week on the readings. Overall, I looked forward to this class. As a junior, the class was a great foundation for my senior thesis as I had an interest in a topic relating to ethnicity.
This is a great Gov seminar and the material is very interesting. It does feel like it is not very open to all perspectives, but it made me think of many things I had not though about before.
