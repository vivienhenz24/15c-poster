As someone who has never studied population genetics, this was a nice intro. I will say lectures and the course overall could be extremely dry (and on problem sets you're like did we cover that in class??). But exams are take home and psets are graded based on completion, which is a godsend.
This content is actually very challenging (especially if you are not an applied math major like most of the class), but the exams are doable when you reference all of the notes and textbookthe textbook was very useful for assignments and getting a broader understanding of lecture material.
I really wanted to like this course but I just didnt. Part of it was me not engaging with the content as much as I could, but a lot of it was overall course disorganization/lack of cohesion between instructors. And a large part of it was the students, who clearly didnt care about the content and were just there to fulfill a requirement. It was sad.
Take this class if you're interested in population genetics. It's a nice low–pressure but detailed overview of the field, and sets you up nicely to take coalescent theory. It's mathy but as long as you spend a little time with it the math is all pretty accessible as long as you've seen calculus and some probability theory before. Homeworks being graded on completion was very nice and the take–home exams are very straightforward as long as you've done all the homework.
This course was a fascinating treatment of the statistics and math behind biology, and will be very interesting to take.
OK class. Not so much work, but you only get out of it what you put into it. Professors are very passionate and teaching team is great.
The lectures are incredibly boring and its too easy to cruise by without learning anything
I loved this class!! AMAZING professors and TFs – some of the more quantitative topics can get a bit complex, but you learn a lot all the different methods/models in population genetics. Can't emphasize enough how great the teaching staff is – they're incredibly passionate about the subject, but are also super compassionate and the psets/exams feel very relevant and reasonable for the class.
Fantastic way to learn interesting material. As Michael once said "it takes three years to discover an unsolved problem in physics. It takes one month in population genetics." prepare for a lot of head scratching but a lot of fun.
It's an interesting survey of population genetics building on topics covered in LS1B. I think Professor Desai's lectures were especially interesting, introducing a more statistical framework for thinking about genetics/mutations, which is great if you love modeling and he really dives into the assumptions and where they fall short.

Beyond lecture, the learning is largely self–driven. The course grew a lot because of so many AM–Bio students joining and I feel like the course staff was underprepared for this number of students. The TF's explanations of solutions to the previous week's pset were super rushed at the end of lecture, like literally trying to squeeze in every problem in under an hour, so it was really hard to follow. It would make more sense to go through 1 or 2 problems that people were most confused about. It doesn't really matter because the psets were graded P/F but like... it made it hard to build a logical understanding of how to approach problems. Furthermore, the answer keys posted online were also kinda confusing, like someone should go through and latex them with thorough explanations because a lot of popgen is in the details (like factors of two, etc).
Very good intro to the topics, focused on diploids a lot but a must if you are interesting in pop gen
