One of my favorite courses that I've ever taken here as an undergrad if you have even an inkling of interest in social sciences (alongside CS) or want to practice and extend your algorithms and theoretical CS background towards thought–provoking and important areas, then definitely take this class! Ariel is amazing he has a unique ability to dissect complicated ideas, processes, and proofs into easily digestable and logical pieces, using smaller examples and analogies. He is also so accessible, humorous, and brilliant as a professor overall.
I highly, highly recommend this class! I had such a wonderful experience, and the content itself is really cool. The course is also impeccably structured; I am a HUGE fan of the lectures and lecture slide organization. Ariel is an incredible instructor and is always excited to engage with students, which I really appreciated. I loved that the course starts from the fundamental bones of the material and works its way up such that you're never lost when it comes to course content. The final project was super interesting and a great experience! Tldr; love Ariel, love this class, definitely take if you can!
I took this course after reading incredible reviews from previous years. Overall this course is solid, but decently niche. I think social choice theory is very hit–or–miss in terms of how much people are interested in it. If you find that you are interested in, you will benefit immensely from the expertise of Ariel, who is a bona fide leader in the field. But even otherwise it's still one of the least stressful ways to fulfill a theory requirement for the concentration. The course has a very collegial discussion environment which I quite enjoyed. I found the psets pretty tough and decently time–consuming. The final project aspect felt quite rushed, with only about 1–2 weeks of work to submit. Overall a different flavor of CS than you normally see, which can be wonderful if you enjoy it, but otherwise a solid course for the concentration.
Super cool class. The lottery is tough (over 200 people applied for like 40 spots), so if you get it you should take it. The problem sets are definitely not trivial, but you have two weeks to do them. The final project has no structure, so make sure you start it early and check it with Ariel. Kind of like a survey course where each class is on something new which is really interesting. Its not a gem but thats not why you take the class. Its a really cool combination of important topics and is more applied than other Harvard cs classes. It makes you think. TAKE THE CLASSSSSSSS
You'll get as much out of this class as you put in. The topics are very interesting and applicable, but are very theoretical. Taking CS136 beforehand is very helpful but not required. Grading is just 3 big buckets (participation, psets, and final project). Participation means ACTIVE participation, i.e. you have to raise your hand and ask/answer a question in XXX number of lectures to receive full participation points. The final project is group–based. Ariel is a treasure!!! He knows every student's name, and is truly helpful and invested in you. Would recommend this class overall, but keep in mind that despite the very tangible course topic, it's still mostly theoretical.
Take this course! It's hyped up for good reason. Ariel is a great lecturer who makes complex concepts surprisingly easy to understand. A CS course with lots of real–world applications.
This is a great course if you're interested in government and mathematics and how the two interrelate. Professor Procaccia is also a fantastic professor, and the Shirley and Itai are wonderful TFs as well. I took a lot away from the course (and the final project helps too!).
This is a great class if you are interested in game theory or theoretical CS and potential applications.
Ariel is amazing–– a great lecturer with great slides, gives timely and accessible feedback, and truly so engaged and dedicated to this course. The assignments are challenging but doable with help from TFs and working with a group, and the entire staff is dedicated to your success. However, the one weakness is that I feel the course may have benefitted from more support/feedback in the final project process. Our group felt rather overwhelmed/worried by the project. Although everyone was readily available to help, perhaps a class period or two dedicated to working on the projects with the class to figure out ideas and work would make a big difference. Otherwise, though, this class is truly amazing and one of my favorite classes of all time, alongside CS136!
Interesting and not too technical views on democracy / voting / allocation etc. 4 psets and a project. Ariel is a great lecturer and very nice too!
This is a wonderful class, especially if you liked the kind of thinking you do in CS124, but want it to be grounded in an important application. I would definitely take it with friends or a willingness to make friends, as I found the psets rather difficult (on average, around the same level of difficulty as CS124 psets, though they were much friendlier as you had more time to do fewer problems, so it was fun instead of stressful). This was also a very interesting exposure to economic theory, I hadn't realized how much of that there would be in this class, and it was cool to learn something new! Professor Procaccia is also an amazing lecturer who adds all sorts of fun historical tidbits that are really memorable.
