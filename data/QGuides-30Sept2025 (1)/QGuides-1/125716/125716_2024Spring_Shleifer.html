This is a great course where you will get an opportunity wo create your own research paper under the mentorship of Andrei, who is very invested in your work, and enjoys working with his students. I would highly recommend this course in your junior spring, to explore a topic you would want to do as your thesis. The final paper is A LOT of work but its extremely satisfying and prepares you well for further research. The rest of the coursework is very light, and the few books that were assigned I found fun.
This course provided a fantastic survey of the political economy literature and the most relevant topics to get a sense of how the field evolved and what research in the field looks like today. This course was my first introduction to political economics and it gave me an appreciation for the creative ways in which economists quantify difficult to measure qualities. I also gained an appreciation for which subfields are most interesting to me.

Professor Shleifer's lectures were thorough, well–explained, and entertaining, and left much room and flexibility for students to ask interesting questions. I most enjoyed the opportunity to get to know and spend time conducting work under Professor Shleifer. He offered pointed and encouraging feedback, always with much foresight. Going to his HBS office was always a treat too. Second to that, another one of favorite aspects of this class was the guest lectures; Professor Shleifer invites quality guest lecturers to present on timely topics, and it aids in the diversity of the content that we consume in the course. Lastly, I enjoyed the diversity of perspectives that were offered by our class cohort. You will learn much from observing and listening to your classmates and how they engaged with the readings.
You are likely looking at this course because of two main reasons: 1) you're interested in writing a serious academic economics paper and want that experience, maybe you're thinking about a thesis or want to give proper research a try or 2) you know the GOAT of the economics department – Andrei – teaches this course. Both are fantastic reasons to take this course.

That said you need a couple things: you need to apply by the deadline, it is competitive so be sure to have compelling reasons and have spoken to previous students in the course before you apply. Be sure to have taken or taking the intermediate series in economics and have some knowledge of econometrics as well as coding. Remember you will be writing a serious paper and so this foundation is essential because if you don't know how to set up a regression how can you be expected to run it. The small weekly assignments don't really matter but for the paper you will need a dataset, a good question and do the analysis yourself.

I love every second of this course, I felt like I learnt so much from our in class discussions and while sometimes the slides can be a bit boring 1) Andrei is the GOAT and will spend lots of time trying to help you with your paper and spend time getting to know you – this is the best part about this course, 2) actually do the readings, and 3) the more time you spend doing research the better not only for you but for feedback and for enjoyment and knowledge in the course. 10/10 one of the best courses I've taken.
This was one of the best classes I have ever taken at Harvard. Andrei really cares about developing student talent, ideas, and research. You never feel strange about booking office hours, in fact, it is highly encouraged. There is a wide support network around the class, and it really allows you to push yourself, explore a new avenue of research and produce some really interesting work. This class emphasizes learning and exploration over being ranked or compared to peers. It has encouraged me to consider other avenues in research and potential further study I found it so insightful. This is the seminar class you hope to take at Harvard.
If you're interested in economics research, this is a must–take course. You can pretty much do a project on whatever you're interested in, and best of all, Andrei makes himself so available to advise you. While he can always offer you some valuable big–picture ideas, he is also great at connecting you with other professors / grad students who are more knowledgeable than him on specific topics. All in all, this is truly a one–of–a–kind opportunity to work on economics research that you have a lot of freedom to direct, but that you also have a lot of support for from Andrei. Lastly in case you're curious, it seems like grading and deadlines are generally very chill.
Take this class! Andrei makes so much time to meet with students and will help you write a really strong paper. You'll get about as much out of the course as you're willing to put in, so spend time on the paper. Andrei also has a great sense of humor and makes class fun and fascinating.
This is a very, very good class. The paper–writing process is very engaging and will allow you to start a senior thesis early. Prof. Shleifer always makes himself available during office hours with feedback to guide you, which makes the process an authentic learning experience. I highly recommend this class to explore a non–conventional economics question that has always fascinated you.
This is the most incredible class you will EVER take at Harvard and probably in your life. In terms of the content, you will be pushed to think critically about the intersections of politics, institutions, economics, society. But personally, you will really be encouraged to produce something excellent and to challenge yourself. I woke up every other day thinking that maybe I should pursue graduate school in economics, and I've never had this thought before. Andrei is incredibly passionate about being a mentor to students and this really shines in the way he teaches this class. We all met with him almost weekly to work on our papers, and you will never meet another professor who is as passionate about student development and mentorship than he is. Not only did this class push me to become a better '"economist"", but through this class I also think I got a mentor that I will stay in touch with well beyond graduation, and that is truly invaluable. TAKE THIS COURSE!! IT WILL CHANGE YOU!
