What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
The grading in this course was super chill and the workload was light but it was still the scariest class I took all semester. Prof. Nancy is not the kind of person to sugercoat feedback, and if you don't understand something or misquote a paper she'll cook you in front of the class. The coolest part of this class is the literature she assigns. It's a great way to get familiar with the methods, structures, and unanswered questions in the field, and it seems like she knows every chromosome researcher in the world personally. A lot of the material is at the very edge of where the field is at, and that was SO interesting. I learned a lot about chromosomes (especially meiosis the meiosis stuff in this class was great) but I wouldn't recommend unless you're a big fan of physical biology and chromosomes as mechanical structures. The final can be frustrating, and starting on it early will take an insane amount of stress out of the class.
This course is like riding a rollercoaster. It is beyond frightening and you think you're wild for electing to do it. But you find yourself in that line again. You find yourself coming back. The challenge is addicting. You will present 3 papers over the course of this course and you will improve each time. You may humiliate yourself the first time – Professor Nancy will not hesitate to call you out, shake her head, or call out "WRONG!". But you will learn and be a better thinker for it. You'll be challenged to think like a scientist in writing your grant proposal – she will have the same expectations of you that the NIH will have of a PI. You will learn how to write a grant proposal; it'll be hard and frustrating, but you will achieve it. If you are an MCB concentrator with an interest in genetics– this course will remind you why you chose it and you will be subsequently reillusioned. It is an affirming course indeed.
I highly recommend this class! It is a little harder of a topic compared to other MCB upper levels Ive taken, but it was very interesting. You just present research papers and write one short research proposal, which I thought was really helpful to learn how to approach experimental design, so this is a good fourth class if you have a busy schedule.
