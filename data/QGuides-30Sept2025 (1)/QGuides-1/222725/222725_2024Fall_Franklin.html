This class was phenomenal in several ways it acts as a bridge between Physics and English by exploring some topics and ideas at the intersection of the two. The class seemed to attract students from a wide range of academic backgrounds, from STEM majors who want to write more and read more literature to students from social science/humanities disciplines who want to learn some physics. The daily 300 word prompts can be more challenging than you might anticipate, but they are good writing exercises that engage your creativity in a way that other classes may not. The true highlight of the class was the teaching team: Claire and Melissa have so much chemistry as a teaching duo, Claire being one of the kindest professors Ive crossed paths with and Melissa always being very insightful and humorous. Kelliane was also a really fantastic TF who truly cares about her students. Another highlight of the course was the final readings, where each of us read one of our pieces to the whole classwe got to hear some really thoughtful and personal stories from our peers. It was a wonderful experience.
This is another class that is really "you get what you put into it." You can take this as a relatively chill class (by physics standards, that is) and coast, or you can put in effort and find yourself with at least a decent amount of work to do. Putting in a solid effort to the essays will require quite a bit of time, and if you're a slow reader the readings will also take up a lot of time as well. The course is not particularly challenging, but definitely intellectually stimulating and exciting. The professors/Kellianne are some of the most engaging (and, honestly, just interesting) people I have ever met, and they display both a passion and an interest in the topic. While I really felt like I was not putting sufficient effort into the class during the semester, I came out of the fog of academic war to realize that I gained a LOT from this class. I am so grateful to have taken it, and I cannot recommend it more to anyone who wants a STEM–styled English class (it's quite wacky, and you'll see that Melissa Franklin, one of the professors, really embodies the class). Messud is an excellent lecturer as well, and her banter with Franklin made the class such a joy to go to.

This is a great class. Claire and Melissa do a good job of keeping the class engaging, but the true superstar is Kelliannethe head TF. This is among the best classes I have taken. Melissa and Claire are amazing professors and they are always very funny. Their funny exchange in class has been a highlight. It is a two hour class but it is not the kind that you wish would end sooner, the first half is usually Claire going over the readings for the week or reading student's essays, the second half is Melissa being funny and talking about interesting physics.

The daily prompts, 4 times a week, are very interesting and opportunities for self–exploration, most prompts are just asking you about your life or for personal reflections on different issues. The section component is also amazing, Kelliane is such an amazing section leader, there are no pressures on deadline and there is usually a one or two days grace period for submitting assignments. All people in the teaching staff, the professors and Kellianne, read our essays which are usually very personal and as a result know a lot about us, and they even say they talk about everyone. I am not sure if this course will be run in the future but if so it is a great course to satisfy your humanities requirement.
