This is an absolutely amazing class. Xingang is the best professor I've had so far at Harvard. He goes at a really fast pace, but he manages to do so while maintaining clarity with what he is talking about. As a result, as long as you are actively paying attention, you will learn the subject very efficiently. The lecture notes are very well written, and contain everything that you need to know for the psets/exams. You should prepare to dedicate a little more time to this class than you might think, because going through the lecture notes is helpful to ensure proper understanding. I can't recommend this course enough if you want to learn GR!
Xingang is a great lecturer; he is extremely knowledgable and excited about the course content. The lectures are very fast paced, so it's easy to get lostcome to class caffeinated! The middle of the course covers an introduction to differential geometry, so be prepared for a heavy load of pure mathematics mid–semester. However, Xingang builds the foundations and motivations for GR well, and the timeline of topics throughout the semester culminates nicely. Overall, this is definitely one of the best classes I've taken at Harvard thus far. Take this class to actually understand the physics and math behind all those youtube videos about the "ripples in spacetime" :D

In this regard, do not be intimidated by the notations because once you get comfortable with the new language of GR, everything becomes easier. The PSETs arent ridiculously time–consuming so its up to you to allot some time to absorb the concepts being taught. The way to approach this class is to slow down and build your own intuition. Definitely one of the standout classes I've taken during my time at Harvard. Xingang is an absolutely incredible person and just a complete joy to be around. He gets everyone excited about the subject and his lectures help bring a lot of clarity and intuition to what is otherwise a very opaque topic. The unit on differential geometry is a bit rough and Xingang's lectures were a bit rapid for my taste at this point, but it is all part of the subject. TAKE THIS COURSE!!!!!! This class was the absolute highlight of my fall semester. I am nowhere near an astrophysicist (or a physicist, for that matter), and yet I continuously looked forward to attending lectures and starting the next problem set. Astro 140 is a fairly low workload course and, despite this, gives a very comprehensive overview on the topic. The course begins with a review of special relativity, then spends several weeks on the mathematical formulation behind GR (i.e., Riemannian geometry), before reaping the benefits and exploring the theory and experimental evidence behind the classical tests of general relativity, gravitational deflection of light, black holes, and finally gravitational waves at a highly technical and mathematical level. Every part of this course was highly motivated and its importance immediately clear. I would highly encourage anybody even remotely involved in science to take this course.
