What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Matthew is amazing in his care for students and genuinely wanting to help you build your academic career (For Gov concentrators) and understand the material. Also one of the few courses that actually helps you synthesize Gov theory and what is going on practically, instead of just being stuck in one or the other. The class also has fellows from Harvard attending every day, all of whom are experts on Japan or work for the Japanese government, and they offer amazing perspective that you can't get anywhere other than Harvard. If you don't know about Japan or want to learn more about technology from a state perspective, take this course. If you want to learn how to write and get published, take this course. Matthew will always be ready to help students in research and actively pushes you to get your works published and be ready for post–grad.
I am glad I decided to enrol in this Monday 9 a.m. class.
Matthew is a great teacher who brings in a variety of perspectives and experience. Class assignments were fair and helpful for understanding the material. We even got to try vertically farmed Japanese strawberries in class.
TAKE THIS COURSE!!! If you are interested in any intersection of STEM and policy and/or doing a deep dive study into a different country, this class is a must. There are varying backgrounds in the class, so you will learn a lot from other students as well as lectures. Lectures and readings are really engaging and teach you a ton. There's also cool guest speakers and more learning opportunities from writing a full–length research paper, a response paper and an op–ed.
Prof. Brummer is amazing! Super kind, and he will set up office hours at basically any time to help you out. Every time I went, we ended up speaking for almost an hour.
The course is well–structured and the readings are interesting and well–integrated into the lectures. We had a transfer program with master's students from Japan sitting in on the course, which was a really special experience (not sure if this will continue in future years, though). Beyond that, the class is small and the mix of Grads/Undergrads also helps elevate the level of discourse.

In terms of work, the class isn't crazy, but you definitely want to put in a good amount of effort to the papers that you do have to write. Luckily, the readings start off pretty heavy but taper off as you get busier working on the midterm/final paper. Grading policy is fair, and feedback on assignments is very helpful.

START THE FINAL PAPER EARLY!!!!
