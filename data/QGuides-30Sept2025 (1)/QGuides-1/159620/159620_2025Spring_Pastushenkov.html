Take it, take it, take it. This class will make you feel like the work required of learning is worth it. The textbooks homework can be a bit much at times, and the weekly vocab quizzes can feel immense, but the grading is structured for you to learn a language and not to slog your way to an A. Also, the people who decide to take this class are dedicated in their own rightno one exactly stumbles into a Russian classso this will give you the opportunity to gain a close–knit community wherever you are in your academic journey. SUCH a good course. I cannot believe I can speak as much Russian as I can now.
If you've already taken RUSS AA, this class is pretty similar, just building on what you know; I imagine I don't need to sell you too much on taking AB. Jemma is a great TF; she's is always very encouraging and tries to make the lessons fun. As long as you stay on top of the homework, nothing in this class should be too hard. The essays might seem intimidating, but armed with a dictionary they're not so bad. This semester, you actually get to read some real book chapters/poems/stories written by actual Russian authors, which is pretty cool. N.B. for undergraduates: if you perform well enough over the year, you can be nominated to participate in the New England Olympiada of Spoken Russian (info online) or be granted an Behr Award with a cash prize, if you want extra motivation to make your Russian even better.
PLEASE TAKE THIS CLASS! The teaching team is truly outstanding. They are extremely supportive, responsive, kind, caring, and so knowledgable. I definitely felt intimidated learning Russian from scratch, but they made it so enjoyable. The course is designed to help you progress as much as possible without worrying about your grades. You'll learn so much and make so many new friends!
Do not hesitate to take this course!! The teachers are fantastic, and the students/classmates are great as well! The workload is really not too overwhelming, but you still learn a lot. Honestly my favorite class ever!!
