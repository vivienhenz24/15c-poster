What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
** This is a great and accessible class for students interested in the topic, but it does require a good amount of reading and consistent participation in weekly discussions. Since the class meets only once a week for about two hours, its not demanding in terms of in–class time – but you do need to come prepared to engage with the readings each week. Discussions are super chill, but you need to do readings to participate effectively because the readings are niche.

Professor Colton is incredibly knowledgeable and always happy to share insights and support students. His lectures tend to be long and information–dense, so bringing a coffee to stay focused is a good idea, though thats pretty standard for a once–a–week, 2–hour 45–minute seminar.

While the final paper is challenging because of its length, the biweekly response papers are quite short and nice. Aside from the reading load, the course is fairly light in terms of written assignments, so when you focus on this aspect, it is definitely worth taking to balance your semester workload and enhance your GOV experience here at Harvard. Grading is perfect and top, as long as you actually show that you read the assigned readings via response papers and in–class discussions.
Take this class!! Prof. Colton is incredibly knowledgeable and you will learn so much about world regions. Be prepared to do a LOT of readings, but it's flexible, and they are really worth it. Great in–class discussions!
It's a class where you get what you put into it!
