What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
For students interested in the topic of death and immortality, I would highly recommend this course. It offers a fascinating and diverse perspective on death, addressing various topics that are very relevant in today's society. The learning experience will allow them to explore the subject matter from different angles and engage in contemporary debates.
This was a nice elective course with a light workload. The material is very interesting but we weren't able to really dig deep because the class was student–led.
Take it. It offers a wider variety of content on different cultures and is a great broad introduction to non–anthropology concentrators
This is a really interesting course! Professor Bernstein is certainly a really brilliant person, though definitely quiet and a bit hard to reach via email, especially if you are an undergraduate :(. If you are looking for an intro anthropology course, or simply are fascinated by death the various roles it plays across the world and time, this is a perfect class for that. I found the students in my class to be incredibly motivated, and many had experience in the field, so it was great hearing them discuss. It's definitely a pretty easy class –– it is possible to get by the class by skimming the readings and vaguely participating in the conversations. There are two papers, but the topics are closely aligned to what we do in class, so they are not challenging if you keep up. With that being said, you get out of the course what you put in it, as with many other gems. The readings are super interesting, I found it to be philosophically and existentially satisfying in many ways, so definitely take this class if that's what you are looking for!
The topics of this class is great, but the execution of it was poor. Professor Bernstein appeared stressed the entire semester and since she let student led discussion take up most of the class, I did not feel that she contributed much at all to the experience. Most of my learning came from the readings and student led discussion, however these learnings were minimal. As someone who has taken many classes in religion/ the humanities, this might be been the most poorly executed humanities class I have taken.
This class has a lot of potential––the topics and readings are very interesting and relevant! It attracts people from so many different backgrounds and disciplines which could make for excellent discussion. However, oftentimes class felt more lecture–style, with Professor Bernstein probing for the correct answers to reading questions. It was the first anthropology class I have taken that felt like there was a correct answer/interpretation. Overall, it is not too strenuous and relatively interesting, but discussions definitely could be better.
