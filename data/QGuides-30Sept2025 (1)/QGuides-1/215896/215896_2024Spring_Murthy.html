This class isn't the most exciting, ground–breaking course out there, but it's a very solid introduction to AI and I would definitely still recommend it to anyone looking for a gentle introduction to neural networks, a bit of neuroscience, as well as the ethics behind AI. The assignments are not too time–intensive and I'm sure there will be some negative reviews about the midterm, but at least for me, it was a very fair exam. The midterm is open notes, and as long as you put in a bit of time to review the content, you will do well so just take it seriously.
Not an easy A, but not a hard one either. Prof Murthy is truly amazing and I love his lectures; it is a shame that people don't show him more love as he is such a sweet guy. The psets were a tad bit annoying but not hard. Final project is kinda annoying but the fact that they split it into parts means you won't be left with mound of work during finals.
The midterm will be harder than you think, so be sure to study! Overall an interesting class, but it can be hard to convince yourself to go to lecture. If you find the subject matter interesting though, there's no reason not to take it (especially for a gened requirement).
Only take this course if you're potentially interested in the subject and/or already have some experience in either neuroscience or machine learning. If you simply want to get done with the STEM GENED requirement, Im pretty sure there are better courses than this to fulfil the requirement. The course material can get a little hard conceptually at times so if you're not really interested in the subject it can seem like a task to get through the material and readings. I won't call it a gem since although the structure of the course allows you to get away with doing the bare minimum in terms of work, the concepts are not the easiest to wrap your head around. Murthy is a highly knowledgable professor and if you follow his lectures and actually pay attention to what he is saying, you will exit the course having learned a lot. However, that is not the case for the majority since his lectures can seem boring to many, again mainly due to the nature of concepts in this class. Grades–wise you'll end up doing just fine so don't worry about those. Overall, I would recommend this course to those who actually are intrigued by the course description and the prospect of combining neuroscience, machine learning, and AI ethics. For the rest, you will pass by getting a fine grade in this class, but won't really enjoy the grind.
