What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Dr. Roberts is great and she is super knowledgeable about the material. Good course to take if you are interested in clinical psychology or want to know more about mood disorders. It is very specific content and does not discuss anything else besides depression and bipolar disorder so make sure you are interested in those topics!
I learned so much in this class! Dr. Roberts is super nice, and she has so much clinical experience and stories to share. At some points, we were being lectured in an 8–person discussion environment, which became a little boring, but for the most part, the class was interesting. All of the content is attention–grabbing, and it is one of the most real–world–applicable classes I've taken both in the psych department and at Harvard in general.
Take this course! Dr. Roberts is a great teacher and highly engaged, and the small seminar format provides numerous opportunities for discussion and developing your own thoughts. It's also a great opportunity to read academic papers and research studies in the field of mood disorders (nearly all the readings are published papers, which you are expected to read and submit comments on ahead of class, so be prepared for this!) and to write your own academic paper reviewing some of the existing literature on a topic. This may sound intimidating, but it wasn't nearly as bad as I expected, and can be an enjoyable opportunity to explore the field on your own! I would strongly recommend this class to anyone with sufficient experience – taking PSY1900 or similar is helpful to understand the statistics in papers – and an interest in the topic.
Great class! You will learn so much about mood disorders, which is useful for everyone, and you will also engage in great conversations.
Take this seminar! Dr. Roberts is very good at sharing her clinical experience and you will benefit from learning from her and your classmates.
