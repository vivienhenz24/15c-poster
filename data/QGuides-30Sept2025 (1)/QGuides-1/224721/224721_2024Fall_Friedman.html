The class is fun when we do more hands on things. We get to take home a lot of things, such as the pot of mud that is still sitting on my window sill and even a climbing plant! However, I feel like the grading was a little iffy especially because it was either a 10, 5, or a 0 and there isn't really a clear explanation or feedback on how to consistently get 10s. For a freshman seminar, I believe that it was an unnecessary large amount of work. Also the course description is a little misleading(?) in a way that the course is like 10% science and 90% about Charles Darwin. Oh, not about his findings but about his life like his wife, childhood, letters, etc.
This seminar was so fun! I would definitely recommend this seminar to any students, regardless of concentration, who are passionate about nature. You get to engage in so many interesting activities, such as walks around the beautiful Arboretum, lab observations, seeing different exhibits at Harvard's Museum of Natural History, growing your own seeds and mud, a field trip to NEC, and many more (make sure to check the syllabus)! Professor Friedman is an incredibly warm–hearted, caring professor who continues to inspire me with his passion for the natural sciences. One thing to know, however, is that this class is more focused on Charles Darwin himself than evolutionary biology and could somewhat feel like a history of science course too. The workload is not the lightest, as the readings can be quite long and sometimes dense. But as someone who loves nature/evolutionary bio, I really enjoyed the readings, especially Darwin's On the Origin of Species. If you don't mind the weekly commute to/from the Arboretum, then I would definitely recommend taking this freshmen seminar!

This is an amazing first–year seminar!! Professor Friedman is a good soul who will work with you if needed and is very kind. Learning about Charles Darwin was fun and I loved learning the things he wrote about. The class also takes place in the beautiful Arnold Arboretum, which is very much worth the commute. I highly recommend this first–year seminar to any and everyone.
This class is far more interesting than it sounds.
Take this class!! Professor Friedman creates such a welcoming and encouraging learning environment for his students. The class is well taught, offers a deep–dive into the history of Darwin and his work, and does a fantastic job of incorporating hands–on content into the curriculum. If you are looking for field trips galore, enjoy discussions and thought–provoking questions, this class is perfect for you!
