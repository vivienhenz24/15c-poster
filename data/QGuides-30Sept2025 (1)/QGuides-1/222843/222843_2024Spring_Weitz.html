This class is definitely worth taking if you have even a casual interest in housing policy. That being said, if you are coming in with little experience or background knowledge, you will have to put in more effort than your peers to catch up, since the level of conversation and essay–writing can be quite advanced.
Take this course if you are passionate about social justice and equity. Housing is a topic I wish we discussed more in–depth at Harvard. Whether you are interested in urban planning, social work, or public policy, the insights gained from this course are invaluable for understanding the systemic issues at play.
A great introduction to housing policy. The reading list was very comprehensive, and having a class group that all felt interested in the subject matter went a long way.
Take this class!! Shanna is so great and makes the course super engaging especially since this is one of the only urban studies courses available to undergraduate students. I really enjoyed this course for my concentration requirement!!
This course was a great overview of housing policy in the U.S. Each week's class was slightly different in structure and I enjoyed the mix of activities we did.
This is a great class for learning about US housing policy! There is a lot of historical basis the course goes through and then extends to current problems like affordability. The course is chill and workload is reasonable.
This is an amazing class! You would not regret taking it! It's not a gem, but the readings and assignments are super interested. If you are interested in urban policies and housing policies, PLEASE TAKE THIS CLASS! Shanna is one of the best professors at Harvard, super accessible, provides great feedback, and offers a lot of flexibility and grace. This is my second course with her and would def recommend!
