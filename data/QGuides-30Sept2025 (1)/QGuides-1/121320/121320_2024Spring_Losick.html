MCB 208 is definitely one of the most interesting courses Ive taken thus far at Harvard, as it primarily revolves around teaching students how to give a formal science presentation. The collaborative, seminar–style setup of the course encourages conversation, as well as open and honest criticism between students to help improve speaking and presentation skills.

To add on, each weeks class is complemented by a free dinner, which is often either pizza or sandwiches from Tattes. When it came to instructor feedback, there were times where the advice they provided students did not seem the most reasonable or seemed out–of–line with modern day presentation styles (such as restricting presentations to all including a black background with white text). However, it is obvious that all feedback comes with a good intent, as Professors Lichtman, Losick, and Greenberg all appear extremely enthusiastic and happy to support students.

In my opinion, the only downside to this course relates back to the fact that letter grades are decided solely based on participation, which can be very subjective. At this moment, while Im assuming (and hoping) that I will get an A, there is no guarantee of this, and I will ultimately not know until the grade is published.

As a whole, given how MCB 208 comes with no problem sets, exams, or projects outside of the one presentation each student is required to present, this makes it a relatively relaxing class, even if taken as a fifth course. An outstanding choice for any student looking to improve their speaking skills!

I would like to tell future students (graduate students) to keep an open mind when going into this class. Although we know that we have our personal preferences when presenting science and we love to get crafty and artsy, it is important to take away some of the key lesson in scientific communication and that is to be as clear as possible when presenting. I also think that if this is your first science communication course, you will learn a LOT. But don't let this class be the bible and don't be shocked if you learn something different in different Harvard courses on how to give a great talk. Every class like this will always take shape in the way the Prof's that lead it think about how to give a great science talk. Also, be patient with those around you especially if they don't know basic jargon and scientific concepts! This class caters to students at both the undergraduate and graduate levels and from a variety of scientific fields which is the beauty of this course.

Lastly, don't take criticisms personally. They are there to train you to accept criticisms and think about them in your own time.

Okay, P.S. HAVE FUN! The class is chill, you get amazing food (sometimes from Flour bakery), and really friendly teaching staff.
