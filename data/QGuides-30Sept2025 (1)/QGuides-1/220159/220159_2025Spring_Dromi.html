This is a fun and interesting course. The professor excellently guides the class through concepts and discussions. The quizzes can be intimidating, but as long as you actually look at the readings you should be fine most of the time. The course staff are extremely caring, so even though the quiz format can be a bit difficult, it really won't affect your grade very much. I think the class is worth taking, it provides really interesting and helpful ways of understand real world media examples of how the social sciences help us understand our world.
Take this course! Gives you a good intro to sociology with engaging assignments. Meylin and Shai are great.
This was a good course overall. Readings were decent, although I would say the most interesting part of this course was the film selections that we watched in class. I was exposed to lots of new movies/tv shows which was particularly fun. Lecture and section could have been a bit more engaging, but weekly workload was manageable and assignments left a lot of room for flexibility and creativity and were mostly enjoyable to work on.
MY FAVORITE CLASS THIS SEMESTER – professor is awesome, content is genuinely interesting, and honestly I would describe this class as fun. Definitely would recommend.
A very fun and enjoyable sociology course! You read a ton of cool theory that you don't learn in traditional sociology courses and apply them to very interesting pop culture elements, including parts of your own life. Lectures are very engaging as you analyze pop culture content in real time and apply theories, so it becomes very easy to understand the material. You'll find that basically anything in life can be analyzed through a sociological lens, it's cool!

Sometimes a fair amount of weekly work doing readings and the occasional discussion post, but well worth it in my opinion.
This is a great course if you are interested in pop culture and media! I highly recommend taking it, although the readings can be heavy at times, but you learn so much. By applying the different concepts to real pieces of pop culture (some you may know), you start to see how these different phenomena impact everyday lives and what we consume. Be wary of the quizzes and blog posts though, it does add up
The way the class is taught is quite unqiue. We usually watch a movie or show in class and then apply sociological theory to it which I found fun. I also found out about a lot of cool movies through this class and I definetly think deeper/try to analyze what I watch more now. The readings felt like too much at times, but they were interesting. Also Shai and Meylin are really kind, open people–they are a great teaching team!
