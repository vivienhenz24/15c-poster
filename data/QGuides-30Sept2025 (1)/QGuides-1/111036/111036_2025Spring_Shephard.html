If you're reading this, you probably have to take this course; there aren't really any alternatives. Here are my thoughts on the course:

– The material is incredibly interesting, more so than STAT 110 in my opinion. This course is also much less crowded than STAT 110, so the office hours are less hectic. If you liked intro–level stats (e.g., AP Stats), you'll be able to prove/better understand some cool results you may have seen before. You'll also be introduced to new concepts that are really cool.
– Both professors are very passionate about the topic, and it shows through lecture. But I think the majority of the learning is done in section (since the TFs often explain things the best in m opinion).
– If you're wondering what background you need, I think it's a strong understanding of probability from STAT 110 and single–variable calculus (chain rule, product rule, and Taylor series). Also, a surprising amount of algebra shows up, so get comfortable with solving systems of equations, isolating variables, etc. (and don't forget your log rules)!
– Finally, this course is notorious for being difficult and time–consuming, but you'll be able to survive! Especially with the right approach.

Here's my advice on how to survive:

– Find a good section TF. I actually went to 2 sections most week because I felt like I didn't learn much in lecture.
– Find good OH, and keep a routine. Start early to avoid the Thursday rush.
– Find good p–set buddies. They can save your life, especially with the code. Related, it's a blessing that ChatGPT is allowed, but sometimes, it gets the code wrong, so be ready to debug and work through the process with some prompt–engineering.
– For the exams, do EVERY practice exam as that gives you the best sense of what to expect. They're hard, so don't be discouraged if you don't understand a question at first!

Wish you the best of luck with the course!

My background for context:
Applied Math concentrator and Stat secondary (hopefully)
Math 1A freshman fall; Stat 110, Math 21A sophomore fall, Math 21B sophomore spring
Math was my weakest subject in high school

My thoughts on Stat 111:
I found Stat 111 to be more approachable than 110. Don't get me wrong. The material is very difficult. Studying for the final was probably the most intellectually challenging experience I put myself through. My brain felt like exploding while taking the final.
However, on the daily/weekly, Stat 111 was easier to handle than 110 because it is structured exactly the same as 110. As a result, you don't have to go through the learning curve that you experience typically when taking the class of figuring out the best study methods/best ways to complete the psets. I also found the 4 problem psets to be more approachable than the 7 problem psets.

My advice on Stat 111:
Utilize office hours, and start the psets as soon as Joe drops the pset (Friday evening). Then go to Saturday and Sunday office hours, and by Monday, you will be almost done with your pset. The 111 TF team is amazing, just like the 110 TF team.

I wish I had also taken more time to understand the material conceptually more. It is easy to get lost in the numbers, and only understand the steps in executing to get the answers. That ended up hurting me in the long run for final studying.

If you are taking Stat 111 concurrently with Math 21B, I would highly NOT recommend this combination. As my learning was greatly impeded in Stat 111, balancing the 3 psets a week with 21B. I would highly encourage exploring the 22A/B series, although I know 22A is offered in the fall. I know my grade in BOTH of the courses would have been higher, if I took them alone, not with each other. This was the leading cause why my sophomore year was brutal and challenging. I'm grateful that I challenged myself but if I could go back and change my decisions, I would have taken 22A sophomore fall and 22B sophomore spring instead.
