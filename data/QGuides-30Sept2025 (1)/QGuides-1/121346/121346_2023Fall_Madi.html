What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Take this class as it will help you to learn so much Arabic is such a short time. I only noticed it when I spoke about unity for almost half an hour in Arabic at the end of the semester, without any preparation for this abstract topic. Also, you will get exposed to one of the dialects, Egyptian or Levantine, and you will be proud of yourself. Prof. Amr Madi is the kindest and funniest professor that I have met and he made this class such a beautiful experience for me and my colleagues. Also, you have to work hard in the class as it is a language class and language learning requires constant practice.
Along as they are willing to put in the work and love Arabic then they will be fine.
This class is still just as challenging as previous years, but the small class size really makes it worth it; it helps to participate better and learn much quicker. Ustaadh Amr Madi is also really kind and encouraging, all the while still pushing everyone to do their best in class. Overall, a good class to take if you want to learn a lot more vocabulary, keep practicing speaking and writing, and learn about more cultural and religious traditions and social movements in the Arab world (although the focus is definitely on Egypt and the Levant).
