I found this class fun, and not that much work, although I think the knowledge I gained was fairly narrow. The Wednesday activities are fun, and Prof. Wang and Kim obviously are experts and love the topics that are discussed. However, we often got into long sidetracks where everyone got mildly lost, especially when Prof. Wang was lecturing. I think there should have been more reading and discussion about Buddhism as a religion (how it is practiced, norms in the monastic community, etc), although I do respect that this is in the end an HAA architectural class.
This class is not necessarily a GEM. You will learn a lot about history of art and architecture but it is also a fair amount of work and preparation that needs to be done for quizzes for which you have to learn many new terms.
Interesting & of a course. The only weekly assignment is a very short reflection that is open ended. Lectures are interesting and you'll gain a deeper appreciation for Buddhist art and architecture. You're exposed to a bunch of mind–bendy ideas about the nature of reality and how Buddhists use physical spaces to convey these ideas.

There is a required section, which they take very seriously. However, it's really fun when you get to go to the art museum for a private viewing of several stunning prints and artifacts.
If you're looking for a cool gen ed that's a good combination of chill and fun, and have any interest at all in learning about Buddhism (even if you know nothing, like I did), take this class!! Professor Kim and Professor Wang are both awesome and super approachable, and so are the section leads Tori and Briana. There are weekly readings and short responses, so it's not a class you can fall asleep in, but I didn't find that the work was ever close to unmanageable either, even with taking two PSET classes and a language alongside it. I went into this class a bit nervous about what to expect, but coming out of it, I'm SO glad I took it.
Also, you'll go to the Harvard Art Museums a few times throughout the semester to look at art, so that's pretty epic, and if you have the time, I hugely recommend going on the optional field trip as well!
Good class if you're willing to put in the effort. Workload for me took anywhere between 4–6 hours, which can be a lot if you already have a dense course load. This class was not my background at all so I found that I had to put in some work to get readings and assignments done, especially since I didn't always understand them the first time around. Lecture / slides sometimes were confusing and I could not always refer to my notes later on since there was some disconnect between lecture and slides. Good class if you want to learn about Buddhism. Not if you don't have time.
