What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course is a great opportunity to hear from world–class speakers, predominantly physician–scientists from Harvard and MIT. It provides better context on the scientific and medical considerations in developing therapies for patients across a range of disease areas and therapeutic modalities. The workload is very light, with a short pre–class quiz on 2–3 papers once per week and one in–depth paper presentation during the semester done in conjunction with the speaker for that class. One limitation of the course is that each lecture is a stand–alone presentation, with the speaker getting full reign over what is presented, so the course does not build on particular learnings.
I really enjoyed this course! You get to read lots of interesting papers and engage with very impressive faculty. The presentation format means that you get to really get know the faculty that you present with for your week which is a great opportunity. I'm sure people in the class got by without deeply reading the papers, but it was a lot of fun to engage/discuss if you did.
