What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
GOATed Harvard Russian department strikes again.
If you're at this level of Russian you probably already know what to expect but Veronika and Liya are such wonderful teachers! I also really loved the final presentation for this course because we got to send a survey out to people in Russia and hear their opinions and it was super interesting. My Russian essay writing skills also got so much better in this class. If you're on the fence about whether or not to continue Russian just do it! It's a great community, teachers are very receptive to feedback, and you learn a LOT.
Ok, so this is what you expect it follows the standard Russian language track from what youre used to and in that sense you probably dont need to read the q at all. But I want to say this, perhaps because I think its important to realize the goals the pedagogy of the class is aiming to satisfy:

Ability to present in a professional controlled environment on select topics in Russian

Ability to write (albeit limitedly) in Russian on demand without mistakes in case and phrasing

Ability to write professionally in a controlled environment with a dictionary on select topics in Russian

If thats appealing to you or necessary, I think the class is good and will make better at those things if you take it seriously, and you only get out as much as you put in in those regards.

I wanted to become a better Russian speaker over the course of my Russian education at Harvard, and in this class I did improve in that from the simple fact that we did speak Russian every day. But the class has a variety of levels and I will say there was a lot of anxiety on my part going to the speaking days because of the weird question and answer aspect of it for whatever film we had that week which usually involved very weird things you probably couldnt watch in public.

That is to say, I think that the course could benefit a lot by building up everyones speaking ability a lot more and emphasizing this. At the end of the class during the presentation many people simply memorized or read directly from flash cards, and in section you will find many people who stammer to get their Russian out; I get it! Its hard and you have to warm up, but the environment focused around these movies and topics arent helping for functional fluency so much as they are for the highly limited vocabulary we learn. And when your goal is that level of functional fluency or maintaining that, the lack of speaking practice can be really frustrating.

In sum, dont expect to change dramatically as a speaker and section can sometimes feel limited or awkward since at this point functional fluency or like quick and rapid use of Russian is sometimes limited. And the vocab topics are kinda small and not frequently used in usual conversation.

Also the project, this was way too convoluted and crazy. I think an oral exam on a chosen topic of Russian where youre grilled on questions would be far better for students. These projects just seem like a huge waste of time when something like that can be both more challenging

But, I do think the class has a lot of strengths:

The teachers:

Veronika and Liya are both excellent and care about students. I think that they are both very kind and funny and supportive and Im privileged to have learned from them since theyre both so knowledgeable.

The students:

So fun and youll make lots of friends

Daily meeting:

Nuff said its the hallmark of the dept and for a good reason!

Course quizzes and exams:

Always extremely fair and fairly forgiving and they make you actually think on your feet for the writing which is good.

But yeah, as much as I love the Slavic department, I feel the need to write this to emphasize that I feel like speaking takes a back seat which is a tragedy since Russian is so fun to actually speak! And I think that the majority of the students want to speak better and listen better more than they want to be able to write essays and movie reviews. But maybe thats just me.
