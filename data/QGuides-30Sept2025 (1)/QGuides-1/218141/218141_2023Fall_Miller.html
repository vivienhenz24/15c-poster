TAKE THIS CLASS TAKE THIS CLASS TAKE THIS CLASS!!!!!! One of the best classes I've taken at Harvard (as a senior STEM concentrator essay–hater). Professor Miller is so special –– it was a privilege to be able to learn from him this semester. I loved the entire structure of the course. It was designed so thoughtfully and intentionally. It was wonderful to read plays and be able to talk/write about them, while also discussing the meta–question about how lists of plays are created in the first place. Assignments were the perfect (and rare!) balance of not being stressful while actually helping me learn and push my understanding further –– they were honestly fun to do and I got a lot out of them. I'm not an English concentrator, and was worried about taking an English class, and Professor Miller does an amazing job of focusing on the content of what we say, without expecting us to conform to specific departmental norms. Professor Miller's feedback is so kind and specific –– he would leave overall comments in addition to over a dozen in–line comments on detailed points. When reading his feedback, I could tell that he was actually reading my work and thinking about it –– I felt encouraged and also pushed to improve. I've rarely received feedback like this in college classes. I looked forward to class every week and am really sad that it's over. An amazing experience.
This is an incredible course. It teaches very clearly why we can't read "every play ever written" and instead shows us how to evaluate a canon and recognize the problems that come with our inability to. It gives us tools for comparing different forms of literature, and is a ton of fun. Professor Miller is an incredibly passionate, knowledgeable, and engaging teacher who people rave about, and for good reason. I loved taking this course with him, and he found the balance of challenging us with the material without ever feeling like I was overwhelmed. The pace of the class is the right type of fast, and I learned so much. Definitely definitely definitely take this course.
I really had limited knowledge of theatre coming into this course, and I feel like I learned so much about theatre history (and the limitations of what we can know about it). I enjoyed that the assignments were very specific and relevant to course discussions! Class discussion boards were lively, and I always looked forward to hearing Professor Millers and my peers thoughts on the readings each week.
If this class is offered again, you should absolutely take it! It is so incredible!!! If you are at all interested in theater, this course provides you with a comprehensive cultural history. The readings each week were a bit long, but so worth it. I was always so excited to do the work for this class. Also, Dr. Miller is an incredible teacher, and knows so much. Cannot recommend this class enough!! TAKE IT!
TAKE THIS CLASS. Derek is hands down one of the best professors here. He's so enthusiastic about theater and drama that even if you're new to the theater world, he makes you excited to study plays. It's also a great introduction to how theater works, and is interesting on a literary and historical level. I loved actually reading the plays, and you actually get to learn how the industry works (and how it's changed) in a great environment.
If you're interested in drama at all, this is a great class to take! Even if you're interested in history and want to evaluate it through a new medium, this is a cool class because of the diversity of plays/wide time–range it covers. Professor Miller is engaging and discussions never lulled.
This class is a low workload and a great introduction to theater but it is not really a literature class. So just know that it is much more about history than it is about the actual plays.
