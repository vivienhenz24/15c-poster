This is a really cool class to take if you are interested in politics and the K–12 school system.
I highly recommend this class if you are passionate about education! The course provides an extensive amount of sources regarding different and interesting topics. You will be led by Professor Paul Peterson, who has been extensively involved in American education for many years. A unique part of the class is you get to interact with very well–known public figures in the education field, like Alberto M. Carvalho, Michelle Rhee, etc. In addition, it allows one to see the broad view of problems in the education system and how research is utilized to figure out a possible solution. Be aware that this class does require some data science knowledge but not extensive. So definitely come prepared for that and having Gov50 experience will help for the class project. It sounds daunting but it is doable. Don't let that discourage you!
If you're interested in american education and public policy, take this class! I loved prof. peterson so so much and he is one of the most experienced and credible researchers in the field. A wealth of knowledge about politics and the educational system.
There's a quiz at the beginning of each class where we don't have a guest but if you watch the videos you'll be fine. There are little to no assignments throughout the semester until finals where you have a presentation, a paper, AND and exam ––– altogether worth 60% of your grade. If you're someone who prefers a more spread workload and lighter finals this class is not for you. The overall content of the class is interesting, but it is very discussion heavy with every class pretty much being a presentation and roundtable discussion. If you're passionate about education I recommend this class and I think it was worth it overall, but definitely a challenge.
BEST class ever. Professor Peterson is one of the top experts in American education policy, so if you are even slightly interested in learning about any aspect of education, take this class. Some of the most famous leaders in the education field are brought in as guest speakers throughout the semester as well, so you will always be informed by the most knowledgeable people in education out there. This is the first class I've had here where I believe the flipped–classroom model works, and the discussions are always very interesting. I highly recommend taking advantage of the opportunity to take this class.
This class was hard but 100000000% worth it. In the methods section you will learn everything about regression discontinuity, instrumental variables, and difference in differences analysis, while in class you will become extremely well versed in the benefits of teacher merit pay and the court cases that played a role in voucher programs' existence today. I learned SO MUCH about American education through the blend of course materials, from research projects to guest speakers. This class was very difficult but very well worth it and I would do it again. They should change the name though –– "The Politics of American Education" does not capture the subject matter at all! How about "Statistical Methods in American Education" or "Quantitatively Analyzing American Education Policy." These titles really reflect all the research papers we read (and wrote!) as well as the way our professor and TF tried to get us to think about policy issues.

Overall, LOVED IT!!!
This is a GREAT class for people who are in the middle of Gov and Econ as fields and can appreciate the use of quantitative methods on policy issues found in Gov. The course does an excellent job in providing an overview about education policy and attracts some interesting people from all different backgrounds. The professor does a good job of facilitating discussion and providing his own background on the topics, many of which he has directly had a role in developing the literature around. In class reading quizzes can become tedious but overall the workload is light for the returns you get on content. While this class doesn't have any hard prereqs, some background in statistics or econometrics is really helpful in interpreting some of the papers presented in the class.
