This is a really great course if you want to get a broad but detailed understanding of international institutions and their functions. Having taken the course, I feel like I am able to understand international discourse at a much deeper level, as this course provides great theoretical understanding of important IR features from institutional design to formal/informal power. There is a lot of information to retain in the course, and so make sure to be fully engaged throughout the semester and keep up with the readings.
The readings of this course are more informative than the lectures so don't skip the readings. Also you basically are only required to do the readings and nothing else the whole semester so try not to lack on those otherwise there is no point in taking this class. You will definitely learn new concepts in the class and through the readings but if you are interested in pursuing international relations, then this won't be the most life changing experience.
definitely take this class and opt for the final paper option! you have the flexibility to apply what you've learned about international law in any way you'd like.
