This was honestly a bit of a boring course at times. Prof. Lee and Betts are really nice and there are some really good/genuine people in the class. But I think our discussions sometimes felt a bit circular, and we kept returning back to the same themes without much variation ("archival erasures" is a phrase we kept talking about over and over again). Even though Prof. Lee gives really good feedback throughout the assignments, I also feel like grading in the class wasn't the clearest to me throughout the semester (I would never know what grade I'd get for a discussion post, and I'd get really different grades across the board for them). The final museum project was great and I really enjoyed researching my items, but it's still a bit of a mixed bag. (Tho would def consider this class for the ability to look firsthand at archival materials!)
This course is not something to be missed. Taught alongside the first ever exhibition on Asian American women at Schlesinger, there is so much energy and salience in the library where this course takes place. Each week, we have riveting discussions and look through many documents, photos, files, reflecting on the voices of many women we never hear about in Asian American history. Co–taught by Prof. Lee and Victor Betts, this has been one of the most magical parts of my undergraduate education and I am so grateful to have been part of an embedded course at Schlesinger. We read theory and writings from Black women archivists, reflected on community–based archives, and brought Schlesinger's collections to life through interactions with the exhibition and the public. Take this class!!
The selection of texts and archival materials for this class is extremely intentional. Every part of the course is thought through, organized, and deliberate; there is so much to learn from every little element in the syllabus. I think this course will only continue to get better if it was already taught this seamlessly in its first semester of offering.

As an aside, I will say that the concurrent opening of the Asian American women's exhibit at the Schlesinger Library during the course improved the class experience greatly. Not sure how the class will change, or if it will be offered at all, when the exhibit is no longer open.
TAKE THIS COURSE! Professor Lee is so knowledgable and kind. Victor Betts was also amazing! You will get so much out of this class and see so many cool archival items.
This class is great I was a fan of Erika Lee before class, but an even bigger fan at this point. I'm so grateful she also brought in a curator at Schlesinger Library, Victor Betts, to teach this course as well. I learned so much about the challenges and discourse around the archive, and can easily talk about that with anyone at this point. I loved the final project and the incredible opportunities Erika would drop for the class. This is a class like no other at Harvard, and I was so lucky to take it.
