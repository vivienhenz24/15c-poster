MCB 63 was a class that I entered with unrealistic expectations due to the Q–Report feedback from the year prior. If you read the Fall, 2022 Q–Report for this course, you will likely notice that many students describe MCB 63 as being a very easy course and a gem of a class, with one even saying that it is near impossible to not get an A. However, this is only partially true, with it being highly dependent on your prior experience within the field of Biology, Chemistry, and Biochemistry.

At its core, most of the students that are taking MCB 63 have either (1) already taken CHEM 27, another Biochemistry–focused class, or (2) already taken or are in the process of preparing to take the MCAT. As a result, many students enter this course with some form of background on the subject. For students that do not have this background, MCB 63 will be significantly harder, and you will likely feel way behind your peers. In the syllabus, since SGAs, which are basically reminiscent of PIEs from LS1a/LS1b are only worth 30% of our course grade, it may seem really easy. However, the SGAs are very difficult at times, with some not even matching up with the list of objectives we were provided with beforehand. It is very easy to perform poorly on an SGA, as they are graded out of just 30 points (so each mistake costs a lot!) Additionally, with them occurring bi–weekly, it makes it seem like MCB 63 is basically always absorbing your time and causing you stress. It gets especially bad for the final two, which are lined up back to back and cover 6 lectures worth of material at once.

Additionally, please know that there are NO GRADE CUTOFFS SET for the course. According to a student from the previous year, they ended with a ~94.5% but still received an A– in the course. For those that are very wary about their GPAs, please take this into consideration as we are unsure how final grades are decided. At the time of writing this, I still have no clue what I will be ending with in the class.

In terms of the class itself, the topics are definitely interesting, but it is very difficult to tell what is important to know/memorize, especially since Professor Viel can get very detailed in his explanations. As a result, it is easy to lose sight of the bigger picture, or to become lost within the minute details of each metabolic pathway. Additionally, he is also relatively soft–spoken, which can make it difficult to understand him sometimes, especially during lecture. However, the content provided is definitely very interesting, as long as you enjoy biology and chemistry! Professor Viel does a good job of tying back what we learn to different aspects of human life, especially in terms of medicine.

Overall, if you are reading this, please just know, MCB 63 is GOING to be difficult if you do not have this type of established background or have not had any exposure to the content. Take the other positive reviews with a grain of salt, making sure you understand the background that many students enter the course with. If you decide to enroll, work hard and good luck!
