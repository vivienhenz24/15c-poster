Comments
This course is amazing. The community service component is obviously the main draw of this course, and it truly is special. Many students in the class had such positive experiences in their community organizations that they have chosen to continue their volunteer work after the end of the semester. Your community organization is not only a great opportunity to connect with the Boston community, but it's also an excellent space to practice your Spanish in a more realistic, day–to–day context. Also, I thought the service requirement would take a lot of time out of my weeks, but as long as you choose a location that's relatively close to Harvard commute–wise, you will be fine.
TAKE THIS COURSE!!! Professor Quinonez is AMAZING and I got so much out of the volunteering portion. I was worried about the weekly time commitment for volunteering at the beginning of the semester, but I found myself actively excited to go and very much enjoyed my time with my organization. Cannot recommend this class enough, especially if Profe Quinonez is teaching it!
Such an amazing course! Great material, discussion, and service opportunity. Glenda is the absolute best, most considerate, caring, and enthusiastic professor I've had at Harvard!
Please take this! I would love to see more students have the rewarding experience that I did.
Be prepared to have to watch a ton of movies –– like one a week –– on top of a long commute to volunteering, and other readings. In general, the classwork is not super challenging. and Prof Quinonez is incredibly sweet and uplifting. The volunteering component is the main attraction and is worth doing if you want to explore what nonprofit work looks like on the ground, or explore what it means to be a strong, vibrant, and successful community organization that responds to local needs and changes your communities for the better.
