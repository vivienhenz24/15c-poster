You should take this class, the volunteering component was especially great!
Take this class for a great opportunity to get outside the Harvard bubble and gain valuable experience working with community service organizations in Boston and slightly outside of Boston (Chelsea). I improved my Spanish speaking/listening skills and helped some people along the way. I also learned about the United States' involvement with South America and the experience of Latinx immigrants (both on the way here and the lives they live and hope to live while here).
The actual class part of this class is mediocre. The materials and discussions are okay, and your ability to communicate in Spanish will only improve slightly because of the assignments. The best part of this class was the outside volunteer work, where you have the ability to practice communicating in person in Spanish and the opportunity to give back to others. If you want to take this class, take it for the outside volunteer work and don't expect to enjoy any other part of the class too much.
The volunteering aspect of this class is such a great way to interact with the community and make a difference, and it's also an incredible way to practice Spanish outside of the classroom, as teachers in the department tend to speak a bit more formally than high schoolers in Brighton haha. I really recommend you take this. It's the best class I've taken from Spanish 10 to Spanish 59.
Take this class! The opportunity to volunteer in the Boston community is incredible and transformative. It is also one of the best ways to learn and improve your Spanish.

TAKE THIS CLASS!! It is super wholesome and I loved being able to connect with the local community. Profe Parra is super understanding of students' schedules outside of the classroom. The assignments and homework is very doable and enjoyable. She does a phenomenal job of fostering an inclusive and welcoming enviroment. I loved get to know my peers, practice my spanish, and feel like I was making a true impact through my service work. If you get the opportunity, definetely consider teaching English to Guatemala kids through EDELAC!! It was an amazing experience and it was sooo wholesome to see their eyes light up when concepts start to click in their minds. You will not regret taking this class and it will be a nice breath of fresh air in your schedule!

That being said, the teacher has a passion for the class and cares for the education and furthering of the students, and the class does an effective job giving a wide scope from which to look at the state of spanish–speaking communities in the USA.
