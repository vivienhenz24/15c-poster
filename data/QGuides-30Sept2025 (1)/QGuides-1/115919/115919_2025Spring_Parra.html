What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class is excellent. Combines a critical focus of learning about the Latinx community in the US with actually working with the community! It's a lot of work every week (in terms of readings and written assignments), but it's not impossible to do, you just have to spend the time to do it. You will gain a lot.
The volunteer aspect is what makes this class really special.
One of my fav classes here. Such a unique and incredible opportunity to be able to apply your Spanish knowledge to community service context. There were a lot of petty assignments sometimes in addition to the community service, so I didn't like the class component as much, but it was still such an amazing class.
Take this class!! Be prepared to be challenged, to grow as a human being, and form lasting relationships with those around you. You should know that Spanish 59 is a very time–consuming course. Between three hours of volunteering a week (for me, this was really five hours including the commute time) and several hours of preparatory coursework (1–2 documentaries and several readings a week), it can sometimes feel like you are actually taking two separate classes at once. All that said, the workload is certainly not a reason to skip out on taking this class. If you know what you are getting into, manage your time, and schedule your semester well, you are set to have a fantastic experience. Profe Fajardo is an incredible professor who cares deeply about her students, their backgrounds, and their lives, and she facilitates active and engaging conversations with ease, even when some of the course materials (documentaries/readings) are a bit entry–level or bland. But more than the in–class experience, what makes Spanish 59 special is a structured opportunity to be immersed in community work in the Boston and Cambridge area. I worked with La Colaborativa in their Economic Development department, and I enjoyed the work so much that I hope to return as a volunteer next year.
