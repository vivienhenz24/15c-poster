What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Kate and Joachim are incredibly nice and knowledgeable people in their fields. They introduced a great plethora of people to the class from various sectors in the museum and artistic industries. Do take this course if you're interested in museums and museum studies. Depending on what the project of that year is, you will probably have a different experience of what the final project might look like. I do wish that we included more perspectives from the Global South, which might be really illuminating.
**This class is extremely friendly to students of all backgrounds, so don't be discouraged from taking it if you've never been in an art class before! Workload is extremely manageable, and by the end of the semester I had researched an object that will go on display soon in the Harvard Art Museum. Highly recommend if you have any interest in better understanding the art world!
This was an incredible course, made even better by the amazing professors behind it. Their support, passion, and guidance made the experience truly meaningful. The final project felt especially impactful since our research directly contributed to an upcoming exhibit
This course is a great way to learn more about art and museums in a very accessible, comprehensive and enjoyable way. This course was a highlight of my semester and I enthusiastically recommend it to everyone who has always wanted to learn more about the art world, yet didn't get an opportunity to do so.
I would highly recommend this class to any student! You will gain a deep understanding of museum practices in a wonderful learning environment. You will learn about ethics, collecting & acquisition, provenance, repatriation, art materials, conservation, curation, and specific art objects. Kate and Joachim are absolutely extraordinary – they are wonderful people, educators, and practitioners. If you are fortunate enough to take it, the class will be a highlight of your time at Harvard!
This class is truly lovely –– engaging, thoughtful, and inspiring. The professors are great: supportive and passionate. Youll come away with new ways of thinking about museums, objects, and the stories we tell through them.
Take this class (please please please remember to apply on time – it is popular and hard to get in but if you demonstrate your own interesting perspectives you stand a great shot!)!
This class had about 5–6 undergrads and the rest (8) were grad students across HAA, HKS, HLS, and the Divinity School. Everyone brought such interesting ideas and perspectives to every class.
Joachim and Kate are incredible professors and you will gain a totally new appreciation for art and art museums by learning everything from art dealers to provenance to restoration and everything in between.
The weekly assignments and readings aren't too long but to get the most out of the course you should do them. The major project of the course is a 10 page paper on an object you pick (that will be going into an exhibit – our kunstkamer objects go on display in July 2025, more on that later) and it's super interesting.
On the objects, this was mainly about Dutch 16–17th century art. Since our collection is going up I'm not sure what the focus will be but it's worth reaching out and asking because if you aren't at all interested in the theme you won't get as much out of the course as someone who is – like we all became experts on Dutch 16–17th century art.
All in all, my favorite course at Harvard and my only Arts and Humanities course and what an incredible way to finish up college.
This course is so much fun and the Professors are so kind and knowledgeable. I really enjoyed this course and look forward to taking more courses with the HAA dept. next year!
