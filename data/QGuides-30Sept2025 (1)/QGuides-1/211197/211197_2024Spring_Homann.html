Comments
Incredible class––one of the best I've taken at Harvard. Lovely professors, mostly interesting readings, cool trips and guest speakers, and very interesting, approachable work.
wonderful professors! very engaging, approachable, and understanding; small class size allows for good discussions; interesting "crash course" into museum practices; unique opportunities!
This course is so, so much fun: there is a field trip and a painting session! Joachim and Kate are clear experts in their fields of curation and conservation respectively, and they are incredibly kind as well. The course invites a lot of discussion about the role of the museum in society today, and you get to hear from the amazing staff in the Harvard Art Museums. The curation and exhibition design component of the class is really interesting, and it's exciting work for an undergraduate. I do think that the scope and process of the exhibition could have been defined and outlined more clearly, as it sort of became a parallel project along with the rest of the course's materials and assignments at the end of the semester. This class definitely changed the way that I think about museums, and has made me want to consider a career in the space. Take it!
TAKE THIS CLASS! Although the course number might look scary, you definitely do not have to be a graduate (or art history) student to take this course. Joachim and Kate are wonderful people to work with from the Harvard Art Museums, and you get to learn more about so many different aspects of the museum including digital efforts, exhibition design, and of course curation. Although the course description didn't make it explicit, I was at first a little surprised that the curation topic was extremely specific (17th century Dutch Kunstkammer), in the end I think I benefitted from learning about a topic I would otherwise never have delved so deeply into. Class meetings can feel quite long, but they're really fun that I never minded! Would recommend for anyone with an interest in the museum world.
Do everything in your power to take this class!! It has been by far one of the most rewarding courses of my academic experience. Kate and Joachim are beyond wonderful and inspiring; and the material itself is really quite fascinating. Every week we were doing something different – whether this was traveling to a private collector's home, seeing the conservation process up close, roaming the museum galleries, and so, so much more. We also had the opportunity to interact with many of the museum staff from different departments, which was incredibly exciting. The readings themselves were so thoughtfully curated and interesting, which made discussions genuinely enjoyable and engaging. I have never felt this lucky to take a class – you won't regret it!
