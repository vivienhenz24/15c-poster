What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I would say to think about your goals in learning from this course because it is a lot of material and at times it is too technical to be relevant.
Fantastic course and overview of many systems, with really engaging guest lecturers. The focus on discussion questions and in–class collaboration is a great component of the class.
This class has really great guest lecturers and the pathophysiology you learn is very useful and applicable to research!
The workload for this course is manageable, but nontrivial. Be prepared to devote some time each day to quiz questions, etc.
Great opportunity to experience clinical cases and learn about the function of organ systems and how they can go wrong.
This class, while rewarding, is a lot of work. This class is a flipped classroom, so you will have to watch a 1 hour lecture + quiz before attending a 1.5 hour discussion section twice a week. Some in class discussions were fun and interesting, but they often felt redundant with the assigned homework. Every week, you will also have a tutorial section, in which you either prepare a presentation on a clinical case for a small group to discuss, a 15 min presentation on the background of the disease, or a 10 min presentation on a novel research topic.

This is an interesting class, but definitely a marathon. Maybe I'm just out of practice as a graduate student, but this was a ton of work.
