This is a great class in many ways. Seymur really cares about students and clearly wants them to succeed. He is accessible to meet with, and generous with accommodations/extensions/etc. The classes aren't always super clearly taught and the midterms are genuinely very hard for many people, but the grading seems to work out for most in the end. Labs are also fun––it's cool to build a robot as a team but just make sure you don't fall behind. Start Psets well in advance and make sure you get help––the teaching staff will really make the class as painless as possible.
This class is geniunely so fulfilling. Make sure you focus on teamwork for the robot!
Take it especially if you love hands–on machining and tinkering––if so, you'll find comfort in the makerspace. EdDiscussion is so helpful, for everything from Psets to CADding because Professor Seymur responds insanely quickly. Lab and lecture feel like 2 different classes so ES51 did sometimes feel like the coursework for 2 classes, where the labs felt like they were worth 1.5 classes and lecture seemed like half a class. Also, Psets and classwork and CAD at the beginning will feel very overwhelming but all of those of the lecture part of ES51 calms down pretty quickly so just stick around. Overall though, if you do love manufacturing, the excitement from that will get you through the class. Best professor, CAs, and classmates!
Great class. You're going to have to learn some of the material on your own, but still very fun and worth taking, especially if you're interested in engineering.
Structuring and starting this class early is very important. Your group for lab can make or break your experience, so recognizing and realizing what your group dynamic is, is support important, as well as communication with the teaching staff. The staff truly care a lot and are wonderful to work with, however this class is very difficult if you don't understand concepts early on.
This class can be very useful in introducing you to different parts of the engineering design cycle, specifically, creating an idea, designing, manufacturing, and reiterating. You'll gain experience with different manufacturing processes like laser engraving, CNC milling, silicone molding, 3D printing, using a lathe, drilling, and using a bandsaw. I'd say it was fun.
There are a lot of little things to stay on top of, which can easily run out from underneath you. That being said, no matter how done with this class you get, you will leave with so many new skills which you will carry for life. The project takes up way more time than you will initially expect, but if your team works together collaboratively and fairly, it will probably be one of your favorite experiences in school.

Seymur is a lovely guy, and you can always tell he is on your side and wants you to to your best. That said, his lectures will get very confusing, and you will probably leave at least half of them having learned nothing. Lab will be frustrating too – there are really just a lot of frustrating components to this class. I'm still confident that you will leave it very glad you took it, though.
I learned so much in this course. Being able to fabricate your own parts and create your own robot is so much fun and super empowering. If you are someone who loves working with their hands and building things, this is the class for you. Also, you will gain a super solid foundation using Solidworks/CAD which is super helpful for Mechanical/Product Design. This course has made me a better problem solver, machinist, and engineer.
Good class. If you're taking it, you probably have to.
You will probably need to do some learning through alternative research like YouTube or online because Seymur isn't the best lecturer and doesn't really answer questions well. The overall course is great to get an overall understanding of engineering though. Make sure to brush up on physics topics as the second half of the class is mostly physics based. Do your Psets as they are very similar to the midterms.
Lab is a big time commitment but easily the best part of the course. Turf Wars is so fun. Lecture is not great and Seymur struggles to explain certain more complex topics. Great guy though – very approachable, helpful, etc.
very fun take it with a light heart – its not that hard
You learn a lot in this class; however, you should be aware that this class takes up ALOT of time, especially during the week leading up to competition. The psets can take a while to complete because of the CAD component. But Professor Seymur is great.
I would only take this class if you plan to be a mechE concentrator. The course has useful content and materials (CAD, design, hands–on assembly, machining, simple engineering physics), but I think the course is currently not nearly as good as it could/should be. It's pretty unorganized, and the lectures are not very useful. I personally had to teach myself most of the material, which isn't too terrible because the content is not very difficult, but it was suboptimal. The lectures also had a bunch of obscure content that I'm pretty sure we did not need to know, but we were told we needed to know everything from all the lectures+p–sets+labs for the midterms, which was very confusing. Also, be prepared to spend a ton of time in the lab towards the end of the semester to finish the final robot project. The class also generally consumes a bunch of time because there are p–sets, labs (where you need to write at least four pages in your notebook each week), and two midterms. Overall, the class was pretty useful but not very enjoyable.
