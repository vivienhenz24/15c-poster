Whether you study Gov or not, take this class if you're interested in authoritarianism! You should keep in mind that the emphasis of the class is on the strategies that authoritarian regimes can use to stay in power. It's NOT a class about the history of authoritarian regimes, nor does it cover discussions of authoritarianism in political philosophy (Machiavelli, Hannah Arendt, etc.). Instead, it draws heavily on comparative politics and political economyso if you're interested in these fields, you should have an easy time in this class. But even if you don't, this class will teach you how to understand (and maybe even respond to) contemporary authoritarian regimes. The assignments are very manageable so long as you plan ahead of time. If you do your homework on the first few parts of the Case Report, then you won't have to put in much additional work for the Final Report. And you don't really have to do 100% of the readingsas long as you focus on what's discussed in lecture and pay attention in section, you'll be golden.
