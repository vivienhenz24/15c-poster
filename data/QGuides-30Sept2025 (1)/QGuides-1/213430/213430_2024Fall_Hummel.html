Would recommend!! Professor Hummel is amazing at what she does. She is an engaging lecturer that is highly knowledgeable on the topic! She also clearly understands the readings she assigns and is very helpful with any questions about the content or assignments. This was a great introductory government course, even for first–year students.

However, my TF, in particular was not the most experienced. This meant that sections were not as enjoyable, and the grading could be more arbitrary. All work is graded by the TF, and sometimes points are deduced for very minor details (that you weren't even aware was expected of you). For example, all the assignment instructions say you can cite your sources in any preferred manner (as long as it is consistent); though, my TF took away points for not doing it the way he desires. This is just one example of how it seems like they were going out of their way to find things wrong with your assignment, even though it wasn't made clear that it was what was expected of you.

Again, I would reiterate that I still enjoyed this class. Professor Hummel is still iconic + a great professor. In contrast to the TF I had, she understood what she was asking of her students and provided meaningful help during office hours. Overall, this class challenges you to think more deeply about global affairs and authoritarian regimes (in a manageable way).
This class is great if you're interested in learning more about authoritarianism and political models, and is totally accessible if you've never taken a gov class before. The workload is manageable as long as you stay on top of readings and space out your assignments, and the grading is very fair
I have mixed feelings about this class. It justifies itself by being very interesting, with fairly engaging lectures, and not too time consuming. There are some truly great insights to be gleaned from the lectures, which will be incredibly useful in your understanding of not just authoritarian regimes, but government in general.

The assignments feel a bit silly at times. The academic paper about which you write an essay may reject a particular hypothesis but you may nonetheless get asked to write your essay as though that hypothesis had not been rejected. It's often fine because all of the writing assignments center around a case study which you chose, so presumably you'll find it interesting nonetheless, but it was the starkest feeling of middle–school style nonsense assignment that needs to be completed as dictated because those are the rules even if not well grounded that I've had in college.
Great gov class. Would strongly recommend for a secondary requirement (or concentration). Very engaging, interesting, and not too too much work. Midterm is very manageable, and the writing is less than it seems (coming from someone who doesn't like writing).
Great course, great professor, strongly recommend!

This is one of the best gov classes I have taken at Harvard (and I have taken many). It is extremely engaging and covers interesting topics. It made me engage in class a lot because I enjoyed it so much and would even read a lot about the topics outside of class. Professor Hummel is absolutely amazing and very knowledgeable. She does a great job in incorporating interesting readings, some short videos in class, and captures a wide array of different views on each topic. The assignments were honestly so fun that I had a hard time sticking to the page limit because there was so much to explore. You get to dive deeper into a dictatorship and learn a lot based on your own interests in this class which is awesome! My TF Shady was also an asset to this class. Sections were extremely engaging and hearing about his personal experience was a huge benefit. Please please don't hesitate to take this amazing course!
