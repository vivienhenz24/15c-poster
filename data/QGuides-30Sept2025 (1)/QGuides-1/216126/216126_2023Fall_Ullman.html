I'd highly recommend this course to anyone interested in the imagination or looking for a higher level psychology course with a relatively chill workload. Professor Ullman is a great professor, and he really wants students to learn about topics that they are interested in. The discussions can be super interesting–– definitely one of the classes where you get out of it what you put into it. The final paper is an excellent chance to explore a specific topic as well!
For sure take PSY1340. Tomer does an excellent job providing us with a variety of readings to get us thinking about how exactly we see or think about imagination as a practice and a psychology concept. I came into this class thinking I had some sort of idea what imagination was, and every single time I left I was reminded that I had no idea what was going on. Even though I felt behind and confused 90% of the time though, I felt like it was a really comfortable space to discuss with my peers and I never felt anxious to say "I have no idea what is going on," which was one of the best parts of the seminar set–up. I also feel as though I was able to hear about other people's thoughts in a way that wasn't argumentative, but contemplative. Aside from creating a warm, comfortable environment, I also really appreciated Tomer pushing back on our ideas and thoughts, because it really made me think about what I believed and exposed me to new information about imagination.
