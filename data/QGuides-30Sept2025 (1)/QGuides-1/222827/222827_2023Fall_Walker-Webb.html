What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course has the potential to be amazing. I don't know if this is the best year to judge how it's going to be in the future since Stevie had an unprecedentedly busy year, so he wasn't present for some weeks of the class. If this course changes in the future to focus more on smaller directing pieces rather than group projects, and you get to direct in real time in class while receiving feedback, this will be a great class. Stevie is a great person to learn from.
I would ask to meet with Stevie before hand and make sure he is committed to teaching the class. He wasn't present for most of our class time and was not at all responsive with emails and being clear about what it is we have to do.
Stevie is a genius, but the course had many structural issues (ex. not everyone in the class got a chance to direct, even though it's a directing class), and Stevie missed many classes which impaired the pedagogy of the class. I think that more experienced directors would get more out of this class, just because there is very little covering of the basics, and you are just thrown into directing projects.
If you simply want a space to work on an idea you're passionate about with little outside influence then this course could work for you. If you're looking for more of a directorial education (technique, history, business insight) then another course may be better suited.
Do not take this course. The professor shows up occasionally but not frequently. The lack of structure is very frustrating. Half of our class didn't get the opportunity to direct anything and were just actors in plays that other people directed. Certainly a misleading course title and description. The result was lackluster.
