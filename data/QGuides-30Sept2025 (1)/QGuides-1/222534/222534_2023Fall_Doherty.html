What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
TAKE THIS CLASS! Especially if you're thinking of going into longform journalism of any sort. Maggie is such a wonderful discussion leader and has so much expertise on art and literary criticism, as well as what it's like to be a working writer. If you're someone who consumes a lot of culture in any form, this class will also be SO MUCH FUN. Reading criticism, both the assigned readings and your classmates' work, not only teaches you about the act of writing criticism but is also a great opportunity to learn about a ton of new art and literature.
I would recommend students to keep an open mind about their final longform criticism, since what I ended up with was not what I thought I was going to write when I entered the class.
Fantastic experience! If you're interested in writing about art in any form, this is the course for you. Maggie was fantastic, the syllabus was very well structured, the workload was manageable, and the readings were super interesting. I grew a lot as a writer from this course.
Take the class if you're at all interested in writing about art, personally or professionally. You will be in good hands––Maggie is a great instructor––and might even come out of the experience with publishable work.
If you want to be a critic, this is the class to take. You learn a lot of useful formal strategies, but this is really not the class to learn how to write good prose, so if you're not confident in your writing in general, probably start with something else. Maggie has real world experience that's probably just as valuable as the syllabus in terms of understanding what participating in criticism actually entails. And the readings are always interesting, even if you don't agree. Actually do the assignments on time, or there's pretty much no point.
If you are interested in discussing and writing about art, this class is an excellent forum for that. The final assignment gives you a very flexible opportunity to dive deeply into the art form of your choice. I enjoyed our class discussions and community.
