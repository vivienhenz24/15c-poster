TAKE THIS COURSE!!! The professor is super nice and the course is amazing if you want to get into the wearable device field and don't really know how to start. It is a little fast paced until the midterm but as long as you go into office hours it's not too bad.
This class is great! It covers so many important topics in just a single class, giving students exposure to many different areas of the wearable assistive devices field as a whole. The second half of this class allows students to explore the state–of–the–art in the field, discussing research papers and even performing experiments of your own.
GREAT super interesting fun class, not the easiest by any means. Brush up on your physics. Accept that most things will be confusing. More worked examples would've been really helpful. Section would have been helpful. But nonetheless, what a cool class!!! and Patrick was AMAZING!
Overall this class was great if you want an intro into assistive devices. It has some logistical problems, homeworks were too difficult compared to course content and communication couldve been better, but the energy behind this course is really what matters for future years. Patrick is super smart and really interesting to talk to, he definitely brings a lot of energy to the subject.
Take this course! Professor Slade is great and the course teaches you a lot, especially if you are unfamiliar with biomechanics.
Patrick, Chelsea, and Haedo are some of the kindest people you will meet. They are always willing to make time to answer questions and genuinely care about the well–being and engagement of all the students. Work is spread out a bit unevenly week to week––some weeks there is nothing due, while others there are several assignments due, so it is important to spread your work out so you are not scrambling to finish several things at once. The homework is also a bit difficult to complete without office hours, so do your best to show up. Knowing physics better probably would have helped me in the course, but the lectures were fun and I truly learned a lot about biomechanics and the assistive devices out in the world today!
Really great and fun. Go to office hours
This is an amazing course. Patrick is one of the best professors I have had at Harvard––so caring and kind while being incredibly knowledgeable about the material. Be aware that you will need physics knowledge for this class, and just a bit of linear algebra. The homeworks are brutal ( longer than they seem and very hard) since they don't totally align with the course material either. These ended up being the most time consuming part of the course. Would have liked for the project to be a little bit longer than just the end of the semester.
The professor is absolutely wonderful. I have never been tempted to skip class. They're always interesting. The assignments are all very reasonable, some just being tutorials for a biomechanics simulation software and some going over the content of the class. The final projects can be time consuming if you want them but don't have to be as long as you put in a reasonable effort based off of the things learned in class. Definitely take this class.
Take this class if you want to learn about biomechanics and applications of mechanical bioengineering. The course material was not too difficult and the final project at the end of the semester was amazing and very fun to work on and apply what I've learned through the class.
Course was extremely interesting and the lectures were amazing. I learned a lot from the class, but in its first year, definitely did leave a lot to be desired. If you are just at all interested in learning how the body moves and how assistive devices are made, then this class is made for you. However, you must have a relatively strong physics background to succeed in the course. Class was great, and with this year's feedback, can only get better.
