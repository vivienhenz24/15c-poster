The class was so interesting, I am so grateful for the experience! The readings were well chosen, and my peers had so many insightful things to add to the conversation. Professor Rothschild is brilliant and so approachable, and Salmaan was an excellent and helpful TF. The lengthy primary source paper was a challenge for me, but I learned a lot in the process!
Prof. Rothschild is a delight to learn from. She has a wealth of knowledge and loves sharing it. If you have any hesitance about taking an economic theory course or a history seminar, banish it â€” working with Prof. Rothschild is well worth it.
Reading the syllabus and looking at the course title, it's easy to envision this class as being the most amazing historical review of economic history. Truthfully, it's a pretty standard history course, and you get out what you put in. If you do all the readings, it's definitely worth it. If you're the type of person to slack on readings, you'll take away very little.
The history of markets and states is a hefty topic to tackle, but the twelve weeks of classes will effectively guide you across a wide range of historical topics and ideas. The readings and discussions were incredible and thought–provoking, and the final paper (if daunting at first) proved a satisfying opportunity for exciting research. Professor Rothschild and Salmaan are amazing, of course, but my classmates brought quite a lot to the table as well: the final few classes are devoted to topics chosen and presented by students, and everyone's discussions of a topic of personal interest (often influenced by personal experience and knowledge) added extra character to a great class. Would highly recommend!
Take this course! Prof. Rothschild and Salmaan were both incredible! You will learn a ton from the presentations & discussions during the semester, and the research paper is a truly rewarding experience (especially if this is your first history course)!
This is a MUST–TAKE class at Harvard. If you are a history or economics concentrator, knowing the history and relationship of the market and state and how economic thought has changed over time is truly helpful in understanding theories. Even if you are not a concentrator in these two areas, this class is extremely welcoming. For those who struggle with understanding theory and abstract concepts, the readings and weekly presentations by students alongside projects truly help you visualize/understand what is covered in the first half of the seminar. Note that this is like any other history seminar where students present each week. The teaching staff is very helpful and accessible; go to Salmaan and Professor Rothschild's office hours to discuss your final paper or any confusion you have on the course material or just to chat. Professor Rothschild is an amazing professor, and she is also great at explaining concepts/answering any questions you may have (she also adds humor during her lectures). Do take this course! You will not regret it!
This course was interesting and uniquely different from all my other courses this semester. I learned more about the history of economic thought than expected.

I would have liked to see more participation among peers in the class. Also, I may not agree with all the perspectives of theories covered, but I can now much better grasp and thoughtfully discuss them after this historical journey analyzing the progression of economic ideas within their original situational contexts.
