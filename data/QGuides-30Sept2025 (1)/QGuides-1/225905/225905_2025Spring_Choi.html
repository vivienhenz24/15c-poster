You will not regret taking this course! The course materials do not take a lot of your time. They are completely manageable. There are not many assignments to complete, but you should spend a good amount of time on the final project. The project can be creative and fun and is entirely up to you with what you want to explore. The small classroom environment makes the class feel more intimate and welcoming when you engage in classroom discussion. Not many courses at Harvard allow you to explore Korean literature and culture in as much depth as this one.
I absolutely loved this class! The readings and films were each so interesting, and I really enjoyed thinking about history and culture from the perspective of literature and film. Prof. Choi is so kind and creates such a warm environment in the classroom despite the heavy topics that the course covers.
This class is one of the best courses I've ever taken in Harvard –– and I don't say this lightly. I've had the privilege of taking many Korean–related courses and this one tops them all. Not only does Professor Choi genuinely care about her students, our wellbeing, and our goals and aspirations, she considers what we may be wanting to learn more about. I never had the chance to learn more about Korean queer culture other than this class, and she did an amazing job weaving together such an intricate history into easily digestible and very fun modules. I always looked forward to coming to this class, and I would always walk out knowing more than I did before. Professor Choi always made us feel so comfortable talking about difficult topics in class, and her attention to detail is astounding. She is always prompt with replying back to emails, and takes time out of her day to ensure we are all on track with projects and assignments. I always felt like she cared about me as a student. She also is somewhat of a magician in that she is able to bring incredible speakers into the class and procure materials that we would not otherwise have access to as a student (such as private screenings) which just goes to show how much effort she puts into this class behind–the–scenes to ensure we are getting the most out of the materials. I don't know how she balanced teaching 3 courses this semester, but despite it all she still made us feel special as a class. I really enjoyed taking this class, and I'm sad that I won't be able to take more since I'm a graduating senior, but to whoever is reading this, I hope YOU get the chance to take this class and that it comes back next semester.
This is an incredible class, and Professor Choi is a wonderful professor. She really has everyone's learning and success as a foremost priority, and you feel very guided through the course material. I think Harvard is better because this course is offered.
TAKE THIS CLASS!! The professor is super knowledgeable and sweet, and most importantly genuinely wants each and every one of her students to succeed. I learned about a broad range of events that have affected Korean society and history from multiple perspectives. This class pushes you to recognize more difficult/less spotlighted aspects of Korea, which broadens your own perspective on culture, history, and society at large.
As an East Asian Studies concentrator, I have struggled to find area courses that focus on Korea (my specific interest). The Korean studies department and community are very small here at Harvard, so it was lovely to see a course like this available, especially as it deals with literature and media. The course was scoping, reviewing materials from many decades of Korean history into the contemporary era, which I also enjoyed. I loved that we read texts by marginalized voices as well (e.g., women, victims of colonization, immigrants), since we rarely are able to learn about such topics in the context of Korea.
Please take this class! Professor Choi is highly organized and passionate not only about her course but also about her students. I was always so excited to come to class every week and discuss the various readings/films with my peers. The class is organized in a way that exposes you to a wide range of topics, history, and media, but it is structured through open discussions that make it more relevant and interesting to everyone, resulting in high student engagement. Assignments are very fair and rely heavily on your own growth and understanding, which develops throughout the semester. Also, great snacks every class!! TAKE THIS CLASS!
If you don't enroll in this course, you will regret it!
This is a true gem. Professor Choi is a little strict in terms of technology use and deadlines, but she is very very kind and understanding. You become a family with the class.
I would let them know that this class is a great way to learn more about Korea through various media such as film, readings, and discussion, and anyone who is interested in Korean media should take this course as a way of better understanding all the Korean culture and media that is currently on the rise in the world.
