What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
it's pretty rare for a class to teach the entirety of an author's works in one semester. this is one of those classes where it is done with great success. if you are interested at all in catullus, you won't be disappointed.
I absolutely loved this course and cannot recommend it enough if you are taking Latin. The course content was super interesting and Professor Philbrick was incredibly engaging and accessible. I would definitely say that this course is a step up in terms of expectations and difficulty from the intermediate Latin courses, so there is a bit of a learning curve over the first couple weeks, but it is super rewarding to work with the course content at a higher level because of it. Office hours will absolutely be your friend. Make sure to start studying for the midterms well in advance (over a week) and to really review all the content before it because they will expect a lot in terms of ability to translate. Every word counts, so make sure to thoroughly review vocabulary and start building a list of new words at the beginning of the semester.
Take this class, especially if it'll be your first 100–level Latin class! Professor Philbrick is a great teacher, and the class is very well–organized. The reading is manageable (starts at around 50–60 lines per class and increases to about 100), and I appreciated the class discussions on grammar, literary constructions, and interpretations of Catullus!
Dr. Philbrick is an incredible and kind teacher. She is very good at structuring the class and changing the amount of work to fit its students' needs and requests. I highly recommend taking this course if you are interested in Latin and/or want to read Catullus.
This is a fantastic class! Dr. Philbrick is wonderful, the discussions in class were great, and I felt like each class I would learn something new about the language or meter or culture.
