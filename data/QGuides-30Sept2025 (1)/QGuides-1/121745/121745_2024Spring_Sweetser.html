What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If you are interested in genetics, take this course because you can learn many rare diseases and pathology. If you are interested in medicine and science research, take this course because listening to patients' stories will definitely empower you on your journey. If you are interested in science in general, take this course because the professors and classmates all fostered an active and open–minded environment for learning and discussion.
This is a really great class and I would highly recommend anyone interested in seeing the connection between research and medicine to take the course. The course had a good mix of undergrads and grad students with different background so it was interesting to see how the knowledge base combined. The best part of the course is being able to get the perspective of patients who have the genetic disease we are learning about. Not only is it truly inspiring to hear these patients tell their story, but it is also such a great way to get context for how lab findings and new drugs/new treatments can really change lives. Overall, I highly recommend the course!
Take this class! It is pretty light in workload, but is extremely interesting! You learn a lot from top researchers in the field as well as clinicians and patients that have first–hand experience with dealing with the different diseases. For pre–meds, I would say that this class is especially valuable in understanding not only the research and basic biology behind different diseases and drug development, but also the more human side and impact of the disease.
Definitely take this class! You get to learn about many different genetic diseases and interact with patients as well as leaders in research on the diseases!
This is a phenomenal class that would greatly inspire you as a physician and/or scientist! Although it is a 3 hour class on a Friday afternoon, and travel is required from Longwood to MGH, Dr. Sweetser kindly provides reimbursement for the way there. Every class is on a different genetic disease topic, with expert speakers, a journal club, and an invited patient speaking about their experience. Grades are made up of attendance and one journal club presentation. Dr. Sweetser genuinely cares about students learning and engaging in the material and the patient stories are incredibly inspiring. Highly recommend!
