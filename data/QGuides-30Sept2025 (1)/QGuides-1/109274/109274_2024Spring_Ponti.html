As a whole, this class is quite engaging, growth oriented, and taught by people who are passionate helping students learn. The STAR system eliminated a lot of the pressure associated with traditional exams (multiple attempts until you get it right), and labs were definitely more centered around learning and getting as far as you can rather than getting each answer perfectly. I looked forward to the demos in lecture as well as the creative setups in lab!

With that being said, something about the structure of the assignments and lectures in this class just never clicked in my head. Lectures are primarily geared towards applied examples of mechanical concepts rather than the concepts themselves, which made them confusing to generalize and apply to examples outside of the ones discussed. As a result, it was hard to even know where to start on the PSETs without going to help rooms (which varied a lot in usefulness depending on which day you went), and I was often left confused as to how I would've ever been able to get the answers myself. The coding portion of the class is also quite difficult without prior experience, as there's not enough time to go through all of the content before it begins appearing on exams.

Overall, the vibe is very much that of an intro STEM course where everyone's kinda stumbling through and trying their best, but the good news is that the teaching staff are very willing to give you help and explain topics to you 1 on 1 that you don't understand, and the grading isn't very harsh.

Assuming that everyone in this course is taking it because they have to:

1) Find a reliable study group before it's the night before the PSET's due and you're left hanging by a thread (in terms of both the PSET and your sanity)
2) Start your work early, and make full use of course resources and office hours
3) Take time before exams to plan which STAR's you want to test out of each attempt, and make good use of your cheat sheet space

tldr: if you're an engineer and want to spend an entire semester thinking like one, you will love this class and thrive. if not, you might be better off going to 15a instead.
