What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Absolutely take this course! Professor Marglin is a revolutionary thinker and will change your mind on contemporary economic issues and economic theory. Prepare to challenge your preconceptions of economic theory and don't hesitate to ask questions! Professor Marglin is a preeminent political economy theorist and creates a classroom atmosphere of intellectual curiosity and innovative economic theory based on economic evidence. If you have ever felt that most economics classes are solely skewed to proselytize a conservative and libertarian theory of economics, then this class is for you! Professor Marglin will provide a quantitatively–grounded basis for the plurality of economic theory.

If you enjoy macroeconomics and have any doubts about libertarian economics, then I highly recommend this course. It is very low stress, engaging, and a gem.
I think this is a great course if you are interested in learning about alternative ways of thinking about economic models. Professor Marglin is perhaps the most qualified person to teach this course, he is a lovely person and contributes to a nice class environment.
This is a wonderful class that teaches you to think critically about mainstream economics. I absolutely recommend with enthusiasm, professor Marglin is incredible and cares so much about his students and the content.
Provides an excellent alternative to mainstream economics complete with understandable instruction and materials. Good differing perspective and a good course selection for economics concentrators.
This course completely transformed the way I view my economics education at Harvard. I learned how think freely, challenge mainstream economic doctrine I previously held as gospel, and for lack of of better way of explaining it, I feel like a real economist now. Professor Marglin is an absolute gem of a human being and very well may be the best professor at this school. Enjoy his hand–picked apples and go raise some Cain!
Prof Marglin is amazing, and he brings apples to class. You need to be really interested in the material to enjoy it, otherwise it will feel quite dry as its 95% macro theory. If thats you cup of tea, youll enjoy it. I did.
He has good intentions with the course and I hope it improves in the coming years to be more friendly for undergrads (it's pretty complex and hard to follow). Hes extremely intelligent and his passion for teaching his findings from his book is exciting. The course felt a little more like a graduate level course since it lacked support in many ways. Also, 2 hour lectures without any breaks is challenging to follow. Everyone doses off pretty quickly and sometimes people fall asleep...
Highly highly highly recommend taking this class out of all the econ classes I've taken so far at Harvard, this has been the most interesting. It really makes you question what you're taught in Ec 10/1010. The class in general has a mix of theory, economic history, policy implications, etc. At times, the theory can get quite heavy, but don't let that scare you away and just focus on the logic and big picture arguments.
This is a great way to get an alternative perspective on the economy probably the only way to do so at Harvard. However, it is very self–driven, so only take it if you have the time to do the readings and go to office hours.
