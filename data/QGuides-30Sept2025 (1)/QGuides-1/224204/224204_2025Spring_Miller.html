The material and assessments are approachable, and the course staff proactively support students in this course. Professor Miller is extremely knowledgeable on this subject, though his lectures occasionally strayed off topic or delved into details that might not have been particularly relevant to the course. If you are looking for a course that is more narrowly history focused, this might not be the right course for you, but if you're looking for an approachable history course that is more "applied," this is a great course. To put it succinctly, this course might be more comparable to a GenEd relative to other courses from the history department. This course may be less beneficial to you if you have a lot of technical or historical knowledge on energy to begin with.
I must say this is one of the best courses I have taken throughout my time at Harvard! The content is carefully curated to give a very comprehensive picture of historical energy transitions in the US, UK, China and beyond. Yet, it still successfully delivers rather individualized narratives of such transitions, such as labor conditions for female workers, etc. Professor Miller is truly the best professor you could ever ask for. He teaches with a true passion for the subject at every single lecture and makes himself available. It's very rare to find such a professor here, and you would greatly appreciate that. Take this course!

TAKE THIS COURSE! While certainly not easy, this course was incredibly interesting, and I feel like it completely transformed my understanding of how the world works. As a graduating senior, I am so glad I took this course as I feel like I am SO much more informed about the world and how EVERYTHING works and is powered. So many people today are interested in green energy and climate change, but this course is an absolutely essential foundation to understanding the challenges and difficulties of the problems humanity faces. The first half of the semester has very few assignments, so the second half gets a little busy, but it's nothing unmanageable, and if you go to lecture (which you should because it's super interesting) you will not struggle to find things to write about. You will not regret taking course, and I cannot recommend it enough!!
Professor Miller is a great lecturer who is clearly very passionate about the material. He tries to make the course as interactive as possible, leading to great engagement from the class. Take this class! Professor Miller and Sanjay were both so supportive and provided such good feedback every step of the way. I came into this never studying history or knowing anything about energy, and I think it was such a good class to get a primer on both of those things!
