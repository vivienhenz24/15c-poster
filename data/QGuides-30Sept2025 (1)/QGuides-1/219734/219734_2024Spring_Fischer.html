Absolutely worth taking, Rebecca is very passionate and brings a lot of excitement to the classroom but lecture at times can feel like you're just drinking from a firehose. There is a bunch of stuff you just need to memorize; there are very few processes or concepts you learn that are applicable for a variety of questions, it's just knowing that mineral X has Y structure and forms at Z depth in the crust, across a couple dozen minerals. Once you do have that memorized though you can impress people when you're out on walks!
This class is content–heavy (study well in advance for the final!) but rewarding. There are cool field trips, interesting labs where you get to look at mineral samples, and the teaching team is reasonable and clearly passionate about the material. Some of the content is really surprising and interesting (eg, experiments that use the synchrotron).
I loved this class! Rebecca is so clearly and outwardly passionate about the subject matter that it creates a fun and energetic learning environment. The lectures were also focused heavily on Q&A, which sparked interesting discussion that deepened my knowledge of the subject matter. I also liked the gradient of starting with the basic building blocks to learning about individual minerals and moving up to learning about deep Earth and planetary petrology. This structure gave me an appreciation for each step and made learning the material satisfying and fun. The class overall was a blast, the field trip is so, so much fun, and the teaching staff is wonderful. If you are looking for a class on minerals, this is the best option!
This class is phenomenal. It has a great teaching staff and gives a great overview on numerous minerals and rocks. Exams require a lot of memorization, but as long as you pay attention in class and prepare adequately (don't wait until the night before to study) it is very doable.
This was one of my favorite courses because I love collecting rocks/minerals and wanted to learn more about them. You learn a lot of fascinating things about crystal structures, identifying minerals, the deep earth, and planetary objects. However, as with anything, you should choose a course that you want to take because it fascinates you, since each person's level of interest will be different. If you have a soft spot for rocks/minerals, this is the place to be.
To the rock nerds out there, I would definitely recommend this class – I like pretty stones, and I'm glad I know more about them now. I think you do need to bring in some level of prior interest or familiarity to enjoy the class, because it gets into the weeds very quickly and it's easy to lose yourself in the tall grass. But the assignments are not so bad and the exams were approachable even with a minimal level of paying attention in class and studying.
