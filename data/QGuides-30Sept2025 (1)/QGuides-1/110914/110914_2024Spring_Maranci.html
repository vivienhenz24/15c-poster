What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I really enjoyed this course. It's a great sample of so many different topics but you get to hear from different professors every week that are leading their field, so experts essentially. I really liked that we covered so much but were able to leave with themes and broad takeaways about the region and if you take it as a sophomore you'll meet professors and be introduced to topics you may want to explore in the rest of your time at Harvard.
This class is not a ton of work. Attendance and participation matters so show up (ready to listen at the bare minimum). It is exciting to learn from different professors in their areas of study. There are weekly reflections which are not difficult, but just tedious.
This is a really great course. The teaching staff are incredibly kind and encouraging. Class discussions are very open. I learned a lot from this course and I would recommend to anyone who is interested in learning more about the Middle East!
The lectures were too disconnected. Every week was a new professor talking about a new field. While some of them made the effort to give us an introductory primer to their field, many of them talked about something way too specific and you don't know what you're supposed to take away from it. For example, you pull up to class and hear a lecture on Yiddish literature by looking at a specific Yiddish play. Next class you're talking about Politics in the Middle East. Like?? I'm just not sure what we were expected to take away from specific classes like those which did not provide any background. Would not recommend this class unless you have to take it.
This course is ok. Only take it if you really want diverse knowledge that does not dig deeply into any subject matter
