I would recommend this class as a GENED if you are at all interested in climate or the environment and want to dive into more economics or philosophy on the topic. It is HEAVILY economics and philosophy–based. Lectures are mostly talking and drab but helpful for completing the writing assignments. Writing assignments are fair and a great way to explore philosophical writing .
This class will make you feel super pessimistic about the state of climate change. However, I would nonetheless strongly recommend it. The writing assignments were mostly reading comprehension based. If you have some experience in debate and/or Model UN, sections are sometimes reminiscent of that energy in terms of intellectual thought but without the competitive / cut throat nature of those actual extracurriculars. Overall, the content was super interesting, the work load wasn't too significant, and I took the course pass/fail so wasn't actively trying to do well but still got good grades on the assignments.
TAKE THIS COURSE!!! Super interesting. Super straightforward. Taught me how to think more critically about climate topics. You will honestly learn so much, and the class is super chill. Definitely one of the better GenEds.
This is an interesting, manageable, and engaging course!
While the content of this class is sometimes depressing, it is interesting and relatively straightforward. The writing assignments are fair (4 assignments, 4 pages each, answering a set of questions), and the readings are also fair. It's not going to be the most crazy interesting life–changing class you take at Harvard, but it was interesting enough to keep me engaged during lectures and section. Prof. Stanczyk was a great and engaging lecturer; I liked how he incorporated current climate news into the beginning of each lecture. He also brought his dachshund Nina to lecture every other week and let her run around the lecture hall, which definitely made my class experience significantly better.
If you're interested in climate change, TAKE THIS COURSE! Professor Stanczyk is a very passionate and knowledgeable lecturer! Previous q reports have expressed that obtaining an A on the assignments is not possible. THIS IS WRONG. Depending on your TF, you can get an A on the writing assignments as long as you attend office hours and ask for feedback!!! If you're not that interested in the topic of climate change, you may find a few lectures boring or repetitive. Also, be sure to do the readings. All of the graded assignments solely rely on specific readings and it can seem like a lot if you put them off for too long.
Lectures can be a bit repetitive / boring at times, some readings are really interesting. Engage with your section leaders and it'll be more fun. Assignments were not difficult, but did force you to think which is good. Generally the content doesn't feel particularly surprising, and you'll really only get to deeply engage during section (and only if your classmates want to lol)
Its not the most interesting course, but youll learn a couple cool things. Grading is weird, but worth taking for P/F
Would absolutely recommend to people interested in working on climate policy or in climate science. I also would recommend to anyone who is interested in public policy more generally, as many of the topics we cover have applications outside of the narrow scope of climate policy.
A great gened! A gem even. I think the topics in this course were incredibly, incredibly interesting. The readings are a bit long to me but I'm also a stem concenrator lol. Also the assignments are very simple and straightforward (basically short answer responses)!
This class is interesting, makes you think about the different ways to tackle the problem of climate change. Be warned: You have four essays for this course, four pages each. The grading is really hard.
This course is amazing and the TF Isaijah is absolutely amazing!!! If he is not your section leader, make sure he is. If you are interested in the topic, take this course.
I really enjoyed this course, which I was worried about since the Q Guide score wasn't the best. I learned a lot about the various considerations and current situation about climate change and its severity, as well as various interventions that are being considered (or could be). The work felt manageable, and it seemed like the papers had clear expectations for grading, which was nice. The class made me realize, more clearly, the dire straits our world faces right now, and has made me more attuned to climate change efforts, and potentially working to help combat it.
Great class and perfect for a semester for a heavy workload! Class is highly relevant to today's issues, although can sometimes feel a bit depressing due to the lack of action about climate change (we are learning why it's so hard to legislate climate policy). No exams, only 4 short papers and a group exercise during section!
Don't take this class unless you already have a deep background in climate science and enjoy diving deep into philosophical discussions.
Do the readings and engage with Professor Stanczyk! The class has some really interesting things going on, but you have to do your part to get them. unfortunately Rachael Goodyer is no longer a TF of this course, what a great TF who went above and beyond to teach the class, but still good class just hope that you get a good TF to walk you through any questions you may have!
This is a great course, whether you're looking for a gem (which I found this class to be, at least for an Ethics and Civics GenEd), or you're looking to learn more about the topic of climate change. Lectures and readings were pretty interesting, and the assignments took little time to do and were very clear and well–written (2 group assignments you do in section + 4 written assignments, each one is a 4–page essay where you read a text related to the current module and understand it, then you answer a few questions about it – mostly trying to draw strong arguments for and against it). Sections are a pretty great space for saying whatever is on your mind regarding the topic of discussion. Overall pretty great gened.
Really fascinating subject matter and sections always pose really interesting questions. Writing the paper assignments themselves really helped me think creatively about these different arguments, but BE WARNED the grading is quite quite harsh, especially for a gened. You will need to put a lot of time into these papers and frankly the papers do not provide students with enough flexibility with its pretty small word count to ideate at the depth that the TFs expect. There are many comments about elaborating on points or missing certain features of the argument, but when I'm already a few words away from being very over the page limit, this seems unavoidable. Overall though, the learning experience itself is quite fun and valuable!

This class was very interesting! It was one of those great classes that has very manageable work but I feel like I learned and gained a lot. Great for if you are interested in learning more about the moral dilemmas surrounding climate change and Stanczyk is an awesome, engaging professor.
