A really great and well–taught class! Mitrano is incredible, and so are Hiro and Daniel. Demos during class were super engaging, and the lab component was surprisingly fun (you get to spend 4 weeks on a final project of your choice). This class made me fall back in love with physics after the super dry 15a and 15b classes.
I thoroughly enjoyed this class! The material is covered at a pace that is both engaging and manageable, the demonstrations are incredibly insightful, and the course staff is wonderful! You can tell that they really care about students understanding of the material above anything else. Professor Mitrano is a great lecturer, and his unlimited time format for midterms felt like a much better reflection of how well we understand the material than timed conditions.
This was a great course and definitely helped me understand the world around us better. The lectures are a bit math heavy, so do not actually worry about the derivations or things, just understand how the professor got to the resulting equation. Do practice problems as this is the only way in which you will begin to really understand how to put all the concepts together.
I definitely recommend taking this class if Mitrano is teaching it, with Hiro as a TF. Mitrano is a phenomenal lecturer, and he is very easy to follow during lectures, especially since waves are not an easy concept to understand. Hiro is a phenomenal TF and great at teaching during sections, which are super helpful. This is a great class that will take some time, but it is nothing too heavy.
The final project of this class is also super interesting, depending on the direction you choose (its free range, btw). It also isn't a huge time commitment outside of scheduled labs, so the difficulty is not too extreme. Overall, I would recommend this class.
Definitely take this class. You can gain a lot out of it, especially if you are looking for a class that is comprehensive in nature.
Take this class! We at Harvard are incredibly lucky to have an entire class just dedicated to waves because they are so fundamental in further physics topics. Understanding the concepts in 15c feels so cool because they have so many applications in other physics fields and beyond. Prof. Mitrano is an amazing professor and go to his office hours whenever you can!
This is easily the best of the 15c series. I found it to be extremely interesting and assignments/exams were fair. Problem sets were hard but office hours and section were very helpful. Lab was much more interesting than the other 15 series classes, particularly with the independent project. Professor Mitrano and Hiro did a great job leading the course and they both conveyed a clear love for the subject. The inclusion of regular experimental examples during lecture were fantastic!
Pretty straightforward 15C course! Very supportive teaching staff who will answer any of your questions. Midterms and final are pretty chill. Psets are pretty doable. No textbook, but there are many resources provided you could turn to.
15c with Prof. Mitrano is a nice class! Concepts can sometimes be difficult to wrap your head around, especially if this is the first class in waves you take. But Mitrano and Hiro are super friendly and always willing to help you fill any gaps in your understanding, as they really want you to succeed. Definitely recommend going to their office hours to ask questions or just to get to know them!
Make sure you are very comfortable with linear algebra and differential equations. Some of the math on the problem sets can be very tedious and difficult.
Really good course!!! Prof Mitrano and Hiro are AMAZING. They really want you to succeed, and are always there to support you. They ask for and take feedback really well. The course content is very interesting, truly just waves in so many different forms. Morin's textbook notes are very helpful for psets and section is really useful. The psets are a bonding experience, and office hours are game changing. The tests are way easier than the psets, so don't stress about your grade as long as you're willing to put in time for the psets. Mitrano will tell you about his lab which is very cool and the teaching staff all take the time to get to know you. The 15c lab is lowkey very long and somewhat boring, but the class makes it worth it and the lab is a req.
If you're interested in learning about waves and how they permeate every aspect of nature, take this course!
The material was interesting, applicable in many areas, and demonstrated how wave physics is a part of several if not all branches of physics. It was very organized, had good communication about deadlines and exam dates, timely and reasonable grading, and several resources for students to take notes and reinforce understanding. I've taken several of the other physics courses, and this is one of the best–taught and most organized I've encountered, and interesting and relevant.
Professor Mitrano was kind, attentive, and invested in making it an effective and enjoyable course, and our TF was helpful and cared very much about doing the best job he could do (Hiro is our hero).
Overall, this was a great course that did exactly what it was supposed to doteach wave physics in a rigorous yet approachable manner, and help students have a positive experience with physics.
