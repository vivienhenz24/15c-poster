THINK BEFORE TO TAKE THIS CLASS
Math 25B, like Math 25A, is challenging. Psets can take long (Pset #2 is infamous for 20 hours) and midterm/final are hard. However, like Math 25A, it was a very rewarding class. I have matured so much in my mathematical journey. We covered point–set topology, traditional real analysis (proofs of integration, differentiation), and even some differential geometry at the end and now I feel ready to take some more advanced math classes. A background in proofs and linear algebra is sufficient, but prior exposure to multi var calc was useful for me but definitely not necessary. Chris was amazing and the CAs are all very smart and willing to help. One piece of advice: don't fall behind in this class. It's hard to catch up. I suggest doing the textbook reading before class, go to class, and then review class notes and material before starting psets. This was you can triply reinforce the material.
The fact that this class has an in–class midterm and final in addition to relatively time–consuming problem sets means that it's a pretty big commitment. A lot of the material in 25B is not very intuitive (at least for me, especially in the second half of the course) and so is more difficult to process, especially in the big picture.
I would highly recommend this course! It is a lot of work and fairly fast–paced, but really rewarding. The material covered is super interesting and provides a great introduction to Analysis. I think really differently about calculus and generally the real numbers after taking this class, and that is due in large part to how well–taught the course is and how fascinating the topics that we covered were. This course also has such a tight–knit community formed through working on the psets and going to office hours!
An important thing to keep in mind is that a lot of reviews for this class are very instructor–dependent. Sadly, Chris is leaving Harvard this year so look at who the instructor is teaching this class before making your ultimate decision.

With that said, this class changed how I thought. Coming in with little proof–based experience (taking 22a the prior semester), this class fundamentally altered how I thought about mathematics and logic. Math 25a takes your intuition from calculus, rips it apart, and puts it back together, all while leaving you with a greater understanding than a computational–based class could ever provide.

The first third of the class––dealing with point–set topology––will seem a little dry, and at times very abstract and difficult to think about, but it will be worth it once you reach the second third of the class: sequences, series, differentiation, and integration. From there, the last part of the class feels a little less interesting––moving into sequences of functions and ultimately multivariable calculus (of which you will pick up notions from differential geometry along the way).

However, because of how much this class augments your proof–writing skills and builds mathematical intuition, I cannot recommend this class enough. This class will be hard––some weeks, you will spend 20+ hours on a PSET and it will be mentally exhausting. However, it will all be worth it. I strongly urge anyone on the fence between 22b and 25b to take the latter––it will change your brain!!!
This review should be considered generally meaningless for prospective students since, to my understanding, Chris Eur will no longer be teaching this class in the future. For prospective students, please look for reviews from previous years in the event that previous instructors like Wes Cain are teaching 25 again.

This class covers metric–set topology, sequences and series (for numbers in chapter 3 and for functions in chapter 7), continuity, differentiation (both single variable and multivariate), and differentiation (in single variable, multivariate, and with its coverage of differential forms and rudimentary exterior algebra). Depth of coverage is pretty satisfactory for each of the topics, with each of the topics getting 1–3 weeks worth of coverage in the course.

Unlike many other students, I felt that 25b was indeed easier than 25a, but this is probably because algebra makes no sense to me and is the bane of my existence. 25b combines several different mathematical areas to achieve what it wantstopology, analysis, algebraand I thought was pretty neat. 25b also did not have any KEY, centralizing theorems (in the way that 25a had with rank nullity, jordan form, and spectral theorem), instead working with many smaller theorems/definitions in tandem to articulate its theorems (this might be why people think its harder?). Homework was definitely harder in 25b compared to 25a, albeit shorter in length (my overleaf documents were a lot longer in 25a). Moreover, Chris did a great job toning down the difficulty of exams25b midterm/final felt MUCH more fair than either of the 25a exams.
Math 25B can be a challenging experience, but is definitely worth the effort. It is intended for students with a strong background and interest in mathematics, and will offer you an ample overview of real analysis, and multilinear calculus, with a glimpse of topology. Familiarity with linear algebra (perhaps from 25A) will help you get through the last month of the class. I would strongly recommend you to form a PSet group (perhaps you already have one from 25A), as homework can be long (on average 15+ hrs), and challenging.
I would hesitate before taking this class. If you're really passionate about math, and you find it beautiful, then I'm sure you'd enjoy the class. However, this class was a fair bit harder than 25A, especially if you're not as experienced with math.
While it is a useful class, the homework is challenging, and Rudin is dense. I would recommend supplementing with another, more readable textbook.
The 55ers will try to tell you that 25b is harder and less fun than 55b. This is a LIE! It is (for many) harder content than 25a, but there is so much support from the teaching staff and everyone wants you to succeed. I felt much more engaged and intellectually challenged by 25b than 25a. The community is great but also chill, and everyone has varied concentrations/interests. TAKE THIS CLASS!
If Chris is teaching it, take it. He's a fantastic dude, and he's hella funny (he gets the whole squad laughing like every single class many times)
