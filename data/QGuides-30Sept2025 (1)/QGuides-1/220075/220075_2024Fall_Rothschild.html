This is one of my favorite classes I've taken at Harvard. Of course, you have to be willing to put in the work to write an essay every week. However, because you're writing so much, you will get an excellent opportunity to hone your authorial abilities and develop your writerly voice. I wish I had taken this course earlier, so that I could have used topics that I explored in this class for other papers or explorations.
Please take this class if you can! I took it as an elective and had no prior background, but thought it was fantastic. It does a great job of exploring the effects of climate change in terms of the impact of individuals and local histories at different levels of scale. The professors and teaching staff are the sweetest people ever! They really care about the students (they brought us cookies every week! and when it was warm we'd go outside and sit on the grass!) and they are all incredibly knowledgeable . There are also some amazing guest lecturers!

The class really encourages exploration and the weekly assignments are super open–ended. You can do really creative things instead of formal academic writing, and the staff are so supportive of that. Even for topics that you might not be as interested in, you can definitely tie the assignment into something you care about. The class is half lecture, half small–group discussion, and everyone's really friendly and engaged. The readings are genuinely so interesting. I strongly recommend!
TAKE THIS CLASS IF YOU CAN!!! It's a really amazing experience that has low stakes and super high rewards. It's a great class regardless of your concentration (it's really amazing for both humanities and STEM students). The assignments are incredibly manageable and allow for a lot of creativity and exploration. The professors are all incredibly kind, helpful, and insightful and lead really great discussions about the course material. Truly one of the best courses I've ever taken at Harvard!
Professor Rothschild and Ghosh are the absolute best! This course is the absolute best! Really I think anyone interested in spending time talking about climate and environmental issues from a variety of angles should take this course. The weekly paper assignments seem intimidating at first, but the course is balanced so that the time you take writing is taken out of reading time. It's also super helpful down the line because this final was a breeze. I had already written my final by December! All I had to do was pick a few of my papers to fix up and be creative about which I chose so my portfolio had a theme.
I can't say enough good things about this course. As a senior, I am so thankful that I took this course! It was easily a top five course I have taken at Harvard. In terms of vibes, it was my favorite. They have a super kind sick policy too for those who aren't diagnosed with anything but get sick a lot. I imagine if you have accommodations that this course would also be super generous too.
The course material way okay, but the course was incredibly disorganized. The course administration seemed unprepared and unable to facilitate group discussions, almost as if they are anxiously waiting for the 2 hours to end, just like everyone else. The weekly prompts were uncreative.
Take this course if you are interested in exploring climate change in a low–pressure and very encouraging environment!! The course staff are all SO NICE, we got cookies every class, and the conversations were all fairly interesting. The workload is very manageable – we had weekly response papers (but all completion based) and the final portfolio was just four of those response papers revised.
TAKE THIS CLASS. Professor Rothschild and her staff are so passionate about the topic and come from a range of backgrounds. I am so grateful for the opportunity to learn from them. The weekly readings are manageable and engaging, covering a wide area of subjects (history, fiction, news, science, data). The weekly writing assignments were one of my favorite weekly homework assignments. I loved exploring different styles of writing and different parts of climate change with open ended prompts. The final portfolio is also such a great culmination for the class.
The course, assignments, feedback, professors, lectures and conversations are truly unparalleled, you will not regret taking it.
This was a very fun way to complete a History seminar requirement. My other seminar followed the standard, 15–20 page final paper with heavy weekly readings format, so having weekly writing assignments and less reading was a nice way to have a different feel to a seminar. That being said, while it allowed for a lot of writing practice, I left feeling like I had "learned" less than I might have in a typical seminar. We didn't cover many of the topics with much depth, and with the (necessarily) lighter reading load, I didn't pick up as much from the readings as I'm used to from History courses. Regardless, the writing assignments allowed for a lot of freedom and were enjoyable overall; be warned though that writing 4 page papers every week doesn't actually save you from doing a decent amount of work at the end of the semester. Revising for the final portfolio still required a lot of time. I would definitely recommend having potential ideas for the portfolio theme in mind early on to avoid scrambling to find something in the last few weeks.
