Recommend CS271! Note that this course is on the research of data visualization, and not on data viz. itself.
AMAZING! Johanna, Johannes, and Simon are all WONDERFUL, and who wouldn't love an excuse to sit down twice a week and talk about data visualization?!?!? Definitely take the time to critically engage with the readings before class and leave comments on Perusall, you'll get so much more out of the discussions if you feel solid about the papers going into them. The final project is so much fun and you are extended so much creative freedom for where to take things. I put in a dozen hours a week into this class because I made the choice to dive deeper into my project and make something that I'm proud of/is publishable, but this is totally an example of you get out of a class what you put into it.
It's okay, don't need 171 to take. Talk to course instructor as early as possible on final project, they tend to have a lot of comments and they may take off points at the end because it doesn't fit their view of what a good project is. In general a good gem for AB/SM candidates, interesting class. This class IS NOT how to use matplotlib or seaborn better, you actually learn about the research field of visualization.
You should definitely take this class, but do note that you get out what you put in. If you ChatGPT the reading responses and slack on the group projects, you will learn nothing. Actually engage in the readings and do good work on the project, and the class will be very enjoyable.
This is a pretty chill CS grad–level course. There are weekly paper readings that you have to submit discussion posts for, and a semester–long project, but nothing out of the ordinary. If you're interested in visualization techniques and reading about current research, this class is a good choice.
271 is a great class, definitely take it if you're interested in learning more about visualization! The topics covered are super interesting and diverse, and I enjoyed the readings overall. Would recommend pacing yourself for the project though, otherwise it can feel like a lot towards the end of the semester.
Very useful and interesting class!
