TAKE THIS CLASS! I was so disappointed to find out this is only offered every other year because I think more people, particularly premeds who are used to hard science courses, should take this class. Learning about the history of medicine and science, philosophy, and religion brings a humanistic and deeper understanding of medicine as it stands today, and this course has changed my perspective on how medicine is practiced. Mark Schiefsky is an excellent lecturer and intentionally structured the course around a set of central themes to reflect historical debate and thought. Though he had to deliver some week's lectures virtually, I found that he was extremely accessible both after lecture and over email. He also makes an effort to personally get to know every student, something I cannot say for many other of the small lecture–based courses.
Also, for the premeds out there â€” this course counts towards the global health and health policy secondary as well as the premed English requirement, according to the premed advisors. Still, regardless of the requirements this course fulfills, I highly recommend taking this course simply for fun. I thoroughly enjoyed all three of the essay assignments, (one of which involved crafting a philosophical dialogue with ChatGPT, which sounds intimidating but was surprisingly fun!). In terms of the time commitment, I found that this course was very manageable with my other courses.
