What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Just take it!!!! I have been in many “unique” courses at Harvard—about costume and scenic design, a professionally directed and designed theater show, non–profit producing, improv comedy, k–pop culture, Japanese fashion history and theory, tattoos, art about the AIDS crisis, the list goes on and on—but this is by far one of the most fulfilling and interesting classes. An unmatched professor and group of students who will challenge you in ways you didn’t know you could be challenged, all while extending you an incredible amount of support and grace. I will remember this course for a long time and look forward to incorporating what I learned into my future endeavors.
You will enjoy this course if you feel strongly interested in the ideas the course tackles. It might be difficult, however, if you consider this as an introductory survey course.
This is an amazing course, and I would highly recommend it. I took this course as an undergraduate student with some background in Visual Studies but limited knowledge of Japanese History. The graduate students I met and the professor were incredibly inspiring and encouraging. A background in Visual Studies and Japanese History is essential, as these will be prominent topics discussed in the course.
