Amazing class! Great lectures! Mediocre section. At first, I was hesitant about enrolling in a psychology lecture class due to having to come to class 3 times a week. The class looked interesting but I wasn't sure if I wanted to commit. However, I'm glad I did because it was my favorite psychology class I've ever taken (although it made me regret not choosing sociology as a concentration but I'm a senior so it's too late oh well). Although I think it falls in the category of general psychology, a lot of the assigned readings were social psychology papers. I've never taken a social psychology class before, and now I wish I had.

Anyways, great class. Obviously, some days I thought it was average or mediocre but as a whole I thought it was really good.

One of the best courses I've taken at Harvard! Professor H is an amazing teacher and knows how to get everyone engaged in the material. Workload is light, and all of the assignments feel important. You get to pick what topic to focus on throughout the semester, which meant that I always got to work on things I was interested in. I would recommend this course to anyone!!
Take this class!! I was initially quite nervous about this class but don't let the application tasks scare you. They were honestly not as bad as I was expecting and I came to really enjoy them. We got to explore a stigma of our choice throughout the whole semester and I really appreciated the academic agency that afforded us. Lecture was very interesting and I felt very comfortable with participating. I learned much more about stigma and psychological concepts than I was expecting to. Section was also quite engaging and I loved the discussions. All of the gradings felt fair. The final assignments were pretty manageable, as well as the readings. Speaking of, do the readings! They will be discussed in lecture and you will be expected to comment on them. I felt like Prof H and Sama, my TF, really wanted us to succeed. While you will have to put in some work, it is worth it and I highly recommend this class!
This is a great class if you're interested in stigma and its effects on mental/ physical health. You get to pick a stigmatized group and run with it all semester. Dr. H is a wonderful professor, passionate about the topic, and very knowledgeable as this is his main research. The weekly assignments are not too bad, and the final involves a research paper/memoir paper + a presentation on your stigma of choice. Highly recommend as a psych elective!
Take this class! I stumbled on this class by complete coincidence and it ended up being the highlight of my semester. It teaches you a lot about stigma in real life contexts and how it predicts health outcomes. I feel like I started looking at various situations involving health and disparities in a new light after taking this course. The class is extremely well structured and the flow of the topics comes naturally. Prof. H is also extremely knowledgeable, literally any question you have about the topic he can answer. Because the class is small, you also get tp hear from everyone and their experiences and thoughts which makes the course all the more enjoyable and insightful.
Take this class! Manageable workload, a super interesting topic, and you get to choose what stigma to explore for the semester. This was my favorite class this semester!
