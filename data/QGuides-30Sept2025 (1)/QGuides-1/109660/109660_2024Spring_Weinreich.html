HISTSCI 98 is a very valuable course, but its structure can really use some work. On the one hand, it is great preparation for the thesis. In all likelihood, even if you've written 25 page papers before (which I had, before this course), you've never written anything with the breadth of primary and secondary sources you're expected to use for this paper. The process is an enormous learning experience: you realize how much time you need to write something you're proud of, the importance of looking for sources early, being willing to change directions if necessary, and being attentive to your citations. As I prepare to write my thesis this fall, my experience in HISTSCI 98 will definitely inform how I balance my time. This all said, the course also has many components that are a complete waste of time. The early preliminary assignments before the chunks don't feel useful –– especially the exploring archives assignment. In the future, it may be useful to start even earlier with the assignments actually related to the paper, such as handing in topic ideas, or notes that you've collected on your sources. The course feels a little lopsided with the work being very very light at the beginning and ramping up in the middle. Additionally, the course meetings were not particularly enlightening. It seemed like most people didn't really do the readings and I'm not sure that any of the readings substantially altered how I was approaching my paper. Because there was no coherent topic to the tutorial (other than writing a paper), it just didn't feel like there were stakes to discussing the readings. I almost wish there were topics for the tutorials, like there are in other concentrations (e.g. history of medicine, history of technology, etc.). Finally, in the future, for the spring iteration of the course, I think it's important to review how the assignments line up with the semester, compared to the fall. We had a five page chunk due on the day we returned from spring break, which really didn't feel kind, especially since the professor had said he wouldn't be available by email during the break since *he* wouldn't be working –– the structure felt a little tone deaf.

In terms of class, it's not the most fun course but some of the readings are cool, and if you find a good paper topic it can be very rewarding. The class is heavily structured so that you have to write a couple pages of your paper per week during the latter half of the semester. This can feel bothersome but makes reading week and finals much less stressful if, like me, you would otherwise write the paper in the few days before it was due. My advice would be to frontload a bit on finding a topic you care about.

Super helpful, best HS required class I've taken.
Well, if you're taking this course you probably have to take it. So I will give some general pointers. First, I think that coming into the semester with an idea of what you want to write about is extremely helpful. Narrowing down at least a topic of study before the semester will allow you to use class to your full benefit and will, in general, make your experience more chill. Second, be aware that the course is quite chill at the beginning of the semester (think 1–3 hrs per week) and that towards the end of the semester, once you start drafting, the work spikes a lot (think 8ish hours per week once you start drafting, with potentially many more during reading period. I think I was spending 4–5 hrs per day on the essay for the week before it was due). This is kind of unavoidable, but the more seriously you take the drafting assignments the better the last few weeks of the class will be. Finally, don't be too intimidated by this course! 25–30 pages is a lot of writing but the course prepares you well for it. The classes are really small and the teaching staff is super supportive and gives a lot of one on one advice and support, so you're in good hands.
Do your assignments on time! Make sure you get value out of them (i.e. don't do a poor early assignment just to check the box; if you put effort into every small piece your final paper will be very easy to put together). It seems daunting to write a 25 page paper but it is very doable if you are organized and go bit by bit. The course schedule works so much better than cramming. Overall, I loved this course and grew as a writer, researcher, and historian of science!
