This is a somewhat frustrating course. The ultimate goal is completing a 25 page paper, however you won't start working on it until week 6 or so. This course feels like a repeat of the sophomore tutorial, except this time you submit a final paper.
I highly recommend this course! While it isnt easy, its very manageable if you stay on top of the work. You truly get out of it what you put into it. Choose a topic that interests you but is slightly outside your comfort zone to challenge yourself. Youll feel incredibly accomplished when you submit your final paper. Be intentional with what you write for the mini ungraded assignments, as they will be very helpful when you reach the final stages of the project!
Your project for the entire semester is a 25–page research paper that acts as an introduction to writing a senior thesis. I believe that the assignments throughout the semester could be better structured to act as milestones of finishing a 25–page paper on time, rather than making it chunks of the paper that aren't has cohesive as they should be at the end.
You have to take it if you're a HS concentrator lol. But anyways, I really liked it, because it's pretty much a whole semester just to write one paper, and you meet for class once a week just to check in on how you're doing and get some tips on how to do your paper better. I really liked the freedom and flexibility of this style of course. Also, Simon was such a good instructor and always gave such helpful and more importantly, specifically ACTIONABLE feedback on papers so that you'd know exactly what you have to do to get an A. Also, bc the whole semester is just writing a paper (you don't rly start till after the 5th or 6th week of the course, bc the first part of the course is kinda a super short repeat of HS 97), you should start as early as possible in writing and get as much done as you can earlier on in the process (e.g., the writing chunks often have a page minimum of 4–5 pages, but if you happen to have more time that week, you should do way more), bc then there will be more instances where your instructor can give you more specific feedback so that you can guarantee an A. And also, if you do it early enough, it is definitely possible to be basically done the paper by the end of thanksgiving break (if you're taking it in the fall).
HS98 is notably harder than 97 and 100, so just be prepared to have a solid weekly amount of work, especially in the latter half of the semester. However, it is still very flexible, supported, and structured. They really want you to succeed and will be super helpful and flexible in order to do so! I think I kind of underestimated the amount of work it takes to write a 25–page paper, but it is very doable. Make sure you pick a topic you're really interested in.
Although this is a required course, think of it as an opportunity to grow as a researcher, writer, and historian of science. It is a great place to learn about something that you are deeply passionate in, and will also make it clear if that something is what you want to write your thesis on. I would recommend leaning into the course! Be curious about your classmates projects
Really great tutorial! I loved the structure and thought we got a really amazing amount of guidance. I'm very proud of the research I was able to produce and would recommend anyone daunted by the paper to go for it.
Take the scaffolding assignments seriously, and you will have a good time. You have to take this course, so use it as a chance to write about something you like and find out if research is for you or not.
This class helped me confirm that I want to thesis in HistSci. It's super flexible (almost too much that I didn't really set boundaries w/ myself and deadlines were almost like a suggestion) and allows you creative freedom to research practically anything you want.
The work load increases at the end significantly, so it's better if you start writing earlier.
This is a Hist Sci Requirement. It is pretty guided– and helpful feedback is provided, 25 pages is still a lot but can be manageable if you really start early.
Tutorial is honestly pretty fun. The small class is nice, we almost always got released early since 2 hours is a long time for 5 people. The paper writing process was tricky, but was also very step–by–step and that helped a lot. It also was so nice going into reading period with an already written rough draft.
Don't wait until the last minute to gather sources and write.
TAKE THIS COURSE! It will serve as a thrilling experience as you do your own research and write your own paper. Stay on top of tasks and ask MANY questions. Course readings are illuminating and scaffolding tasks are helpful. It may seem daunting at first but you got this! Take things step by step. You will learn a lot and make sure to reach out for support and feedback from staff.
