Take this class! Professor Wang's is a great lecturer, the readings were all interesting, and sections and individual sessions were both helpful for understanding course material.
Great class! Highly highly recommend if you're at all interested in reading some of the great works in modern Chinese literary history
I cannot recommend this class enough! I came into this semester pretty uncertain about whether I wanted to take this class because I was worried it would be too hard (I had just finished 140a). The class was certainly a challenge I had to block of quite a bit of time over the weekend to do the weekly readings and at times it felt like I was reading at a snails pace but overall it was extremely doable. The teaching staff provides so much support to make it possible. There are vocab lists every week with key vocabulary that helps a lot for the readings. Because the texts are a bit challenging your Chinese improves quite noticeably over the course of the class which is great.

You also have a one on one meeting with the TFs every week to answer questions, discuss the texts, and get feedback on your paper. The paper is super scaffolded because you only have to turn in a short chunk of it every other week. I will flag that you have to come up with your paper topic extremely early in the semesters, though, so I would start thinking about that right away.

The one caveat I will say for this class is that there are *a lot* of assignments and deadlines. But the grading is extremely generous and there's extra credit, so while you have a fair bit to do every week grades are not a concern.

Ultimately, it was extremely rewarding to feel that years of studying Chinese has paid off to the point where instead of learning to read in Chinese I am now reading to learn. The stories are so delightful and Professor Wangs lectures are absolutely phenomenal, providing historical context and analysis of the texts. I learned so much about Chinese history and culture through this class. Please take this class!!!
David Wang's lectures are amazing –– you can always count on his lectures to make you laugh and also think more deeply about Chinese society and history. This course is not light unless you have a strong Chinese background because the readings can be very long and there are many small assignments due each week. However, this course is very manageable and the course staff are super understanding, kind, and provide a lot of support. Though the many small assignments due each week can feel a bit tedious at times, they also serve as a guardrail so that by the end of the semester, you're not cramming the final paper (you pretty much have the whole thing written by the end of it). I would recommend this for anyone with a decent background in reading Chinese and who's interested in learning more about Chinese literature and history!
Take it! You get introduced to so many amazing and influential modern Chinese authors. Wang Laoshi are Liu Laoshi are both amazing lecturers, and the course materials are super well designed (for example, you get a vocabulary sheet with each reading that provides definitions and pronunciations for pretty much every character/phrase you may not know). Lin Laoshi and Wang Wenfei Laoshi were also wonderful instructors and added so much value to the course <3
One of the best classes out there! You will not regret taking this class. The amount of effort you put in is rewarded by the competency in Chinese language and culture gained. Prof. Liu deeply cares for her students, and Prof. Wang is super funny and engaging. Although the amount of work required is on the high end, this class is a must–take.
Especially if you are not a heritage speaker, this class will require a lot more time from you when doing the readings. The short stories can take a couple hours to read, but the homework and assignments are really easy and graded generously. You will become much better at reading Chinese by the end of this course. However, you may feel like your speaking skills will deteriorate, especially compared to other Chinese courses at Harvard. The emphasis is much more on lecturing and reading/writing, not discussing and speaking in class.
Please take this course!! Professor Wang and Professor Liu are both such amazing teachers who truly care about their students and provide entertaining + insightful lectures. You will be exposed to a wide array of modern Chinese literature (most of which I didn't even know coming into this course) and will leave with so much valuable knowledge in Chinese lit and history! There are weekly assignments but they are relatively low–stakes and TFs are also very supportive!
