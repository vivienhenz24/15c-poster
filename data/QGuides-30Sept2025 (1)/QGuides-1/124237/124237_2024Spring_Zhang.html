I LOVE CHINESE BB!!! I love the way the course is run. THIS DEPARTMENT IS LIKE A WELL–OILED MACHINE, everything goes so smoothly. ALSO I LOVE THE TEACHING TEAM!!! They are so encouraging, funny, and amazing at their jobs. for giving me the confidence to learn a language I was always too afraid to pick up.
As someone with no prior Chinese experience, I found this class to be VERY hard – a lot harder than ba. The rigor of this class turned me away from pursuing a citation.
Comprehensive review incoming: The course staff are some of the most empathetic, caring, enthusiastic educators I have ever met. The grading is not punitive and rewards effort. The course is interesting; every lesson is exciting, and really gives you the opportunity to improve your Chinese. Some assignments are not super helpful and feel like busy work, but I appreciate how low–stakes the assessments are –– I was never worried about my grade, and this let me focus on improving my language skills without trying to game assessments. It can be hard to fit Chinese into your schedule as it meets every day, but in my experience the course heads have been more than helpful in accommodating students who need to come to class at different times each day. Course materials – text book, audio recordings, grammar/vocab handouts, homeworks – are all very useful resources. Feedback is given with care and in a very constructive way – students are allowed to grow in their language skill without feeling insecure. CHNSE BA or equivalent is sufficient. This is an excellent, if not slightly time–consuming (meets 5 days, homework, projects, revision for 4 major tests, dictation twice a week, individual session outside of class), way to meet a language requirement. I cannot stress this enough: the course staff are the heart and soul of this subject and my semester was greatly improved by knowing them.
TAKE CHINESE!! My biggest regret is not starting in the Chinese language dept earlier. The teaching staff are wonderful, class is fun, and you learn so much so fast. Chinese BA/BB have been some of my favorite classes out of my three years here!
Start on the homework earlythe Bb homework is noticeably more difficult than the Ba homework; if you don't come from a Chinese–speaking background, it would be helpful to spend time on your homework and learning during office hours.
I needed to satisfy the language requirement and my friends recommended Chinese, so I took it, and I'm so glad I did!! Chinese at Harvard is AMAZING, everything is organized well and designed in a way that will maximize your language learning. Every lecture is engaging and you just get the sense that the teachers really care about you and are invested in your progress. There are definitely easier language classes you could take, but I don't regret my choice to take Chinese at all. It has been so rewarding to see the progress that I've made, and I'm now planning to continue with Chinese and get a citation.
