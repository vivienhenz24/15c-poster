What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Great course!! Eli is a fantastic professor and if you are at all interested in climate teleconnections and atmosphere–ocean–biosphere interactions you should definitely take this course. The psets are challenging but totally doable with office hours and working with your peers. Start them early. There's no final, just a final project which is a really fun capstone to the course.
This is a really robust EPS class. Get ready for a lot of math and physics. However, the big picture theories and ideas are taught in a very manageable way and you can get a lot out of the course even if the physics is challenging. Eli is a great lecturer, so class are always incredibly interesting. Start PSets early and get help from the TF and the professor. I would highly recommend this to anyone who feels like they want a good class to warp up and combine things that they have learned in other atmosphere/ocean classes!
This was one of my favorite classes Ive ever taken at Harvard. It broadened my knowledge of our climate systems and I feel I have a grasp or general understanding of the basic climate dynamics that drive our earth system. This is definitely a challenging course for an undergraduate, mostly in the problem sets, but manageable with the right preparation. It isnt too big of a time commitment outside of the difficulty of the psets (which again might be easier or harder for you depending on your math background). Eli is a wonderful professor, very excited about the material, and one of the best to teach the course. Strongly recommend taking this course, just know it would be one of your more intermediate to hard courses on your schedule.
