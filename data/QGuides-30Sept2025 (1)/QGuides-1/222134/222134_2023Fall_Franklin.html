This is a great course! You should go to lectures because they are really interesting and engaging (and required). You should do the reading because they are also interesting and allow you to engage more actively and thoughtfully with the material in lecture and section. There is a lot of reading, so be prepared to read a lot if you read everything. But it's not too stressful because sometimes you can get away with just reading some of the readings or reading the readings later. Not recommended, but possible if necessary. There is a LOT of writing because you have to turn in writing four times a week. It's kind of difficult to manage, but there was a lot of flexibility in allowing us to turn things in late sometimes without docking points. Again, not recommended because it will pile up, but definitely nice so you can still learn from the course and get the full experience and not fail the class. We don't go into EXTREME depth about literary or scientific concepts, it's kind of somewhat introductory for both (which is good for accessibility) but sometimes I wanted a bit more depth in our discussion of topics. However, lectures were still super fascinating and I liked it.
This was a new class and I don't think they're offering it again, but if they are: it was a cool class, but shouldn't be cross–listed as a physics class. It is mostly English. The physics component of the lectures stopped about halfway through and even when we were talking about physics, it didn't really get anywhere. It is a super cool creative writing class to write and think about time, so take it if that is what you are looking for!
Take this class! The writing prompts are so much fun and the course readings are super interesting. The content you learn from lectures is fascinating and varied, and I guarantee everyone who takes this class can at least get something they like out of it. Sections are probably the best part of the class and the TFs are so kind and knowledgeable! I honestly wish I could've put more into this class since the reading and writing is a fair amount of work, so only take this class if you are willing to devote several hours a week to it and it'll be 100% worth it!
Expect to be challenged, but also rewarded.
I recommend this course with the highest enthusiasm! This has been my favorite course I have taken at Harvard (I am a senior). I have wanted to take a course about writing about science and this course was everything I hoped it would be, and so much more. Prof. Messud and Prof. Franklin's lectures are incredible; I learned so much from both of them and I appreciated their complementary approaches. I also really enjoyed the guest lectures they arranged for the course: it was so cool to hear from the authors whose work we were reading! I learned a lot from the course readings and appreciated the variety of styles we encountered. The course readings are also really fun and engaging; we read poetry, fiction, essays, science journalism, history of science articles, and much more. I also really appreciated the teaching staff who were very knowledgable, helpful, and dedicated. I regularly attended the professors' office hours as well as the teaching fellows' office hours and I found these sessions to be extremely helpful. I learned so much about how to creatively communicate about science and how to use forms of creative writing to think through scientific ideas and develop my understanding of these. If you are interested in science, creative writing, and/or how these connect take this amazing class!
This class is awesome.
This was a fun, quirky class that I would definitely recommend! It's taught by a dynamic group of professors and TFs with very different academic backgrounds, who all make an effort to get to know all students, and it attracted students with a wide range of personalities, concentrations, science and writing experience, and interests, which was a definite strength of the course and made for some fun discussions. The workload was also predictable and consistent. As a physics concentrator, I appreciated this course more for its writing aspect –– I haven't ever had room in my schedule to take a time–consuming literature– or writing–intensive course, but I was glad to have the opportunity to take a course that prompted me to write a moderate amount, consistently, over the course of the semester, in order to stay in–practice and hopefully improve!

There was a weird assumption that kept being brought up throughout the course by both professors that English concentrators don't understand anything about physics or vice versa, which I think 1) goes against the purpose of the class and 2) made me a bit annoyed as an English concentrator who hasn't taken a physics class since tenth grade and yet understood the vast majority of the physics concepts that were being introduced in this class. The readings were also heavily white and male, way more than I would have expected from any class taught in 2023? Unless the course improves, I wouldn't recommend it.
