What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This is a great course for anyone interested in neglected regions in international relations discourse or the post–Soviet period outside of Russia. Central Asia features in many of the themes of the times, such as climate change, ethnicity, China–US competition. This course is also a welcome diversion from the usual conflict areas and provides valuable insights on the region.

The assignments are very fair and the reading list is reasonable. The readings are much less theoretical than those of courses like Gov 20 and are thus easier to get through.
This class filled a gap in knowledge and is very well structured, providing historic context and then examining 9 "powers" and their interests in the region. Would recommend. Absolutely manageable workload. Professor Kassenova is very approachable and knowledgeable about the area, and is eager to help students pursue their particular interests in the region.
Really interesting class. Super interesting if you have some interest in Central Asia. Feedback received is minimal, so start a dialogue with Professor Kassenova early.
I think this course is unique in terms of content, and Professor Kassenova has lived experience in the region. However, if you prefer structure in your courses, this class may seem disorganized. Some assignments are unclear, and the grading is not as light as it may appear.
