Professor Remoquillo is truly one of the best professors at Harvard, and one of the very best in the humanities. She is invested in the individual research of each of her students in a way that most professors don't care to be. Her feedback on student writing is thorough, concise, forward–thinking, constructively critical, and very informative. She also cares deeply about building a strong course community, connecting us with people from other institutions doing important academic work, and is just super cool all around. The syllabus is very fun, and Prof. Remoquillo's slides never fail to eat and engage everyone. It was a 9am, but I came every week excited and energized!
This class was AMAZING! I couldn't have asked for a better introduction to Asian American studies at Harvard. I looked forward to readings and class discussions every week; every single thing we read, watched, or did was curated with so much intentionality. There was so much warmth, joy, and learning in this course, both because of Professor Remoquillo and the other students taking the class. Our learning went both wide and deep, and we were encouraged to engage deeply across the course content and our own experiences. I've never felt both personally touched and intellectually challenged by a class until this one; my favorite course this year, by far!
One of the coolest classes I've taken at Harvard. It's a pretty demanding course because the readings/films are super interesting, but can be pretty dense and time–intensive. The grading is also pretty conservative and can lean on the harder side. However, if you're passionate about Asian American studies, this is the best class you could take. I couldn't recommend this enough. Prof Remoquillo is absolutely incredible–––very engaging and helpful professor. She is so passionate about the topic and super dedicated to all of her students.
This is an awesome class! I love how Professor Remoquillo truly cares about us and always takes the time to check in with us at the beginning of each class. It is also clear that she knows so much about Asian American studies and truly loves teaching us about it. Just know that if this course happens to be on a Thursday again, you may have one final class during reading period.
You should absolutely take this course. Professor Remoquillo is a WONDERFUL professor and person and you will be extremely fortunate to have her as your professor to guide you. This course is very challenging in how to engage deeply, personally, and academically with Asian American communities, ethnicities, and history through a very intriguing, interesting, and exciting study of space and place. Beyond just analyzing and interrogating how Asian Americans, both in the U.S and transnationally, navigate belonging in relation to physical geography, this course challenges, disrupts, and broadens how we see (and don't see) Asian Americans moving across multiple sites of geography; culturally, emotionally, intergenerational, temporal, culturally, and more. You will get very close to your classmates as Professor Remoquillo is amazing at fostering a collaborate learning experience that is very meaningful and rich. Be ready to participate, read the texts closely and deeply, and listen thoughtfully. This course will also challenge your writing in many great ways in how you understand Asian American Studies, but also in your articulation of your thoughts, your sense of self and general understanding of the course and its relation to you. The readings challenged me, the writing challenged me, my classmates challenged me, Professor Remoquillo challenged me, and I've learned so, so much from both. This course has changed my life and I cannot recommend this class enough. I really, really, love this class.
