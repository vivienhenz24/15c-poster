I recommend Psych 1 not only to students already interested in psychology and/or related fields, but more broadly, to anyone interested in gaining more scientific, accurate, thorough understanding of people (ergo, society and themself). Psych 1 efficiently and effectively provides an introduction to the main areas of psychology, while also giving you insight into how psychologists design experiments, test and come up with hypotheses, draw and revise conclusions, assess the believability of claims.

From experience in the course, I got the perception that while psychology faces difficulty with gathering accurate evidence, arriving at sound conclusions, and even just in de–abstracting ideas, the challenge lends to a process of development in the field that goes beyond the scientific method in its astounding intuitiveness and ingenuity. In true social science fashion, Psych 1 felt similar to philosophy in that the ideas are packed with moral and ethical interpretations and consequences, but also remained rooted in scientific practice and thinking with enough technical detail to stay grounded. Psychology touches upon heavy existential and humanistic topics, and Gilberts perspective on these topics felt simultaneously sobering and inspiring, and truthful yet optimistic.

The readings and lectures are both very high–quality (Gilbert is super compelling, both in the classroom and on the page), and they complement each other very well. Often, lecture is key in understanding and gaining intuition for a concept that is presented in a more abstract, definition–heavy way in the textbook. Lecture also provides a simple, memorable ways to think about ideas. On other occasions, the textbook goes into the level of detail needed to solidify ones understanding and clear confusions. The textbook also contains a lot of content that you wont catch during lecture, and these topics often show up in some basic way on tests.

Unless you seek it out, Gilberts Psych 1 does not come equipped with a lot of guidance, as the class boils down to attending lectures and reading the textbook, with no other required assignments or homework. Your understanding is entirely measured by your performance on the two exams (quick statistics: on both exams, median was around 37/50, A–zone cutoff was 35/50). The advantage of this format is that you have tons of freedom to prepare for these tests and learn the content however you would like to, and most weeks of the course are pretty stress–free. Also, I have heard very good things about the Chapter Zero Book Club, which many have described as their favorite component of the class.
