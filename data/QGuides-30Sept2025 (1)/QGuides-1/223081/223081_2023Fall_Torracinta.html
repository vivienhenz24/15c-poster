The greatest class I've taken at this university –– and I study Computer Science.

Prof. Torracinta is a kind–hearted, lovely person who genuinely cares about his material and what students think of it; the class clocks in at about 2:30h (with a 15min break in between!) but it honestly never felt like I was held there for longer than I would've been, it never got boring or mundane at all.

The readings were fantastic: the class is titled "Being Human since 1945" but the actual material is about a lot more than that, and it strongly recontextualised assumptions or facts about modern popsci culture, things like gender division (how was it that we decided chromosomes determined gender, even though in 1903 the consensus was that it was a more complex issue?), eugenics, the minimum wage, the philosophy of psychology through Ian Hacking, to end on a high with posthumanism and cyborgs. A smorgasbord of great classes that I've come away feeling very enriched from.

Workload is also fair: it's <100 pages of reading per week, 3 thousand–word reading reflections three weeks apart, and a final project (your typical research paper, but you can also choose to write a feature–length article or make a podcast), although participation is strongly required (I mean... it's a seminar class lol).

Overall, I would not be surprised if this became one of the crown jewels of the HISTSCI department in a few years, and the class becomes nigh–impossible to actually lottery into. A great, great time!
