This class is a mixed bag.

Pros: For a CS class, it's pretty easy –– basically no time outside class the first few weeks, then 1–2 hours on non–project weeks, and 8–12 hrs on project weeks (of which there are only 4). It's taught lab–style (like CS51), which I find much more engaging than lectures. It will also teach you a lot about NLP approaches overall, including simple statistical models, older neural net architectures and linguistics–inspired algorithms. (I liked the linguistics stuff, though I got the sense many others found it confusing.)

Cons: If you've taken CS 181 or seen a lot of ML basics (naive bayes, n–grams, logistic regression, gradient descent), the first half of the class feels largely redundant. Plus a lot of the labs about different neural networks can feel less about understanding the architectures or concepts and more about messing around with pytorch to get tensor sizes right. Then there's the grading. Up to the last day of working on the final project, only the first two weeks of labs and *none* of the projects had been graded. I never had any idea how I was doing in the class, nor do I know now, which was very frustrating. The teaching staff in general seemed like a mess (deadlines were wrong or changed late, etc., though I got the sense a lot of this was Prof. Shieber's fault).

Also: this class is NOT about state–of–the–art NLP. Attention (the architectural innovation at the heart of all LLMs) is the last day of class and feels very rushed.
