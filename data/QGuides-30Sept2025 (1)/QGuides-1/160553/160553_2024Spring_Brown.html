Everyone knows this class is challenging. You're probably not taking it unless you're a candidate for B.S. in engineering. You will gain a lot of confidence and capability throughout the course, but it will probably be painful. A couple of things I wanna relay:

1. Your choice of advisor I believe will heavily impact your experience. Having someone whose expertise is in the field and better yet, in the project itself (ie. if you're doing a small project that's a shootoff from an existing one) will be soooo beneficial. You'll survive even if it's not the case, but you'll probably have to do most of the problem–solving and project mapping on your own (not an all–bad thing––– I was in this boat, and took a lot of pride in the self–guided nature of my project, even though it meant I spent more time at roadblocks than some others may have).

2. WORK AHEAD WORK AHEAD WORK AHEAD. Guys just trust me on this, work ahead as much as you possibly can. The course will go from 0 to 60 between first and second semester, that is, it asks nearly nothing of you in the first semester. This caused me to accomplish basically nothing in this period. You can think you have things planned out, but once you go to actually execute, unexpected problems are going to pop up and you're basically going to redesign your entire project. Therefore, do more than you think you need to. In the second semester, you're probably going to be putting in 2–3 days at the SEC where you spend legitimately 12+ hours there. If you can start getting concrete stuff done in the first half of the year, this might make second sem less demanding and stressful. Students (at least in my experience) tend to want to do the bare minimum required to not feel overcome with anxiety (aka. we don't push ourselves willingly into academic turmoil). But I strongly urge you to spend some late nights working on your capstone in the first half of the year. It might make a world of difference in your anxiety levels the next semetser.
Honestly, ES100 is a sad way to wrap up your time at Harvard. It's hard and time consuming. Many assignments/events aren't graded, but you'll lose points if you don't submit or attend them. So, you can't gain points from them, only lose points. Frankly, it's a ridiculous, Draconian grading system. We only take ES100 it because it's required, otherwise nobody would ever take this terrible course. Hopefully it'll change for the better one day, but I wouldn't hold my breath. Good luck.

While it may seem daunting no matter what your project is, this class is extremely rewarding, if stressful. How well your project works should not be your focus. Instead focus on structuring the design, build, and test phases will the project requirements in mind. Good luck!
