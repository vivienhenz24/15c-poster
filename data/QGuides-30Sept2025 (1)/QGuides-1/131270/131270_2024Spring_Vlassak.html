This is the only class I have taken where reading the textbook is EXTREMELY helpful especially because Professor Joost has bad handwriting and it is hard to decipher the board. Also, ProfessorJoost has a policy where if you miss the first exam, the second exam will count for both. In general, the first exam covers material from the first 5 psets and has an average of a 50–55%. The second exam only covers psets 6&7, and has an average of about a 70%. Therefore, it will honestly be more beneficial for you to skip the first exam and just take the second one as you will probably do better and it covers less material.

To be honest it didn't matter that much though because the content didn't match up super well for any of the exams either. The content of the course is pretty foundational for engineering majors in my opinion, but the course is taught a bit dysfunctionally.
