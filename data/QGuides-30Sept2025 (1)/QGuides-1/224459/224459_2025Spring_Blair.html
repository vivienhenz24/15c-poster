What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If you are interested in early modern Europe, don't hesitate to take this.
Do not think that this course is only half the work in a semester because it's half the credits. It is just as time intensive as any other course and at times more.
This is an excellent course that challenges students to think beyond modern conceptions of the world–– allowing students to return to a socially and intellectually tenuous time where difficult questions about the way the world works were being asked.
If you have even the slightest interest in early modern intellectual history or book history, you should take this course!!! Professor Blair is a genius and an incredible teacher. You will learn a ton
This is a well–paced course that covers almost every topic about the intellectual culture of the early–modern period. It is a great introduction to the craft of book history (for writing the final paper) and also a great survey for anyone who wants to know what work is currently being done in the field of early modern history. Professor Blair always has information to share when students have questions beyond the readings, and the seminar format fluctuated a little, when there were some "lecture–worthy" topics, on which Professor Blair would spend helpful minutes instructing us.
This course was a great way to learn about a range of different topics and to survey the work being done in early modern European history generally. It's rare to be in a course with peers who have such wide–ranging interests but this is precisely what made this course so enriching! Ann is also a wonderful, caring professor whose dedication to the course was evident. The year–long format felt like a big commitment but it was one I'm glad I made.
