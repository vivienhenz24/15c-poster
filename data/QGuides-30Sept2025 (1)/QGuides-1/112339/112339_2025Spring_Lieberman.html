This is a great class! The whole teaching team is extremely knowledgeable and really cares about student success. Section assignments can be a bit annoying and don't always feel like they connect directly to the temporally–aligned lectures, but everything ties together nicely at the end of the course. If you do not have any background in evolutionary biology (or just science in general), you may need to spend some real time on the readings each week to truly understand everything. Most of all: go to class and take good notes!! Would definitely recommend this course to anyone interested in it!
This class is the most general GENED you can take, which definitely makes it out to be a lot. This class is barely any work during the semester, but the 40% cumulative final is definitely insane for a GENED. This class is going to be incredibly interesting for the first few weeks, but after the midterm gets to be incredibly repetitive and you often find yourself writing the same thing on the assignments. This GENED is definitely one of the nicer science GENEDs you can take – its really chill until you need to learn all of human evolution and climate change in two weeks. I would definitely recommend this class if you arent a STEM major; if you are a STEM major (especially bio) its an incredibly easy GENED and will cover things you learn in all your other classes so it gets to be redundant. For a humanities, or non bio major, I would definitely recommend this class cause it teaches you a lot of important things about your health and human evolution. TL; DR; NOT A GEM BUT ONE OF THE BETTER SCIENCE GENEDS – TAKE IF NOT A BIO MAJOR

I definitely walked away with lots of fun facts. Take it!

TAKE THIS CLASS. Seriously so good. Professor Lieberman and Professor Uno give fantastic, in–depth lectures which are still accessible for a humanities student (like me). The teaching staff are also incredible, and clearly enthusiastic about the material. Labs were without a doubt my best experience in a section so far –– they were held at the Museum of Comparative Zoology, and involved easy but engaging activities to apply what we covered in lecture. This class gave me a survey of earth science, climate science, human evolution, and human health, much of which was very applicable to day–to–day life. At risk of sounding like they paid me to say this, this class is what a Gened is supposed to be. Interdisciplinary, fascinating, with enough work to keep me from slacking (one pre– and post–lab per week), but not more than an intro class can justify. Do the readings, especially since there's not too many of them and the pre–labs (as well as the midterm and final) are based on them. Cannot recommend this class enough!!

This course requires a significant amount of work, but will also teach you a lot of material! I do think the amount of material is on the higher side for a Gened so you will learn a lot but be prepared to review for significant amounts of time before exams (they were 60% of our grade). But Professor Lieberman is a really engaging and passionate lecturer who is also a leader in his field, so would recommend it overall!
