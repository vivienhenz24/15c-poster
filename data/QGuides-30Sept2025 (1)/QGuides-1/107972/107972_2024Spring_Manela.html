This is a GREAT course! If you can get into it, you should 100% take it. That said, don't underestimate how much work it will be. Prof Manela does a great job of setting expectations in such a manner that everyone takes this course seriously. You have to do the readings and actually participate in the discussions, and the final research paper must "contribute to knowledge with a capital K." This can be a bit intimidating, but it made this course one of my favorites that I've ever taken at Harvard because it really pushed me to do my best work and I got a lot more out of this course than I have from a lot of other courses I've taken. Prof. Manela is really great and really nice and the class atmosphere was wonderful. Definitely take if you get the opportunity, but make sure you start the research paper early in the semester instead of waiting until you're in the middle of midterms. Starting early was my saving grace.
This is a really great class! Manela knows literally everything under the sun. He's like Megamind. But in all seriousness, it's such a great class, I feel like I learned so many new things that impact how I see the world today.
This is a really fantastic course. All the readings are both manageable and interesting, I was always excited to do the work for this class and it never felt like a chore. The conversation in class is always interesting, and very well balanced between the students and the professor. Highly recommend.
Firstly Prof. Manela is just WONDERFUl and you should take this course just to get to learn from him. He is just brilliant (and very funny) and extremely approachable. The course was fascinating, and allows you to conduct original historical research which I think is a must–have experience at Harvard. It's important to remember though that this is a course on US–China RELATIONS. Not on just the US, or just China, or something else your work has to connect both countries. That's different from taking a history of China course. I would strongly encourage anyone interested in diplomatic history to take the course you really grow as a historian if you're a history major, learn something cool in a fun and accessible way with a great professor if you're not a history major, and it's just an all–around good time
take it! great class with a great prof.
The class readings are very interesting and I really liked a lot of the readings. However, the discussions were mostly a Q&A session with the professor (who is very knowledgable!) but I personally wished for a more seminar–like environment. The research component was interesting and I loved hearing about other student's research and insights. It's more of a problem with Harvard's history seminars but I honestly preferred meeting more frequently and having shorter class sections.
The readings for this class are the best part. They're insightful and really interesting, and the discussions then are often great. You'll learn a LOT in this class about both relations between the United States and China, and more broadly about how to approach original research in history.

The course structure is a little lacking, though. Grading isn't very transparent, and the feedback you will receive on assignments before your final paper isn't always helpful. You usually won't know a lot about how you're doing until the final grade comes in.
