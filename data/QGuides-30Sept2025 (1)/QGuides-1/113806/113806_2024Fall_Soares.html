As we know, Portuguese is very similar to Spanish. This course does a wonderful job of highlighting the differences between two, but it is very easy to want to settle back into speaking Spanish with an accent if you are not careful. Take advantage of the various components of the course (reading, writing, listening, speaking, etc.) to really engage with the language. While we do cover a lot of material (especially for an introductory language course), everything is presented very logically and older topics resurface later in the semester to really help cement vocabulary, orthography, and syntax. Along with a general understanding of the Portuguese language, we also learned about the differences between regional accents and Brazilian vs. European Portuguese. You have the opportunity to choose which accent you want to roll with, so really stick to one and practice it as much as possible.
