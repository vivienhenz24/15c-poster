What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
The course that is most rewarding in the department! The professor is really kind and the students who chose to take this seminar are also very self–driven. I think every history student should take this class!
Take this class!!! And take it as early as possible. I have learned so much from this class about history as a discipline –– we read foundational history texts and I understand the role of the historian much more now. Know that this isn't your traditional history seminar –– you're not learning about particular time periods or regions as much as ways of framing and constructing history. But this class will help you in all future history classes, so its value to you is higher the earlier you take it. Professor Chalhoub and Dana are also both amazing –– go to their office hours and get to know them both.
I would tell future students to take this course, especially history concentrators, because it helps lay the foundation for historical research and allows to understand how our own biases can influence how we construct historical narratives.
Take this class! It is probably my favorite class I have taken at Harvard. While no background is necessary, I think having taken a history class already (especially HIST 97) really enhances the experience. It will completely challenge the way you engage with history and historical writing. The perspectives you read are relevant in all future classes. Prof Chalhoub does a wonderful job leading discussion. The workload is incredibly reasonable, and I strongly advise genuinely engaging with the readings because they make discussion so much more enjoyable. Getting to dive deeply into one historian's work throughout the semester is also a wonderful way to engage with history.
