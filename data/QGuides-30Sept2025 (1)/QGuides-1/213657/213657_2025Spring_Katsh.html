Amazing class on the ideas of harm, crime, punishment, and criminal law. I don't think class made me (more) excited to go to law school, but it was still fantastic. You honestly learn a lot about super interesting ideas like free will, neuroscientific perspectives on free will, what constitutes harm, how we conceptualize crime, etc. The workload (readings + reading response papers every once in a while) are super manageable (honestly, could be more for a class that is an upper–level seminar) and you learn a lot just by going to class. Advice on doing well is to take notes on the readings WHILE you do them/shortly after the corresponding class because it will make studying for the midterm quiz much easier. Finsh your response papers early, go to OH and chat with Gabe, and spend a lot of time/thought on your final paper, and you will get a lot out of this course! Highly reccomend.
GABE IS AMAZING!! While the final paper is definitely a conceptual challenge, Gabe prepares you incredibly well for everything in the class. His lectures are great, the readings are hard but manageable, and the grading is fair. Wish there was more prep before final paper but its still doable!
If you are interested in different theories of punishment and how the debate surrounding free will complicates these justifications, liberal neutrality, and theories around why we criminalize specific behaviors, or are interested in going to law school, TAKE THIS COURSE. Gabe is an excellent teacher who balances making sure we are understanding and going over the complex philosophical concepts but also facilitating discussion on how these concepts apply to both current events or theoretical situations.
Gabe is an incredible professor. You have to be prepared to put in the work for the midterm and the final paper, but the topics are so interesting and the discussions are so compelling it sometimes didnt feel like work at all. Cannot recommend enough
Super interesting readings and discussion topics. You also learn a little about criminal law though most of it is philosophy. Gabe is also super friendly and accessible to ask questions.
Gabe is one of the best professors I have had at Harvard and this Gov 94 is one of the best classes I have taken (and his other Gov 94 is another one of my favorite classes as well). This course is everything you could want: the reading isn't too consuming, but it's immensely engaging and you learn a lot from it; the discussions are rich, very related to real world events, and they push your thinking; the material is exciting to think about especially if you're interested in the American legal system; Gabe is an encouraging instructor who provides great feedback and makes himself very available for office hours; Gabe also uses Poll Everywhere to direct class discussion quite often which I think is very effective. In short, being a part of both his seminars was a highlight of this past school year and I think anyone would be really lucky to take this class. I hope you will enjoy it as much as I did!
