Comments
This fantastic class will help you connect with an Indigenous language and new ways of thinking. Participation is key, and you can expect to learn a lot from Professor Mendoza Morí and your classmates.
This class is super interesting!! Especially if you want a small class or simply want to learn more about Quechua, I would highly recommend taking this course.
It's a very lovely class! Not too difficult, though it does require real thought throughout.
If you have a strong connection or affinity for Global Indigeneity, definitely apply to take this course. The Quechua–language learning component isn’t as big of a component as I thought, but the people you’ll meet taking the course opens up a whole new world.
This class is truly eye–opening to problems that millions of Indigenous people face and yet are often ignored by society. It teaches about history that was erased and tells the perspective from those who have been voiceless for many years. You should take this class to truly learn about the people who inhabited the Americas before colonization and how they have persisted since. It also teaches you many phrases and words in Quechua in a supportive environment.
Américo is a great professor with a passion for this topic. Being able to really engage with the content and broaden my understanding of Indigenous issues about not just Andean communities but efforts globally was a big takeaway of mine. The class isn’t too time consuming and is consistent on assignments and expectations. Make sure to participate (not just once a class, really communicate your thoughts); it matters so much more than you think. Use OH to your advantage as they really provide insight about how you can make the most of the class. I would’ve liked to learn more Quechua through the language–learning component, but I am more than satisfied with what we were able to practice
