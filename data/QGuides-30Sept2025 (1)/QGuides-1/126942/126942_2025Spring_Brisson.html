This course was quite easy when I took it. There was only two days of class and few homework assignments. While I did continue my French and get more comfortable, I do not feel as if I have improved my skills as much as I would have liked.
French 30 is a great class to improve your French speaking and understanding skills. You do a lot Podcast assignments, but also a couple of writing assignments, so it is definitely a more holistic approach compared to the classes prior.
Don't take it. The class structure sucks, the assigments are poorly developed and there is not consistent work structure
The class moves really slow. If you want to become fluent, I don't think this is the class for you. The assignments felt like they popped up out of nowhere and I'm sure you could get through the class without really learning anything.
French 30 is a good course for fulfilling the language requirement if you have some level of French background (the levels of speaking especially were widely varied within my section so don't be worried if you are not the best at speaking). I personally did not find the actual contents of the course very interesting, but you do learn a good amount of necessary grammar, some of which will likely be review from previous courses, but in much greater depth. The assignments for this course are not very heavy, but they do have a quick turn around (usually 3 or 4 days) for the podcasts and articles/redactions. The exams were fairly simple, all focused on grammar and writing; as long as you pay attention during class and practice the grammar you will do great on this component. I don't feel that this class vastly improved my French when it comes to speaking (which was my main goal going into it), but it was overall helpful as a refresher for the French that I had learned prior.
This class is chill and there are not many in person assessments!
