If you want to take a Harvard class that truly feels like a Harvard class, take this class. Professor Damrosch is one of the great professors at the college, and even if it was a purely two hour lecture, you'd learn so much that it would be worth it. The course readings are all fascinating and discussion is fantastically free–flowing. If you have any interest in literature at all, take this class!
You really get out what you put into it; there's a lot of grade uncertainty because it's all weighted on the final paper
Take this class!! Professor Damrosch is one of the leading scholars in his field, and all the readings are amazing. It is not a crazy amount of work (weekly reading and discussion posts), and the conversations are super fun! The one thing to be careful with is that 100%. of your grade is the final paper, and you will most likely be competing against Juniors/Seniors who concentrate in Humanities. Other than that, this class is perfect!
This is a great course if you want a reason to read. However, the reading load is quite heavy (at least from my STEM concentrator opinion). You basically read a short novel every week. There is only one big assignment (10 page final paper). Other than that, you are just expected to post in a weekly discussion.
This was a pretty solid course. Interesting readings, although sometimes a bit random/off–theme (for example, there was a whole unit about memory and reflection which kind of came out of nowhere). There is one long paper, but it is manageable. Discussions were frequently interesting even if they were sometimes tedious. Took this as an elective so not sure how it compares to other complit, but I enjoyed reading some unique works on the boundary of what it means to be a novel from throughout history.
This is a great class! The readings are well selected, and Professor Damrosch is very insightful and good at leading sections. While the reading load is on the higher side, it's definitely worth it to do all the readings to get the most out of the class.
Take it! You will learn so much!
This class is so incredible because of Professor Damrosch! Hes so smart and invested in his students – I cannot recommend it enough.
It's a great class if you want to invest your time reading novels. Recommended to take.
