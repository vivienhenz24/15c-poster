Julie is a fantastic professor, and this class taught me a lot about politics (even though I thought I knew a lot already). Highly, highly recommend.
I fully recommend this class. Julie is a great instructor and she is passionate about what she does. Beyond that, she is extremely accessible and wants you to grow as a student and person. One thing that stuck out about her from the remainder of my professors this semester was her questions about who I was as a person: where I came from, how I was finding Harvard, what I liked and why I liked it, etc.. As a first semester freshman, this meant a lot to me.

As far as course materials go, most of them are interesting. Some are a little bit on the longer side, but Julie provides reading guides each week that highlight the main parts of passages to be read. These are extremely helpful, and they taught me a lot about how to read things I was assigned in other courses. The final project is super straightforward, and there are milestones to keep you on track throughout the semester (I think this works for me, but if you don't like this structure, keep that in mind). If you are selected for this class, I highly encourage you take the opportunity. It was really insightful and I learned a lot about holding government actors accountable.
This seminar is super interesting and very captivating for anyone interested in politics/government. Julie is a truly amazing person and you can tell she is super excited to teach this content. She cares deeply for the class and works hard to involve our own interests in the course framework. The Capstone project is also super cool as you get to research your own accountability mechanism and explore individual passions related to the content.
I highly recommend ranking this first–year seminar as your top choice! Dr. Weaver is excellent at moderating discussions and asking thought–provoking questions to shape your conversations about accountability. You'll be able to extend all the learning from the discussion past this class and blend your knowledge of real–world examples with the theory you learn in weekly readings. Dr. Weaver also gives excellent feedback throughout the course on your final topic focus, and she is willing to support your research on any idea.
Professor Weaver is great and is a good connection to have if you are interested in democratic backsliding. She made the environment very chill and not stressful at all. Do the readings and connect the topic of discussion to outside experiences. Accountability is a very interesting topic. It can get a bit repetitive towards the end, but the final project allows for a lot of creative freedom.
Definitely take this class!! Julie is wonderful and facilitates engaging discussion. The topics are super interesting and relevant to current events. Overall, I think that you get out of this class what you put in: if you do all the readings and put some thought into making your final paper about a topic you're interested in, then I think you'll learn a lot; conversely, you could probably get by with doing not that much and then not feel like it was a meaningful experience.
Be attentive and speak in class!
This class provides an amazing introduction to not only political accountability theory, but to political science research and discussion in general. The reading is substantial but not overbearing, and I found that many of the readings were engaging, easy reads because they center around narrative examples of accountability in action. Professor Weaver provides useful advice on how to read large portions of text in a productive manner, and her feedback has made me a better writer and thinker. For future Government concentrators especially, this seminar will give you confidence and enthusiasm as you enter the field!
