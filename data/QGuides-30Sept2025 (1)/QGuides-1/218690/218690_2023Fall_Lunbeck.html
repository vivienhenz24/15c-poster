genuinely my favorite course I've taken during my years at Harvard! could not more strongly recommend a course! I absolutely love lunbeck, she is such an icon, truly the kindest, warmest, most caring professor I've had the privilege to get to know! the readings are so fascinating, as someone who doesn't take many reading–heavy courses + always struggled a bit with keeping up with readings in other humanities courses, I devoured the readings in this course + found them to be so fascinating and eye–opening, augmented by insightful discussions in class. it did feel like a lot of reading at times (for me at least), averaging 100+ pages per week, but I found it all very valuable to building + expanding my worldview! would recommend the course just for lunbeck + allison (who was amazing as per usual, so understanding + encouraging, extremely helpful with pinpointing interesting directions to take my essays), as they are absolutely the best!! I'm really going to miss this class!! if you're fortunate enough to get in, absolutely a must–take.
take it!
This class tackles a lot of questions and issues super relevant to our own lives, and each session is simultaneously therapeutic and educational, which I've really appreciated. Professor Lunbeck is a wonderful teacher and shares so many interesting insights into the readings we examine, and Allison is simply a gem!
I loved this course! I highly recommend it; Professor Lunbeck is so kind and knowledgeable and the material is interesting. Start your precis papers early and go to office hours for the assignments if you need guidance. I appreciated how this class challenged my understanding of society vs. the individual when we consider psychopathology. 10/10
this is such a fantastic class. if its offered again, please take it. not only are the assignments creative with the prompts, but were allowed a lot of freedom to write about what we desire for the papers. there were some weeks where the readings hit so close to home or described a feeling or phenomenon that I had seen/heard/experienced before, but couldnt put a name to, so it was extremely satisfying & enlightening to read those. the professor and TF were so so kind, and you can tell they both generally cared so much about the material. Even though its a long ass, we had smaller group discussions a lot that helped the time pass by a lot faster. this was my first histsci course, and the professor helped in really making me feel welcome & comfortable enough to share my thoughts every class. the grading was nice too and the comments provided helped me improve on my next papers.
Take this class if you are interested in the psychopathologies of today! Great class! Totally recommend!
