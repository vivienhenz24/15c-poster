What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
The material was interesting but I would not recommend this course. The instructor was teaching for the first time and it really showed. Made huge deals out of the smallest things and paid an incredible level of attention to detail that's almost impossible to please. The presentations were graded on a scale of 1 to 10, but papers only received letter grades? Grading wasn't really consistent that way. We also wasted a lot of time in pointless debates amongst the class, where we were learning nothing. Heard that she invited students to discuss her feedback but would get offended if you disagreed with it in the slightest.
Overall, it was a good course, and I feel like I learned a lot about challenges with EVs in the US and worldwide at the end. The instructor was clearly knowledgeable and experienced about the subject, but it seemed she initially struggled to find an appropriate teaching style for undergraduates, especially after coming back from the corporate world. In fact, I personally found some of her responses/reactions to students in the first couple of classes quite questionable (cutting off when students were still speaking, etc.). Her teaching style has gradually improved, and the course felt more interactive and comfortable. Another thing is this course can easily get too technical. It might be a fit for some people, but I would note this as a reservation.
