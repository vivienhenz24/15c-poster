What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If you want to learn about how to study antidepressants, stress and susceptibility, and depression and anxiety, and/or want to learn how to read, write, interpret, and critique scientific writing, this class is perfect! It provides an incredibly deep and comprehensive dive into all the methods of studying psychiatric topics in depression and mental health disorders and illuminates the underlying mechanisms and current research on the topic. It can take a bit of time to get into if you have very little experience reading papers and discussing investigatory techniques, but I think anyone who doesnt have experience with these topics and wants to learn more should take this class. It was tough at first, but I learned so much that Ive gone on to use in my research and proposals! Its also made reading scientific papers so much more intuitive and easy for me. Overall, if youre interested in psychiatric disorders and research, you should take this class!
take the class to learn at a low entry cost. although class was pretty disorganized at times, dr. burek is super sweet and i know she has great potential to teach this class even better going forwards!
This tutorial gives you the opportunity to really learn something about a field of research – the papers are not always super easy (some are, but a few were more difficult) but they provide an excellent survey of the field of stress susceptibility and resilience at the molecular level, and Dr. Burek's honest and fair but constructive feedback on assignments is very helpful in developing into a better thinker and writer about neuroscience research, while still putting you in a position to do well in this course provided you put in an effort. My only real complaint about this course is its meeting time: 6–7:15pm on Mondays does not sound like a fun time to have a class, and indeed it is not.
