Take advantage of the small class size and participate a lot in discussions. Also ask a lot of questions about the problem sets because they were just as interesting as content introduced in class.
If you have any interest at all in how language works, I would really recommend taking this course. It gives you an introduction to a lot of linguistics without the stress of a graded course, and the professor takes advantage of that lack of pressure to be very exploratory in his approach to the subject and allow all sorts of different viewpoints on how language operates to be heard in class. The readings are also phenomenally interesting, with them speaking to very deep and fascinating facts about language. As a math/logic oriented person, I found the formal aspects of the course particularly interesting, but there were also sections that were particularly interesting to the sociolinguists in the class, so there was a good mix. Professor Chierchia is wonderful; he very clearly cares a lot about his students and is deeply excited to speak to people interested in his subject and make the seminar a welcoming space for them, by organising optional out of class events, making himself eminently available for questions and help, and giving everyone a chance to speak in class. The class is structured around student led presentations of the reading topics each week followed by the Professor going a bit deeper and then a freeform discussion, which in my view takes advantage of the seminar structure quite effectively. This is a general theme – Professor Chierchia takes advantage of the less stressful structure of a seminar course to take a very exploratory, curious approach to the class that I feel is very good at sparking your interest. I will definitely be coming back to the materials from this course several times in future, they are incredibly interesting. The class also just puts a lot of interesting questions in your mind I feel.
Gennaro is a really great teacher in the small section environment. He clearly cares about all his students and is always there to help out. The content can be very confusing, but the class does not require much work and has some really interesting themes once you start to understand formal language and theories.
Take this course! Gennaro is a wonderful professor and really cares about his students, making this course such a gem while you also learn a lot. It is a very chill class and we had pizza and cannolis on multiple occasions.
Overall, this is a pretty chill seminar if you're interested in linguistics! Gennaro is a very sweet, wholesome old man who reminds me of my grandpa. He's really very passionate about semantics, and I think explains things quite well. If you're confused or struggling, he's more than willing to spend time to explain it to you. I also appreciate his effort to simplify technical notation or concepts for us he doesn't just expect us to understand really dense and niche material. His slides are also pretty intuitive and easy to follow.

The P–Sets are kind of light work, only 4 of them and you get one every 2–3 weeks. Some of the problems are really quite hard, though infrequent and on the shorter side. I took this class alongside 21a and Expos and didn't find it to be a very busy class with lots of busy–work, but all the homework assigned is quite meaningful and helpful. Grading is also charitable, if not generous. On the whole, a solid class for those interested in linguistics. Just be prepared for your 1–2 presentations, and skim the readings before you sign up for which class content you'd like to present. Some of it is quite hard, especially the readings on formal modelling, and logic notation which are a bit more technical.
The professor is one of the nicest professors I ever met and feel free to ask questions especially on the PSets!
Definitely take this class! The professor is an absolute delight, and the support he will give you is unrivaled. It's a wonderful experience, and you will not regret taking it!
