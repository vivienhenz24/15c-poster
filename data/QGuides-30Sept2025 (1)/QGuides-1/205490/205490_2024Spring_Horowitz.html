This class can sometimes be weird, and readings can be heavy, but content is very interesting. Prof. Natterson–Horowitz will sometimes cold–call (or question) details from the readings, so make sure you do them in depth. Be active and engaged in class, and bring your name tag to every class lol (even the last few classes).
The first 3–4 seminars were amazing. Professor Natterson–Horowitz is super passionate about this research, her presentations are really engaging, and at that point in the seminar, the concepts were really novel and exciting ways to think about human health.

However, the rest of the semester kind of devolved into repeating a lot of the same ideas. Professor Natterson–Horowitz is obsessed with Nicholas Tinbergen and the course talked so much about him and his life even though his biography wasnt part of the syllabus. I wish we had talked more about the research papers that were on the syllabus instead of just recounting the content of the book chapters. Professor Natterson–Horowitz would cut students off if their responses werent worded /exactly/ how she imagined. Students who made it to her office–hours were fed the lines to say at the right time in class.

The questions on the exams and the final project showed that this class lacked any meaningful rigor. By rigor, I dont mean difficulty but rather the practical applicability and scientific soundness of the material taught. Harvard students in an upper–level biology course shouldnt be testing a hypothesis by writing an experiment where they throw water–balloons at 1000 randomly–assigned mice to stop them from masturbating and then compare their lifespan as some sort of metric of survival. If you tried to do anything more realistic, like an observational study or looking at tissue samples for XYZ proteins as a measure of health rather than just measuring lifespan, you would get points deducted. It felt silly. The final presentation was all about researching random parts of Tinbergens life (eg: his gut microbiome or social embarrassment) and linking it to how it could have contributed to his depression to support Professor N. H.s hypothesis about why he killed himself. Most people found whatever study popped up first when they searched depression + [key word] and were just trying to say what she wanted to hear.

The course content overall felt like it was trying too hard to be relevant by being edgy (sexual assault, eating disorders, etc), where in reality, most of it is just pop–science.
Take this class! Professor Natterson–Horowitz is extremely knowledgeable and you will learn so much. I'm doing a secondary in HEB and this is one of my favorite classes I've taken.
