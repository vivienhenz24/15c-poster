I am not a big fan of this class. I'm not sure how another course like Math 101 would be for satisfying the discrete mathematics requirement, but I recommend it with reservations.

First, this course is flipped classroom style, meaning you watch the lectures on your own time and then during class, you work on problems with your assigned group. It was a bit annoying at first having to watch lecture during my own time, but I think that the lectures themselves were nice and helpful, especially since I was able to pause at any time. The one thing that frustrated me was that when we got to class, we would have a lecture upwards of 45 minutes on the content in the lecture that was supposed to be watched at home or on completely new material. This was frustrating because it took away time from being able to work through problems, and it defeats the purpose of this class being flipped classroom. I think that there needs to be a set decision on making this class 100% flipped or 100% lecture style. I don't think the class style the way I saw it was productive to my learning.

Honestly, I don't even know how helpful the problem solving in class was, mostly as a result of my groupmates. The aim is to collaborate on problems, yet there I had several experiences where individuals would just interrupt and try to get their thought/idea down as if they had something to prove to everyone around them. This then made it difficult to try and figure out problems myself when I was struggling since they would just fly through them.

Additionally, there were an abundance of office hours yet not enough at the same time. Most of the office hours were crammed on Sunday (which makes sense since the weekly PSets are due Monday), yet there were hardly any on Fridays or Saturdays. There were a few scattered around the week, but I just had class all day three days a week making it difficult for me to go to those. A lot of the office hours were also at the SEC, so that made it hard to go to them as well since my other classes were in the yard.

The cohort of CAs in this class were extremely helpful and kind. There were several review sessions during reading period that helped mmensely in preparing for the final. Adam and Rebecca were also good overall, but I felt like they could be condescending at times.

Finally, this class meets 3 days a week at 9:45 AM at the SEC. I'm a morning person, but this was still annoying. I think the fact that it starts "off" from the FAS courses made it difficult for me because it caused me to push all my other classes later, and I had to schedule the rest of my classes to go until 4:15. Again, this made it difficult to go to office hours, and it made it hard to study and truly understand those concepts as well.

Overall, I would try to shop another class on top of this one that satisifies the same requirement to see which one suits you best. I think a lot of people did enjoy this class, but I don't think it was the right fit for me given my schedule and learning style.
