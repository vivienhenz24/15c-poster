Section for this class is really interesting –– my TF, Emma, was amazing! I did not find lectures to be as useful for engaging with the material. Overall, there is a fair amount of work in this course, but I got a lot out of it.
good class as long as you do the readings. cannot stress enough how important actually doing the readings is. if you set aside time to set them done the class will be overall easy, but the readings are long and difficult so management is important
This course is more challenging than you think. Very challenging and dense reading, hundreds of pages per week. However, Shai is a great prof and the Tfs do an amazing job of explaining the dense reading. Very interesting content and I liked our media analysis. Field trips to the museum and library were also fun.
If you take this class, you need Emma as your TF!! She is amazing! The course as a whole is also great, and Shai is a wonderful teacher. I liked the mix of formal lectures and more informal visits to museums. That being said, I might not take this course if you're not a sociology concentrator or have an interest in sociological theory because the material can be pretty dense at times. But overall a great class!
I thought I would hate this class because it would be a basic boring requirement class, but I LOVED IT! I genuinely thought it was very interesting and I enjoyed going to section to unpack the theorists. It is taught very well and the assignments are not too bad. Its just important to learn how to read in a way that you get what you need without having to read every single page with copious margin notes
Jane was an amazing TF. Dromi is a great lecturer!
Though the readings are heavy, being able to interact with the original texts of the sociologists will be an experience that will help grow your critical thinking and understanding of the theories. Being able to review the theories in section and be able to apply it in modern day examples in lecture was very helpful and fun in understanding the theories.
Going into SOCIOL 97, I was nervous about taking a sociological theory course and what the courseload would look like. Honestly, I think the hardest part of the course is in the first couple weeks because you get thrown off the deep end a little bit with Weber, Durkheim, and Marx all in a row. I felt quite confused and getting lost in the dense theory, but I think the teaching staff does a great job of guiding you through it and helping you understand their theoretical traditions/perspectives. I ultimately feel like it was worth it to learn about them, even though it was challenging at first, because I'm glad to have a working understanding of the canonical sociological theories as a jumping off point for my future interaction with sociology and the social sciences in general. Basically, this class is not a bad class to fulfill a requirement and the teaching staff is quite understanding and accommodating (Jane is a great TF). It might feel slightly intimidating at first, but after the first few weeks the theories become much easier to read and grasp, so don't worry!
The class is interesting and you will learn a lot but please do the readings ahead of time because they are quite dense and can be hard to understand.
This course is extremely reading–heavy. You can't always skim and get what you need out of the readings. The teaching staff provides a lot of support for the midterm and final paper. Do beware that discussion posts are graded harshly. Attend sections (part of your grade anyway) and lectures! Shai is a great lecturer so whatever I didn't learn from section, I would understand during lecture.
Lecture was very boring, sections were great. Only two major writing assignments, so work very hard to get a good grade on both or else your overall course grade will not be good!
You are most likely taking this class because it's a sociology requirement, but this has genuinely been one of my favorite classes at Harvard. My TF, Jane, was incredible so supportive, caring, and passionate about the course material. These readings will shape the way you see the world, and I've grown to love theory even more through this class.
If you are interested in delving into social theory properly, take this course. You get out of it what you put into it – you can technically skirt by barely doing the readings, but it is worthwhile for your own knowledge to dedicate the appropriate time. Grading is fair, not an easy A but not harsh either.
