If youre looking for a gened to fulfill your science and technology in society requirement, take this class! It was a wonderfully paced class that really made great use of all the class time and homework assignments each week to teach you about how to use and apply generative AI tools. I really enjoyed the lectures, assignments, and readings and thought that they were all well designed and picked with care to help you build an understanding from the ground up in an accessible way no matter what your background was. There were many interesting guest lecturers throughout the course that offered unique perspectives from academic and industry points of view. You have a lot of creative freedom to explore and challenge yourself with GAI tools for many of the assignments and the course staff would happily meet you halfway to help you achieve your goals for your final project. It was a great class that didnt feel like it was wasting your time with busy work; every assignment felt meaningful while also managing to be low stress and often open–ended. You can definitely learn a lot and get a lot out of this class. Personally, I felt that it taught me how to use GAI tools in much more creative ways than I thought it could be used, and as the tools continue to evolve, I think its a fantastic course to take to get a baseline understanding for how AI might shape the future.
GEM!!!! so so so much fun and really easy workload. even the final project and exam felt definitely not as difficult as other classes' finals. overall had a lot of fun in lecture, section, and outside of class experimenting with AI. I really loved this class, probably one of my favorites.
Take this course! Possessing knowledge about AI and its future implications is absolutely necessary in today's world. The course is really easy (almost no out–of–class work) and requires no technical experience. Nevertheless, you walk away having learned so much about how AI works from a technical standpoint, how to use AI responsibly (always as a co–worker and coach, and never as a crutch), AI's future impacts on society, etc. AI is moving at such a rapid pace that if you do not work to understand it now, you'll miss a major opportunity.
This is an interesting class that perhaps in a future iteration would be better and more worth taking. As someone with no background in AI and without much STEM experience at Harvard, it was cool to learn in a low–stakes setting a bit more about how Chat works. That said, so much of this class is busy work: I get that practice is necessary to work better with AI tools, but a lot of it can be boiled down to "use ChatGPT to do [task]". It's also cool in a vacuum to consider the ethical implications of AI, but it coming later on in the semester when other deadlines are piling up just meant for me that this class fell lower on my priority list. Which is a shame because Logan is an entertaining and knowledgeable lecturer.
