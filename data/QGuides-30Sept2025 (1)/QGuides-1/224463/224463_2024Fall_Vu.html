If this course is offered again PLEASE TAKE IT! This class focuses on real finding in political psychology and each class we deep dive into different studies and scientific papers. I never truly understood how to interpret data from a scholarly article prior to taking this class, but Professor Vu was such a great teacher and was so good at breaking down concepts. She was also so knowledgeable and passionate about the subject matter and it made me excited to learn. The class was unbiased and we really let the data speak for itself instead of letting partisanship get in the way, I learned so many perspectives and opinions that I would not have learned had I not taken this class. Also, I loved the assignments, there was a lot of room to be creative which I appreciated, never thought I'd say this but I looked forward to writing the papers in this class! But the highlight is honestly Professor Vu, if she doesn't teach this class again but teaches another I strongly suggest you enroll in one of her classes. She cares so much about her students and want all of us to succeed. She is so professional, personable, funny, she is such a great educator and I hope she is here to stay! Again, PLEASE TAKE THIS CLASS YOU WON'T REGRET IT!!

She also has us use Perusall for the readings so either way you have to do them but they are really helpful and the entire class is based on the readings. Take this course. Participate and put effort into the assignments; you will gain so much from it.
This course was unlike any other psychology course I've taken at Harvard. It provided a really thorough overview of a scientific field as it should be taught –– a series of discussions that show an approximation of the truth. It was really interesting to see papers that showed bias in the research design or interpretation of the results, and I think political psychology is the perfect area to show how bias can affect research. The coursework really helps you to identify your own political motivations, biases, and emotions and develop your own understanding of how psychological factors affect political thought and behavior. All in all, this course is an experience I think all social psychology–interested and political science–interested people should have.

This class is what you make it. This was Professor Vu's first semester here, and at times I could feel the experimentation. However, when we gave her suggestions, she took them. She moved away from the fear–inducing cold–calling and incorporated more group work/conversation, which was definitely comforting. At the beginning of the year, if you came to class without doing the readings and got cold called, you were in hot water. However, as mentioned, we moved away from that after mid–semester feedback. Don't forget to do the Persuall; it should be an easy 100%. If you don't get full credit, just as Prof Vu why. Scroll consistently for an hour on the doc and leave 4 spread–out comments, and you should get full points. Prof Vu's computer policy went back and forth, so if she tells you to close your computer, just do it. It makes it uncomfortable for everyone in the room if she has to tell you again.
