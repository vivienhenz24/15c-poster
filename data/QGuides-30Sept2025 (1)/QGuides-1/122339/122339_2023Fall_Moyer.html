This class was an experience that I'd definitely say was worth it – the people you meet are great, the labs are designed to both get you to meet new classmates and learn how to work really well in small groups in general. I took this class originally thinking it was going to be more on the biological/genetic engineering side than the mathematical–modelling side (though there is still a decent amount of anatomy), but I still ended up learning a lot.
If you're thinking about taking this, two things to note: First, Computer Science (Matlab) experience isn't explicitly necessary, but having a very basic background of useful CS skills like how to debug, what a function is, etc. come in VERY handy over the first few labs while you figure out everything else that's going on the class. Second, the class was a lot more work than I expected. There's your typical STEM schedule of 3x a week lecture plus lab, but travel to and from the SEC is basically another class's worth of time in your schedule due to transportation, and having both a weekly PSET plus a weekly lab report made it almost 2 PSET classes worth of work a week. Which is definitely manageable, just...don't take it with three other PSET classes or anything.
Also TAKE LECTURE NOTES. The equations and work you cover in class are very similar to what's on the PSETs and quizzes (basically midterms), so if you understand those pretty well, you'll be fine, but the posted lecture slides after class are like half–blank so it's really important to be there every day or get someone to fill you in on the work you missed if you can't go.

Additionally, some coding experience is helpful; this course involves some substantial MATLAB data analysis in labs and psets. If you have no coding background, this coding will take significant time and may be frustrating. But learning to code here can also be rewarding and useful in future classes.
