Hank Yang does not usually teach this course. It may be better when the usual professor is teaching it, but as it is now I would not take this class if it was not required. The labs consisted of the TF doing the lab while students watched in small groups, then filled out the lab report (essentially a problem set). There was minimal support and not much direction for the optional course project.
I liked this course far more than I thought I would. A large reason for this is probably Hank's brilliant instruction. He is one of the best professors I've ever had at Harvard and, while he won't be the professor of 155, I highly recommend that you take a class with him.
Go to every office hours because they are very helpful. Take detailed notes in class and save all our work as it will be very helpful for the midterms.
ES155 is required for EE, but it's truly and awesome class! Control theory is so interesting. Make sure to have a PSET group to grind through the work (or attend office hours regularly) and you'll be chilling. I recommend checking out the textbook for more comprehensive definitions of class materials! :)
Such a great course! You might feel confused during lecture, and that's okay. A lot of the learning comes from rewatching the lecture while doing the problem sets, and researching certain topics/properties (YouTube has a ton of alternative tutorials and diagrams that supplement the lecture very well: Brain Douglas, MATLAB channel, Steve Brunton (legend)). The lectures cover a lot of the derivation for the topics, which for me was very hard to follow. Knowing them is great, but it is not essentially. Mainly, you should know how to apply the final result (how to use them on the problem set). I didn't read the book but I'm sure it would clear up some things.
This class is a great introduction to systems and control, and Hank is a very approachable professor!

While having a background in linear algebra is not required, I think it helps tremendously with the first half of the course. For the second half, no background is necessary. Personally, I found the first half a bit more mathematically challenging and the second half more conceptually difficult to grasp, but this may vary.

Small note: start pset 3 early! It's quite time consuming, but luckily an outlier.
Heng is an amazing lecture!!! Hopefully he teaches it again. He is very smart and comes to class well prepared. He reviews the material in multiple occasions and makes sure students get it. At times you might loose track of the long mathematical formulas written in the board if you don't pay attention during class so either pre–read on the material or watch lecture afterwards for maximum benefits. Psets are manageable and exams aren't too hard: they resemble the psets.
Dont start slacking off towards the end because it ramps up and its easy to fall behind. Lecture notes were great.
Very good class
Overall a very interesting course that teaches you about controls and the practical applications of linear algebra in engineering (and also how to learn matlab along the way). Hank is a fun professor who goes really in–depth in his lectures so you can get a pretty good understanding of the general scope of the topic (without boring you with too many specifics). I didnt have any experience in matlab before this course, but I still think I did fine. However, I highly recommend some linear algebra experience (Math 21b, 22a) before this course because pretty much the entire first half is reliant on it. Still, highly recommend.
