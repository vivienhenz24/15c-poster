Comments
This is a cool class. I left the problem sets wondering how Prof. Vishwanath's brain was so big (the problems were interesting).

It's also way easier than I would've expected. The five PSets probably took on the order of 8 hours on average, and the material is basically that of 143b. If you've taken 143b, I doubt you'd learn a lot from this class, but it could still be worth taking because the time commitment is so low and you could selectively go to the more interesting lectures. Our in–person midterm was literally just matrix multiplication, and the take–home final was similar to a problem set.
This course provides a great combination of computational practice and conceptual ideas.
Very good course, generally quite well taught. Ashvin is a very clear, thoughtful lecturer. A more relaxed course than 251A, it feels like it covers a little less. But it is still challenging.

Problem sets were of varying difficulty, but generally quite helpful in learning the material well. Midterm was very reasonable.

Scattering was a bit confusing, but generally quite interesting course!
Ashvin s a wonderful lecturer, and the lectures are very clear. There are not too many homework assignments, which is very nice. You still get to learn a lot about a wide range of quantum topics. Loved this class!
Prof. Vishwanath manages to cover a good amount of basic quantum information theory, which is fascinating and very useful, while also covering the basics of quantum mechanics from a perspective that makes the mathematical underpinning (through representation theory) clear. The problem sets are always very satisfying and instructive. Would highly recommend.
