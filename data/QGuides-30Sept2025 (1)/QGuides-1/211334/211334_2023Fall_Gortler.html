The best way to clear the Linear Algebra requirement
As a standalone class designed to fulfill the linear algebra requirement for CS, I think this class is great. Having spoken with Prof. Gortler, the purpose of the class is not to completely cover linear algebra (hence discrepancies with 22a/21b), nor is it meant to go very in–depth with multi–variable calculus. Rather, it focuses on applicable material, and specifically, in our psets and in–class examples, we saw how the material could be applied to computer vision (Prof. Gortler's expertise) and machine learning.

The strengths of this course included its small class size (25–35), heavy assignment waiting (80% pset), minimal exams (20% total for two exams), and the quality of assignments. The one problem set per week offered both standard math problems and Matlab hands–on activities. I thought this was interesting, and the difficulty of the problem sets made them engaging and thought–provoking, while the time allotted minimized stress.

The lecture element of the course could have been better. The slides that Prof. Gortler used simply paraphrased the book, and a lot of the necessary visual/supplemental content was worked out on the board, but not explained in the slides or the book. In general, a lot of the concepts are somewhat abstract and it's made challenging because of the impossibility of visualizing high–dimensional plots. However, I think 2d or 3d diagrams in the slides or books for reference could have been helpful. Additionally, while it's clear how much Prof. Gortler knows about the material, his explanations were sometimes hard to understand.

In general, I think that so long as students attend class frequently to see the material not in the slides and put in an effort to try and get to know Prof. Gortler, the class experience is great and the work is very manageable.

Partner psets are very helpful if you have a heavy courseload/committments in the fall – one person does the LA/Multi work and another does the MATLAB. That being said the Math 22a psets aren't too hard, while Am22a psets usually have 2 questions that are HARD and the rest are easy/medium. A lot of people miss Am22a lectures because of the early morning long commute – and the online lectures are NOT that effecient at getting caught up in the material.
