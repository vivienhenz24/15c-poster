This is an AMAZING history course. You will learn so much Jewish history, and Professor Penslar is a great lecturer, very entertaining and engaging, so lecture will always feel interesting. The course does have a decent amount of assignments and grades at a higher standard than some other history courses, so I would recommend taking at least one other history or writing course at Harvard before taking this one.
The key to this class is forming connections between what may initially appear separate concepts. We revisit ideas from the beginning of the course throughout the entire semester, to it is critical to understand the overarching themes. You will not get a lot out of this class if you do not do the readings. Especially if you are not a history concentrator, they are a heavy lift, but definitely worth the time. Professor Penslar is a brilliant scholar and excellent conversationalist, so take advantage of office hours. Ultimately, this class will change your perspective on history if you engage deeply with it.
This is maybe my favorite course I've ever taken. If you are interested in history at all, TAKE THIS COURSE!!! The readings are a lot of primary sources, which is amazing, in my opinion! Prof. Penslar is an amazing teacher, and so is Tsiona, the TF. The material is well structured, and I can see through lines throughout history. I feel I have a comprehensive understanding of a lot of the material, learning a lot but still understanding it on more than a surface level. The assignments are engaging and difficult in the best possible way. An amazing course.

However, this is definitely an intensive course. It's not that hard to get a pretty good grade, but it is a lot of work. It is a lot of reading, and about 500–700 words ever other week, as well as a written midterm and final. It's a lot of writing, and a course that is best suited for people who already have a passion for history or other social sciences/humanities. If you are uncomfortable doing a bunch of reading and writing, you might find this class incredibly difficult. But, it is 100% worth the challenge, it is a super fun course, and if you put in the work, you get a lot out of it.
This is a great class for anyone at the college interested in the History Department. I took it as a first–year and a non–Jew and felt very comfortable and accepted amongst the instructors and peers in the class. The topic is somewhat niche, and the class is largely about engaging with personal identity for many students, but you will get no less enjoyment out of the good humor and interesting perspective Dr. Penslar provides if you are not of Jewish origin.
Take this course! It will be excellent whether you are Jewish or not Jewish (Jewish history isn't just for Jews –– anyone can be interested)! I am Jewish, and had a decent knowledge about Jewish History before going into the course, and even so, this course taught me so much. The readings are also super, super interesting –– the primary sources are fascinating. Penslar is an AMAZING lecturer. He is so thoughtful and also makes sure to engage students during lectures. TAKE THIS CLASS!!
Take it!
This course is wonderful; there are fun outside–of–class events you can attend. Professor Penslar truly cares about his students; you will learn a lot. There is a decent amount of work to do, but it is always relevant to the class.
This was a great course!
Take this class and any other with Professor Penslar! He is an amazing guide through complex and difficult subjects, taking an inquisitive approach to the past while ensuring everyone feels welcome to question and disagree with one another. His lectures are informative and hilarious. I particularly valued how the lectures and readings compliment one another. They do not rehash the same territory. The readings are intense and vary from week–to–week but not overwhelming.
