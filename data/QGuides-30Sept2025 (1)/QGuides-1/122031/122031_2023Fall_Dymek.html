Comments
If you come from 20b, do not take this class unless you feel comfortable about your german skills. THIS IS NOT A LANGUAGE LEARNING CLASS. You will get the most from the class if you can do the assignments without having to look up every single word that appears in the reading. I took 20b and felt okay about my german, and i wont say that my reading didn't get better after taking the class. You will be able to read advanced texts and will have the skills to look up words and decipher them. This is genuinely a good skill, but having to read read these texts with little to no guidance turns doing homework into a herculean task. Its dry and time consuming. And that's without the grammar, that you are expected to do by yourself and is barely discussed in class. TLDR: take another german class if you want to learn german, take this class if you wanna get better at reading.
Take this class!! You'll learn so much. If Prof. Dymek is teaching it, it will be your favorite class of the semester. It's very flexible and there's never pressure to be perfect. If you're walking the line between intermediate German and fluency, this class will definitely tip you over the edge.
I recommend. Do the work, and you will come out with a better understanding of German grammar. You can also take advantage of Dr. Dymek's office hours and insights as a native German speaker. Her insights lend themself to building better intuition with the German language.
Lots of fun! Take it if you want to have a very "conscious" understanding of German grammar. Though other courses may be better suited to speaking and listening abilities
Overall: Grammar rules and sentence parsing for German. You will read many philosophical texts mostly to translate and parse them rather than to talk about the content (if you want to talk about texts, take Dymeks 119B, there might even be a section taught in German if theres enough interest!). Sentence parsing is identifying parts of speech within a sentence to understand the sentences structure.

Professor: Dymek taught the course wonderfully. She is very passionate about the subject matter and it really is infectious. Though it is likely the course will return to being taught by Burgard.

Textbooks: The first two are required. The next two are not all that necessary. Mostly used dict.cc and dict.leo.org. Parsing texts are provided online. You will also need a very specific set of pens for parsing (https://www.amazon.com/Pentel–Arts–Color–12–Color–S360–12/dp/B0006Z5HSY?th=1) You won't need things immediately but just FYI.
1) Lehr– und bungsbuch der deutschen Grammatik Aktuell (978–3193072559)
2) Schlssel of above (978–3194072558)
3) Oxford German Dictionary (978–0199545681)
4) English Grammar for Students of German (978–0934034432) Useful for students unsure of their command of grammatical concepts and functions

Topics covered: including but not limited to: Imperativ, Plusquamperfekts; Transitive und intransitive Verben, die schwer zu unterscheiden sind; Rektion der Verben; dass– und Infinitivkonstruktionen; Modalverben zur subjektiven Aussage; Passiv, Futur I und II zum Ausdruck der Vermutung; Nebenstze, Konditionale/Konsekutive/Konzessive Nebenstze; umzu/ohnezu/anstattzu; Relativstze; Demonstrativpronomen; Adjektivendungen, Adjektive und Partizipien als Nomen; Adverbien mit Dativ/Akkusativ; Zustandspassiv; Partizipialkonstruktion; haben/sein mit zu und Infinitiv; Gerundivum; Konjunktiv I und II.

Course structure notes: Homework before every class: book exercises with self–corrections using Schlssel, and translation/parsing of a text. Four exams for grammar and parsing.
Make sure to review grammar rules regularly!
