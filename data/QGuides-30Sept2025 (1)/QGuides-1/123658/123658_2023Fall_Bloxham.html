What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If you like or are interested in boats and/or sailing, this is a reasonably fun and enjoyable course. It is definitely easy and will make your freshman fall less stressful. Though the material can get dull at times, the professor is really nice, and interesting to talk to, and the pizza is great!
This class is a very stress free course. It is a fun learning environment where you get to enjoy all of the material without having to worry much about it.
You do not have to be a sailor to take this class. I personally am not and yet I took away so much. I loved the physics aspects and using the basics to explain more complex ideas. It is absolutely a gem.
The Science of Sailing is a great option for freshman seminars with a very intriguing subject matter! Jeremy is very passionate about what he does and makes the course very informative. The content is quite heavy, although the only lengthy assignment is the final presentation, which has a lot of options to present upon.
This class, in my opinion is one of the greatest ways to start off your Harvard experience, especially if you are really enthusiastic about sailing. Professor Bloxham is one of the kindest and knowledgeable people you will meet at this campus. He will always answer any of your questions on physics topics, and he will always take care of you. The section meeting are enjoyable to be at and food is provided. I can honestly say this is a fantastic course and I'd recommend it to any one with some sailing background and some physics background.
This class is a very low time commitment.
If you like physics and / or sailing and want a very low key class with no homework and pizza every monday take this class. It's not the most interesting one even if you love sailing since the instructor is kinda boring, but it's definitely a great way to explore physics and sailing in a very chill environment.
