What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This is a really excellent class. Prof. Madrid is a phenomenal seminar leader and assigns fascinating, albeit challenging, readings which inevitably lead to stimulating discussions. He approaches the material with a serious but open mind and a strong sense of humour. The result is a class environment in which everybody is encouraged and keen to share, and in which the professor feels as much a participant as the students. If you are interested in histories of the archive, historiographical and phenomenological approaches to music and sound art, or decolonial thinking more broadly, this is an excellent class for you (even if you are not rooted in the Music Department).
This course is a thoughtful reflection on archives and their utility for the necessary construction of critical knowledge, as well as for familiarizing and empathizing with social dynamics out of the U.S. context. It requires extensive reading and analysis.
This seminar on the sound archive was excellent. The readings were well connected to each other. Be prepared to read several long chapters / articles every week (more than other graduate seminars).
This course is the best for any student who is interested in archival studies.
