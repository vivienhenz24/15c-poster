Overall this was a very interesting class that made us think about the microbial world in a different way than we previously had. That being said, it is probably one of the more work/time intensive seminars.
Amazing class for anyone even remotely interested in the sciences and particularly the life sciences. With occasionally dense reading material (textbook and research reports) and long lectures with a passionate and knowledgeable professor, it can be overwhelming in the first couple of weeks but is a wonderful class once you settle into it.
Take this class for sure if you have any interest in biology and micro–organisms

This class was amazing!! Not only was it extremely educational but it was so enjoyable and fun and we really bonded as a cohort. This class opened my eyes to a whole new world and has truly transformed with way I think about life on our planet and science generally. When applying for seminars I was looking for something that would expose me and challenge me in a new way and thats exactly what this course did. I learned to read, analyze, and report on scientific research papers and even write my own sort of science report on a major symbiosis discovery! We explored tons of different symbioses from all parts of the planet through reading, presenting symbioses to one another in class, and through films and videos. One of our favorite parts of this seminar was the field trips! We went to the New England aquarium, the Arboretum, and toured the herbarium on campus! In this seminar you will spend time discussing with the groups, learning from Professor Cavanaugh's extensive knowledge AND you will get to spend time in the lab!! Know you will need to do the assigned reading and always be prepared for classthis is the best way to get the most possible out of this course.
If you have the opportunity and any interest in biology or organisms, TAKE THIS COURSE!!! I cannot emphasize how much I recommend this seminar as it completely changed how I look at biology but also the world we live in; I learned about microbes, not as harmful enemies but as complex symbiotic entities that are fundamental for life to exist. Professor Cavanaugh makes class extremely interesting, entertaining, and at times, hilarious, making me look forward to class every week. The class is full of amazing FIELD TRIPS and labs while requiring a manageable amount of work that is not burdening. The class book "Multitudes" explains topics in such a fascinating way that makes the material engaging, unlike many dull science textbooks. I will most likely be an AFVS concentrator but this was my favorite class this semester! that's how good it is!!

Take it! This is a fun class. But, warning, it is expected for you know general biology so having taken AP Biology makes this class more understandable.
This is a great class if you have a general interest in microbial symbiosis and studying bacteria. If you are only interested in a few concepts on the syllabus, some of the lectures and readings might feel a bit slow. For example, I was really interested in the human microbiome but we didn't cover that content till the last few weeks of the course so some of the earlier concepts did not necessarily peak my interest. However, even if some of the topics are not in exact alignment with your interests, there are so many fun experiences that make this course worth it: field trips, lab, growing plants, field work. You spend a couple of Saturdays on these field trips so make sure you have availability for those. Overall, not a demanding class and very few assignments for a class that meets only once a week.
