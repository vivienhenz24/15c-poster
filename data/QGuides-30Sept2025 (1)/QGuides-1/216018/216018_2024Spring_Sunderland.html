This is a great course! At time the assignments can take some time but overall it is well worth it!
This class is excellent and a must–take as part of the ESE or ESPP curriculum! It provides a balanced overview of toxicology from both scientific and regulatory perspectives, and is plenty approachable from either area of interest. The course project was fun, the teaching staff is helpful and enthusiastic, and the workload is both manageable and valuable for learning. Be prepared for some tricky concepts and more quantitative assessments, but overall, I dont really have any complaints with the course and would not hesitate to recommend it to anyone interested, regardless of background preparation.
I would so recommend this course! I didnt think I was interested in environmental toxicology before taking this but it was the only elective that fit my schedule, and then I ended up so interested in the material and lectures and I loved it!
This class might make you a bit depressed about the state of our environment, but it also equips you with the awareness and tools needed to start making positive change. Elsie is an attentive and diligent and grades/ evaluates many assignments herself.
Great course for learning about toxicology topics. Assignments are fair and helpful and you learn a lot. Elsie is great! Take this course!
Take this class! I learned so much, and Professor Sunderland is such en enthusiastic professor. This was an engaging class with people full of different academic backgrounds and experiences, which made learning so much more fulfilling. This was such an interesting course simply because we seamlessly integrated math, chemistry, and policy into one learning experience. Our assignments and exams were difficult but fair. Definitely expect to spend some time outside of class on problem sets, as often times it was applying knowledge you learned to a new set of issues, Exams were difficult, so expect to spend some time preparing for them as well. Overall, I cannot recommend this class enough, and it has been one of my favorite classes that I've taken at Harvard.
This is a fabulous course, and I highly recommend taking it if only to take a class with Elsie Sunderland. The major strength of this course is the way it helps students grapple with real–world problems. It does so by incorporating diverse readings, class discussions, and nuanced explanations of policy challenges with the scientific foundation, modeling, and application of environmental toxicology principles. It's a great class for anyone in ESPP, ESE, EPS, or anyone who wants to learn about the intersections of environmental science, community issues, and public policy. The course material covers really difficult environmental issues and human/environmental considerations, and I came away from it feeling like I had a toolbox of perspectives and skills to start to tackle said issues in the real world. It was also one of the first ESE courses I've taken that explicitly covered environmental justice issues, which I appreciated.
I highly recommend this course! Elsie is a fantastic professor, and you get a great overview of toxicology in the U.S., as well as learning effective science communication. You put in the work when you need to, but its not too hard of a class at all.
It's a great class, and Prof. Sunderland is really hands–on in giving feedback perhaps more than any other class I've taken.
Elsie really is a wonderful expert, teacher, and person, and every class that she teaches carries her passion for the subject. I did struggle to pay attention in class when we talked about the specific characteristics of chemicals, but nothing that we talk about is crazy detailed chemistry and is very reasonable to study and review for the midterms. The homeworks can be hard to figure out, but office hours and teaching staff support are readily available.
This is the class I felt most torn about this semester. I thought the topic area was really interesting and important, but the organization of the material did not always work for me. I feel like we bounced from topic to topic. I loved learning how to use the models and wished the class had focused a bit more on modeling specifically. If you have background in environmental engineering and are interested in better understanding how to quickly understand the threat that chemicals pose or if you are interested in the science that underpins policy decisions, then I recommend this class! It's really informative and Elsie is super kind. The final project was quite challenging (this year it was a 5–7 minute video) because I had never done something like that before, but I think it will be different next year so don't let that influence you. Really cool subject matter that is important to know, but the class in general could be more streamlined.
You will enjoy this class! Make sure to go to office hours, though.
This class is a really interesting combination of science and policy that is great for anyone interested in toxicology or the sociopolitical impact of chemical pollution! Despite pretty much being a PSET class, the workload isn't terribly heavy and the grading on assignments is generous. The atmosphere of the class is very community–oriented and the teaching staff are all friendly and supportive. The projects were also very engaging and creative (making graphics, filming videos, etc) and it was neat to have a real impact on the sustainability initiatives at Harvard.

All that being said, I did come out of this class slightly frustrated at the structure/style of teaching. The content on assignments often felt beyond the scope/practice we were equipped for after lecture, and the midterms felt beyond the scope of the assignments. The calculations you are expected to do are very dense given the amount of time allocated for them in class (this became particularly challenging for me leading up to the midterm exams). There's also a baseline expectation of having some coding/Excel knowledge for some assignments, which isn't gone over particularly clearly in class.

As a whole, this class covers a very interesting intersection of environmental and social topics that gives you a wide sampling of issues without putting too much stress on your schedule. However, if you do not have previous experience in chemistry/engineering topics, expect to struggle a little with the course's more technical aspects.
