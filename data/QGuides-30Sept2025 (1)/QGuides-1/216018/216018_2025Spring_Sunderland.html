Everyone should take this class: it is vital information to learn about the world around you! The course content is very broad and you really get to stretch your mind and learn about so many things! It is also very important to learn about environmental and public health and safety. The course staff are lovely and the class discussions are very fun.
this class is a great elective! workload is managable, and so is the final project. definitely go to lecture becuase you'll have no idea what the slides are saying unless you're there. midterms and assignments are doable – utilize office hours!
161 is a wonderful experience! Elsie is a great lecturer and the content is consistently interesting. There's lots of focus on how to make regulatory decisions based on the science, which ended up being really interesting as a person who mostly focuses on the science side.
This is an excellent class––one of the few truly interdisciplinary classes I've taken than hasn't felt redundant or forced. The material all meshes really well and I emerged with a pretty complete understanding of the basics of env tox science and policy. Elsie is amazing––she has a very classroom–dry sense of humor and her teaching style is very responsive to students' needs. Highly recommend!
This course is amazing! Professor Sunderland is a wonderfully engaging lecturer and all the topics were well–taught. The breadth of material in this class, from chemistry, environmental modeling, to risk management, is unlike anything else I've taken here. These all felt intentional and well–linked to one another, so by the time the final project came around, everything seemed to click. A great course to count for the ESPP Science Requirement too.
If you're interested in engineering, I don't think that this is going to be the class for you because it's very chemistry and human biology based. The content can be interesting but it is difficult to engage if you aren't interested in public health. You can definitely pick up on the key points in the class and the exams are really fair. Where you will probably learn the most is the final project because you are actually using everything that you learn and applying it to real life situations but when you learn the content it can feel a little bit abstract.
This class was excellent. The selection of readings were really useful and enjoyable, and the assignments tied in nicely to the material we learned. Elsie is a wonderful professor that cares a lot about her students, and she gave really good feedback on all of the assignments we submit, including the midterms. The class ramps up for the final project– it was super rewarding but there's not always a clear direction. Overall, take this class if you're interested in it!
Manageable class. Not too many assignments and pretty easy midterms. Prof is very locked in, but supports conversation. I would recommend this class to students interested in the subject, but who don't want rigorous mathematical analysis or modeling.
Take this class to understand how pollution issues manifest in our society! The class definitely leans more heavily towards being an EPS/ESPP class than an ESE one but there are still some strong foundations in math and environmental science (understanding differential equations, chemistry, fluid transport will help a lot). Elsie is an awesome instructor and expert in the field and is super approachable and willing to engage/empower students. The course covers a lot of very important topics in the field of environmental toxicology/ pollution and I consider it an essential to anyone interested in the field of environmental protection!
Absolutely brilliant instructor and TFs. You will enjoy this course if you are interested in environmental science, particularly in the policy as well as the science in regards to risk assessment and risk management of environmental toxicology.
This class is great! Professor Sunderland and the teaching team were very passionate about the material and I learned so much!
