The course is challenging but worth taking. You will learn skills and develop intuition that will be invaluable for future endeavors. Dr. Chen is very thorough and challenges students with hard assignments and exams, but very gracious in grading and providing great feedback. Best course I've taken at Harvard.
A reasonably enjoyable course with interesting assignments that change every year. Make sure you know how to write efficient code, otherwise your assignments might take a long time to run. Also, I loved Tom's stories about old Soviet mathematics papers and the history of statistics!
Broad introduction to GEEs, GLMs, Mixed Models. Homeworks are not too time–consuming.
I think mixed modelling/random effects and GEE's are necessary tools/language for a statistician, so if you have no previous experience in these methods, BST245 is a great choice and use of an advanced elective for Biostatistics PhD's. This class is a useful review of these topics, both with an eye towards the statistical considerations but also towards the computational/optimization of the resulting likelihoods. Tom's class is a great balance of rigor and breadth. Assignments can be a bit involved but are worthwhile
