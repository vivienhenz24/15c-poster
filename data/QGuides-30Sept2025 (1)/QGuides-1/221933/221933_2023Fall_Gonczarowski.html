This was one of the cooler and chiller classes I have taken. It introduces you to a new way of thinking, and the material you go over is actually very interesting. The only thing I will say is that some of the lectures can be a little boring as it can get into pretty dense math, but you really don't truly need to understand the complex math and its proofs. As long as you understand the major conceptual takeaways you will be fine. Definitely not much work outside of class as there were only 3 psets all year (1 per month), each of which was based on completion. Each one took 3 hours max, and that was because I cared about actually getting the answers right, and being pretty thorough. Additionally, every student has to take notes for one lecture and then write a page about your takeaways, and my only qualm is that this was pretty harshly graded given that they give us almost no info on what they expected. Thus, I would really make sure you write these in depth. Finally, most of your grade (75%) will depend on the final project which is also not much work as they do a good job of spreading out the milestones. It is a group project which is nice too, so doing it with your friends is really chill and you get full ownership over choosing your topic. If you choose a fun topic, it will actually be enjoyable. I have yet to receive my grade back for this final project, but seeing everyone present their project was really cool and low stress as the presentation wasn't graded. Overall, I definitely recommend this class as Yannai is a great person and professor.
