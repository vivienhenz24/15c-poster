Dr. Fierman is so incredible! Listening to her lectures in class helped me understand the content so deeply –– content that I thought I could not understand at all when doing the readings myself. She explains things clearly, encourages thoughtful discussion, and creates a supportive learning environment. On top of that, shes a wonderful mentor who genuinely cares about her students.
It's such a shame that this class won't be taught again because it was truly so enriching and enjoyable. I've taken many Political Theory/Social Theory/Social Science classes, and this one was by far one of the most thought–provoking and useful ones. I feel like I really got to gain a deeper understanding of foundational texts I had read in other classes before, and got to learn so much more through the readings on the syllabus and Prof. Fierman's explanations. The syllabus for this class is excellent, and you will truly enjoy doing the readings/wish you could spend more time with them.

Additionally, Julia Fierman is an incredibly kind, bright, and engaging instructor. She's great at commanding the classroom while staying open, warm, and on an eye–to–eye level with her students. In each class, she gave a mini lecture about the readings before starting our classroom discussion, which were incredibly helpful and easy to follow.

The reading assignments were very useful for getting a closer understanding of some texts and took just the right amount of time.
Be prepared to read and read enthusiastically! Some authors are challenging, everyone agrees they are difficult. Dont let this stop you from reading!
This class is fascinating, and extremely thought–provoking. It is very difficult, but rewarding, and the professor is wonderful!
Julia (the professor) is effervescent, and explains deeply interesting anthropological topics passionately and beautifully. The response papers can take a ton of time out of your week and the readings are theoretically challenging but the class is really rewarding if you have the time to engage; and everything is graded lightly.
Well Julia is leaving. But if she werent, I would tell them emphatically to take it.
This was a phenomenal class. I do not think it is going to be taught really much in the future but I think that the texts and the class were amazing and I would highly recommend to anyone in anthro or adjacent
