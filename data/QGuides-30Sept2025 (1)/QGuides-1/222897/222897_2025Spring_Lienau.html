It's an interesting class that I'd definitely take if it were smaller or changed fully to a lecture format, but I think it was just too over–subscribed as one of the rare offerings in this place in Southeast Asian studies. Professor Lienau clearly intended it to be taught as a small, intensive seminar, but having almost 30 people made it difficult to have sustained and relevant conversations about the text. Which is a shame – the readings are wonderful!
This class is nice in that the readings are relaxed and I got a great overview of countries in SEA. However, the grading is very tough be warned. Also you can rewrite your essays but the feedback is not helpful enough to actually improve.
Take this class! It was super interesting we go through a lot of different types of literature and media, from poems, novels, to movies and documentaries. You will learn so much about Southeast Asia, and as someone from the region, I found myself learning so much more about the rich cultures that surround me. Prof Lienau is great, and very passionate about what she does. She asks interesting questions for discussion and we had several mini–teaching sessions with language preceptors and so I enjoyed that a lot!
The way that the course is structured provides students with a lot of forgiveness. The topics themselves are interesting, and I learned much about the historical context of each of the countries despite this being a complit class.
I would highly recommend as one of the few courses on Southeast Asia. Professor Lienau does an incredible job with facilitating class conversation, grounding literary sentiment in historical events, and encouraging deeper insights. Renee was a great TF that provided thorough and supportive comments on essays.
Take this class if you would like to learn about Southeast Asia!! This is one of the few classes at Harvard that touches specifically on this geographical area and it was fascinating to learn more about countries I had never been exposed to. Professor Lienau and Renee are also both extremely kind and understanding people who helped make the class a very welcoming environment!
This class is amazing! Would recommend for those interested in taking a comparative literature course
I think this course was not prepared to handle a large number of students, as it was first offered last year and was taken by fewer than 10 people, which I heard made for better class discussion. Class discussions with our class often ended up being the same few people talking. Renee gives great feedback and is great at helping you outline your paper if you ask her.
