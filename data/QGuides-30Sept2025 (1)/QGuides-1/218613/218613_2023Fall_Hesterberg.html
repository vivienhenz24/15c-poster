This course is very very good. I cannot endorse taking it enough. If you work with code in any form you should take at least one algorithms class to understand how to think algorithmically and efficiently. This is important most obviously for software engineers, but I think Data Scientists should take an algorithms course as well. This course explored fundamental algorithms like sorting, graphs, etc, but more important I think was the focus on "computational problems" and showing that there are many ways to solve a given problem that may have tradeoffs or specific contexts where one approach is more effective than another.

The course has a very generous grading system which is R+, R, R–, L. It is relatively easy to get an R on most assignments via help through OH even though I mostly tackled the problems solo. Definitely try the problems before going to OH so it is easier to understand what people are asking about and talking about. There are often extra credit questions that can help get from R to R+ and these are definitely doable so I recommend you try them even though the syllabus says that R's and R+'s count the same in the final grade for the most part. There are also up to 5 revision videos which address what you got wrong in an assignment that can move your score from an R– to R or L to R–.

The midterm was the main weakness of the course in my opinion. I felt a lot of time pressure to do the exam in the 75mins class time and spent more time finishing a few questions than I should have. The way the grading is done for the exams you basically get credit just for writing down an attempt even if it's just a line of thinking based on concepts from class. It would have been good to understand this before the exam so that I could allocate effort/time more effectively. That's advice you can take.

Overall, it is a very accessible and well managed course with lots of opportunities to do well from a grading perspective and the exams are fair; despite getting around 60% in the midterm because of the time constraint I got almost a perfect score on the final. Definitely take the course.
