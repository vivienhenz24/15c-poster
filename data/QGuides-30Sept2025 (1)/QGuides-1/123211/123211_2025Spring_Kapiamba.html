Straightforward class, not very problem–solving focused, mostly follows textbook
I would tell future students not to take this class. I expected an interesting introduction to topics in geometry. Instead, I was met with a highly disorganized, dysfunctional class. There was no enthusiasm generated for the topic. Lectures were bland, boring, and confusing. The syllabus said that one should be able to take this course with a fairly small math background, but a strong background in both analysis and algebra were assumed. We were given the first midterm in this class in the last 10 years (not exaggerating) and the material on the test was several orders of magnitude more difficult than the homework. We were not provided with study materials. Not even the solutions to the PSets, only a few of which have been graded since the beginning of the class.
This was a very solid geometry course. Very intro friendly, and the professor was a good lecturer.
The beginning of this course touches on the foundations of Euclidean geometry and should be review from previous Geometry class exposure. Once we get into projective and hyperbolic geometry, that's when things get interesting. Alex is an enthusiastic lecturer and I could tell he was very knowledgable on the matter. He was a little fast at times but it was in order to make sure the lesson topics were covered.
This course was a gem. The first quarter is high school Euclidean geometry, the second quarter is coordinate/vector geometry, and then the final half was new material for me, but still quite trivial. It's hard for me to recommend it since you'll learn next to nothing.
It's pretty easy, but the grading is kinda cooked...
