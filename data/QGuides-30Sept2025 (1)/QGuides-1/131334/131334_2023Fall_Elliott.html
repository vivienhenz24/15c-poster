What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course will prepare students to read, interpret, and use a wide variety of Qing–era primary documents – from palace memorials and state–compiled annalistic histories to local records and even sources from unexpected corners, like scripts for dramatic performance. The regimen is intense but rewarding, gratifying.
There is a lot of work. Expect to do a lot of reading and translations.
If you want to conduct research on Late Imperial Chinese history, or even if you think you might want to do so in future, you need to take this course. Qing docs has been absolutely essential for my development as a scholar, and has been probably the most important class I've taken at Harvard. Without it, I doubt I would have the basic skills necessary to conduct research about the subjects I want to study. Be aware that the workload is high, so don't take it lightly, and don't take it alongside too many other courses. That said, Prof Elliott is deeply considerate, and his vast accumulated knowledge on this subject means he is always around to help and guide.
