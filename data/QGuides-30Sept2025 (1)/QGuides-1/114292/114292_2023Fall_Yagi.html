Language classes are hard. Be prepared to spend a lot of time on this class. I spent around 10.5 hrs/wk for 14 weeks this semester (including class time).

...and I don't think that's quite enough. If you want to truly learn the vocabulary and Kanji, be prepared to spend more time creating decks and memorizing them.

That being said, being able to create a presentation on a social issue and a novel was an amazing feeling.
Please consider taking a language class, even if not this particular class. If there's anything I learned from taking this class, it's that language classes are genuinely very special (this was something I did not at all get from my high school Chinese class experience).

They are perhaps the only avenue to act stupid without being judged. Pretty much every lesson, every single person in my class makes at least one mistake, with some people, myself included, making mistake after mistake sometimes in things like reading from the text or responding to Yagi–sensei's discussion questions. I will say that though it is a very humbling, that kind of feeling—that everyone is dumb sometimes—is special and, in my opinion, really important to have.
