What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If this class is taught by LJ, take this class for an easy A and advanced doctoral credit.

If you have a fairly solid understanding of Probability and Inference I, then this class shouldn't be hard at all.
The most stressful component is a short in–class presentation that I believe is completion graded. At worst you may be asked to make some modifications or improvements before your slides are shared on the Canvas.

I think it's a blessing and a curse that LJ prioritizes teaching intuition. Especially when it comes to the martingale theory, the in–class presentation of the material is mostly chalk–talk presentation where at times intuition is prioritized heavily over rigor. This is great for seeing the material for the first time and maybe understanding how it was motivated before it was made rigorous, but I don't think anyone with a mathematical background would be satisfied with the presentation without going on to read the texts. At least the texts alluded to throughout the class are quite rigorous.
Excellent class on both theories and applications of survival analysis. Highly recommended.
