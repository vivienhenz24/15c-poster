Great class. Shlomo is an excellent lecturer and if you pay attention in class the content makes so much sense. It's roughly weekly programming psets and a 2–week final project. Lectures are mostly (but not all) theory, and then the psets are in OpenGL which is out–of–date for industry but useful for understanding the fundamentals.
Getting a pset partner is not up for discussion, it is an absolute must.
Start the problem sets early and go to office hours. They often seem daunting , but once you understand what the actual tasks that the homeworks are asking you to do everything is much simpler.
Great class, lots of coding. PSETs can be hard to start independently, but instructions usually clear. Lectures are pretty interesting. Completion of the PSETs felt very rewarding!
This class is great in terms of CS classes, but also you are working in an arcane language where if you encounter a bug you can't do anything about it until the next OH because there is nothing about it online. The system of having partner psets is good but you really need to have or make a friend in the class or else the class will be very not fun. The final project is very fun and you can play around with it and have a blast. One thing about the course is that some times it feels like all of this that you learn is already abstracted away in Unity and Blender so you have no reason to know the details, and that is true, but I also think that I appreciate the knowledge I gained in this course and understand those tools much more which is also very helpful.
Definitely one of the more fun code–heavy CS classes in the department! The first 6 psets are super doable especially with office hours, but the last two are a bit frustrating. Not needing to do a final exam makes up for most of this, and the grading itself is generally kind as well. Shlomo is amazing, the TFs are helpful, just make sure you ask for the solution code at the beginning of every pset (since they build off of each other), and go in with a helpful partner or do it on your own.
I recommend CS175 with enthusiasm if you are looking for graphics skills. It is definitely not a data analyzation or computer theory class, but more on how to create computer graphics. Knowledge with C++ is highly recommended. Also, pick a good partner!!
Not a gem, but pretty easy for a CS course. Lectures aren't worth attending. The content is cool but the psets aren't fun/interesting. Mostly a lot of implementing OOP for boring stuff like rotation, scene trees, keybinding, interpolation while all the graphics stuff (lighting, meshes, shaders) is done for you.
**This course is structured beautifully in a way that it lets you explore the course material without the pressure of any finals/midterms, which was the biggest plus point of the course by far. All of your grade depends on the PSETs and hence going to office hours and grinding them out will pretty much guarantee you an A in the class. You'll get what you put into this class. If you attend every lecture, read the book by heart, and try every PSET by yourself without any OH help, you'll definitely grow a lot more as a graphics designer. The obsolete technology concerns are true though, and it was frustrating at times knowing that there was nothing wrong with my code but the set up itself that was causing the error (no joke the error cost me and my teammate 10 hours for one of the PSETs). My best advice would be to find a helpful teammate, start the PSETs early, and follow the textbook thoroughly if you want an easy A on your transcript and also some cool graphics experience. It is not a life–changing experience, but it never promises to be one either.
I really enjoyed this class, but I also feel like probably more people took it than should have. I took it as an elective because Im interested in systems programming and wanted to understand how graphics work. Computer graphics as a field is both deep and hard, but this class did a good job of gradually exposing complexity. Because it doesnt have exams, I think theres a perception that this is an easy / gem class. Ive never taken an easy / gem CS class here, and this wasnt either. Take it if you want to learn the content, but be ready to put in effort. If you do, it will be rewarding.
I HIGHLY recommend the class. Shlomo is an engaging instructor. Even though his lectures can be confusing at times, I recommend going to office hours for clarification. The psets are manageable if you start early and go to office hours. Please have a partner for every psets.
TAKE THIS CLASS. Just start assignments early and go to office hours. If you do this, the class is manageable!
Great for those curious about graphics systems underlying modern animation/games/videos.
