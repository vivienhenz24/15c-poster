This class is entirely based on your psets and the two exams. The lectures are pretty confusing and hard to follow with the psets being really hard to solve without going to office hours. However, the exams are better with them being a little harder than the practice exams which are not too bad. The TFs were great especially Peter. They also catered bbq for the last lecture
This class is scary at first but once you figure out how it works it's not so bad. The lectures are dense and involve lots of derivations, but don't worry, you're not expected to duplicate these. The psets are also difficult but doable if you go to OH and get hints. The exams are not bad at all though. They are easier than the psets and mostly involve mathematical manipulation, applying formulas, and recalling basic knowledge from the course. The lectures towards the end get really complex, just appreciate it and focus on the basics for your exam!
I did not enjoy Chem161. If you have to take it for your concentration, I recommend taking another class for the requirement if at all possible. The TFs were very friendly and helpful and the best part of the class, but lectures are confusing and hard to follow, and I found the content to be dull and did not spark any interest in the topic. But I might just not like StatMech so your mileage may vary.
This is a decent and very straightforward class. I was intimidated at first because lectures can go pretty quickly and are essentially all complicated derivations, and the psets are usually tough, but the teaching staff is super understanding and supportive. If you decide to take this class for a letter grade, make sure you can make it to at least one weekly office hour because the psets are quite challenging and there aren't many office hours available. Tests are fair and easier than the psets, and the teaching staff seems to be pretty forgiving with grades. The only components of the class are the two midterm grades and the pset grades, which means you can miss lecture/section without penalty if you have things that come up.
This is not an easy class! But its doable, and its fair. The teaching staff wants to see you succeed and learn, so while you have to work hard, its definitely possible to succeed. Its very math heavy but the concepts were actually pretty cool.
This is a fantastic way to learn statistical mechanics! It is a manageable, but sufficiently challenging, workload that really gets you to master the material. Sections and office hours are quite helpful with the psets, and I'd recommend getting started on them early. Lectures balance fundamental theory with useful application very well. As a chemistry and physics concentrator with friends who took physics 181 instead, chem 161 is much better at allowing students to develop an intuitive understanding of stat mech and how it effects chemical and physical processes that we encounter in research.
This class is very interesting! I took away a lot, especially considering I was hesitant about the material before the class. Definitely glad I took it the TFs are very responsive and always happy to help with psets and content outside of class time.
I took this class as a mechanical engineer and petitioned for it to be counted as an advanced science class. There is a reason that nobody else does that. The course isn't bad by any means, but the material felt so far out of reach for me that I just spent the whole semester very confused. If you're an SB engineer and have no other options (like me), then its definitely do–able, but if you can avoid it, I'd look elsewhere. If you're not an engineer, maybe this class would be a better experience? I'm really not sure, which was a theme this whole semester.
Enjoy the class!

This class is very manageable, and is not too much of a time sink if you can attend an office hours session. Typically, only one session is necessary to get a PSet underway, if you start in advance. The class is helpful in understanding the origins for thermodynamic properties, so if you plan on doing chem at any higher level post–grad, I highly recommend this over another PChem class.
