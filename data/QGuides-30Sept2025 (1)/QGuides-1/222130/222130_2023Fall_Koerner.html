What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
There will be a lot of Shakespeare.
Future students should know that Professors Greenblatt and Koerner are phenomenal pedagogues, and this class is one of the best–taught at Harvard University. This said, it is immensely challenging, something which is both wonderful and intimidating. Anyone who says that STEM classes are more difficult than those within the humanities might consider auditing Renaissance Ego Documents for the sake of their own enlightenment. The readings are fulfilling, and those attracted to this course are incredibly bright––really try to get to know your peers if you end up a student in this class!
This is a great class! The syllabus is fantastic, and covers some of the greatest hits of Renaissance literature. Stephen and Joseph are fantastic teachers: eager to help graduate students work across disciplinary boundaries and keen to think with the entire class about what counts as an "ego–document." I'd highly recommend this seminar.
