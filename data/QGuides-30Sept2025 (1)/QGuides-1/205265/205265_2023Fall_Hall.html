What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Take this course! The reading load can be a lot, but it's not always necessary to read every reading and page. It's a great primer on the mid to late 20th century, and you will feel more informed about the world after taking this course. Professor Hall is a great teacher and is an expert in the field!
This was an amazing class, and I genuinely feel that I learned so so much from it!! Would highly recommend to anyone who is interested in political economy / political theory!
Take this course! It doesn't matter whether you are a gov concentrator or if you have absolutely no background in political economy, this course has something for everyone. The conversations are so engaging and Prof. Hall makes it so easy for everyone to say something. You learn so much from him, from your peers, and from the readings. And its an environment where you can ask questions and Prof. Hall will sit down with you and explain it or throw it back to the class. This was honestly the highlight of my week.
If you're considering taking this class, then it is for you. If you want to have deep and meaningful conversations about the future of the global economy and learn from one of the very best among Harvard's Government faculty, then take GOV94rg.
I would highly recommend taking this class especially if you already have some background in political economy but want to learn more. I learned so so much about political economy in the US and western European nations. The seminar discussions were thought provoking, mini lectures at the start of class were informative, and content itself was really interesting. I do recommend that if you want to contribute to the discussion thoroughly do the readings because the seminar is very lively (its also nice to already have background knowledge in this subject otherwise its easy to get a little lost in the discussion).

The main drawback is that since the course is focused on the US and Western European political economies it lacks some diverse perspectives. It would've been nice to at least have more readings from women writers and POC within the US and Western Europe about diverse experiences with the Western political economies, particularly how these political economies have impacted certain groups differently. Overall though, I thoroughly enjoyed the class.
