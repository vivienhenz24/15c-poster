GOV 1199 is my favorite class that I have taken at Harvard! I feel that I have a much stronger grasp on the dynamics of post–Soviet conflict after taking this course. I also learned many practical negotiation skills, including how to address my counterparts and understand the nuances of negotiations. This does not feel like a class, but an experience! I would highly recommend to any student who is remotely interested in international security and the Russian sphere of influence.
TAKE THIS CLASS!!!!!!!! The funnest class I have ever taken at Harvard. We only had six lectures in the entire semester, and I looked forward to them so much!! The entire class is based around three Saturday negotiations, and those negotiations were one of the highlights of my semester. The class really bonded through them, Taylor and Arvid are great.

The grading of the class is pretty good too: six 1000 word essay and an in–class midterm. No final. And the class ends about a month before the end of the semester.

Long story short, this class is AMAZING. You will love it, a you will end up with a great grade.
This class is great if you have at all any interest in what's going on in the European theatre (and even if you don't, I think after taking the class, you will) or want to gain some very concrete skills to take away from a course into the rest of your life.
The course was an amazing experience overall but the grading is incredibly inconsistent. Do it for the experience, not for an A.
This is a great class. You'll learn a ton of useful things about negotiation and post–soviet states. The work you do is mainly preparing for negotiations. There isn't really any useless busywork, which makes the input of effort really commensurate to how much you learn in the class. Bell is a great and engaging professor who knows the topics inside and out. Highly recommend this course overall!! Super useful !!!
THIS IS AN ABSOLUTE MUST TAKE!!! This course was life–changing for me, you rarely get to pick such a hands–on experience class with 30 people role–playing with you in real–life diplomatic negotiations. Preparing for the midterm exam was a bit painful because the range included three whole books, but actually the exam wasn't that hard, and this process really pushes you to know the regional knowledge. you also get to talk with guest lecturers who most likely were diplomats that were at the actual negotiatons that you just simulated in class. writing strategy and analysis memos is also something you will not get to learn in other classes, and really help you to know how to break down difficult, gray–area situations.
Do the readings, put serious thought into your assignments, and I promise you that you'll enjoy this class. The negotiations were awesome.
Take the course! You will improve your negotiation and interpersonal skills, as well as learn about post–Soviet conflicts.
