What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This is a THE introductory class if you want to study fiction filmmaking, you get a decent budget and the feedback is great. It does not have a specific structure, but that also makes the professors more flexible to different film ideas or production workflows.
Don't go into this course expecting to learn to write a script. Or to direct. Or even to hold a camera. Come ready with an idea for a short film and be ready to mostly figure out how to produce it on your own. However, you will have immense support from your teaching staff for this course IF (and only if) you push yourself to ask for it.
Be cautious of taking this course if this is the visiting professor's first year. Lack of experience in teaching can lead to a course with little to no clarity that teaches you very little.
If Alain is the one teaching, this course is a wild ride. Class time is incredibly unstructured and usually consists of critiques, which can be helpful for the student whose work is being shown, but is a complete waste of time for everyone else. That being said, you have a lot of freedom to create whatever the hell you want, so if you want a class that doesn't teach you the basics and gives you basically the entire semester to make a short film, go for it! Don't expect to be taught how to use hardware and software, though.
