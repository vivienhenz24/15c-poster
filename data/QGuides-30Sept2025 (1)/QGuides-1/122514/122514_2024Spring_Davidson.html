Take this class if you are a STEM person who needs to fulfill the humanities divisional. The homework assignments are weekly psets, but they are more like puzzles, so they were very fun to work on. Class is interesting and close to no work. Kate is such an enthusiastic lecturer so lectures were always engaging. She makes a big effort to make sure this class isn't stressful, so it was a gem to balance out other harder classes.
Linguistics 83 is definitely a chill class, which you'll likely hear from other students. However, some of the later PSETs can be challenging, so be sure to go to office hours. Office hours offerings are very limited though, which is super unfortunate. PSETs are graded a bit harshly but they aren't worth too much of the grade at least. Section is SOOOO important for understanding – it usually allows the concepts to click and is super helpful for PSET problems. Exams are chill and are basically just closed–note psets in the classroom. Professor Davidson is super sweet and enthusiastic about the class.
TAKE LING83! Especially if you're CS or are at all interested in language! It's a great intro course and you will learn a lot. The faculty is fantastic.

Even though this course didn't take much of my time, it was definitely a highlight of the semester.

Great introductory class with amazing TFs.

This is a good class. The PSETs are not difficult, but they are time–consuming because each language is different and you can't get straight examples from the textbook. Professor Davidson is very passionate and she gives great lectures.
I would strongly discourage students from taking this class unless they know they already have an interest in linguistics. I went into this class thinking I might enjoy the field, and although the content does seem interesting, the professor and class structure quickly detract from any enjoyment I may have otherwise gotten. The lectures are impossible to follow and lack a purpose.
Luckily, there is a textbook that can get students through the PSETs and the middle portion of the class. Assignments are incredibly easy, and the book is easy to follow, although dense. The last two units however ARE on the final and are NOT in the textbook. That means one has to attend the last few lectures despite them feeling impossible to sit through.
If I were to do this class over again, I would only attend the last three weeks of lecture to avoid the pain of making myself sit through lectures at the beginning.
TAKE THIS COURSE!!! This was one of my favorite classes at harvard. Professor Davidson and the entire teaching staff is super fun, engaging, and incredibly supportive. The assignments are enough to keep you on track but not ever overwhelming, and the topics are so interesting. The projects are fun and the two tests are not bad at all. I would recommend to everyone, regardless of background. It will make you think about language in a new way.
This course is both pretty interesting and a gem! There are only six p–sets and they're all doable in a couple hours, the exams are basically the same level of difficulty if not easier (and only worth a combined 20% of your overall grade), and the projects are fun to do as well. Great class to take if you're interested in language at basically any level, or even if you're not. Take it if you need to fill the arts and humanities requirement or need an easy and fun class to fill out your schedule. If you have a little bit more linguistics knowledge some of the phonetics unit might seem a little iffy, but overall still a great course.
I took this class because I had been interested in trying out linguistics, and I did gain a solid understanding of linguistic basics, but I also found out that it's not my cup of tea. Definitely an interesting course (you learn about Noam Chomsky, universal grammar, morphology, phonetics, language change, and more), but also not super applicable to other fields. On the bright side it's pretty gemmy since there's only one discussion post a week (tip: start with the summary at the end of chapter readings), a pset that is fairly easy if you go to section, and in–class midterm and final that the psets prepare you well for. Sections were the most useful part of this class for me, as my TF was great at explaining problems, but you can probably skip lectures if needed –– they're interesting but not necessary for course assignments.
I'd highly recommend this class as an elective or to any future linguistics concentrators! Professor Davidson is lovely; she is super engaging with her lectures and her excitement about the course is palpable. Sections were great, and taught basically all of the skills needed to solve the problem sets. Exams were quite straightforward, and it wasn't a hard workload overall. Well worth the time and space in your schedule!

This course is a great intro and survey course for linguistics. The tests are very reasonable and were pretty much the same as the practice exams.
All you need to complete assignments and take the exams is the textbook. Lecture was more about explaining why a particular topic was interesting. At times, though it can be difficult to complete some of the pset problems, but that is easily fixed when you go to section. I would definitely recommend the class. Linguistics was a topic I knew absolutely nothing about beforehand. While we didn't have the time to go super in depth on the topics we covered, I feel I now have a great basic understanding of linguistics that acts as a foundation to learn more.
