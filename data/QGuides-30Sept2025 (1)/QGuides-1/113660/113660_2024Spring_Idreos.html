By far my favorite course I have taken in the college. Stratos is an incredible professor and does a great job of picking out research papers to read. The research projects are also very interesting and a great time. It was a great experience getting to work with Utku.
the research component is a pretty good learning experience
Very solid class. Highly recommend to take CS 165 before taking this class. I was one of the few students who did not but was enrolled in CS 161 concurrently, and I was able to make my way through the class. If you've taken CS 165, you get to work on a research project, and if you haven't you get to work on a systems project. I ended up doing the systems project and learned a lot, but would have learned the same plus more having done CS 165. However, the experience of discussing computer systems with relation to machine learning in class during the second half of the semester was interesting to me personally. Besides the paper reviews, the main assignment is working on the semester long project, so just plan ahead and budget your time well.
This course provides the extremely valuable opportunity of letting students get involved in systems research. You learn how to read research papers, and you work on a research project. However, the most important thing is that students should really take CS165 before taking this course. That's because the systems foundation that you build in CS165 is highly beneficial for the projects that you work on in this course, and, furthermore, CS165 is a prerequisite to work on research projects (as opposed to a systems project) in this course.
Great follow up to CS 165
You should absolutely take this course!! If you've already taken CS165, you'll get to work on a really cool research project with DASlabs. In terms of workload, there's very little work until the last month or two of class, after which there's a two–page paper review for each class plus your final project (either systems or research, depending on your background).
If you are curious about the inner workings of big data systems and enjoy reading research papers on this subject, this is the perfect class for you!
Take this class! You learn a lot and its a great enthusiastic environment. Be wary if you are doing the systems project; it is a lot of work.
