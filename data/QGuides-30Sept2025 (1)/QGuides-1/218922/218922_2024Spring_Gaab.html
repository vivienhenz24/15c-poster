What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course is so worth taking! Professor Gaab is a great teacher and resource, and I wish I had reached out more to her over the semester.
Prof. Gaab is incredibly knowledgable and gives her whole self to this class. She is incredibly accessible and supportive during OH. She even came to the Ed Secondary graduation and commencement!

However, I was taken aback by some ableist comments made in lecture and in class discussions directed directly at particular students. I don't think this is reason not to take the class but it was just surprising by how the course's discussion culture did not allow for students to really draw off of their lived experiences, especially considering that so many people seemed to identify as having a learning differences.
For instance:
– For our final project, Prof Gaab said that not enough Autistic individuals have the education credentials to fill our proposed district level job position. Aka she said that Autistics aren't qualified to work in professional education. We gave her statistics proving the contrary and she still had reservations.
– Prof. Gaab gave us feedback that our group slides were not visually accessible to the blind, but we literally had a blind person in our group (not that one person represents everyone in a group but you know that feels a little weird)
– My classmate that proudly identifies as Autistic was frequently targeted and antagonized in group discussions not only by the teaching staff asking fairly combative questions, but also by other people in the class who seemed to have a lot of expertise but simply disagreed with their research.
Take this course! Teachers are receptive and open to feedback, assignments are interesting, and content makes you reflect on the world around you
This class and your learning is completely up to you. Prof Gaab won't quiz you on the readings, so it is up to you to do them and really engage in the course. I'd highly recommend though that you take advantage of these incredible resources and the professors!! Also, do not be intimidated by the fact that this class is filled with graduate students– they are all super friendly and want you to speak in class.
