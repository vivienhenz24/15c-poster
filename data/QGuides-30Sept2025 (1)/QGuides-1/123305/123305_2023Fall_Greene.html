This course will cause you to think very critically about the criminal justice system, crime regulation, and how the concept of free will applies to it all. The course had a very diverse set of students, from students who concentrated in Biology and Neuroscience to students who concentrated in Government and Social Studies. Students had such unique contributions to the class from personal anecdotes to expertise in a subject that made taking this course really interesting. Professor Greene does a wonderful job covering concepts and I would recommend taking the course.
Extremely interesting course. It is very well taught. Take this course if you are interested in engaging in the intersection of philosophy and psychology. This is a seminar so it is discussion based. Be prepared to do readings and engage. There are some weeks were we read longer readings like supreme court cases. The assignments also take sometime and there are four 300 word compact papers, three presentations of papers, and a final paper. So it some work, but you will learn a lot and come out the course with a really cool understanding of Free will, psychology, determinism, and responsibility.
Take this course if you can. Prof Greene was one of the most insightful, interesting, and impactful professors I've had here at Harvard. He genuinely cares about his students, and you will learn so much by taking this class without being stretched too thin. The materials are very interesting (subjective, but if you're interested by the course name/syllabus I can't imagine you would be bored by the readings and discussions). Learning through discussion is such an enriching experience, and the stress level of the course is just enough to keep you on track & engaged but doesn't make you a checked–out robot just completing the work for a grade.
Oh you will not regret taking this class! Professor Greene is a brilliant, amazing instructor and an articulate and funny lecturer. It's 100% a privilege to learn from him. He put so much effort into teaching this class. He wrote a syllabus of over 17 pages to help us succeed and provided detailed feedback on everyone's compact papers. And the course discussions were wonderful. I learned a lot from Professor Greene and my classmates. You will learn so much about the topic and about your own beliefs throughout the course. You will also learn how to write better. You will take away so much if you do the readings and engage in the class. Highly recommend this class to anyone who's interested in ethics, crim law, and psychology.
ABSOLUTELY TAKE THIS CLASS!!! It's truly the best class I've taken so far at Harvard, and Ik everybody always says that, but seriously. The professor gets so involved in group discussions and always asks thought–provoking questions to instigate TRUE ideas. He's so interesting and always has a take, but naturally seems open to changing his mind, which in turn falls over the discussion and class. An actual safe space in classroom form, I cannot recommend this class enough. Also the reading components are super simple and the usually pertain to some interesting fact or article, really good stuff! Honestly, I've used many of the readings and discussions we've had in class in real life, so definitely a course you take something away from and not just take to get by!
This was truly such an engaging and thought provoking course. Professor Greene excels at engaging conversation and prompting healthy disagreement. This course covers very weighty and controversial topics, but it always manages to do so in a manner that makes it a safe space to hold different opinions. Professor Greene will undoubtedly challenge your perspectives, no matter his personal opinions, and he pushes every student to truly understand their own opinions and question the grounding of their assumptions. On top of that, he is just a genuinely kind and caring guy. This was definitely the best seminar I have taken at Harvard and it is incredibly reasonable in terms of workload and difficulty. The course requires deep thinking and thoughtful writing, so it is not necessarily easy, but it is not meant to stress you out or trip you up at all.
Take this course! It is an amazing course, that makes you think very deeply about ethics. I feel it genuinely made me a better, kinder person more committed to my ethics. The class also really helps you learn to write better and more concisely, in a way that is extremely useful and few other classes have done.
This class is so great that Im writing my first ever review, and Im a junior. Professor Greene is excellent at testing your beliefs, and hes incredibly kind and approachable. The class itself deals with a lot of interesting material, the assignments are rigorous (in a good way), and section is fantastic. The class is more philosophical than it is legal or science–y, so keep that in mind, but I wouldnt let this deter you from taking it if youre interested.
This is my FAVORITE class I have taken at Harvard. I would strongly recommend this class to anyone (in any concentration) passionate about these topics, and no background is necessary (perhaps some understanding of philosophical arguments but the readings are very engaging and accessible), but again, would only recommend taking this class with a strong interest in the class description, because every student selected for the class is extremely passionate about the topics, extremely engaged, and ready to form opinions and arguments (with an open mind). Professor Greene is absolutely fantastic and engaging and knows how to encourage discussion while also providing guidance where necessary. He is clearly very knowledgable and a few of the readings we read were written by him. This course is essentially completely discussion based, truly following a "no right answers" motto, but with readings that offer evidence and philosophy allowing for strong arguments to be formulated. This course not only progressed my academic knowledge on these topics but also caused a reevaluation of my morals and beliefs I have and truly feel like I grew as a person in my perception and understanding of myself and the world. The workload is extremely manageable and the assignments provide lots of flexibility in what you write on, which enables you to pursue specific topics you are passionate about. The final paper follows a similar format.
Professor Greene is one of the best facilitators and lecturers I've ever had! He knows exactly how to take complicated but important ideas and say them in dead simple English while sparking meaningful discussions that make you question your beliefs and reconstruct them systematically. The 2–hr time block was a little tough because its such a long time to sit in a room and discuss, but other than that the course was great.
Definitely take this course! The material is so fascinating and so important to people of any academic background. It is challenging and will force you to reconsider many of your world views, but it will be worth it. One of my favorite courses I've taken at Harvard. The work load isn't easy but it's very reasonable.
