Comments
The final exam had some questions that were not exactly unreasonable in their own right, but were a bit much for an exam. They would be much better on a problem set..
Survey of various topics of interest in public economics field; not very focused in one area or another, last few weeks of the class are rehash of Ec50 if you've taken that before. Definitely less mathematically intensive than 2450A
I took this course as an undergrad who is interested in pursuing a future PhD in economics. I think this was a good introduction to graduate coursework with a balance of classic theory, modern empirics, and beginning to do your own research with a project proposal. The course is a very broad overview of public economics topics/methods that sometimes feels disjoint, but is generally clear and well taught. It's definitely doable as an undergraduate as long as you feel comfortable with solving models at the level of Econ 1011a.
Take this course if you have the slightest interest in public economics (or society at large, for that matter). You will get a thorough introduction to the topics debated in the literature, including the theoretical and econometric background to fully grasp the frontier. Note that this course is mostly disjoint from Ec 2450A, although some taxation–theory is included when necessary.
