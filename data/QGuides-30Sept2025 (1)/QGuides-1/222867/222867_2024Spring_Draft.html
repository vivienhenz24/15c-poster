TAKE THIS COURSE. Dr. Powell has an incredible way of moving from molecular to psychological to physical to societal –– he does a great job of validating everyone's specific and diverse interests in pain, and really grounds us in the bleeding–edge literature of the field. He's also so personable and does a great job of facilitating class discussion and community.
This is a great seminar. The content of classes varies a lot between lectures, presentations, debates, and discussions. Dr. Powell is a great teacher and makes the classroom environment fun and engaging. The class is a good survey of many different relevant aspects of pain, from the molecular basis to social implications. Because it only meets once as week, it can be difficult to retain the more complex content (particularly the biological content), but it is a good exposure to what real pain research looks like.
Dr. Powell was such a great instructor! Super passionate about his work, and I had a lot of fun with the novel assignments he came up with.
Definitely take this course! Dr. Powell is an excellent professor, and you can tell he really cares about his students understanding the information beyond a surface–level. He also makes an effort to make the classroom environment actually laid back and inviting. Highly recommend.
A great neuro seminar with a wonderful instructor!
I found this class super fun, and the class helped me see a lot of growth over the year in how I evaluate available scientific text and make use of them in my writing. Plus I got to learn a lot of cool and relevant information on the state of pain research. I absolutely recommend taking the class.
Take the course if Dr. Rasheem Powell is teaching it! He's such a great teacher, who actively tries to use different mediums of learning to get the message across. I might be partial because we also had great personalities in the class that made it all worth it. But my advice to you: this is a 12–person class; take advantage of the small size! Get to know your peers well and get to know Dr. Powell well.
