Professor Yoda is amazing. Seriously. You may, like me, be interested in taking this course because you took her GENED on Anime as Global Popular Culture. Do it. I concentrate in a completely unrelated topic (economics) and have no regrets taking this course. The workload is extremely manageable, and if you enjoyed the material for that class, you'll probably enjoy the material for this course, too.

Did I mention that Professor Yoda is amazing? The course is entirely worth taking for her alone, and you will definitely find something interesting in this class.
I highly recommend this class. It is available to everyone, and is not an overwhelming amount of work!
I would definitely recommend this course! Professor Yoda is such a gem of a teacher, and she is extremely knowledgeable in terms of Japanese literature, media, and discourse surrounding girlhood, consumerism, and a number of other topics we discussed in class.

I headed in with no other idea in mind than the fact that Kitchen by Banana Yoshimoto was on my to–be–read list and was also part of the course curriculum. Apart from reading some contemporary Japanese novels before, I did not have a lot of knowledge about Japanese history or discourse surrounding girls before this class. Professor Yoda, though, is extremely skilled at bringing all students up to speed to be able to discuss these topics, regardless their previous background.

There was a wide variety of readings and media assigned this semester, from academic articles to movies to novellas to graphic novels, and I think each provoke intriguing questions about girlhood, consumerism, media, and even about life itself (as all works usually contain some kind of universal truth of humanity).

The class is discussion–based, so you should watch/read the materials to get the most out of it. There are 3 papers (and no final) for the class, and all are pretty manageable and short (ranging from 3 to 7 pages) and don't require any research outside of the assigned course materials.
I loved this class! The perfect intersection of cultural studies, women's studies, and media/literature studies. Professor Yoda is amazing. Assignments and readings were so interesting and very not hard.
