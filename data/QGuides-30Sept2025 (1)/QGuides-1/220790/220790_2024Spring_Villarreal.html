Such an incredible, informative course with a caring teaching team! Definitely recommend it if you want to have a meaningful learning opportunity
This course was different from what I expected, but still a positive experience. It taught me to view ethnic studies through a new lens of solidarity, healing, and love. The course staff were really phenomenal and caring to an extent unlike any other course I've experienced at Harvard. This course truly cares about students of color and is an integral part of Harvard. That said, I hope that future iterations of the course can be improved by spending a bit more time actually engaging with the phenomenal readings the course assigns!
Take this class with Dr. V! It offers an entirely different worldview and has been essential in shaping how I view academia and the crossover between social movements and education. I wish there was Dr. V had another undergrad ethnic studies class, but I think she otherwise only teaches at HGSE. Hopefully I'll be able to take another class with her!
I cannot recommend this course enough. Dr. V is a remarkable educator. She has made me and so many other students in our class feel so seen and supported. It is evident that she takes education seriously, and she will encourage you to do the same in both academic and nonacademic spaces. I feel so very lucky to have learned from Dr. V and broadly about the practices of Ethnic Studies. If you are interested in education in any capacity, or simply want to be a better community member, this class is a must.
TAKE THIS CLASS. SERIOUSLY. if you take one course at Harvard, let it be this one. you will end the semester an entirely different person, and you will be grateful for it.
Please take the class!! Dr. V, Jas and Mamfatou are amazing and so supportive. The class gives me a sense of community that I rarely find in other classes that I have taken at Harvard. I have learned so much from the class.
Vulnerageousness may be the scariest but itll also be the greatest life skill and you wont have to do it alone in this class :)
Ethnic Studies and Education was transformative in so many ways. This course challenged my longstanding perspectives on just about anything and empowered me to take agency over my education. Through taking this course, I gained a community and a newfound home within the world of Ethnic Studies which is something I will cherish for the rest of my life. You won't regret taking this class!!
This course was the first time I felt seen in a course as a person of color and multiple ethnicities. The teaching team was so amazing, and I felt so impacted by each lecture. I wish I had been able to take this class earlier in my Harvard career, as it would have really shaped the way I went about the rest of my studies.
