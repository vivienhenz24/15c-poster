SCRB 195 is a great survey class into the world of stem cells in medicine. You read cutting edge research and get to talk to many leaders in the field. Prof Rubin is also a fantastic lecturer who generates enthusiasm for the content he teaches. Project can be a bit tough, but support is available. Very much enjoyed this class!
SCRB 195 is the best science class I've ever taken. I learned an immense amount about the underlying science of diseases such as Alzheimer's and Parkinson's, but more than that, I learned a lot about novel therapies being developed for these devastating diseases and helped me feel like I was truly learning about the bleeding edge of science. This course made me excited to be in the SCRB community and to be a part of the biotech industry. The final paper challenged me more than a lot of assignments I've done in classes but in the process, I learned to think critically about gaps in the treatment landscape for a disease, and leverage all that I've learned through my scientific training to propose a novel therapy to bridge this gap. I thought the final paper was unique in that it gave me an opportunity to think deeply about the decision points –– especially pre–clinical –– to bringing a potential new therapy to patients. Finally, the teaching staff of SCRB 195 is incredible. I found both Lee and the TFs to be dedicated, enthusiastic, and incredibly helpful to providing guidance throughout the semester. In and out of the classroom, my interactions with them were rewarding and one of the highlights of my semester.
strongly recommend this class – Lee is great and the course material is really interesting! Was cool to learn about the therapeutic side of stem cells, since most other classes just focus on the basic science. The group project experience depends on your assigned group, but I had a good group and the project was an interesting way to propose a new therapeutic
The guest lectures were all fantastic. Lee is a great teacher and his content was very interesting. The papers provided are all fascinating. The teaching staff are very helpful and understanding. There are many interesting individuals taking this course that you get to interact with. In this class 75% of your grade is group work that you will almost undoubtedly feel out of your depth completing; this isn't innately a bad thing, and you learn a lot very quickly, but it induces a lot of grade–related stress. I believe taking this course Pass/Fail is not an option, so make sure you have the space in your schedule to really commit to the process of the group project. Feedback could definitely have been more comprehensive and given at more points throughout the semester. A strong biology background is helpful, but everyone seemed to feel equally out of their depth; this course goes somewhat beyond the theory learned in previous classes. There were times I thought taking this course was the biggest mistake of my time at Harvard, there were times I was very glad to have taken it. Not at all an easy class.
Communicate with the TFs and Dr. Rubin about your project early! They will give you feedback that will likely require you to change the scope of your project several times. Give yourself time to figure out what your modified project will be!
This class is a great way to learn about the clinical applications of stem cell research! Dr. Rubin is super enthusiastic and he presents the material in a very clear way. I honestly wish there were more assignments in the class though, because a lot of your final grade will depend on the final project. Not gonna lie, the final project is a great learning experience but it's very very difficult. To craft a stem cell–based therapy and explain how to develop it, manufacture it, conduct clinical trials – it's not an easy task.
