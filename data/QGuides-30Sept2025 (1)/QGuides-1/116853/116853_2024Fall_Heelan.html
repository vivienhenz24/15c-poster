I'd really encourage all history concentrators to write theses! I promise it's a fun experience (if you have a topic you like, which ideally you do), and Dr. Heelan is so wonderful in making sure that everyone is on track, has access to all the right resources, and is doing what they need to. She's extremely approachable, and when you inevitably feel overwhelmed she knows exactly how to fix that. The thesis is a deeply fulfilling process, and I'd definitely encourage anyone to write it. It's super cool to come out of college with a serious academic work you can call your own, and Dr. Heelan will help you get there.
Write a history thesis! It is such a fun process and you will go through it with amazing people (both the students and instructors of History 99). You won't regret it.
Writing a thesis is HARD but this class provides good structure. They dont hold your hand though so be prepared to work a lot on your own and be very self motivated.
This class was very helpful for the beginning stages of thesis writing. The assignments are all geared towards progress on the thesis and the history conference, so nothing feels like busy work. Meeting frequency was also helpful, having some weeks with no class allowed me to focus on the actual thesis writing.
Dr. Heelan does a fantastic job structuring and running this course. The history department is so supportive of students doing history theses you should write one!
This is not designed to be a demanding course, but truly will be the product of the effort you put in. Thesis deadlines are fixed, so use the class time to ask questions, solicit feedback from Dr. Heelan and Will (who were great course leaders), and get acquainted with the broader thesis community. The course is very loosely structured, and stops meeting weekly towards the end of the semester, which requires your continued self–discipline and forward planning to stay on track. Even if some weeks were light on tangible content, the Thesis Conference was an excellent way to begin seriously thinking about organizing the research; once that was over, I felt much more confident about the direction of my project.
Carla & Will super kind. try and use their deadlines to keep yourself accountable. You got this!
Writing a thesis is hard, but rewarding and fulfilling.
Use the time and resources this class provides carefully to help you with your thesis. The history team are amazing and this class has been designed for your best interest.
This course is very well–structured for thesis writers and provides lots of support! For instance, there is a handbook that helps thesis writers navigate the process from writing a prospectus to thesis chapters. If you are a history concentrator writing a thesis, this is a required course.
