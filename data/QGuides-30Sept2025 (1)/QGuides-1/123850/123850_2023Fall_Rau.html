If you're interested in learning about language families and how languages are related to each other, if you study Classics, folklore, or any Indo–European language, this class merges a lot of interesting ways of thinking and the methodology learned in historical linguistics as a thought framework can be applied in really interesting cross–disciplinary ways.

if you have any linguistics background take it! It's really interesting and not too much work outside of class time – if you don't have any ling background tho it may be a bit of a lift
If you're interested in language change and the history of languages, this class is really cool. Lectures can get a little dry sometimes, but they're usually interesting. Our class was only 11 people, and the small class vibe was really fun. PSETs were sometimes tricky to figure out, but (I always did them with a partner). Professor Rau is an expert and very good about our random questions. Aljosa (the only TF) was really chill and approachable. I think it was his first year TFing the class (possibly first year teaching?) and you could tell a little. Things were a little chaotic but he's so nice, and I don't think it took away from the class.
