I would highly recommend applying for this fellowship. Arthur and Bethany (the TF) are absolutely amazing: Arthur does a phenomenal job at conducting the seminar, responding to questions and ideas on the spot, and making you think about the readings. Bethany was truly incredible when it came to feedback and answers to pre–class reading responses, feedback on the different steps for the final paper, and support in office hours. The class definitely requires a lot of time in order for you to get something out of it and it demands a lot of your attention and brainpower, but it's so worth it!! I've rarely taken a class at Harvard that has taught me so much.

Additionally, the peers in the class are among the smartest people I've met at Harvard and it was a joy to get to learn from them over the course of the semester. The readings were usually pretty dense, but definitely worth taking time for. This class is certainly a class where the more time and effort you put in the more you get out, and there is a lot to get out of this class!
Take this course!! The Safra program was an essential part of my sophomore year. There aren't many other things like it at Harvard –– it's a great academic community where everyone is very engaged. You'll get a lot of dedicated support that is harder to find in other humanities/social sciences courses. The course itself is fascinating (and accessible even if you're not a phil major – lots of people are gov/social studies). The seminar is also very well–run –– assignments & group meetings encourage you to get the most out of each week's readings, and the course's final essay is structured in a way that doesn't conflict with other end–of–semester work. Prof. Applbaum and Bethany are great –– they're knowledgable, fun to talk with, and want to see you succeed. Take this course if you want to have lots of interesting philosophical discussions & join an engaged, tight–knit academic community :)
Apply for the fellowship! Its a truly lovely group of people that are quite genuine and push you to be better. This seminar is not a typical seminar, please be aware of the lecture component and how this sometimes entails less discussion time amongst students.
This class is fantastic, and you should absolutely take it if given the opportunity. You will engage at a high level and in an intellectually rigorous manner with the nontrivial and highly relevant questions of how interpersonal relationships, societies, and governments function and when/how they are and aren't ethical, learn from and join a highly intelligent and engaged peer group, and enjoy catered lunches during the weekly sessions – what's not to like about that? Definitely take this course, but be aware that 1) the group is a lot of social studies concentrators, 2) having some philosophy background is not necessary but very useful, and 3) Dr. Applbaum is fantastic and you will learn so much from him, however despite this being a seminar, his teaching style is a bit more lecture–like than inter–student discussion–based
Apply to the Safra Fellowship if you're interested in ethics! It's a great course and has been an important, supportive academic community for me at Harvard. Arthur is also a wonderful person to chat with about philosophical ideas and to have in your corner. Could not recommend applying more highly!
A wonderful experience all around. Any student would be lucky to take this course.
This course changed my entire perspective on life. I learned SO much. First of all, obviously I learned a ton about ethics. Many of the readings have different takes on life and society. And they allow you to engage with readings and perspectives that I never really considered, on such a high philosophical level. I think this course should be mandatory for every student at Harvard. Beyond just this, Professor Applbaum is AMAZING. He is so knowledgeable and challenges you to really re–evaluate everything you have ever thought. He has the perfect questions and answers that you actually feel so excited to go to class everyday. Although the course is three hours long, it TRULY does NOT feel like it. It goes by SO fast. Beyond just the content, the discussions in the class are amazing. Everyone is so smart, but in a way that it challenges you to do better. This class was by far the highlight of my week. You come to this place with students who might not share the same opinions as you, but you model civil discourse in the best way possible. I am so sad that I will not be taking this class next year, and I do not think I will ever find a class or a space where I feel so comfortably uncomfortable. Thank you so much for an amazing year. 10/10 course. 10/10 professor. 10/10 TF. HIGHLY HIGHLY recommend.
