What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Take this class!! Professor Turman is great, and the content is really interesting –– it's all the things that you think of when you think of classic French culture, but you look at the ideas through a nuanced lens. Took this after French 50, and I feel like the series very much improved my French writing and speaking abilities. There is considerable homework for each class, but it is worth it to improve your French. Language exchange is also a plus!
There is a LOT of work. The assignments take so, so many hours and sometimes there are two assigned for the next class, where each one is extremely long enough on its own
This class is a great opportunity to learn about various industries in France. It becomes very manageable and enjoyable once you settle into the routine of frequent homework and writing assessments. Discussions depend greatly on your participation, so talk when you have something to say! Lastly, the foreign exchange part was probably the most practical part of the course, but it was sort of treated as just an addition to the other work. (We'd have to schedule the meetings on our own and then write reflections as we continued with the other work in the class.) Although you only meet twice a week, you still improve your speaking skills. I took this class having taking AP french + one more year, and I was more than well–prepared. I think this class is a good way to see if you'll like studying French at Harvard.
