My thoughts on OEB 55 are many and complicated. Ill start by saying I really have learned a lot of ecology in this class. I dont know if Id pursue many of the topics we covered further, but it was really helpful in gaining some intuition for how the world around us functions. The field trips were really fun, as was getting to know the TFs and their research. If youre interested in pursuing ecological field work, its a great class to start getting an idea of some quantitative methods and conceptual understanding of ecological processes.

Unfortunately, communication was a big weakness. Due dates were frequently adjusted at the last minute, it took a long time for field trip data to be posted (which is fine, but it would be great to know when to expect it if there are delays!) Some of the lectures also felt poorly–paced and werent very thorough. I think that in general, a lot of this material requires a very deep conceptual understanding to get an intuition for how it can be applied (on field trip reports, psets, exams, etc) and I didnt feel like I was getting that understanding from lecture. Office hours were VERY helpfulEvan and Rhemi were both amazing resources.

The workload is quite backloaded, especially if you dont go on the first field trip. This isnt necessarily a bad thing as long as you plan for it, but definitely made some weekends pretty rough. I put down 4 hours/week in the Q, but that ends up being distributed as a few 1–hour weeks, a few 3–hour weeks, and like two 15–hour weeks. I had almost no out–of–class work for the first half of the semester, but in the second half was juggling two psets, two field reports, and a final (including a pset and a field report due during reading week. I know thats just how the timing worked out, but still. Uncool.) If you dont have experience working with data/writing longform scientific reports, expect to spend 10+ hours on the field reports. (Which is to say, dont be like me and take this before stat/other intro classes. Bad idea.)

On the whole, I do wish Id taken something else, but Im still not so regretful that I did 55. I learned a lot in this class that doesnt get taught in other courses, and have already had opportunities to apply it outside of school. Still, if youre looking to clear the ESPP requirement I would look into OEB 65 instead (I havent taken it, but know people like it).
