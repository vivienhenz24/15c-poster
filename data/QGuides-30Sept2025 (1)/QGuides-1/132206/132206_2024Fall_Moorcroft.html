What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class is rather disorganized: the hardest assignments are definitely the field trip reports, especially if it's your first time writing a report. The field trips are cool and are mostly full day affairs. The content is cool and interesting and there is some quantitative analysis. Proficiency in R is helpful. There are very little study materials, which means that you may rely on studying just lecture slides. Take notes often.
This is a delightfully refreshing ecology class (especially after taking the abomination that was OEB10) in which you not only learn a lot about the development and history of ecology as a field, but get a nice and rigorous background on most major areas of ecology including a lot of surprisingly thorough mathematical detail. The amount of material covered was impressive and I wouldn't underestimate the task of studying for the midterm and final – it's not a cakewalk, although I wouldn't say it was too difficult either and most things seemed fairly graded. I would recommend being fairly comfortable with differential equations conceptually – you're not actually going to do any calculus, but you work with them so extensively that it's good to be familiar with their form and properties.
Personally, I really enjoyed this class. At times it did feel slightly unorganized and there is a lot of content. However, if you put in the work and ask lots of questions you will be fine. It is a foundational class for OEB and is a great way to start exploring different classes in the department!!
If you're interested in ecology or conservation, I would recommend this class. Professor Taylor is incredible, he's a great lecturer and very passionate about ecology. Professor Moorcroft is also very passionate, but his lectures are less engaging. Overall, this class is very doable, if you apply yourself you can learn the material, but there isn't much holding you accountable for learning the material(only 4 PSETs, and 1 midterm) so I recommend doing a little bit of self study every week; this will make the PSETs and exams much easier. Also, the field trips are really great but don't procrastinate the write ups because they do take some time. Overall, this a great class for ESPP and IB concentrators.
Ecology is an awesome topic!! This class will expose you to a lot of content and lots of different areas of research. Come prepared to put in work to learn the content on your own, because lectures are not always super effective –– but it is interesting knowledge to obtain. The course staff is super knoweldgeable and friendly.
You will learn a lot in this class and the professors are very experienced in the field. However, the disorganized nature of this class made it hard to recommend without reservations. Be prepared to have to adjust and change your schedule on a whim. Also, it is difficult to know what we should be prepared to do. For example, the final exam formula sheet we were given was from 2 years prior and had formulas we hadn't even learned in class. The midterm scores ranged from 99.5 – 64 and was definitely a time crunch. If you have no background in ecology it can be hard to intuit some of the questions and world problems. The final felt better than the midterm but study a lot!! It is very easy to lose points.
It's objectively a pretty cool course, but the class is quite disorganized and grading can be a little harsh in my opinion. The field trips were really fun though, and it's clear that the instructors are really passionate about the topic.
