This course is what you make of it. If you have a true interest for the inner–workings of renewable energy, take this course. At times, this course can get in the "weeds" of certain topics. Not too much work on a weekly basis but the final presentation and 12–page paper takes a ton of effort. The midterm was probably the hardest part of this course, start studying early!
Very interesting course that makes you passionate about the subject but is not too difficult!
This class covered an extensive amount of content, and was an amazing introduction to the world of economic climate science. Additionally, lectures were very interesting, and the workload while sounding like a lot for a GenEd was very manageable in comparison with other GenEds. Would definitely take.
This class is pretty easy, only assignments are 3 psets (although they are long and kinda confusing) and weekly readings. Study for the midterm and try not to procrastinate on the final paper.
This is a really great course!!! I would highly recommend if you're interested in the scientific mechanisms behind climate change and the effects on the environment (first half of the course) and then want to explore potential clean energy solutions (second half of the course). The first half of the course is a science class, and this is what you'll be tested on in the midterm. The second half of the course is much more student–led in the sense that you'll be primarily working on your own final research paper/presentation, and lecture material is not tested. It was a really great way to get the science gened requirement completed, and Professor McElroy is a legend in the field. It's such a privilege to be able to hear his anecdotes.
Great class to take for gened. Lectures are a bit boring but try to stay attentive and take notes for the weeks before the midterm. The midterm exam definitely requires preparation – adequately study for that. Final project and presentation super easy and low stakes. Overall definitely a gem – recommend.
This course provides a pretty broad overview of the scientific processes involved in climate change, the details of renewable energy sources, and the difficulties and benefits governments and communities encounter when changing from fossil fuels to more sustainable sources. The course touches upon both historical and recent research in the field, and the professor has been working in this field for a long time so he has a lot of knowledge on the topic. In terms of workload, it's pretty light and as long as you pick a good topic for your final paper and start early it should be manageable.
If you like the material, then take this course. Professor McElroy is very passionate about the topic and it is a pleasure to talk to him. Make sure you take advantage of your resources.
I would only take this course if you have to (secondary/ESPP requirement). The psets require a fair amount of work and attendance is graded and mandatory. Prof. McElroy is brilliant and very informative, although his lectures are lacking engagement. Overall 6/10.
