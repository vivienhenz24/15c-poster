An insanely good class! I personally learned a lot, and Professor Santucci is a lovely person who deeply cares about the students. She is an amazing lecturer, has a wealth of knowledge in the field and sometimes brings really interesting people to talk about therapeutic models that we cover in class, but in more detail. The class is really well balanced and completely doable. The only thing that threw me off balance a bit were the 3 pop quizzes that we have during the semester, but after providing mid–semester feedback, Professor Santucci adjusted and make it more fair for students. Really, take this class if you have the chance! It is one of the best classes I have taken at Harvard.
I LOVED this course! It's a fantastic way to learn about different mental health treatments and particularly how different techniques are employed by clinical psychologists. Lectures were always so interesting, with a solid mix of open discussion, group turn–and–talk, and listening. Each class opened with a mindfulness activity which was really enjoyable (and educational), we got to hear from some featured speakers, and lots of the slideshows included engaging video examples of psychologists working with patients. Prof Santucci is phenomenal. She creates a safe space for discussion, asks great questions that make us think, and delivers the content in really accessible ways. Her kindness and compassion in her teaching made all the difference, too. Workload–wise, she kept assignments to a minimum; this class is really feasible while still feeling like you're getting the most out of your learning. The final presentation/paper are very open–ended (picking any everyday behavior to treat), so personalize it and have fun with it!
