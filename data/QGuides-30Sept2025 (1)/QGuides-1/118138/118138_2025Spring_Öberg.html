Comments
ISM with Karin is an extremely well organized and thoughtfully constructed course. Although there are a lot of assignments (weekly readings and problem sets), the difficulty is reasonable, and I have learned and retained information more than in most other grad classes.
This course is super fun! I learned so much about star formation, planet formation, and the different phases of the interstellar medium. I would definitely recommend starting the problem sets early, because as they are very interesting they are also a bit challenging at times. Karin is an amazing instructor.
This course is a fantastic survey of the vast and varied science of the ISM. Professor Öberg's lectures are very cohesive, engaging, and well–organized, and the curriculum covers a good mix of breadth and depth. The problem sets can be a little time–consuming, but I felt that they were very valuable to my understanding overall, and the exams were really fairly written. I would highly recommend this course!
This course seems applicable to basically all astronomy subfields, and I found the subject very useful to learn about, even for my research topic which I had initially thought about as separate from the ISM. Prepare to spend a lot of time reading in preparation for lecture.
This class has a fair amount of assignments, but they are very fair and completable within the expected time commitment 0 hours per week. Karin is a wonderful lecturer who focuses on the "big picture" of topics and some the details that matter the most. After taking this class, I feel much more confident calling myself an astrophysicist!
