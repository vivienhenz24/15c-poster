This is the most important and transformative class I have taken at Harvard. I say this as someone who is certainly going to be a K–12 teacher, and Beth is pretty clear that her goal is to convince every student in the class to become K–12 teachers. The ideas and scenarios that we discussed in class are so thought–provoking and so important for anyone to consider – if not as a future teacher then as a future parent who will have children in the school system. I came into this class feeling very prepared to be a teacher and realized that there is so much to know and learn about the job – it is truly one of the most difficult careers there is. You will learn in this class that to be a teacher is to be a learner, and that there are many quick judgement calls made that teachers will reflect on later and hope to improve in various areas. You will learn that there is always work to be done and there is no perfect teacher due to the thousands of variables that create unique classroom experiences every year, every day, and every class period. You will learn to reflect on your own experiences in education and to grow beyond that of your own teachers. Take the class, it has some of the best stuff that Harvard offers.
Absolutely wonderful introduction to what it's really like to be a teacher, and the types of decisions you'll have to make as a teacher to craft an effective classroom. The class covers a wide range of important educational topics, but perhaps the most useful is the impromptu class discussions that often resulted from our own curiosities and Beth's storytelling. She has incredible wisdom and gives great practical advice regarding teaching, which is really nice to see in a school that is so theoretical. Class assignments are not stressful and are really engaging, they get you to think about who you really want to be as a teacher.

And the classroom environment is so comfortable, with a ton of engaging activities and real–world discussions. Everyone brings their own perspective on teaching, and while the class may leave you with more questions than you started with, you'll quickly realize how challenging (but exciting and enriching) teaching can be! A can't miss class for those interested in teaching.
