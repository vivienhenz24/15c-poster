Comments
This course truly reflects its title. If you feel drawn to a seminar–style class focused on reading, writing, and discussing themes of death and immortality, or if you're simply interested in exploring philosophy, this is an excellent course choice! The class is relaxed and low–commitmentjust bring an open mind and a willingness to read and share your thoughts.
This class is amazing. It is exactly what you would want from a freshman seminar. Readings are incredibly thought–provoking and interesting, and importantly, not incredibly long nor tedious. The discussions are thoughtful and peers genuinely care about the subject matter. It also isn't super–depressing! Professor Chen is also amazing and so friendly!
This class is a great introduction to the topics of death and immortality as examined through a philosophical lens. The quality of the class however depends on how closely you read the assigned readings and the group of people you take it with (the more comfortable this group is, the better the discussion).
This is definitely a philosophy seminar, which I wasn't fully aware of going into the class. The readings are mainly dense, abstract philosophical takes on the topics of death or immortality. Some of them were interesting, but our seminar discussions tended to be drawn out and boring since they lasted for two hours each Wednesday.
If you are looking for a FYS this is definitely a great one. While there is weekly assignments and some papers, if you actually want to learn and enjoy it this was great for that. I probably won't study philosophy as a concentration, but I loved getting to study it just for the fun.
The class is very interesting but is also pass/fail as it is a freshman seminar. Take the class if philosophy is interesting to you, but if you are concerned with credits/ GPA than look for a different class.
