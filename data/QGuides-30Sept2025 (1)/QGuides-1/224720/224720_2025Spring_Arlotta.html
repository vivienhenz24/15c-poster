Absolutely incredible course. It was so inspiring to learn about the development of the field, and Professor Arlotta clearly cared deeply about each student, while recognizing that people's courses are tough, and thus making her class very manageable. In this course I was exposed to discoveries and labs that are otherwise inaccessible to anyone else on Earth, which was an insane feeling that made me respect and appreciate this course, and Harvard as a whole, so much more. Take this course if at all possible, even if you aren't on a medical/bio track.
Take this class!! It's so fun, it was probably my favorite class this semester. All of the material is super interesting and engaging, but it's very approachable. If you want to put in extra work outside of class to gain an even better understanding of the material you can, but it's by no means necessary
You dont have to do any heavy reading, just show up and engage. The material is truly fascinating, and the discussions make you think in new ways. Id absolutely recommend taking this course!
ABSOLUTELY TAKE THIS COURSE!!!!

It was such an amazing seminar. Getting to learn from Dr. Arlotta and discussing cutting edge research in the field is so enriching! It has a very nice reading list and gives a lot of perspective not only on the current research but also the criticism it faces and the direction it is going in!
This is a great freshman seminar to take for the enjoyment of learning. Paola is very enthusiastic and incredibly knowledgeable, and if you engage you will get a lot out of this course.
TAKE THIS SEMINAR!! Really cool stuff regardless of your background. There were bio/neuro majors but also non–bio majors, but Prof. Arlotta made the content digestible and encouraged asking lots of questions.
