It definitely is easier than some other expos however this was probably my least favorite class of the entire year and I took some other pretty brutal classes. Found the work painful, and unclear. Again not as bad as some other expos you will get graded fairly and at the end of the day the teacher is nice and wants to help you. Just think this class could have been more informative, and better structured.
Take it if you want to have a class that is more about just the writing. Also, the teacher is lovely, so if anything, do it to get to know a dope professor.
Take this class, best expos!!! Great preceptor! I wasn't interested in environmental justice but my friends recommended it, and I'm sooo happy I took this.
BEST CLASS EVER! TAKE THIS EXPOS CLASS WITH DR. LANE! I learned so much. The workload was manageable. Dr. Lane is one of the sweetest teachers I've met on campus in my first year. Her feedback was also very helpful, and I came to appreciate writing even more through this class. The collaborative and capstone zine projects were really enjoyable. Expos essays can be challenging to write, but I learned so much throughout the process. If you have an interest for the environment or environmental justice, this class is the perfect Expos to take. I am so glad I took this class!!!
This is one of the best expos classes you can find at Harvard. Dr. Lane organized parties during class time and had a field trip to different places. I really enjoyed making a zine. Take this course!
wonderful class. Dr. Lane is the goat, truly cares about students and about learning!! if you are interested in environmental work (I am), this is absolutely a great class. even if you aren't I would still recommend.
THIS CLASS IS AMAZING! If you have even the slightest interest in environmental justice, I highly recommend this class! Dr. Lane is an amazing, caring professor who has so much experience in and passion for the field, the readings are genuinely interesting and make you think about how you think and engage with the environment, and the assignments are manageable and more importantly FUN (the final zine in place for an essay is one of my favorite components of this course). I genuinely don't think there's a better expos.
This is easily the best Expos class. You have two papers and one capstone project (which is making a zine). Readings are super interesting, and class discussion is so easy and inviting. Instead of response papers, you have a field journal, which is so fun to write in (you can make collages, drawings, etc.), and they help you think about your writing and the course materials. Dr.Lane provides really great feedback and is so educated in the topics we discussed. You also get to watch The Hunger Games, and some classes will have snacks! Overall, this expos class was not super stressful, and I feel like I really improved my writing.
This is genuinely a great expos to get. There are two papers, so you have to make both count. But, Dr. Lane is so quick with feedback. Just reach out to her and you will do 10 x better on the papers. There are also weekly field journals and two creative zines at the end of the semester, and I thought they were super fun to make and a cool way to apply my research to other audiences.
