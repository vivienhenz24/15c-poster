If you want a course that challenges not only your writing, but also your thinking, you should absolutely take Breaking the Norm! Dr. Goldberg provides great feedback on essay drafts and is always open to meeting with students outside of class to discuss their work one on one.
This is an amazing Expos course to take. The readings and films are all very interesting. The feedback Dr. Goldberg provides is incredible. There is so much freedom on what to write about as well, so it is perfect for students who enjoy exploring their interests.
Breaking the Norm is a great option for Expos 20! Dr. G is a thoughtful preceptor who will give you ample feedback on how to prepare your papers. If you're interested in what constitutes our norms, or what is considered the normative, the course readings will allow you to explore that intellectual interest alongside the writing exercises of Expos.
I would say Dr. Goldberg cares a lot about the effort you put into this class.

This class was helpful in comments on drafts and final papers. It also was given the opportunity to read works that explored topics I had not originally spent much time thinking about. In regard to learning essay writing techniques and structure, I learned less through the lectures (they felt rushed) and more through the feedback on essays.
Take this Expos!! Dr. Goldberg is an amazing teacher and Breaking the Norm is definitely one of the best options for completing the Expos requirement!! The workload is manageable and you will get a lot out of the course.
Take this class only if you are interested in gender studies, race, class. Don't take this class if you want to take an easy course and not think much. The class seems to self–select kids who are really interested in this stuff and it can be difficult as someone who was not.
Dr. Goldberg is a great teacher! He is very kind, understanding, and his feedback on essays is very helpful and specific. Definitely recommend starting on drafts early so that you can get early feedback. The workload in this class seems to be average for Expos 20.
This class is an amazing Expos section totally different from the slog that the course is reputed to be. Dr. G is super nice, and grades very fairly. It's not a whole lot of work (you really only have to skim the readings) but you do come out of it feeling more confident/prepared in your writing skills. I would 100% recommend ranking this section first I had it maybe fourth or fifth, but am so glad I ended up in this section.
If you take the work seriously, this class will teach you how to do analytical writing well, so you will definitely improve those skills. The workload and grading is on par with a typical expos class––not extremely light nor excessive. Grading is fair, but be careful if you don't consider yourself a good writer. The class is a mix of lecture and discussion, but more heavy on the discussion. Dr. Goldberg gives helpful constructive feedback on the drafts, so make sure to put effort into that. If you can, sign up for his office hours and have some questions prepared. Finally, I suggest at least skimming the course material to make sure you are interested in reading the pieces / watching the films.
