Art of the Con as taught by Professor Ian Shank is definitely an enriching course. More than just learning about essay–writing, analytical reading, or plagiarism, this course really taught me about perspective and shared life lessons that I will continue to carry with me during college and beyond. This class is not easy by any means (and your grades may not always reflect your ideal ambitions), but I think students who are open–minded and willing to work hard will surely experience success.
You quickly realize that the intro the set up of your argument/essay is where you will invest the most time. Really put time into that for the first draft. The first draft is about the ideas.
This course does earn it's reputation for being graded difficultly, but it is well worth it, and students can expect to do well with a little bit of effort. The con stories we learned about on were extremely interesting. As someone who generally is not a big reader, I thoroughly enjoyed the essay readings as well as some of the other cons we looked at in this course. Most importantly, Ian is an amazing instructor. He is very funny and makes the course fun, but is also excellent at teaching the material in an understandable way. As someone who was not thrilled about expos, I was shocked to find that this was my favorite course this semester.

Circling back to grades, it is true that this class is not a free A. However, Ian provides value feedback and is always willing to help, so good grades can be achieved through taking in his assistance and thinking hard about the essays. This class is not difficult in an hours/week way. The readings are generally short, and I never felt bogged down by the work. We had 3 essays, but work outside these was sparse and ungraded. Rather, the course is difficult because it challenged me to break my bad writing habits and change the way I approach academic essays, which proved to be surprisingly enjoyable.

I recommend this course to anyone, especially to those that are not excited about their expos requirement.
This class is somewhat rigorous in the marking but the expectations are carefully laid out in the assignment descriptions. Be sure to value your conferences and truly understand the writing material taught in class to be successful in these essays.
Take this class!! This is easily the best section I've been in at Harvard. This course will challenge you but will make you a much better writer in the process. The readings and assigned films are extremely interesting and will make writing each essay ten times better because you'll be engaged by the topic.
Professor Shank is engaging and makes an effort to create a space where students are comfortable with each other and to speak up. He also makes good use of diagrams when explaining on the blackboard. He is also very accessible for meetings if you want to ask questions, clarify concepts or talk through your ideas. If you want to do well I would recommend GO TALK TO HIM, set–up a MEETING and TALK THROUGH YOUR PLANS/IDEAS, he will help you flesh them out.
It is 3 papers which is very heavy and Ian is a strict grader (probably expect B+/A–) i think grades improve as the class progresses. The attendance questions were fun though and i felt like there were many fun moments. Content can be interesting and final paper can be on anything you want so at least its not that bad.
Prof. Shanks The Art of the Con was one–of–a–kind. Hes an extraordinary professor, willing and able to turn students from solid writers into great ones. Moreover, he teaches us how to think critically and creatively. Once, when I asked why does this matter?, not only did he help me out during the class but pulled me aside afterwards to make sure that I was following along. He went so far as to tie what we learned in class to my experiences in theatre, because he cared enough to remember that fact about me and make it part of our conversation. He knew that I cared, and didnt dismiss my concerns as insubordinationthats the mark of a fantastic professor. This isnt even mentioning that the materials are plain fun, and Prof. Shank does a great job of keeping things entertaining and educational at the same time. I loved every minute of this class, and recommend it wholeheartedly to everyone looking for an awesome Expos experience. Thank you, Prof. Shank!
This course is an absolute standout! Readings are relatively light and often quite entertaining. Ian's conferences and feedback, as well as partner exercises in class, can be very helpful during the writing process. Most of all, the class is just very fun. Ian is one of the only teachers I have seen make the typical "icebreakers" actually work to foster a classroom environment that legitimately felt like a friend group by the end.
Overall, while grading is maybe slightly stricter than average, the fun materials, more than adequate resources to improve your writing and get feedback, and extremely fun atmosphere make this an easy recommendation.
This is an amazing class!!!! If you are a soulless creature and want to just punt Expos for an easy A, don't take this. You will need to have genuinely good work, be thoughtful through the writing process or have any form of joy in your heart then I would absolutely recommend Art of the Con for you. The readings/texts studied are actually very interesting, the sections seem to attract an amazing slew of funny and thoughtful people and Professor Shank does an incredibly job of injecting a form of fun and chill vibe right back into college.
