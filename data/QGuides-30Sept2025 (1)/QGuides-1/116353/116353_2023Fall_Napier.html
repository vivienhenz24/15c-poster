Only take this course if you enjoy Jane Austen!! I made the mistake of choosing this course not liking Jane Austen's writings before. You will be diving deep into Emma and Jane Austen the entire semester so be prepared. Dr. Napier is an excellent instructor but he is very demanding and the pace at which we read Emma is fast. He cares a lot about the course and you will learn a lot from him. However, the course did get overwhelming at times because Expos is usually not the priority in comparison to your other classes. Make sure to manage your time well and stay on track with your draft writing and readings.
Cannot recommend Dr Napier or "Austen & Us" enough!! Make sure to stay current with the reading so that you can participate in discussion. Go to all available office hours. Go into it with a growth mindset about your writing – even if you were a great writer in high school, be ready for lots of feedback and room to grow. This course is meant to push you and Dr. Napier will really help you become a much better writer. If you love Jane Austen, this is the class for you.
I went into this class having never read a Jane Austen book in my life. To be perfectly honest, I was a little nervous, but now I'm really glad I ended up in Austen and Us (for reference, it was ranked fifth on my list). The course moves quickly enough to be interesting, but you can also process the information. Class discussions are fun, and help you better understand the book. When we have papers, there is an extensive revision process, and you can always find help if you just ask.
I highly recommend taking this course (as long as you are not a procrastinator – if you are, and plan to change that, then take it!). Dr. Napier is an amazing professor and excels in his instructional teaching. He provides excellent feedback and truly shapes you into a better writer. While this class is a bit more work than other Expos20 classes, you truly learn to become a better writer and learn to appreciate classical works such as Emma.

Take it! It's a dynamic and entertaining classroom and the essays are not graded as harshly as you would expect going in.
Do your readings and annotate everything. It will help you to be prepared for class discussions. AND participate in class discussions!! They will help you better develop your skills in developing an argument. This class is not bad at all and if you have a good prof (like me) they will give good feedback and help you through.
I highly, highly recommend this class! Dr. Napier is a wonderful teacher who genuinely cares about students' growth as writers. I received lots of individualized feedback on my writing that really pushed me to challenge myself and experiment as a writer. Class discussions were interesting and assignments were engaging.
I would highly, highly recommend this class! I am a STEM major, but Austen and Us ended up being my favorite class during freshmen fall. Dr. Napier is an outstanding professor and really wants his students to succeed as writers. He gives extensive feedback on student drafts and is always accessible for office hours or writing conferences. Class discussions are engaging and informative, and the content is interesting too. During Unit 1 you read Emma, and during Unit 2 you compare the Emma and Clueless films. During Unit 3, you can write a research paper on any Austen–related topic you want (this can be anything from Bridgerton to Twilight). The community in the class is very tight too, and many students will become friends outside of the course and meet up together for Austen movie nights. This course really got me to grow both as a writer and as a person. I would highly recommend that students take this class, whether they are fans of Austen or not (having prior experience is not at all a requirement).
