Take this class! You will learn so much, become so close with your peers, and meet Ross who is lovely. It's by far the most useful Expos class. you will learn the most in this expos class. take it!!!!!
This is a great Expos course! You will learn a lot. It is difficult, but definitely very rewarding. Although most people who take this class are pre–law and interested in the humanities, there are a few STEM students every semester. I'm a STEM person and this was my first ever humanities course so I was struggling a lot at the beginning, but Dr. Martin offered me so much support. He is an amazing professor and is really there to help his students. Reach out to him for help, and check out the Writing Center; you will see yourself getting better and better as the semester goes on. Dr. Martin leaves a lot of feedback on papers, and it's very important to read those and implement them into your revision to grow as a writer and develop your arguments. The court cases we read are very interesting. I think the best part about this class is the in–class discussions. I have learned SO much from the diverse thoughts and perspectives of my peers, which makes taking this class an invaluable experience. Please do consider taking this class, even if you are not pre–law but have the tiniest interest in how Supreme Court decisions are made and how they influence society, you will learn a lot and enjoy the experience!
If you are going to take this class, make sure you are a very strong writer. Some other expos classes will teach you about how to write an essay and what is expected, but this one does not. In my opinion, he is also somewhat of a difficult grader. In the beginning of the course, the reading is very dense and heavy. It will take a while to read and understand them. The cases will also seem a little boring. However, for the most part, the number of pages for reading decreases and they become more interesting. There is a good community in the class and the professor tries to make everyone comfortable. The class is more of students holding the discussions nd choosing which direction to go in rather than the professor. Even for conferences, he wants you to lead them and ask what you want to talk about. You will learn a lot and the class can be very intersting and useful, but be prepared for the workload, expectations on essays, and the teaching style of the professor. If you are interested in the Constitution and a pretty strong writer, then I would recommend the class. If you need more guidance, maybe pick a different expos.
This is an excellent Expos. It shouldn't be considered a gem, but the readings aren't too bad and the discussion is very enjoyable. Dr. Martin does a great job of structuring the discussion around the readings while still incorporating the real world and connecting to current events. You will get plenty of feedback on your writing, and Dr. Martin tailors the feedback to your personal situation to help you grow throughout the semester.
I recommend this course who has any sort of interest in law, Supreme Court rulings, politics, or is simply looking for a rewarding challenge. Ross is an amazing professor. He truly cares about his students and is very accessible for questions and feedback. He is also very flexible with due dates and receptive to feedback from students. While the class begins with quite a big lift, over time the readings definitely become much easier and more rewarding. I felt very supported throughout this entire class and I would definitely recommend it to anyone interested in the subject matter.
This course is the BEST. Professor Martin is a very good teacher, and probably a better orator. His discussions in class are very cohesive and convincing, and he teaches us to do the same. I learned a ton about how to structure a well–built argument. Take even if you aren't interested in pre–law. <3

This course is overall amazing! Dr. Martin is such a caring professor who really wants each student to succeed, and he works his hardest to make sure that everyone has a successful experience. The course has its challenges, but Dr. Martin is nothing but supportive, and I would say we all came out of the class very very satisfied and as much better writers. As someone who just wanted to learn more about SCOTUS cases, I learned a lot, and I feel that I really improved my argumentative and writing skills. Would highly, highly recommend. I highly recommend this course, especially for anyone interested in law. The first unit was a little tough, and the class was difficult because you are reading and writing about real Supreme Court decisions, but Ross Martin is not a punitive grader; he is incredibly intelligent and will help you improve your writing skills. At the same time, you will learn about important and interesting cases.
Spectacular class with a spectacular instructor! I know some people say course is hard, but I actually think it's extremely manageable –– readings can vary a lot in length week–to–week, but oftentimes they don't take more than an hour or two per week to be prepared for class discussions. His grading is also super fair and reflects your effort and growth over time, plus you will know what to improve upon from one essay or draft to the next thanks to his feedback.

Ross is hands–down the best Expos instructor out there –– he puts incredible time and effort into giving detailed, actionable, targeted feedback + conferences, and he's just so evidently invested in your development as a writer and person. He also does a great job of cultivating both community and civil/insightful/fruitful discussions in class. I came into expos feeling that writing was already one of my strengths, but I've still been able to grow so much in my writing and argumentation abilities thanks to this class. Finally, this class has taught me the invaluable skill of how to between the lines of court cases (and many other forms of writing) to find insights, perspectives, and connections to other texts and real–world contexts that I definitely would not have been able to discover before.
You will be challenged a lot and at times feel stupid, but at the end of it you will grow as a writer incredibly. If you get this class, you're in luck, because you will be leagues ahead of your peers in writing in the next few years since it pushes you so much.
