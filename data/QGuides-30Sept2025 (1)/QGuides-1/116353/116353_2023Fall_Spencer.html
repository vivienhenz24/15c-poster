What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
The class had a fair amount of reading to do, and some of the readings were quite dense. However, the science fiction texts we read were pretty interesting. The essay prompts were very reasonable, and I think I was able to improve as a writer, even though writing isn't my favorite thing.
This is a very solid EXPOS section. Dr. Spencer is very kind and pretty chill overall. The readings and especially the films were super good choices and very engaging if you like science fiction. Dr. Spencer gives excellent, detailed feedback on all of the drafts you submit, and the individual conferences with him were very helpful to producing something final–draft worthy. All of the essays gave free reign to find an interesting topic within texts as well as having a choice between 2 different possible works to study for each one. Overall, I would say I had a pretty good experience in this EXPOS. The essay–writing pace can feel relentless sometimes, admittedly, but aside from the three major essays the workload is minimal.
Great class, would definitely recommend. Sci–fi is a great genre to study
This course is fairly easy in terms of workload. There's not a ton of reading or assignments. Just be sure to put time and effort into the papers because it's not an easy A. You won't get below a B, but an A isn't a given and since there's only a couple graded assignments they impact your grade a lot.
I loved my section of EXPOS: Sci–Fi Others. Dr. Spencer is a fantastic professor and the course content is really interesting. If you like sci–fi, this is a great section for you! More broadly, if you stay engaged, participate in class discussions, and do all of your assignments, you will be successful in Expos. You really do get out of it what you put into it. Even if you don't see yourself as a big reader/writer, try to lean in and engage. You can learn many valuable (and widely applicable) things about writing.
Refine your rough drafts if possible. If you have a rather solid foundation, it will make the feedback/endnote comments easier to approach.
