This Expos class is a gem and make this your top choice!! Dr. Gold is extremely specific and provides a variety of ways to comments and feedback (a draft conference, video, and comments). Her unit curriculum is extremely organized, and you know exactly what you are going to do for the rest of the semester if you're the type of person to plan ahead. The readings are incredibly interesting even if you aren't interested in discussing feminism or any social issues (which was the case for me, and I feel a lot more comfortable discussing these issues). Dr. Gold doesn't cold call on you if you aren't comfortable, and she really tries to understand your learning and writing style and makes the class a comfortable space for discussion. She has a clear rubric that she grades off from and has a lot more assignments and participation to help your grade, so the two major essays don't completely determine your grade. The capstone project is a lot better to do and allows you to flex your creativity and apply the topics you've learned to the real world rather than writing a third essay. She is very quick to respond to any messages on Slack as well and is extremely funny and engaging. Be sure to utilize the Writing Center or any outside opinion or help edit your essays since class mainly talks about the readings and discussing how to write essays better. She gives you lots of time to work on your essays, just don't procrastinate. Overall, an incredibly fun and interesting class, and I don't regret making this my top choice.
