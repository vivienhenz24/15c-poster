This is by far the best expos class to take. Dr Goldberg is endlessly supportive and constructive in his feedback, and the class environment was so social and lively. His grading is very reasonable and the prompts/materials for the essays were ones I found interesting and wanted to spend time working on. I wasn't ecstatic about having to take expos but this ended up being my favorite class of the semester. He'll work with your distinct writing style and process to improve it rather than setting strict expectations about the style of essays you should be submitting. He also has a plethora of crazy lore that he'll randomly drop throughout the course if you're lucky. I would advise you to be engaged and enthusiastic in class discussions/ice breakers and be social with the other students and with Dr. G. Take notes on what you find interesting about the short stories and films as you're watching and reading, and get a head start on your draft well before the deadline. If you show up excited and care about the class, you'll have the best time ever. I'm so sad that this class is over and I wish my other classes were anything like it.
