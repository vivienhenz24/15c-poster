This class definitely wasn't hell, but it also wasn't one I was super excited about. If I were you, I'd take a different expos; though I'm aware others really liked this class, I didn't find it particularly enjoyable: the assignments often felt dry (though they were easy), and class at times was really boring.
Strongly recommend.
Thinking with Conspiracies was a great Expos section. Prof. Garcia is an excellent mentor who is invested in all his student's work. Organisation of units in the course is very good, so do not worry if the content of the first unit (history) seems difficult or odd as this changes for the bulk of the course (units 2 and 3) to something more accessible. Final paper is specifically interesting, and a great experience at the intersection of essay writing and academic research. Not an easy Expos section, but definitely one where I learnt a lot. Prof. Garcia is very open to work with the class on deadlines/extensions and in my experience a fair to lenient grader. Enjoy the ride!
It is important to know that I will be speaking to the content and nature of this class as I do not like expository writing or writing classes in general. Even with that, this class still made the burden not so bad. There are three units covered over the semester for this class. The first unit was on the Blood Libel from the 15th century. This topic really was quite interesting, but maybe for only a week or two. After that, I was personally over the topic. The second topic was much more modern, we reviewed Graham Hancock and an episode of Ancient Apocalypse from Netflix. I found this topic to be much more interesting throughout the whole unit. It led to some great in–class discussions and some good avenues for paper topics. The third unit is all about researching a conspiracy theory of your own choice and allows for a lot more freedom when it comes to sources, research, and ideas. Throughout the course, the professor gave phenomenal feedback on each response exercise, rough draft, and final paper which really helped improve my writing.

Whilst I am not a fan of expos in general, I would recommend this class as a top choice for expos 20.

(Please, for your own sake, do not procrastinate the drafts of your papers until 2 nights before they are due like I did.)
Take this course if you are interested in improving your writing and learning about the origins of conspiracy theories and their appeal to ordinary individuals. I would definitely recommend it! Prof. Garcia is also a kind person who cares about your success as a writer.
This course is really centered around improving as a writer, not as much as learning about conspiracies. For your essays, you will discuss various conspiracies, but most class time is ultimately centered around how to write, such as forming a question and thesis. The work isn't difficult, but writing a quality paper does take time. Every other week there is usually a draft or revision due, so those weeks are more stressful, and the off week is more chill.
