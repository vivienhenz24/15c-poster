What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If you're interested in learning about this class and doing some original research in the archives, take this class! The classes themselves are honestly a little boring, and be warned most people in this class are going to be of the activist mentality (so I wouldn't expect to change any minds in discussions lol). Regardless, Prof Cole is pretty good at being available outside of class and giving feedback.
The content was super interesting, and lectures were incredibly engaging. Start on essays early, so that you can ask additional questions of the instructor because oftentimes, directions for writing are mentioned in lecture but not fully explained. Also, don't be scared to go to office hours, and be confident/come with a plan when you do.
I don't think this particular section will be offered again (or if it does then absolutely take it!), but don't let an extended scholarship course scare you!! I was a bit apprehensive about adding this course to my Expos sectioning form because I saw the extra requirement but thought it looked especially interesting, and it paid off! The extended scholarship for this course was an additional group* project at the end of the year for which I only had to write 200 words, and that was balanced out by a shorter mini essay for the second required essay as opposed to a full 5+ page essay. The group project was also something that we discussed and planned together in class over multiple days, so it was super low–stress and engaging. I can't say every extended scholarship course will be the same, but hopefully this helps :)
This class was really good! Professor Cole is kind and always available to chat through ideas if necessary. The content of the course was also really unique and a lot of our class discussions revolved around current issues on campus and in other nearby campuses.
