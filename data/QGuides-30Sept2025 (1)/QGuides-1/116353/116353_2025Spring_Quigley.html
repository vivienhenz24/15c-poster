I LOVE THIS CLASS SO MUCH!!! Dr. Quigley is one of the best writing teachers I've ever had, she's super understanding, knowledgeable, and kind. Not only is she a great teacher, she's also a wonderful person. I learned so much in this class as someone who is a STEM concentrator with no background in psychology. Every topic that gets covered is very interesting and relevant, and Dr. Quigley creates such a great, safe environment for discussions to occur, even ones about heavier topics like trauma. I had a really fun time with each essay. Dr. Quigley gives us a lot of freedom to explore what we find interesting and she's SUPER reasonable with deadlines and assignment flexibility + gives amazing, detailed feedback. As a queer kid, this was such a fantastic, safe space for me to improve my writing, learn about how psychology intersects with my identity, and interact with a really welcoming and insightful writing community. Highly, HIGHLY recommend!

Also not sure how much I've grown as a writer but that might be a general feeling across the Expos dept.
Gender and Mental Health with Dr. Kelsey Quigley was a wonderful experience. I am yet to meet another student who speaks as highly about their Expos as my classmates and I do. Dr. Quigley is an excellent instructor who understands that "growing as a writer" isn't just about the quality of your essays; it's also about the writing process itself. We spent significant time throughout the semester journaling about our writing processes, coming up with goals for each unit, and discussing our growth as a class.

Dr. Quigley is also very understanding when it comes to extensions. In addition to offering a 24–hour "freebie" extension that can be invoked on any assignment, she adapted to the needs of her students by extending deadlines for the entire class several times.

I expected Expos 20 to be tedious and stressful. Gender and Mental Health was neither of those thingsinstead, it was a highlight of freshman year. Dr. Quigley, if you're reading this, thank you so much.

I know this might be the last time Dr. Q teaches gender and mental health, so, if you are reading this and are considering taking a new expos class with her: TAKE IT. I promise you will enjoy it.
