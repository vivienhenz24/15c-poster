This class is so interesting! It really teaches you so much about not only Hell, but about our own fears and human nature. The readings are super interesting too!
This is a good EXPOS. Consists of 3 essays, one close reading, one where you apply the lens of some secondary scholarship, and one research where you find your own sources, worth 20, 25, and 40 percent of your grade respectively. We read everything from Dante to a Stephen King story, sources are incredibly interesting and he's very thoughtful about them all. In class discussions Adam is amazing and friendly.

I've heard a range of people saying everything from "he's a very harsh grader" to "he's a very kind grader" so I'm not quite sure which is true.
If you were an English kid in high school, I would highly recommend you take this class. There's a decent community, Adam is an amazing teacher who provides useful, detailed feedback designed to help you grow as a writer, and the content is interesting. However, if you don't really care, I might pick a different expos there is significant work to be done if you want to succeed, I think.
Really good readings if you're interested in the subject matter. Adam is a great and engaging preceptor, but can be a little harsh on grading compared to what I've heard of other expos preceptors. He does give very extensive feedback, which I appreciate and believe to have improved my writing. Would overall recommend taking this class.
Love this class!
This class definitely has more reading compared to other expos classes and it kind of depends upon your interests as to whether the extra reading is worth it. You can expect reading assignments before every class alongside the typical expos essay assignments. Read the syllabus carefully before choosing to take this course because of the substantial reading. Overall, the topics discussed in class are engaging and can be applied to many contemporary scenarios so there is a lot to gain from this course's content. Adam is also a super insightful and interesting professor and provides very thorough feedback both in conference and in written feedback.
only take if you are interested in the subject matter. I love the topic, and even I struggled to feel excited about this class in moments. honestly, the Scheffler can be a bit particular at times, which can take the fun out of writing. however, this might just be a symptom of expos in general. I still found the material engaging and think it is interesting as long as you put in effort. just expect the three essays to be a headache.
This is a great expos class, Adam gives excellent, specific feedback allowing you to edit your drafts to his liking. At the end of the day, this is an expos class, and you do need to "play the game a bit" (aka learning what your preceptor likes and doing it in your essays, even if it is not really your style or you would rather write a different way). But that is simply the nature of Expos. That aside, Adam runs a great expos course. Take "The Underworld" if you are interested in Hell, Greek mythology, the classics, and anything along those lines. The course material is so so so interesting and fun, and the readings are not long. My advice: Take Adam's feedback seriously, he knows what he's talking about. A plus: he likes to have fun in class and keep things from getting stale.
