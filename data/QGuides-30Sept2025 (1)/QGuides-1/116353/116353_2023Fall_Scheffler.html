originally, i was hesitant to take this course because past Q reports had conflicting opinions on both this course and Adam, but i am so glad i did. the material is so (!!!) interesting, Adam is extremely thoughtful, and compared to most other expos classes, the reading was very light. nobody likes expos, but this was a bearable and enjoyable class. START YOUR ESSAY EARLY! especially the final one! also be ready to accept criticism, and have to adjust your writing style. i completely recommend this expos!!’ don’t let the other reports scare you off. if you read the syllabus and you are interested, take this class—you won’t regret it.
Take it. I'm a STEM person at heart, and completing the readings and essays for this class was such a nice change of pace from my other coursework. I think in general some Expos 20 classes can be constrained into one extremely boring topic, but The Underworld is basically a lens class for writing about almost any issue the world faces in terms of hell (and you'd be surprised to see how many problems relate back to classical works). The readings are interesting and manageable, and Adam provides an insane amount of support on papers.
Please choose this Expos section. This was probably my favorite course of this semester, and Adam is an incredible teacher—he does an incredible job of making class fun and engaging. The readings from The Underworld, although grounded in Hell, explored much deeper issues of American society, such as the prison system and extreme capitalism, which made them all the more interesting.
100% TAKE THIS EXPOS. Adam is one of the best teachers I have ever had. The class discussions are interesting and fun, there are three essays, each a little longer and more difficult, but you get to really pick what you want to explore.
This class was very interesting, but be prepared to write. In order to produce your best work you're going to need to go through many drafts. Also, start early. The better your draft is, or the closer it is to what you think your final argument will be, will be to your advantage. The preceptor will be able to see your work and critique what you are actually writing about. Be sure to take his comments seriously and be prepared for the draft meetings. Come up with new ideas from him comments and be sure to write down questions you know you want answered. Otherwise, it was a great class! There was always reasonable reading assignments and the content was interesting.
If you are a prospective concentrator in the humanities, TAKE THIS COURSE. He's a harsh evaluator, I won't lie, but the quality of work he's going to push you to do is really high. I'd rather suffer as a freshman than suffer as an upperclassman unable to properly write a paper. On the other hand, if you are a STEM concentrator who will never take another writing course ever, I would recommend you to just take an easier section that fulfills the Expos requirement. But even if you're a STEM concentrator, the work you do is so rewarding, and since academic writing is a basic skill, I'd highly recommend you take it. Also, here's a nice plus: Unit 1 of this class is like a "crash–course Hum 10." Hear me out: you read a lot of the same texts, but you read tiny excerpts of it that are hell–flavored and interesting. He took out all of the boring/confusing passages. Honestly, the readings in this class are very manageable (I'd say at most 2 hrs per assignment); it's the writing that bumps up the average hours, so just keep that in mind if you are going in with a heavy workload.

Adam is awesome and he leaves an essay of comments that are super helpful. His conferences are also very helpful and as long as you take his advice, you'll find that your paper is a lot better.
The material is thought–provoking, Professor Scheffler is cool and easy to talk to, and the discussion in class is interesting. This class is a great way to acclimate to writing at Harvard.
TAKE THIS CLASS!! It's so, so much fun. The discussions we had in class were stimulating, fun, and engaging. Adam is a spectacular professor who genuinely cares about his students. He does a great job of facilitating discussions in class and making things fun. Although I found myself committing a lot of time to this class, in the end, it's SO worth it.
I really enjoyed this course and the materials. Adam is a great preceptor and makes the class constantly interesting and engaging, while also not having too much reading. If you are at all curious about Hell, I would definitely recommend!
