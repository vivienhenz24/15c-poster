This course is absolutely amazing! When I got this course after the lottery, I was honestly slightly upset because I'm verrrrrrry afraid of horror movies. But The Uses of Horror is a lot more than just watching scary movies. One is able to get a better understanding of so many aspects of life and how it relates to the things that scare us. In one semester alone, I have cross–applied the topics in this class to so many others. Mande is a great professor! She makes the class very interactive and once per unit, to make sure we understand the subject matter, we play a game or two that are primarily focused on the subject matter, but it makes being in class fun! If at any point you have felt scared to be in expos 20 because you come from a school that may not have taught you everything you need to know, Mande does a great job of fitting everyones unique perspectives and writing styles. Her assignments (in class) and presentations are also pretty helpful. The workload is nice because we mainly do discussions with a few handouts in class. When we do get homework (other than our essays), it is typically a few short stories, a movie, or some research articles. It isn't very strenuous, but it does teach a lot.
Mande really really cares, the readings are so good and also manageable, and the research is fun. An awesome class.
Make sure not to leave essays until the last minute. It's not a good idea.
If you have to take any Expos course, take this!! Mande is the MOST Wonderful teacher I have met in college, and she is so so helpful in every way. She is so understanding and so supportive! Also, all of the readings are so interesting and diverse, giving you new and interesting perspectives on horror. Also, the last essay was a delight to write with all the freedom we had!! Lastly, our class was such a community, with all of us ending as such good friends!
Please take this course! Dr. Zecca is super understanding and sweet! Please reach out to her either through email or even just for additional office hours!!
I would tell future students to really dig into this class. The readings are really fun and even after they get challenging they are still interesting. Dr. Zecca is incredible so definitely reach out to her with any and all of your questions while taking the course. Take advantage of the feedback cycles so that you get the most out of them as well. I highly recommend this class, especially if you have any interest in horror, writing or film.
The course is a feminist course because the majority of the things we talked about related to feminism. It is not truly scary readings. It is more societal scary and many of the readings are metaphorical and difficult. to understand.
If you are a fan of horror, I urge you to take this class. While the goal is to develop your academic essay writing abilities, being able to do this while engaging with interesting readings/films from the horror genre was so much fun. The third unit of the class allows you to select sources for your analysis, and that gives you an opportunity to do more research on your favorite horror novel or film. Ultimately, you will want to choose an Expos class on a topic that interests you, so if you love horror this is the one for you!
