As someone who did IB higher level writing for my last two years of high school, I feel EXPOS has similar expectations, but IB may be somewhat harder.
Overall, this is a good expos. I think my writing and analytical skills improved a lot. In comparison to what I've heard about other Expos classes, I would say this one is slightly on the harder side. However, it is very manageable if you put in some time. Make sure to ask all of your questions and schedule extra meetings if you need.
DO NOT TAKE THIS EXPOS. I though it was going to be fine because Professor Brown had good reports on past classes. Unless you are an expert in "magical realism" and "othering" stay far away. Professor Brown's comments are useless and have a pretentious tone. He always seems like he's trying to catch you making some analysis mistake when this is literally an introductory course. Also, he is very difficult to make meet with you...he all but refuses to hold office hours....I saw him say no to people asking to meet on multiple occasions. Stay far away.
this class is incredibly difficult despite it being expos20. the class is not structured well. there were little to no student–facilitated discussions and it was mostly dr. brown talking while going on tangents. he looks for very specific things in essays, and when you do not provide it, you're kind of cooked. despite that, he is unclear about his expectations and provides very vague comments. if possible, i would suggest taking a different expos course.
Do not take this Expos class. Professor Brown is very nice, but he does not lead the class well. He talks for 60 minutes each time about really vague ideas he has very narrow interpretations of, making it hard to take anything away. Class discussions were never student–led, and I would often leave class hearing people say they had no idea what he had just lectured about for an hour, so it felt like a waste. The assignments were also very open–ended, which could be a good or bad thing, but the grading also felt so arbitrary.
I will say that I think my writing improved over this course, so it wasn't for nothing, but any Expos section should do that for you just by flexing the muscle. There are more rewarding and less stressful options out there (stressful being not about workload but essentially guessing what you need to do to meet his standards). I also just wouldv'e structured the class differently––all the texts/movies are magical realism (well, almost all), but it didn't feel like there was a theme to any of them or time to dig deep enough to get anywhere meaningful.
This is a really good course that will strengthen your ability to write high–quality analytical papers. Selection of texts was a lot of fun, and you have a good deal of agency over what you get to write about. The only thing I would caution about this class specifically is that when writing an essay, make sure your thesis is as good as possible before submitting the first draft, because if it's weak, Professor Brown may not give much feedback on the rest of the essay.
