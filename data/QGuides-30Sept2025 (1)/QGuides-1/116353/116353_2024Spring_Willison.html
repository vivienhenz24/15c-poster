What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Considering this was the first iteration of the class, the class was run quite well. The concepts are interesting, and the essays, though stressful, were well integrated with the curriculum. Dr. Willison unfortunately somewhat left us to our own devices for the third paper, and became unresponsive when we desperately needed feedback on our essays; otherwise, he provided excellent and in–depth comments on all our essays. This is not one of the expos classes that you can mess around in and get an A, but I would genuinely consider giving this class a chance––you will learn a lot, even if you're not necessarily interested in philosophy.
Be careful about taking this class. It is a lot of work, the readings are incredibly difficult, and the 'language' portion of the topics takes up far more time than it should. However, the professor is very knowledgeable and will push you to become a better writer.
Amazing class!
This course is challenging, but generally interesting. If you take this course, you should be comfortable with a heavy reading workload consisting of largely philosophical, hard to follow text. If you're interested in this material you'll have a great time. Otherwise, you will likely find it hard to stay focused. Prof. Willison is a very cool guy, and his feedback is incredibly thorough.
