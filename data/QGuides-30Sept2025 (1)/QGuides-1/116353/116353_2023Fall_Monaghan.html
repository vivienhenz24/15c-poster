What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
The expectation is that you will come in with quite strong academic writing preparation. Definitely do not expect much one–on–one assistance or for your hand to be held.
This class is the hardest Expos out there, but it is also the best. You will have to adhere to formatting standards and source authentication that no other Expos will do, along with having lower grades for the level of work when compared to peers. But it will also make you the best writer that you can be and you would be remiss to not take it with IF you are going to be a history major. Otherwise it may be overwhelmingly tough and not have a future payoff for your academic writing.
Maybe don't take this class? That sounds harsh, but it's my honest suggestion. This class is hard (to the point where I would get pity when I told people that I was taking "Noncombatants"). Although your writing will definitely improve (Dr. Monaghan provides great feedback), I don't believe it is worth the hard work. There will be a lot of readings (some of which will be very confusing ), and the expectations for grading will not always be clear before assignments are due. If you do choose to take this course, I highly encourage you to start using the Writing Center ASAP (even as early as your first draft). Once you get to the Research Paper for Unit III, the First–Year Librarians will be your best friend.
Again, I would 100% recommend this course. The time commitment is not that bad, the material is interesting, and you learn a lot about your style and how to improve. Dr. Monaghan is so nice and helpful! Hopefully you will get to see her sweet cat during your zoom conferences like I did! Overall, high praise!!
Take it if you want to get better, not if you want an easy A. There isn't an easy A.
