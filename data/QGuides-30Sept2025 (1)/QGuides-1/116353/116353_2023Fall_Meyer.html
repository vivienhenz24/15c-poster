TAKE THIS CLASS!!!!! I came into this semester not really liking writing, but this ended up being my favorite class by far this semester. The units and readings are super interesting, and there isn't a ton of reading which is nice because I felt like I got to focus on the content and on my writing. This course also introduced me to sociology which has been awesome, and our class discussions were really interesting. My writing improved tremendously, and the writing focus of the course really helped me make a lot of the basic components of an essay pop in my writing. If this course looks remotely interesting to you, definitely add it to your Expos rankings. I felt like I learned and grew so much in this course and I cannot recommend it enough.

Dr. Meyer has a ton of enthusiasm and interest in the material, and it really shows. She was super engaging, and I always felt welcome in her class. It was really clear that she cares about teaching and about us as students. Her feedback on my essays was super helpful, and she always gave us additional time to talk with her.
Take this EXPOS! There's a lot to take away from it about the nature of work in the US and how a lot of our values are in flux depending on who we are and what we strive for in a job. I also felt like I received great feedback on essays and Rachel is an effective teacher!
Be prepared to work hard. This is NOT an easy expos. However, it is VERY rewarding if you have any interest in being more educated about the sociological world around you (or if you are having a crisis about your future 9–5 job.) Every person works, so it is applicable to everyone.
Dr. Meyer is amazing. This course is hard, but expos is hard in general. Although this may sound like common sense, the most important advice I could give to future students would be to pay attention to topics and make sure you're truly interested in it. I didn't think too much of topics and got lucky with this one (it was very interesting), but I can imagine being miserable writing essays about something that you have no interest in. It's about the culture of work, though, so it is fascinating. If that interests you, take this class. Do work in advance, and make use of office hours and email with Dr. Meyer.
Professor Meyer does a good job of spelling out how college–level academic writing works. To that end, do the readings, read the handouts, and you should perform fine so long as you have a decent background in high school writing. Beyond being a writing course, this class is a nice way to test the waters of sociology.
The topics are very engaging although a bit challenging however if you need any extra support the professor has office hours you can attend. The assignments are very doable and they are spaced out well. I would recommend the class especially if you are interested in the sociology aspect of work.
The last unit is particularly hard. The second is the easiest but, from my point of view, the most interesting. I would advise you to approach the professor and ask for feedback as many times as possible. Be aware this is not among the easiest expos classes.
TAKE THIS COURSE!! The readings are short and packed with sociology. Dr. Meyer is a talented professor and will build you into an amazing writer. This course was one of my favorites this semester.
Have an open mind and be ready to learn from your peers, they share new perspectives and make points that you don't consider. Keep up with the readings and be sure to understand them as it makes the writing process a lot easier. Keep up to date with deadlines, everything is given in advance so make sure to take advantage of that!
This class is super interesting. It's also A LOT of work for an EXPOS class. Most of the students in this class struggled a lot because the professor has high standards. Also, the professor's advice tends to be confusing so make sure to try the writing center. I had a really good time with the content, and every single thing we did in this class had intent which made it really worthwhile. Just remember that this is one of the harder EXPOS classes so be prepared.
