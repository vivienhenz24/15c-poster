This is an interesting EXPOS discussing the class structure of the US. When reading the comments keep in mind that Jim always changes the course readings to some extent. The readings are somewhat significant though and you have draft responses as regular assignments in every unit (~100 p. per week). Class discussions are also possible with skimming the material, though actual engagement is helpful. If you're interested in discussions about class, take it. Jim is a good lecturer and willing to engage with various viewpoints. His feedback is helpful. Despite EXPOS generally not being too helpful in improving your writing skills, this one is among the sections in which you actually work on writing.
This is THE BEST expos class you can get your hands on. Jim is an amazing instructor and always is willing to learn from his students, which is a baseline that really helps generate natural discussion. The subject is very interesting, and because it's social sciences, it felt conducive to the more formulaic nature of expos essays, as opposed to expos sections that are more dedicated to literary analysis. This class will help you to understand Harvard and your role in it, and the research component is really quite fun because of the nature of the class.
