I am so glad I took this class! I l got to learn about museum artifacts as well as see them in person in Harvard museums and the MFA. Professor Given is perhaps the best writing instructor I could have wished for. He is supportive, understanding, and is so skilled at helping us understand what is missing from our writing and how we can improve it.
Great expos!!! Definitely more work than others during the first unit, because of long readings, journaling and complex (content–wise) response paper. However, the readings were so worth it (DO THE READINGS), because they are fascinating and prepare you for the rest of the course's conversations. The second unit was a lot chiller and there is no third essay unlike most expos courses, instead there is a fun project. The second paper required rather thorough research and makes you familiar with the process of using Harvard's library resources. Professor offers really good feedback and is very considerate, as well as a good conversation leader. He meets one on one with you, making you feel well–supported.
I really loved the content of this specific Expos class, and it made me lean toward choosing Art History as a part of my education. Dr. Given does a great job at being available to talk with you about all facets of your writing and he does a really good job at making the semester as stress–free as possible. I would say it is a GEM in the Expos department for sure.
Dr. Given is an incredibly supportive and helpful writing instructor. The course is really unique from other expos classes, it is more spaced out, and it is more focused on quality over quantity. Highly recommend.
Expos 20 can seem very frustrating during the process of writing and researching because high–achieving first years at Harvard typically come from an AP English background, where they are taught to write concisely in under a certain amount of time. This class teaches you how to widen your long essay capabilities and hone in on your ability to write detailed, persuasive papers.
TAKE THIS CLASS!!!! I was a bit apprehensive reading previous Q guides, but I ended up ranking this class as my first choice and I have to say that I am SO glad I did. Dr. Given is an incredible preceptor –– kind, funny, understanding, and very knowledgeable about the things we talk about in class. I honestly don't feel like this class was more work compared to other Expos classes, and if you're interested in art history like I am, you will find the things discussed in class very very interesting.
You get what you put in – the readings are very helpful, so make sure to do them.
Take this class if you are interested in museums, law/legal matters, culture, art! Lots of case studies and interesting discussions regarding museums, ethics, and Harvard. We also go to different museums around Boston and Harvard which is super cool.
Dr. Given is a great, enthusiatic instructor who teaches concepts very clearly. I would recommend this class to anyone interested in the humanities who is taking expos.
Look, its expos. Its gonna expos. Youll learn to write argumentative papers! Hooray! Youll get great feedback on your writing, but you may not be writing about things that excite you. Dr. Given is, I think, a uniquely good writing prof. My reservations about this class are really just reservations about the writing program itself. The second half (or possibly last third) of the class was far less exciting than most of the actual content, largely because it felt like we were just slogging through the research and writing skills that the program wanted to throw at us.

However, this class started very strong, and I think its likely one of the better options for expos. I found the first half really interestingI hadnt engaged much with art and its associated ethical questions much, and it was really cool to learn the whole language of discourse in this area. The case studies make everything very salient and are a good combination of more practical and theoretical readings, and classroom discussions were pretty good once everyone warmed up to it.
