This seminar is AMAZING. Professor Nelson is so knowledgeable and truly wants the best for you if you equally show your enthusiasm. I learned so much in this class and the two we presentations we made were invigorating rather than tiring. I wish Nelson taught us more instead of students teaching students for the majority of the class, but nonetheless this was an amazing experience that motivated me for a future in a career of child psychiatry.
I would highly recommend this seminar!! Though the topic is a hard one, as it revolves around unusual abuse and neglect at an early age, it was a very insightful class that I learned a lot in. Professor Nelson is just amazing, he is one of the leading figures in his field of developmental neuroscience and it was an honor to get to know him personally. This seminar was great because it was about 15 people and Professor Nelson was able to get to know each of us individually. He also doesn't hesitate to connect us to his colleagues in the neuro/psychology field, which is amazing for future research opportunities. The seminar revolves around student–led presentations, so there's usually little/no work unless it's your week to present, in which case it is a concentrated period of a heavy amount. Overall, I loved this seminar so much because I wanted to learn if I was interested in pursuing neuroscience as a concentration. I would highly recommend!!
Be prepared to put in the work for the weeks you do present on, but the rest of the time it is pretty smooth sailing.
Be prepared to be comfortable doing a lot of research and presenting for long periods of time. The entirety of the course is watching/giving presentations, so be prepared to engage with other's presentations and give them yourself. The presentations are also all research–based, so it would help to be familiar with reading scientific papers. Overall, the course is so doable but don't put off starting your presentations.
