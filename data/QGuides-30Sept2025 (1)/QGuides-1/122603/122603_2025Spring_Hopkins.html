Great class! Taught well, good pace, engaging and humorous lectures, fair assignments, now Im much more comfortable and interested in a totally new area of math with which I was very inexperienced beforehand. Big fan overall.
The class was great; really well taught and a lot of support if you ever needed an extension. Came in thinking abstract alg would be boring, and left enjoying it a lot, especially when covering simple groups and Sylow Theorems. My only complaint would be a lack of per–lecture notes; that said, Hopkins wrote up a bunch of good notes towards the second half of the class when the lectures deviated more from the course textbooks. Would recommend the class if Hopkins is teaching it.
Professor Hopkins is wonderful! You can really tell he cares about his students and wants us to develop a genuine interest and appreciation in the course content. Id try to take other math courses with Professor Hopkins in the future!
Hopkins lectures are pretty bad, because he doesn't define the terms he uses before using them, so most people are usually lost throughout lecture. There was also once where he spent an entire lecture trying to prove something, but an unknown error during the proof caused the proof to not reach the desired statement at the end, and the whole lecture was a huge waste of time. I don't think Hopkins will read qguide reports, so he might not improve in the next iteration. But if does, I hope his lectures are more organized.

As algebra is a well–established subject, the textbooks are pretty old, and I don't think they are as comprehensive or succinct or clearly presented as more modern mathematical textbooks. If I were to redo this class, I would just skip all lectures and read the MIT OCW notes during lecture time. (https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/res–18–011–algebra–i–student–notes–fall–2021/mit18_701f21_full_lec_new.pdf)

Hopkins is also bad on the psets: there were 3 contiguous weeks in the later half of the semester where we didn't have any psets.

Grading though is fair, and the exams are pretty chill. I think everyone can get a good grade given some effort in this class, and Hopkins is a chill prof.
