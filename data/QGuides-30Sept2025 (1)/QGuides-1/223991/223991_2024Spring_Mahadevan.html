This class is extremely person–dependent. It can be a lot of fun (especially with field trips, painting like Jackson Pollock, various fun demos including a magician talking about perception) individually if you engage, but otherwise it can be very much an attempt for the teaching staff to entreat everyone to have fun with them. The class is designed to have people find more wonder in the world and be curious, and there are lots of opportunities to do so without worrying about a grade, as assignments are mostly based on completion. Unfortunately, it's not a group of self–selected wonderers/wanderers because it becomes a gem. So I think the idea is there and the concept is cute, but it ends up being like surfing around wikipedia with a friend on a porch and doing some unrelated assignments. However, the course seems very open to adaptation and change, so I encourage you to give it a shot and see if it's your vibe!
This response will probably encourage herds of gened takers rush to sign up for this class, but I am saying it as a critique.

This class in many ways reminded me of being in 2nd grade again. Every email starts with ~Dear Wonderers~. The homeworks were basically fill in the blanks, they were fully completion–based, and there were only 5 of them. Besides that, there's only a final project worth like 10%. And, on top of this, there's extra credit available too! The once a week lectures were an interesting walk through many disparate topics in science. This class would have ran wonderfully if people actually cared about the material and paid attention. But, it's a gened after all, and if people aren't held accountable for knowing the material for homeworks or exams, no one pays attention. This is my critique of the whole gened system (that no one ever cares about actually learning in them, just doing the minimal amount of work). I think this class has so much potential, if taught to an enthusiastic set of students, to be a place for so much creativity and learning. The class is quirky, interesting, and has a great course staff, but I think it would be better as a seminar than a gened.
