This is a great class to take! I took it as a concentration requirement and I learned a lot. It's a good intro course that goes over the basics of each branch of linguistics, and it's not too difficult. The class is fun and prioritizes learning and practicing through the psets, which are like puzzles. My classmates were either ling concentrators or seniors who concentrated in Mathematics and were looking for a logic–based humanities credit. If you're interested in Linguistics, or you want an interesting humanities credit and like solving puzzles, this is the class for you.
This class is very interesting. The grading can be quite odd (Some assignments allow multiple attempts and points were taken off on my second attempt for things that the TF had marked correct on my first, the TF took points off for questions I didnt answer even though they were written nowhere on the assignment sheet), but the professor and TF were receptive to asking about the grading in all cases but one. The lectures and sections are very information heavy, but sometimes things are not taught and then tested on the psets. The exams are not hard because theyre open book, and you can drop either a pset, the midterm, or the final, which makes this class very chill.
This is a fantastic survey class for linguistics. PSets are challenging and help develop your knowledge of each unit, midterm and final are quite laid–back and open–note. Lectures are explained clearly and thoroughly. Definitely take this class if Professor Bondarenko is teaching and/or Jian is a TF!!
For fellow linguistics concentrators: take this course (it's required for your degree) if you can't wait until the spring to start LING material. For those who don't need it to fulfill a requirement, take LING 83 in the spring as this class will be difficult and take more time per week than you would wish for an intro–level course. Overall, interesting material, albeit with a heavy syntax bias in the presentation. You can't slack off throughout this course, but if you are willing to pay attention and put in the work, it will be rewarding and relatively simple to pass with an A/A–. P–Sets were collaborative and often resubmittable, which made them less intimidating (try to make a few friends/study buddies in your class to check answers with before you submit!) –– but the P–Sets WERE difficult, so keep that in mind. The course moves FAST and, while the teaching staff is enthusiastically willing to help, my TF was not very helpful when answering questions. Make sure to attend Bondarenko's Office Hours if you're looking for a quick question and answer.

Overall, not horrible but I'd steer clear if I wasn't 100% interested in linguistics in all of its lovely, pedantic glory –– but for all those who truly adore Ling, take the class! I personally enjoyed it.
Please do not be fooled by any comments from previous years claiming this is a disorganized class! As with any class, try to do assignments, readings on time. And have fun with it!!

Linguistics is a very diverse subject. Every other week it feels like you're in a different class since the subject has a lot of breadth. But it's really fun!! And you may not fully understand a lecture on that day. That's why it's important to consolidate the info after hours. And attend office hours, again as with any class, but for this class it's useful if you find it hard to grasp some concepts to do the homework.

In practice you'll have fun because the material is fascinating and sometimes a little funny. Shout out to the bilabial trill!
This class is great! Jian and Tanya were super helpful and really made the class enjoyable. The readings and textbook are definitely helpful to look at before class, as the lecture slides go too fast to take notes (not too fast to understand, just don't expect to be writing things down). I had no background in linguistics, but I love brain teasers and puzzles and this class felt like an academic version of those. It was all just fun to do, and the assignments didn't feel boring or tedious at all. I would highly recommend.
