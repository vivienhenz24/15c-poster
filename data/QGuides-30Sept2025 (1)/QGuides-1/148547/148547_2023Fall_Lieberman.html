What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Don't be afraid to ask questions and to read extra papers!
I would tell them to take it if they are conducting research in immunology or an immunology related field. I would advise them to review throughly their previous knowledge about introductory immunology.
While the course is challenging for people with not much experience in immunology, I think the faculty lectures and recorded lectures were extremely helpful and more than enough to strengthen my foundations of immunology and added so much more detail to it. Among the details provided, I think it's better to know that since I might not remember the nitty gritty stuff all topics so its more important that I focus on understanding parts of it that I truly enjoy learning about by looking into some review papers about it. I also highly recommend that you read the papers for the TA discussion well as I found the aspect of discussing and analyzing them was really amazing although this does depend more on what TA section you are in.
This class will change the way you think about immunology and force you to think about how to advance the immunology field. There's a very steep learning curve during the beginning of the semester but it's worth it
