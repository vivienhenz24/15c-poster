Easy to pass while simultaneously getting a lot out of the course work. Sometimes the professor could be a little bit frustrating in discussions but overall it was an enjoyable course.
Take this class!! Dr. Rinard is a philosophical genius who knows a lot about neuroscience, psychology, and other fields necessary to discuss emotions. She is an engaging professor who knows how to moderate discussions in a way that always keeps the class engaged for the full two hours. She is a wise person who has taught me so much, both about emotions and other aspects of life, and has given me different perspectives on how to perceive my world. I enjoyed this class a lot, it was a nice break from my otherwise packed schedule where I got to talk to my peers and discuss things applicable to everyday life.
I would tell future students to be open–minded and think about personal analogies to include in reflections
Professor Rinard is fantastic at leading discussions – she is able to allow students to open up and share viewpoints while maintaining respect and academic focus. The reading encouraged diverse opinions as there was no one clear answer to many topics. Students openly debate and collaborate, share what they feel comfortable sharing.

Would definitely recommend this course or any discussion section led by Professor Rinard.
Take this class! This is what a freshman seminar is supposed to be: engaging, light and very interesting. You'll learn so much about your peers, and Dr Rinard is absolutely fantastic –– she gives great feedback, is amazing at facilitating discussion and shares her life experiences with us. This is a light course, the assignments are very doable and mostly require you to engage in some way with the readings. This course is about emotions, but above all, about life – how each of us lives it, how we harmonize our ways of living and what is the path forward for each one of us.
I would tell them to not expect to get as much out of it as they'd like. It strays quite heavily off topic into areas likely to be found uninteresting and trite by many.
