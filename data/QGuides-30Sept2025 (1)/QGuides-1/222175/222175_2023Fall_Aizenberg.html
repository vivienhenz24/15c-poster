What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class is awesome exposure to fascinating concepts and is a fun mix of students from different backgrounds. The course work is definitely very demanding though – a lot is asked for weekly assignments with a good amount of experimentation to be accomplished outside of class. Ability to work well in groups is ESSENTIAL – every class assignment is group work, with changing groups, as well as the final project.
It's an interesting class, but didn't feel all that applicable to HAA. Take it if you're interested in going into non–architectural design.
this course really tries to expand everyone's perspective through engagement on projects between engineers and design students who have completely different expertise and approaches. i didn't buy that that would work. but it really did, and it really makes you appreciate all that goes into creating a new product or technology.
The course introduces you to a ton of really interesting developments in material technology, but overall I felt that I could not take advantage of the class' resources because of how it was structured. The first half of the course was dedicated to learning about a new technology every week, but I feel like I just gained a superficial understanding of each technology. You spend the last half of the course on your final group project, and it really is a sprint to the end. But again, I didn't feel like my group was able to full advantage of the technology we were exposed to in this course, and that was a bit disappointing. I just think this course struggles in trying to blend engineers and designers and assumes that designers can pick up the science and engineering models immediately (and vice versa), which was not the case.
I love the vision of this course–– uniting engineers and designers to make better materials at every length scale. While the vision of this course is inspiring, for engineers (especially graduate students) it isn't super rigorous. This is partially due to the mixed students in the class–– design students will not be able to keep up with graduate level engineering and engineers are limited in design. In all honesty, I am not sure how this will be solved in the future, but I would love to see this course succeed. I do not think the night–time demo sessions were necessary or helpful. Instructors are absolutely passionate and want you to succeed. I think Jonathan and Joanna put their hearts into this class and I can't wait to see how it continues to grow. I would recommend it, but more as a fun class where you are learning about productization.
great course to take! highly recommend
