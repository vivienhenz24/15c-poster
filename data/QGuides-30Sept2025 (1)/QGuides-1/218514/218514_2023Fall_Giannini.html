Very engaging class. Tyler's great at facilitating conversation and himself is a fantastic resource and perspective. I actually enjoyed doing the assignments and the class format was varied and creative.
In this class you will be probed to think about issues in your community and how to address them. It's a good class and Tyler is an expert on what he teaches; however, the class often felt unengaging and a bit boring except when we did a simulation at the end of the term.
Take this class if you are interested in understanding the dynamics between communities, industries, governments, and civil societies. Ultimately, this class explores power and the ways that the law can and cannot be a remedy for the harmful actions of corporations. It is so relevant and extremely well taught. Tyler is an amazing professor, litigator, and person to get to learn from and know.
TAKE THIS CLASS!!!! IT IS SO INSIGHTFUL AND TYLER IS AN AMAZING PROFESSOR!! You learn so so so so much about what is going on in the world and what you can do to change that. Highly recommend if you like learning about how to make a change
TAKE THIS CLASS IF YOU'RE INTERESTED IN HUMAN RIGHTS!! Even if you're not, its a great exposure to the field of human rights and really goes in depth on how to advocate for communities in a strategic way. It is by no means surface level in that we really dive into the specific tactics and strategies we can use to fight for change. I found the class to be incredibly inspiring and empowering and feel like I had learned of several tools to employ in the human rights advocacy space in the future. The atmosphere of the class and students was also amazing – super passionate and inspiring students who genuinely care about creating change and are incredibly intelligent. I think this class is great to meet other like–minded people who are passionate about changemaking and see themselves doing so in the future.
If you want to learn about organizing, movements, and building community power, this is the PERFECT class for you! Tyler is absolutely wonderful, so intelligent, experienced, and so approachable. You get to work with him one–on–one, through tutorial sessions, and learn about the current state of not only human rights issues in our world, but the frameworks that enable their existence.
If you're looking for a cool seminar, this is a really nice one! It's helpful for the ease into Harvard and is just really nice to be a part of.
