What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class is very interesting, and Dr. Beach is great! The discussions are generally pretty conversational and we tend to get into the nitty gritty of not just what the paper is showing, but also how it is showing it (experiments and figures), whether we're convinced, and what we think could be improved. Dr. Beach knows a ton about auditory cognition, and is great at facilitating discussion. Because the class is so low commitment I didn't feel as immersed in the content as I often do for classes, but that's just a tradeoff with the time commitment, and I still learned a lot.
You read some pretty interesting papers and learn how to decode science–speak.
So far, this class is on hearing, babies learning language, dyslexia and hearing, schizophrenia and hearing, language and hearing, speaking and hearing, seeing and hearing, and other aspects of hearing. During our last class, we handled a real brain. It's a niche topic but I enjoyed it. Beware that the class is made up of reading a couple of academic papers a week. You will end up presenting two over the semester. Other than that it depends on who ends up in your class. Nice prof!
Take this course! I have learned so much about the auditory system through weekly articles, readings, and class discussions! The small group really creates a supportive and encouraging learning environment and I look forward to this class every week!
Sara is such a knowledgeable and sweet person! She brings a lot of enthusiasm and insight to the class, and it was amazing to get to know her throughout the semester. The readings and the discussions we have on them are super fascinating. The assignments are very chill, and the course overall is a stress–free way to learn about cool topics. The class presentations also feel collaborative and I loved listening to everyone else.
