At its core, CHEM 17 is Organic Chemistry, a class that is known to be difficult and the least favorite amongst a majority of pre–med students. Previously, CHEM 17 had a daunting reputation due to the instructors, but under Sirinya Matchacheep, the head instructor for Fall, 2023, the course was readjusted and made a lot more enjoyable. While the material and rigor is unchanged and remains very difficult, Sirinya is an outstanding instructor and facilitates engaging discussions surrounding the subject. Her personality makes Organic Chemistry, although difficult, slightly easier to tolerate. Even for students that absolutely hate the class, most absolutely adore Sirinya, as she is truly a one–of–a–kind professor and should be the model professor for all STEM classes.

For the course, I only have one complaint about its structure. During Fall, 2023, CHEM 17 made a fundamental change to the grading scheme for the class. Although exams now count for 75% of a student's grade in the course (as opposed to 80% the year directly prior), it came with a major caveat: the exam drop, which had previously saved the scores of many students, was removed. At its core, the exam drop, outside of being a saving grace for many students, was also a great tool to help students adjust to the rigor of the class and better balance the playing field. For students that come from privileged backgrounds and/or that went to really prestigious high schools, many have already taken Organic Chemistry and have already been exposed to the material. However, for students that are coming in with a less prepared background, especially those that are first–generation, low–income, this makes the playing field very uneven, as they often have a harder time adjusting to the material. Going forward, it would be really nice if the course staff could reconsider adding back the exam drop as an option for students in both CHEM 17 and CHEM 27.

If you are reading this, you are likely required to take CHEM 17 in order to fulfill your pre–med and/or concentration requirements. At the end of the day, it's organic chemistry, and it WILL be difficult, but take full advantage of all the different opportunities that this class has to offer, ESPECIALLY in terms of Office Hours, Help Room, PSL Tutoring, etc.
