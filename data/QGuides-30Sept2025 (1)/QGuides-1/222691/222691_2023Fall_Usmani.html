Comments
This is a course that will push you with ideas you might find challenging or uncomfortable. However, most importantly, it is a course that will challenge you to challenge those ideas. It is one worth taking if you want to develop your mind to become better at argumentation and reasoning. The content of the course is incredibly interesting, but the discussion facilitated by the professors is what makes it special.
This was hands down the best course I have ever taken at Harvard. Both Chris and Adaner are extremely kind and passionate about their work and very invested in their students. They don't just want their students to learn information, they are deeply invested in our rhetorical skills and thinking. I've never felt myself pushed so hard in my logic and argumentation, and feel profoundly grateful. This is what I expected a class at Harvard would be: our seminar was filled with incredibly passionate and intelligent students, with everyone tripping over one another to talk, share their experiences, and press a clever point or counterargument. I never felt, though, that anyone was arguing for the sake of arguing, there were not "devil's advocates" (expect sometimes Chris and Adaner). Everyone in the class cared so much about mass incarceration and policing. The readings were really manageable—this is the only class where I felt the professors clearly picked reading that they felt was reasonable to complete, averaging between 50 to 70 pages a week. The result of this was that everyone consistently did all the reading, so our discussions were exceptionally rich—no guessing or staying quiet because you're not sure what's going on. Finally, learning number–premise form has really helped clarify and sharpen my thinking. I have loved the memo assignments, I feel like I'm on top of my game and have produced some of the work I'm proudest of in this class. I could not recommend it more!
TAKE THIS CLASS! Great Professors, great blending of empirical and normative methods, the most academic freedom I have encountered in a class at Harvard. It really feels like you can challenge arguments made by your peers and your professors. Very cool to get insight into their book on mass incarceration. Their conclusions are controversial, but give it a shot and hear them out. There is no pressure to agree with them throughout the course. I will say the final paper is a bit daunting, but that seems to be the case for every Social Studies tutorial. If you are interested in mass incarceration and issues of inequality/justice, this is totally the class for you. The number premise form of the memos might be intimidating at first, but really helps to clarify your logical thinking. I feel like I’ve grown a lot in this course.
This course has engaging readings and discussions. The professors are highly qualified and dedicated to student growth.
This class in incredible. Highly highly recommend. Adaner and Chris are excellent professors who create a fun, engaging and rigorous classroom environment where you learn so much from them and other students. They do a great job of approaching a really complicated and fraught topic with openness and constant questioning that leaves you thinking critically and deeply about your own views.
This is a fantastic class. The material is all very interesting and engaging. You get opposing opinions on the same topic, and tutorial is filled with vibrant debates about those topics. Adaner and Chris are a great duo. They invite criticism, care about their students, and make every class interesting. I highly recommend the course.
