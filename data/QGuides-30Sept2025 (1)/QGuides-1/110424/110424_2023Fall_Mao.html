I have mixed feelings about this class. There are a lot of things I did not like about MCB 60, but in the end, I do appreciate the amount of knowledge I gained. So first, let me talk about the things that need to be fixed: the course structure and assignments. The PSETs and exams were ridiculously difficult for unfair reasons. The PSETs are meant to challenge students to think like researchers, but PSETs should also be an opportunity for students to have a safe–space to learn and actually practice thinking like a researcher. Instead, they are graded somewhat harshly (depending on the specific grader), and the pressure from having to get everything correct down to the last detail takes away from the best learning experience possible. Your homework should be designed so you learn from your mistakes, not for you to be afraid of making them. Unfortunately, these PSETs make up a large portion of your grade, so you just can't make mistakes or else. Also, the answer keys for the PSETs were often confusing and vague as well, so even that wasn't very helpful in the end. The office hours should be places where you can get questions cleared up, but for several of the office hours, the TFs were so focused on not giving away the answer that often they gave very vague guidance that made you walk away even more confused. I do recommend Janice's office hours though if she is still here. Also, the questions written on the PSETs are often written poorly and it can be super confusing on what the question is even talking about. What the question wants is also very vague. For instance, "what does the figure suggest?" Well, often the figure could suggest a lot of things, so you never know what the grader is actually looking for. The amount of time to get feedback is also not that great. By the time you receive feedback, you are already weeks ahead learning way different content. The shortcomings of the exams are very similar to what was described about the PSETs. The exams did get better as we grew over the semester, but the growth process was not the best. The first exam will be atrocious, but they will gradually be ok. Lastly, you should be aware that the flipped–classroom style also takes away from the learning experience. The weekly pre–lecture videos did not do the best job explaining the background we needed to know for the week. The background would be quickly glazed over and then we immediately dive into problem solving. The textbook did a good job filling in the holes, but it is still necessary to have detailed explanations on the week's concepts during instruction time just so we can all avoid confusion. However, we don't get that. The textbook reading assignments take up a lot of time and a lot of the content was never ever mentioned or applied again in class. It isn't the best use of time, but skipping it really wasn't a solution either since there are those one or two things you really need to know. You don't know what is important until later, so you really can't sift through the material efficiently. It will be challenging thinking about the best way to study for this class, but in the end, you will survive.

Despite the structure of the course needing some patch–up work, you will learn and grow a lot over the semester. I now have a better understanding of how the cell and body works like a machine. I enjoyed learning how prions are formed, how cell signaling happens, how stem cells work, and how the origins of cancer come to be among lots of other cool topics. This course covered a lot of great details about how biology works, and it really made me appreciate the fascinating nature of cells. They seem to always have a way around solving problems, and I remember often feeling amazed by the intelligence and adaptability of these building blocks of life. Also, I think MCB 60 did a good job preparing us for lab and research experiences outside of class. Now I have a lot of practice using pipets, knowing how to use centrifuge, doing dilutions, reading gels, counting cells, plating cells, growing colonies, doing Western blot, and also reading research literature.

MCB 60 is single–handedly the worst class I have taken thus far throughout my time at Harvard. While the content itself is very interesting, depending on the instructor, it may feel near impossible to comprehend. More specifically, Vlad Denic, one of the head instructors, is the worst lecturer I have had to listen to my entire life. While he is definitely very intelligent and knowledgable about the subject, he lectures to a class filled with mostly sophomores and juniors as if he is speaking to graduate, PhD level students, which makes it really hard to understand what he is saying. He basically complicates every single topic that is taught, often leaving students more confused than before they entered the classroom. Luckily, Dominic Mao, the other head instructor, is significantly better, and lectures significantly more effectively. If it were not for Professor Mao, this class wouldve been completely unbearable.

Next, in terms of exams, DO NOT UNDERESTIMATE THEIR DIFFICULTY. Although they are taken open–note and via Canvas, they ask topics so complex that having your notes will often NOT HELP. MCB 60 requires you to fully understand the content itself and be able to apply it. Even if you have the definition of a concept memorized word for word, it will do you no good unless youre able to apply it during the exam. The exams are written to be extremely difficult, with the first exam having a class average of a 57%. Although the exams only make up a cumulative of 30% of your course grade (meaning 10%/exam), it is easy to lose sight of points to where it is near impossible to end with over a 90%. Also, MCB 60 restricts TFs from grading our exams, so random graduate students are grading them instead, which often results in NO PARTIAL CREDIT being awarded for wrong answers, which can quickly tank your grade since most questions are worth around ~10 points.

Additionally, although the course used to be very generously curved, many students reported MCB 60 giving out a significantly higher amount of Bs last year. At this moment, I am unable to speak on this yet, since our letter grades have obviously not been decided or released, but please keep this in mind before enrolling. (There are no set grade cutoffs and everything is decided once the term ends).

Overall, MCB 60 is the type of class that you should only take if there is absolutely no other option. The past years have mentioned how harsh and difficult the course is, and I am confident future years will too. If you are enrolling, good luck, and study hard. There is no other way around it, but I wish you the best of luck!
