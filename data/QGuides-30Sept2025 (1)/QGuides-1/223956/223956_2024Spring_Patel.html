Soham is one chill professor. He will give you a lot of deep insight on contemporary and historical liberation movements around the world. He encourages dissent and thoughtful inquiry in the course –– don't feel like you need to show up on the same political page as him. I think this course is most appealing to those who want to understand how solidarity is formed between groups across the world. You will become acutely aware of how this internationalism came to decline in past decades but is making its brilliant resurgence in the moment.

The course is dynamic and open to change to accommodate current events. Multiple times throughout the course, I saw something I learned in class the next day in the news cycle. Take it!
Take this class! Learn! Challenge yourself and its ok to be uncomfortable, you dont grow inside of the box :)
This class is wonderful, one of the only classes that deeply engages with resistance, with solidarity, and with at this school. Soham is amazing, deeply knowledgable, and clearly cares about the subject and his students. Take this class!
This course was great if you want to learn more about topics that are often otherwise taboo at Harvard. This course breaks down colonialism and global imperialism very blatantly and constantly makes sure to draw a connection for how it connects to current events.
Prof. Patel is the coolest faculty member Harvard has to offer, EMR164 was the best class I've taken so far.
this is really a great class to take, especially if you care about whats going on the world at the moment. the classroom always felt like a very freeing space to let all my thoughts go & just speak freely, and it was nice to have that type of environment. the professor is very laid back which adds to that feeling of comfort as well. its nice having a class on campus that focuses on current events & recognizes how everything going on is an effect of colonization and how were still subjected to all its downstream negative effects. I really loved this class, take it !!
As a senior, Soham is one of the best instructors I've had during my time at Harvard. He is incredibly inspiring, engaging, considerate, and purposeful. Definitely take his classes – he will teach you a ton about the content, yourself, and the world around you.
Be open to learning about different theories and praxis; the class centers on a lot of leftist ideology but it's quite and can be applied in various contexts. I enjoyed the readings a lot and they felt less like an assignment. Not necessary to fully delve into the readings but if you enjoy readings theory you will enjoy this class a lot. Would recommend joining the class with friends who may. be interested in organizing since that may foster interesting discussions
This class is a wonderful introduction to forces, histories, and emerging movements of global rebellion, and it is easily the class I have taken at Harvard that has been most in conversation with current events. I appreciated Soham's openness and encouragement to reflect on present issues and to let them guide class discussions and interests. Any EMR secondary student looking for a class should take this one!
I wish more classes were like these. It was absolutely incredible and such an amazing space to foster conversations about liberation, movements, and rebellions. Finally a course that represented my academic interests, Havard needs more of this.
This might just be the most radical class at Harvard. This class deepened my understanding of the interconnectedness of revolutionary and liberation movements tenfold. The discussion–based aspect is amazing and the alternative format with no major papers or exams (just journal assignments and discussion posts) places a crucial emphasis on true learning, unattached to a grade or any product you as a student could produce. The knowledge you will gain from peers and class materials is invaluable as long as you go in unafraid to critique what you disagree with and also be open to changing your mind.
This course is a great opportunity to explore radical histories and theories which very few courses do in Harvard. It's also a great place to encounter some really compassionate, determined voices and opinions. I think some weeks got a bit repetitive since we covered similar themes from week to week, but for someone looking to get the ideas of authors like Fanon or 1960s radicalism, this course is a good intro!
This class fosters such a powerful environment, giving people the opportunity to feel safe and heard on campus. Take this class to learn about global solidarity!
