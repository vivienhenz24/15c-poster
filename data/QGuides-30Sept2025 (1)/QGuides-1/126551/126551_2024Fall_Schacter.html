I would recommend this course with some reservations. The topic is fascinating, but the material is dense and you'll have a hard time if you're bad at memorization. The final paper was due the same day as the exam, which was a bit annoying. The debate was fun, Schacter and Phelps are luminaries in the field and approachable professors, and Adam really cared a lot about us learning the material. I liked Neuro 80 better, as I felt like I had a lot more support –– there are no practice exams for midterms/final, no rubric for paper, etc. Overall good class. Oh, and they bring in other legends in the field (e.g., Gazzaniga) for Q+A which is pretty cool!
I really enjoyed the course! I felt like I learned a lot about the brain, and how it gives rise to the mind. I thought all of the exams and assignments were fair, and the professors were just so fantastic and interesting to listen to. Section is really boring and pretty much a repeat from lecture, but it is helpful for exam studying.
Exams have nitpicky T/F and MC questions at times, but they're not impossible and it's easy to get an A(–). I actually feel like I gained a broad overview of current cognitive neuroscience research. And I really enjoyed getting to interact with cog neuro pioneers across different topics.

Section felt boring and pointless though unfortunatelythey felt like forced office hours, so I think they should be optional
Read the textbook and study early
The tests are hard. Study along the way, and know the content backwards and front. There is no way to make an A in this class other than command of the material, and the material is heavy.
This class has a lot of content, but if you are interested in psychology and neuroscience, it is definitely worth taking. The tests are lecture heavy so if you really study you will be good. The professors are so kind. I am glad I took this class!
This class is overall a good experience! Although (as a current freshman) I havent taken any other classes in the psych department, this one made me feel a lot better about my ability to learn alongside upperclassmen and contribute my ideas to the course. The readings can be quite long and dense, but most of the material thats important to exams is covered in lecture. However, the slides that the professors post arent the best/it makes a lot more sense in–person, so please show up to lecture (it will help your grade so much, especially since the two midterms and final are really the only graded assignments based on what we do in class). Professor Schacter and Professor Phelps are so knowledgeable but dont be intimidated by their extensive background/experience, theyre super nice and approachable for advice about psych/neuro in general, and also on the final paper!
This class reallyd oes teach you a lot about this subject. If you're interested in cog neuro, there's rly no better class to take.
I would recommend that students frequently review course material and meet with the teaching staff.
I would recommend this class. As someone who wasn't super interested in cognitive neuroscience going into this semester, the class opened up many doors for me and made me so interested that now I am working in a cognitive neuro lab next semester. If you're looking to fill a foundational requirement or are just interested in learning about this topic, you will take away so much information. I was genuinely engaged the whole time.
This was a fun class with impressive lecturers! I actually went into this class skeptical but ended up loving my experience. If you are torn between this and NEURO80, this class focuses a lot more on the human brain and psychological behavior!
Overall interesting and engaging course content. If you attend lecture and are engaged, and review with ample time before the exams, you will do well enough. The type of course that, while not easy, exemplifies what we are at Harvard for: to struggle a bit, and learn a lot.
