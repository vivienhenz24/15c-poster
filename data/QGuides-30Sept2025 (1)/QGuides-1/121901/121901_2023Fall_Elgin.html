Comments
You will get out of this seminar what you put into it. The readings are very interesting. I was a little disappointed to see only readings that favored skepticism and almost none that challenged it. Sometimes the class can feel lecture–y, but that's when students aren't participating. When you do the reading, participate, and listen to your classmates, you'll find that you have a lot to say and leave the class with a lot to consider. Definitely on the more challenging side of freshsem, but if you enjoy philosophy, I would recommend.
This is generally a good class. The one problem I had with it is it is purely discussion based so much of the time it is people debating rather than the professor teaching. If this is your thing though, then you will like it. It also can become repetitive at times, as the whole class is based on skepticism. However, the professor is extremely enthusiastic about it and is extremely knowledgable. If you are passionate about philosophy, this class will be great for you. It is also very little work and the work that is given is very interesting.
This class is on an interesting subject matter but sometimes a whole semester of what ends up being basically one singular topic is too much. The same goes for the two–hour time slot: too much of one thing. It probably is better for those with very long attention spans or those who are good at doing random stuff on their computer while also getting something out of class.
Take part in the discussions. The class is more enjoyable and informative if you present and argue your ideas with the other students.
ONLY TAKE IF YOU HAVE PREVIOUS EXPOSURE TO PHILOSOPHY!!
This course is good if you want to take a philosophy seminar. However, the course is largely dependent on the other students in the class. If the class isn't engaged, the weekly meetings will be very boring and difficult to sit through. Professor Elgin is extremely knowledgable and pretty much has a response for any kind of philosophical question you ask her.
If you enjoy philosophy and difficult conversations, I suggest this course. The material will be difficult, and Prof. Elgin will catch you in some single syllable of your argument and prove you wrong, but its fun! The class will not be easy, but you can get through it.
