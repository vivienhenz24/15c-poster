Comments
Everyone should take this class! This is an incredible opportunity to learn a new language and learn about Deaf culture and accessibility conversations. This course and the Professor are just what we need right now, please dive in and build community!
Be sure to keep up with the course materials and ask follow–up questions!
This class is good if you want more ASL practice and to develop your vocabulary. While we do learn classifier uses and how to explain visual and spatial things, you may not develop this skill that much because we don't really have notes or ways to review outside of doing those as practice in class. Nozomi is a very kind and caring teacher, and wants everyone to succeed, but the course is not very organized and she almost never replies to emails while they are relevant. One pro or con, depending on how you see it, is that you really can't skip class. If you miss a class, none of the Canvas materials or the textbook will help you figure out what you missed. That improved my attendance massively, even though it was a 9am, but it was really frustrating when studying for quizzes and exams. Overall, if you are just taking ASL for the desire of learning the language, continue with ASL IV. If you have some specific level of proficiency you need to reach, you may need something more rigorous.
This class is super supportive. Nozomi does a wonderful job of helping students learn in an engaging way. She always is responsive to students, and I feel very lucky to have been in her class. She has so much knowledge, and while it can be hard at times, Nozomi pushes us to continue to grow as signers.
