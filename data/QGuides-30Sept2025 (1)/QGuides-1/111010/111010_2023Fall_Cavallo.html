Take it! It is amazing if you love working in small groups!

The math isn't super difficult, but the workload is what makes this class pretty hard. Still very possible to get an A though.
This is a fantastic class, as long as you're completing the psets and focus on attaining a good understanding of the reasoning/logic behind each formula or concept, you will do will on the exams. Great structure and support with the math question center(mqc)
This class is hard, but I still recommend taking it because it's rewarding (and useful for many different concentrations/future courses). Definitely go to MQC and/or Office Hours. This class moves at a fast pace and it is important that if you get confused, you should work with a TF to clear up confusions before you are too far removed from the lesson.
Math 1B has a reputation for being pretty difficult and grueling and I would say it lives up to its reputation. 3 PSETs a week is pretty hard, and I was at MQC for almost every PSET and still did not finish it in those three hours. All the TFs teach pretty differently, so find one that matches your learning style. Find a good study/PSET group and also do all the practice tests before every exam. Be prepared to put in a lot of time and work .
As long as you do your psets as early as you get them and you do all the practice exams for the midterms, you will be fine. It's tough but if you can manage your time you will get an A.
Take this class if you have not taken Calc 2
If you decide to take this course – and I think that you should – do the P–sets on the day they are assigned and utilize office hours instead of MQC.
TAKE THIS CLASS!! If you don't like math and find absolutely no joy in it, then maybe don't take this class. But if you're looking for an important and exciting math course (especially if it's your first one), 1B is great. If you're going into Harvard without taking Calc BC or covering that calculus material and you're looking for an "easy" course, this probably isn't it; take 1A. But if you're looking for a math class to ease into and you have taken BC (my situation), 1B is fantastic. Granted, the three problem sets a week is a lot, but I like math (they also cut down study times for the exams), so they feel like a break from all of my readings. This class also taught me so much that will prepare me for future math classes, especially as an AM concentrator. It made me glad I "retook cal 2" instead of taking the next level of math classes here.

This class really taught me how to approach math intuitively. In math classes, we often use formulas as foundations to build concepts, and teachers just ask students to "go with it," but in 1B, we derived those formulas. Then, we used those derivations to link concepts together, focusing on definitions of the vocabulary we used and being able to defend our answers based on those formulas, concepts, and definitions. This class has made math even more exciting. It's like a debate and a really fun one.

The staff is also extremely supportive and helpful in this class; regardless of the section TF everyone is eager to help!
TAKE THIS CLASS! Although it will be challenging and Problem Sets will be time consuming, you learn so much and take away so much. Math 1B puts a spin on high school calculus and prompts critical thinking and collaboration, which will make math fun (can't believe I'm saying this). I came into Math 1B, simply wanting to get done with a Pre–Health requirement but ended up loving it. Matt (Dr. C) is one of the most kindest, engaging, and passionate instructors I've ever had and I'm glad I took the class. Go to MQC (I've met so many friends through it) and there will be a special emotional bond that comes out of struggling through the course content with your peers. Some of my Math 1B Peers are my best friends and I love that I got to take away a new academic appreciation but made so many new friends.
Whereas my high school math classes taught me how to memorize concepts, math 1b gives you the intuition to solve unfamiliar problems. Switch your focus from solving problems quickly to really internalizing the underlying concepts taught in each problemthis mentality will pay off on the exam! You will get much more out of the course if you embrace the struggle of wrapping your head around slicing, Taylor Polynomials, and differential equations conceptually.
