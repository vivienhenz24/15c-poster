Just go straight to 21A
GO TO OFFICE HOURS. Find the office hours that tend to be in smaller groups with a TF or the professor, those you get the most questions answered and help you complete the psets much faster. MQC is sometimes helpful, however, when it is full, I find it to not be very helpful when it's hard to both focus with the noise and also have to wait in line to ask a CA a question. If you do go to MQC, go on the day the pset is assigned, so Mondays and Wednesdays where there are less people and can get more value out of that time instead of going the night before the pset is due. On Fridays, there is no MQC, so it is best to attend one of the office hours. The content of this course overlaps a lot with content from AP Calc BC, however, some topics go into much more detail and there are a few things that were not taught in AP calc.
The TF in this class matters SO SO MUCH. If you have a bad TF you risk your ability to complete the problems in the speed required to take midterms/exams and furthermore begin to lose confidence in yourself. You need to have taken calc within a year of taking this course because it is by far the HARDEST math class in the math department for the structure they teach it.
