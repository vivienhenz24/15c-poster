don't fall behind, it will be very hard to catch up, do a lot of practice problems
The horror stories you hear about this class are not real. While there are three psets a week, they are manageable and the content is important; I definitely plan to use what I've learned in Math 1B in future classes and other pursuits. That said, this class does have some major flaws. The main issue is that CAs grade psets while TFs grade exams. If your experience in this class is anything like mine, you'll receive many perfect scores on psets from lazy CAs, and while this is a great problem to have, you'll be convinced that the work you do on psets is correct and therefore should be applied to the exams. Wrong... the TFs grade harshly, and you'll be forced to go back to every lesson on your own and correct your current logic. Moreover, my TF had very poor time management, and we never finished worksheets in their entirety. Safe to say, you'll have to do a bit more work outside of class than you'd like. Still, I never felt too overwhelmed with the workload. I'd recommend this class, but be wary of what I've mentioned.
This class is definetly hard without a background in Calc BC or a strong understanding of Calc
If you're coming from Math 1a, this course will follow an identical structure. If this will be your first college math course, prepare for a considerable learning curve as you adapt to 3 psets a week (you'll get used to it eventually).

The exams are quite challenging so make sure to do ALL of the practice exames that they make available. They are especially picky for the Series unit so pay attention to the exact way that your TF writes out the arguments. Also, they were trying to make a point that people shouldn't go to the mqc just to get their answers checked so they were telling the CAs not to confirm answers which was really annoying.

Ultimately, if you're in this course, its because you have to take it for your concentration. So make sure that you set a schedule for yourself to get the psets done and find a pset group and you'll be just fine.
