this class is so cool but so challenging. do not be fooled by the title, you will spend a lot of time in office hours just trying to start the problem sets. class is very chill in vibes, though. very casually taught, but you're tested on everything so don't slack. ALSO DO THE READINGS!!! exams will test you on small details and big ideas from any given reading, so definitely keep up with those, too. anyone with a background in statistics will thrive in this course, but those without can still do well, just with way more work.
You will learn a lot about analytics and economics through the lense of sports
one of my favorite courses, a must take if you are interested in sports and has so many applications outside of it. Genuinely looked forward to class every day as a last semester senior. take it, you won't regret it! However definitely nicer to take with friends in the class.
At times it was difficult to find the relation to economics so I advise when going into this course 100% understanding that. There are a lot of confusing equations that lack a clear explanation in lecture. The exams also require a lot of specific recall from specific coefficients in lectures/readings to being able to remember which author wrote what and their specific results. I recommend going to office hours for psets as that is how I was able to do well on them. The psets at times didn't make sense (the questions weren't clear if you needed to do math or could just explain). Judd gets very passionate about certain topics and its very entertaining to watch. Attending lecture is a must – the slides don't work on their own without his commentary and laser pointer.
Great class. Really interesting if you like sports. Professor Cramer is amazing and knows everything about sports.
If you're a sports fan, this will be your favorite class at Harvard. The syllabus is awesome and Judd is the man. Could not endorse more. It's completely changed the way I think about the games I watch every weekend.
Take this class if you're interested in learning about how to analyze sports–related problems through an economic lens. I liked the course overall, but felt like the CA support wasn't as strong. Judd is a really nice teacher and makes himself available to students outside of class. I didn't take Stat 110 before taking it, so there were a few topics that I had to watch a YouTube video to learn, specifically the binomial distribution. However, don't be discouraged if your math isn't strong. The exams are largely conceptual, and Judd gives partial credit if you show that you understand the concepts, but maybe messed up the math a little.
It can be a frustrating class because assignments are tedious and it's hard to know what you're supposed to take away from any given lecture, but there is some really interesting information that you learn if you're willing to read through the slides a second time after class. Graded decently easy as well.
This class is awesome! Judd is very chill, and the workload is not too bad at all. Definitely a relatively easy way to fulfill your EC theory–based elective req.
Was not a very well organized coursed. Lectures not well structured and sometimes looked like screenshots from twitter and ESPN aggregated into slides.
This class is not to tough compared to the other classes that fill the same requirement, and the topics discussed in the class are very interesting and applicable.
Was the most interesting class I've taken during my time at Harvard.
Great class if you're interested in sports! Don't be intimidated if you don't understand sports analytics or stats and stuff, Prof Judd assumes no experience. HUGE variability with office hours, they were always being shifted around last minute.
Judd is very approachable and takes you through the course content in an interesting way. Course content was really varied, and took a number of perspectives on issues and content related to the world of sports
Enjoy the lectures! Put effort towards the psets as they serve as practice for the exams.
very interesting material, and super relevant to everyday life. I wish I'd taken this course earlier in college, since I found the material intriguing enough to potentially inspire a thesis, but alas, I'm now a senior and it's too late. took this class as an elective because I was done with my econ concentration requirements, and I can say that it was definitely worth taking because of how interesting the material is. a few drawbacks, however, were the lack of organization of the slides and lack of conciseness with the problem sets (worded confusingly sometimes). Tests were a bit tricky, but Prof Cramer is very receptive to feedback so I wouldn't worry too too much about that. overall, very fun course, decently laid back (no sections!) but with lots of intellectual reward. take it!!
Judd is awesome! The course is super interesting and definitely can be taken by econ secondaries who haven't taken metrics as long as you actually come to class and pay attention. Disorganized at times, but go to office hours for help with the psets and with any questions and you'll be good.
Very easy and interesting class. Very interesting lectures if you are interested in sports. If you know sports well and pay attention in lecture the exams are also trivially easy. I am not sure why other students say the exam is hard. The assignments are not great though, not because they are difficult, but because they are frustrating to do because the questions are framed poorly.
Definitely take this class, and take it pass/fail. The psets and exams are a bit difficult, so taking the class pass fail lets you learn the most you can from the class and enjoy the content. It made me, who previously had no interest in sports, have a lot of interest in sports now.
