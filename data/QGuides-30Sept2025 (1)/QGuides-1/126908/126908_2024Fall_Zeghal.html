This course had great potential but ultimately left me a bit disappointed. It covers a wide range of regions, aspects, and thinkers from the Middle East, including many figures youve likely never engaged with before, which is great. The workload is also super manageable. Quizzes are short and easy, and the only major assignments are the final project and an open–book final exam, both of which are more analysis–based, so theres no need for heavy memorization.

A highlight for me was my section leader Kevin. Hes super chill, engaging, and encouragingdefinitely try to get into his section if you can!

However, I found the lectures difficult to follow. They often felt disorganized and could have really benefited from starting with a clear rundown of key historical facts, events, and figures before jumping into analysis. Without this context, discussions felt reductive and repetitive at times, especially toward the end. Organizing the material chronologically or by country instead of bouncing between concepts might have helped in my opinion.

I also felt the class lacked intellectual vitality. Discussions didnt dive as deeply as they could have into some central tensions and complexities, and I often felt the material leaned too heavily in one direction without enough nuance. Overall, it's a good class to take if you're just looking for a chill GENED to fulfill your req, but it felt like a lost opportunity to dive deeper into some of the central conflicts in today's world.
