I really recommend taking this course!! The topic is extremely interesting and Professor Colton has such an incredible amount of knowledge on the topic. As long as you do the readings and stay engaged in lecture, you will do well. You will be extremely prepared for both the midterm and final. I had little to no knowledge on the topic coming into this course and am finishing this course having learned more than I have in any other Gov course at Harvard. I highly recommend taking this – the lectures themselves are very interesting and Professor Colton does a great job! The TF was also GREAT.
This class is perfect if you want to be educated about the current state of Russia and to be able to have a perspective on what is going on. The readings and materials we are given are really interesting and Professor Colton has a lot of wisdom and first–hand experience to share, which made his lectures engaging. I was pleasantly surprised by how in depth our studies went into the soviet era, and I feel as though this greatly contributed to my understanding of the political landscape today.
I learned a lot in this course, but unfortunately it was mostly from the reading and less from the lectures. The readings are broad, varied, and genuinely quite interesting, but Professor Colton's lecture style is not dynamic and was difficult for me to stay engaged with.
This is a great class for learning more about Putin, his governance style, and the trends in general in modern Russian society. The course is quite fair in terms of materials and assignments are straightforward if you complete the readings. Lectures are interesting and detailed, though a bit dry at times. Professor Colton has considerable knowledge of Russia and has interviewed many influential Russian politicians, giving him a wealth of knowledge that is evident during lecture.
This is a great course given you are very interested in the politics of Russia.
I would recommend this course very strongly to any students interested in better understanding Russia. Dr. Colton does an excellent job of providing a survey of its politics while also providing sufficiently detailed elaborations on particular issues, in both readings and lectures, that even people who have already taken coursework on Russia will find themselves walking away from the course having learned plenty of new things.
If you're looking for a course that takes a comprehensive and thoughtful look at post–Soviet Russia, this is the course to take.
