Absolutely do not take this class, find another way to get a math requirement. This is the worst course I have ever taken at Harvard.

First of all, you better know how to teach yourself because teaching is not what they do in this class. They have implemented a "figure it out yourself method" where you work with your classmates to figure out problems on a topic you have not learned yet, because you have not learned it you spend the majority of the time doing the question wrong instead of learning the fundamental procedures.

Secondly, there are 3 problem sets a week and they are not short (average 4 question with multiple sheets per problem set). Unfortunately, there are not enough office hours or MQC time so they are always overcrowded so you spend so much time on these which is not something you can do with 3 other courses. On top of that, they only count for 20% of your total grade!

Thirdly, they make you take this "workshop" if you receive below an 80% on the calculus fundamentals test they make you take before the course. The workshop is attended by the majority of the course and attendance is required for grade. Because of this nobody cares about it and as it taught by a Harvard student not faculty, nobody does the work in class – it is a waste of time.

Finally, the practice exam materials are not reflective of the real exams at all. They're so much harder than the actual exam (which I guess is a good thing), but you spend so much of your study time trying to navigate the difficulty of the problem instead of focusing on the fundamentals. The exams also have a group component which are 15% of the exam grade and you have to work together after just taking the individual exam to do a whole other exam of problems.

I repeat, do not waste your time taking this course. It's a complete waste of your time and education here at Harvard, it has ruined any passion in math I had.
