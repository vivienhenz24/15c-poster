This course was incredibly enriching and insightful. Professor Light is absolutely wonderful –– she's super knowledgeable, passionate, and extremely kind, and her lectures were engaging. Because she comes from a historical background, this course leans heavily on examining American history through the lens of violence through examples and case studies, which offered a more nuanced perspective on the evolution of gun culture in the US. This course did a good job of not shying away from talking about violence, but because we talked about it so much, I think it made us somewhat desensitized to the magnitude and atrocities of violence. This does not mean we avoided talking about how horrific these acts of violence were, but I think we started to lose an emotional response when talking about the subject. On the other hand, that may not necessarily be a bad thing because guns are such a sensitive and triggering topic. I do wish that we spent some more time examining mass shootings and the messaging of politicians and groups after they occurred, as that is an example of gun violence taken to the extreme.

The workload for this class is manageable while still allowing for the course to be reflective and engaging. I liked the assignments that we had, but sometimes, the directions on what was expected and what we had to do were not clear. Because there are also not too many assignments, your grade can shift relatively easily and is dependent on how your TF grades. I liked the readings a lot for this class though, and our section discussions were also stimulating. Overall, I would definitely recommend this course!
