Absolutely take this seminar. Dr. Block is incredible and if you are at all considering a career in healthcare this class will tell you if that is a good idea! It only deepened my desire to become a physician, but I do know that others took this class and absolutely loved it but admitted at the end that they now know that being a doctor isn't for them, which is so fair! You will come away from this class with a completely new understanding and appreciation for the healthcare system and those that work in it.
This is a fantastic course. Susan is incredibly kind, and gives very helpful, thoughtful feedback. Make sure to do the readings they are very interesting and you will have many opportunities to discuss them in class. The opportunities that you will have to talk with patients and medical professionals are truly incredible.
I would highly encourage future students to take this class because it is not the typical "premed class" you would expect, but it truly opens your eyes to the world of patient care and challenges you to think about tough situations in the shoes of the doctor and the patient. Dr. Block is also such an amazing and inspiring professor, doctor, and mentor who has so much to say about palliative care and why it is so necessary. This type of class or experience does not come around easily so I would urge any interested students to give it a try.
Taking this class would be the best decision you could ever make in your first year here. First and foremost, Susan will remind you how great teachers can actually be; she is so kind, understanding, passionate, funny, and knowledgeable! She brings incredible energy (and cookies) to class every time we meet, and who she is as a person is part of what makes this class so unforgettable. The other parts would be the fascinating material, from facts about the healthcare system to life skills and experiences you will never forget and the encouraging classroom environment. It is hard to describe exactly what being in this class meant to me. However, if anyone ever asks me if I regret choosing Harvard, I can think about this class and the memories I created in it and ask how I could.
I highly highly recommend this class. Profesor Block is a pioneer and leader in her field and builds a great sense of community in the class through discussion and social events such as her dinner party for students. Discussions in this class range from the opioid epidemic to death and spirituality and physician–aided suicide. This is the most interesting class I have taken at Harvard and it will push you to think and grow in ways you never have before.
This class is contingent upon people who are willing to be super vulnerable about their past and their personal lives.
The class is great. The teacher is amazing and very caring and knowledgeable on the material. The readings are interesting can be boring and long at times. The conversations in class were complex(in a good way) and left me wanting to understand more. Assignments were not hard just sometimes time consuming
I would tell other students that it's an interesting course, if the topic speaks to you. However, it is very reading heavy for a seminar.
This course will teach you a lot about the end of life. Susan is very caring about this course and allows for conversations to go in the direction students steer it towards. The readings are often too much in my opinion. Some weeks, we were assigned whole books of 300 pages along with more articles. However, from the ones I did read, I learned a lot. Needless to say, you get out of this course what you put into it. The weekly assignments aren't difficult but eye–opening. I do recommend this course to anyone interested. There are a lot of important viewpoints in this course and Susan welcomes them all. She often brings cookies for the class to enjoy. Overall, this was a great freshman seminar.
