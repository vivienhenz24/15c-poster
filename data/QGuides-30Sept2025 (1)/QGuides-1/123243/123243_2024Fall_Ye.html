good course working out of haynes miller's notes. homeworks take a while to do, but are illuminating. fan ye was a friendly and accessible instructor. greg did an excellent job as TF –– super helpful with great feedback and course policies.
Take this class, but make sure to dedicate a significant amount of time to understanding the material deeply. Algebraic topology is a very deep subject, and it's easy to skim over the details. Make sure you don't!
Great class, good assignments, good lecture notes. Fan Ye is a very learned guy. Greg Wong is a wonderful TF––really put in a lot of effort for the class in terms of grading and feedback. Responds quickly on slack etc. Will be looking out for classes either taught by Fan Ye or TF'd by Greg in the future.
It depends on who is teaching. The content is interesting, and the necessary algebraic machinery is taught along the way. It is not an easy introduction though.
I liked Fan a lot, and he did great on the lecture notes, but I feel like algebraic topology is such a visual subject and we spent a lot of time on the algebra. Looking back this was a good thing maybe, but going in with a vague idea that homology is some measure of holes on topological spaces then being introduced to a rote, algebraic definition of homology on general chain complexes was a little rough. I suppose like a lecture giving informal definition would have been nice. Lecture was very sparse, which in this case was helpful to ask questions and just have conversation. Also I think this class depends on professor.
