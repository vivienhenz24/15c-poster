A must–take if you can take it. Take it for the learning opportunity that it presents, above and beyond what is on the syllabus and in the readings. What you get out of this course depends on what you put into the discussion and conversations with the instructors and fellow students.
This class is lovely and an incredible opportunity to be lectured by three world–renowned professors in their fields. Not only that, but because the students are all typically from varied concentrations, the conversations had in class are extremely varied, fruitful, and interesting. I highly recommend the course.
The professors are cool, but expect very little structure out of whatever is the next iteration in this course series. If you're a Phil/Econ/Math concentrator interested in seeing what the other disciplines in that trio have to say about the course topic, it can be an interesting ride, but given the lack of assignments and generally low buy–in you're likely to get (3–4 people dominated the discussions in my class), it's difficult to recommend this course with much enthusiasm.
I've been fortunate enough to take 248R two times, which has arguably been my defining experience at Harvard. There is probably no more impressive combination of instructors in any classroom anywhere in the world, and each session has made an impact on the way I think about the world. If you are lucky enough to take this class, do so without question.
Like with other seminars, this course is about finding out the opinions of others and you gain the most from talking to the professors and other students.
This class wasn't the most fun. That said, I think I derived enough value from it to be worth the time spent on it. The final paper was long (20+ pages), but I personally didn't mind writing it. The professors are amazing, although class sessions were often quite dry and boring.
