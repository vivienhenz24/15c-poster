would recommend! leslie is a great lecturer. very diverse readings, so it's super accessible, even if you aren't familiar with scholarship in traditional orientalism. will make you think more about the nature of discourse, science/speculative fiction, narratives about asia / technological progress / the future writ large.
TAKE THIS CLASS. Leslie is one of the coolest, kindest, chillest professors out there. A lot of the class involves reading science fiction and watching movies, and the course combines these readings with more theoretical Asian American studies texts in a way that's both fun AND fascinating. Once you know about techno–Orientalism, you'll start seeing it everywhere.
This is the best class I've taken at Harvard, I could not recommend it higher. Not only is the content interesting, but the class materials are impeccable, and the course staff are so thoughtful, intelligent, and kind. This class holds a special place in my heart and has changed a lot of how I analyze the world (& science fiction/art) around me; I think I will still be thinking about what I've learned from this class long after I graduate. If you are at all interested in taking this class, please take it! It is so wonderful!
Take this class! Leslie is super knowledgeable, caring, and the workload is super reasonable. The inherent interdisciplinary nature of the content was super unique among my classes at Harvard, and I also deeply appreciated the diverse breadth of students who took the class, ranging from CS to social sciences to humanities. I really enjoyed this as my introduction to EMR at Harvard, and would love to take more EMR classes!
