Take this course if you plan to write a thesis. Not necessarily because it's a fun or enjoyable class, but because it will put you in the best position to start your thesis research over the summer. The lectures and information are super helpful. However, I felt that this class sometimes rewarded people who had every detail of their thesis figured out from day 1. As someone who didn't know exactly what I wanted to write about from the start of this class, I felt that I sometimes lost points on early assignments because I hadn't figured everything out yet.
This course is pretty much NECESSARY for a Gov (research–based) thesis. It goes over lots of different methods. The TFs (especially Aidan) provide super helpful feedback on what you should do better. Again, it is not even a question, this class is NECESSARY for political science research.
Its a necessary class if you want to decide whether or not to thesis
This is a great class to get you ready to write your thesis! It helps you to think about all the write things and make sure you are prepared for any research you are doing over the summer. The assignments aren't always the most exciting, but they are really helpful and the professors and TFs care a lot about building up your project and making sure you feel comfortable with it.
There were many useful resources introduced in this class, but there were also many things introduced which will be beyond the scope of any individual student's project. There were a lot of technical terms and a lot of emphasizing how hard a thesis will be, which was very stressful. I'm glad I took this course, as I do feel somewhat better prepared to begin my research, but most of that came from seeking outside meetings with my TF and professors rather than from lectures. I would recommend this course, but I would also recommend not reading too much into it if it stresses you out.
I've loved this class! I think it is incredibly helpful for anyone planning to (or even thinking about) writing a thesis. Professor Hagopian and Professor Ternullo are both amazing people and wonderful instructors who really want to see you succeed. I loved hearing their lectures because I thought they were very insightful, summarized the key content well, and were highly relevant to our theses. My 2 nitpicks with the class are (1) I wish the first research assignment was just graded for completion and with written feedback (it is due pretty early in the semester before most students have a good idea of what direction they want to take their thesis and (2) I did not find the activities at the end of lecture to be all that helpful. I thought lecture itself had much greater returns and I honestly would have preferred a longer lecture than working through some of the activities.
If you plan to write a thesis, please take this class! Professors Hagopian and Ternullo are absolutely excellentthe work they spend on preparing the presentations is evident and each week is an overview of a different research method. Even if you don't plan to use that method, it is still valuable to listen in case you plan to use that method in the future. The assignments all build toward the final prospectus and break down the pieces of writing a thesis. Priyanka is a very helpful TF who can precisely pinpoint what is needed to fill in gaps in your research design. Overall, a wonderful course with wonderful mentors.
If you want to write a thesis and need someone to hold you accountable, take this class!! I know I wouldn't have made as much progress towards my thesis on my own, or been so proactive in finding a thesis advisor. As someone who also took two gov seminars, I don't think that taking two gov seminars can prepare you to write a thesis as well as this class even though that is allowed to fulfill the concentration honors requirement.
If you're even remotely interested in research or writing a thesis, TAKE THIS CLASS. I truly cannot recommend it enough. Prof Hagopian, Prof Ternullo, Aidan, and Priyanka are an absolute dream team of a teaching staff. You will leave the class with a full prospectus of your thesis, which is truly invaluable. Although it can be a lot of reading, you will learn so many important concepts that are crucial to designing your research (and learn a lot about your own topic too). This class will make your thesis seem less intimidating and equips you so well to go into the summer ready to hit the ground running with research.
Whoever reads this, I highly recommend you evaluate why exactly you want to potentially write a senior thesis. It is a difficult venture to embark on, and through the semester, you will research your topic of interest in great depth. However, the structure of the class can make it overwhelming, and the canvas is a bit disorganized and tedious.

If you have taken previous research methods, this class won't be that useful. I felt that most of the information presented to me was common knowledge, and don't even get me started on the reading. The readings are so tedious that you would be unmotivated to continue with them. Additionally, you have the pressure of obtaining a senior thesis advisor, and finding an advisor is difficult. However, even if you fail to have a senior advisor, you can find someone later on your senior fall, just don't fall. The section was also simply a repeat of what we discussed in class.

The class is also boring if you are familiar with the content. If you want to learn something new, take the other variant of Gov 62 which is Theory.
If you are planning to write a thesis, I cannot recommend this class enough!! I learned so much about what it actually means to write a thesis, and now I am going into the summer before senior year with a detailed plan of what I am going to do for my thesis and how I'm going to do it. The professors are so great and helpful, and I had a lot of fun developing my research question!
While this class is not the most fun or enjoyable experience, I would definitely recommend it to anyone planning to write a senior thesis. By the end of the course, you will have a clear plan for how to approach your thesis, which I guarantee you will not have otherwise.

If you are hoping or possibly interested in writing a senior thesis course in government using quantitative methods, TAKE THIS COURSE. It is extremely helpful in getting you to think about defining your research question, completing a literature review of your topic, crafting a research design and a theory to answer your question, and how you will gather and analyze your evidence. The smaller assignments build on one another so you can get feedback as you go and so that the final research prospectus is very manageable. This course will put you miles ahead if you want to write a thesis and will get you to a good spot especially if you want to do fieldwork over the summer .
This class is definitely helpful if you are planning on writing a Government thesis, as it covers lots of different qualitative methods you can use and specific ways of thinking about structuring your research question, proposal, and research practice. The section component of the course in particular helps you develop your own idea as you get feedback from peers and its also super cool to watch a few of your classmates' ideas develop throughout the semester as you give them feedback. Gov62 also holds your hand through finding an advisor and applying to grants, which was massively helpful.

That being said, the lectures were incredibly dry, and incredibly long. The second half was always an "in–class exercise" that always felt like a forced high school team bonding activity. I would still recommend this class for thesis writers, but just know that you may not get a lot out of lecture.
