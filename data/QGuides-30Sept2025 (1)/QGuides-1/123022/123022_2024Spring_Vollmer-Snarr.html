What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class seems daunting because of the 5–hour time block, but it is SO worth it! You learn how to apply what you've learned in organic chem to practical situations in the lab. Because the class is small, our class became very close and formed a tight–knit community that I'm very thankful for. The staff is enthusiastic and gives great feedback, and the DC trip is amazing. Being able to engage in policy as well as research broadens your scope of science and gives you new perspective. I could not recommend this class more to anyone who is interested.
1000/10 recommend this class!! Aside from the ways in which I learned and grew immensely as a scientist, this lab taught me so much more. Working with a partner and a TF the whole time fosters teamwork and a great deal of patience, especially. Also, the amount of time you spend with your classmates really turns you all into the best chem family to be found. We all got extremely close, especially during the (AMAZING) trip to DC, but there was a ton of bonding even outside of that trip. If you're even considering taking this class, absolutely 1000% do it.
This is the best course I've ever taken at Harvard. Never before have I seen a course staff (Especially Heidi) who care so much about students. This semester we worked on molecular glue synthesis. I learned organic lab techniques (Biotage, HPLC, inert atmosphere, etc), but my bigger takeaways were with how synthesis research is done. We gave group meeting presentations, annotated spectra, and wrote supporting information for our compounds (like for a paper). The poster presentation at the end seemed daunting but each step was broken down and honestly was not too stressful because of the support the staff gave. You also have a Lab Partner to split most assignments with!!

The science advocacy component of this class was cool and definitely do the DC trip if you sign up! Talking to staffers and congressmen/senators was a cool experience.

I loved the community the class produces. The 10 students this semester really forged strong friendships in this class and this was a powerful experience.

Advice: do as much of the work/assignments in class so you can ask any questions if you are unsure or get stuck. You can leave the lab early and go home, but I usually stayed till the end to finish the assignments and basically not have research work outside of class. Workload is very fair for this class.

Side Note: Chem 135 (experimental synthetic organic chemistry) is taught alongside Chem 100r. Chem 135 is a smaller and faster–paced course with more advanced labs, but does not have the science advocacy component (this may change) and also at least in this iteration of the course was less project–based. Students in Chem 135 tend to have more organic research experience, though it is not necessary by any means and the TFs will help you if you don't in either class!
I loved the teaching staff! Everyone was always so kind and helpful. This course is truly one of my favorite courses throughout my years here. Love lab time with my lab partner as well as the D.C. trip! This course has helped me understand concepts at a deeper level than my current understanding.
This class is a terrific way to complete the chemistry lab practical requirement, and the advocacy trip to Washington is amazing to boot!
A great class to meet new people in the chem department and gain comfort with science research. The teaching staff are the best at Harvard! The class is very work intense due to the 5 hr labs with many graded assignments but the teaching staff are helpful and can be flexible
This class truly presents a chill learning environment and the teaching team is dedicated and patient. There is no midterm and final exam, instead we have a poster/oral presentation at the end of the semester, from which I learned a lot about science–based presentation. The DC trip was also really enjoyable – talking to politicians as scientists is a whole different experience. Put simply, I strongly recommend taking this class.
The class is very light if you keep up with everything. There is generally a lot of down time in lab, so you can generally get the assignments done in there. Definitely check the assignments with teaching staff, because there are certain things that they are particular about, so it is easier to get it checked along the way, than to fix everything at the end.
