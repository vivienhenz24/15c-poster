What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class is a great class to take to learn how to do basic chemical procedures that are absolutely necessary for any chemistry lab. You should definitely take this class if you haven't been part of an organic chemistry lab before. . The Washington trip is super fun and very well organized. This class has a lot of busy work so I would take it when you have an easier course load as this class is very time consuming.
don't take this class just because you want to go to DC – it's a great experience but – take it if you really want an in–depth understanding of the workflow of a lab
Although the amount of class time is very daunting, this class is well worth your time! You learn so much, meet great people, and get to go on a great trip to Washington, D.C.!
All the best parts of lab work, with a high level of personal teaching. All the worst parts of lab reports, with high standards over a lot of writing. The trip to D.C. was great as a learning experience, and helped me find community among chemistry department!
Be wary of taking this course if you have other really time intensive courses or extracurriculars that semester. I had to schedule my remaining 3 classes and their sections all for MW and I basically left class drained every day because of how time intensive M–Th ended up being. The actual lab is pretty fun, we played music and the TFs made a genuine effort to get to know us and make the lab work fun. Heidi is a strict grader (like super super nit–picky) and there was kind of an expectation to either come in outside of lab hours for extra work and to submit work with really small turnaround time (did not happen to me but someone in the class got emailed their feedback for an assignment at midnight and was told to submit the assignment at 11:30am the following morning...with less than 12 hours to get that feedback addressed) which is not great considering this is already a 10hr/week lab class. There are no exams or midterms or anything so that was good and the D.C. trip was honestly amazing. It made a lot of the annoying parts of the class worth it and I really enjoyed the experience of science advocacy and took away a lot from it. This definitely isn't the worst class I've taken at Harvard, but it requires some planning about your courses and a big time commitment, as we had homework outside of lab pretty much every week. I don't regret taking this class over something like 135 or 145 and I'd take it again if I were to choose a lab class again, I would just make sure to plan out the work for this class and also ask for feedback before assignments are due so that the draft you turn in is close to what they're looking for. In the end, grading ended up going great, the feedback was just sometimes frustrating.
Honestly, I really enjoyed Chem 100r! I was very intimated by the 10 hours/week in lab at first, but it's actually not as bad as it seems. And you do really cool research with potential real–life applications which was very cool! The teachings staff is awesome and super supportive too. The DC trip is definitely a highlight! This course really helped me gain a deeper appreciation of organic synthesis and as someone who had never worked in a lab before, I actually feel like I now have real research skills and experience which is awesome.
It takes up a lot of your time, but you will end up loving it.
