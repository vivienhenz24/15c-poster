This course is completely fascinating, and so are hackers. Some of the most interesting histories and people I have learned about, yet relevant to society in important ways that I never knew. Take this course. Really, you should really take this course. It's amazing. Also, some classes have professors who teach something because they've written important work about it, some because they have actual direct experience with the subject...Gabriella Coleman and Matt (TF) have both.
Take this course even if you have the slightest interest in learning about hacker cultures, history, and politics. It's very low–stress, and the class discussions (including with amazing guests) were so insightful!
This was a great class! I highly recommend taking it, even if you know nothing about hackers. It is a really good discussion class, and there are guest speakers who are actually involved in the hacker community, which was very interesting. The only thing I would suggest is to make sure you stay up to date on the readings, since the class was once per week and I found the readings could sneak up on me. Reasonable amount of readings though.
An incredible and unique course that, if you have the chance, you must take! You get the opportunity to engage with anthropology, history, politics, and so much more and leave the class with new perspectives on not just hackers but also digital media, technology, and ethics.
If you're at all interested in internet culture/hackers, this class is great! Prof. Coleman is an expert in the field. One of the best classes I've taken at Harvard.
This class is phenomenal. Professor Coleman is amazing! She is extremely knowledgeable in the hacking space and has so many incredible experiences to share. The readings, guest speakers, and discussions are all interesting and engaging.
