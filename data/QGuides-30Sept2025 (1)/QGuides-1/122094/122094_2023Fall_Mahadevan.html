I suspect lots of the comments here from other people will betaking about the exam. I want to countermand those comments, and also to say a few things generally about this course, which I've broken down systematically below:

1. Instructor
Maha is amazing. He really has a passion for the subject material, and it shows. He's responsive, available in his office hours, and lectures really compellingly. He also does a good job at reinforcing key results you need to understand (things like the Cauchy–Riemann equations, conformal mapping, the geometry of complex numbers, and when it comes to Fourier Analysis, things like duality and links between functional and Fourier space). His blackboards are works of art, and if you show up and pay attention, you'll be fine. The lecture notes are also extremely helpful.

2. Homeworks
Homeworks are generally pretty tricky, and sometimes involve a lot of number crunching. A couple involve Python, but only using it to plot functions and do basic calculations. Get a pset group together and you'll be more than fine.

3. TFs & section
Section isn't mandatory (at least it wasn't this semester). Section problems are really, really tricky, but also (slightly annoyingly), some of the section problems are similar to the ones in that week's homework, so at least check out the solutions to that week's section problems if you're super stuck and need inspiration.
The TFs were good, but I fear that some of them, in trying not to just hand out answers, were sphinx–like in their ability not to answer my homework questions. I also think they (strangely enough) had slightly unrealistic expectations about the class' mathematical ability.

4. Midterm
The midterm this semester was very reasonable. The mean was super high, and if you did the past midterm they gave as revision, and were solid with the concepts, then you were fine.

5. Final
The 36–hour take–home final was very difficult. The problem was that (A) the 2022 take–home, given as revision, was really quite elementary, and (B) the estimate of '4–6 hours' of work in the 36–hour window we had ended up not being very accurate. The questions were conceptually very challenging, and involved applying the material at a quite high level – comparable to the upper end of section questions rather than the homeworks or the midterm. It will probably be graded on a generous curve, but it was, I think, a shock to some people's systems.

*overall*
This is a tricky course. If you are coming into it as an engineer, physicist, or applied maths person who thinks that they can vaguely understand the material, know some formulae, and ChatGPT/WolframAlpha your way to an A, I fear that you won't have a good time. People that are complaining about the exam (and here I fully acknowledge the frustration I am sure they have) probably didn't take the time to *deeply* understand the material as they went along; they probably relied heavily on others and TFs for homework help, and so when it came to the final, of course they were going to be a bit freaked out.
