This is one of my favorite courses I've taken. Amazing course staff and all sections and lectures were fun and made me more appreciative of music and its composition.
This course was so fun!!!!! I learned so much about music theory, got to be creative and compose music of my own, and made some great connections with my peers and the course staff while doing so. Everyone was so nice, and it really enabled me to make progress as a musician without being too stressed.
This was a fun class and I definitely learned some music theory, but this is not the class that I had read about on the Q guide from prior semesters. If you have ever played an instrument or read a piece of sheet music, this class will start painstakingly slow for you. We had Monday and Friday sections that were basically just lecture led by a TF, and those were chaotic, a bit unstructured, but covered a lot of material and were generally pretty engaging. Wednesday lectures always felt like a complete waste of time. Assignments were extremely quick and easy. This class was definitely a gem because of how easy it was, but the class certainly left a lot to be desired. I feel like I could have learned a lot more music theory but didn't. It was nice to have this class in my schedule because of how low commitment it was, but I left the class feeling pretty disappointed and wishing for more.
This was a great intro class. Probably easy for those who are musicians and probably just right for complete beginners. There's lots of room for creativity in assignments, and you still end up with a very decent base of music theory knowledge by the end of the class. The pace was just right. Unfortunately, we didn't do a lot of listening or listening practice, which I thought was a shame.
I did not have any prior music experience and thought this class was awesome. It was a lot of fun and I learned SO much. My only warning is that the expected weekly workload is biased slightly. A significant amount of the class had substantial music background. If you are coming in with no music knowledge, it will be fine and you will learn a lot but just expect a bit more work (3–5 hours).
This class is a solid way to go from not being able to read any music to being able to compose something simple. It covers reading pitch, reading rhythm, intervals, and triad+seventh chords. It was personally very rewarding for me being able to apply what I learned each day to my own personal activities like choir or playing my instrument. I believe experience in the class heavily depended on which section you got into, because there are 2 sections and 1 lecture a week, so most content was delivered via section. My section leader was not the best lecturer (confusing explanations) and didn't read the room very well (didn't know what students were confused about and what they understand or how to ask) but I could tell he was trying his best. The course lectures were really really good though!
This class is really great! Fun, not too difficult, and not too time consuming. The course staff were passionate about the topics, and welcoming to students with any music background. Perfect course to take if you want to expand your knowledge beyond what you've self–studied on Youtube :) Or if you have no background knowledge at all!
Great course with fun and stress–free projects
Consider taking 51a or another higher–level music theory class if youre interested and have some music background. For a first–time introduction this is great.
Very fun and engaging class! It can feel slow in the beginning
This class is a great introduction to music. I learned the most Ive ever learned in a college class, and was able to finally understand how music works and why. The classes were interactive and fun and consisted of studying the theory, analyzing real scores and listening to all types of music, from pop to classical. Nevertheless, it was hard to understand the concepts so fast, so it required me to invest more time outside of class to master the material. Many students had already taken some type of music classes before and are already experienced (some are just taking it to fulfill an intro requirement, and some even have perfect pitch), so their participation and knowledge might be intimidating for someone with no experience, which definitely made me struggle a little more and forced me to never stop asking questions. If you put in the work, youll be perfectly fine.
