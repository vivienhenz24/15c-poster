As the rest of the Q will probably say, the assessments are tough because they're mostly based on the readings. Make sure you know the main claims from each reading (introduction and conclusion), relevant legislature it might be related to (this part is usually in the slides; don't get bogged down with details), and if it was an experiment, what group was tested and the outcome (ie organ donations in Iran). Almost no one goes to lecture and pays attention, so if you do that you'll be ahead of most people because I think people overlooked the value of understanding the readings in a larger context (ie with the lecture slides and libertarian perspective) and didn't have enough time to prepare. Take at least 3–4 days in advance to prep for exams; this is not something you can easily cram for if you want an A.
