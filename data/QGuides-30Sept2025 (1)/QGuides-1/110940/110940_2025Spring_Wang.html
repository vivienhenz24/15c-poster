I loved this class! The Chinese department is fantastic, all of the teachers are so nice and so good at their jobs. The daily class can always be a struggle but your Chinese will definitely improve. If youre considering taking this class you definitely should!
This course is excellent but VERY HARD –– in my view it was graded much harder than 120A. All the usual pros and cons of Chinese language courses at Harvard apply: great preceptors, lovely discussions, Vanserg, meets 5 days per week, two quizzes per week, etc. I noticed that Huang lao shi tended to grade significantly harder than the other instructors. But overall, all the instructors were excellent and very kind, as always! It is a LOT of work though –– roughly equivalent to a pset class. I would strongly recommend doing all the extra credit assignments!
If you're nervous about continuing your study of Mandarin Chinese, don't be! This course and the teaching staff are incredibly helpful! There are a lot of extra credit opportunities, though you will have to put in work (whether that is posting a message on Discord or helping run a cultural event), that are super helpful if you think your grade is on the cusp between two marks. Also, the late policy is extremely forgiving and is shaped around making sure that you actually understand the content and are improving your language skills, while providing leniency when life gets busy and/or tough.
Chinese class is always such an enjoyable time. Wang Laoshi, Chen Laoshi, and Huang Laoshi make the class so engaging with different roleplays and activities. We did activities such as shadow puppets in class. You learn a lot about Chinese culture, and the teachers really help you improve your Chinese language skills. They provide good feedback on work, and they are always responsive to emails and questions.
