What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
AMAZING class, probably my favorite I've taken five semesters into Harvard (as evidenced by the length of this response). I'm a psych minor and thought the class was an absolute GEM –– the readings don't end up being too bad as long as you understand their main points and results/conclusions, and it's really easy to inadvertently hit the (very non–strict) 500–word discussion post guideline while simply explaining your takeaways, so the weekly assignments were really not time–consuming (contrary to my expectations). The "seminar leader" assignment seems like a lot, but it's really just explaining the readings from your chosen week and asking the class to discuss some questions, and Ryan is really helpful with preparing for it. The final paper also ends up being pretty chill because a good chunk of it is regarding your personal takeaways from the literature, which is not hard to write about.

Ryan is an awesome professor, really understanding, flexible with assignments, and responsive. At no point in the semester did I ever feel really pressured by this course because most work just followed smoothly from understanding the readings and was evaluated really fairly as long as it was clear you'd thought about the material a bit. He would frequently go on interesting natural tangents that really helped me 1) mentally connect the course material to other parts of psychology, 2) get insight into parts of psychology I hadn't learned before, and 3) genuinely enjoy and look forward to class each week. If nothing else, I really appreciated a crash course he gave on how to efficiently read a psych paper, which's helped me a LOT with both understanding them and cutting down the time I need to do so, as well as his explanations of some commonly used statistical models in psych papers that I hadn't known about before and served as helpful statistical refreshers. My favorite part of this class was the small seminar discussion feel, since that made it really easy to participate (which was not graded harshly at all) and share/get opinions from Ryan and the class, so oftentimes the class felt like a mutually beneficial dialogue incorporating many different perspectives on why/how something might be the case.

I really recommend PSY 1711 as long as you're interested in some of the seminar topics or personality psychology overall. I definitely understand behavioral/personality psychology MUCH better because of this course –– holistically, not solely limited to each week's specific topics –– because of the helpful frameworks/intuition I got throughout the semester. Not much of psychology I've been exposed to before put this much emphasis into how situational factors affect one's personality both short–term and long–term, and I feel much more able think about them both theoretically and with application to better understand my own life now. The course also covers a slew of topics not really touched on in other PSY courses I've taken, from self–concept to social media's impact on personality, and oftentimes really recent research on those subjects. While I was obviously more interested in some topics than others, I thought the class was extremely enlightening overall and definitely had some new takeaways I appreciated from each week. Overall, I thought the breadth this course covered while still keeping a coherent focus on situations' influence on personality psychology was extremely impressive and helpful to learn from, and the class environment/setting made it really easy and enjoyable to do so.
TAKE THIS COURSE! It is so fun and a very reasonable course load. The lecture may seem scary, but it's not. Ryan is a great instructor too!!
