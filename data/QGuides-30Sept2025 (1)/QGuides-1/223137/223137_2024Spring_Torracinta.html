Comments
I really enjoyed this class – the material, though it spans a very long time period, was very coherent in terms of themes and Simon did a very good job of drawing connections throughout history. I think this is a good class to take if that kind of thinking appeals to you. The readings were quite interesting and I enjoyed class disucssions. Simon is also a good lecturer and great to chat with in office hours.
This is an absolutely fantastic class it's really interesting, and Dr. Torracinta really cares about the topic and his students. I thought it was a great experience and a great chance to learn about topics you might not normally hear talked about in conjunction with one another. The course has a not insignificant amount of readings, which you are asked to write reading questions about at the beginning of each week. Please try to do all of the readings they're really interesting and important. The larger assignments are three unit reflections and a final exam. The unit reflections are not a big lift at all, but I found it really really helpful to go to office hours and talk through ideas and analysis. If Huw is still TFing the course next year, he's fantastic. The final exam consists of a few essays and was open note, so it's also not about memorization, which is really helpful. Overall, I really enjoyed this course and thought it was a positive experience!
This isn't a hard class, but it's not a "gem". I.e. think of this class as a way to balance a heavier workload in an interesting way rather than something requiring 0 commitment (you're graded on attendance/participation, there are weekly reading/writing assignments, etc.)
Take this class!! As an econ concentrator in my senior spring, I could not have ended my academic career in a better way. The content is very eye–opening and the professor makes even ancient history accessible and relevant. Learned a ton without being overly stressed, and was able to participate without an explicit HistSci background.
