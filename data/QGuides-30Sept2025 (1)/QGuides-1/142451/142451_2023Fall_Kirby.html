I think the class gives a good overview of Chinese history. It starts out more comprehensive during the imperial age and becomes increasingly focused on politics, international relations, and economy as the curriculum moves onto the modern age. The class is largely discussion–based, so you must be ready to contribute to the discussion at any given time, both in lecture and in section. The readings are very informative but can sometimes feel like a lot to get through.
The course itself is a wonderful exploration of history, and while it can be overwhelming at times, the lecturers and staff are some of the most talented and helpful you can find at Harvard.
This is an amazing course for anyone who has any interest in China! The first half of the course is a survey of the history of China, while the second part deals with more contemporary issues and events. Professors Bol and Kirby are some of the best in their fields, and they are incredibly great resources. If you are not a fan of cold calling in class, I would probably caution against taking this class. Sections were very thought–stimulating and it prompts you to think about China from various perspectives. Overall, highly recommend!!!
Just go for it, if you're ready to experience something insightful and engaging.
Take this class. If you have an ounce of interest in learning more about China and Chinese history, take this class. It's the full package, its manageable, informative, and fun.
I recommend the class if you're an EAS concentration who wants to knock out a Gened and concentration requirement at the same time. However, be wary of the workload. It's pretty bad some weeks. Also, the professors cold call during lecture, and ask questions about the readings, so make sure you're prepared for that. Lastly, the short paper was 5–7 pages, and the group project was 25 pages with three sentences of instructions provided three weeks before the deadline. If those things sound scary to you (which they probably should), don't take the class.
If you really want to learn, audit this class instead. The workload is ridiculous and the grading is harsh. And the flipped classroom ends up more irritating than rewarding for just having one lecture a week. The knowledge is fascinating but branding this as a GENED instead of an upperlevel History class is very disingenuous.
It's an interesting class and you gain a broad understanding of thousands of years of Chinese history. The discussion of modern post–Mao China is particularly relevant. However, the class is organized and taught quite poorly. The lectures are not engaging, and just regurgitate content from the online modules and readings. (The silver lining is there is only 1 lecture/week) The online Canvas modules and readings themselves were often redundant with each other. There was no active learning or critical discussion in class, it was just 75 minutes of the professors reciting things from the readings. Section was often just call and response of facts as well, and did not provide any opportunity for interesting discussion beyond the level of the course materials. The workload tends towards the high side for a GenEd, with several hours of online modules and several hours of readings each week. It's ironic that Prof Kirby was dean of FAS and yet this class still has such archaic teaching methods that do not foster any kind of critical reflection or meaningful student discussion. If you have to take a GenEd, this is definitely one you can get through, but I would not strongly recommend it.
You get out what you put in. There are a lot of readings and video modules, but fully completing them makes you very prepared to participate in class and section. Without doing the preparation it may be hard to contribute new ideas, which is crucial in a discussion–based class like this. Especially since the professors do cold call, I recommend reading
The class is somewhat hard to evaluate because it's split among the two professors. Professor Bol's part is great and a really interesting overview of Chinese history from the neolithic to the early Qing empire. Professor Bol is engaging and knowledgable and I enjoyed his lectures. Professor Kirby fills in after that with sections on contemporary China, and in my view Professor Kirby has a questionable command on teaching and presents a significant amount of material dogmatically while uncritically engaging with many of his interview subjects which you need to read as HBS cases. I think it's instructive to remember here that his subject of study (contemporary China) requires access to political and business leaders, which in my view leads to a fairly specific relationship with and coverage of those subjects... Most of the reviews of this class from past years focus on complaining about the workload, but I didn't find that to be a major issue.
Require ongoing participation; be critical; need to finish course modules before lectures.
This class will teach you a lot about China, Chinese culture, and the history of China. However, this is a fair amount of work. You have to watch videos, do little quizzes about them, and read a fairly large volume of material each week. If you skip doing something you will regret it. Also, if you're not a fan of flipped classroom (lectures with the most content are videotaped and watched on your own time) then know that this class has that set–up.
Great class. Professors and TF are amazing, and the lectures are engaging. It was a bit more work for a GenEd, but overall it was worth it because I felt like I learned a lot and enjoyed the class. Would definitely recommend!
