Great course for taking on individual projects, Annette really gives you the creative freedom to take projects wherever and supports you along the way to help you get there. This can be daunting at times to work with, but I really recommend talking to Annette for help, she is very kind and genuinely wants to work with whatever ideas you bring. Most of the work ramps up right before projects are due, theres 3 in total, so being consistent with progress or getting ahead with prepping the screen–related materials is key. I will say the steps to use the process itself are rather extensive, which is something to consider for project ideas and if youre not used to slowing down for your art process. Overall it was a fun class and seeing everyones work during and after the presentations was very inspiring to see.
This class is great! I really enjoyed learning how screen–printing works, and met all kinds of artists.
The class consists of three projects which have little to no guidelines/restrictions. You get to choose yourself what you want to do. This semester we made many normal prints, like just artwork on the wall, but also other crazier stuff like a lamp that hung from the ceiling, a supermarket shelf, many sculptures, and even a huge tree with prints hanging off the branches. You get so much freedom (and support!) to explore whatever you want to do. Just be realistic and don't undertake an insane project that you'll have to rush to finish the day before critique.
You learn a lot about silkscreening (obviously), and get to experiment with different techniques like CMYK or mixing colors on the screen itself, but a big part of what I took away from this class was the critique after each project. We all gather around one artwork at a time, hear the student explain their thought process or the meaning behind the piece, and then give our thoughts, compliments, and constructive criticism – what could have been improved, what could be changed to make the piece even better.
All–in–all, I recommend this class. Very fun!
This class is an amazing opportunity to do whatever you want creatively with guidance from professors who both care and have great references and advice.
