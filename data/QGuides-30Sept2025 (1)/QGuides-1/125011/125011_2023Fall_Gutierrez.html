This was definitely my favorite class this semester. I was nervous after seeing the Q reports from previous years, but this course was fantastic. Daisy was an excellent teacher, and my Spanish has improved tremendously. This class is very manageable and very rewarding. I am now considering a citation or potentially a secondary in Romance Languages. The thematic units were very interesting and taught me a lot about the Spanish speaking world. The homework readings occasionally take some time to read and understand, but they are always engaging. The workload is doable and it is easy to do quite well if you apply yourself and study thoughtfully. I can't recommend this course enough.
Response from an FGLI FYRE Student: I am a native speaker but I wanted to take this course to improve my writing skills. The beginning of the course was easy, but it got harder in the middle of the year. I think some of the assignments aren't as clear, so please ask questions whenever you are confused. My preceptor Daisy was a fair grader, but then she left for maternity leave and our work was graded by the head TF Ruy. He is honestly a way better grader and he has a kind soul. For the most part Daisy was a good professor but there were some moments where she kind of made snarky comments. She does honestly want her students to learn Spanish though. She always came to class with a smile on her face asking everyone how they were doing and she encouraged everyone to expose themselves to Spanish by traveling abroad. I recommend this class to improve your Spanish writing and speaking skills but as a native speaker, I sometimes felt that the work was just tedious work.
This class was great. I gained a lot of spanish skills and learned more about culture. the community was great and I learned a lot about the language
If you're considering this as your first Spanish class at Harvard, just take 11 instead. I'd really only recommend taking this class if you have to. Readings are dense, and you're expected to learn the grammar concepts outside of class and come back to class ready to use them. Spanish Dictionary saved my life and will save yours too. Though Google Translate sucks, it's great to use if you download the PDFs of the readings and upload them into Google Translate. It'll be in broken English, but it'll at least be enough for you to get the jist. Though I'd recommend seeing how much of the original Spanish reading you can grasp, if you don't come to class with at least a basic understanding of the text, you will be publicly humiliated.
This class is neither easy nor super difficult. There are an absolute ton of concepts to memorize and classes are three times a week. You will have a reading and often a quiz before each class, so be prepared to put several hours of work in at least three times a week outside of exam studying and essay writing.
Love this Spanish class!! If you are lucky enough to get Daisy, she is phenomenal
