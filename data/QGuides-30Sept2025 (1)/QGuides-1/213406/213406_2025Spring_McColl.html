Take this class! It is very informative in regards to water, hydrology, meteorology, the physics and chemistry of water, how humans have & continue to interact with water, climate change, and much more, and also comes with lots of fun facts. The assignments are all very fair and digestible, and help you apply concepts learned in class while also doing research on a topic you are interested in. That is my favorite part of the class ––– you get to do, for all 5 papers (all 2 pages in length besides the final water profile assignment, which is 4), research on a topic, event, or geographical region of your choice, as long as it fits into the generally wide–spanning prompts of the assignment.

Section is super useful too, and is a great review of what was covered in lecture. It is a really engaging environment that promotes collaboration and effective review, and is super helpful for people like me, who really struggle to pay attention in lectures. Also, the TFs are incredible. I personally had Val, and she was very engaging and very helpful when giving feedback on assignments.

It is not tough to do well in this class; just show up to lecture (attendance is taken), do the weekly discussion posts (which can be done in like 20 minutes if you really lock in), do the assignments, and go to section, and you will most likely do pretty well. The only major test is the midterm, and it is not too tough as long as you put in the time to study.
