**If you're looking for a fascinating introduction to water without a lot of pressure, take this course. Kaighin does an excellent job of presenting complicated topics in simple terms, while trying to involve the class in lecture. The coursework is not too intense and the weekly discussion posts take a lot of pressure off of the final project because they build toward that content. This is a great class to take.
amazing class, great teaching staff. very helpful, chill, and fun. learned a lot in a low–stress environment which is hard to find at harvard. 100% recommend. it's as hard as you make it and relevant to daily life.
It's a good gened, and I recommend engaging with the class. You can get by without paying attention to every detail; the exam is not too difficult, and you should be okay if you study the review and listen in section. Still, the material is pretty cool if you give it a chance, and the teaching staff are incredibly supportive. They are superstars.
Absolutely take this course! As someone who loves water, I loved this class. I wish Harvard offered more classes like this. This is really one of the only courses at Harvard that provides a completely beginner's friendly guide to water issues and the water cycle. If you love learning about environmental science and climate change, this course will be a manageable way to learn (mostly as a starting point). Professor McColl and the TFs were super knowledgeable and friendly, which was great to see!
I absolutely loved this course, and it is extremely well taught. The teaching staff is phenomenal and genuinely seem excited to work with us. I would recommend meeting with them or going to office hours, especially with the essays. The staff is extremely fair and understanding and the topic discussed is extremely interesting. I feel like I learned a lot and I would recommend this class to anyone interested.

This course is a great intro for prospective ESPP concentrators. Good way to get into the department without overwhelming yourself. Highly recommend, especially since it fulfills a GENED and ESPP requirement.
TAKE THIS CLASS! I love this class so much. It was super interesting and the material is not super hard to grasp. I really enjoyed all of the assignments and had a fun time with them. This class is pretty gemmy for most of the course. However, the assignments are clustered towards the end of the course so be cognizant of that before going in. Super chill lectures + section, and the teaching staff are all super super sweet. Just one midterm in this class, and no final except for a final paper that you work towards the entire semester for your weekly discussion posts (~100–200 words). Make sure to study for the midterm though as it is 25% of your grade and the questions can be a bit tricky.
Take this course! It's a great overview of the water cycle and work is very minimal (5 total assignments, 1 midterm). You can definitely learn a lot and you pretty much get what you put in. Lectures are great and Prof. McColl is a super great lecturer who organizes lectures in a way that is easy to follow.
This class was good to take as a somewhat easy GenEd/EPS entryway course, and the lectures were interesting. The work was not hard, but there were a lot of papers throughout the semester, especially for a GenEd. They were graded fairly and did not take me too long, however. If you have to do the Science GenEd requirement, I strongly recommend this class.

Would recommend!

Definitely take this class – it was a decent workload and caused me to think about how fundamental concepts can be applied to greater context and climate change.
Great class, both in content and administration! Enjoyed learning about the processes in the water cycle/weather. The essays were also nice because they have very reasonable page limits (i.e., I cut 1–2 a few lines on every essay to fit the page count) and are very straightforward – I pretty much wrote how I write for papers in science classes. Readings aren't essential (though good review for the midterm), and lecture slides + section are sufficient for making sure you know all the content.
