If you are interested in being pre–med, this class is for you. Even if you are not, you will still learn so much interesting content and take away a lot.
This class is simply amazing. Sabine is a wonderful instructor and cares deeply about the subject. She in extremely knowledgeable and you will learn a lot, simply from listening to her presentations. The readings are insightful and not too difficult, and there’s only one final essay that makes you look at the world differently after completion. **
If you get the opportunity to take this seminar, I absolutely would not pass it up. This class is only offered every other year, so if you have the chance to enroll, I would highly recommend it. Dr. Hildebrandt is one of the most knowledgeable people I have ever met, and if you're looking to get an in–depth understanding of the Holocaust from both a historical and a medical perspective, you will not be disappointed. One thing that pleasantly surprised me about the course was how much we explored the history of eugenics and racial ideology throughout the course. It was fascinating to hear how these ideas took root before, during and after the Nazi regime and how racial science intersected with and directed Nazi medicine. The weekly readings are manageable and legitimately interesting, and although the two hour class periods can feel long, if you stay engaged in class you'll walk out of every session with a ton of new knowledge and a truly changed historical outlook. Can't recommend this class enough!
TAKE THIS SEMINAR! Sabine is such a sweet person. She's very knowledgeable but also adds levity with her great sense of humor. The course content is very interesting — it covers a general history of the Holocaust with a specific highlight on medical ethics. It’s not medically/anatomically heavy in nature; instead, the content focuses more on ethical questions. The workload outside of class is very minimal and doable. It’s just a few readings and the occasional video. The final assignment is just a paper and a presentation on basically anything you want related to medical ethics, so it’s super easy to write about something you’re genuinely interested in! At the end of the year, Sabine even invited us to her house for her family’s annual holiday party, and it was totally a blast.
Professor Hildebrant loves what she teaches and loves to teach. The course is really nice because of the discussion aspect and being able to ask questions in a small group setting.
TAKE THIS CLASS! Low stress, professor is enthusiastic and great at teaching! She is from the Medical School. Class is once a week, and only homework is short readings and prep work throughout the semester as well as a final paper and presentation. The content is very interesting and discussions are engaging. Great for pre–meds or anyone with any kind of interest in history, would highly reccomend!
Anyone who wants to learn more about the holocaust should sign up for this seminar. The subject matter can be quite difficult at times because it involves the murder and abuse of the most vulnerable, but it also puts a spotlight on the few heroes who risked their careers and sometimes lives to do the right thing.
This seminar is a gem. Sabine is a wonderful professor who is very passionate about her work. Her findings are really interesting and she is very knowledgeable about medicine in Nazi Germany and the Holocaust. She's really nice and considerate. The readings for the class are very manageable, definitely more manageable than most seminars. There is only one final paper (2000 words) and a 5–minute presentation. The class can be boring at times, especially if you're not interested in history and have a short attention span (it's 2 hours), but I think that you'll learn a lot of valuable lessons and it will leave you with a broader worldview for sure. Sabine is a sweetheart. She invited us to her house for a holiday dinner and brought it candy after Halloween. She really cares about her students and the work that she does. I highly recommend taking this class, especially if you're taking 3 demanding STEM classes like me. I found my workload this semester to be pretty manageable and I'm glad I took this seminar. The people in the class came from all sorts of backgrounds, identities, lived–experiences, and interests, and learning from all has also been incredibly valuable. I enjoyed watching the final presentations, especially the one about a student's grandfather who was a Jewish surgeon who operated in Germany during WW2. Easy and interesting class, take it!
