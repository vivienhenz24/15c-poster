Comments
The lectures were just not very effective or helpful so you really have to do the readings and rely on sections if you want to get the most out of the course.
This class is pretty disorganized and I did not learn a ton from lecture. I am walking away with more knowledge in general, of course, about the Ottoman empire but I don't feel like I got the best of what I could.
The material covered is very interesting, the discussions are engaging, so it would be great for anyone who is interested in deepening their understanding of Ottoman history. The exposure to historical archives, primary sources etc is really eye opening. However, the lectures are very difficult to follow –– if you are someone who needs thematic organisation or temporal visualisation it would be challenging to follow along with what is covered in class. Sometimes the lectures meander a lot and is difficult to see the connections to the readings.
Take this course if you have any kind of interest in the Ottoman Empire and its territories. The lectures and readings are top–notchâ€”you're probably not going to get to all of the latter, so try to stick to the ones that seem the most important or interesting to you. The assignments and take–home tests are intellectually demanding but still quite manageable, and the lack of in–person exams takes a lot of stress out of the learning experience. You'll learn a lot about the Ottoman Empire as a whole, but you'll also be given a lot of room to focus on topics that you find especially interesting.
Professor Kafadar is incredibly knowledgeable and interesting. His lectures are very free–flowing and sometimes hard to grasp. Like any other class, if you do the readings before hand, and even read ahead if you can, then you will get so much more out of the lectures. I would encourage you to read as much as you can before lectures so that you can interact and steer the lecture to things that you found confusing in the readings.
