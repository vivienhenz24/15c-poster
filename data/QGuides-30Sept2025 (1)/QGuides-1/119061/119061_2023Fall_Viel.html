What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Your experience in this course largely depends on the project you choose, so choose wisely and familiarize yourself with the project leaders! I can only speak for my experience with Clemens, but I really enjoyed the course. We learned research methods, conducted a clear experiment, and had a lot of independent practice with procedures. A lot of time is spent outside of lecture, so be prepared to carve out times to work in the lab.
Ok, so I would say this is a good way to fulfill your research requirement for Neuro or any other biology concentrators. The workload is lighter than Neuro 91 (only 6 hours compared to 15) and they set you up with a PI who shows you the ropes, so that structure is really nice. We have three presentations during the semester and that’s the only work besides weekly lab work, which you either do during the 3–5pm meeting time or on your own time, depending on the lab you’re in. Also, you have a choice of three labs after they each give an elevator pitch type presentation on the first day of class and I think basically everyone gets their first choice. All groups regardless of which lab you choose work out of the learning lab in Northwest so transportation is not an issue.

My only qualm with this class is that I have no idea where I stand grade–wise. There is no syllabus to tell us if participation in lab work contributes to our grade, which is frustrating because half the people in my group never go to lab and collect data. Also, it doesn’t tell us what percentage of our grade the three presentations count for nor did we ever get graded feedback on any of the presentations. Finally, the final paper is a bit daunting because you write a grant proposal that is up to ten pages single spaced, yes I said single spaced. It’s nice that we write a partial draft and get feedback from our PI’s but I guess I wish there was a rubric? Or something that would help me know if I’m on track to get a good grade on it. I also don’t know what percent of our grade this final paper is worth. These are all issues that could easily be fixed and I would love to see them addressed in the next iteration of this course because this really is a great option for people that aren’t ready to take the leap into Neuro 91 :)

Would I recommend this course? Yes…as long as I end up with an A.
DO NOT TAKE THIS COURSE IF YOU ARE A FRESHMAN OR SOPHOMORE! It's a great course, but it is better fitted for individuals with some lab familiarity, and who have taken at least 1–2 upper–level science courses at the College already to be able to fully understand the project, be able to write the final paper, and understand what modulations you're in charge of in–lab.
You will learn new skills in an encouraging environment!
