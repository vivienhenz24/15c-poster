What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Amazing course for lovers of 20th century poetry. One of my favorite courses from throughout the whole of my grad school experience, honestly. We just have the luxury of moving slowly but rigorously through the corpus of a single poet, and her developing concerns about the meaning and responsibility of writing in a modern / postmodern world. Although the course load is not heavy, I think I felt more dedicated to this course than to any other, this semester. Professors take note! Assigning a billion pages of reading is NOT a good way to engage students. Going slowly and thoughtfully –– four poems per week –– is. This course takes that more rewarding, second route.
I am not a poetry person but I loved this class. very chill, only two essays, very thought–provoking, not a lot of outside work aside from the essays but still very interesting and engaging. 10/10
Take this class! I had no poetry experience but the environment of the class was so calm and it was great to slow down and engage deeply with the material. Prof. Ravinthiran is so kind and balances teaching with facilitating discussions really nicely.
