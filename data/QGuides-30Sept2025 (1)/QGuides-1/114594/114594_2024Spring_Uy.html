This class is an easy and fun way to learn about music history! I came into this class with significant past musical experience, but I definitely don't think that that's a prerequisite (it may just take a bit more effort to memorize the terms and composers if you have no familiarity). The workload is light and the class is such a gem overall. The only challenging part was probably memorizing a bunch of instrumental music for the listening quizzes.
***I loved Music 1! Professor Uy and Eloy were absolutely lovely, and I really enjoyed the many different points of view this class exposed me to.
great class — not too taxing and learned a lot! Michael and Eloy are great
The course was pretty comprehensive in that it covered multiple aspects of learning about music: history, performance, composers, musical analysis, etc. We were given plenty of time to finish each assignment and there was a clear goal for each assignment and lecture. The course was very well organized in terms of both overall structure and course materials on Canvas. Dr. Uy is very engaging and very well–spoken!
This was a great course. Dr. Uy was a very engaging and informative lecturer, and the content was really interesting. I think it can be a little redundant or easy if you have a lot of musical experience already, but there are also opportunities to challenge yourself and dive deeper into material if you want to, and the course highlights many aspects of the music world that not a lot of people know about. However, I would proceed with caution since Dr. Uy will very likely not be teaching this course in the future, and other professors may not be able to preserve everything that made this course so amazing.
Take this class if Dr. Uy is teaching it! Dr. Uy is a gem of a person and has honed his pedagogy over many years of teaching Music 1. He may no longer be teaching this course in the years ahead, so the course may change a bit depending on the instructor. Generally, Music 1 is a fun class to engage with music through time and allows you to reevaluate the role music plays in our everyday lives. The listening responses can be quite difficult if you have no music background (and even if you do have music background), especially in the first half of the semester when you need to differentiate between different classical works. However, the class is weighted such that most of the weight is on the assignments you complete outside of class, rather than in–class listening quizzes or midterms. There is no final and different opportunities for extra credit — a true gem.
