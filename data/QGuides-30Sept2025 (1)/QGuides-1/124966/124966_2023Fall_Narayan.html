What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
It is worth the time investment to really understand the homework problems.
Lots of very long problem sets in this course
This was an enjoyable course, and the material is important. The psets take some time but are reasonable.
Focus on enjoying the material as well as learning it. Finding ways to apply the topics being discussed in class to my research allowed me to engage in the course at a deeper level.
Pros:
Course material was comprehensive and in–depth, thoroughly explained a lot of different really important concepts in astrophysics at a very detailed level. Section was consistently very helpful, Misha even offered tips/hints on how to solve the homework problems (without giving up the answer, of course). Problem sets force you to understand the course material really well. They are really hard, but this is fair for a course covering this sort of material

Cons / stuff that this course needs to improve:
1. WAY more office hours. There was just one set of office hours per week, which I could never make because I had a class at that time. It would be really nice to have multiple sets of office hours, especially because the problem sets are so difficult. (Section is not really the same as OH, because section is more like a recap of lecture)

2. Slower paced lectures , or more pauses in lectures so we can ask questions – sometimes I would get confused on a concept 15 or 20 minutes into lecture, and if Prof Narayan just progresses straight to the next concept, I don't really get a chance to ask questions. Since later lecture material builds on earlier lecture material, I would remain confused for the later parts of the lecture.

3. Practice problems for the final exam. Since we didn't have practice problems, I went into the final exam not really knowing what to expect. I am particularly frustrated that there were no practice problems because Ay 200's was the only final exam I've ever taken where practice problems were not given to students.

4. Problem sets are extremely inconsistent in their difficulty. For instance, Problem Set 6 was very hard and took me a very long time to finish, while Problem Set 7 was extremely easy (to the point where it didn't really help me understand the material better) and took me only around 30 minutes to finish. It would be really nice to have just had two regular–difficulty problem sets.

I would rather off myself than take this class again. Just do something else. Hear me now or believe me later.
Hard but worth the difficulty for the topics learned
Super important class and material, will be relevant to any area of astronomy you might choose to work in.
