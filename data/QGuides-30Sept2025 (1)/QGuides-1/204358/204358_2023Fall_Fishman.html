Take this class! Definitely a gemmier SCRB class but it is still really interesting. If you're interested in the business side of things they do try and incorporate that because the class is largely full of MS/MBA students. It can feel intimidating at times but pay attention to presentations and show interest and you'll be fine. Pick a good group to do your presentations with.
A great class and one of the best I've taken here from a content perspective– Mark/Doug and all the guest speakers were truly amazing! it's really cool to hear from people at the top of their field across a range of various disease areas. Zaneta was a great TF and super helpful, although my experience with the other TF was less positive – his grading on papers was quite arbitrary/questionable and he wasn't that helpful when approached with questions. As an overall comment, it would be advisable if the teaching staff made sure each student's written assignment was graded by a different TF, so there is consistency across grading. Upon comparing with classmates, I found that the grading standards seemed to vary depending on the TF grading the paper. Overall, the Professors/lecture portion of the course was amazing, but there were a few administrative issues that are hopefully addressed in future versions of the course
This is a wonderful class, I would HIGHLY recommend.

I came into this class without a strong biology background and have really been inspired by this course. It is a wonderful introduction to this entirely new era of medicine. Each lecture, a new guest who has been at the forefront of a revolutionary therapeutic approach joined, offering a slew of perspectives and approaches. This is definitely a survey class, but a really effective one.

Mark and Doug were wonderful. Both incredibly smart and thoughtful, they really know their stuff and are so passionate about the material. They make a great duo.

Assignments were decent: two critical evaluations, and a few presentations. I liked the emphasis on critically evaluating published work.

This really has been a highlight of the semester, and every lecture was a pleasure. I would highly recommend SCRB 197.
