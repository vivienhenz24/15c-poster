Comments
This class is great. The topic is incredibly compelling and class discussions are interesting. There are minimal assignments and class meetings are once a week making this class a low time commitment (though there are quite a few readings each week). I would recommend this class to anyone interested in the topic or in Indigenous rights.
Take this course! Harvard and Native Lands was the best course I was in this semester and it wasn't close. Alan and Mandy are compassionate, brilliant, and thoughtful; the materials and activities we went through in class genuinely reconfigured the ways I thought about a lot of huge topics (land, power, sovereignty, property). The assignments are fun and interesting — you're writing op–eds, physically observing the neighborhood, producing case studies on real moments in Harvard's long history of Native dispossession — and they feel both more practical and more meaningful than the baseline for history classes. Can't recommend enough <3
This class is really interesting if you can find an area/group of people affected by Harvard that you're interested in studying. Definitely hard to try to get sufficient background on everything for your final paper, but the class really helps teach you how to be a historian in a way that's really relevant today. A LOT OF READING so useful to figure out how to skim secondary sources or get what you need out of them without reading the whole thing, which can be difficult.
This class is literally the best class I have taken at Harvard. The workload is very manageable most weeks, though the final project is fairly time consuming. The class engages in materials and places outside the classroom, has very necessary and valuable discussions about Harvard and the space we occupy, and the research and work done has a tangible impact on Harvard's reckoning with colonialism. Alan and Mandy were excellent and the class environment was great! This was the most engaging and reflective seminar I have attended.
