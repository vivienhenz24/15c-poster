What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
As someone who had zero contact with Jewish culture or the Hebrew language, I really appreciated how the course was taught in a way that represented the culture, history, and depth of the language. Not only did I learn about Hebrew specifically, I also learnt skills to acquire a new language as an adult and I feel that helps me in applying to other areas of learning too. Ran is also a great instructor who treats us as his own and takes care of us emotionally by providing a community. I enjoyed learning this year, and a big part of it is because I learnt a new language.
This course is a little all over the place. I thought the homeworks will too unforgiving and very unhelpful in my studies. We spend a lot of time doing things that never actually improved my Hebrew also.
The instructor for this class is a very cool person and discussions with him are definitely a highlight of the class. However, his teaching philosophy with assignments and amount of workload is something that I and a lot of the class disagreed with. If you take this class, just be prepared to devote a lot of time per week, nontrivially more so than other language classes. You will learn a ton of Hebrew within one year (again more so than other language classes), which is the point of the class, but the class has a hard time respecting the fact that you have other things to spend time on. The 9am is also rough. You might think that you will just deal with it, but thats what I did and simply having a 9am everyday made my year that much harder. Not a fault of the class itself, just a fact. However, I must say that if you do go into this class prepared for the things I listed above, the class itself is a good experience. There's a wonderful Hebrew table every Friday that's a lot of fun, and sections are great. There is an emphasis on learning music written in Hebrew, and the class is generally small enough that it generates a great group dynamic.
You will probably feel stressed along the way, but in the end, I think taking Hebrew for my foreign language requirement was a positive experience. Certainly, I learned a lot of Hebrew in just two semesters and feel pretty good with my command of the basics of the language, which is awesome. Keeping up with the homework is not easy, although this can probably be said about many foreign languages. Ultimately, I think the course fosters a good community and teaches well by the end, and I only wish that our assignments came with a little more flexibility.
