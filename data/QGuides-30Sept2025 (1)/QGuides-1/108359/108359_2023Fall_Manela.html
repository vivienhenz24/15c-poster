This is probably the hardest Gen–Ed class you will take at Harvard, so if you are looking for an easy Gen Ed requirement, please look elsewhere. There is a LOT of required reading in this class –– 80+ pages of reading a week on average–– and you should come to sections each week prepared to discuss them thoroughly. You'll also need to develop very nuanced views of history, as there are so many dynamics at play when observing just one of the US nation–building projects around the world. Lectures can also be kind of boring at times if you aren't super invested in the topic, so you should only take this class if you're passionate about the subject. 75% of your grade comes down to the two essays and the final exam, so make sure you do your best on those.
