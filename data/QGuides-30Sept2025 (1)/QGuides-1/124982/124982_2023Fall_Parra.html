Profesora Patricia Fontaneda Albarran was very nice and understanding, but expects you to learn quickly. You are expected to understand Spanish right off the bat, as she pretty much only speaks in Spanish after the first couple of weeks. While I came into the class with no prior knowledge of Spanish, most people in my class did (I would say 90%). That made it difficult at first but ultimately just means that you need to study more than your peers. The class was not time consuming and I do not regret picking Spanish to fulfill my language req.
You can always reach out to the language center, a very helpful resource in your spanish learning.
Please do not take this class if you have little to no experience in spanish. I would not recommend this course to anyone. I personally feel that it moves entirely too fast and what is expected is not realistic. BUT if you can properly communicate in Spanish already, this class should be easy.
I would tell future students that this is a great course for if you want to strengthen a particular aspect of your spanish.
I have a MOUTH FULL to say about this course, let me just tell you. Patricia has tried her best, and I try not to blame her, however, the structure of the course is really poor. For an INTRO to Spanish class, there is !A LOT! that is expected from you, and Patricia will spend little to know time reinforcing concepts that we've learned in the past, she just expects that you know them. Once again, for an introductory class, this is unbelievable. You would think there is more built–in support so that students can go over things they have learned again and again so that it solidifies in their brain. The whole idea of learning a language is repetition, repetition, and more repetition. There was little to none of that in this course; we would just breeze through topics week after the next. What's the rush? Just slow the course down.
The whole expectation is that YOU make time for office hours and go in and practice with her more... why? Everyone needs more help and practice, why can't you just build it into the class? On top of all of this, the quizzes and exams have been HEINOUS! There are no study–guides, no review, nothing. One time, during class, a student asked Patricia "so, what is the expectation for the exam? What material is good a good idea to review?" And Patricia just said to study everything in the past. Every. Single. Thing? Really? It was insane. The least she could've done is curated a list of verbs that we should know (as they were scattered EVERYWHERE in Canvas rather than in one concise place), and small topics that we should review (for example, telling us that we should know our colors, or we should know how to describe someones appearance, or we should know how to talk about the past). Honestly, this course has been INSANE and one of the worst I've taken in my time here as a student at Harvard. I am just so disappointed and I really hope that the structure takes a huge hit and changes.
This is a good intro, but be careful who you get as a professor. Some grade much harshly in comparison to others which makes it much for difficult if you haven't already taken spanish before.
