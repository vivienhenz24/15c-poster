One of the best history classes I've taken at Harvard. Changed the way I think about different institutions, and Ian is an awesome and engaging teacher. Couldn't recommend more.
Awesome seminar course. Dr. Kumekawa makes this course accessible, engaging, and enriching. The course tackles big questions about important topics, but Dr. Kumekawa provides incredibly useful reading recommendations, discussion prompts, and useful feedback both inside and outside of class. It is truly the professor that makes this class such an excellent one. Dr. Kumekawa provides guidance on how to approach the readings and thoughtful feedback on assignments. Office hours are productive and accessible, so make use of those when working through the course. Highly recommend for a history seminar because it covers different areas of the world and time periods, which is pretty special.
Such an incredible class!! Dr. Kumekawa is so incredible and clearly so passionate about the subject matter.
Most history seminars are really good quality, but this class especially has been outstanding. I felt like I learnt alot from my classmate's insights, and from the facilitation of discussion by Professor Kumekawa. There was a good pace and structure to help us navigate the course, and Professor Kumekawa is really accessible. It also gave me the freedom and space to explore an aspect of the strange amorphous concept of "offshore" –– the assignments were scaffolded well to facilitate that exploration. It also changed the way I approached history; the global nature of the categorical approach to "offshore" helped me realise the intersections of history rather than to see them as silos. All in all, a great history class for both someone deeply interested in the area, and for someone who would like to just get a taster of what studying history is like.
Please please please take this course; it is life–changing and you will not regret it. Everyone, regardless of concentration, should take it as you learn so much, you are exposed to such a wealth of material, and the professor is one you should definitely study under before you graduate. I cannot stress this enough: take this course! I hear that it might be taught in a lecture format over a seminar format in the future but that doesn't change how intensely I wish you to take this course and how fascinating the subject matter is and how amazing the professor is.
Great class!! And wonderful way to be introduced into the history department. Interesting content and manageable workload. Ian is the AWESOME!!
