What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Take this class! It's incredible and so special to spend your time drawing at Harvard! To beware that most students have at least some (and often significant) visual art experience so it's not truly for beginners; the assignments can also be extremely intense and take a lot of time. With that said, I found such a close class community and really learned a lot about drawing and seeing.
This class is great for anyone who wants to do a secondary in art or more
I would advise prospective students that this course is enjoyable and an excellent choice for those seeking an easy elective.
If you take this class be willing to learn a lot about yourself when it comes to drawing. You don't have to be an amazing, perfect drawer. You just have to be willing to try and learn. You will figure out what you like working with and what you don't as well as what you may want to try in the future. The teacher and TF are the sweetest people ever and have a genuine interest in what you draw as well as how you progress in the class. They are both kind and understanding, but make sure you still put in a lot of work even if for some reason one night you couldn't get an assignment done. But yea overall highly highly recommend this course, especially for those trying to fall back in love with drawing!!
Take it! It's so much fun to have art in your life.
I loved this class. I had some experience with drawing already but the class really was accessible for true beginners as well. Assignments varied a lot, and exposed us to a lot of possible techniques and styles, which was really interesting. I found myself gaining a lot of confidence in my drawing abilities throughout this class, and believe I learned a lot through the experience.
This is a good course to learn more about and develop drawing skills. The 165–minute studio can be difficult to endure, but once you get used to it, it can allow you to better focus on the drawing process. You also gain exposure to the drawings of your classmates and drawings within museums and archives.
