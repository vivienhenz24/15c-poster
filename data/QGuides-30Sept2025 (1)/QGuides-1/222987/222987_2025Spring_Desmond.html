This course is a good option for fulfilling a divisional distribution requirement, but be warnedexpectations for the assignments are kind of opaque. If I could redo the course, I would ask a ton of questions before turning things in, because without any clear rubrics I got penalized for things I didn't even know were being evaluated.
Laptop–free class, two–hour seminars once a week. Presentations can seem to drag on at times, but I found that the most constructive learning comes from engaging in discussion and asking questions. Professor Desmond is very knowledgeable about the subject matter.
This course was very interesting, especially if you care about evolution. Prof. Desmond is a great lecturer and made the course very entertaining! As a psych concentrator on the life sciences track, I felt like this was a great way to fulfill my concentration's life science course requirement!
One of the best classes I've taken at Harvard. Professor Abby was so knowledgeable about each topic and was willing to take any questions that thought deeply about course material. Wonderful class –– I came in each Wednesday excited to learn about the topic of that day.
I would highly recommend this course! Dr. Desmond and Jack are both amazing and they truly do care for their students. I learned a lot about evolution and the work never felt tedious. The deadlines do come up quickly, so pay attention and make sure to at least skim the readings before class, you will get so much more out of lecture! Overall, an amazing class and a perfect way to end senior year!
If you've ever pondered the evolutionary history of some aspect of human cognition, this course is perfect! The teaching staff are so knowledgable and approachable, and genuinely supportive whether this is your first time engaging in a large–scale research project or you're well–versed in the endeavor. Lecture component of the course was also great.
This course is a little boring, but the moments when it shines it really shines. There is really cool stuff to learn here, the course just moves slowly to be honest. But the writing you'll do is really meaningful... come up with something you're interested in and the research will be a lot of fun.
Take this class if you want an easy A, but don't expect to get much out of it other than that.
This course is great if you are a curious person and genuinely interested in human evolution, psychology, sociology/anthropology, etc. The lectures are interesting but long, and the assignments are infrequent and pretty straightforward but worth a lot of your grade.
I would recommend taking this if you have a cognitive trait or two that you are interested in studying and have some background to build off of! The written assignments definitely benefit from having some background or personal interest. For example, i wrote about a cognitive trait that is realted to somethign i study from a basic science perspective in my thesis,
The assignments build on each other so the final project / research paper is pretty straightforward. However, it is not always clear what they want to see which is reflected in the grading scheme. Half the lectures felt repetitive if coming from a bio background because its so focused on basic evolution. The other half were incredibly engaging.
This class completely reframed how I thought about human evolution and the traits that make us human. There's one final paper that's the result of several scaffolded assignments that you get a lot of detailed feedback on, which is great for a busy schedule. Professor Desmond is an incredible lecturer and I learned so much from her!
