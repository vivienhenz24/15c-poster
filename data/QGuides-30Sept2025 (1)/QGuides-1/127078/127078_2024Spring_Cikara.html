I wish this was not a required course for the Psychology concentration. Although some concepts are useful to learn, it felt like a repetition of PSY1900 and PSY971, and I do not feel like I learned anything incredibly useful or different. The fact that almost your entire grade is based on a group project (a group you form at the beginning of the semester also) did not feel very fair.
Flipped classroom format, so make sure to take notes/pay attention to the videos you watch on your own time. Really think of a topic that interests you for the research project because you will be working on it all semester and reading literature relevant to that topic; it will make the project a lot more fun if you are into the research.
This class is required and can be dry at times but as long as you pay attention you will be good.
This class is great! The flipped classroom format is actually extremely useful and as long as you watch the lectures attentively every week, do the readings and take thorough notes, you can do well in the midterm without having to study too much. The professor is extremely nice and accessible and really wants all her students to do well. Sections are helpful because they are structured so you work on the final project progressively so by the end of the semester there isnt a ton left to do, making it less stressful. Would 100% recommend, I learned so so much!
This course is really back heavy and hits you out of nowhere. That being said, it definitely was rewarding and the overall project is pretty cool. Make sure to pick your partners wisely! The exam is also pretty difficult, and if you don't have a good grasp of stats, then it require more work.
Professor Cikaras lectures are helpful in understanding how psychological research experiments are created, conducted, and analyzed. Her style of teaching creates a warm environment that encourages students to engage in class discussions. The research assignments throughout the semester lead up to writing the final research paper. For the assignments, there is a midterm exam, research assignments, and final research paper.
Fine class checks off the box
for the psych concentrators who have to take this class, choose a GOOD group!! I luckily chose people (ahead of time) who knew how to code in R since I knew that was a struggle. The class is a bit boring since we learn everything on Canvas (we watch videos) before we attend class so it felt like class time was just a waste. Just be warned that although you collaborate with your group to design and conduct the experiment, you have to write the 12–15 page paper ON YOUR OWN, which I wish was collaborative instead.
I really liked this course and professor Chikara is amazing! The layout is great and the group project is fun! I also learned a lot
