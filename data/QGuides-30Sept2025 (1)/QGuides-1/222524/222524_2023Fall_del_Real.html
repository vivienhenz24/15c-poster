What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This course is an amazing introduction to both architecture and the Boston built environment. Professor del Real always chooses interesting pieces of architecture to examine, and shapes class discussions to be very thought–provoking.
This course helped me see parts of Boston I wouldn't have seen otherwise and gave me a good architectural eye. I really appreciate learning about Prof. Del Real and his understanding of the different buildings we visited. I'd definitely like to see more buildings in the future like The Pru and Encore Boston Harbor.
This is a seminar worth taking into consideration if you are interested in architecture. I had a lot of fun with the site visits, as well as with talking to my classmates and Professor del Real. The class discussions may be a bit lengthy at times, but overall I think it's a good refresher to balance out other classes that you may have in a semster.
Even if you have never studied architecture before but are slightly interested in it, take this class. Professor del Real has a wealth of knowledge about architecture and is also very fun and easy to talk to. The site visits are great, it's awesome that Harvard pays for us to go out and see the city of Boston. The readings can be kind of long, but honestly if you kind of skim them you'll still be okay during class discussions. The assignments are manageable and Professor del Real is understanding. However, when there is no site visit and you're in the classroom, it can be kind of brutal. It's a long time to be in a lecture, even with a little break in the middle. It's hard to stay focused and I often ended up zoning out our doing work for other classes toward the end of a lecture.
This course is a great way to dive into architecture without a large time commitment! However, most of the field trips are very long (from leaving campus to returning), but worth it because it was such a great way to explore Boston.
The site tours are amazing! Super fun and force you to get out of Cambridge. Prof del Real is super kind and helps to build a relaxed classroom environment. Despite this, be warned that on weeks when you're not touring buildings, the 2hr 45min seminars can get extremely boring, as Prof del Real didn't structure them very well/at all. Still, the class is overall interesting –– I came in with no interest in architecture, and I've left with a newfound appreciation for the field. Assignments are very fair and doable. (4)
This class is definitely good. It can get a little long and a little boring though. You definitely get out what you put in. Overall it is a good course and has a good balance of work.
