At Harvard, there are some classes that promise interesting material but fail to deliver. This class is the epitome of those classes. You would learn more, have a more structured learning experience, and all–in–all have a better time if you simply sat down with your friends, perused these readings, and had a thoughtful discussion about them – Professor Jacobs provides no instructive help, and in some cases is an active hindrance. If you must take this class, do so with friends who are interested in the subject matter, and take it pass/fail – assignments are guesswork at best considering Professor Jacobs' inability both to describe what he wants or to provide any coherent feedback. It's a shame, because he clearly also has a passion for the material – but passion isn't enough when you show up each week with a half–written legal–pad page's worth of notes and some half–remembered slides.
Interesting course material but lectures are not great. Professor is super great and interesting and personable but he does not use slides effectively at all and can go on long tangents that are not easy to follow in terms of relation to course material. At times lectures can be VERY confusing but overall it's an interesting and fun course
The lectures are very hard to follow, quite theoretical, and unfocused. That said, Jacobs is a very kind instructor and cares a lot about the class and his students, which makes the course very manageable. The topic itself is also very interesting. Section was FANTASTIC. Would recommend to anyone looking for an interesting class, but you definitely get out of it what you put in.
This course is one of the most poorly ran classes I have ever taken at Harvard. There was little to no structure every class and very poor preparation and expectations for the midterm and final. Section however was one of my absolute favorites and I loved Gayane she was so incredibly supportive. If I was you and taking this class I would do it pass fail and take it with friends because that is the only way to get through this course despite how cool the course sounds.
I thought this course was very interesting. You cover a large breadth of topics which made it engaging. I wish the lectures and material were a bit more structure –– I had a hard time organizing the ideas that we were learning. I also enjoyed the oral exam for the midterm but when I chose the class, there was an option to do an oral exam for the final but this ended up not being the case. I didn't find this overly disruptive but it would have been nice not to have a syllabus change in the middle of the semester after the add/drop deadline if exam format has any impact on individuals' decisions to enroll in the course.
This class is not very focused, and did not really get to what I expected it would. The relevance of many of the readings and a few of lectures was unclear. Guest lectures were not very valuable. Somehow the course covered too much and not enough material. Started off really strong, but fell off after the Klan lectures. Lecture was hard to follow, and I didn't really come away with any valuable information besides a few isolated incidents.
Sometimes the course content is fairly depressing, but it's a really interesting class.
