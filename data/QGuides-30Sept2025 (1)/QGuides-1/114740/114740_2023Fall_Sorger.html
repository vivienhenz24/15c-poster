What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This course is great for anyone interested in biotech/pharma! I would say its very introductory, so if you already have industry experience or have taken a class like CHEM 110, then the material may seem a bit repetitive. Of course, this class also talks about biotech/pharma from a regulatory/economic perspective too, so that is unique. Overall, super great class! *****
Minimal commitment (1 lecture a week with minimal work outside of class). Essentially two assignments: a midterm presentation and a final paper + presentation. The latter can be a lot of work and depends a lot on the quality of partners that you get. But with planning ahead its definitely manageable. The guest lectures are fairly engaging for the most part, with some special gems. Some were hard to sit through, including Stan's lectures, but Peter Sorger is a great lecturer. The class tends to focus a lot on the business/economic/regulatory side, and less so on the science/biochemistry side. It is a class designed for people with minimal scientific experience. I can imagine the ideal student for this class would be a business school student looking to go into biotech investment/consulting or something who wants to get a good background of the field.
This course provides multidisciplinary insights into the pharmaceutical industry!
