The classroom is such a welcoming environment! The professor does such a good job of fostering a wholesome environment for all levels of learners, from heritage speakers, people who have Vietnamese parents that don't speak Vietnamese as often, to those completely new to the language.

– If you are a heritage speaker (i.e., you speak Vietnamese with your parents/family often or growing up) definitely take this class to begin fulfilling your language requirement. Once you learn the alphabet and tone marks by matching it up with the words you already know, you can quite honestly breeze through this class. Honestly, while we covered around 15 grammar structures and 200+ new words, I probably actually only properly learned maybe 1 structure and 10 new words because I already knew the previous ones (though you will certainly learn how to spell all of those new words too, which would be good!)

– If you are a non–heritage speaker, be mindful that Vietnamese is very much a tonal language, so be ready to spend a little bit more time and potentially struggle more than your peers in spelling, reading, and speaking. However, the professor is extremely kind and doesn't care about your level. She will love you regardless.
