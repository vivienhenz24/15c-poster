Take this class if you can! Andrew is so wonderful as a person and as a professor, and there is a lot of flexibility to choose the topic you want to research. Despite being pretty independent, there was every opportunity to ask questions and get help from Andrew. Learning consists of straight science, but also tons of data collection and research skills that I never expected to learn in undergrad. If you plan to write a thesis, this is a perfect class to get started. Designing the experimental protocol and collecting pilot data and presenting it was awesome.
The course was just not what it was advertised to be. I signed up for this class thinking that I would be working on an actual research project in the lab, but we ended up all designing our own smaller experiments that didn't teach us that much. Some people only had one person participate in their study. It all felt like a big trick that we couldn't take much away from since the study data was so limited and not rigorous. It was an easy class, but I enrolled so I could learn something, and there is no way we can learn anything of value from the kind of experiments we did.
If you're an HEB concentrator looking to learn more about building a proposal and research project, you should definitely enroll in this course! Dr. Yegian is a great mentor and you will learn so much throughout the semester!
Expect to design your own research experiment and draft a research proposal throughout the semester. Andrew pretty much walks your through how to write a background section, and all the other sections of a research proposal. After spring break you get to collect some pilot data and then present to the class your project. Super good gateway class if you're interested in pursuing biomechanic research.
