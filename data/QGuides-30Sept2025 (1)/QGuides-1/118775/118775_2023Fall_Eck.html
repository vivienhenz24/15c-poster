This is one of the most rewarding GENEDs you could possibly take. If Harvard continues this course after next year (unfortunately, Professor Eck is retiring), I strongly recommend that you take this class. The week–by–week courseload isn't necessarily insane, but the midterm paper (5 ish pages) and the final project (15–20 pages) will definitely make up for it. For me, this class *defined* the transformative experience that Harvard described in our acceptance letters; it changed my life, my understanding of the world, and especially my opinions on religion. Even though I wasn't interested in religion before taking this course, I am really glad that I took it.

I do have a few recommendations for prospective students, though:
1) Take expos before you take this course. This class is writing–intensive (you will write as many pages of essays in this class as you would in expos, and the papers are cumbersome), and you may find yourself a bit lost at some point, especially for the final paper (15–20 page research paper or case study).
2) Get started on the final project EARLY. Having a draft done by the beginning of reading period will make a world of a difference for you, and your final submission will be a lot stronger.
3) Participate in lecture. The plenary discussions are really interesting and informative, but you will not get any of that if you're sitting on your laptop the whole time.

With the final project, this course really isn't for the faint–of–heart, even though the average courseload was super reasonable. But you will get a lot more from this class than you would from a gem.
