Paul also puts a big emphasis on class community, and my class was quite close–knit; I think it's no accident either. This is meant to be a place where you can foster connection and trust the people you're showing your writing to, which is also very important.

TAKE. THIS. COURSE. I know you read that under every Q guide for a class that isn't awful, but I mean it so so genuinely –– this is definitely one of the most impactful experiences I've had at Harvard. I love reading/literature, and I had absolutely zero experience with creative writing before this course, but you don't need any. There was a decent mix of STEM/humanities people in the class, so don't think that you can't take it because you're an outsider to the English department or whatever. Paul is an incredible writer and thoughtful teacher, and you get to read these amazing and genuinely fascinating stories that are somehow written by your peers??? It's the kind of course where you can put in the amount of effort that you want to, but putting in more work/time will obviously make you get the most out of the class. All you have to do is read two of your classmates stories every week (which is such a nice change of pace if you're used to doing psets every night) and write a peer critique letter to them, and then write your story for your week. And you leave the semester with a completed short story of your very own, which is a cool achievement! TAKE A WRITING WORKSHOP WHILE YOU HAVE THE CHANCE –– you won't regret it!
