I would recommend this course enthusiastically, which is not something I say about many of my courses! The readings are interesting and if you have an interest even tangentially in the subject matter, you can work it into this course. For example, I began the course thinking I wanted to explore conservative legal evolution, and ended the course exploring how country music is a cultural window into conservative backlash. The Professor is clearly invested in the subject matter and provides clear mechanisms to achieve success in the course. The class discussions are easy to follow and explore truly interesting readings! Some of them were so interesting that I kept reading books (like Jefferson Cowies Stayin Alive and Getting Right with Reagan). In the coming election year, this would be an especially interesting and pertinent course to take!
TAKE THIS CLASS
Absolutely take this class! One of the best ones in the history department. Professor Bekemeyer is a fantastic professor, super friendly, accessible, helpful, knowledgeable, and great facilitator of discussions. Final paper is 15–18 pages, so a bit long but nothing too crazy if you've been diligent over the semester in doing the lead–up assignments. The course is designed for you to build up to the paper, which is nice. Readings are super interesting, class discussions are fun, the research you do is pretty engaging and generally relevant in helping explain why the U.S. looks the way it does.
This is a great seminar option. I'm not conservative, but I found the topic super engaging and interesting. Aaron is incredible and super kind. The readings are slightly long but very interesting and the workload is manageable if you start on the paper early. Discussion was great!
Very good course! Bekemeyer is a good teacher and the class is very interesting.
The course gives a very organized and comprehensive overview of the history of the conservative movement from about the 1950s to the present. Professor Bekemeyer is kind, extremely knowledgable, and passionate about the material. The weekly readings are all interesting and feel purposeful. The assignment structure is helpful, with each assignment building towards the final research paper. Some weeks there are a lot of readings but it's pretty standard for a history seminar. Overall, the workload is manageable if you do not procrastinate too much. If you start early enough on the final paper it won't be too stressful, but will be a grind if you wait too long.
Great way to get your seminar requirement done
