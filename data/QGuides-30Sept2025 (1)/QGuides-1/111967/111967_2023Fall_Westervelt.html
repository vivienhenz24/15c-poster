What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
See who the speakers will be and take this course if you want to find out about the different fields of research done within the physics department.
I took this class in order to figure out whether I was interested in academia and research or not. This class was helpful as a low–workload way to do that. I saw that I find the research interesting, but the structure of academia is not appealing to me, and so if I go to grad school it would be a couple years after graduating college and not immediately.

The big assignments are a presentation and a 10–page paper. I found the paper difficult because for some reason I chose a topic I am not terribly interested in, but I see that if you choose a topic you care about it could be a very helpful and enjoyable assignment. The presentation was difficult, however, because they are on topics you probably won't have sufficient background on to explain in depth. Professor Westervelt will help with the presentation, but that also makes it a bit more confusing. For example, he helped me prepare my presentation, and then when I did the example we discussed in front of the class, he said to use a different one and I got rather confused. He also takes over talking for most of the presentation since the point is for everyone to learn about the topic and he will almost surely know more about it than you, so don't be alarmed or worried about your grade if he just starts talking for 10 minutes in the middle of your presentation; the presentation is not formal like that.
