Great class. If you're AM, it's a no brainer. Weekly problem sets, no exams, chill final project and group presentations. Good balance for the rest of the AM classes. Attendance is required MW and the class is at 9am which was annoying, but overall the class was so worth it.
A solid AM concentrator course!! If you're an AM concentrator and you're looking for a class where you can learn without being overworked and stressed out with incredibly hard PSETs and exams, this class is for you. Each week there's a PSET/lab, which is can be time–consuming but the problems themselves aren't terribly hard IF you have experience with python.

This class says no coding experience is required, but I think they aren't revealing how dependent the work in the class is with coding. I was appreciative of my experience with Python (cs32). Not that you'll fail without it, but you'd likely need to go to office hours, the optional Friday sections, and consult online sources, so this class might not be as stress–free then.

But the class meets 2x a week, and attendance is only mandatory everytime for the presentation and lab days, and some random time when he takes attendance during lecture days. However, after the first month of class, you truly don't know when attendance will be taken (even during "mandatory" days). I skipped a lecture class one day to study for an exam, and he happened to take attendance that day, so my grade when down. This was late March, and it was the last time he took attendance. So in total he took attendance 10x for the semester, and even though I didn't miss a class after that, my attendance grade was still a 90. Not to say he was wrong for that, but just to say, be careful because attendance it isn't very routine.

As far as the material, this class was a cool intro to AM, but I wish the classes were more rooted in the code since the PSETs and Lab were so reliant on it. The small taste of the conceptual parts of different AM topics were hard to follow at times. But that's no reason not to take this class! You totally should! You'll likely get a good grade and learn some cool applications, so I def recommend for AM concentrators!
