This is an amazing class to take. This was Professor Franklin's first semester teaching a freshman seminar, but I think she did a fantastic job. If you have any interest in cancer immunology, this class will teach you many of the different aspects of the immune system as well as immunotherapies for cancer. I originally joined the class due to my interest in stem cell and regenerative biology, the department Professor Franklin is within. Despite not having much of a background in cancer or immunology specifically, I felt the readings and lectures were very easy to understand. Plus, I made a lot of great friends in the class with similar interests! I would 10/10 recommend this class.

Professor Franklin is absolutely lovely and so kind (she would always bring in snacks for our seminar)! She is a wealth of knowledge, very engaging, and so, so willing to answer questions. She does a fantastic job at making pretty difficult topics on cancer and immunology (in this course we first explore the basics of cancer, then immunology, and finally tie the two together in our discussion immunotherapy) while still making the material accessible and understandable (for reference I had never taken a course on or done research in the field of cancer or immunology).

There were weekly reading and question assignments that were very manageable and one end of year final project. The project allowed students to delve deeper into an immunotherapy and was not too difficult or demanding.

The course typically struck a good balance between lecture and discussions, but sometimes, especially at the beginning of the semester, the questions (the amount and on the depth of material) could get out of hand, leaving some students confused and causing others to disengage from the class.
