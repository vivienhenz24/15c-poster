If you could at all picture yourself becoming a birder, take this class! Yes, it's a lot of memorization and the exam content can be overwhelming at times but, as a result, you get so much out of the class! Memorizing all the taxonomy sucked but I think I see the world differently as a result. The many field trips are also an amazing way to get outside and take time to more consciously appreciate the natural world. One of my favorite classes I've ever taken but be prepared to really commit yourself to it!
Just take it! This class is definitely difficult at times but overall the best experience! The field trips (especially spring break) are amazing and you will learn so much!
Take this class! This is one of the few classes at Harvard, where I have gained lasting connections to my classmates and teachers that will go beyond the coursework. The field trip is a huge contributor to this bonding. Birding is something I will take with me from this class to the rest of my life.
Absolutely take this class. Dont miss it.
This class is really fun if you take it pass/fail! I don't know how letter graded folks felt, but I genuinely enjoyed every second of it while taking it pass/fail. The class was a little unorganized and it was unclear what you should know from the lecture material, so it was pretty hard on everyone to study. However, being one of the pass/fail students allowed me to just sit back and learn about birds and birding without worrying about which details I needed to know to do well in the course. A banger class to end my four–year college journey, that's for sure!
This class is INCREDIBLE. You learn so much in the field (go on the Spring Break trip), but also the lab and lecture material will give you immense knowledge of local avifauna and beyond.
This is a beautifully chaotic class that WILL make you love birds both scientifically and aesthetically, and was taught by an amazing and incredibly friendly professor and TF. It is true that the course is often not the most organized and it may feel a bit nonlinear and improvised at times, but that's just part of the fun – not too unlike birding, apparently.
If you have any interest in birds, take this class! This class has made me a birder, and has given me a hobby for life. If you take the class trip, you also might make a lot of great friends, which I did. I feel like I really know a lot about birds in every aspect, and every day walk through life seeing birds everywhere I go –– and now I can name and describe them all.
This class is a lot of fun, whether you like birds or not. Your friends might find you a little bit crazy because of how bird–obsessed you become (or how much more you will be). The field trips are a lot of fun and you get to know your classmates a bit better than your normal class. However, the exams cover an impossibly large amount of material and are worth most of your grade, so make sure you have amazing memorization skills or great studying habits. The three hour lab sections can also get tiring. Be prepared to be frustrated at how much material you will be required to memorize, especially if you don't have any background in ornithology. Additionally, the first half of the class isn't even about birds (it's about dinosaurs), which could be annoying if you wanted to focus on modern birds for the whole class. The teaching team (Scott and Jonathan) are great and very passionate about what they do, and it is a pleasure to be in class or go on field trips with them. Overall, a fun class but a lot more work than you may be expecting.
This class has been my favorite OEB class at Harvard. Both Professor Edwards and Jonathan are amazing teachers and the most approachable people that I have had teaching a class at Harvard. The tests can be a bit hard, you will have to study for them, but the material really is great and there is a lot of help in this class. If you are interested in birds or just need an upper level OEB this is a great class to take.
