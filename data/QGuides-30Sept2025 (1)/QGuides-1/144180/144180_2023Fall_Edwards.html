I took this class with none of the prerequisites, and it was very challenging but very rewarding. Scott and the TFs are truly there to help you and are wonderful people. I struggled especially during the first half of the course without a strong background in bio, but I learned quickly
It's a difficult class simply because of the state of the field, but it's so worth putting the work in because you get a lot out of it (even though I still feel like I've barely scratched the surface)
This is generally a great class, and I would recommend it to any students with a serious interest in evolutionary biology who want to explore the topic in much greater depth than is possible in OEB 53. Scott is a great lecturer, and Kelsie and Jerry were excellent TFs as well. The textbooks seem like a lot to acquire and read, but they are not really necessary for the class as long as you review the lecture materials. You get an in–depth survey of population genetics, phylogenetics, and coalescent theory, with an introduction to many of the state–of–the–art computational tools (some not even a few years old yet) used by researchers today to analyze evolution in the wild. The course can feel quite chaotic at times (especially with assignments), but ultimately, it's worth it, and the course staff really cares about everyone in the class.
This is a hard class, but I recommend taking it if you see yourself doing research (like in graduate school) in the future. I definitely took away very useful skills that I will certainly be using in the future!
This course is highly disorganized. Scott is enthusiastic about the subject, but doesn't know how to follow the syllabus and create a realistic timeline for assignments and projects. The TFs have absolutely no idea what they're doing, and half the time they respond to questions with "I have no clue."
Only take this course if you have some coding experience, a really good understanding of genetics, and insane note–taking skills. This class is interesting but it can be very stress–inducing, even though the professor is the sweetest and the content is interesting. It just gets really hard to follow really quickly and I hope that they record the lectures in the future because it is so difficult to prep for exams without them. The last exam and assignment were back to back and the last assignment was extremely difficult and stressful, especially if you are an undergraduate without that much coding experience and if you didn't understand parts of the class super well. I would recommend it if you want to do genetics research.
Don't fret too much about the small details in molecular evolution (unless this is really exciting to you!). This course is wonderful for providing examples of all of the lines of inquiry available to people interested in molecular evolution. There is definitely something for everyone.
I would recommend this course to anyone interested in learning more about the analysis of genomes from a biological perspective.
This class is so interesting and, if you are coming in with some basic evolution and genetics, will change your perspective on genomes and evolution. The course staff are so helpful and willing to answer questions, and the class is usually pretty small so it's a good opportunity to form relationships with your peers. Make sure that you are thorough in your coverage of lecture content when studying for exams – sometimes, concepts that were mentioned briefly will come up.
