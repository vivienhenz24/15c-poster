This was my favorite course I've ever taken at any institution. Please take it if you have ANY interest in machine learning, software deployment methods, or any of those tools you see on industry job descriptions (like Docker and Kubernetes). I will describe some specifics of why this course was so good:

– The project specification was extremely clear. For a course with a semester–long project, this is critical. Pavlos, Shivas, and the team put a lot of effort into designing a reasonable project roadmap and thinking through all the steps and deliverables. We were never at a loss for what we needed to be working on for any given assignment. Also, the project deliverables were evenly spaced throughout the semester, and the project was designed such that students can use it for their professional portfolio.

– The in–class lectures and demos showed a TON of hard work and preparation on the part of the teaching staff. I found the demos (and associated Github repos) to be incredibly valuable teaching tools. Pavlos always made sure the demo would work before class, sometimes testing it multiple times.

– The content of the course is SUPER relevant to essentially any career or project in machine learning, and many other areas outside machine learning. I have looked for a course for years that would teach me Docker, Kubernetes, cloud platforms, Weights and Biases, etc. Many courses assume you know this material, and MANY companies require skills in these areas, but no other course actually teaches it. Pavlos saw that gap and created the perfect course to fill it.

– The strategy of assigning each group to one TF was very effective, because it allowed each group to work with their TF to explain the unique features of their project, and not suffer from inconsistencies in grading between TFs.

In summary, don't hesitate to take this course! Find a good group and establish good communication. I recommend a smaller group otherwise it can be hard to coordinate with everyone. Choose a relatively simple project with a less complicated model–– you will still have a very impressive project by the end of the semester.
