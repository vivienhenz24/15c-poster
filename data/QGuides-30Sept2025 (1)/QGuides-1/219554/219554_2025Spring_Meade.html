This class is a great, approachable Python introduction. Brendan is a great professor as well.
This course is super beginner–friendly, and I like that it was application–driven, so all of the problems that are focused on are highly relevant and are similar to scientific problems and tasks you would probably encounter in the future. That being said, some of the content does get kind of dry –– the lectures do a good job of covering the basics, but a lot of the material could be compacted together, as the lectures/sections usually just consisted of reading through code to different graphs and problems. However, there are in–class exercises and some hands–on work, which were helpful for practicing some of the concepts that are talked about in class, and the teaching staff are so kind and open to questions and helping students out.

One thing, though, is that this class also focuses more on learning how to code without as much insight into the logical problem–solving that comes with breaking down a problem into its different steps, so that can be a bit of a challenge. However, SCI–5 is quite manageable in terms of workload, the homework assignments are quite interesting and can be challenging, but they don't eat up a ton of time to do. I would probably recommend this course to someone over courses like CS 50 for people who want to learn a bit of Python and want to see how to apply it for scientific research, but if you want a more comprehensive and rigorous overview of Python, I would recommend looking into another course.
This course was the intro to python that I've been looking for! It is so much more accessible than CS50, and really teaches you how to code and manipulate data step–by–step. I think it is particularly useful for those who want to do research and need to work with large datasets in python. 100% recommend, generally a chill course, but very informative and helped me actually get started in programming.
Science 5 is a great course for your qrd requirement if you don't have a background in coding! The instructors and TFs are genuinely so nice, engaged, and want you to succeed. I do wish lectures had involved more in–class exercises, since it's hard to learn how to code from a PowerPoint presentation/lecture. All in all, I was really afraid of my qrd requirement but grateful that this class made it as painless as possible!
Take this class before CS50 or AM10. Highly recommend to freshmen (or other classes to balance your workload). If you are afraid of coding and math, this class is a great stepping stone to get your feet wet without ruining your GPA. As long as you attend section and TF office hours, youll probably do well. Pretty much a gem (reasonable workload, good teaching staff, learn skills you can put on your resume).
