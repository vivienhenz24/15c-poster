Be prepared to put in dozens of hours of work a week. If you don't need this for your concentration, taking it pass/fail might be a better idea. It was a more enjoyable and fruitful learning experience for me this way! This course teaches very important skills that are often lacking in many classes today. This class brings astronomy, military/Harvard history, local Cambridge history, and environmental/landscape/visual studies all together! I would definitely take it again, but know that it will probably be the most time consuming class on your schedule. Also, you get to learn how to sail through individualized lessons, have a class on the Red Line train, have a class in the Cambridge Common, and so much more.
It is definitely fun to take the class but it is a TON of work!!! There were lots of times doing problem sets led my partner and I to almost break down crying. Make sure you find out when office hours are in advance and are okay with blocking them off in your schedule because they are essentially necessary to be able to pass
Really think before taking this course! It is very interesting, but the style of teaching is very much unstructured and depends on your willingness to work through the problems on your own and figuring it out as you go. If this is your style, take this course! In addition, this class is somewhat disorganized, so be prepared to deal with that if you take this course!
Astronomy 2 aims to give you an intuition for the movement of the stars and planets across the sky as viewed from Earth and simultaneously teach you the practical skills to determine your position by looking at the sky using the same tools and techniques as pilots and sailors. Expect to do some light trigonometry, addition, and subtraction, and expect to use your hands. Theres a final project and lots of worksheets: its probably the most hands–on class youll take here (and maybe the most practical).
LISTEN TO ME! This course is NOT a gem, or easy, or anything like that. On the contrary, this course requires a stupendous amount of work, both in the classroom and out. This is a very conceptually difficult class because of how new/different the material is (most people have never studied something like this before) as well as the volume of material that is taught on (CelNav is a very complex discipline with many moving parts).
In the beginning of the semester I thought that the Tuesday night labs (19–2200) were going to be relatively easy but as the semester progressed, they became a significant drain on both my time to complete other assignments as well as my sleep schedule. The star/sun journal that they make you keep is also extremely time consuming. The P–Sets can be nightmarish, depending on how well you know the material.
Why you should take this course is because you will never get another chance to do celestial navigation again in your life. My advice would be take this class pass–fail, make every use of office hours (which they are very generous with), and use this class to satisfy your QRD requirement. The professor and the teaching staff are extremely personable and likable people who love students.
Take the class, but don't let anyone say that it wasnt work. The only difference is that this is work worth doing.
Also, Max, you are always in our hearts. Gone, never forgotten
I took it for QRD, its sneaky really hard but its very interesting. If you have the time to properly engage and learn everything then its likely very rewarding.
