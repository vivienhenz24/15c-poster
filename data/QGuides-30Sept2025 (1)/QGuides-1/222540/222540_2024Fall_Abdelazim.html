There are two reasons I would recommend this class. The first is that it is a beneficial introduction about the foundation of almost all the life sciences. It teaches you how to think about chemistry and will be extremely useful in any and all future life sciences you take. The second is the fact that the teaching staff is the best. From Adam to Lu and Khaled to the TF's and CA's, they offer unlimited support and genuinely want to see you understand the material and do well. Although this class is certainly not easy, it is a great environment and will certainly be beneficial in any future life science classes you take.
If you loved AP Chem, TAKE THIS CLASS!! I learned so much in just a semester and gained skills, like learning how to code on MatLab, that will undoubtedly be useful in higher level chemistry courses. Although many of the topics we learned were confusing, the course staff was more than happy to answer any questions we had. I would highly recommend going to office hours even if you don't have any questions, as Adam and the rest of the course staff are also happy to just chat. Highlights of the semester include making a hydrogen powered rocket car (and racing them), assembling a Stirling heat engine and calculating its efficiency, and the final project poster session.
I think you're going to love this course if you love Chemistry, even if you're on the fence about concentrating in it :)
TAKE THIS CLASS! If you are interested in physics and chemistry this class does a wonderful job of explaining how chemical properties emerge from deeper physical phenomena. Adam is a really great lecturer and was super helpful with the final project. The lab portion was pretty interesting (mostly MATLAB with a small wet–lab portion), and I learned a lot of MATLAB over the semester.
TAKE THIS CLASS!! All of the teaching staff are the sweetest people, and the materials really tie everything together from AP Chem although a lot of the AP chemistry knowledge is assumed, so it's not an adequate replacement for general chemistry. This was by far my favorite class this semester.
This class is fantastic!!!

It is so much more accessible than what it may seem on the surface. They definitely weed out at the beginning, and you must be serious about taking this class. It is conceptually difficult, but VERY well structured, and if youre willing to put the time in, you are 100% capable of learning. That being said, heed the prerequisites. They dont say much about linear algebra, but I would strongly caution against taking this class without at least reviewing some linear algebra concepts first. It is also quite math and computationally heavy, relative to any chemistry courses you will likely have taken.

The course staff are through and through phenomenal. Adam is a super engaging lecturer and I stayed interested for the entire period, which is rare. Section was helpful too.

The only downside to this course is the lab. Yes, three hours of matlab a week is indeed that bad. The lab staff were super fun and made it livable, but its a rough one.

You will learn a ton from labs (mainly how to stay alive and not completely crash out for 3 hours while your homemade spectrometer decides not to work). However, some of the labs were only loosely related to lecture, and when you have three midterms, and lab isnt covered intensely, its hard to lock in. There is simply too much to do for the rest of the class.

The final project is fun if you pick something good. You have to seek your own help, because they throw you in the deep end. When they mention a potential project topic during lecture, PAY ATTENTION. Those deadlines sneak up on you, because they also overlap with the third midterm. The third midterm was also the day after H–Y weekend, and the second was after family weekend, so keep that in mind. It does feel like youre constantly preparing for a midterm, but with no final exam, you cant really complain.

Overall, STRONGLY RECOMMEND. You need to be committed to this class, but it will pay huge dividends. Super awesome course.
I can only speak positives about this course! The teaching staff is really strong and supportive. If you go with your confusions and questions to them they will do their utmost to help resolve them. There are so many office hours, so you can definitively find one that works for your schedule. Because of the small size of the class as well you build a really strong support system among students and that also really helps with Psets, as well as final projects. Although it is intensive content there is lots of review. The labs are also very accessible even without any coding experience. They are also interesting because they are related to the lectures but not always exactly so it is expanding your understanding of applications of the content.
Take Chem 10! This has been one of the most interesting classes that I've taken at Harvard so far, especially because the teaching staff work so hard to make the material understandable. It is a very difficult course in its concepts, but the problem sets and labs can be done with enough time and effort. Adam is a great lecturer who makes lectures engaging and entertaining, although sometimes the class feels slightly more like a physics class than a chemistry class. Section and lab are also both extremely useful to learn and apply the concepts from class, and the preceptors and TFs that oversee those are all knowledgeable and friendly.

I think that a strong physics and chemistry background is a must for this course, as it starts pretty quickly with derivations and concepts that require at least an AP–level understanding of those two fields, but if you have that, then anything is possible with enough work. Additionally, even if the course is difficult, it certainly is possible with the help that is afforded by the small class size – any of the teaching staff are extremely reachable and are all very willing to set up times to meet.

Overall, this is an awesome introduction to chemistry, and it will set you up extremely well for the later chemistry track (Chem 20/30/40) if that is what you plan on taking!
This is a fantastic class. Adam is a great lecturer (as is Lu who lectures when hes out). The content can be intimidating at first, but psets will strongly help you solidify your understanding. The class also gets easier as it goes along because it starts with quantum mechanics before going to thermodynamics. Theres no curve, but the exams are very fair and reflect pset content, so you can definitely still do well. The final project can seem scary but course staff will help you with it. The small size makes it much easier to engage with the content. I would definitely recommend this class over PS11 if you have the necessary background

I feel like I understand chemistry, quantum mechanics, coding, and even math much better than I did before. The teaching staff is amazing: informative, engaging, and excited about the material. There are lots of demos, Adam and Lu are great at lecturing, and section and lab are run well. The teaching team cares so incredibly much about the students, are receptive to feedback, and are invested in your personal success. This course was incredible in how it teaches you ideas about chemistry through first principles in a rigorous and clearly defined way (unlike traditional intro chem courses like PS11 and AP Chemistry). It integrates many different approaches computational, stat mech, and quantum mech to give a very clear intuition about properties of matter and light. I loved this class SO MUCH and recommend it to everyone. The teaching staff is SUPER supportive and kind. The psets and exams are not too difficult even though the content is quite challenging to grapple with initially, which goes to show how perfect this class is. You'll learn a ton, but you also do not need to stress about psets or exams at all.
