This class is a lot of hard work, but rewarding. Coming in with some experience with the language certainly helped me orient myself in the beginning when it felt like we were going through lot of new material and vocab, but it's really dedication and repetition that will help you succeed in any language, including Arabic. They say that Arabic is one of the hardest languages to learn, but I think that it really is the most beautiful. You just have to give yourself a chance to immerse yourself in the language, stay on top of the homework every day, and take advantage of all the opportunities you get in class to practice. The Arabic Flow project is such a fun opportunity to practice inside of class, and in–class you will also do a lot of interactive activities that will help you improve your speaking and reading. Don't go into this class expecting it to be easy, but also know that you will be supported if you put in the effort,
Ustaaz Richard is an incredible teacher and mentor. Soaking up his cultural and linguistic knowledge is one of the best parts of this course. Come in with an open mind, as Arabic is very different from many other languages that you may have studied in the past. Have an open mind, be patient with yourself, and be ready to work hard.
Totally take the course if you're passionate about arabic – but prepare to put the work IN. Richard is ultimately a nice guy, although a bit harsh at times, but he is an incredible teacher and will make sure you get the concept. It's challenging but in a fun way.
Make sure you pay attention in class (it will make doing the homeworks much easier)!
Arabic is my most time consuming course, but also the most rewarding.
Ustaaz Richard is an amazing instructor and really leaves you feeling inspired to learn this beautiful language. He always indulges your questions and his enthusiasm for the language is infectious.
Arabic AB was an amazing experience this semester. Ustaaz Richard does a great job of creating a welcoming learning environment, where I felt comfortable making mistakes in expressing myself. Using exercises that engaged different skills (class stories to practice speaking and Levantine dialect), videos for listening comprehension, and writing exercises, I feel like I strengthened all the skills I began building in Arabic AA. Participation is definitely important to do well in this class and improve your fluency. Doing the final vlog assignment, I realized how fluidly I could talk about my daily schedule/life more broadly in casual conversation. I really appreciated the Arabic Flow assignments as well to gain more cultural knowledge and practice listening comprehension, in a fun, immersive way that allowed me to take charge of my own learning.
Don't hesitate to take this course with Ustaaz Richard – you'll make great progress in a short amount of time!
Please take this class with Professor Cozzens, he will push you to learn. The class is a lot of work but you get a lot out of it, and it is so much fun.
If youre interested in learning Arabic, this is a great place to do it. If youre just trying to pick an easy language, this is definitely not it. Theres a ton of work and its a hard language to learn.
