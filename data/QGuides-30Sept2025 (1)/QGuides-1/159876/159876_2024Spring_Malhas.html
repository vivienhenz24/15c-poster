What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
The Arabic program at Harvard is very good, but also very difficult. I didn't have a background in the language before I began, but it's not necessary. Studying consistently every day (~1 hour) will likely get you an A with if you're serious about learning. Attendance matters a lot too. If you do the homework and go to class every day, then this class is very doable. Ustazza Dana is very knowledgeable and wants you to succeed.
This is definitely not a class to take if you want an easy A, or if you are not passionate about the Arabic language. The amount of work in this course is immense, and it will often take up most of your week. That being said, if you have an interest in learning the Arabic language, definitely take this course because despite the hardships I did learn a lot. To put it simply: proceed with caution
While rewarding, this class is incredibly difficult and will take up 15+ hours of your week to keep on top of all the assignments, writing/speaking tasks, quizzes, and tests that will be thrown at your this semester. You can expect a quiz or test around every or every other week in this class, with a writing task every other week. Additionally, there is homework (which usually takes 1–2 hours) every night. Ustadha Dana is a phenomenal instructor and will do everything in her power to make you feel supported and confident going forward in this class. However, before taking this class and if you are aiming for an A, consider your availability and willingness to sacrifice other extracurricular and social commitments before doing so.
Take this as a follow–up to Arabic AA; structured very well although fast–paced and a medium – sometimes heavy workload at times; assignments pile up so be sure to complete those on time; quizzes and conversation/writing tasks are graded generously and you have enough time to prepare several days before in class; the resources and support from the class is great for developing proficiency and practicing Arabic on a daily basis
Dana is a great and passionate teacher. You will come away from the end of this class able to have basic conversations in Arabic. The course assignments are constant and consistent. Make sure you set a time to do them every single day. I liked it because it gave me a routine to my work schedule.
