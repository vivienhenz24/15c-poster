This class is pretty easy and encourages a lot of disagreement over controversial topics, so if you like a more discussion–based class, take this! However, I will say that it's only suitable for certain types of students: Fryer doesn't provide any guidance as to what you need to know for the midterm/final and the class itself is unorganized in the sense that you are in charge of keeping up with readings (which are often sent to you sporadically over Canvas), taking your own notes/reflections, and studying for the exams which by the way MAKE UP YOUR WHOLE GRADE.
On the whole, I recommend this class more than I don't. Content–wise it's amazing– we talked about the economics of everything from slavery to education to crime. I always found myself recounting the class to my friends over lunch. Professor Fryer and this class are definitely one of a kind. He is for sure a genius in economics, has done some incredible research, and is very funny. Overall, a great lecturer. As a professor, you can definitely tell he's not interested in grades which for us students, was really tough. There are no assignments, just a midterm and final exam/paper (you choose) which means there is nothing to boost your grade if you don't do well. Exams were based on lecture slides (which he never finished and expected us to read through) and readings (sometimes upwards of 100–200 pages PER CLASS). He also doesn't use Canvas at all, just emails us the readings a few days in advance which can be tough for people who like to plan or people who don't check their emails. I still recommend the class though. it was so interesting and there is nothing like it at Harvard, NOTHING. fryer always encouraged controversy in class discussions and told the funniest and most engaging stories. I found myself contemplating a lot of views I previously held. also no section is a gem lol

TLDR: very interesting content, never boring. readings are overwhelming. exams are fair but still a little confusing, you'll probably be fine, especially if you speak up in class and he knows you. take it! you will learn so so much (and can always take it pass/fail)
