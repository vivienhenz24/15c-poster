This class is great for learning current theories and approaches to understanding human decision making. This class is a BANGER. Prof Ullman is one of the sharpest minds I have learned from. He makes complex concepts easy to understand succinctly and clearly. He employs a number of really helpful practices in lecture that other faculty should take note of: denoting pauses when topic changes so people can tune back in if they're distracted, frequent class exercises, etc.
This is easily one of the best courses I've ever taken at Harvard, take it.
I loved this class!! Professor Ullman is the most engaging lecturer and finds the best ways to teach concepts in both an interesting and understandable way. It was really cool to get to learn about different aspects of decision making and I definitely think about what we learned making everyday decisions now. The workload is very manageable – you just have to do 4 discussion posts and responses throughout the semester, a final project that can really be about almost anything you want in almost any form you want, and a final exam that is pretty short and almost all multiple choice. I would definitely recommend taking this course!!
Take this class!! Honestly probably top 3 best classes I've taken at Harvard. Professor Ullman is SUCH a good lecturer: incredibly engaging, enthusiastic, and considerate, plus he clearly has put a ton of effort into crafting his lectures. Course content is super interesting with a ton of connections to economics, government, philosophy, and pop culture, and every single lecture it's lowkey like you're a guinea pig being experimented on, it's like this guy always knows what you're thinking. Combined with the light workload, this is a total gem class! As a graduating senior with a psych secondary but who hasn't enjoyed a psych class since psy 15, this class made me remember why I'm a psych secondary.
