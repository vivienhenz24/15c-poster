Comments
This course is really amazing. I would highly recommend this to other people. All the course content are relevant and interesting, and the lectures/sections are all engaging as well!
This class is a great find for anyone interested in learning more about China or film or both, in a low–stress environment! Professor Li is so kind and knowledgeable and wants every student to succeed.
This class isn't just for people specifically interested in Chinese history and documentary. It's also for those solely interested in documentary, personal stories, Chinese politics and propaganda, or other topics. It's especially exciting when Professor Li invites in the directors or main subjects to speak with the class. The main workload of the class is making time to watch the assigned documentaries, and the course staff is reasonable. I'd highly recommend this class.
This is a great course for anyone interested in learning more about documentary, filmmaking, or Chinese media/culture. The assigned films were all interesting and the readings were not extensive, which made for a greatly balanced workload. The instructors are also very engaged and always willing to help the students succeed. Overall, a great experience.
If you're looking for a class that combines documentary and sociology and political science and history, take this course! It's super interesting but also quite relaxed. You will learn a lot without it feeling overly rigorous.
Watch the documentaries, participate in class, and you'll do great
If you are interested in documentary film technique/theory and how that intersects with Chinese history, this class is a must take. Professor Li and Henry make up an amazing teaching staff! You don't need any knowledge of film or Chinese history or the Chinese language, but some students come in having basic knowledge of film terms (there were quite a few AFVS concentrators/secondaries) or understanding of modern Chinese history. This class is mostly on using documentaries to tell the history of China, but you will have time to apply theory to film exercises where you have the opportunity to make a short film and edit together clips. What's also cool is that after some of the film screenings, we will have guest lecturers come in the following week (these guests were the producers/directors of the films).
I highly recommend this course to future students. It offers a unique opportunity to explore topics that are truly meaningful and significant to you.
