What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I enjoyed every discussion in this course. The course will push you to think about what it means to be Latinx in the United States and consider your relationship with the Spanish language. I recommend this course without reservations!
Definitely take this class if you can. I think this was the best Spanish class I have taken here at Harvard. I really got to reflect on my own Spanish as well as learn from everyone else. This class is not difficult to manage, and everything is actually really engaging.
This course gave me the dedicated space to explore my own background and lived experiences as a Latinx person. There were many opportunities to research cultural traditions and history. However, a lot of the diversity will come from students and their own research on assignments. So if you're an Afro–Latinx person, you might find it hard to see yourself reflected in a lot of the content.
While I loved the course material and the class encouraged everyone to participate, I feel like this class is best catered toward first–generation immigrants or students who grew up in bilingual households. The in–class discussions would oftentimes transition to "what does your family do at home in relation to this topic?" and I would be intimidated. As a second–generation immigrant who did not grow up speaking much Spanish, there were times when I felt I couldn't contribute to the conversation as much as I would have wanted to simply because my family doesn't practice many traditions from our mother countries. Nevertheless, it was still a very inclusive environment and I enjoyed learning more about my roots.
TAKE THIS CLASS!!!! You learn so much about Latin American history and Spanish as a language. Work is very easy and majority of the class is discussion based.
