Comments
This class will allow you to cultivate a close reading relationship to Augustine grounded in an intense, speculative philosophical hermeneutic. Highly recommended for anyone interested in the history of philosophy and of theology.
***The class is 80% Doyle talking and 20% student questions followed by more of Doyle talking. This can be great because Prof. Doyle is clearly brilliant and pretty humorous, and Augustine can be fascinating. It can also be difficult to stay engaged for 2 full hours of mostly just listening, especially when discussion goes down rabbit holes, or there's a topic you're less interested in. The class would benefit from some more structured opportunities to engage during the seminar. Doyle also does not proactively give any grades back. It sounds like this class won't be offered regularly.
Not very effectively taught, discussions were relatively repetitive and rarely delved into the meat of the text. Most students did not pay attention during seminars. Professor is not an expert in Augustine
This is a difficult course. Prior experience in philosophy is a must, but the material is phenomenal.
This is a really fantastic course, and Professor Doyle does an excellent job at providing you all of the resources needed to succeed. The handouts are very thorough, and Professor Doyle is generous in offering extensive office hours to help people if needed. That being said, you should also expect to work quite hard for each of the papers, which require you to forward original philosophical arguments.
The class is great for in depth understanding of the idea of conversion. We also received great insights into Augustines views on evil and where it comes from. Great insights to understand todays world.
