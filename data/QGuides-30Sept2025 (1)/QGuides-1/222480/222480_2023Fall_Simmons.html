Such interesting subject matter, and Professor Simmons is extremely engaging! She made even the toughest readings very illuminating and accessible. The progression of the course was a bit odd, but this was acknowledged and I think would likely be changed for future iterations of the course. Overall I think this is an absolutely incredible course if you're really interested in the topic, but I would recommend doing your due diligence, taking a look at a range of readings on the syllabus, and really considering whether you want to write a 20 page research paper on the topic before signing up. This strikes me as the sort of class where it would be very easy to sign up thinking it's one thing and then realize later that it's not that at all, so be careful. Personally this course was very out of my comfort zone, but looking back I've learned a ton and I'm proud of myself for my work on the research paper –– very glad to have taken the course overall. So tl;dr, a really wonderful course but not one to sign up for too casually.
I would absolutely recommend this seminar! The topic is quite interesting and the class discussions were lively and thought–provoking. Professor Simmons is obviously the main draw of the course––she is a wonderful teacher and skilled at pushing us to think hard about things we thought we knew the answers to. There can be a lot of reading, but ultimately the course is not super stressful, aside from the long final research paper.
Take this course if you are interested in the subject matter! It definitely leveled up my philosophical thinking and writing skills.
Take this class! Dr. Simmons is amazing, the subject material is both interesting and relatively accessible, and the class will leave you questioning what you thought you knew about life and death. Be aware that this class is primarily focused on the metaphysics of life and death and its historical context, and while the ethical implications of these concepts came up often in discussion this was not the main focus of the course.
Not a gem, but so worth it if you put in the time. Allison challenges students to push themselves intellectually, but is also very understanding of people's lives and situations.
This is an excellent course. The readings are thought provoking and seminar discussions were always enjoyable. There's a lot of freedom for the research paper, and it can be a very fulfilling experience especially if you find a project you're personally interested in.
This course is great! Professor Simmons is an excellent discussion leader, is very intelligent and lively, and warm and accessible in office hours and outside of class. Although I wish the syllabus were a little different, it was the first time the course has been run, and I learned a lot about how messy the seemingly fundamental and clean distinction between life and death is! Would recommend for anyone interested in philosophy!
Absolutely take this course. Really excellent course on metaphysics with some history of philosophy on the side. Professor Simmon's runs an excellent discussion, and the readings always have a lot to chew on. I cannot recommend this course enough. Metaphysics and history of philosophy tend to be the areas of the field that I am the least interested in, but I took this course because I had previously taken Phil 8 with Professor Simmons and was very impressed with her. That turned out to be an excellent decision as this was easily one of my favorite classes I have taken during my time at Harvard. Professor Simmons is always available to meet and discuss content (or anything else in philosophy) and I found some of the best discussion happened during office hours.

The course itself has a very manageable workload, though reading philosophy closely is always time consuming. There are low–stakes weekly reading responses which you get feedback on, which is helpful. Throughout the semester there are also assignments that serve as preliminary steps in writing a final research paper. At the end of the semester when it came time to sit down and actually write my research paper I realized I was done with all the most tedious parts, and the paper itself was then quite easy to write. I think I produced some of my best writing thanks to this structure, and the feedback on the final paper was extremely helpful. Again, this is a great class and you should take it.
This course is very eclectic––in what other philosophy course do you get to read Aristotle, Descartes, and contemporary work on death & dying in the ICU? And Alison is just wonderful.
