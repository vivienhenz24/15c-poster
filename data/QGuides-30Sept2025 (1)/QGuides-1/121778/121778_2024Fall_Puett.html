This course is GOATED. The professor, Michael Puett, is the nicest, smartest, most well–intentioned professor. The readings are interesting, the lectures are enrapturing, and the sections are enlightening and make you think. Definitely take this course! If you're on the fence, here are some specifics:

Course Strengths:
No mandatory lecture attendance, only 3 "real" assignments (other than weekly discussion posts), awesome Professor, great TFs (very responsive and willing to grant extensions), little time commitment necessary (~2 hours per week on average, but you can get by doing more or less if you want), no enrollment cap, and a vibrant community (the head TF for my year gave us free bookmarks, tote bags, and keychains that are stylized to advertise the course).

Course Criticisms:
The course is reading–heavy (if you don't want to read, you can probably skim and get by but this is not recommended), the lectures are sometimes just repetitions of the readings, and the essay prompts are often extremely open–ended (this is purposeful so that you can write about whatever you want, but it's often very hard to get started on essays– get started early!)
