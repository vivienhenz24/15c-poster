This class is not too difficult, especially if you have some prior knowledge of logic and/or proofs. If you read the relevant textbook sections, you'll be fine with the assignments and exam. The lectures felt a bit scattered at times, but with the lecture notes and other resources, it was not difficult to learn the material.
You should really take an introductory logic course before taking this class. The syllabus suggests it'll be fine without prior experience with logic, but the class is far too difficult to support that claim.
Modal logic is a great class! Professor Richard is a very good instructor though sometimes lectures do get a bit confusing. You are well prepared if youre familiar with math proofs and are curious about the content!
The material for this class is *incredibly* interesting, and you are likely to find it engaging if you have any prior interest in logic. However, the course material and assignments are poorly structured, which is unfortunate given the interesting material. If you have an interest in logic, I would recommend this class, but I would be prepared to do most of your learning through the textbook and through the lecture notes.
Class is fantastic but Richard is a poor lecturer.
