It's the most special course at Harvard. You will learn more than you will in any other class. That being said, it might make you cry or scream or want to crawl into a ball and yell GIVE ME SLEEP PLEASE. But Robb + Tiff (who you won't have, I am sorry) rule. And you are working with sick materials. Don't let the whole cultish 50 thing get to your head–– you shouldn't be putting this over everything in your life. Just put in the work and have fun.
XOXOXOXOXOXO
This course is unlike any film class taught at Harvard. It has a philosophy (very inspired by Fred Wisemen) to teach students the art of observational documentary. The class intentionally uses equipment that does not provide students any shortcuts, and will thus likely take more effort than any other class here. However, if true art comes from struggle, then this class is definitely an example of that. By the end of this course, you are left with multiple pieces of art which you genuinely feel proud of. Ultimately, I can't imagine there is any other way for a student to become accustomed to the AFVS department than this class.
It was a lot of work, but also the most rewarding class Ive ever taken. I met my best friends and also learned a lot from Robb and Tiff, whose enthusiasm for filmmaking is contagious. Working with 16 mm film was also a once–in–a–lifetime experience, and all the projects we did were incredibly fulfilling, especially because we did them as a group. Take this class if you can, its amazing.
This course is difficult and is a massive commitment. It will also genuinely change your life and who you are as a filmmaker. If you get the opportunity to take 50, especially with Robb, I highly recommend making the space to do so. Not only is shooting and editing on 16mm such a rare opportunity, the course itself is so foundational to the department that it's design will truly help you see filmmaking through a completely different lens. Working on a collaborative project like the final documentary allows you to build skillsets for collaborative work with the support of constant feedback that gives space for you to actively adjust and improve without impacting your own creative thought–process too much. Because of how small and intensive the class is, you'll also have an opportunity to build fabulous relationships that you'll carry with you into your filmmaking career. Highly highly recommend!
