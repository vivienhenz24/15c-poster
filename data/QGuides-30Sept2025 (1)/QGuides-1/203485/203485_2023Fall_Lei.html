This was a great class! Professor Lei is a great lecturer and we had some guest lecturers which were also great. I felt like the midterms were an appropriate level of difficulty and you can definitely be successful in them as long as you do the readings throughout the semester. The class was really interesting and as a STEM concentrator with little background in sociology I was able to learn a lot and didn't feel like the ideas were inaccessible because I didn't take any intro courses. This course presents a lot of interesting aspects of the law not only in theory but in reality and I liked the emphasis on real world cases and the history behind certain movements or aspects of the law that are present today. The readings can be quite dense sometimes but there isn't an emphasis on thorough reading and more so on just getting the main idea of the reading which I feel like is realistic and I appreciated that. I would definitely take this course again.
TAKE THIS COURSE ! Really great introduction to sociology through the lens of law and society. Although lectures are sometimes a bit dry, the topics covered are wide–ranging and fascinating. The TF Rachel made the course engaging through welcoming and enjoyable section discussions. Midterms and finals are truly just to assess your knowledge, and the teaching staff are there to root for and support you. Go to office hours and get to know Professor Lei and Rachel––both are wonderful instructors.
This class is a good, overarching overview of the law and its components (jury, judge, citizens) and their interactions with various fields such as technology and sociology. The course stays pretty focused on wider themes, which I found to be great, but keep that in mind when you're choosing to take it.
This class is a good intro class to Sociology! You read a lot of articles that span different sociological methods. The class doesn't necessarily build on itself (each lecture feels relatively independent, and they are not the most engaging). Professor Lei is incredibly kind.
This course is great for people who are deciding if they want to go to law school! I don't study sociology, I just took it because it sounded interesting, and I'm glad I did because I learned a lot about the American legal system. Not too difficult, interesting readings, great professor and TF– overall really enjoyed this class!
I really loved taking this class. Ya–wen is clearly knowledgeable and passionate about what she teaches, and so she brings in her personal and historical stories to tie everything together. I could also feel that she cared for her students and wanted to get to know us, which created a great environment. The course assignments require effort but aren't made to trick you. I would HIGHLY recommend!
Take this course if you are interested in law.

Point blank.
