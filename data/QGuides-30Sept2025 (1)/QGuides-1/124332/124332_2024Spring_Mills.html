The course is pretty fast–paced, but if you have experience learning Romance language it is very doable. The class was relatively low–stress, and we really learned so much in a single semester, so it is just more efficient to take this over French 10. It is necessary to work and study every day but shouldn't take more than an hour a day, except on Thursdays when having to write the weekly composition. Quizzes every week make sure you are keeping up with the content. Anselme was a great professor, but he will not teach anymore as he is returning to France. Highly recommend if you are interested in learning French. It will be my only French class as I will graduate, but I really feel like the class gave me the foundation I needed to continue learning on my own.
This is honestly a pretty good course and an excellent way to complete your language requirement. The fact that you receive 8 credits for one course and satisfy the language requirement in one semester makes it a "really good deal." That offering for this course is completely merited though. You really do learn the full contents of French 10 and French 11 and leave the course as a shockingly decent French speaker. The course is well–structured, though there are times where it feels like you may learn a bit ahead of the pace of course as it takes time to get to tenses/grammatical phrases. The idea of having everyone create a character in a Parisian appartment building is unique and makes the course a bit more fun. All the work that's completed for the class genuinely does improve your French ability.

You'll leave this course ideally at the B1 CEFR level which is pretty good. I actually feel like I can somewhat speak French now and that's pretty remarkable for just a semester of work. Showing up to a class 5 days a week isn't for everyone (and when they say 5 days a week, they really mean 5 days a week since the syllabus allows for very few missed classes). Fortunately, our class met at 12pm which isn't too early, though attendance may be more challenging if this course is offered at an earlier time in the future. I have few criticisms of this course. It did always feel weird that it was run by Dr. Mills technically even though it didn't seem people in the course interacted with her. Anselme, the actual teacher, was excellent and cared about our learning French and fully addressing all our questions. Another thing is that you'd expect a class with ~15 people meeting every weekday to cause people to forge some strong friendships, but, to me, it seemed like everyone in the class liked each other, but that nobody was particularly close. That's not even a strong criticism, just a counterintuitive observation.

If you're interested in French, want to get your language requirement done, or like learning quickly: take this course! It's pretty well–run, you'll learn a lot of French, and the 8 credits is pretty nice. The grading always feels fair, and as long as you attend and submit things on time, you'll probably do very well. The one strong piece of advice I have is to do all of the homework exercises each week. They don't take very long, and while you could just look at the answer key, I don't think most people do because it's a very good learning tool. I always took my time on the exercises and then corrected them after because it always filled gaps in my knowledge in a pretty efficient amount of time and also prepared me well for the quizzes. I'm glad I took this course, and look forward to being able to speak more French. I originally dreaded dealing with my foreign language requirement, but after my positive experience in French 15, I may take more French classes at Harvard.
This course is unnecessarily strict and time–consuming. There are weekly quizzes on vocab + grammar, weekly compositions that take 3 hours, completion hw, and an hour of daily class that you can miss 3 times before it affects your grade (and they are not lenient at all, even when very sick). Also participation (that is not just showing up but actually based on involvement + french usage in class) + first drafts of compositions are capped at 96 points and an A is a 94, so you have to get practically perfect scores on everything to have a chance of an A. Also, everything in this class is unnecessarily difficult to find as they made their own version of canvas functionality that does not make sense
This course offers a challenging but hugely rewarding introduction to French. You will be supported through the learning curve, and come away with a sense of satisfaction and real grounding in French. The course will likely offer something interestingly different and more creative than your regular classes at Harvard.
If you are interested in learning French, this is the course to take to start off. You learn a lot in a very short period of time without this taking away from the quality of your learning experience. All the learning material is crafted precisely to maximize your engagement with the language and, by meeting five times a week, you have five hours of engagement as a bare minimum. So, you can't help but learn the language! This all being said, it was definitely a lot of work with daily assignments, weekly essays, two oral exams, and a final project. But, the teaching staff, and the department as a whole, is super understanding, so if you genuinely want to learn the language and are active in class you will get the results you want, both academically and in terms of learning another language.
