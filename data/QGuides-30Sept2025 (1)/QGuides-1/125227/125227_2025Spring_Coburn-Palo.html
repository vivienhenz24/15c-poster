What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Take this class without hesitation. I took it sophomore spring and used the lessons I learned in my other classes. More recently, I used the tactics on an interview, and I say that it went pretty well. My instructor Nick, his sheer volume of experience, and amazing personality made this class such a joy. You will get an A as long as you do all the assignments, but most importantly, you will walk away with a LOT of new skills. Take this class as early in colleges possible, make it a 4th (not a 5th) class, and put effort into every speech. You will be blown away with how much you grow as a communicator (and so will your friends)!
This is one of the best and most life–applicable courses you can take at Harvard. You will be pushed outside of your comfort zone and bond with your peers more than any course at Harvard.
This class is incredibly rewarding and you will be able to see marked improvement in your public speaking by the end. If you are lucky enough to get Nick as a teacher please take the class. He is a gifted educator. You will form close connections with your class and grow along with them. Everyone loves the class.
Nick is a great preceptor! He really makes everyone feel comfortable and willing to take risks at each speech.
TAKE EXPOS 40. I loved this class, loved my preceptor, and aside from that, loved my class. You get really close with the 15 people you take the class with, because while some speeches can be silly, some are really deep and personal. As a senior who already felt pretty strong in public speaking, I still feel like I learned and grew, all while having a lot of fun with the class.
IF YOU DO ONE THING BEFORE LEAVING HARVARD, TAKE THIS COURSE!!!! Public speaking is necessary for everything you do in life. And, this course may be scary to you. But I absolutely promise that this has been one of the most welcoming, most rewarding, most wholesome courses of my college career. Everyone comes into the course at different levels some are naturally better public speakers, other are a bit more unrefined. None of that matters. Everyone is so, so welcoming and wants to hear about your life story through the speeches you give. Ultimately, what you get out of this course is directly proportional to what you put in. Some might say that this course isn't worth your time, but I'd disagree. I found myself spending hours and hours on each speech, perfecting it to be just right, because I cared about getting better and putting my best foot forward. I learned how to think on my feet and speak impromptu. I learned how to better tell stories and craft narratives. I learned technical public speaking skills that will serve me for life moving forward. And, I made so many new friends. This is a senior–heavy class, so if you're a senior taking this in the spring, it'll be a way to build community and have a tight–knit group before parting ways. If you take this as an underclassman, you'll hear so many stories and persepctive–altering speeches that will help shape the rest of your undergraduate career. Truly cannot recommend this course enough!
