What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I enjoyed this class. Luke leads good discussions. The readings are really good and the books were super engaging. Our section was very small so you REALLY need to do the reading every week. We had the opportunity to talk with a lot of the most prominent translators in the world, which was the highlight of the course.

Do not "mail in" your assignments. Assignments are graded a bit more harshly than a typical humanities course––not unfairly, just be aware that you shouldn't write essays the night before.
This course introduced me to a range of books I probably wouldn't have read otherwise, but am grateful that I did. It was a really interesting opportunity to read contemporary translated literature and many times, talk to the translator to ask about their process and the prize selection in general. Class discussions were always interesting and everyone seemed very engaged with the material. However, I think that one major area for improvement would be handing back feedback in a timely manner. Our first essay was due in early March and was not handed back until the day before our last class period. The delay in feedback made it difficult to show consistent improvement based on feedback.
This was a fantastic and eye–opening course on translation and literary competitions. We read an amazing book each week, and each one was incredibly engaging. We also had some really cool guest speakers. Since this was the first time the course was offered, there were a few tweaks along the way, and sometimes the assignments felt a bit loose in structure. But overall, I really enjoyed it. I recommend this class if youre interested in languages, literature, or courses that look beyond the mainstream. Luke is a kind and knowledgeable lecturer who has a wealth of his own experience translating.
