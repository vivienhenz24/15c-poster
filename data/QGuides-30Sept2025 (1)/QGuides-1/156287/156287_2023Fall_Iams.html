The class is easy if you pace yourself. I really enjoyed it and learned cool things.
A great class that goes into several important topics of scientific computing at reasonable pace and great clarity
The course staff was the best course staff I've had at Harvard. Office hours were fantastic.
I learned some cool stuff about computers as well, and Dr. Iams' problems are EXCELLENT. I learned a lot of math.
I became quite a bit more comfortable coding with NumPy and got a lot better with indexing in Python.
The class is small, and everyone really likes math. So taking APMTH 111 is a great way to make friends.

Some advice:
1. Take the pre–class assignments seriously. You'll clear up some confusion you have even as you articulate your question, or at the very least, your posts will inform the course staff that you're confused about specific material.
2. GO TO LAB.
3. Be on top of the project. Make sure you're understanding the basics of what you're studying.
4. If you don't end up taking this class, PLEASE take another class of Dr. Iams'.
This is an interesting course, but not my favorite AM course. I would take if necessary.
This class feels like a high school class. There is a lot of busy work for no reason – readings and discussion posts weekly, skill checks (mini quizzes) at the beginning of each class, optional problem set questions, etc. It is led with her teaching/introducing some concept that you had read about, you go off into groups and work on a worksheet, then in between problems she presents the answers on the board and then you repeat. It was very repetitive and a slow moving class. Maybe good for freshman year as you ease into AM classes at Harvard, but definitely not advanced content or teaching by any means. The grading scale is confusing, and the TFs often also clueless (though they try and be helpful, so not their faults). Often felt patronized because she likes things done a very specific way for some pretty remedial stuff.
