Take anything Marty teaches! Prof. Zeve (Marty) and TF Taylor are incredibly welcoming and dedicated to helping you learn and bring their unique perspectives working in global health to the table.
This was an extremely well–balanced class (intended to be very high praise), all credit for which goes to Marty and Taylor This was a fascinating class that managed to successfully walk a very fine line between breadth and depth on such a broad subject. A lot of credit here to the teaching team –– the class was originally supposed to be a small seminar–style class, but then they had to adapt when 50 people registered. Having listened to student feedback, they split the class in two each week with each of them alternating which half of the class they would lead in the original seminar discussion.
In another example of the good balances the class struck, the readings generally skewed academic, but the assignments allowed you to go out into the real world and apply what we learned in class –– those who were interested could do an ethnography and an art project in lieu of traditional academic papers.
I know Marty is an HMS professor, but I really hope she comes back for another course –– and I would be the first in line to sign up! She is an amazing professor and really cares very deeply for her students/

I took this course as an elective, and I'm so glad I took it. I learned so much. The topics were all really engaging. Some of the theoretical readings were a bit challenging, but still super fascinating. I really recommend this course for anyone who is pre–med. You'll learn so much about social, political, and economic factors that shape individual health and the health of societies.

This class completely transformed the way I think about healthcare and the various factors that influence health outcomes. The workload is relatively light, but you get out of the work what you put in. I was really engaged in the class discussions, and the readings challenge a lot of the understanding of healthcare that we take for granted. This course makes you critical about what healthcare even is and the different ways that organizations, governments, and individuals interact with it to change it. Highly recommend!
