Comments
your experience highly depends on your TF. some don't care about your goals and are just looking to get financial aid for their phd. you could definitely get by doing a thesis without this course.
This course is great for preparing to write a senior thesis. The syllabus is created around your interests and you get one–on–one meetings with knowledgeable grad students.
If you're considering taking this class, you really should! It's a phenomenal experience, and you'll grow a lot through it.
98b is very helpful in preparing for the thesis process. The one–on–one nature of the course facilitates individualized work and helps with broadening a literature base, forming a more complete plan and prospectus, and addressing concerns.
If you are an anthropology concentrator thinking about writing a thesis, definitely take this class! You meet with your graduate student (your TF) every week and craft your thesis proposal together. I found that the meetings with my TF were incredibly helpful in shaping my thesis proposal!
I had an AMAZING TF, and I was lucky. Regardless of your TF, this class it truly what you make of it. If you're doing multiple readings a week and using assignments well, you'll be prepared for your prospectus. I would use the 4 assignments to scaffold to your prospectus (ex. #1 for me was a roundup of my sources from the first chunk of the semester, #3 was a draft of the prospectus lit review). Damina, Andrew, and Alex are key supports –– use them. And go to Andrew's workshops!
