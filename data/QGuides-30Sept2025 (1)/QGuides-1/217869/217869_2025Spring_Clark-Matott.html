What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is a really fun way to improve your scientific research skills! The expectations earlier in the semester aren't super high, so it's manageable with some harder classes. Be prepared however to write a long research paper for your final assignment. As a biologist myself, I liked that this class allowed me to improve my coding and quantitative research skills. Quite important for a thesis if you haven't done much computational work before! It also is a great opportunity to work on your scientific writing. I would also add that I'm really not too interested in sleep/neuroscience/etc, and I still got quite a bit out of this class.

One more comment: A handful of class members took this as their science distribution. I wouldn't necessarily recommend this class if you don't have any science experience. You have to code a decent amount and write an original research paper on a study of your own design. It's not absurdly rigorous for students who have done scientific research before (unless you're interested in turning your work into a thesis or something), but it seemed to be pretty overwhelming for people who had never seen a p–value before.
