Take this class!
Take this class! It is an amazing introduction to sleep research and Jo is amazing.
This is a VERY self–driven course that I would only recommend to someone interested in a) sleep and b) designing their own research project. If you are interested in sleep and designing your research project, it's fantastic! Due to the flexible nature of the course and primary focus on the final research project (where you design your own question, conduct a survey, run analyses, and write a report), it is very "you get out what you put in." I for one really enjoyed the course and the final project, and I loved the energy of the group everyone helped each other design their projects, people were engaged and attentive during presentations, etc. but do note that the course is not highly structured. Jo is great; very laid–back regarding deadlines, super supportive of the students, highly accessible, and provides great feedback on work.
I would tell future students to do a survey project. Take this course if you want to practice data analysis in the context of sleep, not if you want to learn a lot about sleep science. Do a survey project. Don't attempt a genetic analysis or anything like that unless you have experience doing that.
This course is amazing! You get to deep dive into sleep and study your own topic of interest on sleep. Jo is amazing, gives great feedback, and super flexible, allowing you to produce your best work. There aren't any busy work assignments and everything has a purpose to help you better understand sleep and research processes in general. I am not HEB, but I found this course extremely informative and a fun course especially if you have an otherwise packed semester.
This class is wonderful. Jo is a great professor, and she guides you through the research process without wasting your time on unnecessary assignments. The material is engaging and pertinent for everyone. I'm an Econ concentrator, and I got to use econometric methods to study sleep, which I thought was very cool to marry the two. I wholeheartedly recommend this class as a tutorial/seminar course.
