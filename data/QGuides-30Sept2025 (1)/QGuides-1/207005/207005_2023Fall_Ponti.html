If you are looking for a transformative physics course, this is it. This course is exciting, engaging, and philosophical. Jacob is both an amazing instructor and human being through and through â€” he is extremely passionate about this course and physics/philosophy in general, incredibly considerate, understanding and kind with a great sense of humour. Lectures can be a bit fast–paced (the course in general covers a LOT of material), but Jacob nails the clear explanations, and if you are still lost, you can refer to his meticulous, textbook–quality lecture notes after class and/or attend office hours. While the course can be challenging, grading is fair and (I personally think) quite generous at times. The midterm exams are simpler than the problemsets, and there is no final (in its place a fun project/presentation, where you can choose an interesting physics problem you'd like to explore with a partner or two). The level of understanding you need to develop to "ace" this course (on paper) is not as high as the sheer level of complexity that some of the concepts can reach, but you can (and should!) explore physics more if you are interested (e.g. lecture notes go much more in–depth and often touch upon significantly more advanced ideas/problems).
