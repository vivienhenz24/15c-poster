This course is cool! I am a science concentrator so I had never really taken a class like this before, but I really enjoyed it. This was the first time the class was taught so I assume that there will be some changes as time goes on, but the main assignments are the readings, a video response to one of the readings, three smaller papers, and a longer final paper, all of which were very enjoyable. My biggest complaint with the course is it feels like it moves too fast, but I guess that's a consequence of going from the first films ever to AI.
This is a great course, but I think it was falsely advertised. Whereas the course description focuses on the cultural and political implications of different screen technologies throughout history, the course focused more on the history of how these technologies came to be, focusing on more technical and material aspects (especially towards the end of the semester). This is not to say that the discussions on culture were absent––they were simply not the main focus of the class. As such, this can be a great class if you are interested in History and Science or STS. However, if you are more interested in learning about the politics of representation and/or media studies in general, I would consider other courses. Something to keep in mind is that most course materials were (dry) academic papers and essays, with very few primary sources to engage with. I do think that Emmet is aware of this, and I believe that this will probably change in future iterations of the class. So make sure to take a look at the syllabus!

Emmet is fantastic. He was always readily available to meet for office hours to talk about readings and assignments, and provided good feedback.
Because this course is so discussion based, the experience really depends on the seminar. Emmet is wonderful, nice, and helpful, but if people are not engaged in discussion class can be boring. Try to engage and make the most of it. Also, you spend a week on each technology so if you're only interested in film or tiktok I would be aware that that's a relatively small portion of the course. The final paper is really fun–––go to office hours if you need help, again Emmet is so nice and will help you with any assignment and be clear about what he wants/expects.
DO NOT TAKE THIS CLASS IF YOU ARE NOT A HIST–LIT CONCENTRATOR!!! Coming into this class, I had deep passion and wish to gain knowledge about film, but this was not the class or environment to do so. This class extinguished that flame. As for weekly workload, there is plenty of reading and watching (which is to be expected) and they all revolved around a topic, be it cable television, cinema, or social media. Discussions were quite expertly crafted and facilitated by the instructor. Assignments are quite evenly spaced out and manageable but expectations for such assignments are very unreasonable. For one, we are asked to analyze films and television shows twice (and possibly once more for your final paper) and we are immensely criticized for not knowing the ins–and–outs of film analysis despite never being taught in this course and being told it wasn't expected for us to initially know. It is like telling someone who's never been ice skating before to skate and spin, but getting frustrated when they fall even though you didn't do the bare minimum and teach them how to take their first steps! Film analysis is not something that should be self–taught, and much less by an instructor with little respect for a students writing given expected experience. The instructor did pick out great films and the assignments were entertaining to complete nonetheless. If the instructor was able to discern snarky, destructive criticism for constructive, I would be writing a completely different report.
I'd tell future students that this is a great course and that engaging with the materials makes the experience of in–class discussions even more interesting. Emmet is really kind and works hard to facilitate meaningful discussions.
Take this course if youre interested in the history of film, and how media evolved over time. Id say we focused more on the media itself than its impact or status within the context of culture, however the content was still quite interesting. It would be beneficial to have an interest in media and film itself though, in particular for film analysis as thats what most of the papers were on. Emmet is great, I would definitely take a class with him again. Youll get more out of the class if you do the readings and engage in discussions, otherwise its easy to zone out.
