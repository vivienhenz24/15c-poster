Comments
Professor Saha was incredible. She is so kind and passionate about the subject. Discussions were interesting and the readings/format of the class were great. I learned so much. Harvard is going to miss her!
While Dr. Saha will not offer this class in the future, as she is leaving academia, this course is a prime example of what a Gov 94 course should be. Dr. Saha encourages discussions and allows us to bring in our own experience. I especially loved this class because the class was primarily women, which allowed me to express my thoughts freely, without fear of being cut off by another student. We had discussions on identity politics within the Republican Party, as well as debates as to whether or not Kamala Harris' gender hurt her chances of being elected during the 2024 general election. I noticed gendered differences when I went to Washington, DC, during the semester, which I otherwise wouldn't have picked up on if I hadn't taken this course. This class will truly prepare you to look at the issue of gender and politics through a different lens, even if you disagree with the other frameworks discussed during class. Honestly, I could not recommend a class more, and I am sad to see Dr. Saha leave Harvard. This class is one of my favorites.
This class is a great and gemmy way to satisfy a Gov 94 or American subfield requirement, or a fun elective to learn more about women in US politics. The main work centers around a group capstone project that combines standard peer–reviewed Gov research with policy analysis and policy recommendations.

Two things to keep in mind: 1) Dr. Saha, who previously taught this course, is leaving academia, so the class will have a new instructor, and 2) presentations to the Women's Caucus are not guaranteed to be at the Massachusetts State House or involve any of the representatives. In our year, we presented our projects on campus to the Executive Director.
It's a great course if you want a relatively light experience that teaches you policy presentation. But, the discussions are sometimes unfocused.
