Comments
This is a fantastic tutorial for you if you're interested in postcolonial studies, empire, or anything related to it. The readings were very well selected and covered a range of interesting topics, my only complaint is that I wish I had done them more closely. Yasemin is such a lovely tutorial leader: She is very kind, warm, and totally brilliant. If you're interested in leftist thought, I would highly recommend this tutorial!
I really encourage students who have even the slightest interest in postcolonialism/empires to take this class. It isn't only about traditional postcolonial theory, but we also talk about contemporary issues that have connections to twentieth century colonialism.
Yasemin is one of the best instructors I've ever had, and she is incredibly smart. It was quite challenging for me, which I enjoyed, and it made me sit and take it in over the usual talking that I might have been doing otherwise, but there was plenty of time and opportunity for that as well. The materials are amazing and the course is structured wonderfully
Take this class! This was my first social studies class, and the first time I had to write a paper of this length (20–25 pages). However, I felt supported throughout the entire process, so it never became overwhelming. The readings can be long, but they're definitely worth doing. Start on the paper early, and it should be totally manageable.
TAKE THIS COURSE!! Professor Bavbek is an incredible educator. This is one of the rare opportunities at Harvard to take a course about issues that matter, and particularly those which Harvard does not make it accessible to study. The readings are delightful and challenging, the class meetings are productive and provocative, and the essay–writing process is smooth and fun. take it take it take it
Yasemin is wonderful, and the readings are so seminal to postcolonial theory! This course will get you thinking about decolonization and the postcolonial world in novel ways.
