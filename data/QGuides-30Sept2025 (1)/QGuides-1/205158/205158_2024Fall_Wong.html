Super interesting course that will change your perspective on the power of music. Harvard needs more interdisciplinary classes like this. The final project is pretty substantial but the work is manageable otherwise and readings are enjoyable. Almost everyone in the class is a pre–med musician so having some background in music and bio/neuro would be helpful.
Take this mbb seminar!! The guest speakers are fascinating, it's not too much work (weekly discussion posts/responses, two presentations, one final paper), and genuinely such a joy/bright spot in a week of difficult or dull classes.
Dr Wong is INCREDIBLE! She leads each class with kindness, generosity, and humility while being an immaculately talented and intelligent individual. She looks to the needs of her students first and works to cultivate a loving environment within class. She supports friendships forming within her class and always brings food. Because of her kind demeanor, she has so many impressive friends she brings into class as guest lecturers. She wants to know about the music we perform and lets us jam together on the last day of class. Truly a great class with insights all over the spectrums of health and music!
I LOVE this course! It is an engaging way to discuss the interaction of these three fields and you'll leave with a widened perspective!
If you are passionate about music and health, I highly recommend this course! Not only is it well–organized with a broad range of speakers and activities, but it is with the kindest, and more inspirational teaching staff (Dr. Wong and Dr. Kershenbaum). These two are incredibly well–connected and knowledgable in the space of music and medicine, and the course is worth it just to hear their insight and be able to engage with them. It is one of those courses where you do make of it what you put in because of the seminar style. Since I chose topics for my presentations and papers that I found exciting, the course still felt incredibly valuable and engaging.
If you are or were a musician, take this course. It will change your world.
AMAZING COURSE PLEASE TAKE!!! this course was probably my favorite course I've taken so far. Get your foot in the door for course applications early, because the class is very competitive. Dr. Wong and Ayelet are the most fascinating, knowledgable, talented, and caring people I've ever met. Both of them have more accolades than I can count on two hands, and they were able to foster a wonderful community in the classroom. The content you will learn in this class is something so special, you will not find a curriculum like this outside of this class. There were 30–50 ish pages of reading per week, and an amazing guest speaker would come in for part of each class to talk about their special relationship to music. Everyone in the class is basically a pre–health musician. This class reminded me I go to Harvard –– every single student had some beautiful niche talent that was captivating and so cool. AGAIN PLEASE TAKE!
This class is genuinely life–changing, especially if youre someone who loves music, neuroscience, or exploring the connection between science and the arts. Every lecture is fascinating and leaves you thinking about the power of music in ways youve never considered before. Youll get to hear directly from some incredible leaders in the field about novel research and ideas, and the discussions will challenge you to see how music can shape health, education, and human connection.

What makes this class so special is how it combines science and art in such a meaningful way, and it encourages you to think critically and creatively at the same time. Its not just a class its an experience that opens doors to new ways of understanding the world. If youre even slightly interested, take it. Be curious, ask questions, and soak it all in. Youll leave with a deeper appreciation for the therapeutic power of music and the inspiration to do something with what youve learned. Its the kind of course that stays with you long after its over.
This is one of the best classes I've taken at Harvard! This class teaches you so much about music and its intersection with health and education – from melodic intonation therapy to El sistema – and is perfect for anyone interested in anything health–related. It is a very low pressure course which really allows you to focus on the content and Dr. Wong and Ayelet are the most amazing course instructors ever.
