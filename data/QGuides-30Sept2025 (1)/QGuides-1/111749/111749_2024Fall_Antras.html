It's a hard class but Pol is a great lecturer. I found the exam work to be very different from what we did in class though. It is a rewarding class.
this class is the excellent combinantion of theory and evidence
Day to day the class is super chill. On the weeks where we didn't have psets due, I legit did 0 work for the class. He does assign textbook readings for each lecture but it's not necessary to do them anything you need out of the class you can find in the lecture slides. There isn't that much accountability in the class, so the midterm and final can definitely creep up on you. They were each worth 35% of your grade. The psets aren't bad at all and go over the more mathematical workings of models. Lectures could be a bit dry, as each theoretical trade model started to look the same and there are just so many letters. I did like the intellectual history component though. Overall, not too much work at all, but beware the midterm and final bc they're worth so much.
I liked this course and I think it's pretty easy. If this type of thing interests you, take it! If it doesn't, don't!
This course really helps me in understanding the concept of trade before learning it in more advance level (advance microeconomics/phd level course). Psets are helpful and there is always space to ask questions in class and give opinion. Recommended!
Great course to fulfil the Econ elective with theory pre–req. Definitely buy the textbook and do the chapter readings to really get an understanding of the material. Great lecturer and Kartik is such a great section leader.
This course covers all about international economics frrom Ricardian model to GVCs. There are a lot of interesting topics that are quite related to the world nowadays. Professor Antras is very nice both in and out of classroom. He is always eager to respond to any questions or concerns. TA Jun Gao is also very supportive and helpful. It might seem easy just to solve the models, but you can go very deep to the reading lists and ask for additional papers: all based on what you want to learn. Very good course.
