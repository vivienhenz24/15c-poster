What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class is a great way to learn about trade if you care about international economics, public welfare, multinational firms, etc.; it's also a great option if you want to knock out a theory requirement. The lectures are interesting, section is helpful, PSets/exams are fair, and workload is not that heavy. You'll learn how to structure your economic thinking and analysis and how to evaluate the economic arguments of others.
I'd recommend this class! The workload was very manageable.
Going to section is very helpful, especially for doing the problem sets.
The courses material is very organized and presented in a coherent manner. It makes good connections to the real world, and it gives a good understanding of trade from both the firm–level and the policy–level with careful consideration towards individual and societal welfare.

The professor and the teaching staff were all very nice and articulate in their explanations. I learned a lot!

The grading scheme for this course is completely opaque. By the time of the final, we had gotten back only 1/4 problem sets graded.

This ordinarily may not be a problem, however this class expects you to answer exam questions in a certain style. Furthermore the solutions presented for past exams and for the problem sets are extremely terse, and would not themselves get any marks. Many solutions simply say "good reasoning will get you points" but no actual reasoning is provided, even though the exam questions expect very specific reasoning.

It is even more unclear what to expect on exams, because the professor and the teaching staff didn't seem to communicate that much, which was especially troublesome when the exam's expected very specific wording/reasoning/assumptions...
This is a great class with a great professor. The content is more interesting than many other electives. The exams and assignments are fair.
Attend lecture since they're not recorded, and Anhua's section before the PSET is due is great since he goes over the entire PSET.
