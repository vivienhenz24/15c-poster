What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Chinese 130a focused on social issues within China and was meant to use these topics to teach us how to speak and write Chinese more professionally. I feel that the course taught me many interesting things about society within China, and used readings and online material that drew upon the standard Chinese experience to teach us these topics. That being said, I believe that the pace of the class is too fast. With 2–3 quizzes a week and 1–2 long homework assignments it feels like I am always learning new material, however, the speed at which we move through new topics leaves me without any opportunity to digest the words and grammar structures we are learning and incorporate them into my language use. Additionally, I feel like I teach myself all the material at home to prepare for quizzes, making class time sometimes feel like a waste of time. I wish we had less material to learn so we could truly practice and keep up with what we are learning. I honestly felt like my Chinese level has not risen very much in the past semester.
as long as you do the homework and spend time studying for tests two days or the day before, you will be fine.
Learning Chinese at Harvard has truly been the greatest academic challenge, but the most rewarding one. I learn so much with each passing week and I truly feel my Chinese improving.

That said, this class is not a joke. It requires intense focus and a lot of time and effort. Despite this, the work pays off because you learn A LOT and if you put in the time, you will do well.
Its well structured, the teaching staff is amazing, and Yang Laoshi is incredibly easy to work with, light hearted, and diligent. But the pace and workload makes taking the course or seeking a language citation kind of inaccessible for most people. It has certainly made my time at Harvard more challenging as it means you will have 0 rest days.
