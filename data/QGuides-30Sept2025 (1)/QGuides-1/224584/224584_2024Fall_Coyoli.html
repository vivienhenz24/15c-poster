What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If you're interested in education at all, this course does such a great job of giving students both a wide and deep knowledge of the politics of education in an international context! The course readings are truly so interesting, and I highly encourage doing them with dedication and intentionality to get the most out of the class.

The structure of this course is clear and intentional, and there are ample opportunities for feedback from Julia. I enjoyed the scaffolding built in through low–stake assignments throughout the semester that work toward the final paper. If you take these assignments seriously, they allow you to get incredibly targeted guidance and feedback. Not only does this feedback help you do your paper more successfully, but it also really helped me improve as a writer and thinker!
Def take! Julia is wonderful and an amazing instructor. She is so passionate about everything she does, and will make you a better writer and thinker. If you have an interest in global education this is the course to take! Make sure to do the readings as they contain really interesting concepts which are relevant to each week.
I would tell future students that this class is a really good overview of how different types of organizations and actors impact education policy. There are readings from a variety of disciplines and the discussion was wide–ranging
I recommend you take this course if you are: interested in education, or a first–generation, low–income student in social studies who feel as if the course are sometimes inaccessible. if you are interested in education, this course provided an interesting approach by thinking about education politics in a global context. The global context makes you challenge a lot of the assumptions you may have had, or connections that you would not have otherwise understood. If you are from a marganlized background, the course instructor is phenomenal. She truly cares about students and making sure that it is accessible.
This course exposes students to education across the (primarily Spanish–speaking) world that is thorough and interesting! The class has many scaffolding assignments for the final research paper, so by the time students have to write it, they have already done a bulk of the work due to the way the course is run. This course also provides valuable perspectives on positivist approaches to research and encourages students to take risks with their final projects.
