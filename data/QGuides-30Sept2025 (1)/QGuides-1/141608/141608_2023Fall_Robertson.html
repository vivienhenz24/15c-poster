This is my FAVORITE class at Harvard. Seth is such a funny, exciting, lecturer who makes the topics accessible and relatable, engages with thought experiments and discussions. Ryan is one of the kindest people and the best feedback–giver I've ever met.
If you are interested in philosophy but are intimidated by philosophy classes, take this course. It is not too intense, assignments are very fair, and teaching staff is supportive. It is really interesting stuff and exposes you to a range of philosophies you would never otherwise read (I was skeptical of this aspect of the course and initially wanted to focus more on traditions I had heard of but it turned out to be really interesting and valuable to learn about the other schools of thought, some of which I really resonated with).
I went into this course with almost no philosophy background and found it very interesting and eye–opening. The course doesn't spend a lot of time on each topic, but the breadth was extensive and introduces philosophy that I probably would have never encountered otherwise.
Know that this is a history of philosophy course. Dr. Robertson describes his course as an argument to show there is a lot of good ethics outside of the "Western cannon" and that the way that cannon has been selected is sometimes problematic. He makes the first point through breadth rather than depth. A new thinker, system, region, or idea is discussed in each lecture. It is a sprint across the world and time. If you are interested in seeing all kinds of cool philosophy (see the syllabus), then this course can be good for you. However, if you want to focus on and dig deep into ideas, then department offers courses structured around smaller topics. Ethics, after all is one of the main fields of philosophy.
This class is pretty good, with an interesting overview of philosophy throughout time. It was, however, not very in depth about anything, which is just what you get when you cover that much philosophy in a semester, so be sure you're aware of that before enrolling.
This course is great and super interesting. Also not a huge time commitment.
I would tell future students that the study of philosophy can prepare you for any field you choose. Dr. Robertson is a great instructor to have as an first instructor in the discipline. He explains concepts at a level where everyone can understand. He also encourages class discussions.
Highly recommend. The papers can be challenging, but this class will expose you to a lot of interesting philosophy that is not widely taught. Seth is a great professor and presents the material in very engaging and accessible ways.
this was a great course! Not too demanding but still covered a large amount of history and a wide variety of philosophies from different cultures.
Take this class for a surface–level look into many different philosophies! This especially goes if you're interested in non–Western philosophies which I believe are less mainstream in education. There were definitely readings which genuinely resonated with me, so do the readings and if you'd like, go deeper into ones you really liked. Lectures can get a little dry at times, but the content is still really interesting. A great introductory course to philosophy.
This is a class to take if you're interested in philosophy at all. You'll learn a lot about many years of philosophy from all across the world, and Seth is a very knowledgeable lecturer. This class will make you think critically and challenge some previously held ideas, but has a completely manageable workload!
OVERALL:
I liked it. Seth is really, really knowledgeable. He clearly knows the readings like the back of his hand and has all sorts of great anecdotes around the history of philosophy. He's so nice and brings a TON of enthusiasm to the lectures. The readings were excessive, but the papers were really cool to write. Most of the class felt like passive learning, though.

FOR PEOPLE WHO HAVE TAKEN OR ARE LOOKING AT THE HAPPINESS GENED:
If you took Happiness, don't take this; it's pretty redundant. Also, if you're choosing between this class and Happiness, take Happiness, which presents more ways to digest the material and has more engaging lectures. The Happiness readings are better, and there's a decent amount of active learning.

If you are at all interested in ethics or philosophy, you should 10000% take this class. It's super interesting, covers so much ground that it's never boring, and has wonderful lectures and sections. This is an excellent intro course to philosophy. While it is a history of philosophy course (meaning you will not be sitting around a table debating the meaning of life every day), the papers smoothly introduce students to philosophical writing. Lectures do drag occasionally, but there are some really helpful analytic points in each one, and I carried many concepts that were unpacked in lecture into my papers. If you are looking for a space to sit and have philosophical dialogue, I'd say that happens in sections. Thus, there's something for everyone!
I would emphasize that this course should not be treated as an intro Phil courseit focuses particularly on non–Western philosophy (such as Buddhist, Hindu, Sikh, Confucian, Mexica) philosophy. This course is amazing if that is what you are interested in, and it is important that Harvard offers a course that broadens traditional definitions of philosophy. However, if you are hoping to get an in–depth exploration of ethical/moral philosophy, or exposure to classic Western philosophy, this course probably isn't for you. Additionally, since this course can be considered a GEM, I would not call it particularly rigorous with regard to the intellectual community it producespeople do the readings here and there, but I wouldn't say that everyone is engaged with the course material.

This class is a fantastic introduction to ethics. Seth is one of my favorite professors and is an amazing lecturer, and the section leaders give great feedback. The assignments are very fair and interesting, and I think there's a lot of important personal knowledge to be gained from this course.
