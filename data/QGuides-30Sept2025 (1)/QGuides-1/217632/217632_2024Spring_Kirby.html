not an easy class, but quite interesting. kirby is also kinda funny and i enjoyed hearing him lecture in his half–serious way.

Don't get me wrong, I don't know that I've ever learned more about global dynamics and the influence and importance of the US and China. But as someone who went in with no knowledge whatsoever on the topic and not much of an interest in history, there was definitely a big learning curve to start because there are a lot of readings (150–350 pgs/wk) and It's hard to read (or go to class having not read) when you know nothing. Both class and section are very discussion–based, so you have to understand the topic and be ready to share. Outside of this, there aren't many assignments, so reading was the biggest commitment other than the group debate (presentation) and final essay. Learned so much and very happy about that, BUT AT A COST.

Much more work than your typical gened, but I think that's a good thing. You get a lot out of this course, and its content is becoming increasingly important given the rising tensions between the U.S. and China. If you're remotely interested the topic, I'd go for it, just know there are a lot of readings. The teaching is also skewed towards being pro–China. The professor nearly never criticized the Chinese government (other than some off–handed remarks), whereas the U.S. (and Harvard) are heavily criticized. This class is super awesome and really teaches you a lot. Professor Kirby is truly an amazing professor and the way he teaches is very engaging. The readings can be dense (you're also not allowed any laptops during lecture) so you do spend quite a bit of time a week doing the readings.
A lot of reading, but not as difficult as everyone says. Super interesting and you will learn a lot!
I wish I could write a better review for this course, but I can't. The content is actually interesting and quite useful, but the pedagogy and logistics are really not well thought out. First, the professor does cold call, which is fine, but does not have a mechanism for ensuring all students are chosen at least a baseline number of times throughout the semester. This would be okay, if participation were not a huge part of this course's grading structure. This can be made up in section, but it is still frustrating. Additionally, there is extremely minimal feedback given through the semester, so you have no idea what your grade will be by the end. The one assignment I received feedback for was returned many weeks after submission. The one nice part of the course was section– it was actually engaging and helpful for understanding content. It is a shame that the logistics and lecture component were not well organized, considering that the material is actually important. There are probably better ways to fill this Gened requirement than this class.
Take this class if you are very interested in China and have some time in your schedule to dedicate to it.

Fantastic course if you are vaguely interested in anything US–China, but quite heavy workload especially if you are taking for a letter grade. Prof Kirby and TF Preston were both amazing, I learned a lot!!
This class is very interesting, has very engaging/interesting guest speakers, and does a great job outlining US/China relations overall. I think this is a great class to take pass/fail, but I would be cautious signing up for this class if you want a good grade. They have not been very transparent with grading, and they are grading the class on a curve.
