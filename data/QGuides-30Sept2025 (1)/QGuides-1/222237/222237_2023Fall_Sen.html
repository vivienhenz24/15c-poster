I would not recommend taking this course. It is an extremely light lift, and again quite inspiring to have such direct access to such distinguished professors, but the course had very little to offer.
This class is certainly quirky. It is poorly organized. The professors changed the topics mid–class, they would never finish what they had planned to talk about, and there were no assignments over the course of the year except for the final paper (worth 100% of the grade). Prof. Sen is 90 years old––he has interesting things to say and he really cares, but he's definitely a bit past his prime. The only assignment in this class is a 25 page final paper for all students (including undergrads), which is difficult. Office hours were good, readings were interesting, but that's on YOU to actually do (since there is 0 accountability for students beyond the final paper). The teaching team offers support if you ask for it. Honestly, it's a crazy class but probably not worth taking unless you want to take a class with Sen specifically.
This was a one of a kind class because it was co–taught by Professor Amartya Sen and Professor Sudhir Anand. They presented the main takeaways from influential research and scholarship on health inequalities, including intersections with gender, class, and race. It was a very interdisciplinary class, combining philosophy, economics, political science, and many other academic fields. Overall, I'm very glad I had the chance to take this class!
Amazing content, professors with an world–renowned background, but little to none teaching capacity.
