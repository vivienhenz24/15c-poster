Comments
fun class, low stakes and honestly not that much effort required. make the most of it, ask questions and talk to your classmates (everyone there likes something about language!)
This is a course requirement for linguistics concentrators so you can't really avoid it. The quality of the class really depends on the people teaching it and if their topic aligns with your interests. Anabelle was pretty cool and graded assignments quickly but Natasha was maximally disorganized and graded so slowly it was very frustrating. For the final paper, we were initially told that it was due by the end of reading period but it was instead assigned on the last day of reading period at 11:55 pm after lots of emailing and probing by several students in the class. So bottom line is that the instructor matters a lot for quality and engagement with the course. Otherwise, it is fairly chill and there is not sit–down exams which is a huge plus.
