TAKE THOROUGH NOTES!!! However, I do know the entire course of the republic and empire, which is quite impressive. It helps if you have some background knowledge of Rome.
This was an incredible class! It was definitely a significant amount of work at times with weekly quizzes, readings, two papers, and two exams, but honestly the assessments allowed me to learn and retain the material in a really deep way. Professor Coleman is an amazing instructor and her lectures are so engaging. The course really shaped my perspective on Roman history and more broadly, classics as a field. I cannot recommend the course more!!
This course is an excellent survey of the geographic, political, and social history of the Roman Empire. This is not the course for anyone seeking an easy A, but if that is what you are looking for, then you should have chosen a different college. Professor Coleman is a leader in the field with tremendous knowledge to share, and Arsen is, hands down, the finest TF I have had so far at Harvard. He is an effective lecturer, runs an excellent section, and, like Professor Coleman, is genuinely dedicated to teaching. He has been helpful not only in this course but also in helping me to chart out my academic future. We were very fortunate to have had them as a teaching team. The history of the Roman Empire is fascinating and relevant to many of the social and political issues of the 21st century.
This class is good if you're interested in history, be it as a hobby or as a concentration. You will learn a lot about how studying history actually works in an academic setting. The class might help individuals who are on the fence about their concentration (such as myself) discover what they would truly enjoy studying. Don't come in thinking this class is easy. It's a pretty hard class, and you will have to do all the readings and study hard to do well. Despite the difficulty, you will love this class, and the time spent studying will all be worth it by the end. Beware of the quizzes, as they contain lots of small details that you could easily overlook. Pay close attention in lectures and with the readings and you should be fine.
This is a course that has the potential to be one of your favorite Harvard courses, but has room to grow. As it stands, the amount of work required out of students is far too demanding to allow you to focus on other classes or activities outside of the course. If taking this class, I suggest you have an otherwise easy semester. Assignments are graded quite harshly and the class is very demanding. It is interesting, however, to learn about Roman history from Prof. Coleman, as she is an expert in the field. if Arsen is still a TF, take the course, as he is a fantastic instructor and a wonderful person.
Lectures are very interesting but fast paced, you will often be scribbling notes at full speed (no laptops). Tons of reading, some of which can be dense and dry. Overall though, very interesting class, great people, Professor Coleman and Arsen are great.
This course covers a great deal of material in a relatively small amount of time. It is not an easy course, and Professor Coleman's grading style is not gentle, although it is completely fair. It goes through all of Roman history from before the founding of Rome up until the decline and fall of the empire, and it works in analytical method and practice, archaeology, material culture, literature, and several hands on field trips. It is extremely comprehensive and fantastic preparation for more advanced Classics coursework. Because of this, I feel it is essential for anyone concentrating in Classics (regardless of what track they are on), and that is probably why it is a concentration requirement. Professor Coleman is an exceptional professor and her passion and enthusiasm is infectious. If you were previously not interested in Classics or the Ancient Roman world, there is a strong likelihood that this course will awaken some interest in you at least. The way the course is taught when Prof. Coleman teaches it relies heavily on details. She says that details provide the substance that allows us to make conclusions about life in the ancient world (or something to that effect), and she tests your knowledge of details. This means that you will come away from this course knowing a lot of details and specific information. Overall, the course is extremely rigorous which I think this is one of its greatest strengths. Professor Coleman tries to "take" her students to the most important sites for the study of the Ancient world, and so every class, she presents on a location and all of her slideshows involve art. You get a sense of the geography of the mediterranean, and by the end it feels like you've done a bit of travel. You also really learn to recognise, look at, and gain information from Roman artwork and material objects. The TF, Arsen, was a really knowledgeable section leader and he had helpful feedback on my work.
Let me just preface this comment by stating that this is not an easy class. Kathleen Coleman is definitely not an easy grader (though she is a fair one), not inflating assignments in the manner that most classes might do. While this realization was at first difficult, in hindsight, I very much appreciate this approach to teaching. I think that Coleman's approach could be described as a sort of "tough–love" –– she grades a bit more harshly not to be mean, but to spur growth. After I got a lower grade than expected on one of my assignments, I met up with her, during which she told me that she gives students grades such that inspire improvement and push one to his full potential –– an approach that inspired me to learn much more in this course than I think I otherwise would have achieved. Looking back on this course, I have fond eyes; in response to the higher expectations, all of us students formed deep bonds which I know will last far beyond this class. Coleman is also an excellent lecturer. Lectures are thematic rather than purely chronological (education, slavery, economy, etc), though she does include timelines in her lectures. She delivers lectures in an engaging and memorable manner, making studying much more streamlined. Assignments are very well thought–out and the expectations are clear (though demanding). All in all, I highly recommend this class to any student who truly wants to learn about Roman history. This course is by no means a gem, but anyone who puts in the work will enjoy this class and be glad that he took it.
