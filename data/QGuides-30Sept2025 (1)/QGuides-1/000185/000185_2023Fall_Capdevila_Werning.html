What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If you're remotely interested in architecture or design, you should take this course! I had a great experience, and learned a lot. The readings are very interesting, and it's absolutely worth putting in the time to read them thoroughly and think about them. The course has definitely broadened my understanding and enjoyment of architecture. Also, Remei is an amazing professor and cares a lot about this class and her students. I had a great time.
This class is what you make of it. Professor Capdevila–Werning is an incredible philosopher and writer, and I loved the few readings by her that she allowed us to read. Take this class if you are committed to the readings and being present in seminar. The field trip to the Gropius House was one of the highlights of my fall, and writing about it for our first paper was a really smart and engaging assignment, that grounded our philosophical discussions (which can feel removed from actual architecture) in an actual building we just visited. Also, Professor Capdevila–Werning is just one of the most caring and empathetic professors I've ever had, often starting class with a check–in –– she genuinely cares about you!
Really great course! It was super–enjoyable because it is versatile in its material and media. The readings are very interesting, and you're getting out of this class as much as you put into the material, but it is great to be a part of the discussions if you had done the readings. Overall, the professor is amazing both as a professor and a person.
If you have any interest in architecture, art history, or philosophy take the class! It's very interesting and it'll change the way you approach many disciplines.
