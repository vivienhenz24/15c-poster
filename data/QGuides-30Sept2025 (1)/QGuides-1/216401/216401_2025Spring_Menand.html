This class has the potential to be incredible. The material is FANTASTIC and more important than ever today. Prof. Menand curates really well. However, if it is structured as it was this year with lengthy student presentations each class you might not feel like you're getting the most out of seminar. The best parts are discussions and when Prof. Menand lectures –– there wasn't enough of that this year. There was also a final exam this year, which was a pretty hefty lift.
Prepare to do a lot of reading + be willing to make yourself familiar with legal jargon and court documents. Use office hours and your peers to navigate this course. It's super rewarding, relevant, and interesting, but you have to be willing to put in the work.
The content is super interesting, but the class is structured poorly. Menand knows his stuff, but he lets the students lead the class too much (e.g. presentations taking over an hour). It would probably be better as a lecture course. Additionally, the reading is super excessive and typically has no bearing on the discussion in class. But if you put the work in, you'll learn a lot. Menand leads great discussions when discussions are actually happening. You will also be confronted with a lot of interesting legal dillemas.
Enjoyed the course and learned a lot. The volume of content that we covered is impressive. Some aspects of the course were very ad hoc but overall a positive experience.
The class is lowkey hard, during the semester its presentations which feel fine but theres SO much content and Menand is not super helpful about going over everything. The final was hard. But he tries to give everyone a good grade.
While this class will introduce you to critical cases in Con Law, take this only if you are prepared to spend copious amounts of time on the readings and not hear Prof Menand actually lecture very much. This class feels very self–guided at times, especially for the final exam, which was incredibly difficult to prepare for and required an extreme amount of content memorization. All of this information is relevant and feels timely (especially as we discussed lots of Harvard legal developments) but be warned about the workload. This is not what I'd call an English class, and probably would be best taken if you have Gov experience.
If youve spent some time with con law then you will be find and definitely get a lot out of this course. If youre new to this, the course will be very difficult but at best, educational and somewhat fulfilling.
The syllabus for this course was fantastic and Prof. Menand is really excellent at facilitating discussion in seminar (probably the best instructor I've had for this). Menand is a great writer and provides super helpful feedback on written work.

The main problem with this class was that group presentations occupied about half of seminar time every week and were not very helpful except for the people doing them in a particular class.
