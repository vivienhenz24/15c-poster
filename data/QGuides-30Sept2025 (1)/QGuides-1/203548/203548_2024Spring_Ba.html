The concepts covered in this course are interesting and important (and can be applied broadly), and I enjoyed Demba's style of teaching. He is evidently very knowledgeable about the topics/field and that makes his lectures very engaging. It is a difficult course (esp leading up to the midterm) and as someone without much background in this domain, I felt like I was playing catch up a lot of the time.
Unless you have AI, CS, and substantial maths knowledge, or have a lot of time to learn, then perhaps stay away. However, if you check these boxes, then Demba is a wonderful professor, so I would recommend it.
The course notes stopped aligning with the actual lectures around lecture 9 or 10, so if you miss a class or need a better explanation for something you are kinda screwed, especially because Demba speaks so quietly it's nearly impossible to hear him on the lecture recordings, and because there are so few opportunities for section and OH's, all of which this year took place at 10:30am, when I have class every day. Essentially, there was extremely little support for the students this semester, and I felt like it was me against problem sets that I did NOT understand.

If you did alright in stat 110 and the other pre–reqs, you very well may still not be prepared. I feel very strongly that you need greater probability knowledge than Stat 110 prepares you for.
I really liked this class! There are only four psets, and most of them were not too bad. Pset 2 was definitely the hardest, but we had a full month for it. It still took MANY hours, but beyond that, the course was not excessively heavy. Furthermore, Demba is really nice, and he covers a wide variety of topics, from machine learning basics to new techniques in deep learning. If you need an Applied Math graduate elective, or you're looking for challenging material but not a crazy amount of work, take this class! But be sure to brush up on your linear algebra and probability.
This is the best machine learning class at Harvard. This class is so much better than CS 181: while CS 181 gives you a recipe book of machine learning concepts, AM 231 / ES 201 builds intuition and motivation behind these concepts. In addition. Dembas care for pedagogy massively adds to the experience of this class. Demba is easily the best lecturer I have had at Harvard!
Demba is WONDERFUL. highly recommend taking this class if you're interested in machine learning or deep learning, especially from a more conceptual perspective.
Take it!
It's a good AM200 class. Can be difficult at times, depending on your linear algebra background. Second half of the course is easier than the first and there are a few really interesting guest lectures. Demba is a solid professor who is kind and willing to help.
This is an excellent course. Past Q Guide criticism on some more dated material was really addressed this semester. Demba updates the course every year. He is also a very kind professor!
This is a class that gives you as much out as you put in. I would recommend you find a pset group/buddy to do the psets with, since they aren't always trivial. Go to section and office hours, they really help. Think about topics for your final project early, since time flies and there is much liberty in what you do, but you need to set your own benchmarks and progress check–ins, since that is all left up to the student. Study really hard in the beginning leading up to the midterm, because the class calms down immensely in the second half, but you'll need everything from the first half to do well on the midterm, and probably will use some of that in your final project.

The class is very well structured, and there is ample support for deliverables. Just make sure you're on top of content, because everything will be tested in some way (psets, midterm, final project).
