What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
The opportunity to work with the Parker Quartet is amazing. That being said, you only have one class for rehearsal other than the final performance. As such, don't make your composition too difficult to play with lots of new symbols.

Experience with music is needed, as the class doesn't teach you music theory or counterpoint. As a non–music (CS) major, I learned those on my own reading from textbooks and YouTube videos.

You get as much as you put in! I didn't have time to get feedback and have one–on–one meetings with the professors that much during the semester.
I'd highly recommend this class! Especially if you're interested in composing for string instruments/quartet.
This is a really fun class. It really is low pressure and now stressful at all. It allows you to explore all the ideas of what composition is and you get from it what you put into it. Making the final song is a lot of fun and having the Parker Quartet perform it and work with them is a huge privilege. Take this class if you really want the opportunity to compose a song for a quartet, because that is the main point of the class. In terms of learning a lot about music theory and how it supports composition that is not really the emphasis of the course so just have that in mind.
