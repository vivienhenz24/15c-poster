This course is the best biology course I have taken so far (I am a sophomore). I went it into to it having a vague understanding of cancer but coming out of it, I feel like I know so much about various aspects of cancer and cancer research. Definitely a good MCB class to take! I enjoyed the homework assignments (not too brutal, always stimulating) and the course staff is very helpful. I will say that the course staff was a bit disorganized/the office hours are kind of short, but getting into the rhythm of the semester is helpful.
This course is a super manageable option to fulfill an intermediate biology requirement for MCB/CPB. If you are familiar with cancer biology or have significant wet lab research experience, it will likely feel boring at times, but there are ample opportunities to explore optional readings to enhance your learning in the classroom. Course staff are very understanding and helpful, and the workshop component of the course is a nice way to take a break from long lectures. In my opinion, very little of the content from workshops and papers makes its way onto exams, but these course components (along with assignments) are great at familiarizing you with the course concepts you'll end up using on the exam. All in all, this course might not be the best option for you if you're interested in a super rigorous biology course, but it's great if you're looking to fill the requirement or are looking to dive into cancer biology for the first time!
This course is very interesting and dives deep into the cancer research world. Take this course if you hope to learn about the history of cancer research and the biology of how cancer manifests due to mutations in the body. The teaching team is very supportive and makes this class a very good learning environment.
MCB 66 is an amazing class!! You should definitely take this course! Sam is a great instructor, and the lectures are very easy to follow. The concepts are very interesting, and the course also teaches you how to read papers, interpret graphs, and analyze the data. The sections are very helpful in discussing papers, and the sections help a lot when answering the problem set questions. The grading is very fair, and the TFs are very approachable and super supportive. The exams are not made to trick you, and all the questions and concepts asked in the exams are discussed in class. Overall, it is a great course with an amazing course staff who is there to support you and guide you through the course! Good luck:)
