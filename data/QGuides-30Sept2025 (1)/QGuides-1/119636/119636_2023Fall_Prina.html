What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
As a non–AFVS concentrator, it was amazing to meet students from this department that I would have otherwise likely not met. The teaching staff is very supportive and I learned a lot through their feedback. I also loved learning about other people's work when they held presentations. I definitely challenged myself with the project I took on for the class and I am very happy with the result.
If you are looking for critique, do not take this course. If you are looking for structure, do not take this course. If you are looking for instruction, do not take this course.

If you are looking for a self–driven, wholehearted way of working on your studio practice and personal artistic goals, take this course.

In other words: do not take this course if you are looking to learn in a more structured way (e.g. learning how to sculpt and receiving critical feedback on your sculpture). Take this course if you want to be able to make something, receive credit for it, and hear what Stephen Prina finds positive about it.
Be ready for the two long presentations.
