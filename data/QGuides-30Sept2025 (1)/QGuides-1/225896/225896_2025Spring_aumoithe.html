What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class is very interesting for those wanting to learn about the intersection of race and public health. BUT the bi–weekly quizzes, midterm, final paper, in–class debate, in–class presentation, and weekly section for such a small class got to be overwhelming. Many of the assignment were quite vague or were given detail the day before. Interesting class and I hope it organizes itself better in the future.
Take this course if you are interested in how responses to pandemics evolve over time, have a general interest in history, or are interested in how to articulate social determinants of health or how race impacts pandemic response. Allocate proper time in your schedule to do the readings and adequate study for the quizzes, as they are a substantial part of the course and your assessments. Very interesting course with great professors!
I think every public health student should take this class. So much of what we are taught in public health school is presented without much or any historical context, and this course was an incredible helpful corrective to that pedagogical practice. Also it was an honor to take a class from an amazing team of public health historians who did a great job of selecting excellent readings and other source material.
Make sure you are ready for harsh grading and make sure that you put in more effort than a lot of other histsci classes.
The course instructors dont seem to have a solid grip on what they want this course to be which led to a lot of confusion and frustration among the students in the course. I would not recommend this course if you are just starting out with the content matter, this course is better suited for committed AAAS concentrators
