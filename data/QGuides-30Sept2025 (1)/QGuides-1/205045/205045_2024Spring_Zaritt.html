I took this course with partly because I wanted to better understand my own Jewishness. It definitely helped me in that way.
Take this course if you have an interest in Seinfeld, Curb, or similar. The course does a great job at putting into perspective the way this type of humor has evolved and manifested. There isn't that much work, which does allow you to spend more time thinking about the few assignments that you actually do have!
This course is interesting if you like comedy. The course staff is very nice and cares about the students! Sometimes the lectures and readings were confusing and felt difficult to follow.
This is a great way to learn the theory behind some cultural favorites, and, if you are Jewish, see your background in a new light. The materials are intriguing, though the Freudian theory is challenging, the course is expertly taught, and the workload is manageable. My only note is that sometimes discussions about women and the female Jewish body took on strange tones, which might be difficult at times, especially now – not a reason to not take this course, as I am almost certain it was attributable to literary analysis, but do not be surprised by it.
This class is very theoretical. If you want to take this class because you want to watch your favorite comedians, you're probably better off skipping. You will spend the first several weeks working on theory and then even when you watch comedians, much of the class is about analyzing why the jokes are actually cruel.
Take this course! The workload is very light, and you just get to watch comedy specials and read articles about comedy for homework. The lectures/section can be a bit dull, but totally worth it, especially if taken with friends.
This was a super fun class! The readings are fun (lots of TV shows and stand–up) and the worklaod is not too much. Professor Zaritt is definitely a character and lectures were sometimes very entertaining. You also get to do a creative final project, and the professor and TF put on a comedy night at the end of the semester to showcase the projects which is really fun!
This class would be antisemitic if it was not taught a a Jew and even so it is still a little bit off. This class was just not funny. The assignments were fun though.
This class was honestly kind of a disappointment for me. I had really high expectations for learning more about the mechanics of humor and getting a better cultural appreciation for Jewish humor. Unfortunately, the lectures were extremely dry, there was basically no discssion, and the professor clearly has a specific lens he wants to view Jewish humor through that makes it very difficult for students to actually provide their own thoughts. It's fairly little work and the material was funny at times, but you can just watch Seinfeld on your own and not really miss out on too much.
Super interesting class if you like comedy and want to learn more about its mechanics. Plus great if youre Jewish to learn more about humors role in your culture!
A really fun and interesting course, with not too much work and great final project options.
I think this class studies a somewhat niche subject, but it's worth it – especially if you already have an interest in the material or are generally interested in comedic theory. The readings can be a little dense at times but I think it's balanced out with the variety of authors and clips to watch, and the assignments are fairly fun to work on and play around with.
Take the course if you have any interest in the subject. It's pretty easy overall, but if you have no interest in Jewish humor you're just going to be bored out of your mind in section, lecture, and when doing readings/pre–lecture materials. Prof Zaritt was great at leading the course, answering questions, and keeping discussions productive. Course staff were very flexible with stuff in general which is great. Don't take this course thinking you'll be laughing the whole time, though. You spend the majority of class breaking down how humor and jokes work, so you're basically ruining the humor of it. But, I can still say I laugh at stand–up and movies afterwards.
This is an interesting class. The first half is a little bit of a slog to get through the theoretical portions, but the second half is really fun and interesting. Would recommend.
While in theory this course sounds amazing, it unfortunately did not stick the landing for me. The syllabus and course material made for a revolutionary college course for me as a Jewish person interested in comedy, but the teaching of the material did not do it for me unfortunately :/ definitely shop it and if you like the style take it, but don't force it
