What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This course can be a bit grueling at times it is heavy in literary criticism/theory readings, and part of our section's weekly assignments were to summarize the structure and arguments of most of the papers we had, which was difficult at times because of longer page lengths. However, the somewhat tedious work does pay off, as I developed substantially as a reader of literary theory in this course, and am much more confident/literate on the subject. Your experience in sophomore tutorial will also very much depend on who your professor is.
This class was extremely difficult to sit through. I wish student voices had been more actively sought and discussion facilitated.
Please try to take the other sophomore tutorial, if you can
Professor Donoghue is a caring and diligent professor who gives insightful comments on your work. Some of the readings can be hard to get through, though it is important to read them carefully/beforehand if you're hoping to get something meaningful out of the course.
Take it! Donoghue is very kind, and if youre an English concentrator, it is a privilege to get to know him and spend time learning from him. I hope to take more of his courses while Im a student here
This class can be really tough to be honest. The final paper is no joke. You will find yourself spending hours thinking through just a couple sentences and meticulously changing the order of words or the words themselves. You can get through it, though! Just use your brain and follow your gut.
Be willing to read, discuss, and write!! The most I got out of this course was through participation and taking time to really grasp the concepts we were learning –– they're actually very interesting (critical theory, different writing styles, etc.).
The 3 hours stretches of class were kind of brutal, and a lot of it became tedious and hard to concentrate because we didn't really get breaks. If oyu can get over that, there is decent discussion of literary criticism, and writing the sophomore tutorial paper alone really helps with teaching how to do research and in–depth analysis. I like how self–driven this course was, and Professor Donoghue always made himself available for us.
