There are not classes like this usually at Harvard with such an impressive guest professor with such a robust background exposing students to a niche at the intersection of art, architecture, and politics while inviting them to participate. This class integrates urban planning, architecture, politics, installation art, and theory, and is a very interesting class that allows for discussion and growth.
PLEASE TAKE THIS CLASS!!! This was hands down my favorite course Ive taken here. Prof. Jordan is one of the best professors I've had at the collegehis depth of knowledge about architecture and design, along with his global experiences in the field, made the course incredibly interesting. He is also so kind, thoughtful in the way he teaches, and intentional about the structure of the class. The discussions were also amazing! Everyone brought unique perspectives from their diverse backgrounds, which made our conversations about designing Black spaces and Blackness in general so nuanced and thought–provoking.
