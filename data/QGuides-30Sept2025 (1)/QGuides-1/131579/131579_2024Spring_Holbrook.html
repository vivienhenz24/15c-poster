Great class! If you're interested in plant biology, this is a great introduction with relatively low expectations and a lot of optional but very interesting content. The lectures cover a ton of material but you're ultimately tested only on a small subset. You can get as much or as little out of the class as you want! It's a great, low–stress bio class
Very fun class. The work is straightforward, grading is fair, and assignments chill
This is a lovely and interesting class. Your satisfaction will depend on what you put into the course. I sometimes felt that expectations were not clearly set, and labs swung from having too much work to finish in time to leaving an hour early. However, I do not regret taking this class and learning more about plant evolution.
As a junior in OEB, this was perhaps my favorite class I have taken in the last 3 years. Lectures are super interesting and provide a well–balanced, relevant and holistic overview of plant biology. Missy and Elena are so friendly and enthusiastic about the course material and are incredibly knowledgeable about most aspects of plant biology. The field trips provide great opportunities to get outside and witness some of the course themes in action. Definitely recommend taking it if you have any interest in plants or evolutionary biology.
This is an AMAZING class, and one of those rare classes that build great community. Missy and Elena, as well as the TFs are all super nice. We also have food–related SNACKS every single class, and at least two field trips which enable you to know your classmates, and get to know the resources Harvard offer at the Harvard Forest and Arnold Arboretum. The labs are also pretty chill and hands–on –– Daniel is such a great TF who is really enthusiastic about plants and helped us explain anything we don't understand. The quizzes are not so hard and if you spend around 30 minutes reviewing you will be fine, and there are many opportunities to get extra credits. Please take the class if you have any interest in plant biology!!
I highly recommend this class! Missy and Elena make the material accessible and fun, and the field trips and snacks are amazing. The workload is reasonable, with in–class quizzes (that aren't that bad) and a pset due once every two weeks. Labs can be a tiny bit rushed, but the TFs are super chill so you'll be ok.
Study everything on the slides. Take homes are terribly difficult and due biweekly. Final relatively okay would make sure to study everything on the slide material as they asked questions not on the study guide. Would also memorize the tree.
This class has a lot of assignments. It will take up more time than you think. The content can be interesting and the professors definitely generate enthusiasm for the course material, but be prepared to put in work.
I would go to office hours for the take home assignments, even if you feel confident answering them. They tend to look for very specific answers and you'll save so much time working with the TFs or professors.
This is a fantastic class, especially if you go to lectures. The teaching staff is great, and lab is really fun. I highly recommend for anyone, even just for fun.
It's a really lovely course, 9/10. Taught by knowledgable and genuinely enjoyable professors. The field trips are excellent, the lectures are really enjoyable, and plants truly are wonderful. Completely and utterly different from any biology you might be familiar with, but ubiquitous in our lives. Course moves rapidly, lots of vocabulary, and lab is a little disappointing because the registrar cut the length this year. Topical snacks in the middle of every lecture are a nice bonus.
TAKE THIS CLASS. If you are a plant person, this course will be one of the best things that ever happened to you. Even as a non–STEM concentrator, I felt like all the material was very manageable and relatively easy to understand (although you may need to brush up on your high–school bio), and the teaching staff was great. Take homes can be a bit time consuming, but just make sure to over explain yourself and you'll be fine. Field trips and lab were very enjoyable!!
This is my first and only science class in college and it was very manageable! I would encourage non–STEM majors to take this!
This class is so interesting, fun, and friendly. Would highly recommend for any concentrators or non–concentrators looking for electives! The field trips are great and you will leave with the ability to see more cool stuff in the world around you :)
