Pretty fun since you get to propose a personal project to develop as your final, and you can request the supplies youll need. It can be disorganized at times, but nothing overbearing.
I have heard bad things about most sophomore tutorials for AFVS, but Harper's class was actually very good. If you get the chance to have this class with her, I will definitely recommend it.
Sharon is... interesting. Be prepared to take feedback given by her with a grain of salt and know that you may get extremely frustrated by there being no syllabus and her writing it/updating it as the semester goes on. Your time may not be respected and you'll have to be flexible if the experiential learning approach of this class continues. She picks favorites and frequently disrespects those whom she doesn't take a particular liking to (for no apparent reason). In critiques, she even compares students to one another...which is simply unprofessional. The class itself is fine and you gain a lot from going to different talks/watching different films–– the instructor just makes things unenjoyable at times and is overly–particular, passive–aggressive, critical, and sassy about everything.
Go in with an open mind and explore!!
This class provides a really great opportunity to try out different mediums and explore different areas and resources of the AFVS department. However, the class itself is extremely unstructured, and we had multiple classes where we went over time / started at different times, and we weren't told that there was a section until the day before the first section, causing scheduling issues. You can't get out of taking the class, so I'd say use and learn from the resources and use the class as a way to both explore and meet other people within the department. It was really nice to be able to develop and explore a project and topic on my own, but I wish there was more support and less "busy–work" time–fill–in events and "show and tell" discussions.
Expect this course to be very unorganized. There's not enough set curriculum to ensure that the professor teaching the course is actually able to lead the course in a productive direction. If you're happy to just work on your own and use the class primarily for funding, studio space, and gear you'll be fine.
This class is horribly organized. It regularly ran over time (sometimes by 1.5 hrs!). We didn't receive a full syllabus until well into the semester. Even then, it would change on a dime. The students are great!
