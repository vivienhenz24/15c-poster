You get a lot of freedom for the final project, but you really want to work on it outside of class. Most of class feels like just talking about readings and artist talks from section, so if you want to try out new mediums you kind of have to do that on your own
This course is not meant for seniors and I am disappointed it has not been modified to accommodate seniors. It is not a good use of time for anyone who has already spent multiple years in the AFVS department.
Dont look at this report go dig into the professor and their other course evals.
Overall, one of the more stress free courses in the AFVS department! Very open assignments and you can put in as much (or as little) as you want. Mostly, get to know the other students (especially those you haven't taken courses with before). Use course time to explore different ways of making. Use the course budget!
i think this class needs a lot of reform
just be prepared for it to be very chill in some aspects and not chill in others. Like creatively its very interesting and exciting, but then randomly there's the anxiety that if you miss a class your grade will tank. Idk, just stuff like that. overall enjoyable and chill, but expect to make your own timelines and deadlines. It's four assignments, but the how, when, where, and why of them is completely up to you. Also, class periods sometimes (NOT ALWAYS!) feel very much like "this could have been an email," so just brace yourself for that.
I remember reading in the Q before taking this class that you'll get out of it entirely what you put in, and to use it as an opportunity to make projects you're passionate about. I went into this class with that mindset, and I feel I made some projects I'm really very proud of, and for that reason, am more glad I took this class than didn't take it. BUT. I found the use of class time frustratingly empty and often so, so unrelated to my artistic interests that I was really left wondering why I was in this course beyond the requirement. If you're taking this, you're an AFVS major so you don't really have another option, but I encourage you to try to put your all into the projects and make something that you can look back on and be proud of and have grown from b/c otherwise there isn't much to gain from this course at all, I feel.
Dont think too much on the projects for each assignment. Go on the trip to nyc if theyre still doing it. Start all projects early.
I fear if you want to be an AFVS concentrator, you are required to take this class. It is a nice opportunity to bond and meet other AFVS concentrators, as well as explore with mediums of your choice! You get a budget to order materials! However, the course itself depends a lot on the instructor, who changes every year. The class included 3 studio projects, weekly "experiencing"attendance to guest speaker lectures, film screenings, or gallery walksand reflection discussions both as posts in canva and in class. (and meditation sessions but I think that was just this year's professor's methodology). Overall, the class was unhinged, fun, and unpredictable! Very artistic!
