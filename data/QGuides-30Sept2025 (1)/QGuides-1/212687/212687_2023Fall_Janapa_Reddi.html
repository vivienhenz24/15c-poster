Vijay and the TFs are awesome! Would recommend taking this class if you have any interest in ML and/or hardware.
This class is much more about practical ML than about ML on tiny devices – while a lot of the lectures emphasize this setting, much of the take–aways are applicable to any–scale applications. It's a good place to gain a diverse perspective on what it takes to apply ML in real–life and the second half of the lectures is particularly interesting with plenty of interesting people being invited to give a talk (many of whom are industry leading experts).
This course offers a valuable foundation for students with an interest in the convergence of Machine Learning (ML) and Hardware. Although the curriculum is likely to evolve annually, students can anticipate a comprehensive overview of various topics without delving too deeply into any single area. The Teaching Assistants and Fellows were consistently supportive and effective in addressing challenges we encountered. Additionally, VJ stands out as an excellent lecturer who is both knowledgeable and approachable.

In summary, regardless of their existing proficiency in Computer Science, Hardware, or Machine Learning, every student can expect to gain meaningful insights and knowledge from this class.
This was a great class. While there is not time to learn each area of TinyML in it's full depth, I felt it gave an excellent overview of all components and provided us with enough resources to refer back to if we need to gain more expertise in one specific area. I feel as though I have a good understanding of when and where TinyML would be helpful in my research and have gained the skills needed to be able to read and analyze papers in this area to learn more.
Definitely apply for this class! It is very manageable and worthwhile. Professor Janapa Reddi is a great instructor who is passionate about the subject matter. The teaching staff are very accessible and care a lot about making sure everyone is having a good learning experience. I cannot underscore enough, this class is so fairly graded – the work isn't too heavy, so if you put in the effort you will reap the rewards. The project ends up being a bit rushed but the teaching staff are supportive. There can be a bit of busy work at times between paper reading, chapter contributions (this year we helped Professor Janapa Reddi compile a textbook – not sure how it will look next year), chapter peer reviews, homework assignments, and then the final project – but generally it's manageable. It's a unique lens into a cutting edge field. There isn't much programming to be honest which can be good or bad – good for making workload easier, but a bit unfortunate for depriving us of the opportunity to learn some of the technical fundamentals underneath the black box of Edge Impulse. Overall, highly recommend!
CS181 or 182 can't even hold a candle to CS249r or the amount of passion VJ and the TFs put into the lectures or course materials. From building real ML systems to contributing to a state–of–the–art ML Systems online textbook to learning about cutting–edge research and application of TinyML, this course has so much to offer. Best ML course at Harvard. It's not even a comparison.
Definitely take this course!! Compared to most of the other ML courses at Harvard, this class actually teaches you to apply ML concepts to the real world. The workload is very manageable, with a few assignments throughout the semester and a final project. The papers are very relevant to current developments in TinyML and ML systems. Writing a textbook chapter on ML systems also helped improve my own understanding of the field. Overall, a great course that you can get a lot out of, especially when you design a final project of your choice.
Though well–intentioned, this class was poorly organized and unnecessarily time–consuming, with unclear expectations and inconsistent communication from the teaching team. I would only recommend this class if you already care deeply about TinyML or if you're trying to fulfill a Computer Science systems requirement without writing tons of code. Also, I'd want future students to know that the 2023 version of the class sprung a lot of unexpected disturbing content on us (such as showing hateful, sexual or violent images produced by generative AI models without any content warning).
Great class that you won't regret taking!
