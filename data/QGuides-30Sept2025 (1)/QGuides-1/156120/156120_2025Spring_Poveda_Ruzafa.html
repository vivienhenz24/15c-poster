Comments
This is a wonderful course, and it is a shame that it is not offered every year. Alejandro is a wonderful lecturer who is only there to help you. The material itself is challenging, but the psets are manageable and the grading is lenient, since Alejandro is most interested in you learning the material, rather than particularly struggling. If you have even the slightest interest in set theory, I highly recommend this course, but even if not, if you are interested in immersing yourself in a fascinating math course, I feel that this is a must take, when offered.
Really interesting, really hard! I think pset problems were written without a TON of care, so sometimes there were problems that we totally weren't prepared for, which was stressful. Be prepared to use OH, read external texts, ask your friends, etc. You also get resubmits and the grading (and everything about the class) was really chill so it wasn't hard in that sense. I didn't find it to be super well taught but I did think the content was cool!
This class was AMAZING under Professor Poveda but he is leaving :( Be warned that it has the potential to be wildly difficult under anybody else, but the content is sincerely extraordinary.
This is an excellent class! If you're interested in the underpinnings of mathematics, I'd highly recommend.
Alejandro is a legend. I don't know if I would recommend this course if he's not teaching it. The material is very interesting but sort of removed from the rest of the mathematical sequence here. I thought this class blew Math 55 out of the water in difficulty, but YMMV (and I didn't take 145A).

I would suggest considering any class Alejandro teaches if you have the background– he is a wonderful lecturer and just generally a wonderful person.
Unfortunately, this course, in its current structure, is not a great introduction to forcingAlejandro is an *excellent* lecturer, but his Homework assignments were not built off of the lectures at all, and the relaxed grading allowed me to get away with a perfect score in the course without *truly* understanding at *all* what forcing is. This should not be your first course in set theory, and a background at the level of Math 145a is *certainly* necessary, especially having a bit of exposure to large cardinals. If the assignments are changed, if more applications are discussed during lecture, and if the Course Assistant is held to a more rigorous standard, this could be a *very* successful course, especially as Alejandro is an organized lecturer who cares a great deal about his students!
