I'm happy I took this class. Kristie is a great teacher and I learned so much. Her method of 'productive struggling' sounded scary at first, but by the end of the semester, it was crazy to realize how much I'd learned and was able to do independently on QGIS. I'm leaving this class with a critical eye for maps and a newfound appreciation for spatial visualization!
I'm so glad I took this course. QGIS is a great software to learn and this is an extremely supportive learning environment to do so. Kristie is the best. The small seminar style is great for getting more instructor help and also creates a classroom environment where everyone is much more open to leaning on each other for support. I highly recommend.
Professor LeBeau is amazing!! Super fun, understanding, and cares for all her students.
Take this class! Kristie is an incredibly caring, passionate professor. You might have some moments of struggling with the labs when you are doing independent work, but that struggle helps you learn the material so much better, and I felt so equipped by the end of the class to work individually with the software and to work on my own projects outside of the scope of the class. Also, Kristie is always available for office hours and incredibly willing to guide you through things that you struggle with in a way that is helpful and productive. That being said, going to office hours is really important, and I would encourage future students to plan time on labs wisely around the office hours.
Take this class!!! It is really interesting and fun. Kristie is great instructor. You will definitely have to dedicate a decent number of hours outside of class to complete assignments though.
Take this course if you have any interest in maps, spatial data, and/or responsible data visualization! All social phenomena are spatially situated. Maps are both extremely common in our day–to–day life, and extremely effective at representing complex social data. This class will give you skills in both creating effective maps and being a critical consumer of other maps you encounter. Its a really useful set of skills!
Dr. Kristie is approachable, passionate, and down–to earth. Don't be late to class though. Picking up a new skill is never easy, and productive struggling can make it even more difficult. However, by the end of the course, I was able to create maps without even needing to reference my lab manual. I recommend going to office hours and starting early to give yourself enough time to ask questions.
It would be beneficial if future iterations of the course had at least one dedicated section to work on coding, even if optional, outside of office hours. This could help prevent unnecessary struggles. That said, as this was the first run of the course, there were some challenges, but overall it was a valuable experience. Once Dr. Kristie and the department find the right balance between independent work and support, the course will be even better. It's wonderful that we have this opportunity within the Soc department.
