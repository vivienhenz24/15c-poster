What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Expos 10 is great if you're not confident in your writing skills. It develops them from scratch, going more complex throughout the semester. There are a lot of exercises that helps you start, helps you move on when you feel stuck. Definitely recommend it for international students, independent of desired concentration.
This is such an interesting class! Sections are so fun to listen and discuss in. Participate! It is genuinely helpful to understand if you are going in the right tracks, or even bring new ideas to the table. Do your readings and be prepared for meetings with your preceptor. Read the feedback you get before the meetings, take notes about the questions you have, and take notes during the meeting itself. There is no wrong questions (as cliche as it sounds). The writing is complicated itself (gotta be sophisticated, and they are usually wrong so ill suggest a full day to write them), so engage and use the resources to not make it more difficult for yourself. Have fun!
Class is solid and isn't very time consuming. If you participate in class and get assignments in on time you should have no problem succeeding. Definitely don't underestimate the three papers because they do expect a lot out of you. Also the conferences with the professor are very helpful so making the most out of them is important.
This class is a great opportunity to get introduced to college writing in a no–pressure environment. The preceptor is very friendly, and your classmates are there to learn with you, not compete.
