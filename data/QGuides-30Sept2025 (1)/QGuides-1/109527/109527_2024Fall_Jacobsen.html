What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
While the lectures are pretty dry, try your best to absorb as much as you can! The material is actually pretty interesting and your appreciation of the field trip will be that much more if you try to understand the background before going. The trip itself is super fun and a great chance to learn some field geology skills.
The strengths of this course was its hands–on learning during Friday labs and dissecting mineral composition data for our final projects. A clear highlight is the trip to Norway. I am a person who learns a lot more when I am actively engaged, and I would say I learned so much more during the trip than what I learned in lecture. Even though Stein is knowledgable about the subject, his presentation and delivery of lecture materials leave much more to be desired. He has an extremely dry teaching style. He is very monotonous and that's because he is mostly reading off of his incredibly text dense slides. These slides are useful and are really big resources, but it is not good for presentation. If you want a class that will keep you engaged, this is not the one. If you want to learn more about minerals and petrology, I would recommend other EPS classes. Psets are tedious with excel but not difficult. They feel unrelated to lecture material, but they help us learn skills/calculations that may be useful in practice. The lab is fun because you get to look at cool rocks.
Please for the love of god take EPS 54 before this – otherwise you will walk away with very little. Also, the field trip is amazing and is definitely the part that I learned the most from, so make sure that you are taking this on a field trip year, but please do not only take this class for the field trip and waste time the rest of the semester.
This class is really worth it if you have existing interest in rocks and minerals, even if you have no prior experience although I'd recommend having some sort of chemistry/EPS background. The trip was amazing (but might not be offered every year so make sure) and mostly consists of driving to different sites and looking at rocks all day which might not be for everyone but I loved it. The content is almost necessary to learn in the field which may be partly why the lectures are not very engaging or helpful.
I really enjoyed EPS 110, but partially because I went into it with the expectation that there were many aspects of the course which I wouldn't fully enjoy. I am not super interested in mineralogy, but pretty much enjoyed every other aspect of the class. Stein and Anna are lovely and the field trip was INCREDIBLE! It is a field trip of entirely rocks, and i'm not sure the class would be as good without it... The community built by going on the field trip together was wonderful and the psets (while tricky) were not that bad at all and loved the no–exams nature of the class. Mixed review, but I am glad I took the class.
