Overall, I enjoyed this class. Fiery was a fantastic lecturer (I saw another Q–guide saying his lectures sound like a podcast, which I totally agree with). The topics he talks about are interesting and diverse ranging from more technical in the beginning (e.g., model–based vs. model–free learning ) to more philosophical/social (although still technical) in the end (e.g., moral judgments).

I think this course could be improved by providing more clear guidelines about exams. Practice exams would have been nice, but I understand those can be hard for a course like this one because they might leave some ideas excluded. However, more clear guidelines about exam aspects such as the number of questions (which wasn't accurately provided to us before the midterm) would've been helpful. I also think the lectures lean very experiment–heavy, which makes sense, but it was hard to see these experiments in the aggregate and understand how to synthesize everything/memorize it all/know what to memorize before exams. Also, exams were mostly fair but would have some nit–picky textbook/lecture questions which would, at least, trip me up, as someone who read all of the textbook, watched all the lectures, and took extensive notes on both. But exams are graded on a curve (although final course grade isn't), which is nice.

Also, section was great. Srijani (my TF) did an amazing job reinforcing the topics taught to us in lecture, and it was very clear that she cared about our understanding/learning.
