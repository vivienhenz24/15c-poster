This class, no matter what it says in the title, is NOT a music class. I entered into this as a music concentrator with background in music technology feeling very confident that it would be a fun and easy class for me, where I would learn some new stuff, but not be particularly challenged (after all, it was a GenEd.) I was wrong. A more accurate description of this class is an engineering class taught by a guy who likes to play guitar. That's literally it. While Rob Wood, the course head, seems to be a great guy, his lectures are boring and intensely math–heavy. You may think that you have enough math background to deal with the content (so did I) but unless you understand advanced multivariable calculus, take it from me: you don't. Fortunately, you won't be tested on any of that stuff, but you will have to sit through the twice–weekly lectures, with Rob apologizing profusely for the dryness of the material, but doing nothing to actually change anything about it. As for the labs, they will vary greatly depending on your TF. Some of the TFs for this class are really organized, super knowledgeable, and willing to help. Others have no idea what they're doing and seem to want to be literally anywhere than in that lab, teaching that class. The lab work is not easy, either – especially if you don't have a strong STEM background, or if you're an Arts/Humanities student. The projects take a lot of dedication and time (way more than what is expected from most GenEds) and aren't very rewarding, either. I know this class sounds cool, but please, please stand back and think very hard before taking it. If you're into engineering, you'll love it. Anyone else: you've been warned. Music students, especially: don't expect a nice easy music GenEd, because that's not what you're going to get.
