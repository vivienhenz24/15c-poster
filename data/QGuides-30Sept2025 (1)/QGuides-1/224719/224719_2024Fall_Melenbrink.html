TAKE THIS CLASS!!! Its so fun to work with your team to build a boat (you get to keep it). While it is a lot of hands–on/physical work, it's so rewarding. You also learn a lot about boats in general in this class.
MAKE THE MOST OF THIS COURSE. It is incredibly competitive to get into so if you do get in, obviously be grateful and MAKE THE MOST OF IT.
This class will be your favorite i promise!
This is literally one of my favorite classes coming into freshman year. Melenbrink and Bobby are absolutely amazing and you will not regret this class at all. There is almost no work outside of class, so it means its the one class in your probably very heavy load that is actually relaxing. Its incredibly fun, you get to keep the boat and literally the building process is just so therapeutic and fun. There is definitely a video online of us just testing it in the pool(that's our first test, our second is at the river itself), which is called something like "Harvard Physics Students in the Pool?"
This course is as hands–on as Harvard gets. You literally build a functioning boat from scratch. Classes are three hours in the warehouse – get ready to use a lot of epoxy and power tools. You work in groups of four, and each group takes the canoe plans provided and can change anything (dimensions, style, shape) according to their preferences. At the end of the semester, you take the canoes out on the Charles. A great class, super fun, very chill.
This is an amazing seminar! There is little homework outside of class, only a few readings that are interesting. All the work is completed within the class hours, and it is very cool to see the end results with the canoe fully built! Also, you will be able to use the canoe once the class ends, which is awesome since you can take your friends across the Charles.
This class is rad!
This is a very fun and engaging course, you will get out of it what you put into it. It is very similar to a woodworking class except you get more control on the intricacies of what you are designing. If you enjoy building stuff and want a course which you will be excited about actively participating in I highly recommend taking this course.
