What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I looked forward to this class every day. My Japanese has improved so much, I'm so grateful to Asakura sensei and Nakatani sensei.
Be ready to invest a lot of time into the class. The more work you do, the better you will get; but this course tends to balloon out of proportion with its requirements and assignments.
Japanese 140 will allow you to improve so much! Compared to previous Japanese classes, it's less busywork and allows you to focus on actually knowing the grammar and readings. As always, the workload is a lot, but definitely better than the previous classes. Also as always, the senseis are incredibly kind and the classroom environment is very enjoyable. You'll also get so much helpful knowledge about language in context, e.g., how to write a formal email. In conclusion, I would highly recommend Japanese 140!
This class is great, the senseis are great, but be prepared to put in the work! This class requires consistent work, as there are quite a few pieces of homework, quizzes, etc. and there is also a final essay to write. The kanji also gets pretty hard, especially for some of the texts in this class.
I think every course in Harvard's Japanese department is incredibly well–taught, and the teaching staff are all wonderful. If you enjoyed Takehara Sensei's class, and/or Yagi Sensei's class, then I believe you will love this one as well. I have more fun in this class than any other course at Harvard.
This class is quite difficult and time–consuming, but because it meets every day, you can almost sit along for the ride. Just put your full effort into each class, and your Japanese will improve.
