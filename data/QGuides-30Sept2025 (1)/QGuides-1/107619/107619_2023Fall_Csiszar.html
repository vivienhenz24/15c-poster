This is a very solid histsci elective and chill class. If you've ever wondered about how scientific knowledge becomes public, why scientific papers are terribly written and locked behind a paywall –– and how law, the internet, misinformation, etc. are shaping all that –– this is the right class. Among many other things, you will come out of it realizing how ridiculously how screwed up scientific publishing is.

For a seminar, the readings are relatively short and often super interesting. Sometimes discussion can get a little slow depending on whether people have done the reading, but overall it was still solid. And luckily, Professor Csiszar has great things to say about all the readings. He's also super helpful with research papers (and brought surprisingly good homemade chai tea to class for us a few times!!).
This course is great! Please take it!! Prof Csiszar is super knowledgeable, very enthusiastic, and really kind. He clearly cares about his students and the subject matter at hand. This course wasn't a super big stressor (other than please start the final project before the deadline is hitting you in the face) but you learn a lot and get a lot out of the class discussions. Definitely work taking and thinking about!! The subject matter is broader and deeper than you might first imagine when thinking about science communication, and that's a great thing â€” you learn so much!
This course is very interesting. It is more theoretical than I expected (we interrogated questions such as "what is a public?" and other similarly abstract concepts), but that was not necessarily a bad thing! Professor Csiszar is obviously extremely knowledgable and passionate about the history of science, and that came across in the expertise he displayed during his mini lectures each week. He was also very caring and flexible, which I really appreciated.
Professor Csiszar provides clear and constructive feedback, so don't hesitate to actively seek improvement. Engage in class discussions, collaborate with peers, and view this as an opportunity to not only enhance your communication skills but also to develop a deeper appreciation for the role of effective science communication in our world.
If you are a histsci concentrator or are interested in histsci more broadly, this is a perfect course! You can be at any point in your harvard journey or histsci arc and still feel capable to succeed in this class. Professor Ciszar is incredibly enthusiastic and knowledgeable about histsci topics, and I learned so much from him! I highly recommend this class.
I'd definitely take it! It's very interesting and Professor Csiszar facilitates discussion really well and is also incredibly supportive.
