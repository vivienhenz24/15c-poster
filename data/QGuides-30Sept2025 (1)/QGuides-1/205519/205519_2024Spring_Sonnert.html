This is a great class to take to fulfill the science divisional distribution requirement, as there are no labs and the assignments are very straightforward and fairly easy. The class is built on group activities and discussions, so there is no pressure to have background knowledge or prepare extensively beforehand. Overall, a really pleasant and enjoyable class!

This course is decidedly a gem. It's fun and easy, but it really is a waste of space if you're taking it as one of your four courses. I took this as a fifth class just for kicks, and I could enjoy it because I had intellectual fulfillment through my other classes. Add this class as a GPA boost or a thought–provoking extra course, but don't expect to learn too much from it. The main drawbacks of the course are that he assignments are relatively unstructured (perhaps on purpose?) and that it meets at 9 AM.
I was hesitant about the topic at first, but I am so glad that I chose to go through with it. Professor Sonnert is so kind and passionate about the topic, and he seemed genuinely happy to watch students from so many different academic backgrounds develop their own interests and have meaningful conversations about the state and future of space exploration. As a person who finds the topic of space to be moderately fascinating but isn't all that into the search for extraterrestrial life or similar pursuits, I certainly found myself in discussion with people on the opposite end of the interest spectrum, but I was not alone in my beliefs either. This was a great way to fulfill a divisional distribution requirement, and it is a class I will always remember!
