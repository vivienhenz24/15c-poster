I have a much better appreciation for the discipline of mathematics and I enjoyed that I was pushed to think differently about a subject that honestly I didn't pay much attention to before. With that said, it was a lot of hard work. Three pset drops made it bearable but still, it felt difficult at times to keep up.
Start the problem sets early. Go to office hours, but not before giving an attempt on each problem yourself. Be advised: You will be thinking of math in a different way than you are used to – be prepared to be challenged. I found that the most limiting factor for me was the time. The material is quite interesting, and the time given for problem sets is adequate, but if you have two–three other problem set classes (like I did), doing well in this class becomes incredibly difficult.
Take the class!
LOVED THE COURSE. genuinely one of the courses at Harvard where I *learned so much* and I am so grateful. Math concentrator now. chill and fun. TAKE IT TAKE ITTT
I think the math concepts I learned will end up falling by the wayside, but this course was incredible for me. First, I make connections to the course material in ALL of my other classes. The basic concepts of formal math are so applicable everywhere. Second, learning to write proofs has honed my problem–solving skills, and I am glad I learned it even though I'll never use it again. Finally, learning to read mathematical symbols is, I think, the most important thing I got from this course. In prior math and physics classes I always tend to panic or zone out when I see a ton of symbols getting written on the board. No longer! I am now completely desensitized to notation overload, and I feel confident when a professor starts writing in Greek on a chalkboard.

Some specific advice:
Don't stress about the tests. There is a CHASM between the difficulty level of the problem sets and the tests. The tests tend to be the most basic possible question based on the material, while the problem sets can be very difficult at the beginning (office hours are good).
This class has very early midterms, which can be difficult but it also means that you won't have to study for this class and a normally–timed midterm at the same time.
There are three problem set drops, and problem sets are graded very lightly. The CAs took months to grade our PSETS; we were getting psets from early November back after the final, so bear in mind you may not be getting much feedback until it's too late.
Don't read Halmos, if it is assigned again. It is extremely confusing and the same material is covered in the other textbooks.
Good class!
The course is not THAT hard for any concerned. It all really depends on your instructor so do research on them to see whether they are good at teaching or have experience in beginner–level teaching. Gammage is a good teacher and he cares deeply for students, though the lectures were lost sometimes, he is amazingly intelligent and it is a spectacle to see!

Also, make sure the TF's when you take the course are good! They make or break it. Luke was a TF this year and that man is the GOAT so if he TF's at all take the course! Alot of 55 kids tf this course and while that may sound good, a lot of the time they end up not being the most helpful in actually helping you through beginner content.

Do the homework, practice for exams, and ultimately you will be good!
