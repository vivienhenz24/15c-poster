If this is ever taught by Prof. Taubes again, I would strongly recommend taking the class!
Math 212 was a great class and we covered a surprising amount of material. Prof. Taubes is always very kind and willing to help and support you in the course, which was much appreciated.
I think this course's problem sets are divorced from the actual material. I also found some of them extremely challenging, more so than other intro grad courses I've taken.
If you are interested in analysis, you should definitely take this course. It gives a great introduction to some more advanced topics in real analysis, primarily functional analysis, operator theory, and partial differential equations (note: the latter two areas are presumably a result of Prof. Taubes' version of this course). The content is broadly very useful to learn, both if you are primarily interested in analysis, or looking to learn analysis for other subjects in mathematics. The final project was great, especially for giving a more holistic view of the course.
This course was incredibly poorly taught. The problem sets were sometimes extremely difficult and the CAs were apparently unable to solve the questions themselves. I learned very little due to the poor instruction, so would not recommend unless the teaching staff changes.
Professor Taubes is very kind and clearly cares a lot about PDEs. However, his lectures are oftentimes unclear. Sometimes, you can't tell what he is trying to prove. However, Math 212 as taught by Prof. Taubes is as good as it gets at Harvard. If you'd like to take graduate analysis and don't want to go to MIT, take Math 212.
Solid class. The content is important, though I feel you would get a similar understanding from reading/solving Evans. Cliff is very reasonable when it comes to extensions/etc. but sometimes hard to follow as a lecturer.
This course was quite bad. In fact the worst math class I have taken at Harvard. The content itself is not sophisticated, but the lectures were very poorly given. They were really confusing, and hard to follow what even the professor was talking about. Many students learned the material from other sources themselves since the lectures were totally useless. In fact, the lectures made me forget the things I already knew.

Grading on the problem sets was extremely slow. They really took their time to grade so you did not get frequent feedback on how you are doing. Also, the scoring was extremely inconsistent (across psets and students).

Overall, I would NOT take this course unless you are 100% confident in the ability of the lecturer and the CAs. This was not the case for this year, so it was a pretty bad experience.
Harvard does not offer many analysis courses, so if you are interested in the subject, you should take it. I would recommend reading an undergraduate analysis text e.g. Rudin beforehand or Math 114. If there is anything you will learn in this class, it is the fundamental theorem of calculus, integration by parts, fixed point theorems, and tons of bounding as expected. You will get as much as you put in.
Take this course it is both fun and informative!
