Take this seminar!! (you should absolutely rank it 1st without a doubt)

This was by far the highlight of my freshman year and my favorite course of the year. Prof. Hart's combination of expertise, humor, and masterful discussion facilitation is unrivaled and made this course an incredibly fun yet insightful experience. You learn so much from the readings, Prof. Hart, and (most of all) the perspectives of your fellow classmates. You will get a ton out of this course if you are willing to voice and depend your opinion but equally willing to change it. The readings are probably longer than those of other seminars, but they cover so many important ideas and prepare you well for what is always a very engaging and productive discussion. For me personally, this course has totally transformed how I think about both the theory and the practice of government policy and how they relate.
This class is very good, although I would say it is not elementary. I would recommend at least a little bit of prior economics knowledge before taking it. The discussions were always productive and the feedback from the professor was also very appreciative of the work. The small size of the class provided each student with the opportunity to build a group of friends who are all interested in different topics but are free to express their views on any of them, which is a good addition. Lots of readings sometimes but it is worth it, even when it is harder to comprehend for introductory economics students (yes, I am talking about Coases paper).
Take this course!! You get to be in an intimate class with a Nobel prize winning professor who is humble and extraordinarily kind and thoughtful. No, prof. Hart is not an entertainer like a Ec10 professor would be, but he gets to know you personally and cares deeply about his students. He is thoughtful and reasonable and addresses the topics with nuance and an open mind. Youll be very lucky to get to study with him!!
Take this class! Discussions were very enriching and student driven, so I recommend being proactive in speaking up and actively listening to get the most of it. Hart is such a sweet, funny prof with an iconic British accent/humor and incredible set of life experiences getting to know him better one on one throughout the course was really cool, and I recommend taking initiative to set up individual office hours etc. For additional incentive beyond the amazingness of Hart and the fascinating topics you get to read/write/discuss in a low pressure environment, the class also counts for Econ elective credit :)
This class was amazing! This was a seminar style class and all of us interacted with eachother respectfully and were able to learn from other perspectives. By the end of this course, I was able to learn from my classmates and Professor Hart on a variety of topics such as climate change, affirmative action, gay marriage, and more pressing issues.
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! Professor Hart is amazing, and you will learn so much from the discussions. It is definitely not the easiest freshman seminar; you should take it if you genuinely enjoy discussing current issues and thinking about these issues from an economic perspective. However, if you enjoy open discussions about really cool topics, you will love this class. The readings can be long, but they are really interesting and are always on multiple perspectives on the topic so you are always learning. You are required to write a 2–3 response paper every week about the readings before class, but this does not take too much time because you will almost always have a lot to say about the readings. I absolutely loved this class, and I loved having Professor Hart as my teacher. If you want a chance at getting this class, make sure to rank it first.
