This class was not very organized. Programming projects were returned/graded extremely late (although feedback was good and grading was not too harsh) but I did not know how I was doing in the class because grades were returned very late. There was not enough resources to prep for the midterm exam (although exam was not too horrible). Sometimes TFs would not reply to emails or reply to Ed discussions super late. Also, the instructions on the programming projects were often not clear. That said, the were not too difficult and bi–weekly. Also, the class is small and not many people come to lectures so be aware of that as well. I would say take this class if you are really interested in the topic – it's one of the easier advanced CS classes.
This class is great if you want to get experience with more applied CS. Its one of the few CS classes at Harvard that arent theoretical. The class has project based psets which are basically long coding assignments. Then you have one midterm in the middle of the semester that is very fair. Finally you have a final project, which is exploratory based on what you learn in the class. Overall a great way to get to tech experience.
if you're on the edge about taking this, and you're not super interested in networking, don't take this course. this class is offered to the extension school, which isn't bad, but there ends up being 5 people in lecture all the time, so lecture isnt very engaging even though prof minlan is very nice, understanding, and an overall good lecturer. the topic is very niche and unironically not very applicable to swe work unless youre a senior level swe (even then idk), and u can pick up 75% of the content by reading the textbook (which is p good for this course). all in all, im not sure how much i took away from this course. its not hard to get an A due to the generous extra credit (u can get like a 160/100 on one of the projects if u do the extra credit), and it seems like the TFs just check to see if the tests pass on your code and if they do, you get a 100 for the project. section is pretty helpful, Ed support is meh, TFs are nice but grading is slow. no final; just a final project which is pretty simple. memorize the slides and read the textbook for the midterm, i think the mean was around 48/60 this year
