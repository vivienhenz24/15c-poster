TAKE THIS COURSE!!! If you have some interest in imperialism, both past and present, in the Americas I HIGHLY recommend it. The readingson topics like tourism as a form of neo–colonialism, silence in history–writing, Native American assimilation as a form of exclusion, and moreare extremely entertaining. This was my first Hist–Lit seminar and it was so good, I decided to concentrate in the department!
I LOVED THIS CLASS AND I'M SO SAD I'M NOT TAKING IT ANYMORE! I was a first–year in the class and I took this instead of a first year seminar and I have no regrets. Getting to meet people in other grades through this class was so fun: we had a good class community. Prof. Waits is such a personable lecturer and lovely human being in general. I enjoyed the structure of the class, where Prof. Waits would provide a historical overview of the readings we did and we had a discussion about our readings. I appreciate how Prof. Waits made an environment where everyone was quite willing to speak up. If you're willing to do the readings, willing to speak up in a seminar, and willing to write and think, then please consider this class.
This course is wonderful!!! I cannot recommend it enough –– Hannah is great, and you'll learn about topics you've never considered. There are a lot of assignments, but it is worth it.
Take this class! Dr. Waits is such a great professor and the topics each week create such a compelling and eye opening discussion. The weekly reading responses can be a little annoying, but otherwise incredible.
Take this class if you have an interest in American history or are an American History H&L concentrator – it fulfils a number of requirements and is a great class that will likely cover things you've never even heard of!
