TAKE THIS CLASS!! Dr. Craycraft is amazing and the class is so fun and very chill.
This is a fantastic course! Dr. Craycraft has so much passion and expertise on Appalachia, and the community around the course is so empowering as an Appalachian student.
Take this class. It was one of the best classes I have taken. Highly reccomended.
This course is an excellent survey of Appalachian life, history, and folklore, and Dr. Craycraft is fantastic across the board when it comes to facilitating discussion, providing feedback, and being accessible to students. Definitely recommend taking this course if you have space for an elective / especially if you're interested in the folklore and mythology departmentsince this was the first year it was offered I can only imagine that it will get better from here.
If you are interested in Appalachia at all and you need a class with a light workload but interesting content, then this course would probably be a good fit. We didn't spend as much time as I would have liked on what I thought of as the actual folklore of the region, such as stories, music, and superstitions that have been commonly held in various parts of the region, but these things were at least touched on, and we had some lively class discussions from which I always took away something interesting. I think that there was a little too much focus put on trying to develop an alternative story of Appalachia than the one that is commonly told and not enough focus on the things from that narrative that are accurate, but the content was still interesting and informative.

This course is a wonderful introduction and orientation into Appalachia as a regional paradigm. Through many aspects of folklife, Professor Craycraft discussed Appalachia in its most nuanced forms. Looking into sub–groups and smaller, less visible histories is a key theme throughout the term! It is clear that Appalachia is not a concrete term! There are so many opportunities to learn about and invest yourself in an area of inquiry you are interested in! This class made me interested in concentrating in FolkMyth, and you should totally take this! Very good for folks interested in place–based studies and the interdisciplinary humanities. This is also great for people who want to know more about how folklorists think and work.
