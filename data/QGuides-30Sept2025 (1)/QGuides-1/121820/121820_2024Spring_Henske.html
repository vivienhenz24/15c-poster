Do not take this class unless it is required. If it is required, consider finding a way not to take it. Be ready to sacrifice parts of your rotation in order to attend this class. The amount of work required to do well is not worth the payoff.
Fantastic course! Definitely learn a significant amount of information across a broad spectrum of topics in metabolism and how alterations in metabolism can present clinically. Dr. Michel is an incredible educator and his enthusiasm undoubtedly makes the course. Having a 9am course three days a week is a significant commitment and the final presentation is a fair amount of work, but I think that, if you are willing to put in the effort, you will gain a great understanding of metabolism that should translate well to your research goals.
The workload for the class isn't necessarily a lot but the grading is very particular. Lots of time needs to be spent on assignments and the presentation if you want to get an A. Grades aside, this is a very interesting course taught be many of the experts in the field! Discussions really encourage us to think critically about the topic at hand.
If you are interested in metabolism and how it relates to human biology and disease definitely take this course! The sections and in–class participation components in this course are engaging and make learning about metabolic pathways fun.
Don't be dissuaded by the first week, it gets better!

I would say approach this course with caution. It is an interesting course, but it is controlling. They require you to be present everyday in class and fill out a google form. They expect you to be an expert of everything when doing the problem set (going above the course material and what is taught through independent literature search) as well as the presentation. Their feedback is helpful I will admit, but no matter what you do it seems like they are finding something to critique you on.
