Consider the assignments ahead of time and gather a series of topics that most fascinate you to effectively tackle the course's essays.
Take this class! Prof. Harris is very good at laying the foundation for the essays you'll be writing in the course. The course is very fascinating, even if you're not a psychology major. It's also taught in a very approachable style, and Prof. Harris has very organized slides and Canvas page layouts. I really enjoyed this class, and I felt like I had a lot of freedom over my essay topics without being lost or unclear on the prompt.
This class is excellent for students who want a broad overview of many findings in Developmental Psychology, spanning many topics. The course is primarily lecture–based, though questions mid–lecture are encouraged. It is perhaps not a course for students focusing on a very specific topic in Developmental Psychology, or who want to develop methods training. That being said, it gives many of the tools necessary to evaluate the studies themselves, especially in their design and assumptions.
This is a very fun class to attend and Professor Harris has lived a long life of hands–on Development Psychology research. Definitely take this class if youre interested in DP, want extra chances for lab assistant positions, and want to take a class with an amazing and impressive professor! Lectures are easy to follow along, interesting, and consist of learning about direct studies that contributed to areas of Developmental Psychology. Papers allow you to explore the topics you are interested in. Questions and in–class discussion is encouraged. Take this class!
Incredible course. Professor Harris is so knowledgeable and the material covered is so interesting. The commitment is not huge (though I did find myself spending extra time to dive more deeply into the topic covered). If you are interested in developmental psychology at all I would highly recommend!
