This course was amazing! Each professor and week taught students to think deeply and broadly about not just the "what" but the "why" and "how". Every question of insight into the broader questions or works was met with enthusiasm and interest by the teaching staff. the workload was good and the guidance through the final project was outstanding. Loved this course!
If you are interested in writing about art, please take this course!!! The class is not the traditional history of art course – it is theme based rather than chronological. I LOVED THIS CLASS – it made me think about the world differently and appreciate art galleries so much more. Due to this class I now intend to major in HAA. Be warned however this class does take time. Every week there are over 100 pages of quiet dense readings, a lecture, a looking lab (where you get to see the artwork discussed in class in person at the Harvard art museum) a section, and a two page response paper. There is also a final project worth 40% of the grade that you have to balance on top of this. Often on Fridays there were additional meetings. These Friday meetings were a highlight e.g. our trip to the Isabella Gardener Museum so it's a great class to take if you want to get out and explore Boston. Yes, it is work, but for me it never felt like 'work'. Professor Lippit was so supportive and always brought cake to section. Would 100% recommend if you are ready to properly throw yourself into the class and learn lots.
DO NOT TAKE THIS CLASS. I have so many reasons, but the first is 1) you have no idea where your grade stands. It is all based on your response papers (which you don't get back ever and they're graded based on so much subjectivity it is frustrating) and your final project (which is not explained well AT ALL, it wasn't until the end of the semester when the idea of what the project was is truly fleshed out in section, its not fun) . 2) The communication in this course is awful, the final project prompt/deadlines weren't even posted onto the canvas site until after the first deadline and there is different things being told to different sections. If you have friends in other sections you guys will not cover the same material, nor will you have the same experience in the class overall. 3) There is too many components outside of this class apart from lecture, the looking lab is the most tedious to get through sometimes, often they don't benefit your understanding of the material and not to mention that if you miss one its not taken lightly. 4) Section is scary, if you don't go in super prepared with your readings highlighted and discussion points ready you're basically done for, two other people will dominate the discussion and your section leader won't make an effort to open the discussion up to everyone. If you voice the 'wrong opinion' you also will be verbally pushed into a corner until you agree with the rest of the section or your section leader. I found that often words were taken or even put into your mouth, it wasn't productive or fun. I felt like I was the only one who felt this way about this class, but after speaking to others (most were declared HAA concentrators) I found out I was not the only one. The only issue is that there is a loud minority of people that will tell you this class is a gem and that they love it, don't listen to them.
This is a great class for anyone interested in learning more about art history, visual culture, and the humanities! I took this course as an introduction to my concentration and found it to be very beginner–friendly, especially if you have taken AP Art History.
This is an amazing course. It is definitely a lot of work and you will spend your weekend doing the readings and writing the response paper. But, it is easy to get into a good flow with this class and the content is not too hard. I would recommend going to lecture having already written the response paper because it can get very confusing quickly if you do not already have a direction set for the week. Also, I would not take this course if you have never taken Art History before. It pulls on analytical knowledge that is not specifically taught, especially with the final paper. That being said, it is my favorite course I have taken and I loved going to lecture. If you want to expand your knowledge of art and culture, this is the course for you. The staff is also very supportive and it is a great way to get to know some professors in the HAA department.
Please take it!
I loved this class! It was a great overview of art pieces and traditions that I had never heard of, which prepares you to be a citizen in the world. I would also say that it is not necessarily a gem –– you definitely have to do some work for this class –– but it is a good class.
I learned so much in this course. It teaches you how to think and write about art in a way that makes sense. It helped me improve my writing, discussion, and presentation skills, which will help me not only in this course, but also throughout the rest of the courses I take at Harvard.

Take this class! The lectures are engaging, looking labs are really incredible, and you learn so much relative to the amount of time you put in. I would especially recommend this for STEM people looking for their one humanities class.
Great class. I highly recommend taking it if you are eve slightly interested in art
Definitely take this class!!! You learn so much about a diverse breadth of artwork and it will change how you see and understand visual objects.
As a non–HAA concentrator, this course was interesting but more involved than I would've expected, particularly in terms of the time commitment per week. The professors and teaching team are excellent and the variety of subject matter covered is intriguing. However, weekly response papers were a heavier lift than expected due to the complex nature of some of the prompts, and at times I struggled to see how the units in the course were connected to each other – it sometimes felt like randomly jumping between styles and regions without a clear direction or purpose in terms of understanding the broader "so what" of the course. Nonetheless, I appreciated the chance to learn something different to what I am used to and am glad that I took this course overall.
Take the class! The readings are not that difficult and often introduce very interesting ideas/information you may not have previously known. The reading always feels related to the lecture and is so important for writing your weekly response papers.
