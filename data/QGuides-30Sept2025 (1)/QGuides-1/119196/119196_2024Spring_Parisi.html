Problem sets were fine, my biggest issue with this course was how needlessly difficult they made exams which were not really reflective of the practice exams, was also just in a really bad section so word of advice, you'll get a sense of your instructor's teaching after 2–3 classes, if it doesn't work, take a different section time, your GPA will thank you
Great class, very rewarding.
This course is hard (definitely harder than MATH 1B even though I was told the opposite). Be prepared to study a lot in your own time. Also, KEEP GOOD NOTES because so much of this course is cumulative; unlike MATH 1B, which is taught in 3 almost–distinct units, all of MATH 21A builds up. So, if youre not comfortable with parametrizing curves and surfaces (which is something you learn pretty early on), then youre screwed for the rest of the course because that skill is needed for the integral theorems you learn in the later half of the course. How I survived was to redo/reorganize my notes later in the day after section while the material was still fresh in my brain. Dont procrastinate because it builds up. Finally, go to MQC because itll help you manage the 3 P–Sets a week.
A very solid course, I learned exactly what I hoped to by taking it. This course moves at a decent pace but as long as you stay engaged during sections and use your time outside of class well, the workload should definitely be manageable. You should definitely go to the Math Question Center to work on the homework sets, as the CAs were very helpful and you can get a better understanding of the concepts being covered by going over them with a classmate.
This is a good class overall in that it gives a lot of practice in preparation for eexams, the PSets are on the shorter side and doable in office hours and the TFs are very accessible and helpful. It prepares you well for exams.
Good class!! As long as you've taken the prereq's I think you should be good. If this is your first math class at Harvard, the 3 PSETs a week might be a little hard. It's not too bad though. Get a good PSET group and you're set, and the CAs and instructors are great!
