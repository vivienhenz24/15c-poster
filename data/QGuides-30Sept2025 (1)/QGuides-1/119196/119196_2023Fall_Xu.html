I took Math 21a as a requirement, but ultimately, it ended up being one of my favorite classes this semester. Since the class is taught in small sections, you build a really good bond with your table, the TF, and the course assistants, and the class environment is very supportive. They really emphasize working through hard problems (and there will be many of them!), which makes the process of learning very rewarding and solidifies your understanding well. I absolutely loved the teaching method of this class and I could not recommend that more. My only reservations with this class are that you should not underestimate the workload of 3 psets/week (the psets are not very short and you will need to check answers or go to MQC for help) and that you have to be prepared to spend time really solidifying these concepts outside of class or before exams. However, if you need to take this class or want to go further in math than Calc I/II, I really really enjoyed this course and would recommend. (P.S. Fanhui is an awesome TF!)

They love to make you think critically about topics, so you want to make sure you have a good conceptual understanding of each idea so you can easily apply to the problem at hand.

Reasons to take this course:
– You learn from experienced professionals about math that is highly applicable to many fields.
– The TFs and CAs truly want you to succeed and I enjoyed spending time with them.
– You will have an excellent support system for asking questions about psets at MQC or Office Hours.
– The course heads are receptive to feedback and want to know how to improve the course.

Reasons to not take this course:
– It is very, very hard. This class was the hardest class I have every taken and will be my first class where I did not get an A. If your math background isn't as strong and you are shooting for a perfect GPA, I would recommend you seek a different class. I would, however, argue that learning is more important than the grade.
