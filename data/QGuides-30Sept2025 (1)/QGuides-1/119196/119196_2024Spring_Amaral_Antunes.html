Start studying early for exams (like a week or more in advance). Review your notes and PSETs consistently throughout the semester to stay on top of the material. Go to MQC, office hours, and any other support resources for help with PSETs and studying. Have one–on–one meetings with your TFs when you have concerns!
If you're looking to fulfill some type of calculus requirement, you're probably between the 21 and 22 series, and while I can't speak for the latter I found 21a very manageable. While three PSETs a week sounds daunting, they're all of a reasonable length and contribute to your understanding quite well. I also found course staff always eager to help!
Go to MQC. I know you'll be reading all these and see this a bunch of times, but seriously, go. It is such a good place to get your questions answered and to receive assistance with your work, and if you're comfortable with the materials, it's also a good way to make friends and to "trauma bond." Additionally, Jonier is the BEST TF.
