What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is one of the last methods courses you'll receive in the history of science ––– treat it as the testing ground for all your written ideas and theories!
I would first of all tell future students that the course isn't as terrifying as it seems because students all dual–submit the final. I would also say that it allows students to critically think through what exactly it is that they are engaging in when they decide to pursue historical scholarship.
Under Professor Alam's leadership, this methods course has been eminently useful in learning about the depth and craft of historical work, along with its pitfalls and responsibilities. I have definitely grown as a scholar from taking this class under her guidance.
