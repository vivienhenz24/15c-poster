This review will correct a few misconceptions I had before I entered:

1. This class will be hard if you haven't done calculus recently. This is not true. I took AP Calculus BC 4 years ago and did no calculus or advanced math (other than Stat 110) since that point. I was not at any disadvantage.

2. This class may be useful to me in the future. Although this is theoretically multivariable calculus for the social sciences, much of the content bears zero relation to the social sciences (e.g. using vector operations to find a line parallel to a plane). Alternatively, what is the possible social science application of finding the triple integral of a hollow cylinder in R3? If there exists such an application, it was not made clear to us. The workshops theoretically exist to show practical applications of the class, but only 2 of the 6 workshops had any relation to the social sciences (the others referenced geography, meteorology, and astronomy).

3. This class is a better way to get ec honors than Math 21a. In fact, 95% of this class is exactly the same. If you compare a pset with someone in Math 21a, every problem will be the same. It appears that the singular difference is the last week of class. In the past, Math 18a offered just 1 pset per week. It now has 3 full psets each week (plus a mini–exam, 2 midterms, and the final). There is no longer any reason to take Math 18a over Math 21a unless the section time is better.

4. It won't take that much time. Actually, Math 18a has been the most tedious class that I have taken at this school. This starts with the quantity of instruction: 3 mandatory sections per week plus a workshop on most weeks. This far exceeds the instruction time for most courses at this school. It also includes the frequency of exams: a mini–exam, 2 midterms, and a final, which is also far greater than most courses.

By far the most egregious aspect of this course is having 3 psets each week. This frequency is unforgiving and disrespectful to the rest of your life. During the week, it means that you have to complete one pset per 2 days. This interrupts your ability to dedicate time to any other part of your life: other classes, extracurricular activities, family obligations, and so on.

Having a pset due every 2 days limits your ability to devote any two consecutive nights to anything other than math. The 3 pset drops available does little to remedy this issue (after all, this covers less than 10% of all psets). I have spent more time on Math 18a than on any other course I have taken at this school, including: Ls1a, Ls1b, Stat110, and Ec1123.

All in all, there is absolutely no reason to take this course if it is not a concentration requirement. And even if it is, you should just take Math 21a.
