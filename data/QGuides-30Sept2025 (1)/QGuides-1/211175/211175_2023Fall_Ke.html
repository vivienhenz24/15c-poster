What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Take this class! Professor Ke covers so many recent/useful aspects of high–dimensional statistics, network analysis, and text analysis in an accessible manner. Professor Ke really cares about equipping you with the right tools to pursue research in these areas, not to mention she's one of the kindest and funniest professors I've come across at Harvard.
There is no homework and the only thing is a final course project, which is done in about a month. The topics are appealing and have both theory and applications parts.
Take this class if you'd like a gem and the opportunity to learn a good variety of interesting topics! However, be prepared to review a lot on your own if you'd really like to retain the material, since there's no homework to keep you accountable to reinforcing concepts. If you don't care too much about retaining the material, then that's a great fit ;)
A very chill course, essentially a seminar. It was essentially Prof. Ke presenting a diversity of different topics in areas of statistics I presume she found fascinating, including sparse inference, community detection, and some language model stuff at the end. The brevity, survey–like, and surface–level nature of the lectures, however, combined with a lack of assessments outside of the project, make it easy to phase out. The project itself is very open–ended; find a topic that interests you, devote a couple days to it, and you should be golden. You really get out what you put in (in this case, the attention you pay).
Great class! Just ensure your attendance at each lecture and submit a solid final project, and you'll do well.
