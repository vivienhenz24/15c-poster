Comments
If you are even considering it, take this class. It is a one–of–a–kind, unique experience. If you are interested in evolution, animals, marine biology, development, or fieldwork, or just think the course description sounds interesting, lottery and take it. Don't be scared by the exams! They are difficult, but the trip and class community make it bearable.
This is not an easy class. It's a lot of content to memorize and understand for exams but honestly worth it because I feel like this was such an important and once in a lifetime opportunity that I'm glad I got to experience before graduating! I really enjoyed Cassandra's lectures on genetics and the different ways animals develop or begin development even within their own phylum.
This class is an excellent class. Lecture is interesting and engaging. Both professors are phenomenal ( and we had an amazing TF too ) . Lab is fun and interactive. Exams have a lot of material to study, but the professors recognize that on exams as well. The field trip was one of the most incredible parts of my Harvard experience. I was able to learn so much in the field and Bocas was an excellent research base to study at. It was so amazing to apply knowledge from the classroom to the field. Gonzalo and Cassandra do an excellent job of facilitating the trip as well. Made some great friends in this class too!
Great class! You learn so much about invertebrate biology and development and also how scientists in the field actually do research on these specimens in the lab and the field. The lab reports are long and always require drawing specimens but the labs themselves are very flexible and allow you to pursue specimens and experiments that you are most interested in. The field trip portion was one of my favorite experiences and actually inspired me to pursue summer research! Amazing professors and TF's and the small class size allows you to grow really close with your classmates!
Take this class. If you like zoology or development at all take this class. If you want to work with specimens in the field take this class. Just take it.
This is an incredible class that I feel so privileged to have taken. Gonzalo, Cassandra, and Arianna are all incredible. I took this class without having taken biology or chemistry since high school. Though I wholeheartedly recommend this class to anyone considering it, ****having limited background will set you back quite a bit.**** I think large portions of the exams will come a lot easier if you have some sense of lab intuition or exposure to developmental biology. Exams were pretty tough in general because you were either designing experiments (which is where a lack of experience will bite you) or trying to remember a small detail from one of the lectures. All in all, though, you should definitely take this class if you have the opportunity.
