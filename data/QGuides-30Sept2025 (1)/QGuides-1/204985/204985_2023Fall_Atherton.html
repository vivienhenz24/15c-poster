TAKE THIS CLASS!! If this class is ever offered again, do not hesitate to take it. Even if you are a STEM concentrator or have never been interested in literature, you'll have a good time in this class. It's a fairly easy class with interesting readings, fun class discussions, and a nice atmosphere. The readings can occasionally be dense, but they aren't long. The readings were always interesting since we got to learn more about Japan's history through poems and fictional stories. I loved how the class covered a wide range of genres to dissect traditional Japanese literature (e.g. plays, short stories, poems, novels, etc.). There were simple weekly Slack assignments, three papers, and one final exam. The papers were fun to complete since you get to dive further into the texts from class, and the assignments are really easy and fun to complete. The professor, David Atherton, was so funny and enthusiastic during lectures. Even if you're having a rough day, he will make you laugh and know to check in on you. If this class isn't offered, you should at least do a class with him. He is so much fun; he provides snacks during class sometimes; he's always cracking jokes; and he makes everyone in class become friends. The lectures in his class are always engaging since he let's us talk to our peers about the readings. He'll encourage people to speak, but he never cold calls or forces participation. It's a chill, fun atmosphere. I had Kassandra Diaz as my TF, and she was the best!! She always gave me inspiration for my papers. She was great facilitating discussions during sections, and she made me see the stories/poems in a different way since she has a deep knowledge of Japanese history. She's also a fun person since she sometimes held a picnic–style section with class outside while providing snacks!
