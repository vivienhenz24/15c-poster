Recommend with enthusiasm for those with a good memory, do not recommend for those without a good memory. Overall, very fun class with very interesting material. The professor teaches in a very coherent and sequential way.
Really enjoyed this course. Came in having never taken an art history class, but was just generally interested in Japan and architecture, so I thought it would be an interesting class. After finishing it, I cannot recommend the course enough. Professor Lippit is not only so knowledgable, but his lectures are so engaging. He makes sure every lecture is clearly structured and covers a distinct topic, though he weaves topics together very well. TAKE THIS!
Prof. Lippits course offers an unparalleled introduction to Japanese architecture. The lectures are well illustrated and intellectually stimulating. Anyone with an interest in architecture or Japan should take this course!
Absolutely take this class. Professor Lippit is one of the best professors out there. His lectures are always well formatted and packed with information but still easy to follow. This class is one of my favorites I've ever taken. If you are an HAA student this class will probably feel pretty easy, but not in the way where you don't learn anything. I imagine non–HAA students might find this class more difficult than expected considering you can't skip lecture/not study and do well in the course. If you do come to class and take the time to study (not much time at all), it is smooth sailing.
One of my favorite classes I have taken in college. Professor Lippit is truly the best and such a joy to listen tohe is quite literally the best lecturer I've had (senior here). Please take this class, along with the woodblock print course.
I took this class for concentration requirement, but if you are looking for a gem, this is NOT it. Content is great, lectures are amazing, but the exams and weekly assignments are very much like straight memorization or spitting back facts.
Everything about this course was amazing, it's so clear that the lectures, assignments, readings, and sections were thoroughly thought about and structured. Professor Lippit is an absolute gem of a person; every lecture was so engaging, and it's so clear how much he cares about the material and how knowledgeable he is. He's also so incredibly kind and cares about his students! I liked that this course was also so much more than just about architecture, as it covered engineering, environment, urban design, history, and so much more, which gave the class a holistic look at Japan's built environment in its totality. The workload is also not too bad; there are response papers every week, and they do require some thought and some time with the readings, but the grading system for them makes them not too stressful.

There are not really any shortcomings with this class, I think my one thing is that there is not a lot of facilitation for your own analysis and reflections on the architecture outside of what is given in lecture and the readings. I do wish that were was more space for discussion or writing towards more open–ended questions about the architecture, but because there's just so much content, it makes sense why there isn't, since there's just so much to get through. The exams aren't too stressful, but it does take time to remember the dates and locations of each site and not mix them up. Overall though, this is a class that's wonderful because it caters towards students of all types of backgrounds and exposures to architecture (including having no prior experience), and you will come away with a larger understanding of not just architecture in Japan, but also how architecture is shaped by the culture, politics, and traditions of the past and present.
Overall, take it! Lecture is great: erudite, sometimes witty professor with very interesting, thorough discussion of urban forms and famous buildings. It makes the sometimes grueling sections and questionable midterm worth it.

This course provides a great overview of so many building types and time periods in Japanese architecture. It may seem like a niche topic, but actually connects really well to history, politics, and urban planning more broadly. There are weekly papers, but it's only one page double spaced so isn't too much of a burden, and the exams are easy to study for because the teaching staff is very clear about what they will entail. I took this course as a STEM major for fun, and I would definitely recommend this course to anyone else with any amount of interest in architecture!

This course goes far beyond architectural fundamentals but offers a rare and intimate exploration of Japanese history, culture, and religion.
