I think that this course is a must–take for undergraduates who want to do theory and with good math background. Much more in depth than undergraduate quantum, more focused, and more applicable. The course is well–structured, and any hard math concepts are covered again in section to keep everyone on the same page. Overall, fantastic course, and I am coming away with a solid understanding of the techniques used to approach quantum mechanical problems.
I thought that this was a great course. Prof. Khalaf went out of his way to make the course feel very cohesive. Every topic felt like a natural next step from the previous topics that were covered, and the course felt like one large exercise in constructing a quantum mechanical framework in the most intuitive way possible. Patrick was also an incredible TF who went out of his way to make every topic well–motivated and as intuitive as possible for someone who is relatively new to the material.
Definitely take this class!! I highly recommend. Not only is it probably required, but it's also extremely useful, and I unexpectedly enjoyed every part of it. Eslam and Patrick are wonderful: they do a great job at explaining the material, and they are super receptive to feedback and questions. They are one of the best teaching teams I have ever had. I gained a much deeper intuition behind many more complicated quantum topics, and learned some nice math tricks along the way. It's one of these classes in which you're reminded of the joys of physics. It's great.
This was an incredible course; I'd highly recommend it!!

Physics 251A is a really nice quantum course. It follows Sakurai (with some deviations) and offers a highly motivated approach to QM.

Eslam was a wonderful lecturer. The lectures were well thought out and really good. He would post lecture notes shortly after lecture, aiming to revise and elaborate on anything that students found confusing in class. I found this to be a very nice and thoughtful approach. This is the first time Eslam has taught 251, and you really wouldn't know it; he put together a very polished course.

Eslam actively sought feedback throughout the semester and responded/adapted to it swiftly. He regularly made it clear through his actions how much he cared about the class, the students, and helping us learn most effectively.

Problem sets were challenging, but well–written. They really helped solidify concepts and further our understanding of the material.

The exams were tough (long for the time allotted). However, Eslam's intention was to make them doable, and so I think this is just a bit of trial and error as this is his first time teaching the course. I suspect by next year they should be more manageable.

Section was great. Patrick would answer any questions we had, go over difficult parts of lecture using a new perspective, and/or dive more deeply into something we didn't have time to fully cover in lecture.

I think the highlight of this course was Eslam and Patrick. They clearly cared so much about it, and it really made 251 a pleasure. Just as an example, Patrick covered two lectures on Perturbation Theory. He tried reading the textbook, and also looked for other published approaches, but didn't like any of the explanations he found. So he basically pulled an all–nighter to come up with a new way to teach perturbation theory –– his lectures were really great. That's the kind of care both of them put into this course.

All in all a wonderful course, and I'd highly recommend it!
Definitely take advantage of office hours and go to section, they are very helpful.
This class is a great class to take when starting a PhD in physics with many other first–years.
