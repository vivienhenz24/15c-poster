Physics 195A is a great class that allows you to apply many skills/concepts that you learned from 143a (or an equivalent QM class) to different models of crystalline solids and investigate their electronic/thermal properties. Although the class lists 15 a/b/c and 143a as prerequisites for the class, students in this class come from a range of backgrounds. Some of my classmates were sophomores with only 143a under their belt, and some of my classmates were graduate students with a lot more coursework/research experience, and we were all able to learn and succeed in the class because Prof. Mundy makes the subject so approachable. The problem sets are reasonable in difficulty and usually relatively short; the midterms were also very reasonable in difficulty, and I especially appreciated Mundy giving us infinite time to work on the midterms ("doing good physics takes time and shouldn't be restricted by time limits" is her approach). The final projects were also fun and low–stress, and you get to hear from your peers about various interesting topics you may not have been introduced to before (e.g., superconductivity, quantum information topics, etc). Lastly, special shoutout to the TFs in the class, Junkai and Daniel, who were always very approachable and helpful in section and office hours (and were also just funny and friendly people) –– they made the class a pleasant experience!
