Literally one of my favorite classes I took this semester. Jasanoff is, of course, wonderful and brilliant, and I genuinely looked forward to each class discussion and tutorial because I knew I'd learn something new and come away with interesting insights/takeaways. The best way to describe HIST97 is being onboarded into a club an academic/scholarly one, that is. You get to engage in the work of research and scholarship with history PhD students and experts in their field in a small group environment which, as a history concentrator, is just amazing. I loved my tutorial as well and Kalpana (though I'm not sure she'll be TF'ing next spring) is super helpful, provides great feedback, and always makes herself available to answer questions or refine your final paper topic. I'm so so glad I took imperial history.
Someone compared this to Expos but for History. In many ways, that is true. However, I enjoyed this class way more than Expos. I feel like my writing genuinely improved. You really will get as much out of this class as you put in. Even though it was a required class, it was great.
This course convinced me that history was the right choice for me! Professor Jasanoff is amazing, so knowledgeable, and very passionate about her field, the TFs were great and always gave very helpful feedback. I genuinely enjoyed working on the assignments and the readings, in particular the more theoretical ones, were very interesting! Highly recommend this 97 because of the professor's passion for teaching and because of the broad range of topics covered!
People say this is the most difficult history class you will ever take: that is not true. You will get to write a lot, but you always get to revise and are given a lot of support. I also really love Prof. Jasanoff, and she's a fantastic teacher to have in a Seminar setting. This class is a good, if at times boring, time.
I went into this class with zero expectations. I thought it would be a boring sophomore tutorial, but it ended up surpassing my expectations and being super interesting. It felt like a proper introduction to the history department, almost perfect in terms of learning all the foundations and being engaging enough. Maya Jasanoff is a fantastic professor, incredibly knowledgeable and quite possibly one of the best professors that I've gotten the chance to learn from. She's very passionate about sharing everything she knows. The TAs were all very kind, patient, and helpful as well. The level of detailed feedback we would get on our papers from our assigned TA was ridiculous, I think nearly every sentence had feedback and this is even if you got a solid grade on the paper. In total there were three mini papers and one final paper. It seemed like a big task to complete so many papers at first, but I found the mini–papers to be extremely helpful in the end: each mini–paper helped guide our final paper which was essentially a topic of our choice.

Overall I'd recommend this course 100% if you are looking for one of the best HIST sophomore tutorials to take. It won't be an easy class, but I think every HIST sophomore tutorial has very similar workload, so I wouldn't worry about that. I think that the selling point for this tutorial is the professors and TAs for sure.
This is the course I absolutely needed to be successful in my history classes!!! I'm so glad I took it because research and historical thinking is so much less intimidating and daunting now. Yes, the final project is long, but because of all the scaffolding assignments I actually had most of it already written by the time we got to the final due date (very strange feeling for someone who is used to procrastinating all my papers!). The structure of the course is super helpful for learning what makes a good research topic, project, how to use different types of sources, how to analyze historiography, etc. Super relevant and well–taught!
Don't be scared by the syllabus, it's not as bad as it might seem. Professor Jasanoff is one of the most preeminent historians of our time but truly cares about teaching this class and wants to form a relationship with each student. The tf's are amazing too. Great class
