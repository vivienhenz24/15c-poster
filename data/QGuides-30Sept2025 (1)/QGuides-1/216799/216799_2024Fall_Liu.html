This class is extremely interesting and the lab component is excellent. That said, it goes through a lot of material quickly without much testing and so to grasp the material you need to put in quite a lot of extra work when reading the papers or doing the lab. You can get away with not doing the extra work though if you only care about a grade.
This is a chill and very cool class––the professor genuinely cares about the subject and his students, the material is really interesting and the professor takes the time to answer questions that don't pertain exactly to class material. Lectures are pretty typical, but the lab component is one of the best I have taken so far––you get to work on a fascinating project that has exciting results at the end, with very supportive, patient, and helpful lab staff.
**Lab is fun and you get an overview of the latest in engineering for neuroscience

Great low–stress lab where you get to stain and clear mouse brains and learn some microscopy. Also interesting lectures which act as a survey of modern neuroengineering. Professor Liu is very knowledgeable and has good explanations
You should definitely take this course, if it is close to your interests. It is light with assignments, making it safe to take with an otherwise heavier courseload. Additionally, the lab is extremely cool – you get to image a brain! – and is safe for computational people (does not require substantial prior lab experience).
Take this class! Professor Liu is excited about the latest neural engineering tech and wants us to be excited too. The lecture component is interesting and there are no exams – just presentations where you get to explore more cool research. The lab component is also fun – getting to do IF imaging of cleared mouse brain tissue is pretty dang cool.
Great class, there's such cool technologies in Neuroengineering right now, and it is so cool just to have the chance to learn about all of them and how that research is conduced. The lab component to this class may also be the best lab component at this school, as the brain imaging is just so cool. Does take a lot of time outside of class unfortunately to make sure brains are prepped for imaging, but other than that, it is a relatively simple and straightforward class. Loved it.
Keep your presentations under 15 minutes and everything will be fine.
The lab component of this course is awesome!! You get to slice and image fluorescently labeled mouse brains and can really see the neuronal structure within your sample (which is actually quite beautiful). The lectures are not always the most engaging, but they do present an overview of some current Neuroengineering innovations. You do two midterm presentations, a final presentation, plus a final lab presentation where you get a little bit of freedom to explore what interests you. Only other assignments are labs reports which are pretty straightforward and a final paper/project, none of which I felt were graded too harshly. There are no exams so really you get out of it what you put in. Overall, I really enjoyed this class and would recommend taking it!
