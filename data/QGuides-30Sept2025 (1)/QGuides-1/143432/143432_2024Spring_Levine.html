This is what every STEM class should be.

Pros:
– the right amount and length of assignments (or, if psets were too long, extensions were given immediately)
– content difficulty (it is hard, but not too hard)
– fair exams (tested on the content we learned, clear expectations)
– Margo's enthusiasm and flexibility (if you get a chance to take a course with Margo, do it. She is supportive with extensions for individuals and the whole class. She is excited about the content. She adapts to how the class is feeling and makes it fair. She made a 9am fun)
– it was really only a MW 9am with occasional Fridays (5, which were given at the start of the semester)
– I had friends in the class so doing the psets with a group was helpful (def do this)
– only some questions on the psets were graded for accuracy (usually about 1/4–1/2 of the questions), while the rest were graded for completion
– grading was kind (large participation and essentially guaranteed 10% for submitting a summer or practice problems every few weeks)

Cons:
– questions on Ed were answered late or sometimes never answered, especially in the week before exams
– practice problems for exams were few in number, but appropriate for the exams and not a huge problem
– we were a bit behind on content so content covered on Monday could be due on the Wednesday pset, making it hard to do them over the weekend which threw off my schedule
– section was not consistent weekly, and we only had one section in the last third of the course (it was not recorded but definitely helpful to attend and only offered at one time)
– matlab was hard to learn but we were allowed to use chatgpt

OVERALL: This class is fair. Margo is great. I would recommend taking it with friends for a pset group.
