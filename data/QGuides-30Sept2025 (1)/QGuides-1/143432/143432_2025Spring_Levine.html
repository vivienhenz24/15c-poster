I greatly enjoyed Math 21b. For fellow 21b enjoyers, I would recommend this course because it basically goes into the "why" and "how" of a lot of concepts that are simply stated to us. In AM105, we delve into how these are derived.

However, it was so much work! It was truly a lot of busywork, so much so that it took up the majority of my time. My mental health took a severe downturn simply due to the overwhelming nature of this class. It was a lot to think about, and a lot of work to do. The midterms count for 50% of your grade, unless you do "extra homeworks" (Student Choice), after which the midterms are "only" 40% of your grade. The PSETs, which involve approximately 25–30 pages of handwritten (or Latexed) answers, took me around 15 hours a week to do, and I'd have to do about 1.5 PSETs a week due to the "Student Choice" assignments. The PSETs take a very long time to write up, as you have to explain your thought process in painstaking detail. They're good practice for the midterms, but even so, the midterms often bring up concepts briefly mentioned in class with a throwaway comment from Margo.

Class is not organized very well, it flows according to Margo's whim. Margo is a sweetheart, but as a professor she's tough. We often go off on tangents in class, or we don't cover topics very well, or we simply brush over a concept which is actually quite important. I wish she would pay more attention to teaching us the important topics instead of delving very deep into small nitty gritty details that don't quite matter.

However, the teaching staff truly saves the course. Margo's, and the other TF's office hours (Ashira, the Justins, Jacqui, etc) are lovely and extremely helpful. I would recommend going to office hours and truly understanding the concepts inside and out, as they'll simply build and build.

Overall: I would only recommend that you take this course if you are an AM major who truly loves 21b and its ilk.
