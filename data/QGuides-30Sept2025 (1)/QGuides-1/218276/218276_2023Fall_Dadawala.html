Amazing class! Highly reccomend!
TAKE THIS CLASS!! It is so fun and Vikrant is a great professor. I loved learning more about the diaspora and engaging with a variety of source material.
Take this course. If you have any interest in South Asian history, no matter your starting place, Professor Dadawala will feed your interests and offer interesting reading materials to think about. Readings are moderately heavy but are all interesting and well–selected. Papers are relatively short and fun to write.
TAKE THIS SEMINAR! Truly one of kind, with a professor that cares so deeply about the material and his students. This course is very low–stress and high–reward, with such fascinating readings and interesting discussions. It was a privilege to take it.
As a minority within AAPI/South Asian studies/scholarship, though we were unable to fully explore the entire South Asian diaspora/world and we primarily stuck with the parts of South Asia with British colonial history, I still learned so much about methodology and I have such a strong vision for how I can leverage different methods and modes of learning to conduct my own research and inquiry into culturally relevant and "hidden" histories which can bring lesser discussed aspects of the diaspora to light. I absolutely loved this class, and could not imagine any other instructor teaching this course. Vikrant is dedicated to each of us, and he has inspired me to inquire into many different areas of interest.
Take this class!!! Vikrant is amazing. You learn so much about the South Asian diaspora, which gave me a chance to connect with my heritage in a really nice way. (Everyone in our class was South Asian but it's definitely open to everyone, and Vikrant does a great job of making sure the conversation isn't solely focused on our identities.) It's definitely very manageable –– the readings are very interesting, and the 2 response papers were a bit stressful but Vikrant guides you through them. As someone with a non–humanities background I was a bit nervous going into the class, but I'm so glad I took it!
If this class is offered again, then absolutely take it if you're interested at all in South Asia or the diaspora. It taught me a lot that I didn't know going in. It did spend a lot more time on the indenture system than I had expected (as opposed to modern migration out of South Asia), but it was fascinating to learn about a topic I hadn't really been exposed to before
While the overall topic of the class seems interesting, the class readings and discussions can start to feel pretty repetitive. The course spends the bulk of its time focusing on the colonial indenture period in history, which I did not find as engaging. I would have appreciated it if we moved through the timeline faster and spent more time on the present day diaspora and immigration, which I find to be more relevant and interesting, rather than just 1–2 weeks at the end. The class was also split between premeds who were just there for the english requirement and actual hist–lit concentrators, which reflected in the class discussions and overall participation. Vikrant is a great guy and a chill grader though and seemed really passionate about the course material. Overall, its an easy class with very few assignments and it was definitely nice to meet only once a week and not have section, but it does get boring fast
Do the readings or you will not extract anything from this class
