I would love for people to learn and take this class, and I wish I could suggest it without hesitation. Unfortunately, the effort put into constructing the exam, or lack of effort, puts a pretty bad light on the class, in terms of class structure. When a professor weights 50% of the exam for your entire grade – meaning it will really be the only tell of your final grade at the end of the semester – and then leaves loads of severe mistakes and incorrect wording in the exam, it feels disrespectful. When so many questions are corrected to the class mid–exam, it becomes very easy to get even further confused and confuse what the exam is really asking or testing. If you are going to put less effort into the final exam to ensure it is testing students on the content of the course, not on the ability to confuse students with strange wording, then lower its weight to the final grade.

Again, I loved this content and topic, it is a critical element of economics and understanding social behavior. I just wish there was care and consideration into the class testing structure. If, as a teacher, you do not care about the concept of grading, then you should lower the significance or the final exam. If you do care about grades and critically testing student knowledge, that is fine, but then ensure you actually create an exam that tests students on the course material, not their ability to interpret a passively written sentence that hasn't been spell–checked.

To note, every teacher is very nice in this class, and the guest lectures were super interesting to listen to. Take this course if you can get past the uncertainty of whether your final grade will be an A or a B+, and isn't predicted by any of your semesterly coursework. If you can, then you can really make the most of this course.
