What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class is so hands–on, and gives you an amazing opportunity to use high tech microscopes that Harvard students have access to. There could be more guidance from the course staff on how to use the microscopes (I mostly learned through the HCBI trainer and just figuring things out myself), but I can definitely tell how passionate and knowledgeable the course staff is about the course and helping students. The final project was definitely a huge ramp up in workload from previous assignments (this course has no psets, just a "take–home" quiz every class) but was very manageable in the midst of finals week if you plan at all.
I loved this class. By far one of my favorite courses I have taken at Harvard. While this is a gem in terms of content and fun working with microscopes, you do need to go to lecture and engage with the content as the optics part can be challenging. Also the grading is a bit lagged so you have to really be on top of your grades to make sure you are not failing!
It's a fairly low–intensity course, with lectures that may bore you. That being said, the "work{ they have you do is quite cool, mostly involving learning how to use Harvard microscopes.
This course teaches you about microscopy in an excellent hands–on way. You go from using simple light microscopes to more advanced microscopes at the Harvard Center for Biological Imaging with which you can create 3D images of samples! I have come away from this course feeling confident having the fundamentals to learn how to use any microscope that exists. It's an incredibly useful skill as a biologist and a lot of fun too. Also, there aren't many other opportunities to do microscopy as an undergraduate so this course is precious!
Its somewhat unorganized but also probably the most relaxed 2nd semester bio class you can take
This is a great class. You will learn a lot about microscopy, you will not learn much about biology. So, if you are enrolling in this to satisfy the premed requirement, don't expect to come out with knowledge necessary for the MCAT. However, the class is quite relaxed and I enjoyed the environment. Labs were also very chill and while they took a large chunk of time, they were very manageable. Sometimes expectations are unclear which can make the assignments confusing. However, the teaching staff is extremely kind and do want students to succeed. I felt comfortable reaching out to them with questions, concerns and always got a response back!
The labs can be painful at times but overall great experience and cool results!
This class presents a comprehensive overview of what discoveries in microscopy looked like in the past, and how the latest technological feats allow students to make their own discoveries today. While the first few weeks are quite busy with the extra lab times, this time ends up being really helpful when you are taking your own independent images. There are things in this class for everyone to enjoy!
This is a bucket list class! Super fun, exploration–based projects, and a manageable amount of optics taught to back everything up. The course introduces a bunch of microscopy techniques which you can try out during the final project, including some very recent developments. I had a blast doing all the projects for this class.
Are you even slightly interested in microscopes? This class literally gives you a microscope to take home for assignments. You also get to interact with the imaging core at Harvard (HCBI) that you would likely never interact with otherwise. This is a very unique class and while you may need to put in some hours from time to time, it is well worth it if you are interested in the class content.
This is a great class! Take it!
Take this class instead of LS1b. You will learn practical microscopy skills.
Take this course! Definitely take this over other intermediate MCB courses for your requirements. It's a fun course where you get to explore a lot of new techniques in microscopy. The grading is relatively chill and there's always room to improve your grades on your labs. It's a small course so you can build really good relationships with the professors!!
