This is a fantastic course! Make sure to get started on your independent projects early, but other than that, enjoy it!
This is such a unique and fun course that prioritizes learning and curiosity over grades. It provides you with the practical skills to confidently integrate microscopy into any your own research if you wanted and it was useful to your research question.
I highly recommend this course *if* you are genuinely interested in the topic and willing to put in extra effort for the independent projects. The course staff is phenomenal and you get tons of hands on experiences with microscopes! Do NOT just take this as an "easy" class because there are lots of small assignments and you definitely get out what you put in. The independent projects were a highlight of my semester––I just wish I had more free time to try out more images. I feel incredibly fortunate I was able to take this class!
I loved this class!! Definitely one of the most fun classes I have taken at Harvard. It is extremely hands on and exploratory. The two independent projects require a good amount of work, but the whole process is so much fun and I actually enjoyed taking the different images.
This class is amazing! It is super cool to learn about microscopes and then have the opportunity to take your own images and troubleshoot different techniques. I gained a lot of hands on skills which will be useful for research in the future. The course staff is also so nice and very supportive.
Amazing class!!! Very hands–on; you get to take a microscope home and try imaging in the HBCI! Coming out of the class with greater appreciation for microscopy and better understanding of how the history of cell bio unravels and how microscopes work
TAKE THIS CLASS. This is one of my favorite classes that I have ever taken at Harvard. The staff are great, the projects are fun, and the knowledge you receive at the end is rewarding.
To future students taking FAS–MCB 68, be prepared for a challenging but rewarding experience. The course provides a great balance of theory and hands–on learning, especially through the lab sessions where youll work with advanced microscopy tools like confocal and fluorescence microscopes. These tools are key to understanding cell biology at a deeper level, so make the most of your time in the lab. Stay engaged with the material, ask questions when youre unsure, and take advantage of office hours and peer discussions. The course is fast–paced, but its also a fantastic opportunity to develop skills that are essential for future research. Dont be afraid to troubleshoot and experimentits all part of the learning process!
I would definitely recommend this class. The teaching staff are amazing and the assignments are fun!
The class concept is very cool, and taking images of any specimens you wish is an amazing opportunity. You get to think through the lens of microscopy (pun intended), looking for specimens on your walk to class. You also get to be trained on super advanced HCBI microscopes, which is a once–in–a–lifetime opportunity. The lectures are boring, unfortunately. The teaching staff puts a lot of effort into them, but the material can often seem disconnected and more like a story from someone's life rather than an educational lecture. The lecture slides are super long, and it's almost impossible to take notes from them. But overall, it is one of the better courses to fulfill your intermediate–level bio req.
This class is amazing. You will get exactly what you put into it. If you want to get good with microscopes, this class will take more hours each week, but it is SO worth it!! This class has taught me invaluable skills for my scientific career!
The professors are passionate about the course material, the microscopes we use for the final project are quite cool, and the workload is reasonably chill!
