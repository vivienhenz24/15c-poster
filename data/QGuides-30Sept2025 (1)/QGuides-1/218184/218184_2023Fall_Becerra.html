Great class. Teaches you to critically engage with the concept of health and all its iterations in society
This is one of the best med anthro classes you'll ever take (or even more generally if you're interested in medical humanities/social sciences at all you should take this class)!! The faculty are INCREDIBLE and are so personable and accessible and make this class so fun. It is a lot of reading so be prepared for that but it is so worth it as everything was incredibly interesting and we had the coolest discussions all semester.
TAKE IT –– WONDERFUL TEACHING STAFF AND YOU LEARN SO SO SO MUCH!!!
If you are unfamiliar with social medicine but have experience in upper–level social sciences courses, this course will be wonderful for you! If you have extensive experience studying social medicine, this course can, at times, be a bit redundant because it both functions as an introduction to social medicine and offers deep engagement with the discipline. Otherwise, the class community is incredibly tight–knit, the teaching team is out of this world, and class is held in the Adams faculty deans' house with weekly treats!
It's a good course to take if you're interested in understanding healthcare systems on a macroscale. The readings make for good discussions (arguably too good in that you'll never have enough class time).
