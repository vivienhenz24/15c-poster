The best course Ive taken at Harvard, taught by the best professor! As one of the only non–social studies concentrators and a pre–med student in the class, Ive gained an incredible understanding of our healthcare system and feel prepared as a future physician.

Matt is an outstanding professor who strikes the perfect balance between structured learning and dynamic discussions, ensuring we get the most out of every reading. He genuinely cares about each student's paper topics and consistently provides thoughtful, detailed feedback.

The readings are meticulously selected, each serving a clear purpose in deepening our understanding. Matt fosters a truly unique discussion environmentone where even if you dont speak, youre actively learning from him and the diverse perspectives of your peers.

I came into the course with some knowledge of the healthcare system and its political complexities, but now I feel significantly more prepared as a future physician to better understand and advocate for my patients.
Matt is great! this class is much more poli–sci than other social studies tutorials and often focused on topics other than in the readings. Heavily methods focused discussion, much more suited for gov/econ/pre med people.
Take this course! SS98WD w/ Matt is a delightful course that we'll provide you with a diverse base of knowledge about American healthcare dynamic. It will also support you and provide you the space to do substantive original research on a topic of your choice. We even visited Countway in Longwood and looked at archival documents! This course had a great mix of Social Studies and non–Social Studies, which made it all the more interesting. I think its a great choice for either. The course helped me produce a paper that I'm quite proud of and we'll help with my thesis down the road! Thanks, Matt!
Be prepared for this course to be heavily methods–focused –– readings are often discussed from the point of view of how the author researched the subject, instead of the content itself or your reactions to it. Matt is very kind and thoughtful but also extremely leninet with deadlines, so be extremely careful if you're the type of person who will easily fly a past deadline without that pressure to meet them. A cool introduction to the social science of healthcare.
This was a fantastic junior tutorial to take, I strongly recommend you take it if you have any interest in healthcare/the American medical system. Matt is a wonderful tutorial leader, extremely engaged with the content and supportive of the students. When I needed to change the topic for my seminar paper, Matt was very flexible and allowed me to pursue new directions right away. I also really appreciated the scaffolding approach Matt takes to the final paper, which makes it a semester–long process rather than just finals week. I learned a lot form this class and I'm sure it'll be helpful with my thesis and other work going forward.
