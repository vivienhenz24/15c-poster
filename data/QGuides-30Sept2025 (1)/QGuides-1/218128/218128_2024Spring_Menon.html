For context, I am a STEM concentrator who took this class as an elective. I had not previously taken any courses in the English department, though I am an avid bookworm:

This was the type of class where I got out of it exactly what I expected: reading some classic novels, while getting important and interesting context about them. Professor Menon had phenomenal lectures and a lot to say about each of the books she chose. With that component, I have no complaints.

Once we hit the third (out of four) book of the semester, Little Dorrit, many of my peers began to check out and not do the readings, which led to much more dissatisfaction with the section component. The activities we did often felt like a waste of time, since when half the section was just looking to say something, conversation lacked engagement and productivity. This is not a dig on any of the teaching staff, just a comment on the reality of what happens when there's a lack of student engagement. Additionally I found that ~1/3 of lecture was wasted time, particularly the Q&A portion(s) in which students often asked peripheral/tangential/hyper–specific questions. Professor Menon had amazing and intelligent responses to those questions, but I wish that was saved for her office hours so that the 150 minutes we all had together each week were most productive to the whole.

I would recommend this class to anyone who wants to read the books on the syllabus while getting a more thorough understanding of them through lectures. The assignments were very fair and straightforward –– there aren't really any surprises, and Professor Menon is a very structured professor/lecturer. If you don't intend on reading the books (or don't intend on going to lectures), I would advise against enrolling.
