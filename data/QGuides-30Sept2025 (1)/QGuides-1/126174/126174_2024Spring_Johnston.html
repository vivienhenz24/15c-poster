Only take this class if you are genuinely interested in the content. While the final is a take home and there are only six psets that each arent very long, there is little material supplementing lectures, so you basically only have your notes. The prof and TF were both outstanding. Still, it was hard to tell what the direction of the content was, i.e why we were covering certain things because there were no units or concrete topics we covered.
Definitely worth taking. Problem sets are confusing but dont take long, You learn a ton for the amount of work you put in.
A great intro science class! I would highly recommend this class if you are interested in marine, atmospheric, or geologic sciences OR as a related field for OEB/other biological sciences. It provides a really great basis for the chemical and physical systems of Earth that influence biology. Dave (professor) and Haley (TF) are very supportive and create a relaxing and fun environment in the classroom. The problem sets can be challenging but there is always help available to complete them.
This class is my favorite one so far at Harvard. Prof Johnson (Dave ) is so knowledgeable and understanding. He gets to know the students really well and is always giving time for questions. Sometimes he's a little disorganized, but he just wants you to engage in the class so there's no stress about being tricked or messed with on pets. this class is both interesting and low–stress and covers real–world topics. RECCOMENDED WITH ENTHUSIASM
TAKE THIS CLASS. Lectures are fun, there is always help when you need it, workload is very reasonable, and most of all, the content is so relevant and extremely interesting.
Amazing class! Dave and Haley are always so willing to help and concepts are described in a way that makes sense to each individual.
I highly recommend this course. Professor Johnston is a great lecturer and really enjoys teaching. The PSETs are great, not long, and it is really nice to have a final take home PSET instead of an in class exam.
This is a really nice, pretty easy EPS course! It's very relaxed in pacing and Dave is super friendly and approachable. I didn't learn as much in this class as I hoped to, but for a 50 level course, it's really approachable for both EPS and non EPS concentrators. If you're interested in learning more about climate tech too, this course talks a lot about that.
No background is needed for this course. The strength of this course is that it is really an intro class. We go over the same concepts several times with each time going a little bit deeper into the foray. So, we are always progressing but nothing too jarring or too quick in this class. Dave always stops to ask if we don't understand anything and is really big on the whole there is a no dumb question thing. I really appreciate it as someone who doesn't have the best background for this kind of thing. I wished we had powerpoints and slides that can help with understanding because sometimes the lecture material seems to be sprouting from Dave's mind, and Dave is going to get it because he has a PhD but that leaves in a wild loop. The psets were different from the material taught in class because we are asked to deal with real data sets, which is really cool, but also really jarring considering that class only goes over the fundamental concepts. You would need to go to office hours to really understand what to do for the questions that require special equations. If I were to be totally honest, I think I can only apply those equations to the problems that I saw on the psets and it would not be applicable anywhere else, but still very cool regardless. Haley is very cool with grading and helps my understanding a lot with her comments. Haley will not be TFing this class anymore though because SHE GOT HER PHD and is too cool for school now. I would say this topic is relatively niche. You will be in class with people who are semi to very interested in this topic so group assignments are always fun and passionate.
I'd highly recommend taking this class!
