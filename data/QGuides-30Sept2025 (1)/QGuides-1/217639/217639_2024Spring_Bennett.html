Overall, I can say with utmost certainty that I do plan at pursuing an entrepreneurial business in the future, and I wholeheartedly credit this decision to this class. Overall, this is one of the courses at Harvard that TRULY changed my life.

Hands down the best course I've taken at Harvard. If you are looking for an opportunity to take an HBS–style course as an undergrad, take ES–94. As someone interested in entrepreneurship as a career path, this course was incredibly helpful and opened my eyes to a lot of unique parts of the startup cycle.
Make sure to read the case studies before each class so you understand what's going on. There is very little work in this class (and they don't take attendance in lectures) so it is very easy to not take the course seriously. If you want to truly benefit from the course, make sure to read the case studies, attend lectures, and attend office hours with the professors.
This course was a really great introduction to entrepreneurship (yet I still cannot spell the word without autocorrect's help). Using a case–study method, the class relies on the participation of students and participation is a large portion of the final grade. Because of this, if you aren't comfortable speaking in a larger classroom setting, it may be challenging for you to get the most out of the course. Also, conversations do tend to be dominated by the loudest or most confident group of students; be prepared to be pushy to have your voice heard. Developing this skill is essential to your success as a student generally soif reading that made you hesitantI encourage you to take the course and push yourself!

The cases are all very interesting. Do not get bogged down by the intricacies of the exhibits, which might contain more complex financial analysis. Most of the exhibits are not discussed in detail unless an instructor wants to emphasize something specific; in that case, there is always one student that can answer more informatively. The bulk of the cases are easy to understand to outsiders with no prior background in economics or finance. The guest lecturers were, by and large, very high quality. Hearing their stories was very informative and valuable. If you want to ask a question, be prepared to be loud.

If you want to start a business, I highly recommend this class. I learned a lot from the cases, instructors, and guests. Note that it can be difficult to get meetings with the professors for office hours. Again, that loud crowd will shamelessly book each and every opportunity, no matter how limited or how often they've attended. Reach out to course staff if you have been trying to sit down with someone and haven't been able to do sothey will help.
This course teaches content that perhaps no other course covers. Given how important startups and venture capital is to our society, I'd highly recommend everyone take this course. The people and guest lecturers that you meet are truly famous and highly–respected, and the practical education that you receive can't be found in other courses.
