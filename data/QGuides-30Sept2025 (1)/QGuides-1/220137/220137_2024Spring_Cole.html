Comments
If this makes a difference to anyone who is pondering which workshops to apply for: In this workshop, only fellow students provide written feedback; prof here limits his comments to in–class, verbal feedback. I don't know if that's the norm for most other beginner level workshops, but I do know that some other workshop professors (ex. Messud) do provide written feedback. Also, while prof is obviously an expert in what he does, there were some moments of racial insensitivity from him, where I/my work was confused with other people of the same race/ethnicity on more than one occasion –– some who were in my workshop and some who were not. With other profs at Harvard who have done this, they usually apologize in earnest. Prof here did not –– made somewhat of a joke of it one time and made excuses for it another time.
Teju Cole's great. He's really funny. I think what makes or breaks a CW course is more about the professor's personality than their actual writing. After all, every single one of the CW faculty are incredible writers, but not every single one is someone you want to hear yap for long swathes of time. But Teju Cole's personality makes this course both fun and informative.
TAKE THIS CLASS! I improved so much as a writer and student, and I loved the readings/workshop atmosphere. Really conducive to learning and growing.
This class was so fun, and I am so glad I took it. All of my classmates were wonderful and interesting, and reading their writing was a great opportunity. I really enjoyed the feedback sessions, and it did not usually feel like a three hour class. Teju could be abrasive at times, but he was generally a wonderful professor and quite insightful. I highly recommend applying for this course!
