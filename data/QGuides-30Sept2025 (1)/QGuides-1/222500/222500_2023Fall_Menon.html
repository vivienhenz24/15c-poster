I have very mixed feelings about this course. On the one hand, the syllabus is very well crafted and the whole notion of "reading politically" is one that has interested me for a while. Many of our discussions in class were fascinating and probing. And the class was just a lot of fun, partially a result of having a lot of great students in the seminar.

However, the course suffered from two main issues in my year:

(1) We didn't get any grades back until the last day of class. This is partially a function of there just not being many assignments, but likely this can be rectified in the future.

(2) More importantly though, while Professor Menon is awesome, super cool and smart, I don't think she's amazing at facilitating discussion. In nearly every class, significant proportion of discussion would come down to us discussing her opinions on the secondary sources / novels. In some cases, this was fine because it felt like there was some back–and–forth. But in some situations it felt she was sort of forcing her own perspective onto the class. I don't have a problem with professors expressing their views per se, but I think in certain cases here it came at the detriment to open, productive discussion. This is all to say that I think this class should just be taught as a lecture where Prof. Menon delivers arguments on the text. You could either do that where you spend the first 45 mins of seminar with her giving the argument and the rest in discussion, or just as a full–blown lecture class. That version of the course would be fantastic, I think, because Prof. Menon has clearly thought deeply about all of these issues and she could give the full version of these arguments instead of haphazardly presenting them in seminar.

So if it's taught as a lecture, I'd definitely take it. If a seminar, it's still a really interesting class with good readings but it might prove a bit frustrating.
