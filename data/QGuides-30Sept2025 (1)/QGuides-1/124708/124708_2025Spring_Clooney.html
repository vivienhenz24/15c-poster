What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Great class, great text, and not too heavy of a load; weekly discussions were graded pass/fail. The big group discussions made it hard to express myself easily, so I think I enjoyed reading and writing more than discussing.
I really enjoyed this course and the critical conversations that we had in class. I think the whole group was very knowledgeable about the subject matter and I was grateful to have the opportunity to learn from my peers and Professor Clooney (who has so much wisdom on this topic). I also think the Gita is such a fascinating text and as someone who grew up hearing whispers of different parts, it was really meaningful to properly dissect and compose the meaning behind the texts. For all those reasons, I would definitely recommend taking the class. The one caveat is that the papers were a bit difficult to write and I wish there was more guidance (ie. a rubric) on how to write them properly.
This class will take a lot of time with weekly reflections + 15 page midterm and final. You also need to have a prior understanding of the Gita and related Hindu texts to actually participate in and understand much of the discussions. If youre really truly super interested in the Gita then take the class, but otherwise skip.
This is an amazing class for anyone hoping to do a deep dive on the Bhagavad Gita. I implore anyone taking this course to ENGAGE WITH THE COMMENTATORS! Doing this lengthens each week's reading requirement, but it is so worth it and deepens understanding/engagement by miles.
Tough grading
If you are a student of Sanskrit, Hindu Studies, South Asian Studies, or a similar field, or are at all related to Hindu ways of thinking, TAKE this course. I waited 4 years at Harvard to take this course; it is not offered frequently, so when it is, capitalize. There is a difference between reading or listening to the Bhagavad Gita once during an afternoon or a long car drive, and internalizing its principles over the course of a semester via intensive reflection, study, feedback and discussion. You will learn to understand yourself and your role in this world, you will get a grasp on the truth of your thoughts, emotions, and desires, and you will grow in profound ways over this course (provided you put in the required effort).
