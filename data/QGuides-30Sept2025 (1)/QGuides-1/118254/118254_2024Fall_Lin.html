PSY 1900 is not a difficult course (for anyone of any level) nor is it a bad class, it is just unfortunately tedious and somewhat poorly organized. To figure out when things are due or what is required of assignments, students often end up having to ask multiple TFs and the instructor until a solid answer is given. There are just too many assignments and deliverables that do not offer any educational value (for instance, the lab videos need to be watched prior to section every week even though the exact same content is delivered in section). The lectures operate on a "random attendance" basis where they check attendance when they choose to; this would not otherwise be an issue if not for the fact that the lectures do not provide many critical insights to the material being discussed. In lecture, many of the statistical test examples used names and ideas from movies and cartoon shows; this was not in and of itself an issue, just that my expectation coming into this class was that I would have some idea of how to apply this to the behavioral sciences and using more concrete examples would have been helpful. Even if you have zero prior background in statistics, learning the material thoroughly will get you by –– but if you expect any rigor to the material being taught, please look elsewhere in the course catalog.
