What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I would recommend taking this class if you have some basic knowledge of African architecture and native building material, because if not, lectures could be disorienting or confusing. The class material is also very broad and expansive, and it could be difficult to follow along with all of the content because of how the material is structured weekly. It is also not very structured.
Highly recommend this class! Professor Blier is a phenomenal teacher who is well versed in the subject and presents it in an interesting way. The material spans across many years and regions giving you new and interesting material to work with. You also get to focus on your favorite subject for the final paper.
This is a lecture and reading–heavy class, and the readings, in most instances, are for your own edification more than in–class evaluation. Nevertheless, I recommend both attentive listening to the lectures and reading the assigned texts, as each is genuinely interesting! African architecture is so innovative and smart, and so often gets short–shrifted. If only we could've seen some of these buildings in person!
