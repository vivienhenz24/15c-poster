Take this class! Snacks every class to keep you energetic, engaging course material, and wonderful discussions
It was a beautiful learning community where we examined how we can care for each other in our work and daily lives. Everything I have learned in the class has already connected to my life and has allowed me to be more intentional with my work as an educator.
Take this course if you want to spearhead a meaningful and fulfilling project in a community that matters to you!
Care in Critical Times is an absolutely wonderful experience, especially if you have been struggling to set aside time for community service during the school year! Andi and Ami are lovely and kind instructors, and while the course session is rather long, the class is divided by a break and includes both activity and discussion, so it never felt too long. However, as you enroll in this class, you should note that you will likely need to have a partner for your care in the community project in mind by the first class in order to begin work on your project in a manner consistent with course deadlines. You should also note that the style of readings varies widely over the semester, so you will probably need to sample readings from several weeks to see if they're of interest to you; in general, however, the readings were manageable and not too difficult to parse, though the time required to complete assigned readings varied widely from week to week (from as little at 30 minutes to as much as 5 hours). Beyond the care in the community project, weekly assignments are rather sparse (probably less than an hour per week, averaged over the semester), so this course is super manageable if your care in the community project is an extension of an existing extracurricular effort (as seemed to be the case for most of the students in the class). I didn't find our discussions to be super academic, so this course coudl serve as an interesting introduction to care in an anthropological sense regardless of your prior background!
Highly recommend this course if you're interested in public service/thinking about working for a nonprofit! The teaching staff is great (both Andi and Ami care a lot and they're such kind, smart people). A big portion of the class is that you partner with an organization of your choice to work on a project that benefits a specific community (I know it sounds vague, but it's because it could truly be anything you want). I loved working with my organization and I've learned a lot about what goes into an effective partnership and what it means to be both an intellectual AND a moral advocate for the communities that I wish to stand with and serve. My definition of care has been radically changed and I will continue to bring the principles this class has taught me throughout my career. HOWEVER, with all of that said, the actual class itself wasn't necessarily my favorite: the care journals/audio explorations felt more like busywork than anything else and the discussions about the readings were not the most effective (it felt like a select few students led the conversation and a lot of the time the conversation lacked direction, there was definitely a lot of awkward silence and blank stares at each other). Also, some of the things we did during class felt pointless. Everything was definitely done with good intentions and I see the value, but personally a lot of it did not resonate with me. Would still recommend this class though, just be aware it's a decent time commitment (not the class itself, but you will be volunteering at least 3–5 hours with your organization on top of taking this class and everything else in your semester).

This class is really wonderful –– Andi is so thoughtful and intentional about the syllabus, class schedule, snacks she brings (/bakes), and how she creates a community. Very engaged/thoughtful class
I would highly recommend this course! Andi is an amazing professor and really cares about her students and the community she builds in her class. You will have many interesting discussions about the concept of care in all its different forms and meet many kind and empathetic students.
