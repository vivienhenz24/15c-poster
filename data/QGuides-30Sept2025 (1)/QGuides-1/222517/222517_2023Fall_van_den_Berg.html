Laura is an amazing writer and human being. I really felt like I learned a lot about craft here. She's also always willing to meet and read over drafts, which I found incredibly valuable, and I thought her style of feedback worked really well for me too.
Take it — by far the best + most useful workshop I've taken at Harvard and I've taken 5+ of them. Revision is so rarely taught + is very daunting to most writers, and this class really does deliver on its promise to give you a toolkit + an excellent mentor to get through it.
This is an incredible class! Anyone who ever aspires to be a writer should take it. The class helps you hone strategies for revision and opens your eyes to different styles of writing and different methods for improving a piece. Laura is wonderful, supportive, and gives great feedback. The class also has no specific writing assignments, so it's a great space for working on pieces you already care about.
TAKE THIS CLASSS!!!!
This workshop was really interesting because many of the different students in the workshop were working in very different genres, which allowed us to get a lot of different perspectives on our work. Laura is great and really excellent at facilitating discussion.
When you feel ready to take the next step with your fiction, take this course! The art of revision is a crucial skill to learn. I can't believe I hadn't taken a course like this sooner.
This is a great course! Finishing a piece of work is something that not enough time is devoted to, and this course is a fantastic way to build a toolkit and really reimagine how you think about writing!
This course was fantastic. Writing workshops aren't always offered again, but I really hope that this one is. The course is well–structured and organized, the readings are useful without feeling overwhelming, and the two rounds of revision with feedback are super helpful. The first third of the course (before workshops start) has a very light workload. Reading your classmates' work/writing critique letters and doing revision exercises on your own work makes up most of the time spent on this class. Once workshops start, be prepared to spend some real time and energy. There's some flexibility with deadlines for everything except your own workshop if you need it.
