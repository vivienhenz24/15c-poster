What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Professor Dymek is an amazing lecturer with a profound understanding of all the theoretical film texts she discusses in class. Pay attention in class and absorb her analysis like a sponge because her conceptual breakdowns of various theorists like Freud, Butler, and Laplanche, etc. help you parse out rather complex and dense texts and ideas you read about beforehand. She is incredibly intelligent and passionate about what she teaches, and you cannot help but be inspired to write, create, and have honest conversations about queerness and social constructions of various kinds, including gender and sexual repression/freedom. Take this class even if you are not queer, and maybe even especially if you are not queer!! There is so much to explore about why we act the way we do due to intrinsic natural factors or environmental ones. I went into this class with rather concrete ideas of queerness and social operation in my head, but came out with certain beliefs I've had challenged or reconstructed. She makes sure to ground her ideas in theoretical texts, and the film repertoire alone is a reason to take this class!

Every film is carefully chosen, and if you are aspiring to be a screenwriter, or filmmaker, this class is also for you. There is so much to learn from the directors of the films we watched: their choices for different shots, the mise–en–scene, etc. Professor Dymek also discusses these choices and their affects in class. There was no section, so it's weekly recurring and that is also just such a plus if you have an otherwise pretty packed schedule.

All in all, take this to be inspired by Professor Dymek â€” she's awesome and by the end of class you will be inspired to go out there in the world and make change –– to tell new stories and share new perspectives. Even for final projects, she's receptive to different ones: the classic essay format, or you can write a script and/or make a film and accompany it with a shorter essay –– of varying lengths depending on what you choose. She's accommodating because at the end of the day she wants to encourage her students to put things out there for the world to see (for example, publish a paper or post a short film on YT), and in my humble opinion that is what Harvard professors ought to do more of. She also makes herself available and will also support you throughout your process at every step of the way!!
