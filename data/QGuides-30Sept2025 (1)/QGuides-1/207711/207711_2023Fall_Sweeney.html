The class is great at teaching you how to identify and subsequently resolve clashes between society and technology. It aims to train "public interest technologists" who think about these issues. However, the course does not focus on the impact of technology on specific areas of society, such as democracy or the economy. These subjects are touched upon, but the course is really more about finding new clashes instead of solving well known ones, as students might expect from reading the course description or syllabus.
The dynamic duo of Professor Sweeney and Professor Beyrle made this course fantastic! They're both very charismatic, knowledgeable, and great lecturers. Professor Sweeney has actively shaped a lot of the issues we discuss in class, so her perspective is unique and valuable. Professor Beyrle runs the course extremely efficiently, giving the most in–depth feedback I have gotten in a lecture course. Take the course if both of them teach it again! Previous Q guides say that the course was disorganized, but it shaped up this semester.
Take this 100%. It's not high stress at all, but you'll learn more from it (even if you're a passive listener like I am) than almost any other course on policy.
This class is literally amazing changed the trajectory of my career and opened my eyes in so many ways. Everyone should take this class

This class isn't really what you would expect it to be, in that you aren't really looking at governments and the ways they have responded/changed in relation to technology. Instead, it is about how you can use research to study a specific area of technology/society clash. That was disappointing to me, although it may not be to you. It's a fine class that has its moments and does make you engage with current events in a great way. But you don't really have the interesting discussions on tech ethics or value/technology alignment that I was hoping to take away from this. It is really a lot more about the minutia of creating a "tech study plan" that to me was pretty unengaging (it could be interesting to you though, just not what I expected). I didn't find classes that interesting, although on occasion an interesting point would be brought up, and then go unexplained. First couple units are the most interesting, and then you get to the "framework" part of things.
A lot of assignments, but none are overwhelming. GO TO THE REVIEW SESSIONS!
This course was a lighter workload***, however, it moved pretty slowly and the pace made it a little repetitive. It was not the most riveting course I have ever taken, and Sweeney was gone most days so she did not lecture very much. However, if you are interested in innovation or startups this is a great course for you. If you are interested more on the government side of things, I would say that I did not learn any new information from this course in comparison to other gov classes.
This class offers a supportive and engaging crash–course in tech–society issues. Even if you start the course with little knowledge of tech science, you will rapidly build up a baseline of knowledge, and become well–versed in a practical, systematic approach to these issues.
