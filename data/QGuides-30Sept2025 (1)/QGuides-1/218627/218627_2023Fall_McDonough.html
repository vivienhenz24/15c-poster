What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This course was traumatizing, but in a good way. Shepherd is difficult to read at first so you will have to make time to fully give her your attention. I think it is important for students to have multiple philosophy classes in their tool box as this course is more self–directed. Overall, both Professor Simmons and McDonough are supportive of all their students.
If you enjoy philosophy and especially the history of philosophy, you should take this course. Shepherd is a very interesting long–neglected philosopher and Professor Simmons and Professor McDonough are awesome teachers. Moreover, the course is very discussion focused so you will learn a lot from your peers and have plenty of opportunities to share your own thoughts. Overall a great course.
Shepherd is an understudied figure in the history of philosophy, but her material feels right at home in early modern European philosophy. Alison & Jeff are extremely supportive and incisive with feedback; you will never regret taking a class team–taught by these two.
Lady Mary Shepherd clearly did not want the laypeople reading her stuff. It is dense, with long, run–on sentences trying to explain very complex ideas. I didn't understand a thing I read until I had gotten through both of her books completely. I think I needed to have spent about 5 hrs/week on the readings to have been completely prepared for class. That being said, I finished my final paper with an essay that I was truly proud of. If you're a masochist and want to put in the work, I think the result will be incredibly rewarding.
This is an amazing course that is very well taught and is excellent for learning how to do history of philosophy. Alison Simmons and Jeff McDonough are both absolutely wonderful professors!
