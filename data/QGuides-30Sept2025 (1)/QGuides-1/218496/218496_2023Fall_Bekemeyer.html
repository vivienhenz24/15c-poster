Great class! Aaron is fantastic, the readings are fascinating, and the in–class discussion is great. Aaron takes every comment seriously, which is rare in instructors –– he would always respond to a student's comment, and the board was always full by the end of class. You definitely need to do the readings, but when you do, you'll be set up for success.
Take this course only if you have preliminary knowledge on capitalism, crime, and punishment. Class discussion is great and you will learn a lot from this course.
Overall, this is a good course. Aaron is a great Section Leader with a strong understanding of the material. There is no busywork, as all assignments contribute to the final paper. It's helpful to have a few ideas of what your paper topic might be before enrolling in this course.
100% take this class if you are interested in 19th/20th century U.S. History and are willing to put in a bit of work. This is probably the best history class I've taken so far (as a junior in the concentration) and I don't say that lightly. I learned so much from the readings –– which are all awesome and very intentionally chosen so definitely spend some time doing them...or at the very least doing part of them since they are like 200 pages a week –– and the discussion is very centered on discussing the readings in depth so it helps you really understand them. There were weeks when I wasn't able to do the reading and it was fine, as Prof. Bekemeyer is so so nice and really just wants to help you (so if no one knew an answer he would help by asking more leading questions lol like he wouldn't leave you out to dry with the discussion). The assignments are a typical scaffolded history assignment structure where you lead up to a final paper on a specific topic you choose based on primary source material – pretty standard here. Prof. Bekemeyer and Sanjay are great – it's the perfect mix of seriousness –– decent amount of reading, discussion cannot be bs'd and the assignments also are taken decently seriously (although I wouldn't say are graded super hard, pretty reasonable). It's a great course for a history concentrator (like me) but it is also a good class for someone who is scared to take their first history seminar/might not have as much of a history background. It's pretty intense on the amount of reading and the final assignment is a 15 page paper creating an original argument based on primary sources (aka you can't just summarize what has been said about your topic before) which can be a bit tough if you haven't written a paper like that before, but if you are willing to push yourself I'd definitely recommend. Prof. Bekemeyer and Sanjay are so supportive and I highly recommend going to their office hours. Overall one of my favorite courses here and I'd recommend it to anyone at all interested (with the caveat that you need to put a good amount of effort/time in lol).
