What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Based on the ratings for this course in spring 2024, the course this year was much better, but still rough around the edges. Many of the lectures were pretty dry, but a good handful of them were more hands–on and helped you practice parallel programming. The assignments were also much more pedagogical this year, e.g., most problems would have you write code in steps rather than all at once. The final project (parallelizing a solution to whatever problem you're interested in) is still a bit of a pain. The milestones for the project are too front–loaded, e.g., after you present your midterm proposal before spring break, the next step is to just implement your entire project by the start of exam period. Mentors were assigned for each group, but without a concrete checkpoint between March and May, a lot of groups were kind of lost and scrambled to do everything a week or two before the deadline (which is kind of the group's fault, but it could've helped to have more structure in the execution of the project). Overall, the course is improving, and it could be a good choice if you want to learn some parallel programming without going nuts like CS2620, for example.
Take this class! Ignacio, Chuck and Chris are phenomenal and I'm grateful to have taken this class this semester.
Try to not just use GPT for everything and actually learn some cpp... Otherwise you can do this class super easily but not have learned anything.
It's a bit of a drag sometimes to get through assignments and other required materials but if you are into a computing field it's good exposure to stuff that may be useful one day. even if you don't retain all the info during it at least you will know what is out there and how to look for it. This course is like exercising: you don't really want to do it but you feel better after it and you know it's good for you long term.
The course teaches lots of useful content. I would have liked the assignments to be more challenging.
Not a hard course, may not be the most useful too.
This is the best course I've taken at Harvard and I strongly recommend taking it if you're even curious. The lectures are engaging, and difficult. The material is technically complex, but the assignments are incredibly reasonable (much easier than the material I found), which made me feel like I had some extra time to dedicate to reviewing the harder concepts from the lectures. The project topics are admittedly a bit constrained, and come project time I was spending probably 20–30 hours a week on just the project, but we chose a bad topic, so invest meaningful time early in a project design.

Your peers are also likely to be...unaccommodating... on the cluster towards the due date of the project, so plan accordingly

The teachers are incredible – I think Chuck, Ignacio and Chris all have a unique flavor of HPC they bring to the lectures, and the lectures are an awesome balance of material and practical coding or demonstrations. They know their stuff and are truly receptive on the Ed board to fairly technically difficult questions if you're stuck. If you ask for help, they don't let you struggle alone, which is really cool.

If you're curious, take this class.
