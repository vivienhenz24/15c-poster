This class is not difficult, but take it with caution. Everyone is always on the hunt for a gem, but make sure you're genuinely interested in not just the content, but the kinds of conversations you will be having.
For context, I'm a white straight woman who took the class. I will say my experience was not overly transformative. I've always been interested in activism, especially feminist activism. A lot of the time, though, I felt like a lot of conversations did not really require my perspective but I had to speak in section anyway for participation's sake. I found that the feminism module was a little bit diluted and heavily scrutinized in the class. However, the entire midterm paper did focus on it.
The workload is not crazy. Weekly discussion post, midterm paper, final project. Do with the readings what you will. If you want to indulge for academic vitality's sake, definitely do that. If you don't have the time, don't stress.
Major tips:
– GO TO THE ARCHIVES EARLY. It takes a while to find materials that jump out at you when you're searching through so many boxes. The process is very foreign to me as a STEM concentrator (and most people in general) so make sure to give yourself some wiggle room and at least a solid hour to sit in the archives and flip through. I had to go twice since I ran out of time. I also waited until after spring break to go. Don't do that.
– Go to lecture. They do take attendance and the Gened policy is strict.
Final verdict: Take the class if (a) you are interested in activism AND want to discuss difficult topics in section and/or (b) you identify strongly with one of the three social movements. If you're just trying to fill the Aesthetics and Culture requirement, maybe explore some other options first.
