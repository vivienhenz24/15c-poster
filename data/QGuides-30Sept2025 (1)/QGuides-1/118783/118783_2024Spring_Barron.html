If you are a music student looking to learn about the musical history of Gospel, do not take this class, because you will be disappointed. This is not an upper–level music class, nor even an introductory music class; it is a theology class. Beyond this, its also the singular most disorganized class I have ever taken: assignment instructions were continuously unclear; there was supposed to be a section component which just mysteriously disappeared after the second or third week; the professor showed up late to class on a regular basis; the readings were insanely long but also we never got tested on them; it was just a total mess. What should have been a fun and informative class was just hugely stressful because none of us ever knew what was going on, and as one of the few members of the 40–person class who did not grow up in the Gospel and/or Baptist tradition, I always felt uninformed and ignorant (but also, despite my best efforts, never learned enough to catch up). This class was just awful, and I would not recommend it in any way.
Where to begin... the teaching staff was amazing. I learned so much about the history of the black church in America, which I wasn't really expecting. The assignments were definitely very cool and unique. But they took up a lot of time... like seriously several all nighters before the final presentation because of how much was due all at once. It was intense!! This is definitely an upper level music class and not gem–y at all in my opinion.
It's one of the most interesting course I've ever partecipated to, personally I discovered a new world that I didn't knew.
Dr. Barron makes this class worth it. While some of the assignments are graded on a varied scale, the actual lecture component is fantastic.
Take this class! This is my very favorite course at Harvard. Traveling outside to see real gospel music in Boston is super cool. Every class was a joy to be in and I didn't miss a single one (and Im never very good with attendance). The professor has excellent music taste. Seriously you need to take this.
This class is incredible and may be one of your must unique academic experiences at Harvard. The professor and the students are also incredible, and taking this course will likely give you some amazing experiences.
I would not recommend this course to non–div school students. It really isn't a music class, it's a theology class. I also wouldn't recommend this class to someone who doesn't already have a strong background in African American Christianity. Finally, I wouldn't recommend it to anyone who expects it to be like a normal, organized college course. It truly isn't. It's the most disorganized course I've ever taken. Unless your calling in life is to be a gospel musician or preacher, don't take this class.

Very very interesting class in theory. All the aspects of this class were fun in abstract – however during class it was mostly Dr. B answering questions about gospel music and the black church from people who had no background knowledge. If you were raised in black church culture, or even vaguely familiar, it was not very engaging to learn what you already know. The courseload was VERY manageable and the content was pretty interesting so I would suggest the class nonetheless.
