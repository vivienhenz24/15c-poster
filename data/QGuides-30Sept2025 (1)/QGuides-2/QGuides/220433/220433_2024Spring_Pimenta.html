What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Undoubtedly one of my favorite classes at Harvard. Joana is superhuman, she takes such good care of you and gives such thorough and constructive feedback to your assignments. The type of things watched in class is definitely not everyone's taste, but I think it is rare to watch such material and these out–left–field screenings can show you so much about the possibilities of film, even if you don't choose to prescribe to their ideas. The turnaround rate for the assignments is quite intense (by the second half of the semester it's basically a short film every two weeks), so expect to put a lot of time into this. But it's worth it. Take on the challenge and try on the assignments, and you'll learn so much not just about cinema, but about yourself.
time commitment, too many assignments, rambly teacher
TAKE THIS CLASS if you need a comprehensive intro to film technicalities and plenty of inspiration for your future projects. Joana is very knowledgeable and always appreciative of effort and enthusiasm. A quick disclaimer, some assignments leave you feeling confused about their goals/expectations but sooner or later you come to understand that that is being done for your own creative freedom.
The course definitely focuses more on cinematography than photography, but is overall a pretty good introduction to film as a practice. You do have to come up with stuff to film, which falls into director territory, but there's no pressure to make narrative work if that's not your thing. This semester, ~20 people was just a little too big for studio–style critiques / made critique days go very slowly. Joana and Madi are lovely and have lots of thoughts and experience, and you watch a lot of films (I found some interesting and engaging and some horrible –– sidenote: if you're prone to vasovagal responses, you should *definitely* sit out the screening of De Humani Corporis Fabrica). Highlight of the course for me was learning how to use the various equipment, particularly the cameras.
