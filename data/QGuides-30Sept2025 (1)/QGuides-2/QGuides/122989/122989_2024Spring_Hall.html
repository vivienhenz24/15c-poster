If you are considering this course, be prepared for an intellectually challenging exploration of one of philosophy's most significant figures. This class demands active engagement and a willingness to tackle complex philosophical texts. You will benefit immensely from doing the readings thoroughly and participating in discussions. This course is particularly rewarding for those interested in ethics, philosophy of law, or anyone looking to understand the philosophical underpinnings of modern democratic values. Be ready to question and rethink your morality, justice, and human rights assumptions.

This course, being a tutorial, shifts its topic and proctor every semester. I consider myself lucky to have had such a great experience with the course because I think it is largely dependent on the proctor and topic. Nonetheless, one thing that seems consistent for this course is that you should anticipate putting in a good amount of work. Another word of advice is to pick a topic you are genuinely interested in because it is a great opportunity to really learn about it.
My course likely won't be offered in the future (Kant, with Luke Ciancarelli). But if there is anything with Luke or about Kant, take it. You won't regret it. Kant is so good, interesting, and important in philosophy. And Luke is such a wonderful instructor.
do the readings and attend every class – this is hard material but if you put in the work, it is an extremely rewarding class – never have i loved kant as much as i do now! it is hard though and you get what you put into it!
Luke is an amazing professor who knows any Kant–related material (and dozens of supplementary sources for it) like the back of his hand. He facilitates a great discussion section, and when the class gets side–tracked it is always to talk about something interesting and fulfilling. This was my favorite class this semester, and I would recommend anything Luke teaches in the future.
As someone who was a bit intimidated by Kant's name before taking this class, I can say that the experience of taking it nonetheless allowed me to grow as a philosophy concentrator and person seeking self–knowledge. The course is designed to start off a bit simpler, by focusing on the building blocks of Kant's ethics, but then adds nuances by considering debates on aspects of Kant's system or by reading the works of other contemporary scholars. The discussion–based parts of the class also allow students to feel involved in the course topics while learning from each other as well. Moreover, the instructor is organized and effective at presenting information but also in listening and taking student questions seriously. I would recommend any student that has an opportunity to take this class to do so and be ready to carefully engage with the material. This class is one that kept me thinking outside of the regular meeting times and one that I will carry with me as I continue my philosophical pursuits.
As expected, Kant is challenging but Luke is a wonderful instructor! He was able to break down concepts into more digestible forms and facilitate meaningful discussions. Biggest time commitment is doing (and trying to understand) the weekly Kant readings, but it's rewarding to come to class every week and realize that you understood more than you thought. Really enjoyed the class overall!
