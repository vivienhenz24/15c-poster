What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
10/10 recommend!! Amazing intro class for learning to use a camera and letting you go in your own creative direction. Sharon and Claude were very supportive, both creatively and technically. While there's an additional 2x a week meet up for digital lab where you work on editing your photos, the photography process itself is really a you get what you put in situation so there's a time flexibility to work with which was really nice during the busier moments of the semester.
SHARON HARPER SECTION! She's the best. The class really helped me push forward my understanding of narratives and sequencing in photography. I came in with some experience already in digital photography, but this helped me even more.
The course is great and if you have an interest in photography please take it. However, be aware that it requires a pretty serious time commitment. On top of the class, you have lab twice a week and setting aside time to shoot takes longer than most would expect.
Cool opportunity at Harvard! Try to be in the second critique group, especially if you tend to have busier weekend (one group always has to go a couple days early than the other)
I would warn them of the professor's teaching style – so disorganized yet highly passive aggressive and scrutinizing of any student errors, that were usually a reflection of her own disorganization.
Take this course if you want to learn something new and are interested in photography. It is a lot of work, so only do it if you are actually interested. Sharon and Claude were both lovely and I learned so much!
