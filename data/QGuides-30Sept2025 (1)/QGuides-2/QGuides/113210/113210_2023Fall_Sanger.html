I took this class as an undergraduate. Obviously, I do not know my grade yet, but it is certainly not an easy class. That being said, it is very doable. I will say, the actual class meetings themselves are mid at best; however, most of the value of this course comes from the incredible people who take it and the readings that you do for case memos. If you are interested in IR, I would recommend.
Best course at Harvard! Do not miss this opportunity.
This course will be a lot of work, but it can really teach you a lot in terms of HOW to approach understanding national security issues. It touches on a wide breadth of regions/topics (China, Russia, nuclear strategy, cybersecurity, etc.) so you won't get an expert level knowledge on any given topic –– it's more about being able to quickly identify US national interests and underlying developments in each situation. Lectures are incredibly engaging. Part of this is the 3 different perspectives you get –– Washington, press, and military (if they keep a similar professor lineup for next year). It's also not like most undergrad IR courses that tend towards theory. We spent most of class discussing current events and how they tied in with course concepts.
You will get out of this course what you put into it. Give the strategic options memos their due time/attention and you will learn more.
Not all aspects of the course will be satisfactory, but this is a worthwhile class to take nonetheless. The people you meet (including the instructors, guest speakers, but most notably your classmates) will have a lasting impact. The assignments are killers, but stick with them long enough and you will gain worthwhile skills.
Take this class if given the opportunity. A true capstone of a Harvard education. The Professors and teaching staff are out of this world and will share with you invaluable knowledge and perspectives which shift your understanding of the press, national security and strategy. The classes can be daunting as there are lots of students with prior experience and there is a lot of them talking. Make sure to go to extra case sessions and office hours – it was in these that I "learnt" the most. Lectures are often largely discussion based and could have benefitted from more teaching.
Best course at Harvard so far! /undergrad
This course might be the most interesting and informative course you take as an undergrad. It is a ton of work, but the lectures are superb –– easily the best that I've had in any lecture course as an undergrad. Assignments are tough and I would recommend going to office hours for additional feedback, but I would highly recommend taking this course if any of the subject matters interest you. Graham, Derek, and David are the best. You will learn a ton.
This course requires a written application –– if you get in, you should definitely take it! One of the most unique courses I have taken at Harvard. The professors are incredibly knowledgeable and clued into the national security scene. There are also expert guest speakers every other week.
