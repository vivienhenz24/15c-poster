Take this class! The teaching staff are the kindest in the world, and the lectures are always fun and interesting, with a lot of hands on components. The teaching staff are really good at explaining concepts very clearly even for people new to EPS. I took this as my science requirement and I'm really glad that I did. Above all, the sections also make this class totally worth it –– I got to see a lot of cool resources, museums and visualizer labs, at Harvard that I'd never been to before even as a senior, and they were always a lot of fun. I learned about a lot of rock names but more importantly, it was just heartwarming to be around the teaching staff's joy for geology and it made me feel more connected to this Earth we live on. Even if I don't remember all the rock names, it gave me a renewed appreciation for the Earth from a scientist's perspective, Overall, this is a really fun class with very little stress and really cool people and content! Highly recommend.
This is a great intro class if you're uncertain about EPS or are trying to fulfill a divisional requirement. There are a variety of Earth Science topics discussed, and the workload is quite manageable if your schedule is packed with other courses. A majority of the content is conceptual, not applied, and the field trip was very engaging for visualizing the class topics.
Take this class! Especially if you are a non–STEM student looking for a great intro STEM class. The material is super interesting, the TFs and prof are SO nice, and you will love it. The field trip we took was excellent too!

I loved learning about rocks and minerals and climate change and cool species that used to exist on the planet. It's really not an overwhelming class, and the teaching staff is so helpful. It's a great overview.

The teaching staff, especially Professor Fisher, were so supportive as well. I loved this class as a non–STEM concentrator because I feel like I learned a meaningful amount of information without becoming overwhelmed. Take this class!!!
TAKE THIS CLASS! If you are a social science student like me, this class is a perfect, stress–free way to get your science distribution requirement done and over with. You are not expected to know anything about the subject and the teaching staff are super nice and accommodating. Only 4 labs throughout the semester that are all pretty easy, and a midterm + final that you will be more than well–prepared for, even if you can't make it to class often.
