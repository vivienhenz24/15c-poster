Comments
Take this class! Professor Pizzorno is genuinely so enthusiastic about it, and it is a lovely class. I personally was expecting coverage from the Assyrian to Roman Empire (which is *technically* true), but you mainly cover Assyria up until the last two lectures, so beware if that is not exactly what you're looking for. I came out of this class a lot more interesting in learning about ancient history, so that's a plus.
This is a really cool class! What little ancient history I had been previously exposed to was limited to very western history (i.e., Ancient Rome and Greece), so this was such a great breath of fresh air. The history itself is super fascinating; it was really cool to explore how early empires led to methods of control that future governing bodies would also use. The class itself is super accessible and its clear Professor Pizzorno cares a lot about the topic. The professor also has some awesome visuals in every lecture; it's clear he puts a lot of thought into effective ways of representing information, and I found that to be SO helpful in a class that deals with occasionally dense information.
I did not enjoy it, so I would only take it if you are extremely interested in the subject matter. I did not feel encouraged by the TF or like I could be proud of my work due to the way she communicated, but she was welcoming otherwise. The professor's teaching style did not work for me, as he just talked and there was barely writing on the board.
