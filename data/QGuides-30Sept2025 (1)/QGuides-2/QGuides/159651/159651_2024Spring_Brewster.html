Comments
take advantage of junior year to figure out the actual deadlines and guidelines for it from the seniors.
DO RESEARCH FOR THESIS. Great way to cap off the work you have done as an undergraduate.
If you want to do a thesis in MCB you will be taking this course. It is a highly structured course that is like research for credit with added expectations. Outside of going to lab to complete your research, you are expected to attend workshops and complete intermediary deadlines as you work towards the final thesis. I found these workshops and deadlines extremely helpful to not leave all of my thesis writing until the last minute. Monique and Dominic have done a great job at making sure each thesis student feels adequately supported in their thesis journeys.
Writing a thesis is not worth it if you think "I'm already doing the research, might as well write it." You should only do a thesis if you are really passionate about your research, as it ends up taking so much more time than you expect, and can be really tedious. There is not a lot of guidance/support, so you have to figure a lot of things out yourself, or with your PI/mentor if you have a good relationship with them.
The MCB99 course was a good supplement to thesis writing, and low commitment. This allowed me to devote most of my energy towards actually writing the thesis and doing experiments, which I appreciated. Some of the course structure was poorly managed in terms of time, but it was nevertheless useful.
Your lab will make or break your experience with the thesis. If you have any concerns, even minor, make sure they are clarified before beginning the thesis process; the MCB department will not advocate for you with your PI, and it's up to the student to manage these relations.
