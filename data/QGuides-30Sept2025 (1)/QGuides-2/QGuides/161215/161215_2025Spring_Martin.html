This class is well worth the effort you put into it. This is one of the top classes at Harvard.
This class is worth taking because it's very manageable, and the rigor is not bad at all. Do not take this course if you want to learn a lot of new things about thermodynamics.
This is a good course, though not comprehensive. Scot is a good professor and likes engaging all students (be prepared for cold calling). Assignments and demos were all applicable to engineering. I am glad I took this course, however, if I wanted a in–depth thermodynamics experiences, taking stat–mech or a quantum–based course would be best.
Would definitely recommend the course! Great course material and very well organized class. You can really learn a lot about thermodynamics.
Would highly recommend taking 112, its a great option for thermo! Prof Martin really cares about this class and is very enthusiastic about the material, which makes the learning process feel more engaging. Problem sets are sometimes difficult but doable with office hour help
Great thermodynamics course that can be applied across most disciplines. Thermodynamics is often perceived as a difficult topic to comprehend, but I definitely think that Scot made it seem endurable and easier to understand. Some concepts felt a little blurry, especially when you're looking at diagrams. However, the books and lectures are mostly helpful. Section and office hours are definitely a key component of the course because that is where most of the application occurs. Also, be prepared to present every 2–3 weeks in this course. Most presentations are around 3 minutes and not too difficult if you put in the effort to learn the topic you want to discuss. Scot also likes to use cold–calling to induce classroom engagement. I personally learn better in smaller sections/courses so definitely take this course if you need or are interested in thermo.
This class is great for learned thermodynamics. The pace is nice and slow and it doesn't speed up throughout the semester which is great for more difficult concepts. Going to section is worth it because you get more experience with practice problems and it's a bit easier to ask specific questions. You'll always know what to expect with work because it is very consistent from week to week. I absolutely feel like I learned thermodynamics this semester and the real life applications that it has.
