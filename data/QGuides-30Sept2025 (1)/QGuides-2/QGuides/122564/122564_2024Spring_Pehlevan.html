If you're a freshman or sophomore planning to concentrate in Applied Mathematics, this course is a must! You'll learn a lot, starting from the basics, and delve into modeling mathematical problems in real–life scenarios. You don't need prior coding knowledge; they teach you everything from scratch. Also, there is minimal work outside the lecture. The sections are optional. I recommend you to attend if you are going to work in the lab.

I really enjoyed this course. I learned a lot about coding and its applications to interesting topics. The psets helped me me improve my coding and math skills. I would surely recommend that AM majors and non–AM majors take this class. It is worth it
This course is a gem that is also interesting and useful. It is a nice introduction to applied mathematics, and the course consists of 6 units each covering different topics (machine learning, information theory, etc.). The class has no exams, only 1 HW and 1 lab per unit (so 11 PSETS total). There is also a final project, and you give 2 in–class presentations . If you put some effort into the HWs and lab, you will have no problem getting an A in this class, and the grading is not harsh. Attendance is also only required on presentation days, which occur roughly once a month. Solid class overall, and a nice basic intro.
If you are interested in applied math, I would highly recommend this class. Cengiz's lectures are interesting and engaging, and the TFs are fantastic. The course is taught in modules so you get exposure to a bunch of different topics which is great. Go to office hours you will learn a lot from the TFs and your classmates.
For AM its close to a gem. PSets can be challenging but get help from others or go to OH and its fine. Don't have to attend lectures or anything really and there's no final so its mostly just about doing the Pset each week. Pretty easy class assignments wise, but content–wise is still a bit challenging at times.
