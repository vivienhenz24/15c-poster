What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
143b is a very useful class even for advanced undergraduates. It provides a more computational approach than graduate classes.
In 143a, you learn how to exactly solve the Schrodinger equation in two or three systems. In 143b, you learn a dozen different ways to approximately solve the Schrodinger equation in pretty much any system. 143b is almost a survey class in that it covers a little bit of everything; path integrals, band structures, stat mech, the dirac equation, approximation methods, etc. This means that you might not enjoy a decent chunk of the class if it happens to cover material you're not a fan ofe.g. for me, I didn't really like the middle half of the class, because it covered solid state and AMO content I'm not interested in. However, because of the breadth of the course, you will definitely take away something useful and you're probably enjoy at least portions of the course.
Girma is hit or miss. If you need a teacher to generate enthusiasm and excitement around a topic, he's your guy. If you're looking for someone who is more pedagogical in a traditional sense, then you may find it difficult to learn from his lectures. Although, while he is eccentric and wild, he is always there to provide support if you ask for help and his office hours are always available. Definitely worth shopping at least for one class if under consideration
Take this with Girma!! Quantum 2 is so important for all physics, and he goes above and beyond to give you a sound overview of the content.
Professor Hailu is an amazing lecturer and while the psets are hard, they are very much doable. It is definitely worth reading the lecture notes beforehand or having them pulled up concurrently during lecture.
