This is a good course if you are genuinely interested in the Caribbean, but you are required to learn a significant amount of information that if you are not interested in could be a problem for you. Doing the readings and paying attention in lecture is not only helpful but somewhat required to do well on the midterm and the final exam, but again, as long as you have a mild interest in the Caribbean that's totally doable and likeable.
I knew this course would not be the easiest coming in but chose to take it anyway as I was interested in learning more about the Caribbean. While I learned some interesting things and am really grateful to have learned from people like Professor Patterson and guest lecturer Wayne Marshall (super cool btw), I can't say that this would be worth it for everyone. Lecture isn't always engaging and I relied a lot on section like many others to get the key concepts. Even then, to do well on the midterm and final exam you have to know the material from readings and lecture. In addition to the final exam there's also an 8–10 page final paper which feels excessive especially since it's due around the same time (last day of class). I'm unsure how grading for the course is done, but I do know that it isn't the most generous with handing out A's. If you're looking to just walk away with an easy A this isn't the course for you. If you're willing to put in some time and genuinely want to learn about the Caribbean, I'd recommend.
Take it!! This course is very interesting, covering both the history and current state of the Caribbean. Professor Patterson is super knowledgeable about the Caribbean and very passionate about the topic.

Section mostly tells you what you need to know for the exams and paper, so definitely attend section because lectures don't discuss the structure of the assignment. Readings are dense, however they are not required, although they will help you engage with the material and do better on exams. The end of the semester is a lot since it is a final paper and a final exam, however it's a fair class since there is not much work throughout the semester outside of the midterm.

Overall, this course is great. I'm glad I took it and absolutely recommend it to those looking to complete the History gen ed!!!!!
