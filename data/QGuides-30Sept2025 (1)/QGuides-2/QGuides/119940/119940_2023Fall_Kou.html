What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
It's a solid, useful, not–too–heavy–workload course that will help fill in your foundational knowledge of probability and statistics and make you more comfortable with multivariate settings, particularly normal random matrices. For me it was also a really useful way to brush up on my linear algebra, because I was rusty. That said, a warning: if you're really well–versed in linear algebra, then the first few weeks may be boring to you because they are focused on reviewing key concepts, especially projection. Something distinctive about the course is that Professor Kou gives a lot of geometric insight behind the statistical methods we're learning. So for example, if you're taking this course, you've probably learned about t–tests, maybe even multivariate t–tests, but do you know what they are doing in terms of projections and angles?
This class starts off with a lot of linear algebra review (which was fantastic!) So don't get discouraged by the lack of statistics in the beginning, it all comes together eventually.

For the most part, assignments were sparse but pretty difficult. So, the average hourly work is manageable, but quickly picks up at times. For example, I spent 0–2 hours on the class for the first week, but close to 10–12 hours on the first assignment. And so on.
STAT 230 is a good course and teaches a lot of interesting material. Homework is usually not that hard except for one hard problem, and the final exam was similar. I think this is a good course for anyone who is doing the AB/AM in Statistics or a PhD in Statistics.

For prerequisites, I think people should have taken the following: Stat 111, 210, 244 and CS 181 / MIT 6.3900. Professor Kou spends a lot of time reviewing basic material but his explanation of things like LDA isn't ideal for someone saying it the first time. I think he does the more advanced material much better.
This is one of the most challenging Stat classes I've taken over the past four years, but I definitely learned a lot highly recommend, if you're interested in deepening your understanding of statistics and can put in consistent effort to learn the topics!
I would say that almost a half of the course material is covered by 244 in a more time efficient manner. The other half of the course is linear algebra heavy and very theoretical, so I would recommend taking this course only if you really believe you'd be interested in the topic. Also, be aware that there are no lecture notes available, so attendance is mandatory.
Definitely take this course if it's offered! Prof. Kou is a great lecturer and while the course covers truly a lot of material, you will not feel very stressed from week–to–week. Workload is manageable, and the geometric intuitions you get from lecture are very rewarding.
