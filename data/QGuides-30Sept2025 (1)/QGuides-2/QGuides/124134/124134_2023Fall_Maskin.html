Comments
Jimmy Stratton, the TF, is the single best teacher I've ever had. He is absolutely incredible. For that reason, I recommend this course even if you're an undergraduate considering whether or not to take it.
Rather than preparing by reviewing undergraduate–level microeconomics material, make sure to be familiar with some of the necessary mathematical tools (e.g. implicit function theorem).
Having taken 1011a beforehand, I found this course fairly manageable as I had some background with modeling (but certainly difficult & time–consuming, especially during Ed's half). I highly recommend investing time into developing the skills & intuition to set up and solve verbals. Eric's half was incredibly interesting!
Ed goes super super super fast in the first quarter. I still learned some stuff through lecture, but it's impossible to understand everything. Eric Maskin explains things very clearly and at a good pace in the second quarter.
If you are prepared enough, I think this is a great class that will give you the opportunity to have a very solid background in micro theory.
This course was very difficult for me, but I found the content clearly applicable to research so I am glad I learned it. My advice would be to do the homework with a group because you will be able to help each other through it. Collaborating will prevent you from getting stuck (and frustrated!) on the homework.
The class is a lot of work!
