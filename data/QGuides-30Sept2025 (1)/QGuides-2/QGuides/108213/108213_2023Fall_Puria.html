If you're an undergrad considering whether you want to take this course, I would do so! It may be a little difficult if you don't feel comfortable with reading papers, but the teaching team is super enthusiastic about the material and is very approachable. Talk with Sunil if you're hesitant about enrolling!
the class is great for an introduction to many concepts relating to auditory (peripheral) perception. The papers are interesting and it is great to be able to talk to some of the writers of these papers and the pioneers of the field. Lots of time will be spent reading the papers, but it will be worth it! This class is all about helping the students to learn and the feedback received on homeworks are exceptionally great.
It is a rich and dense course where you learn a lot about acoustics and hearing. However, it might require a vast time commitment if you don't have enough background knowledge on the topics discussed.
If you don't have a background in this area, the new material can feel overwhelming at times but the support is there for you!
Good course. There's a lot of material, but the emphasis is on learning, so the classroom atmosphere feels fairly relaxed. However, it is a lot of class time and there are often multiple assignments a week, so I'd only take this course if you're really interested in learning the subject matter. But if you *are* interested in sound and hearing, this is a great introductory course. Doesn't assume too much background knowledge.
