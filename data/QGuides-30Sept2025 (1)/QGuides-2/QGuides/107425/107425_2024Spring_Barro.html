Comments
The teaching staff really cares about students, which is great. You will honestly get out of this class what you put in. You could coast through and get an okay grade, but engaging with the staff and content will result in a much better experience. I recommend really being interested in the course topic if you plan to take this class.
Take it if you are very interested in the intersection of economics and religion and want to spend time researching the topics you choose for your essays. They give you a lot of freedom to work on whatever topic interests you but also expect rigorous work. I found it a fulfilling experience! (also satisfies both theory and writing requirements for econ concentrators)
This course has very interesting material (and guest speakers) and the workload is not bad. I think the only downside is the grading might be a bit hard to predict, but the course instructors are very accessible and very nice.
Good option for ec writing requirement. The class workload is very easy to manage as long as you start the paper a couple weeks in advance of the due date. Interesting a comprehensive look at the intersection of ec and religion. I wish the class had some more interactive component to make it more engaging (e.g. students presenting the readings, or discussion)
This class is interesting but takes a lot of self motivation. You cannot put things off to the last minute, and you will do best if you adequately prepare your research and ideas. Not all of the readings are necessary to do well but it is definitely helpful to have done at least a couple of them each week. The teaching staff is great and there are a lot of cool guest lecturers.
