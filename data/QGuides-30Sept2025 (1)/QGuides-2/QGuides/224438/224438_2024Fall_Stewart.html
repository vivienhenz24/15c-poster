TAKE THIS CLASS! Truly one of my favorite classes that I've ever taken in all my four years. The discussion was so engaging, and the merging of undergraduate with graduate/PhD minds pushed me to new limits of understanding. As long as you stay on top of the readings and are brave enough to participate consistently in class, you'll learn so much. Stewart is such an amazing teacher who cares so much about his students and will make sure you understand what you need to — he cares about your education, which is valuable in a place where few professors have the time or patience to do so. During the semester, I was thinking so much about what I'd learned and I know I will continue to carry that into my post–graduate life as well.
This was perhaps the most interesting and rewarding course I've taken at Harvard. Professor Stewart is ridiculously smart (both in general and when it comes to all things Walter Benjamin), and he provided an incredible survey of the life and work of Benjamin. Would take again if I could.
Enjoy !
The course is great, but you must be interested in this kind of engagement where you follow the thought of only one thinker, and an especially erratic thinker. However, even if you're not interested in Benjamin you may still enjoy this course. Professor Stewart is very very enthusiastic about the material and he really shows great passion for it, and he will help you understand its significance and to construct larger themes.

Do you want to read about Marxism in the age of imperial wars? Do you want to analyze image culture and modern art? Do you like funny little fragmentary writings? Of course you do. Take this class, man.
Take this class. Benjamin is a thinker who's uncannily relevant to our present moment, and whose methodological approach is worth turning over collectively in this level of detail.
Prof. Stewart is one of the most helpful but rigorous professors I've encountered at Harvard. Takes the material and the students seriously, but not a hard–ass by any measure. Very engaging and knows A LOT, even beyond the course topic. You will have to think hard and, as a result, work hard, but visit his office hours and he will make sure you are connecting the right ideas for papers. Take this class!
I would recommend taking this class if you're interesting in Benjamin or any of the things he writes about. It's very refreshing to take a class at Harvard that prioritizes depth over breadth. You'll also probably find that the class is really useful for other classes in related departments, and surprisingly relevant today.

This course has changed the way I view the world. Dr. Stewart is a fabulous instructor who creates a really homelike class culture. I was wowed! Perhaps my favorite course I have taken! TAKE IT if you want to be challenged and stirred and mind blown!

The class was the highlight of every week and I left buzzing with excitement, clarity, and even more questions than when I started with. I learned so much from Dr. Stewart and my peers. Really an amazing class.
