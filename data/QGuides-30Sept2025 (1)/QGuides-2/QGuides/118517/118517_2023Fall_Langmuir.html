This class is not a gem, but it is really interesting and thought–inspiring. Professor Langmuir is awesome and cares deeply about the information. The labs are kind of annoying and make you feel like an elementary school student, but most of the time you finish them (early) in section. As long as you complete those and study the resources for the exams, this is a very manageable class. Reading is helpful but definitely not required.

You should know when signing up that it's not really about climate change or exoplanets, although those are both discussed at certain points. It's more of a history/narrative of our universe and our place as humans in that context.

I still think if you are interested in the environment, this class gives incredible perspective about planetary creation and destruction. It's up to us to save our planet, and it starts with understanding its amazing history.

My advice if you take this class:
1) Pay attention in lecture, and use the lecture recordings to review. In lecture, it's important to get the big concepts that he's talking about, and then you can review the smaller details after. Personally, I found lectures a bit fast, which is why I liked rewatching the lecture recordings.
2) Read the textbook. It's a bit annoying, but it can definitely help with understanding. Even if you get everything in lecture, it can help fill in the gaps and really get how things work.
3) Start early. I really think this class is quite manageable if you just go to lecture and read the textbook as he covers each topic.
