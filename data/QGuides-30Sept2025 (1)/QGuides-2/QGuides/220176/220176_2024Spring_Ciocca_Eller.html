What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Course provides an excellent foundation to understand racial inequality! Small course size so you get to know the other students well. Prof CE is very available for feedback and support, and she is very attentive to student feedback, sending 2 forms throughout the semester and even changing the end of year curriculum because of feedback. Discussions were relaxed but engaging. Seek support when needed, especially for writing assignments
I would HIGHLY recommend taking this course. Professor Ciocca Eller is an incredible professor who is deeply committed to everyone's learning. The course gives you a strong theoretical background, as well as compelling case studies and encourages your own growth and reflection. While it is a challenging Sociology class and the discussion requires you to prepare for class, it is well worth the energy.
This class is 2–3 hours of reading per week (you should read efficiently). The discussions are moderately interesting but the class period is too short to get super in depth. We had around 15 students in our class. The assignments are 1 short presentation (5 min on a topic of your choice), 2 midterm papers (5pgs, pretty light) and 1 final research paper (15–20pgs on a topic of your choice). Space out your work on the final paper; it's no small task. Dr. Ciocca Eller is good at handling the material respectfully as a white person, and picks out great key points from the readings.
The class was great! Our class was small, so we had discussions most classes, which was helpful in seeing how others understood the course and getting a better sense of the materials, along with the lecture portion of these that gave an overview on the readings. We reviewed a lot of different understandings of race, and the course leads to reflection of your beliefs. Would recommend, and Prof. C.E is very kind and creates an environment that was comfortable and respectful so we all could share our beliefs. Every class started with a check in which helped Prof. C.E. Gauge the room and adjust accordingly, which was very helpful and thoughtful, especially on difficult days.
I really enjoyed this course because I learned about race in a new way and could analyze how the theories applied to the case studies. It was a really good academic learning environment. Students were really open about talking about their experiences but also relating it to the literature. I looked forward to this class and found the discussions really valuable. The reading materials were nothing too intense, but I will say it was a bit on the lengthier side. However, I think it is manageable. The writing assignments were really interesting and the instructor provided good feedback. Overall, if you're interested in thinking about how race creates social inequalities in education, criminal system, and more, I would suggest taking this course.
10/10, take this class! Great class, the professor is truly there to support you, and the workload is manageable. Fair warning, this course has a medium to high amount of readings (around 100 pages per week), so keep that in mind as you plan your schedule. Still, the weekly assignment workload is low
