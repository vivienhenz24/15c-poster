If you like math and are willing to put in a lot of time into this exam, take the class. The PSETS take up a lot of your week and the exams take a long time to study for. There is a lot of built in support for PSETS and overall questions.
Make sure you have enough time in your schedule to take this class! The 3 psets a week are longer and more challenging than 21a in my opinion. The math is a lot of fun and the course is well–taught. If you have a choice, take the course with Roderic. 22a is likely easier if you have any proof writing experience at all.
Hardest intro math course with the longest PSets –– in terms of how interesting the course content is, I liked it less than 21a but more than 1a/b.
Taking 21b off cycle meant that I had far fewer peers around me to help me but it also meant that if I went to MQC or office hours, I was much more likely to get all the help I needed so pick your battle. Also, all the exams had 20 true or false questions and were often where I lost the most points since they're purposefully designed to trip you up.
Avoid this class unless you are prepared to WORK! This was my third math class here and was REMARKABLY harder that 1b and 21a. This class made me change my concentration. Be careful!
Difficult content, but not worst than 21A. Definitely redo all the PSets and worksheets as a study method since the content is very similar to exams. Matt is an awesome course head who is willing to meet individually to help you too.
I loved 21b. I know that's a hot take, and I don't know if it'll apply for next semester because Matt won't be teaching it, but I think I really liked this course because for once I made a good move where at the beginning of the semester I went to Matt's office hours to ask questions on literally lecture 1, and since I didn't have Matt for section, his teaching was transformative on the learning I had during my own section. Matt's teaching gives a great balance between micro and macro understanding of the material. Just go to his OH, and you'll be set.
