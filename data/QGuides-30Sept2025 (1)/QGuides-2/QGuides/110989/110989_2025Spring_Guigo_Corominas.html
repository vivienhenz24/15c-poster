Comments
This class is wonderful! The course staff (CAs and TFs) are essential to keeping the class moving smoothly, and they effectively teach the material. While the concepts can be challenging, using the resources in the course and staying on top of the material are essential for success. The class teaches a lot of real–world concepts that are interesting and make the learning worthwhile.
Take this course but be prepared to do the work. Psets are considerably longer than psets in 21A. It is VERY WORTHWILE to get to MQC or Office Hours to ensure your thinking is correct for psets. Overall, the course is very well structured and direct, if you prefer practical applications of the math rather than proofs, theory, etc.
Take this class if you’re willing to put in the work! If so, you’ll really enjoy it—one of the coolest math classes I’ve ever taken, with a lot of great applications to everything from population dynamics to sound production. The psets and exams are quite fair, and although the 20 True/False questions on each exam can be brutal, the rest is totally doable if you study! Make sure to take advantage of office hours, CA help, and the video resources that they post on Canvas :) As a person who isn’t usually a fan of math, I’m pleasantly surprised by this class!
This is a tough class. The math is interesting, but as someone who did not need to take it for any requirements I would say that it was fun, but I might not take it again if I could go back in time.
This was a required course and I learned a LOT of valuable material. This course most definitely improved my math capability a lot, but the sheer volume of work seems to have taken away the joy of making the math click. I felt too often like I was just trying to get through the material and not absorbing it. Compared with 21a, the workload for me was roughly double, mainly because the material doesn't always build on itself like 21a did. Expect to spend around 3 hours on each of the 3 PSETS each week, and a few more reviewing stuff from class. Don't underestimate this class. The guardrails are in place, but if you're gunning for a good grade and/or want to absorb absolutely everything, make this class priority #1 for the semester.
After every week, make yourself a review guide with all the important formulas/ideas/topics from each section. All of the content builds very quickly, and it is important to stay on top of. Making the review guides helps you stay caught up and is incredibly helpful for exam review!
21B is SIGNIFICANTLY more work than 21A. PSETs went from about 1.5 hours per PSET to anywhere between 2.5 and 4 hours. This wasn't because the course got harder (I actually had taken linear algebra before whereas I had never taken multivariable calculus), but just because the PSETs were longer (consistently 5–6 questions) and linear algebra questions take longer on average.

Definitely make sure to go to quiz sessions for the late days or just for review purposes. Late days are really helpful in this course and I couldn't have gotten through it successfully without using them.
Personally, I liked 21a a lot better because it felt like all the content built on top of previous learning and the work was concrete. 21b is nothing like that, in that the content can feel disjointed and unrelated to each other, and a lot of the concepts are deeply abstract. I still found it an interesting course, but it takes a lot of time to understand what's going on.
