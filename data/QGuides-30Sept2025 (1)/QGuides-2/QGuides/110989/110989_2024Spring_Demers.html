Make sure you have the time for this class. You have 3 psets a week, which get longer as the course progresses (some take up to 5–6 hours per pset), 1 quiz section a week (if you want more pset drops — I 100% recommend), and 3 sections a week. In addition, if you need help with psets, you’ll be at MQC/Office Hours pretty regularly. I had a great TF, but I still felt lost in class most days.

Please please please do the pre–readings!! I know it can seem like a burden, but it will make your life so much easier. I only started pre–reading in the last like week or two, and it made my life so much easier, so please start from the very beginning. Also, everything in this course builds, so make sure you understand the material as you are going along, and ask questions immediately, as opposed to waiting for the few days before an exam. This course moves very quickly, and missing even one section will make your life a lot harder.

Exams always have 20 t/f questions and the rest are open response. I personally find the open response to be so much easier than the t/f. The t/f is absolutely brutal (on the second midterm, I lost all my points on the t/f section), because with the exception of a few, most of them are a) very nit picky and require you to find obscure counter examples b) test for a level of understanding that we just haven’t covered in class. Tbh I am not sure how I would go about studying for the true/false. Not sure if this class curves..

If you are taking 21b after 21a, be prepared that learning Linear Algebra is a totally different process. Whereas Multivariable is all about learning specific processes of computing and being able to replicate it in slightly different contexts for the exams, Linear Algebra is all about learning a set of rules in 5 different ways and being able to apply the rules in all kinds of contexts. The computation itself is not particularly difficult, but really fully grasping the concepts is. This makes the exams quite difficult, especially the true or false. If you don't understand a specific class, make sure to take the time right after to learn it and gain an understanding of it because it is easy to fall behind.

Unfortunately, I had a very busy schedule the semester I took 21B and couldn't attend any of the sessions nor office hours. But as many of the other courses in the math series, I feel like these classes operate as if they're the only class on your schedule. There were certainly some good things about this course; the exams were fair for the most part, and unlike other math courses, we had an in–class review session before each exam. If you like math, as I do, then you will probably find the content to be pretty interesting as well. However, if they don't shorten the PSets, you are in for some ridiculously long ones, even longer than those of Math 21a. Some of them have at least six multi–part questions, some questions having up to eight parts. In short, make sure that you can start your PSets early to hopefully avoid feeling discouraged as you struggle to keep up with the workload. I would strongly recommend that you do not attempt to take this class alongside any other heavy STEM courses, like STAT 110, but if you do, be as on top of planning and scheduling your study time as you possibly can. Also, try to at least skim the pre–class readings, as many concepts are not entirely clear when presented in class. Finally, beware the 20 true/false questions on every exam!
