Really interesting class. A diverse array of topics are covered but there are a lot of really thought provoking lectures. Would recommend to anyone who has taken at least stat 210 and wants exposure to interesting ideas.
This is a great survey of many topics at the intersection of statistics, AI, and data science. Do not expect to become really well–versed on any given topic. I think the idea (which I agree with) is to expose you to many of these topics that you can then explore in more detail based on your own research interests. Professors Meng and Daoud are great lecturers, and Kyla (the TF) is one of the best TFs I've ever had.
This is an amazing course! I thoroughly enjoyed my time in this class. I learned a ton and everyone on the teaching staff is so thoughtful and approachable. I highly recommend this class to anyone who is interested in using statistics and machine learning in the social good space! Professor Meng is super enthusiastic and is such an engaging lecturer, and Kyla really went above and beyond as a TF to help the students.
Really well–considered course and materials. Youll take out what you put in – discussion is great in the class. It is possible to do get by without doing all the readings (they can be a lot especially if you lack background on that specific topic) but doing them helps a lot with understanding classes and makes the homeworks go much quicker. Great lecturer and TF!! Honestly all round amazing course, one of my favourites. And you get the option to do a super cool course project with a lot of feedback and freedom
The more you involve yourself in the course, the more you learn.
288 is academically life–changing. Some of the most fascinating topics in statistics and data science. Take it!
The best part of the course was the other students in it – it's a great group of students interested in using advanced statistics to improve the world. Prof. Meng is a funny and engaging lecturer, but the lectures often leave you wondering what you've learned. The material fluctuates between a superficial survey of hot–topic statistics issues and attempts at deep mathematical explorations of particular topics in sampling, causal inference, and machine learning. The psets and projects seemed to not rely on the lectures much, so you often didn't know whether you had learned new material.
The class is great, and Xiao–Li is wonderful.
