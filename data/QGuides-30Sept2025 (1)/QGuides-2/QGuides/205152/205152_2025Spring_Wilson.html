If you are looking for an introduction to medieval literature –– or perhaps to fulfill your earliest guided elective for the English concentration –– this is a good course to take. The floor for entry is fairly low and Professor Wilson makes complicated ideas more accessible.
An interesting course overall with a great syllabus. The lectures and class discussions can vary wildly, I assume, based on your group––but overall is a good class. It makes medieval literature very accessible.
This class is the best class I have taken here at Harvard! The professor is extremely nice and enthusiastic about the subject. The class itself is very interesting, even if you have no interest in fanfiction. Though if you are interested in fanfiction or medieval stories (or medieval–esque books), this class would be one of the most fascinating one you can take. The creative assignments are fun to do, and for the essay, you can use AO3 as source for one of them
– This class was so cool! We got to read, classic medieval texts and also learn about old and modern adaptations of them. My favorite part was the creative projects, like writing a fanfiction and the open ended final project. Class discussions were lively and interesting. I recommend this class to anyone interested in Chaucer, in understanding medieval reading culture, or in fan fiction.
This class is great if you have an interest in medieval literature OR fan fiction/adaptations! We spent a similar amount of time on both and I appreciated the background on both topics. It feels like a lot of assignments and sometimes it was confusing about what you could write a paper/creative adaptation on, BUT the papers were not bad and there were creative options for a couple. Professor Wilson is also really helpful if you have questions about the department and want to learn more.
I definitely recommend this course. Professor Wilson is great and super understanding. The grading in this course seemed a little harsh at first, but overall it was not too bad. Lectures were mostly interesting, and there was a nice balance between group discussions, Professor Wilson's Speaking, and time for individual comments. Sofia was an amazing TF and super helpful during the SSGK portion of the course. I will say that you definitely get out what you put in; the course is not unreasonably demanding, but you cannot do absolutely nothing and still pass.
What a FANTASTIC class!! I would strongly recommend if you have any interest in medieval literature, adaptations, or creative writing. You will analyze movies, poetry, songs, fanfiction on AO3, and medieval texts as well. Class is consistently engaging, and you will learn so much!!!
Class discussions were a bit strange. Readings are good if you put importance on them.
Be prepared for your view of what you think fiction is to change.
I highly recommend that you take this class. You definitely don't have to be an English major, just enjoy reading, learning new things, and having so much fun with assignments. Professor Wilson is such an engaging and dynamic lecturer, that you will feel like you are always learning and improving.
