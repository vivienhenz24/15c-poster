This is a solid gened! I came in knowing next to nothing about capitalism (even what exactly the term means), and left having a solid understanding of how/why the US economy is the way it is today. Keep in mind that this is a history class, not an economics class, so you start by studying the economy of the native American tribes in the 17th century, then talk about colonial America, the pre–Civil War period, and then the modern developments in the 20th century. My only gripe with the class is that it is very US–centric (unsurprisingly, given the title), so it only answers the question "how did the US become capitalist" and not "how did capitalism become a thing in general". The class also assumes a somewhat detailed understanding of American history coming in. As an international student, I never really learned who the Pilgrims were, what the Civil War was about, or that cotton was grown in the US while growing up, so I definitely experienced lots of gaps in my understanding while trying to do readings or listen to lecture and had to put in extra time to compensate. And hearing things like "I'm sure you all learned that in the second grade" was sometimes a little discouraging. Oh and the other thing is that there's a lot of work – three major writing assignments, weekly reading notes, lecture summary notes after every lecture, midterm exam; but lectures themselves are really good especially for a gened and they do a good job of ensuring you pay attention. Overall, I'd still recommend taking it regardless of your background especially if you don't know much about capitalism/economics.
