Professor Vega–Durn is very kind and warm towards students and encourages us to think deeply about the assumptions we make about migration. Professor Luce, who I believe was visiting this semester, was also very engaging but was a bit overbearing at times with her photojournalism expertise. Overall, this course was good and technically was not difficult, but what was frustrating at times was the lack of cohesion between assignments. We did not always receive feedback on assignments (e.g. journal entries, Headlines) and some assignments were not on the original syllabus that was presented to us at the start of the semester. So I felt at times that the course was a bit disorganized
Take this class. Professors Vega–Duran and Luce are phenomenal, accessible, and willing to personally invest in you in a way that I haven't found in any other part of the university. The course is a fantastic mix of applied journalism techniques, interpretive theory, policy, and interesting guest speakers from the professors' combined networks. If you're interested in migration as a policy area or photojournalism this class will push you out of your comfort zone to grow and become a better advocate or journalist.
My favorite class I have ever taken at Harvard. The professors care so much about the course, the topics it covers, and their students. The class is so interesting, and the assignments are so fun to do. The conversations we have are so important, and you learn so much. I loved it and would take it a million times again.
I genuinely appreciate the diverse range of guest speakers who were invited.
Take this course! All of Professor Vega–Durn's courses are the best. It is clear how much work and passion she and Professor Luce put into this course. It also offered such a unique opportunity to interact with professional journalists! Highly recommend
TAKE THIS CLASS. Prof. Luce is so passionate about the course and you will learn so much. The photojournalism element of the course is amazing, not just because you get to talk to incredibly engaging and knolwedgable people from the field, but because through guest speaker sessions you learn about borders from people who have been there themselves. Every class felt like I was getting a VIP run down of current events. Prof. Luce also has so many stories to share and they are all so relevant to what is going on in our world now.
If you are interested in migration and would like to branch out into ways it can be expressed (ex: photojournalism), this is the class for you! Professors Vega–Duran and Professor Luce have an incredible amount of experience in their respective fields and facilitate visits from leading journalists and photojournalists. A very unique class experience that has creative assignments and mixes academia with creativity.
Great course! Would recommend for all aspects of it!
This class is amazing! My favorite class I have ever taken! Nearly every week a different journalist spoke to us – so many invaluable insights into the industry but also different geographical areas around the world and their conflicts/social problems. Extremely relevant and not dry or overly theoretical. Every week we look at different newspaper articles and so incredibly inciteful to see how language has changed regarding immigration since Trump has taken office. I now want to take EMR as a secondary. The Professors are extremely knowledgeable and so so accessible and really want to get to know you and help you. This is the kind of course that motivates me at Harvard. Truly special and unique. It made me critically evaluate what I see in the media. The assignments are built to develop critical thinking and skills rather than pointless memorization. Please take it!
