If you don't want to code, do not take this class. The code is not covered in depth, and there are not enough course materials to learn it on your own. If you don't mind coding/have experience with r code, then this class will be fairly easy.
This course is a lot better than the average math class. If you are actually interested in doing math that relates to the real world than take this course. I felt that the 4 quizzes and the final were not very hard, because the course actually cares about you and doesn't try to make you struggle like other math course will. The only thing about this course that I wasn't aware about before is that we do a fair amount on coding with R but it is very basic and easy to learn coming from someone with absolutely no coding background.
This is a good class! Might take a while to get used to Colab, but everyone is really nice and the teaching team really wants to see you grow!
Take this class but be aware of the fact that it is newly established and has less resources than other math courses. I didn't hate my time in this class and feel that it is the best option for those that need to fulfill it for Econ or anything else, but be prepared to spend at least 5 hours on PSETs when you have a coding component, if not more. You are expected to write code on your own which is incredibly difficult without decent exposure to it in class. Brendan and Matt are great, but they have limited office hours and CAs are only helpful if they have had coding experience before and can actually explain it to you. Make sure to know EVERYTHING in detail for quizzes and the final exam. This course is a good alternative if you want to avoid more intense math, but be aware that it is actively being shaped. GO TO OFFICE HOURS.
