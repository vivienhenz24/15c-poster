Start the homework's early.
Enjoyable class overall. An opportunity to learn a lot, but also it covers many different materials briefly and doesn't dive deep into particular platforms. It's the nature of the class so if this is something for you then definitely would recommend :). Also great guest lectures!
Absolutely take this class if you can! It's a great mix of "normal" (math/physics on the blackboard) lectures and talks from people working at the forefront of the quantum platforms field. The amount of work is super reasonable as well, the problem sets are not too long but help solidify the concepts from class. As someone more involved in the theory/computing side of quantum information, I feel like this class really helped round out my knowledge of the field.
This course was chill and pretty good for its first iteration. I would say it is more of a topics course since you learn a bit about each of the different platforms that people have built for quantum information processing. Assignments are mostly reasonable (there may have been a few very mathematically tedious problems) and the final presentation is very chill.
This course is one of the best that I have taken at Harvard! This field is new and very fast–evolving, and there is no systematic way of learning wide–ranging topics. Professor Semeghini did an amazing, amazing job of discussing various quantum platforms studied worldwide. The course has taught me how to read papers in different subfields of AMO physics, and I think that is a unique feature of this course – it really brings you to the forefront of research.
If you want to be an quantum computation and information experimentalist, this course can help you determine what platform you want to work on.
The PSETs can be a lot. Be weary of taking a class, in addition to this one, which also requires weekly PSETs.
This course will be a very good beginning if you want to continue the research in AMO experiment direction.
