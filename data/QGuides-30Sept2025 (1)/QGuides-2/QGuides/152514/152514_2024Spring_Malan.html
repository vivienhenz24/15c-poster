What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
CS50 online is not bad if you have coded before! Actually super manageable and the work is really enjoyable.
The content is really fundamental and useful for further learning of CS or any related skills. This is the most valuable course I have taken at Harvard. Though it is challenging, I am confident to self–learn a new language now. I will continue to work on my final project, an iOS app, after graduation (this is my last term).
CS50 is a good introduction to computer science. It is holistic and covers a number of useful topics and does not go very deep into any of them. If you are trying to figure out if you are interested in computer science, this could be a good class to take.
I took CS50 to decide if I wanted to study CS. The answer to that question is now No. I loved my TFs and I thought the Psets were good however what the course is trying to teach is not what I wanted to learn. The course is great if you want your hand held while learning how to make a concrete final personal project. I am confident I can now Google my way out of any problem. But I am not confident that I have any deeper understanding of CS than when I started. CS50 essentially takes the mile wide and inch deep approach to teaching. The course could be greatly improved by taking the time to really teach us a programming language with its applications. All CS50 really teaches you to do is code fun projects in your free time. Also, the course is very much not designed for STEM concentrators. If you want the programming you learn to be applicable to your STEM pursuits, this is not the class for you.
On a side note. taking CS50 online was the best decision of my semester. For a course I did not love, being able to watch lectures on x2 speed was incredibly helpful and it lessened my frustration with the pageantry of the course.
Finally, the grading in this course is very frustrating. It is almost impossible to get full points on a pset. No matter how many hours you spend at office hours, you will always lose a few points. This doesn't sound like a big deal but it can be very annoying if you are used to classes that give full credit on assignments. It is also something to keep in mind if you switch to letter grading.
It moves quickly, so make sure you understand the fundamentals from each lesson. They will all come together nicely in the final project.
Overall, take this course if you want to see if you like computer science.
If are considering CS50 spring:
can say it is worth it. Yes – you have less hype (hackathon, fair, dinners), but if you find those nice–to–have, then spring is a decent choice. Malan is a GOAT lecturer, but watching him on X1.75–2 speed is a good call.


If you are debating between CS50 to CS32:
as I have not taken 32, here are my two cents – CS 32 is good you don't plan on taking any other CS course (or a few) and are looking to finish the course with a nice applicable skill set. However, CS50 makes you think like a Computer Scientist. it might be too broad at times, but I feel like I can now learn other CS skills more easily than a CS32 veteran would.
The course holds your hand, take time outside of the class to explore coding further so that you don't feel as though you need a crutch. Interestly I also took 6.100A at MIT and they really complemented each other. 6.100A does more difficult coding but only does python so accessing their problem sets online will really make you feel like you have more of a grasp whilst doing cs50 will give you better overall understanding of computers and coding languages.
especially if computer science isn't close to your area of study, you should take this class. It will open your eyes and made me realize that I like this sort of thing!
Take this class if you want to learn to code. It moves pretty fast, so just be prepared to put in the work if you lack coding experience.
This class is the best possible introduction to computer science
