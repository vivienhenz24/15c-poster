I think that the class will surprise you and get you excited about the RNA field as a whole
Really great class on RNA biology. Very good lectures and Ryan is super knowledgeable and kind. No busy work which is amazing. Very happy I took it.
I really enjoyed this class. The topic is interesting and the small class size makes it feel engaging and personal. The two lectures per week alternate between the professor lecturing and students giving presentations on a weekly paper we all read. There are only two psets, which means there isn't too much work, but it also means each is worth 15% of your grade. I struggled to concentrate and take effective notes at 9 am, which came back to haunt me when I did the psets, since the class slideshows are not very in depth. However, the TF was incredible and helped me think through the problems I was struggling on, so I ended up doing well on them.
Definitely take this course if you're interested in RNA biology. The course covers fundamental concepts as well as recent developments in the field. The class starts from basic concepts and gradually prepares you to throughly understand the contemporary research directions, opportunities, and more importantly how to read, digest scientific literature and figures on RNA biology. The psets, although time consuming, are super helpful in digesting the concepts, techniques, papers covered in class and brainstorm on how to design your own experiments for specific problems. RNA bio is not necessarily within my field of research but is something I wanted to learn about in detail. I was looking forward to the class every week. By far, the best class I've taken at Harvard. I will adopt Dr. Flynn's teaching style when I become a professor myself.

Be aware though, the assignments take a long time, but it's definitely worth it. Dr. Flynn is such a good professor.
This is a wonderful course. It's intellectually interesting, with diverse scientific topic coverage, clear and helpful course materials, and several well–spaced assignments that are academically engaging but not too high–stress. Ryan's lectures, in particular, are awesome, as he's a clear and natural speaker who is also obviously a young leader in his field and knows an incredible amount about this subject. Whatever scientific topics in biology/biochemistry that you're interested in will probably be in here somewhere, because RNA is everywhere. Selection effects lead to a very chill and smart cohort of students who are willing to get up early enough for 9:00AM lectures at Sherman Fairchild. Ryan and Petar are both legends. I took SCRB 157 as an elective and would recommend the course completely.
