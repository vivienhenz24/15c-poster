Fisher is BRILLIANT! If you are taking Hum10 or not, this course provides great analysis for both works with reading guides that make comprehending such comprehensive works of literature easier. While yes, it might build up with response papers, knowing the course is pass/fail really helps you focus on your ability to understand the work as opposed to caring about your grade. Fisher also provides amazing feedback on every single part of your writing, really helping you develop your analysis better than Expos courses do. GO TO OFFICE HOURS!
This class changed my life. My roommates started a quote wall in our room and I swear every week I came back from this seminar I added a new quote from Ulysses to the wall. the most beautiful book with so many moments where Joyce described an emotion or human experience that I never even realized was significant enough to be conscious of, but I've experienced my whole life. I just love this book. I see myself in all the characters and I understand myself differently now. I could debate the strengths of Leopold bloom and Dedalus all day, and literally walking out of class my classmates and I would often continue the discussion. there could not be a more perfect book to read freshman fall, it is all about the conflict between ambition vs. just sitting back and enjoying your life and the fruits of your labor. not to be too on the nose, but this is a tension I experienced often having just gotten into harvard and unsure what my next immediate goal in life was

Caveat: I am embarrassed to say I did not do all the readings. that is my biggest regret. because you can gleam so much from discussions when you have your own thoughts and preconceived perspectives on the reading. do not take this class if you are not willing to read ulysses. it sounds obvious, but freshman fall has so many demands on your time and your energy. not everyone is willing to dedicate multiple hours a week to reading James Joyce. this is a very self selective group

HOWEVER, as someone who skimmed the readings (and wished they'd read them more thoroughly) it is definitely possible to glean major revelations solely from listening to Dr. Fisher in discussions. if you are crunched for time I recommended skimming the chapter, and then really reading the pages Dr. Fisher recommends we write our essays on each week, because those pages are often what we will discuss in class, and it is nice to have deeply analyzed a page or two on your own, even if you didn't have time to read the whole chapter.

go to office hours! Dr. Fisher's office is so cute and he has the deepest perspectives on any topic you bring up.

discussions are longtwo hours a class. I recommend taking notes, and annotating your book, especially quotes you love. you will feel so intellectual, and it will give you nice memories to look back on. plus everything dr fisher says deserves to be written down and memorized

I am so grateful I took this class what a beautiful opportunity I will always treasure
Take this course only if you are committed to the study of literature, particularly one so complex as Ulysses. Although sources are provided to help you get a sense of each chapter, you must do the readings if you truly want to understand Ulysses. Going into this course, be prepared for chapters that can, sometimes, be tedious. As long as you are enthusiastic about the readings, section discussions will be interesting and worthwhile. Professor Fisher does an excellent job of guiding you through the two works of literature you encounter in the course. He gives consistent feedback on the weekly response assignments. Make use of the audiobooks and chapter summaries when you read Ulysses. Go to office hours––I wish I had scheduled time earlier on in the semester.
Like Hamlet, this class is a tragedy. Professor Fisher is so awesome that is hard to leave class feeling like you have done enough. Professor Fisher makes an effort to get to know you as an individual and provides weekly feedback on your work. It is an incredible experience and I highly recommend. Right now, it is winter break and I want to re–write a few papers to make them better.

Anyways, Professor Fisher is super friendly and understanding with extensions, class absences, etc. I would highly recommend taking this course if you want to talk about literature. Do not get psyched out by the course title. The main way that complexity shows up in the course is that the two texts are highly complex, but other than that the course functions a standard literature seminar. We participate in a guided discussion for two hours once a week. Lots of fun if you are into that.
What a brilliant course, taught by an even more brilliant professor! This course was the highlight of my semester. The books themselves are, of course, quite exceptional. Hamlet and Ulysses are very, very important works of literature that I am very happy to understand now. But I think that the really central experience of the course is Prof. Fisher's way of teaching Ulysses, which honestly surpasses many of the best monographs on the book. Speaking as someone who has dug into the secondary literature on Ulysses in my free time, the words of Prof. Fisher on the text are literal gold for the serious student of Ulysses. I truly wish he'd written a book about the novel or Joyce in general. He is also incredibly kind, and cares about students so deeply (in some ways, he resembles Leopold Bloom in his kindness and care for how others might feel). An honor to have taken this class, seriously.
Plus, the weekly papers are actually really good in the long term in making sure you get something out of the course, especially since it is very easy to give up without further incentive for a book like Ulysses. A lot of work for a freshman seminar, but if you are committed to learning from probably the person who knows Ulysses the best at Harvard, this is your chance.
I would tell them that they should take this class if they are excited about the content. Prof. Fisher is amazing and such a sweet and kind person.
Take it if you like literature!! There is more work for this first–year seminar than others, with one single page (~500 word) response paper a week and weekly readings, but if you are willing to put in the work the class is such a gem (in the sense that it teaches you so much)! Genuinely changed the way I think about life and the world around me. Professor Fisher is also such a great professor. He meets you where you are at in discussions + it's so interesting to even just listen to his perspective and analyses.
Please do not take this class if you are simply looking for a fourth, and think that a pass/fail Freshman Seminar will be easy. In addition to reading two chapters of the behemoth that is Ulysses, a one–page paper (single spaced) is required each week. Professor Fisher gives detailed, insightful feedback on each one, and will make it known if the quality or your work is declining as the semester goes on. The seminar itself is not at all Socratic, but mostly lecture with optional audience participation (I mean this neutrally). Please do take this class if youve always wanted to read Ulysses (or say youve read Ulysses) Prof. Fisher illuminates not just what is brilliant about Joyces formal innovations, but what is funny, human and heartwarming in the text.
