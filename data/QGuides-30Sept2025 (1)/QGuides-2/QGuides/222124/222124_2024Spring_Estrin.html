What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class is incredible! If you have ever wanted to touch and pick up a 2,500 year–old vase with your hands, this class is for you. We went to the museum every week and took advantage of Harvard's incredible vase collection to learn about ancient Greek vases. It was a very engaging and inspiring way to learn about art history. Seth is also a very kind professor and reasonable with his assignments.
Professor Estrin is a fantastic seminar leader. This course goes to the art museum to see artifacts almost every week close up, and you get to be very hands on with Greek vases yourself! I was initially worried about this class becoming repetitive over the course of the semester, but it reaches so many different areas to immerse yourself in an understanding of Greek culture and antiquity, but in the context of vases. So it felt Very fulfilling each week! Definitely enjoyed this course, one of my favorites.
This course is an absolute must–take if you are interested in Greek history, myth, and antiquity! Between the sheer amount of hands–on time with Harvard Art Museum object collections, the interesting weekly readings and discussions, and a variety of engaging field trips, the experience presented by this class never failed to be enjoyable and value–packed. The workload is also not bad, save for some lengthy weekly reading assignments, and the class is also quite approachable to someone with less of an HAA–related course background. Would certainly recommend giving it a look!
Take this class! It is so fun and such a unique, interactive Harvard experience. I enjoyed doing the readings, assignments and going to class every week. I would also recommend this class for non–HAA students; Professor Estrin is very accessible and makes the discipline so digestible.
