This past semester has been incredibly transformative: it has not only enriched my understanding of leadership but has fundamentally altered the lens through which I perceive community dynamics. The discussions in section and during the lecture Q&A sessions were not just academic exercises, rather they were vibrant exchanges that allowed for the exploration of diverse perspectives. One of the standout aspects of the course was the emphasis on experiential learning. Through the midterm shadowing project, I found myself immersed in the challenges and complexities that my leader of choice faced, an opportunity that I never would have had without this class encouraging me to reach out. This hands–on approach not only solidified theoretical concepts that I had learned in class but also equipped me with invaluable skills that transcend the classroom setting. Over this past semester, I found myself not only learning critical skills and applicable leadership theories but also developing a network of like–minded individuals with varied backgrounds and experiences who shared a passion for positive change. The sense of community within the course mirrored the very principles it sought to teach. This course has left an indelible mark on my intellectual and personal growth, shaping the way I perceive leadership and my role within my community. I am grateful for the opportunity to have been a part of this transformative journey, and I am excited to carry the lessons learned into future endeavors, both as a leader and as a member of communities committed to positive change.
