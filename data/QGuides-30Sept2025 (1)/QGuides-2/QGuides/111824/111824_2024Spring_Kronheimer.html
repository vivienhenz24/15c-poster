This is a very good course to take if you're interested in geometry. Prof. Kronheimer is a great instructor who always offers important insights to these topics that are really hard to find in any text. The material offers up many connections between different areas of math. This is all in all a very great experience; it is a very difficult course and you will put in your hours, but with everything we covered with Prof. Kronheimer, it is well worth it!
The course overall was great! The lectures could be hard to follow, but you end up piecing together the information you need to understand from the problem sets and by talking with other students. Prof. Kronheimer is also great to talk to in OH to get much better analytic intuition, and his approach to the subject is very analytic overall (as opposed to a complex/algebro geometric style). Overall, 10/10, but remember to switch branch and ramification points and give Laplacians the correct sign.
This course is ok. I think the problem sets are very well designed, and whenever Prof. Kronheimer writes he is very insightful. I also think the final project is a very nice assignment. However Prof. Kronheimer is not a very clear lecturer. He is rather quiet and mumbles and isn't exactly clear in what he is doing at any given moment. Also, this course would benefit from having a clearer direction. I personally felt that a lot of the time I did not really know why I was learning a given result and ideas were just being floated in front of me. Even just a few words about why we are covering the results in the order that we are and what guiding questions there are in the course would be very helpful, I think.
Riemann Surfaces is an essential class to take for students interested in algebraic geometry and analysis/topology. This course will provide a firm background in the study of complex manifolds of complex dimension 1, and in doing so, will provide you with an opportunity to learn about the deep connections between complex analysis and algebraic geometry over C.
this class is great + prof. Kronheimer is very kind. you should be prepared to spend some extra time with the textbook if you want to understand the material fully, but it's worth it!
Great course on Riemann surfaces with Professor Kronheimer, but unfortunately will be much much harder next year when it's taught by Siu. This version of the course had a very topological perspective (with Professor Kronheimer himself being a low–dim. topologist), and totally alternate courses could have been offered with much different perspectives (when McMullen teaches it, there's a lot of hyperbolic geometry, sheaves, cohomology, line bundles, etc.).

Overall, the lectures are rather dry, and the official textbook Donaldson is not good at all (as all of Professor Kronheimer's graduate students will happily tell you), but you can clearly tell that Professor Kronheimer has a good heart and is genuinely very excited about the topic, both in class, through his many many excellent handouts, and through conversations in office hours. This translates to very interesting and instructive homework problems, which I had a lot of fun doing.

Overall, I'd recommend this course, especially if you're an alg–geo / alg–top pilled math concentrator that doesn't want to do any actual analysis (the material of Riemann surfaces gets very geometric in a lot of places, though be warned that analysis is sometimes involved too!).
Great introduction to Riemann surfaces. Difficult but also very rewarding!
Math 213BR with Prof. Kronheimer is great! The class is very fun, and Prof. Kronheimer is one of the nicest professors I've met! The materials are certainly very doable if you go to office hours. Math 213A is definitely not necessary.
