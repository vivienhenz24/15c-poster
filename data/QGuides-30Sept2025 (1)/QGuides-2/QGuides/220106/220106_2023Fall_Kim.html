TAKE THIS CLASS if you are interested in Asian American theater at all! I think Professor Kim is amazing, and many of the students in this class are immersed in Harvard's theater community. They're all so kind and well spoken, and you will learn just as much from them as you do from the readings. Even if you have not done any theater in the past, don't let that deter you!
TAKE THIS COURSE!!! If not for the incredible syllabus, take the course for Ju Yon alone who is an absolute icon and one of the best seminar leaders. As someone with zero background in theatre, it definitely was a little daunting at first but 100% worth it. You get close to people in the class through the scene presentations, and the discussions are overall a very supportive environment with a lot of space for growth and learning. 100% recommend!!
This course is very discussion based so as long as you do well in that environment, I'd absolutely recommend taking this course!!! Ju Yon is incredibly supportive, understanding, and genuinely puts her heart into each piece of this class. As a theatre lover, I was also super happy to get exposure to a lot of new works that I hadn't encountered before and found some of my new favorite plays through class readings. There's definitely a decent amount of reading/watching outside of class but if you're interested in building up your vocabulary in theatre or Asian American work, it's absolutely worth it.
This is a phenomenal class! Prof. Kim is one of my favorite professors that I've had since coming to campus. The class was less theatre–y than I expected (we didn't read plays aloud, only acted for two select presentations), so be prepared that the theatre is engaged in a very intellectual manner rather than as art itself. The class is a great community, and Prof. Kim makes sure to set up a few opportunities for students to go see plays in Boston, NYC, etc.
Just an amazing course. I took it as an English elective, and I never knew I was interested in theaterI couldn't even really name an "Asian–American play" before this classbut it introduced me to so many great works, and I truly looked forward to class every week. The weekly discussions on Canvas were always so interesting already, and continuing them in person with Ju Yon's lecture to guide us really forced me to think deeply about art and identity. She's an amazing professor, she was always happy to meet and discuss, and she gave such helpful feedback on all the assignments. You get such a great mix of students of different backgrounds, both culturally and artistically speaking. It was so fun to step out of my comfort zone and act and dance alongside such talented people during scene presentationsand you couldn't have caught me dead participating in any sort of performance before this class. Take it!
This is a really fun, unique, and interesting class. The range of plays that you read is really comprehensive and the connections between each are really interesting. The performance–based assignments are actually low–stress even for somebody who is not in the theater world at all and they reveal new details and understandings of the pieces that are really fascinating. Prof. Kim is very sweet and engaging as well. Overall, an excellent class that I found myself connecting to and referencing in my other social science courses as well.
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! Ju Yon is so so knowledgeable and kind, the plays are fantastic, and my classmates leaned into the class which led to really meaningful discussions. A lot of the students in the class will have a background in theatre (and perhaps even Asian–American theatre)if this is you (as it was for me), this is an incredible class to learn more about the Asian–American theatre canon with an amazing professor.
Take this class, if you're considering it!! As long as you are on top of the readings, you will get a lot out of the class and out of the discussions. Students from many different artistic backgrounds take this class, and it is all the more better for it.
