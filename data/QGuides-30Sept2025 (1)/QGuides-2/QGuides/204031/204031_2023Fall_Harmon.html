READ THIS REVIEW IN FULL – I tell it as it is:

This is a phenomenal and really fun course. Ana is an absolutely amazing instructor, and the TF will definitely also be awesome (Cassie was wonderful!), and they are both very kind people. This course is a tremendous amount of fun, and you will be pushed to move in front of others and connect with yourself and others in ways that you have never before.

If Gaga, namely moving in bizarre and vulnerable ways in front of others, sounds really scary, or if you know you wouldn't be interested in such a thing, do not take this course because it is "easy". First of all, if you are clearly disengaged because you are "too cool" as you look around and don't commit, this will probably be noticed by others and by the teacher and TF, and *gasp* you might not get an A (I don't know how the teacher grades, just a guess that doesn't seem unreasonable). Second of all, forgetting the grade, your lack of effort can affect and bring down the overall vibe, which is just unfair to your peers who are interested in creating a really special, intimate environment where people are happily moving and connecting with one another in a judgement free zone. ASK YOURSELF: Can you envision yourself moving your hips, spine, arms, legs, neck, and face muscles slowly and in different ways as you make eye contact with a stranger? If this sounds terribly unpleasant or **doesn't sound exciting**, in that you don't aspire to be able to do that, I recommend you really ask yourself why you want to take this course and how you reasonably imagine yourself expressing yourself and exploring in an environment where something like that could and will happen.

On another note, the founder of Gaga, Ohad Naharin, has been characterized as moving in a "feminine way", and there is an intimacy and softness of heart that is integral to large parts of Gaga. If you in no universe can see yourself delicately (or sometimes aggressively) moving your body in "weird" or unusual ways, I just don't recommend taking the course. Gaga isn't supposed to turn into a rave (where you move weirdly but in a lost, drunken, out of touch way), but a conscious exploration of our bodies, our movement, and how we connect with these and others around us THAT DOESN'T BREAK DOWN IF EVERYONE STOPS AND STARTS STARING AT YOU (This does happen a few times in groups! Can you dance in the middle of a circle with 30 people? Are you EXCITED to do that and be VULNERABLE in that way!! Reflect –– if you were raving and everyone stopped, you would probably become uncomfortable and lose interest. These are fundamentally different ways of moving with others).

I loved this course and encourage others to take it if these things sound interesting. And if one is a bit shy, that's natural of course, and you will get more comfortable throughout the course. But I think the most important question is *Do you want to make yourself deeply vulnerable in front of others?*. If not, don't take this course. If you truly want to make yourself vulnerable and are open to exploring intimacy, take this course. I hope you've found this helpful, and I really encourage you to reflect before signing up.
