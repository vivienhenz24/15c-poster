This course does a great job of building computer vision principles from the ground up. I came in only knowing some buzz words about deep learning in computer vision, but I really enjoyed learning about more classical approaches and tracing how the field has evolved over the ages. Professor Zickler's passion for the field is evident in every lecture, discussion post, and conversation, and that made learning really enjoyable. Weekly assignments were generally very reasonable, with a few that were more open–ended and challenging. Familiarity with Python and NumPy helps tremendously.
Great class that covers in–depth many facets and topics in computer vision. Some of the prerequisites can be flexible, but a strong background in some of them would be very useful.
Take it. The midterm tests the basics, although it covers a lot of topics.
This class is great! I would totally take it. I've taken MIT CV class before taking this class, and I would take this class before the MIT CV class because I think this class covers some of the materials more in great detail, and I think you will benefit a lot from taking this before that course.
I would really recommend taking this course! Todd is an absolutely terrific professor, and you can really see his passion for the material and for helping his students learn—particularly if you attend his weekly office hours, which I really recommend. The work is decently middle–loaded—the PSETs will get harder after the add / drop deadline, but the last few are shorter than the middle ones. Be ready to do a ton of debugging (most of the PSETs is usually programming in Numpy), but you'll get a ton out of the lectures and the overall content. Plus, it's so cool to finish a PSET and create your own optical illusions (and actually understand how they work)! This course is a fair amount of work, but I think that the work is definitely worth it!
One important thing to note is that this class is more of a applied math class than a computer science class. There's a ton of programming on each PSET, but most of the programming is using Numpy to model mathematical equations. The final (which is in mid–to–late November) is pretty much entirely applied math–oriented, so be ready for that.
Take it, but make sure you have no need for a good grade and are willing to just digest as much as you can each week. Can be demoralizing if not, but it's a fun course.
Every time I said I was taking CV everyone asked if I was taking it at MIT. I looked into it and the MIT course is massive and may students said they had a difficult time getting help. The CV courses at Harvard are smaller and excellent. I really recommend taking this course from Prof. Zickler. He really pushes you but you come out on the other end having learned a lot. He's the one putting you through all of it, so show up and ask questions in his office hours.
This course is great! Professor Zickler is a fantastic lecturer, he did an amazing job of explaining how computer vision algorithms work. I got a lot out of this class–––it's one of the best I've taken at Harvard.
This is a challenging course but worth it because the topics are very interesting and you learn a lot.
