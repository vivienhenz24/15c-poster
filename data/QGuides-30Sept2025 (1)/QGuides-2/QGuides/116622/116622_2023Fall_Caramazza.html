Depending on wether you have a TF, lectures may or may not be recorded. If they are recorded, be grateful. If they are not you need to attend every class because without the context of what he’s saying you want understand what’s on the slides. And it’s the only learning material. And when you’re in class take a lot of notes on the lecture because weeks later when you look at a slide with one graph and no words you won’t know what the point of the slide is.
Also if you’re not interested in the subject maybe don’t take this class. The first lecture he gives is a pretty good summary of what the whole class will be about.
There’s no assignments which you might think is great now but realize what that means. Basically all your grade is the midterm and final. So where’s in another class if you get an 85 on an exam you can count on homework and section and other assignments to boast up your grade, an 85 on an exam is pretty much you’re grade. We begged him for extra credit and he allowed it. He’s a sweet guy. But they grade exams strictly, and it’s paragraph format so you have the ability to lose a lot of points.
Anyway, if you are interested in the topic you’ll have a blast. If you’re not, you’ll wonder why you did this to yourself.

It's a good precursor class to find a niche interest!

This class is fascinating! The material is super complex but Professor Caramazza is extremely passionate and willing to help. Although I was lost for most of the lectures, I enjoyed the material and it truly challenged me. One of the best classes I've taken.
I loved this class! There wasn't much work, and I found lectures very interesting and engaging. Professor was very available outside of class to clarify things if he couldn't in lecture (although he always tried).
The weighting of the grades were changed midway through the class such that the midterm and final became 50% of our grade each. In all, this is definitely a class where everything is staked on the exams, and that's something to be aware of throughout the course; be diligent and study the course material throughout the semester so you won't be as stressed when exam time comes. The teaching staff does really care about the students, though, and wants us to succeed. Our instructor spent 3 hours going through every single lecture again for our final exam review, which was super kind and an amazing testament to him as a teacher.

To that end, the materials were truly fascinating, and I really enjoyed Professor Caramazza as an instructor; he's a great lecturer and makes concepts very clear. I am glad I took advantage of speaking with him during OH because he's a truly great thinker and amazing to learn from!
Professor Caramazza and Maggie are the absolute best. They answer your questions and will make sure to take their time eplaining things if necessary (both in class and in office hours). However the class is a lot of material. This past year the class was based solely on 2 grades, the midterm and final, so if you didnt do the greatest on either it was rough. Thankfully there was an extra credit assignment for the midterm but thats not guaranteed (my suggestion: definitely advocate for what you feel is necessary along with your classmates, we only had extra credit because the call COLLECTIVELY asked...be smart yall, that's all I can say on that for incoming years). If you study for the class, reveiw the lectures, and take decent notes you should be fine though. But otherwise, it is definitely a malleable class. We agreed upon how long our breaks would be and had a few class discussions every so often to shake things up. Its definitely a long class though, if you have a short attention span like me, I certainly wouldnt recommend.
