This is a phenomenal course –– it's especially rewarding if you've been taking lots of physics and math courses and want to see all the material applied. Definitely take advantage of Eli (he is very approachable and incredibly knowledgable) and Elle was the best TF (hw help sessions were a lifesaver). Other than the take home final/some stress before psets were due the course is definitely very manageable.
TAKE THIS COURSE! Eli is a wonderful professor (truly one of the best I've ever had) and has a really approachable teaching style. He made an effort to get to know all the students in the class and always greeted everyone with a smile. The class grading is a mix of problem sets, presentations, a project, and a take–home final, almost all of which I thought were well thought out and fair assignments. The problem sets can look challenging at first, but go to office hours and you'll be fine (and fed!).
I was really not expecting to enjoy this class so much! I am not and never will be a physics girlie, but the passion with which Eli teaches makes the physics very cool and fun. It's just really cool to learn more about the ocean. The psets are hard, but office hours are open, available, and very helpful.
This is an excellent class taught by one of the best professors at the university. Eli is fantastic at explaining concepts in a digestible way in class and keeps lectures interesting with fun stories. He is very good at helping you build intuition for understanding complex phenomena before diving into the math to explain them.
This class was hard, but I felt that it was very fair. The problem sets were challenging and can sometimes feel unapproachable, but the teaching staff does a great job of explaining it. The pace is really good, but the lectures can sometimes feel dull when they are focused on deriving equations. This class is very math–heavy and strikes a good balance between breadth and depth. I learned a lot!
This was a really really good class but it was definitely hard! If you have to take this as a concentration requirement, I wouldn't shy away from it. Eli is a fantastic professor and makes the tough material digestible. He also adds in a lot of fun components to the class to balance out the challenging parts. The psets are difficult but there were only 7 of them, and we had homework help sessions (they were essentially a cross between office hours and section) that made them doable. It's definitely more of a traditional math/physics course than a conceptual environmental science class, but I feel like I really learned a lot of physics in this class!
As a complete beginner to the EPS department, I highly recommend taking this class! Eli is a phenomenal instructor who cares a lot about his students and extremely knowledgeable. He is very willing to answer questions and re–explain concepts until everyone understands. Class passes extremely quickly with the 5 minute breaks every lecture and Eli's jokes. This class has a good balance of learning about the different currents, how they are observed, and math modeling of currents and other ocean phenomena. There are also fun activities too! If you are interested in the ocean or currents at all, take this class!
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! Eli is an incredible professor, my favorite that I've learned from at Harvard, and this is by far my favorite course I've taken. Lcture was engaging, and despite the sometimes difficult math, Eli's charisma always made everything more digestible. He always circles back and explains difficult concepts, and never moves on before everyone felt comfortable with concepts. He's willing to work one one–on–one with hard material, and his office hours are a great way to get extra help. He also makes the class incredibly fun with a field trip, presentations, video projects, and zeta–drawing contests. He truly cares about every student enjoying the class and succeeding in it. Most worthwhile class I've taken at Harvard.
This is a very interesting course and Eli is a great professor. The topics can be a bit confusing at first and Eli moves through derivations quickly so be sure to go to office hours. Course notes posted on Canvas are comprehensive and help a lot with PSETs, and take home final is much less stressful.
