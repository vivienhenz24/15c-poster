While the course material may seem to lend itself to discourse that is driven by political leanings, this course is catered to understanding the law/constitution and the way that justices interpret it as opposed to what students would argue should be the moral way to interpret it. There are politically controversial topics raised in class (reproductive rights, affirmative action and equal rights), but the class doesn’t lend itself to political arguments revolving them. That being said, if you are interested in taking this class for political arguments I would recommend to rethink taking it. I, however, really enjoyed this class and the way that it was structured and would definitely recommend taking it!
Professor Fallon's course on constitutional law is excellent and provides a deep, rich background on today's binding precedents and the factors that shape how the Supreme Court decides cases. Where the course falls short, however, is equity — all students are required to pay hundreds of dollars for access to the casebook that the course centers on and which contains required readings for every week in the class. If you can't afford the casebook, you are unlikely to succeed in this class, and as an FGLI student it presented a major barrier for me. You'll learn a great deal from this class but you should not go in without a full understanding of the financial barriers that students who take it face.
