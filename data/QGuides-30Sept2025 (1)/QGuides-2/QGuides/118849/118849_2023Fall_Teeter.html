TAKE THIS CLASS! You will not regret it. Professor Teeter is an amazing lecturer, making this course an enjoyable way to learn more about the Pentateuch and the Former Prophets. Any faith background will enjoy the variety of perspectives provided, but if you are a Christian in particular, this course is incredibly meaningful to understanding the early history of the Jewish people and the variety of scholarly opinions surrounding the possible origins of these texts and the themes therein.
Highly recommend anyone who's even vaguely interested in this course to give it a shot. For those who are looking to learn more about the Judeo–Christian religious tradition from a more formalized structure, Professor Teeter does an amazing job breaking down highly, highly detailed and dense analysis into engaging and thought–provoking content. From a persective of pure literary analysis and interpretation, this course turns the Old Testament from what seemed to be a contingent, unusual, and extremely hard to read text into something that's just teeming with parallels, interesting meanings, and clear patterns. I was sold in the first two classes (maybe even the first) when Professor Teeter explained his approach to deconstructing and truly understanding this very specific form that is Jewish literature. For those who are looking to take this course for personal enrichment in regards to their own faith or interest in religion, I will say that this course has helped changed the way I read the Bible for the better. The extra criticism into the sourcing of the Bible combined with the immense focus on deliberate detail in the Bible actually helped increase my trust in Scripture as a trustworthy religious document for faith. On top of of course producing many more questions in regards to Christianity, it also substantively answered a few personal questions I had.

In terms of the mechanics of the course, the course (especially in its system of office hours + weekly responses) really rewards attempts to dive deeper into literature (the secondary source materials they give are really quite amazing). Yet it's also highly forgiving and there was never a point in the course where I felt burdened by the work or afraid that the course was "out to get me." Even if it's just to fulfil a humanities divisional distributional, I would highly recommend taking a bet on this class.
Take 100%%%% Teeter is such an amazing Professor and such a great lecturer. It was always a joy to be in class! The work sometimes is a lot but it isn't difficult and you learn soooo much, so the time makes it work it!!!
As an Econ concentrator, I took the class originally to fulfill a requirement for graduation. This class sparked something in me, though. I've always been a social science type of kid, but it made me genuinely find a calling in the humanities. This class combines history, literature, and religion in the most perfect way imaginable. I have a much much more appreciation for the Bible, even as an ardent nonbeliever. My preconceived notions about the infantile nature of scripture were challenged. I had my interpretations and biases, as a result of the context that we live in and I was raised in, challenged. I came to appreciate the truth of nuance. It truly, truly, was a transformative experience.

The expectations of the course are very straightforward and set in the syllabus. The exams are quite easy (I ended up with over a 100 in the class), and Professor Teeter provides a study guide that lets you know specifically what to study. The course does a good job of catering to individuals who have no prior experience with the Bible and those for whom the text is familiar literature. You learn a LOT in a very short period.

Also, Professor Teeter and Byron are two of the nicest faculty members at Harvard that I have ever met. Professor Teeter exudes this positive energy and contagious excitement about the content. Byron's a grading machine, getting papers and exams back to you sometimes within the same day. His comments were always kind and constructive. Genuinely wonderful people. Get to know them, ask questions, go to office hours, and think about how you can apply the takeaways from class to your own life. I can't wait to take the second half of the course.
This is a fantastic class that will greatly depend your appreciation for and understanding of the Hebrew Bible. This class is ideal if you want to learn how to read the HB according to the textual clues that it provides. Do not expect a particularly "religious" understanding of the text, but the Professor certainly respects and is fascinated by the material, so he has many wonderful insights to share. Workload is very reasonable, and grading is not very strict. Ideally talk to someone who has taken the class before –– that's what convinced me to take it!
