Great class! Super helpful to learn GIS earlier on in college especially if you're considering going to grad school. Lots of students from different schools—Chan, Kennedy, Law—since GIS is so applicable. Lectures did feel a bit fast at times, but it helped that most of them were recorded on Zoom. I liked the format of the class in that there's only one weekly assignment, along with smaller ones and project proposals that build up to the final project. Dr. Chen and Bora are so very nice and patient during office hours.
The course format or the way that Professor Chen teaches needs to change significantly. Most of the class was lost during lab sections because she moved super quickly, and she was not receptive to that feedback (we tried.) I basically did the labs asynchronously during lab time by following the guides that she created, and my TF Bora was really really great about helping during office hours.

I really enjoyed the course material and heard really good things about the class when it was taught by a different professor. It is a fun topic, and has the capacity to be a good class.
Good introduction class for data science in the humanities
This is a straightforward, well–organized introduction to ArcGIS pro and its tools and applications. Teaching staff is helpful, generous and available. Highly recommend for anyone without previous exposure to these methods who would like to incorporate them into future research.
New professor for the course in Fall 2023, so some of the past reviews are outdated. It is a fairly easy course that will make you comfortable with using the basic functions of ArcGIS. That said, lecture can often be uninspirational and occasionally unorganized. If you do not come into the class with a clear understanding and motivation for why you want to use ArcGIS/what you want your project to be on, it may be hard to stay motivated. Granted, the workload is fairly low.
Wonderful course that enabled me to use ArcGIS in my research. The professor was especially great in this course and designed the lectures, homework, and assignments well.
Take this class but with reservations. This was Dr.Chen's first semester teaching this course and she goes VERY fast. Don't be too alarmed as there are written instructions that you can follow along and recordings of each class. If you have a slight interest in maps and/or data collection this is a class that you should consider taking, just be prepared for the actual class to be a bit boring and sometimes frustrating.
OVERALL: Take this course! Dr. Chen and TF Bora are great, and so, so kind. This class is a low–stress way for people to learn geospatial analysis – this is a very useful tool whether you’re concentrating in Government (and taking this as an elective), or not. I felt I’d learned a lot throughout the semester.

TEACHING: The pace of the guided demonstrations was fast at the beginning of the semester, and it could be a bit difficult to follow at times. However, Dr. Chen was very receptive to feedback. Class typically consisted of a lecture by Dr. Chen, and then a guided demonstration on ArcGIS. There were also guest speakers throughout the semester.

HOMEWORK: I found myself referring to the recorded lecture videos and agree the assignments take 5–6 hours per week. The final project seemed to be more “you–get–what–you–put–into–it” – some people had more complex projects, and others had more simple projects. Whatever you decide to do/explore, the instructors are there to support you and provide advice when you need it.

Tips: Book office hours! Dr. Chen and Bora are a wealth of knowledge, and there’s a lot to learn about geospatial analysis outside of the 2 hours of weekly class time.
I think this course is very helpful, but only if the material aligns with your interests. There is a lot of self–learning involved.
I took the course because I wanted to learn GIS this semester and would not have the opportunity to do so in the future. Dr. Chen was a bit new to teaching and there were therefore a few kinks to work out in the class, but she and the rest of the teaching staff were very helpful and understanding and I still learned a lot, despite some week–to–week frustration.
