Harvard's classes normally are (imo) too theoretical and you end up knowing lots of proofs and bounds but might not know how to implement something with real–world applications CS 109B is the perfect complement for this and you get lots of practical work as well as the ability to discuss modern ML techniques. A lot of students dropped after the first lecture because it was more mathematical, but that was probably because it was given by Alex (stats) and is very tractable for anyone who has taken Stat 111. Admittedly, most students in this course don't come from theory backgrounds and some more math in the theory would be nice at times. But would highly, highly encourage, especially for students who have already taken more theory–heavy courses, as the perfect way to balance math with being able to build cool things that work.
