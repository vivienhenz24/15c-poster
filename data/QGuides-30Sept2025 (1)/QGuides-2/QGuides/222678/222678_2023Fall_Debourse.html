What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Celine Debourse is absolutely amazing. Super passionate about her work and very eager to help with any work assigned throughout the semester. This class was a great introduction for me to learn more about Ancient Mesopotamian life and culture through the lens of ritual which also provided much food for thought about ritual processes within our own, modern society.
You will get as much out of this class as you put into it! Do you weekly response papers and take them seriously. They are the space to help you integrate learnings and ask questions to deepen your knowledge about ritual and Mesopotamia.
–
If you have an interest in religion, or just want to learn something new from a cool perspective, TAKE THIS CLASS. Prof. Dbourse knows a lot, both about ritual theory and about Mesopotamia, and she is very good at communicating that knowledge in clear presentations. Moreover, she does something almost no other humanities professor at Harvard does: she assigns readings that are *actually doable* – there really isn't too much, which encourages students to actually do them and come to class prepared. Beyond this, she's very flexible on deadlines, and generally just really nice and approachable.
This class was by far my favourite this semester. I highly recommend it. To those even remotely interested in ritual studies and/or Assyriology, this course is a must.
