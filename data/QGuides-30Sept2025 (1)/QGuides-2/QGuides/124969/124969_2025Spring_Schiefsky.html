It is important not to miss any class, but especially lecture because of the amount of detail provided in class.
Not a total gem due to the term paper and final but a very fair class overall. Gives good intro to ancient history and philosophy behind medicine. I enjoyed it as Classics & Premed, thought it was an interesting integration of both of those areas.
Great class! Lots of readings. Be aware that the final is a lot of memorization (100 words, 62 passages + essay) but it was not too difficult. I really enjoyed the class.
If you are interested in philosophy, and the role of philosophy or the humanities in medicine (pre–med or not), I think this class is super interesting. There is a great selection of readings, and to take away interesting insights that you may one day apply in medicine, make sure to do the readings and spend time interacting with the material on your own.
Not the most engaging lecture. The research paper is an interesting final project. Topic area itself is interesting.
** This is a great gem of a course if you have any interest in philosophy or medicine. If you already know a lot about ancient philosophy, expect some material to be review. However, there is also some obscure stuff to satisfy the curious and more advanced undergraduates. I especially enjoyed the sections on athleticism and women's health. Professor Schiefsky is really nice and a great lecturer. The class gets the gold star of RESPECTING your TIME. It lets you engage most with the parts of material that interest you the most, but you still come away with overall mastery because of the final exam.

The one thing I did not like was a group project presentation. Groups projects are not the best already, and watching everyone else's presentations themselves every class dragged because (respectfully, and myself included) students do not really know how to give an engaging talk.
Take this class if you have a STRONG interest in classics and the history of science/medicine.
This is a good class about a very interesting topic! It was a very accessible introduction to ancient medicine for anyone with related interests. The course might have benefited from a bit more theoretical structure and analytical depth, but it was overall very well done, and Prof Schiefsky is obviously very talented and passionate. The rest of the course staff was kind as well. The group presentation was not a terribly high–stakes affair, and there was plenty of time to work on the term paper. We haven't taken the final exam yet, but the structure of it is a bit more intense with many term and passage identifications in addition to a written essay.
Somewhat dry lectures, but interesting content.
This class is great! It offers a very interesting perspective on medicine. Learning about the original philosophy that undergirds modern medicine is fascinating and I would recommend this class to anyone who is pre–med.
Take this class! The course is very accessible to anyone (you don't need to be classics or philosophy major) and you will learn so much about various approaches to health and healing in the ancient Mediterranean world. It also draws connections to modern medicine, so definitely take this class if you are pre–med. Even if you aren't pre–med, take this class! It will shape how you think about health and how to live a healthy lifestyle
Great teaching staff, especially Iman was an amazing section leader. Workload is manageable – Make sure to do the readings to get as much out of lecture as possible. Recommend for classics concentrators and people in the life sciences interested in philosophy and history of medicine.
