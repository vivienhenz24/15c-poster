What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
A decent class on measure theory, Lp spaces, and Fourier, but lectures are sometimes hard to follow/important details skimmed over
HT seems like a good, kind person. However, he teaches straight from Rudin/Stein and does not add much to the content besides maybe some proof explanations. You could literally skip lecture and read the textbook and only miss a little bit. Prepare for the psets to be questions from Rudin or Stein. So in that sense it's an okay class... but I would MUCH prefer a better teacher for this course that has incredible applications to math, engineering, and physics.

The TF, Jarell, was instrumental in me not dropping the class. His section was excellent and he gave great feedback as a TF.
Previous iterations of the Q Guide have commented negatively on Prof. Yau's lecture style. Although there is some validity to these complaints, Prof. Yau is overall a very good lecturer. He often provides commentary on the results we're discussing that give insight into how a working mathematician approaches the subject and his work in general. He also peppers lectures with many entertaining anecdotes which also give insight into how the world of academia works.

This course doesn't give the most rigorous treatment of measure theory in my opinion. I did fine on all the homework and exams, but I do feel that my understanding is still only partial in some areas. However, you do get a very good overview of the big picture of results, which is maybe all that you can hope for given the limited time.
