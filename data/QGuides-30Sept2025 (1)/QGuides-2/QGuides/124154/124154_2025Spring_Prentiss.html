Take this class to learn a lot about waves and a good bit of math too! I took 21b concurrently, which is totally doable since most of the necessary math is taught during lecture or can be learned as you do the PSETs. You also don't need to have taken 15b, although the EM–wave content near the end of the course will probably be easier if you have. Overall, PSETs and exams were very fair and not too painful. Content–wise, the first half was a little dry, but not as hard, while the second half was much more interesting in my opinion but conceptually required more effort. Definitely go to office hours if you have any questions at all about the PSETs, which you probably will because the PSETs did not always align or seem entirely relevant to what we were learning in class, but they were still figure–out–able with office hours and not too long, which was nice. My biggest (and one of my only) complaints about this class is that section could be massively improved. I think it would be better if students were guided through the section problems during section, or, at the very least if they could check their solutions to the section problems against a detailed answer key to make sure they leave section with an understanding of how to do all of the problems. I often left section feeling much more confused than when I arrived because I would leave with a whole list of problems I had no idea how to solve, and solutions would not be posted until over a week after the section. Thankfully, section is optional so you can just skip it if it is as bad as it was this semester! Lab, on the other hand, is not optional, but really not bad at all! Actually pretty fun and there is a lot of support!
This class is super useful to take before quantum/condensed matter. Prof Huth is a great lecturer and also quite accessible outside of lectures as well. Highly recommend! Maras labs are also really incredible.
15C is pretty similar in pacing to 15B and A, but is much more disorganized in terms of content. Staying focused on the idea of deriving equations for waves and the various tools to understand them (Fourier transform, reflection/transmission, normal modes) and then their application to different scenarios is a good way to stay grounded. Some equations are just given without derivation which is slightly annoying, and there are a lot of tangents into optics, sound, E&M, etc.
u have to take it. just pay attention and don't fall behind. huth's notes are handwritten and hard to follow so I would take this class with morii who has typed notes and provided handouts to follow along each lecture.
15c is a very solid class! The concepts are interesting and approachable as long as you have some background with linear algebra and mechanics, and the workload is manageable if you start your PSETS early and go to office hours. Huth's lectures are engaging and he's very welcoming to questions in class, which isn't always the case in physics courses. The exams are fair and aren't meant to trick you – if you work hard you'll do well. The labs were also my favorite part of the course. The final project gives a lot of room for creativity and flexibility, and you really get an experience proportional to the effort you put in.
