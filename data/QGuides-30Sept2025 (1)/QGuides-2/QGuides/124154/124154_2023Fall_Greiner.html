Really great class, highly recommend taking it with Huth if possible. Huth is a great lecturer, super accessible, and well organized. His lectures are on par with the best I've had here so far. It is similar to the other 15s in the series in terms of workload and difficulty, probably slightly easier than 15b even. Labs are 3 hours and you get to work with some pretty cool equipment but be warned there is a final project which requires some individual initiative. You will leave with a solid understanding and intuition for wave phenomena.

This is the first reasonable Physics class I have taken at Harvard. If 15A and 15B were trial by fire for you, you can breathe a sigh of relief here. The class is small enough to cater to individual students' needs, the material is interesting, and class and section DIRECTLY prepare you for the psets and the exams. If you've grown used to being asked questions about concepts you've NEVER heard of and spending 12+ hours on psets, that will not the case in this class. Would highly recommend!!

And the lab is longer than necessary –– it could have easily been 2 hours without impacting student learning. If you have to take 15c, it's not awful, but if you can take a substitute I'd recommend that (or take it with a different head professor).

Honestly only take this class if you need to. If you're a physics concentrator you most likely need to take this class for the requirement, and Physics 15c is just a bullet that you need to bite. Waves aren't necessarily the most exciting but they're cool enough. Take it with Huth if you can– he's super empathetic towards his students and understanding, and the class is very manageable. Huth offers the best version of physics 15c.
If you're reading this, you probably have to take 15c (unless you're one of those engineering kids who takes this because it's easier than ES 100–and–whatever, to which I say, bah, humbug). Thus, I'll tell you it how it is. 15c is a pretty standard introductory physics class. It's fine. It's nothing to write home about. You'll learn waves, a couple of differential equations (although they tell you the answers, and if you took 21b you know them already (side note: take 21b before 15c)), and a bit of electromagnetism that if you're like me, you forgot promptly after the 15b final. The problem sets are fine, they're comparable to 15a/b, and the exams are fair. Professor Huth is good, and he's a wonderful character, he'll tell you all about his niche passions that (tangentially) relate to waves. So, aspiring physics major, enjoy. It's not that bad.
John's ultimate goal is to have us leaving with knowledge that will actually last us past an exam but also with proofs of how we get to the equations we use. It moves at an easy pace to follow (at times slow) and john really emphasizes asking questions (go to his office hours)! I also thought it was interesting and the applications were good. the material (psets and tests) well reflected what we were learning. Section was awesome.
Huth is an incredibly kind, easygoing professor. I would highly recommend taking 15c with him, as his version of the class is the most relaxed of all the 15s.
Read the lecture notes! Although attending lecture is crucial there is often much more content to be found in the written transcripts.
I was not a huge fan of the subject matter for this course––it felt like a drawn out version of 15b. I did not feel like waves was the most engaging topic to learn about.
