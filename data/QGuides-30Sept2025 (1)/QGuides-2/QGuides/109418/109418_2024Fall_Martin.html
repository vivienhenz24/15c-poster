What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
It is a very thorough survey course of the latter half of the twentieth century with a particular focus on the Soviet Union, the countries of the Warsaw Pact and Yugoslavia. A very useful introduction to the approaches historians and political scientists have taken to study socialism and post–socialism. Prof. Martin is an excellent lecturer, while Alex Jackson is a great TF.
This is a great survey course for anyone with an interest in understanding both a monumental moment in history as well as its present–day consequences. It covers economics, politics, culture, and social relations very well while painting a very cohesive picture of how these factors all worked together during communism's demise and aftermath. Doing the readings carefully is totally worth it, both for understanding the course material and for finding new concepts and authors to read about further, and the lectures are incredibly detailed and do a great job of explaining how pre–1989 history came to the fore during the core years that the course covers.
Professor Martin is clearly an expert in the field, but that comes with him making comments about things that he thinks are obvious and that everyone knows. Lecture slides are a bit unorganized, but Martin is really a good professor, serious scholar, and the readings were good. Take this if you're interested in this important history.
This is a fantastic class! I didn't expect to learn so much in such a short amount of time, but now at the end of the semester I know so much about the Soviet Union and its dissolution. However, it does take a bit to adjust to the structure of the class. Prof. Martin's teaching style is pretty traditional (he does veer off track sometimes), where he lectures at the front of the class with slides that are either filled with text or just include a picture. The lecture slides are hard to understand at times, but if you pay attention and look up key events/details later, you'll be fine. The course only relies on the readings, two essays, and a final exam, so the weekly workload is managable and the essays are graded fairly. The final exam was tougher than I expected because you had to quote readings in your responses from memory, but the course staff lets you know ahead of time and provides a study guide. Overall, I highly recommend this class if you're into learning about the Soviet Union.
I came in not knowing what countries were in the Soviet Union and came out having learned so much about the Soviet Union and its impact on 21st century Europe. This course covers a pretty broad timespan and a LOT of material, but does so with detail and nuance. The lectures are dense but very informative and the readings are well–selected. Assignments are very manageable and useful for learning. My biggest recommendation is to take a bit of time each week to read a basic background of the topic, as both the lectures and the readings dive right in to specifics. Overall, I learned so much and felt the information was very relevant. Would recommend!
If you wish to understand the Soviet Union and why Putin is the way he is, I would highly recommend this course! Readings and lecture may be a little difficult to follow, but they are selected because they offer valuable insight into the topics they explore.
This class is very interesting with one semi–major caveat that the lectures are frequently chaotic and disorienting. I enjoyed the experience and learned a lot but not for everyone!
