What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class was one of the most disorganized classes I have taken at Harvard. I would strongly advise against taking this class – there are many more engaging MBB seminars and electives to take. If you're interested in the material, you can get just as much from reading the papers on your own – the course itself does not complement them or teach you very much. We received no feedback on any course work until we begged towards the end of the semester, and all of our grades were published publicly to the entire class. There was never any time for class discussion during the actual seminarguest speakers would come and present their research to us, then one of us would present a paper, and then we would be out of time. Ultimately, I really wish I had spent my time taking a different course.
I would tell anyone who is interested to take this class! It is a great opportunity to learn about a scientific field in a more chill environment than what you would find in other seminars. Javiera is a wonderful instructful who teaches and learns with us as we present our papers. She is also down to answer any questions you may have, even if you think that they are rudimentary (which can be difficult to find in other science courses on campus). If you are at all interested in the gut microbiome (or the human microbiome), I highly recommend taking this!
This class is interesting, but pretty unstructured as it stands. You'll read different papers each week about the microbiome in different systems regarding mental health aspects, but we never really discussed the papers together (one person would present). The class is just very all over the place in terms of content, so it's hard to gain an understanding or key takeaways about how the gut brain connection really works.
