This course was engaging and covered interesting content, which made the readings enjoyable. However, some readings were quite challenging to understand without a solid background in biochem or neuroit was definitely more biology–focused than I expected. I personally did not enjoy Perusall as a reading tool, and I felt that participation was assessed too methodically. Many of us participated just for the points, and given our limited background in the subject, it was difficult to ask productive questions or make insightful remarks.

Javiera is very enthusiastic and approachable, and she genuinely tries to make the class enjoyable! That said, most classes are taught by guest speakers, followed by student presentations, which made the course feel a bit self–directed at times. While the course content was interesting, there are likely other MBB seminars that offer a more structured and rewarding experience.
This was a great class! The content is super interesting and there are frequent guest speakers who provide real world perspectives. The workload is not too heavy (usually two papers to read and comment on each week), and grading is not too harsh. Paper annotations are graded by an AI program through Perusall, which was kind of annoying since it sometimes took off random fractional points and we couldn't view the scoring breakdown without emailing the instructor. However, this wasn't a big deal beyond being irritating. The small class size allows you to ask questions and engage with the content, and it was interesting to hear from people with a range of concentrations, since MBB connects a wide variety of fields of study.
This class is fascinating if youre interested in how the gut and brain interact, and how they influence mental health. The topics are engaging and relevant, with guest speakers at each lecture.
This class is amazing, it is so interesting. This is one of those classes where the assignments are limited and low stress and so it allows you to actually spend time to learn the material. the readings are cool and not that long at all. You get to pick what topics you do your projects on and they are graded fairly so worrying about your grade should not be a concern. Javi is a great instructor and she in knowledgable in these topics and invites many other guest lecturers to educate about so many different topics.
This course is an excellent way to meld different life sciences while also being approachable to those who do not know much about neuroscience, or those studying neuro who are more unfamiliar with biology.
Take this course! The GBA is fascinating and Javiera is a wonderful professor. Assignments are manageable and there is a lot of support throughout the way.
