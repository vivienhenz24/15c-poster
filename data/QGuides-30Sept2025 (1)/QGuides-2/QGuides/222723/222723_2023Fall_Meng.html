What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I wouldn't recommend this class to anyone looking for a stat elective. The content is fine and you can tell that Professor Meng cares a lot about the topic, but the psets and TF support are not great. Although there are only 3 psets, each one was insanely long and so time intensive. Some of them covered math that we never talked about in class and going to office hours is not helpful. Some of the TFs had an attitude that made them seem kind of rude. On top of that, all the assignments were graded very harshly by TFs with little to no explanation.
It's rewarding, but a difficult course – with sparse office hours, a fragmented class (mix of grad, undergrad), and challenging content, make sure you know what you're getting into.
Xiaoli is great and generous teacher. Take this course if you want to expose yourself to a broad range of content around privacy. However, beware that you may not have a very structured set of content to learn and go through. If you thrive in a more unstructured environment, this course is for you.
This class was interesting but really disorganized. No one knew how to do the problems on the first HW because we had been taught so much material at once, the outside readings we had been assigned sometimes used different notation/methods than what we did in lecture, and there were no lecture notes or textbook (they published lecture slides, but those were very hard to follow if you weren't actually sitting through the lecture). There were enough bonus points and other assignments that I wasn't super worried about my overall grade, but the homework assignments were way more stressful and difficult than they needed to be. My TFs were nice and trying to be helpful but I think the overall disorganization made that hard as well.
It says you should be good to take this course with stat110/stat111 background, but imo you need more than that. the assumed stat knowledge was challenging, and this course is way more of a lecture–style course than seminar course. Professor Xiao–Li is very passionate about DP so that was wonderful to experience, but I was more interested in the philosophical aspects of the courses and would have preferred to see more of this. I took this course with the intention of being able to use my background in a new way/see things from a different perspective and do believe that this was accomplished, but feel that with more iterations of the course, this can be achieved more
