This is an excellent course. The readings are all interesting and the lectures are engaging.
Prof. Berker is just outstanding! He is always extremely well–organized, and you get handouts every class that are fantastic summaries of the material for discussion that day. He has also seemingly perfected having a discussion–based style in a medium–size class, and the majority of class time is spent discussing student questions/responding to student objections. He is also very available for chats after class and in office hours.

As for the material, Berkers metaphysics course specifically focuses on a modern toolkit called grounding, and almost the whole course is spent working through the details of the abstract structure of grounding as opposed to applying it, though it is widely applicable. So be prepared to use your logic abilities, and you will get out a framework for analyzing hard philosophical questions.
This is one of the best course I have taken so far! The course provides a very good and thorough overview of many important topics in contemporary metaphysics that are also related to questions in other areas of philosophy. While the course is definitely more technical than most philosophy courses, it is very well taught and well structured.
A real pleasure and a must–take course if you're at all interested in the cutting edge of analytic metaphysics. Take for Berker, whose energy, enthusiasm, and clarity are unmatched. He has an incredible talent for making one's questions sound so much smarter than they were and then answering them on the spot. A model of impeccable pedagogy, humor, and kindness. This course was a delight from beginning to end, and I urge you to take it, reader.
This really was a great class. You learn a lot about recent work in metaphysics, including material that (as far as I know) isn't usually covered in undergrad classes. I highly recommend it! It's worth noting, though, that if you haven't done previous work in philosophy, and if you haven't done any logic before, parts of this class might be challenging.
This is an excellent class with very interesting material! If you are at all interested in metaphysics, this is a really great introduction to contemporary ideas and topics
If you are even considering taking this class for any reason, just do it. It truly is just a wonderful class. The readings can be a bit rough sometimes, but Professor Berker has detailed handouts on each reading and follows the ideas in the handouts and reading quite closely in his lectures. The lectures themselves are also great. Professor Berker is clear and understandable, and he is constantly engaging and asking questions with students. Class often felt more like a laid–back seminar where Professor Berker would present the readings and then turn things over to the students to debate and express their own ideas and comments for the majority of the class. Course staff was excellent and very understanding with extensions. Papers are graded very reasonably, on time, and with detailed feedback. Overall just an amazing experience, and I cannot recommend this class enough.
This is a difficult class, but is a fantastic way to develop your skills in philosophical thinking, writing, and argumentation. By the end of the semester, the class seemed fairly divided on whether people believed grounding was a productive and coherent philosophical concept to work on or not, and people's engagement with the course seemed to depend heavily on where they fell on that spectrum.
