Dr. Bell is such a kind, unique professor! As someone who is used to very structured classes, this class is not that, and that was hard to adjust to. It's entirely student–structured, as we create our own syllabus. In the end, the individual project and community gathering were very fulfilling. However, we did not stick to the syllabus we created, and several student suggestions were glazed over or skipped entirely in discussion (e.g., "The Color Purple"). I understand prioritizing students' health and checking in, but spending half of a 2 hour 45–minute class checking in and out or talking about the merits of the class made me feel like sometimes we went just to chat instead of discussing the readings we wanted to dive into. I also wish Dr. Bell took some agency in the class at times, such as trying to decide a date for the community gathering or providing feedback on our projects (she never saw them because of the structure of the final presentation). This class is very very different and if you do well with limited instructor input or guidance, go for it. If you're a bit more "type A", you will find this harder to adjust to. I don't regret taking the class as it took me out of my comfort zone and it was lovely to have my first and probably only ever class of Black women but I wish we did more of the syllabus we agreed on.
This is an amazing course!! Dr. Bell is incredible and this was one of the more impactful classes personally I've taken. It is important to note, however, that due tot he collaborative nature of the course, things can feel like its piling on the end of the semester if you don't start early.
Dr. Bell is an incredible personas a teacher and human. I'm really glad that I took this course because I got to know and meet her. On the other hand, the class was incredibly disorganized especially by finals (the worst time for the class to be disorganized). I feel like if on the first day, we collaborate on the syllabus, then we need to know exactly what all our hard deadline assignments are in September, not in November or December.

Most of the biggest deadlines felt spontaneous without any clear guidelines or advice on what was expected. The promo video and community gathering 90 minute lesson plan were highly stressful because it felt like they were burdens being added onto our final season, when guidelines for both could have been clearly highlighted and articulated on the first day of class.

I recommend taking a course with Dr. Bell because for the most part it will not cause you academic stress, and will encourage you to pursue and independent project of your choosing
Dr. Bell is a visionary and a light. This course helps you to hone media analysis skills while learning about foundational Black feminist scholarship and literature. Overall, I thoroughly enjoyed taking the course!
Take this course if you have the opportunity! Dr. Bell TRULY cares about her students and wants to see us succeed. While the course material isn't the lightest, it is truly important for the way we view society, womanhood, and femininity. The workload is what you make it. Definitely do the course readings and assigned materials, as they make the experience more robust.
If you get the chance to take this course with Dr. Bell take it! You wont regret it :)
