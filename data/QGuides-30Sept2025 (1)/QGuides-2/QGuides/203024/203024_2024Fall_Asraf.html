I really really enjoyed this class. It is a lot of work though, and Ancient Greek is not easy (or intuitive)!! But Nadav and Sarah are super helpful, have a lot of OH, and genuinely love Greek and you can tell. I also really love that hey incorporate ancient Greek history, art, and culture into the class, so it feels like a very immersive experience. If I wasn't a senior I would continue Ancient Greek, so my only advice is to take the course earlier rather than later because the Classics department at Harvard is so so awesome and I would love to read ancient texts in their original language.
Take this class!! I had such an excellent, enriching experience, and the course was extremely fair. Language courses here can be tricky to navigate because some are unpredictably difficult but not this one!! You get a lot out of it (historically, philosophically, etc.) so definitely consider it
Take this class! If you're interested in Ancient Greek, or even if you're looking for an interesting way to fulfill your language requirement. It's not like other language courses that throw a mountain of vocab at you every day. You work through the grammar and vocab in a way that gives you time to really internalize them. Nadav is a great professor and tells you exactly what you need to know, do, and memorize to succeed. At the periphery of the class are discussions about classical Athens, Ancient Greek history, and the texts that define the era. Even though it's totally introductory, it still feels like you're immersing yourself in a bit of the culture that you'll hopefully study more in depth later on.
Follow the directions of the tutors and review in time, you shall perform well. The texts and grammatical exercises are valuable trainings, better not skip.

This is a great class. Nadav is so sweet and makes a very good learning environment. I think that each class handles a manageable amount of material. Just keep up with all of the forms, because there is a lot of them. The exams are fair (not too hard or too easy) and the class is a pleasure to attend in the mornings. You also get to learn about ancient greek history as you go and it is all super interesting.
I would highly recommend this course to future students. The workload is very manageable and you make a lot of progress in just one semester. Learning even just the foundations of Ancient Greek opens up so many opportunities to pursue areas of personal and academic interest.
