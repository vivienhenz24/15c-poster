Comments
This is an excellent class that introduces you to why economic crises happen (from the American markets perspective).
Fascinating course tracing U.S. economic history from the late 1800s until the 2008 Financial Crisis. Excellent selection of readings and the final project provides the opportunity to pursue an economic history topic you're passionate about (very flexible!). As an Econ concentrator, this class helps situate the concepts you learn in your economics classes within a broader context and allows you to track the evolution of economic thought over the past century.
Really interesting class. If you are a history concentrator and are interested in the economy, I highly recommend that you take this class. It is one of the better history classes offered. Take this class! You will really enjoy it.
TAKE THIS COURSE. If you are interested in the Great Recession/Depression, major shifts in American labor/economic policy, even if you are not an Econ/Hist concentrator, this course will be great for you. The final paper seems a bit daunting at first, but Kuchinski really prepares you for it by breaking it down. Just keep on top of the weekly discussion posts, as sometimes you'll forget about them. The readings, guest speakers, and movies are super interesting. Kuchinski is one of the sweetest professors I've met here and she will gladly help you in office hours if you need.
Fantastic course overall. Dr. Kuchinski is accessible and a fair professor, and wants the students to succeed. Readings may sometimes seem overwhelming and a lot of content but are absolutely necessary to complete to be successful in the course. Not a course where you can skip the readings and glide by. I've now taken multiple courses on the history of financial crises in my time as an undergrad, and it's nice for one to be more History focused as opposed to be purely Economics. Would recommend with enthusiasm, particularly to Economics concentrators who are looking to fulfil their seminar requirement for a secondary in History.
I took this course for History concentration credit and was initially a little apprehensive because I wasn't coming in with a strong economics background. However, Dr. Kuchinski makes the subject matter extremely approachable and ensures that every student has the opportunity to really engage with the course content. While the readings may become dense at times, they are really engaging and the weekly discussion posts helped keep me on track with the reading. There's a lot of creative flexibility with the final research paper and even though it may seem long, the assignment itself is broken down so you aren't starting from scratch at the end of the semester.

I would highly advise you to do a preliminary glance into these crisis before the course because I felt like a brief primer would have been helpful. Overall, the course load is not bad with a few weekly readings, discussion posts, and a few final assignments.
