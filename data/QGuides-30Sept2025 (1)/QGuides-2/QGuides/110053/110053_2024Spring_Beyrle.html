It is a great class and you will learn a lot! Very engaging.
An engaging class overall that is pretty chill and fun to just attend. You learn about some really cool work being done in applying computer science and tech to public policy, and really dive into the power of generative AI and play with ChatGPT a lot. Would recommend!
Super cool & fun class. The project aspect may sound intimidating (it did to me), but the teaching staff make it feel really easy, although you still come out with at least one project you can feel really proud of– and I loved having the flexibility to pursue my own unique interests.
This course teaches you a lot about chatGPT and content moderation in the current age and how it is being used in the Government. This class is so manageable!! I recommend for CS people looking for an opportunity to create an independent project, there are many opportunities there! I taught myself Tableau and used Jupyter notebook for data analysis which is a great thing that came from this course.
Awesome class. Latanya and Leonie teach students how to conduct their own simple yet meaningful experiments on technological tools and their societal impact. Low workload outside of class, but it is definitely worth putting work in for the assignments. Best applied class in the gov department!
My favorite class of all time. The teaching staff is so amazing and kind and helpful! Latanya, Leonie and Didi are so smart and well–spoken as well as happy to help and engage with students. I put in a lot of work for the final paper because I love the topic but you can definitely so much less work by building on a unit assignment for the research paper.
If you're looking for an intensive class about technology society clashes with a strong technical element, this class really is not it. I thought it was interesting, and appreciated the open–ended vibe, but felt like it disappointed me in some ways. First, I don't think methods were covered appropriately, especially as someone who has never taken a formal research methods course. Second, I think the course was not as technical as I had hoped, as a CS–GOV joint concentrator. Lastly, I was disappointed that most of the larger assignments, and lots of the smaller ones, were jampacked into the final two weeks of the semester. The class workload was very light up until this point, which did not work well with finals and final projects for other classes. The final paper is worth 80 points in comparison to the 60 points that the other three papers are worth combined. In other ways, this class was very good. The professors are very experienced and knowledgeable. It was nice to be surrounded by people who are also interested in Govtech or STS. I think the class just needs more structure.
This was a nice class to take after 1433 although at times there was a lot of overlap. I would highly recommend this class if you come in with a research idea, topic or field of interest. The staff have excellent access to resources and want to help you succeed. Utilize them!
