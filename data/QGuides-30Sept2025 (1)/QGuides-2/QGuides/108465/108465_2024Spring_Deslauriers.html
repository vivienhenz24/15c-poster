Office hours for this class are incredibly helpful and I definitely recommend attending them. Also, make sure to keep up with the readings for this class.

Unfortunately, I did not retain anything from lecture. Everything I learned in this course I learned from the textbook, office hours, and doing psets. It felt like lectures were given to us as though we were expected to already understand the material or have some sort of background knowledge, and as such terms and notation were usually left unexplained. It almost felt as though we were expected to be teaching ourselves the material before lecture, and then treating lecture as a review session. It often felt as though lectures bounced around, and I didn't always have a clear idea of what it was we were supposed to be taking away.

Additionally, for all the emphasis put on our conceptual understanding, my takeaways from this class are almost purely the mathematical explanation of introductory QM and the concepts underpinning this math with very little tie–in to the actual physical processes at work. All in all, I feel like the vast majority of what I learned from this class was on my own time through sheer force of will, and I can very easily see how someone could go to all the lectures and complete all the problem sets and still leave the class with a very shaky understanding of quantum mechanics.
