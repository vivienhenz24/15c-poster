Pset content sometimes feels tangentially related to what is discussed in lecture, in addition to just being purely mathematical derivations (sometimes no mention of physics). While Eslam's 143a had some growing pains (as it was his first iteration of undergrad QM), this course could not have been better run. I had heard many awful things about QM (with other lecturers) prior to taking this course, and I was beginning to worry that the iteration of QM I took would be similarly bashy and unintuitive. Yet while I still know so little about quantum, I really came out of this course feeling that I understood it.

Here are several reasons why this course went so well in my mind:
– Eslam is an excellent, clear lecturer who pains to give us a substantive introduction to QM; when we clearly had no idea what he was talking about, he adjusted the course to improve our understanding.
– Considering this was the first iteration of the course, Eslam was accommodating, humble and very willing to make adjustments to the course.
– The course materials (written lecture notes in particular) were broad, deep and understandable. Some of the best concise lecture notes I have worked with.
– The psets were disgustingly long (hence the high amount of hours14I worked per week), but often they did strike at interesting/important topics in quantum. The psets were proof heavy, and I will admit they were torturous some weeks, but we built a lot of the mathematical intuition behind QM through Eslam's psets.
– Sections were well–run and super helpful.

I will note that the exams were quite difficult for me. The first exam was impossible for most people (avg was like a 60), but the second one was more reasonable. The final was also pretty impossible.

Because Eslam is the 251a lecturer, you can trust that he will give you a great introduction to QM. If he is teaching it again, I could not recommend more to take his course. While you will not have an easy time in it (unless you're as smart as the people who left the test room an hour early during the final), you will learn SO MUCH quantum from an amazing lecturer.
