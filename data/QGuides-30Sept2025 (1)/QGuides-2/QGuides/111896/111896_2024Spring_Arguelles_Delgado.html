Physics 15b is a great intro to E+M. The course materials are well–organized and super helpful for learning the material. The textbook is a great resource, and so are the lecture notes.
Carlos is an amazing professor. The lectures are great overall (a bit fast–paced but manageable), and had amazing demonstrations/experiments throughout the course. The lab component is better in 15b than in 15a (no prelabs!).
One thing I recommend to any student taking the course in the future: if you get the chance, go up and have a conversation with Carlos. Ask them about anything related to physics that interests you and you'll definitely end up having an incredible chat (for example, in our mid–semester meeting, I asked Carlos what was on their chalkboard in the office, and they were more than happy to spend the next 15 minutes explaining right–handed/left–handed neutrinos to me). If you take this class, put forth the effort to learn as much as you can from Carlos!
This is a great course!! I loved it and Carlos is such a sweet lecturer. The lectures can be quite confusing at times, but going to section and doing the psets helps my understanding immensely. Exams are very fair and similar to the psets and I loved the group exam component of it. Don't fear taking this course at all! There are so many support resources for you. As long as you go to lecture, do the psets, and give in your best effort, you'll do fine :) I would recommend having knowledge of multi–variable calculus though –– everything in E&M is inherently linked to Stokes' and Divergence theorem.
I would honestly take 153 instead of 15b. I didn't have back ground in 15b, but this course is honestly pretty trivial if you have a strong grasp of mechanics and multi. Overall easy and fun way to satisfy the E&M requirement though!
A solid course on E&M. Carlos makes it much less stressful than it could be by adding a group portion for each exam that counts for 20% of the grade and also providing a lot of guidance on how to prepare for the exams.
Honestly, past Q–reports will say that this an awful class, but it really isn't that bad. The psets are pretty unnerving (certainly my hardest pset of the week, and I had some pretty hard classes), but there's a lot of help in OH. The CAs for this class are great, and so are the TFs. Make sure to study the psets for the midterms (seriously, you'll thank this comment later).
If you enjoyed 15a, I would recommend this class. Definitely come in comfortable with algebra/calculus, as well as familiarity with E+M. The content can seem a bit tough/hard to understand in lecture and psets, but the exams were actually a lot easier than expected overall. I don't think the class is as hard as people make it out to be (grading–wise); conceptually it is a bit of a step up from 15a but totally doable.

Many people choose to take another physics class between 15a and 15b to avoid taking 15b in the spring. There is a reason. The lectures are not well organized or thought out. They also often have typos or skip over derivations. The problem sets are just taken from the book, and so are the exams to a large extent. There were errors in the first exam, and the practice materials were not reflective of what was actually tested (there were no practice exams– only suggested problems from the book, and half the topics covered in those problems were omitted from the exam). You will get A in the class because of the massive grade inflation (and the fact that half the exam is problems you've seen before), but you will not learn. If you have the flexibility to take this class in the fall instead of the spring, do it.
Physics nights and OH are the best resources in this class. If you have Carlos as a teacher, redo the past homework problems as a preparation for your exams.

I would happily take another class taught by Carlos.
