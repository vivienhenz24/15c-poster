What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If you have any interest in Africa or development more broadly, I can't recommend taking this course enough!!! The professor is incredibly knowledgeable and accessible, the readings are comprehensive and interesting, and the seminar–style of the course is nice for talking about the course content and information without being lectured at. The culminating project for the class is a 20–page paper, but don't be intimidated if you've never written at that length before (I'm a non–history major and I hadn't). The class arranges with the history department writing fellow so you're able to get extra support for writing as needed; utilize that resource and I recommend starting the paper early. Ultimately an amazing class and strongly recommend it!
Really interesting course. Pretty heavy reading load but if you're willing to put in the work it is so worth it. Prof. Akyeampong is a pleasure to work with, very patient and insightful.
Take this class! You learn so much and the professor is genuinely one of the kindest and most intelligent professors I've ever met. I wouldn't recommend taking this as a first year because the amount of reading you have to do is a bit overwhelming but once you get the hang of it, an amazing class!
I would definitely recommend taking this class if you are a history concentrator. Other relevant concentrators may include economics, social studies, AfAm, and some others. But I think that it's good to have a solid understanding of macroeconomics and/or world history beforehand. Professor Akyeampong is very captivating, helpful, insightful and understanding. He does a great job at facilitating discussions.
This is the perfect class for anyone interested in international development in any capacity. The historical foundations that the course provides should be required for anyone interested in studying Africa or development more generally. The reading load is manageable and deeply interesting. Prof Arkyeampong is a genuinely kind person who invests so much in his students' success. I feel so lucky to have taken this class and highly encourage others to do so as well.
Professor Akyeampong is a very thoughtful teacher, and I am glad that I took this elective even without a history background (let alone working knowledge on African history). His course provides a thorough and engaging overview of African post–colonial and post–independence development. You will mostly be grappling with political economy texts which embrace a lot of technical content from both economics and political science, which is a welcome challenge if you are not from these fields (or history). It is certainly a course where you will get more out of it by putting in the work. You should be prepared to dedicate focused time for reading the assigned texts and conducting some light research on your topics of interest. Discussion time with your peers will seem arduous at first, but the insights and learnings you get from each other will be wonderful. Professor Akyeampong's class is a wonderful opportunity to grapple with the nuances and characters of modern African history especially as an outsider, and the Professor is an excellent guide.
