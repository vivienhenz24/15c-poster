What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Please, please only take this class if you are strongly interested in differential topology. It is NOT an easy class. It makes 55b look like a cakewalk, and Guillemin and Pollack is not an easy book – it makes Munkres look like a nursery rhyme. Concepts like manifolds, immersions and submersions, the Preimage Theorem, the Inverse function Theorem, Morse theory, and differential forms will be useful in future math classes; I feel like oriented intersection theory less so.
Prof. Ballinger is amazing: both a creative problem writer (exams were pretty interesting) and a good lecturer (generates lots of enthusiasm for the material). At the beginning, the course felt a bit fast–paced and towards the end a bit more slow–paced, so maybe taking the time to work more slowly through the beginning could help in getting people to really understand the material, but this wasn't a big issue!
William really flew through the material, so hold on to your hats.
I can't speak to how this class will be with future professors, but it was really tough. The lectures went super fast and the content is quite difficult. I have no idea how the grading will shake out, but I'm guessing the percentage of straight As will be not that high. However, I learned a lot and that growth was rewarding. It's an interesting topic and definitely expands your brain, especially if you've never seen topology before (and probably even if you have). Was it worth it? Maybe not. Am I grateful for the experience? Yes!
This course is an important one, but I personally found Professor Ballinger's instruction a bit hard to follow and Guillemin and Pollack is not the best textbook. The CAs were wonderful for this course, but you may want to self–study a bit before coming to class. It is not a particularly challenging, but can feel a bit disorganized and hard to follow.
While I thought the overall content was interesting, I think that the actual course itself was badly taught with a textbook that required a significant investment to actually understand the content. I think there are probably better/more relevant mathematics courses that you can take to fulfil your topology requirement.
Course material is hard to begin with. The lectures are not understandable and a waste of time. the textbook is decent but still requires help from office hours preferably from a CA who is good at explaining. Any assignment or exam that did not stick to book material (which a majority of homeworks did) was so unreasonably difficult and inaccessible that imo its pointless bc I didn't learn or gain anything from it even after I could understand parts of the solutions. I did learn some material decently well but that was purely from relying on external resources, the textbook, or some CAs.
