What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is a course that deals with many parts of Latin America in a very fast pace. The discussions tend to be complex, and the students should expect to read and write a lot to keep up with the course load. As part of the class, the Sawyer Seminars brought many interesting guest speakers that were very relevant to the discussions in class.
This course is a great introduction into the issues of Afro–Latin America, led so effectively by Professor Alberto and Professor de la Fuente. The discussion was among the most active for a seminar I have ever taken. The readings were similarly rich, even when I did not believe they would be relevant to my interests.
This is an amazing course on a topic that is rarely taught at Harvard. Prepare for A LOT of reading, as a full book and several shorter articles are assigned each week. That said, all of the readings are so carefully chosen and teach you so much about the history of Afrodescendant mobilization in Latin America. The professors are super encouraging and are always available to meet during office hours. I also really appreciated the Sawyer Seminar component of the course because we were able to hear personally from Latin American Afrodescendent activists who are working on these issues on the ground.
Amazing experience that goes beyond the materials and sessions, but be prepared for a really heavy workload considering the number of credits
They need to be trained in Spanish or Portuguese Language and to have enough free time on Fridays because classes extend beyond 2:00 pm and can go until 3:00 pm
