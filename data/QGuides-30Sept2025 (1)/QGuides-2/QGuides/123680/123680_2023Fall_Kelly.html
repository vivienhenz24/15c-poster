This class is difficult even if you have taken calculus before. The section leaders and CAs are very very nice in my experience but the course for me was really not taught very well. There was a lot of being thrown in the deep end to try a problem on your own in class instead of everyone going over it with my section leader in the first place. In the end, I feel that we could have covered the base material of this course, L'Hospital's rule, derivate rules, definition of limits and continuity, related rates etc. we could have done this faster but for some reason it was longer and more difficult. I think this is because they emphasize you "understanding not memorizing" and I don't know how much that really worked.

I short, if you get placed into this class (at least with my experience this from this year) it will not be easy so please plan accordingly. My grades were not bad, still very much A range but it was a struggle. 3 PSETS a week is A LOT. Practice exams were harder than the real exams. It could have been way worse in the grand scheme of things but if you're planning for a light class (even if you're good at math) be prepared for this to take up a fair amount of time.
If you aren't entirely confident in your AP Calc AB scores, this is the course for you. A PSet class like this can be a drain on time, but the resources available to you will make it more than doable. I highly recommend utilizing the Math Question Center and office hours. If you can take this class with Professor Kelly, do so (probably the 9 am). Also, if you are an incoming first year, don't underestimate the difficulty and importance of midterm exams.

I took this class as a first–semester junior on the Econ honors track. If you can take this class earlier, absolutely do it. 3 PSets a week is a lot, and the sooner you can get that done, the better. That being said, it is a fantastic class that teaches you the fundamentals of calculus. PSets are a lot, but doable and you get a lot of help from CAs and section leader OH. Exams are also very fair, even easy. If you need to take this class, or you want to learn basic calculus, absolutely go for it. If you're choosing between 1a and 1b, honestly take 1a unless you took BC Calc in high school: math at Harvard is a different beast from HS math, and you may appreciate a lighter introduction –– I sure did!
