Oliver is a nice person and he tries to make the class fun, but one of the general complaints from most students is that you dont really learn much. Lecture attendance is mandatory, yet I never really felt like I got anything out of them and always had to re–learn the concepts outside of class. We also often had to learn things for exams that we either didnt learn in enough depth (for example, we got tested on the chain rule before even officially learning it) or werent even relevant to intro–level calculus at all. Grading on exams is very harsh; for example, even if you get the correct answer, you will lose credit for the entire problem if you dont include a specific step or piece of information that he wanted you to include but didnt specify. Despite how hard it is to get decent scores overall in this class, Oliver does not offer any opportunities to improve your grade. I did like the CAs as they were really chill people, but most of the grading and obviously the teaching isnt done by them. My final grade in this class ended up lowering my GPA pretty significantly, and Im extremely frustrated as this really isnt the type of class that should be negatively affecting your GPA this much. Unfortunately, for this class and instructor specifically, the results I got did not even closely match up to the amount of effort I put in. Again, Oliver is a sweet person and I appreciate that, but there are a lot of aspects he lacks in when it comes to actually teaching math, and I cant say I had a good experience in this class overall.
