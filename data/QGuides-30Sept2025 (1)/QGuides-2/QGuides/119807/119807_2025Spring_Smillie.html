What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If you take this class, expect to dedicate a lot of time to it. There will be four papers assigned each week, each with a required discussion post. Papers range many topics in the biomedical sciences, so most of the articles will likely be out of your area of expertise, meaning they will take more time to read. It's not necessarily that the course material is difficult to understand, it is just that there is a constant stream of work throughout the semester – you will consistently have a paper waiting to be read for this class.
At the end of the semester, there is a assignment to write a 5–page (~4000 word) proposal. The class unfortunately did not provide much context on best practices in writing a proposal, other than a tip sheet from a different university (which was helpful). The proposal will take a lot of time, as it is also required that you write it about something outside of your area of expertise.
In taking this course, you will learn how to get the main ideas from a paper in a timely manner, and will gain experience writing proposals. While both of these are good things to learn, you will have to spend a lot of time in order to do so.
You can choose to BS this class and spend an hour per paper or take it seriously and spend 4 hours per paper. The course will not reward you or entertain you for trying harder, so you're on your own if you decide to put in effort
If you are not a BIG student, this class would be an interesting way to be exposed to a wide variety of work being done in the computational biology/genomics space. The grading is not too harsh BUT it is quite a bit of work every week, especially towards the end of the semester when there is a large final assignment with no in class time devoted to learning about or working on it. I would not recommend this class if you are not very interested in this field and/or if you do not already have a strong background in biology, as there is no lecture and we go straight into reading scientific papers, which can be really challenging.
This class is only as stressful as you want it to be. You can decide how much of the paper you want to read (just know that your participation is being tracked on an Excel sheet).
