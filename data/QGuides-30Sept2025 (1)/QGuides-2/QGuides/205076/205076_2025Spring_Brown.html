Keep up on the readings!
Take this class!! Professor Brown and Professor Johnson are amazing, I loved taking a course with two professors because they had such a great rapport and were able to bring great insights into discussions, bouncing off of one another. Assignment–wise this course is pretty chill, only one large paper at the end of the semester (which is super flexible) and then weekly discussion posts. Nothing is graded in the class, you just receive a grade in the end, but I anticipate they will not be harsh graders. I took this course as a young undergrad (this was actually technically my first history class of college), and I got a lot out of sharing an intellectual space with so many advanced undergrads and graduate students, but I will say that a lot of the material likely flew over my head because of a lack of background in things like Caribbean history or the history of capitalism. It may be more beneficial to take this course with a bit more foundational knowledge under your belt to make the most of it; however, it is definitely doable with less background if you are okay with figuring things out as you go. Go to office hours––I was intimidated at first but they are so open and want to guide you! The reading load is HEAVY (200–400 pages a week) so I ended up spending a lot of time on this class this semester. It was frustrating at times when I spent hours on a complex reading and felt like I walked away with a low understanding of it, but the other students a are likely just as confused by some of the readings. This is a class where you get out what you put in, especially when it comes to the readings. Overall, this class is a great way to get to know some INCREDIBLE professors and engage in a super intellectual environment with a ton of cool students, talking about some of the most relevant themes of capitalism, African American history, intersectionality, organizing, etc.
