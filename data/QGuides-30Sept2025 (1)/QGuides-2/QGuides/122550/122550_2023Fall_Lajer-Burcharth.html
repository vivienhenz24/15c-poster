TAKE THIS CLASS! Very interesting material and gives you a basis on art history and how to analyze art.
This class was incredible. It prompts deep thought and visual analysis as well as broader historical and cultural understanding. If anything, this course teaches you how to be an observant and analytical thinker. By the end of the course, you will start recognizing visual patterns and be able to scrutinize every detail in a work and derive meaning from every intentional detail. The readings can be lengthy, but I often skimmed them and felt that it did not hinder my performance in the class dramatically. They will help you write stronger weekly response papers, understand the lectures on a deeper level, and participate more effectively in the section. Lectures are delivered by numerous different professors, with some more engaging and exciting than others. Some lectures are conversational, organized, and engaging while others are not concise at all and feel as if a script were being read for an hour. The looking labs were my favorite part of the class. Once a week, you go with a small group and a TF to the Harvard Art Museum or other locations on campus to view pieces that are similar to the one that you studied that week. This part is very engaging, casual, and fun, and you will realize how much progress you have made in your visual analysis. By the time the section rolled around, I was often a little bit tired of the material, as it does become slightly redundant after readings, lectures, and then the looking labs. However, some sections were fascinating and also fun as you sometimes get to explain your own weekly response papers and hear from others as well. These sections were personally my favorite, as you got to see the work that your classmates put incredible effort into such as designing their own monuments.
