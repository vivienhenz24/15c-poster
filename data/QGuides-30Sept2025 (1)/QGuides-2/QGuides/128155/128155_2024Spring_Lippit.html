Great course, Professor Lippit is amazing and so kind
This is such a great class. Professor Lippit's lectures are always filled with details and so inspiring to listen to, and he is very knowledgeable and accessible outside of class. The sections are also a highlight as we get to see the print collections of the Harvard Art Museums at the Art Study Center every Friday, and Carolyn is a very erudite and kind TF that makes sections fun to go to and learn more about the weekly topics while we look closely at the details of each print. Please take this class!!!
The course is extremely well planned and presented. The instructor comes half an hour early to every lecture to prepare for each talk, which is eloquently delivered without pause with so much information it is impressive that he always ends on time. Each lecture starts with a helpful review of the previous. The reading materials are not difficult but eye opening to the history of print design, and the weekly viewings at the Harvard Art Museum are always great. It is a real privilege to see so many prints, many of which are well known, in person, as photographs of them never do justice.
PLEASE TAKE THIS CLASS!!! The subject is so interesting, and the class is really not much work besides a weekly response paper (minimum 1 page) that is pretty loose and graded easily. You get to see woodblock prints up close from the Harvard Art Museum's collections every week, which is an experience you wouldn't have otherwise since they are not on display in the museum. Professor Lippit is one of my favorite profs at Harvard since he is so kind and approachable... he remembers so much about his students which shows how much he genuinely cares about us. I had a conversation with him recently and instantly feel much more hopeful about HAA as a concentration choice. His lectures are very engaging and organized. The midterm and final are standard HAA style (ID, comparison, short essay) and not too bad! Loved this class, would take it again if I could.
This course was a quintessential HAA survey course, and executed perfectly. The professor and TF were both extremely invested in the course and did a great job at pacing while sharing the maximum amount of content possible. The assignments were helpful and fun, and made for a great learning experience.
I absolutely loved this class! It provided a very comprehensive overview of the subject, and did so in a way that was digestible for all. Professor Lippit truly is the best and he is super willing to get to know all his students. Lectures were organized very well and we got to see some of the most phenomenal prints in person at viewing sessions. Workload is reasonable, and prepares you well to understand the prints at a higher level. This is a must–take for any HAA concentrator!!!
If you have the chance, TAKE THIS CLASS!!! As someone who had always been interested in how Japan had such a distinct visual culture, this course answered so many questions. But beyond that, it made me realize how so much of modern art in the world was influenced by this dialogue and exchange between eastern and western cultures. Lippit gives the best lectures, and the sections are super cool because you get to look at these famous prints in person. Looking at it from a broader perspective, I think this course is great if you want to explore a new avenue of aesthetic appreciation. I definitely look at most art in a different way now – after studying processes, the development of techniques, and the plethora of influences that can be seen through visual media.
Take this course! One of my favorite courses at this school, and Professor Lippit is an amazing lecturer. The topic itself is really cool, and this course will give you the tools to get the most out of looking at any Japanese woodblock print and situating it within the context of its history.
This course was one of my favorite courses I've ever taken at Harvard. It was incredibly enriching and enjoyable–––I looked forward to section and lecture every time. The exams did take a lot of time to prepare for because they require a lot of memroization
Highly recommended if you are interested about Japanese Art
If you have any interest in Japanese traditional to modern arts, TAKE THIS COURSE. I've learned so much about woodblock prints and Japanese history during this course. Professor Lippit is the absolute greatest and is so supportive of all of the students. Don't fear the memorization aspects as they are manageable and the readings are quite interesting! I'd just say make sure to go to lecture and pay attention as it is easy to miss things because the lectures are quite packed.
Professor Yukio Lippit is amazing! If you aren't taking this class, definitely consider HAA 18J, those two classes were definitely among the best classes I've taken at Harvard. While the topic of Woodblock Prints might sound niche, they might not be as unfamiliar as you think, through Hokusai and Hiroshige prints or works of Japonisme. You will get a great overview to looking and understanding the tradition of prints and their reception in Japan/abroad and the course really leverages the rich collection of prints owned by the Harvard Art Museums, making section a really special experience where you can see prints without the glazed covers. I can't recommend the course enough!
