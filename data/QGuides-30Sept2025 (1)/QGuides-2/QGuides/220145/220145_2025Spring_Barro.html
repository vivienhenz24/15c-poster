Comments
This is a small, advanced tutorial which has lectures that focus on macroeconomic theory. Prof Barro tries his best to make the content digestible. He knows a lot and is happy to talk to students. Overall, you take away however much you put into it, because there is a lot of freedom and not a lot of structure, besides 2 presentations (one paper, then your paper) and your final paper. This can be good or bad depending on your preferences. Independent research with lectures and student led presentations, basically. Fun class!
Amazing class, you get what you put into it. Talk to Prof. Barro and be engaged he is very insightful and the classes small format presents a great opportunity to have discussions and get to know everyone in the class better.
this course is the most "choose your adventure" of any I have ever taken. Essentially no mandatory readings, only a few assignments in the whole semester, and infinite freedom in your paper topics and structure. prof barro does a mix of lecturing on growth models (which I found to sometimes be a little too pedantic, but generally interesting) and talking about papers (which depended on the quality of papers, but was generally incredibly interesting). there is lots of in–class presentations and discussions. if you want the opportunity to do your own thing with a little economics research, this is your chance, and prof. barro is wonderful to work with and talk to. if you want to get hand–held, this is not for you.
