What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is an insightful class with many thought provoking topics at the intersection of contract theory and organizational economics. Prior background in solving such problems is very helpful to do well on the assignments and exam; however, if grades don't matter to you, take this class to learn the big ideas.
This course is amazing if you *really* like the content, and, if you don't, I would imagine it is quite boring. Oliver's lectures are amazing, and the class is not too much work (a few hours a week). The class grade is 80% final exam, which can sound scary, but don't stress about it you will be fine! The class is fun, tight–knit, and intellectually stimulating.
As with many graduate–level courses, I would recommend this class to students who already have a clear sense of their research interests in economics. This course will be especially valuable for those interested in economic theory and formal modeling, or for those studying relationshipsformal or informalthat involve issues like moral hazard, adverse selection, or contractual incompleteness.

The course offers an excellent overview of core models in contract theory, and Oliver Hart is an outstanding lecturer. The lectures are delivered using chalk and board, which I found particularly effectiveit allows students to follow the material step by step and helps the pace stay clear and thoughtful.

I strongly recommend keeping up with the readings, as they are important for fully understanding the material. That said, the course can sometimes give the impression that the theory of contracts is largely settled. Personally, I dont believe thats the case. To get a better sense of current research, Id suggest supplementing the course with some additional reading on recent work in the field.
Great course overall. Would recommend. Oliver is sometimes hard to follow but is very knowledgeable and really cares. Jimmy is the best TF! He is very flexible with any deadlines.
To me, Oliver was one of those instructors that completely shifts our way of viewing research. His classes are clear, easy to follow, intuitive, and have a clear direction. It's just a great course. It has a lot of value added and really elevated the types of questions I'm asking. And I hope your TA is as good as Jimmy, because he took the course above and beyond. Literally couldn't have had a better experience and my favorite course in Cambridge so far.
This is a well–organized course with good professor and TA. It would be a very good option for students interested in the topic!
Take this class if you're interested in learning more about contract theory, although do be prepared that it is very mathematical.
