NBD 209 was by far and beyond the best course I have ever taken at Harvard. The opportunities for direct patient interaction and to learn from clinicians and scientists at the forefront of the field are unmatched. I strongly recommend this class without any reservation to future students. The trek to Longwood from Cambridge is so worth it!
Just take it.
If you're thinking of going into medicine in any capacity (physician, biotech, research etc.), you should take this course. It was my favorite course this semester. It was equal parts inspiring to hear directly from patients and physicians and fascinating to receive the clinical/research background from leading physicians and scientists. I was exposed to so many medical branches of neurobio, something I hadn't had the chance to in any of my other courses so far. If you're on the undergrad campus it's a bit of a hike, especially at night when the M2 comes only every 30 min or so. The course went over time a few times (by 30–40 min) but it was more on time toward the end. But if you have a research/volunteering position in Longwood a few days a week, this course is great to pair with!
This course is so amazing and wonderful. It challenges you to learn more about research techniques and gives you a breadth and depth of research so you can read an article and understand it. Also, the patient presentations are super compelling, and made me cry on more than one occasion. It is very human and real. Easily one of the best I have taken in Neuro department and at Harvard overall.
Each and every class in this course will deeply change the way you see disease, science, and people. You will leave the course with more understanding of what neuroscientific research and neurological medicine entail, and more passion to care for the people who seek medical care for neurological diseases.
This is one of my favorite courses Ive taken at Harvard. The speakers were absolutely incredible. Lecturers are all at the top of their field and very willing to engage with students in discussion. Further, they brought patients with neurological diseases that were extremely open and honest about their lives. It was a real pleasure to hear their storiesan opportunity not afforded to many undergraduates. Though some weeks were especially fantastic, all of the lectures imparted some wisdom I will carry from this point forward. Importantly, during this course, you will:
– Hear the most cutting edge research for neurological disorders, from ASOs to gene editing techniques.
– Learn from patients with these disorders and, in many cases, actually using one of the innovative therapies we've discussed.
– Discuss papers assigned by the speakers for that week, so all of the selections tended to be extremely recent and directly connected to the course content.
The course is really a delight. The assignments throughout the semestermainly, a presentation and take home finalare not painful. If you have the opportunity to fit this into your schedule, I highly recommend it. The lecturers are all very open to questions, answering thoughtfully and comprehensively no matter how basic or advanced the inquiry. Though you should not let the distance to the medical school dissuade you, note thatif you want to make it back to the main campus before 9pmyou have to leave class a couple of minutes early to catch the M2 shuttle at 8pm.
This course was a fantastic survey of a variety of neurological diseases and their therapeutic approaches. The 2 biggest value adds are (1) the lineup of speakers is just honestly incredible. The course truly draws the best of each field (2) Patient visits really contextualize the disease for you. With this aspect of the course, you are able to bridge the gap between an NEJM article and actual patient's experiences.
Very eye–opening! Every week revolves around a specific disease. On day one, there is a physician–researcher that brings in a patient to share their experience, which is followed by a presentation on the disease and research advancements in the field. On day two, there are student presentations on journals selected by the guest which aims to foster discussion. It is a very well–thought out setup! As someone who is not really research driven, it was hard to stay engaged or always understand what is going on. I was mostly in awe of the patients aspect, and was sometimes unsatisfied by the huge research lens. Nonetheless, I grew to appreciate it.

The staff is super kind. On days that class runs late, the staff makes sure to provide dinner for students! The TFs meet the students a week prior to presentations to go over the paper and answer any questions, which is very helpful.

The take–home–exam is hard but a very fair assessment. It will make you appreciate the research component of the class, and it's worth taking the class if it's offered your year :)
