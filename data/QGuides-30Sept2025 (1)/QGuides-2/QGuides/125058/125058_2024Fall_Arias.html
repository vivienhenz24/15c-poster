What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

I would tell them to pay attention and do the readings.
Spanish 11 is just what you'd expect. Some prior experience with Spanish would definitely be beneficial, however its not required if you work hard and do all the homework. If you don't like the teaching style of your section professor, SWITCH IMMEDIATELY, this will make your experience in an already demanding course more stressful and unhelpful that it needs to be. Juan is great, if you get him your confidence in Spanish will increase greatly, as will your speaking abilities. Homework sometimes seems a bit like busy work especially with the long readings/writings but it'll pay off in the end. Also, try to engage in class discussions and participate frequently as this will make the class time well spent instead of a long 1.5 hours in another language where nobody participates.
As someone who hadn't taken spanish since the summer going into 10th grade (during covid), this class really eased me back in a perfect way: it was just the right amount of challenge. I loved the way the course is structured with 4 projects and 4 exams that follow a theme, as well as the homework assignments preparing us for in class practice of those concepts. Further, just simply talking in Spanish in class completely helped me learn vocabulary and grammar. The TalkAbroad assignments were really interesting as well! Professor Juan was amazing and so supportive!!
