This class was wonderful !! Highly recommend if you have any interest in art history ! if you do take the class, DO THE READINGS. While the class is pretty low maintenance, doing the readings is really important in getting the most out of the class.
For anyone who's taken HUM 20, this course is basically HUM 20.2. It's nice to hear from a range of professors in the department but it also meant each week was potentially hit or miss and you could never go too in depth into any one subject. In general though I think it was a great class.
This class is a requirement for HAA concentrators, but it was very well taught. Professors rotated through and gave lectures on their topics of research / expertise. Would recommend actually doing the readings because you would get the most out of the museum component. There is a weekly response paper, but otherwise it's a really chill and intellectually stimulating course.
I loved this class. My TF (Roxane) was amazing––– she gave phenomenal feedback on our weekly writings and did a great job helping lead our weekly class. I really did love this class and there was absolutely zero pressure week to week. Just do the readings (you dont "need" to, but I think all the students agree that it really did help you get more out of the class) and join into the course discussion. I learned alot, met a ton of faculty, and always looked forward to going to class. Roxane, if you see this, thank you. I know the entire class loved having you as the lead :)
Good survey course.
This class was a good introduction to the HAA department and the work that the professors are doing in the department. Some of the presentations were more interesting than others depending on the week, but the discussions after were usually quite interesting. The weekly reflections, though they sometimes felt tedious, were good because they kept me engaged with the material and was a good way to keep writing even though there weren't that many larger assignments in the class.
This course was a very great introduction to understanding how the different HAA professors approach art history. There is a lot of in person viewing at the art museum which was very enjoyable, however, there will also be a lot of readings in preparation for each seminar. Overall, the class was great and the assignments had good feedback that helped prepare for the final paper.
Even as a survey, it is worth taking to meet the other concentrators and get to know the department better
