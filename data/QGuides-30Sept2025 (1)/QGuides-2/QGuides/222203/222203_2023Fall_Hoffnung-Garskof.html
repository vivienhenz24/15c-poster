A good class if you’re pre–law and interested specifically in immigration. Covers repeated trends of exclusion and discrimination within our immigration system, but also how the law has changed in a positive way over time to become more inclusive in some cases. Class is reading–heavy and participation–heavy and it was one of my heavier loads of the semester. Reading quizzes are fine once you understand how to take them and use the reading guide, but can be time consuming because they’re not open note so you have to read every part of the reading in detail. However, I would recommend the class if you’re interested in the material and willing to put in the work.

And I learned about the field of immigration law, from court–watching and from currently–practicing lawyers who came in to speak to the class. My TF was so lovely — she made section such a safe and welcome place, and the classmates I got to know through group projects, assigned "teams" and seats in lecture, and through section, were such great, passionate people.
