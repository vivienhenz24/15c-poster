The lectures were well–developed. The course did not include much fine detail and instead focused more on developing our intuition about macroeconomics; without prior experience in macroeconomics, I valued this approach. It allowed me to understand macroeconomics as a whole before deciding whether I want to explore the area in more depth. However, if you have taken macroeconomics in the past, this course might not be challenging enough. Additionally, some of Jason's lectures felt dry and dragged on a little. The content was interesting, but I think he could have presented it with greater tonal variation and expressivity.

The guest lectures were incredible. When David and Jason discussed topics in class, they mentioned some of the most important research on that topic, and the guest lectures were usually part of that research! I felt lucky to be exposed to such giants in economics!

However, I would have liked a more diverse range of guest speakers. Most of them were academics or politicians, and I would have liked to hear from more industry experts, such as bankers.

The psets improved my grasp of the content. I especially liked how they reflected present–day events, like the effects of Covid and tariffs. Through them, I applied what I had learned in class and became more confident with the material.

The problem–solving sessions are an incredible resource. The CAs helped me work through the psets and understand the concepts involved. They were all nice and supportive.

The unit review program is also quite valuable. By studying for them, I consolidated my understanding of the material. The feedback I got after taking them showed me how well I had learned the content and where I should focus my studying.

Because the problem–solving sessions and unit review program are so useful, I would highly suggest attending them

The tests do a good job of testing our understanding and problem–solving skills. The practice tests are a very useful resource to study for them; in fact, I think some of the questions were essentially identical to the tests.
