What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class definitely pushed me out of my comfort zone. I took this as an elective with no prior background or experience in film and I felt like it was very doable. The course was a great balance between learning the technical aspects of film and working a camera, while also understanding how to be creative and what can be defined as nonfiction videomaking. Sky is awesome and showed us some really great films throughout the class, he encouraged participation and really helped push us!!!!! Highly recommend to anyone who's ever thought of trying film and videomaking. Everyone in the class was at different levels of experience but the environment made it really easy to learn and grow with each other.
One of my FAVORITE courses in my 4 years at Harvard.

Here's what I gained:

1) Broadened my understanding of what a non–fiction film can be
2) Enriched my appreciation of film and ability to communicate ideas in film–language
3) Taught me how to use a professional film camera
4) Taught me how to edit videos in Adobe Premiere Pro
5) Created three short films that I'm proud of!

I had little to no film experience before this class and came out of it feeling confident that I can use filmmaking / video making as a story telling tool. It's a great mix between watching/discussing films, learning technical skills, and creating your own short films. You are encouraged to take creative risks and give/receive honest feedback on your projects.

If you are a creative thinker, are openminded to feedback, and can figure things out via google / youtube / chatGPT, and are willing to put in the work, you'll do well. In weeks before a deadline, filming & editing will take a lot of time, but it's fun work. Wish I would have taken an AFVS class sooner!
TAKE THIS CLASS! It is such an amazing introduction to filmmaking. The content is engaging and relevant and it provides students with the skills and experiences to enhance their creative abilities. I loved learning from Professor Hopinka and believe that every student who has the opportunity to work with him will learn an immense amount.
This is overall a good class. You have three main video assignments you are working on throughout the semester, so you kind of have to keep yourself accountable for filming and editing on a good schedule so you're not all doing it the day before. Sometimes it feels like you have no work and other times a lot. My year you got to use real, professional cameras which were pretty cool. You don't know your grade at all throughout the semester until the end; so you kinda just vibe in the class. Sometimes we would do screenings of documentaries and talk about them, other times we would talk about our projects or have editing or shooting workshop days, and other times we would screen our projects and get constructive feedback. Generally the feedback was good and useful, and the projects were fun. However, a lot of the film screenings were lowkey boring. But still I think I would recommend. It's supposed to meet twice a week for 2 hours 45 minutes but our teacher was chill so we always got out at 2 hours. Not as intense as some other art classes I've been in.
