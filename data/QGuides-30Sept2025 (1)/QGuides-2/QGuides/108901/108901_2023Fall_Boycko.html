This is a great class!

Pre–reqs: If you haven't taken 10a (which I did not), then you'll be fine. Just spend some time (2hrs) getting familiar with supply–demand graphs, how to interpret shifts, understanding the impact of waiting lines & quotas as well as taxes & subsidies and you should be all good! In terms of math, make sure you know how to take a derivative and solve for turning points of a graph. If you can do that, you'll be okay. This isn't a particularly mathematically heavy course.

Exams: Exams are the majority of this course's grading (about 70%). However, exam multiple choice questions and long problems map very well to homework. Aslong as you do, and re–do, your problem sets and quizzes, you should be all set for the exam.

Lectures: Lecture slides are a beautiful study resource. You should go to lecture (you get 5% bonus points on the year for going to lectures), but if you miss/ don't pay attention, you are fine, as the slides are very easy to learn from.

Book: honestly I opened the textbook twice. I wouldn't say it is too much of a necessity to have it or do the readings.

OH: tutors were great. Many abundant office hours every day. I didn't go to section, but some people found them useful, others not so much. Again, slides were available.

Frustrations: if you come from a mathy/ rigorous STEM background, you may be announced by how "intuitive" rather than mathy some of the explanations are. I personally wish I pushed myself a bit further and took 1011a, but I still think this was a great course.
