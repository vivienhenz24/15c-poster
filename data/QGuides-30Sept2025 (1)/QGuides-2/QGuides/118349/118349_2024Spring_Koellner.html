However, if you're looking for a low–stress way to fulfill your humanities requirement, this class may be a good fit. We were supposed to have 6 problem sets, but it ended up only being 2.
how is this a class? i learned nothing. there was absolutely nothing no philosophy component, and it was just a messy physics class which only confused me more. it is soooo easy tho, it is hard to be mad at it.
This class is alright for satisfying a requirement (only two easy homeworks) but not much else. We covered the Doppler effect and Bondi k–calculus, and followed some interesting sources. Lectures were hit or miss, but section was quite good. Feedback was also fair. The course deviated significantly from the syllabus, which proclaimed to use "Notes on Geometry and Spacetime" by David Malament as the "main source" but instead added it as an afternote for bedtime reading if desired.
Very relaxed class. Your grade is purely based on problem sets, and we had two in total. Material is interesting, but kind of repetitive if you are a physics concentrator. In this iteration of the class we didn't get to General Relativity and mostly discussed special relativity and EM.
Great class for expanding your thinking about the world we live in. What you get out of it depends on what you put into it.
Course was a bit disorganized but easy to complete. I feel I would have learned more otherwise.
Mind–blowing subject matter. Lectures are interesting but would have been better if interactive. I learned a lot from the psets, but would have preferred smaller, incremental assignments for re–enforcement and to track progress. Section was very helpful

I am not entirely convinced this class ever happened. As hard as I think, I cannot find anything I learned from it. There was no TF until 2 months in. No psets until after spring break and even then only 2 in total. As far as free credits go, I'll happily taken them; as far as I came to university to gain some marginal amount of knowledge, I have no idea why I did not drop this before the 5 week deadline.
If you're really into math and physics and are motivated to get to the truth of the matter, the spirit of this course is for you. There is no need for an extensive background in math if the topic appeals to you; in fact, the focus isn't on mathematical rigor but rather the scrutiny and development of the big ideas in physics. If you focus on the details in this course, you may get frustrated. The texts for homework are less than concise and occasionally this course may be misleading on some of the smaller details. But with the right mindset and motivation to put in the work, you'll find this very enjoyable.
This course, at least this year, was incredibly unorganized. While it was very easy, it was also very unsatisfactory. I would not recommend.
