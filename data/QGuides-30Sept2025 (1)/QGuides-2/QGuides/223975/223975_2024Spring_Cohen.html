This class was phenomenal. I didn't realize how much we'd learn about research methods, which was a nice surprise. I feel like I'm walking away prepared to conduct research in this field. Marema and Jessica are both great to learn from and the classroom environment was refreshingly collaborative. The readings are also super interesting! I liked the way the course was structured from contraception to post–partum experiences. We had the opportunity to hear stories from people in the stillbirth space, which isn't typically included in maternal and reproductive health conversations. I liked that we kept guest speakers to a minimum, for the sake of consistency.
Jess is awesome – so much of the course hinges on her own research, and her vast network of colleagues whom she has collaborated with add so much insight and corroboration to what we learn in class. When she brings in guest speakers– it inspires those of us who aspire to have similar careers to continue working on these important issues.
Take the course. if you are genuinely interested in the topic you should learn something. Jessica Cohen is incredibly knowledgeable and very sweet.
Take this class if you are really really interested in the topic or want to learn more about it.
This class, in its first iteration, offered a lot of potential to dive deeper into maternal healthcare and pregnancy health. I learned a lot about the status of maternal healthcare in the US and how we as a nation continue to fall short in all aspects of healthcare, and it was definitely interesting to take on a more analytical lens through reading past research studies. That said, this class has a heavy statistical component that was not clearly stated in the syllabus/course summary, to the point that most of the class is just knowing how to read and dissect a research paper. That's not hard, per se; it just makes class time a little less exciting and engaging. But overall, still a good course that I would likely recommend but to keep in mind what one would want out of a global health course.
Dr. Cohen and Marema are FANTASTIC and are absolute stars in this course. Lectures were well constructed and were great combinations of overviews of quasi–experimental approaches and issues in maternal health around the world. The literature is highly relevant. Low work load with HIGH yield of learning that you'll be able to use in conversations professionally and otherwise. Section was lecture style and less interactive. Overall it didn't contribute to the learning since the statistical methods covered were covered in lecture enough to understand the readings. Would have been a great opportunity to cover more literature! Overall a course you must take and you'll be really glad you did.
