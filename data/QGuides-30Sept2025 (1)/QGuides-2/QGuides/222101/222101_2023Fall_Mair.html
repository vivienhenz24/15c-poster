I am not sure how useful machine learning methods will ultimately be for my research (e.g., for more 'data driven' methods, there are still a lot of experimenter degrees of freedom). If you are really pressed for time, it might be worth some pre–preparation on your part to figure out how much your work needs these methods, since the investment you need to put into the class is definitely non–trivial. But, if you have some time to devote to understanding tools leveraged in psychology more broadly – which still might be beneficial to you down the road! – then there is a lot to gain from this class. Learned a ton, even if I do not take it all 'back to the bench' tomorrow.
As always, Patrick did a fantastic job providing an intuitive understanding of state–of–the–art machine learning techniques, with a large focus on hands–on, practical applications to data using Python. You walk away from the class with ready–to–go analysis pipelines for a wide range of ML and NLP applications. Patrick went to great lengths to ensure that the content of the class stayed up to date with recent developments in the fields of ML and NLP, which is a huge feat given new things are coming out every week!
