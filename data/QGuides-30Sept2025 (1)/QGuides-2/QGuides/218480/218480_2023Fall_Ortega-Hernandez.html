This class is a really good choice for a first–year seminar! Even if you have just a little bit of interest in fossils and paleontology, take it! Javier is a really kind and thoughtful professor. He's also a really cool person! You also might get to go digging for fossils and keep what you find!
Even if you have absolutely no background in paleontology but are interested in fossils, you should take this class. The assignments are very simple (usually just readings with the occasional presentation) and you'll get to see a lot of cool fossils.
course was not very engaging and was entirely listening to presentations for two hours. however there was very little work and sometimes the topics were interesting. make sure you are passionate about the subject before enrolling in this course, especially considering seminars don't give you credit for anything
This is a great class if you are interested in paleontology. The discussion topics were on the whole interesting, and the workload is light so it is a minimal pressure environment to explore something new! Javier is a great guy too, and he provides us with plenty of opportunities to explore the subject practically.
Great course with a passionate professor, would definitely recommend
As an incoming first–year, I felt a bit overwhelmed with my course load and wanted to find a seminar that filled my intellectual curiousities but was not too much of a burden. This course is for you if you feel the same and have an interest in fossils, climate change, and what they can tell us about the past/future. There were three short presentations (not bad at all), most readings were optional, and no tests, which reduced the stress of force learning. One qualm – this course takes place at 9 AM. Beware if you are not a morning person. Hopefully, they change it because interesting conversations are inevitable in this class, but it depends on whether you can stay awake.
