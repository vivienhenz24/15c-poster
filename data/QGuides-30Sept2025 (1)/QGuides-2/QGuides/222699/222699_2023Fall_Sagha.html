This is a good class to take if you want to understand more about global politics, especially the role that Iran plays as a powerful actor in the Middle East. The workload is pretty light, and you'll definitely learn a lot.
This class is absolutely necessary to anyone that wants to pursue a career in foreign policy, especially in the US context. The understanding of transnational Shi’ism is a prerequisite for foreign policy that is both successful on a national and a global level.
If you are interested in contemporary Middle Eastern politics, then this class is perfect for you! It focused mostly on Iran, but with about a week dedicated to Lebanon and later to Iraq. Lectures do tend to run long though
If you are interested in Islam, take this class. Good intro to Shi'a Islam and as someone who is not Muslim, I found it very illuminating to learn more about the origins and current state of Shi'a Islam. Professor Sagha is super knowledgeable and was able to provide a super detailed answer to every question someone asked which was just so cool. Overall, would definitely recommend as I think most people would learn a lot.
Take the course, Professor Sagha is such a wonderful lecturer!!
Take this course, even (or especially) if you have little background in the topic! Professor Sagha is a great lecturer, his knowledge of and enthusiasm for the subject matter is palpable. The readings are interesting and the combination of theology & politics is really well thought out. The historical slant of the course is also much appreciated – I've taken other courses about the Middle East with a more wonkish "poli sci" orientation which were much less interesting and informative than this one.
Love this class! Professor Sagha is incredibly kind, understanding, and knowledgable. Go to his office hours and talk to him, because he has a wealth of knowledge. Definitely a more advanced Middle Eastern politics course, so while anyone could take it, significant interested in the Middle East/Shi'a Islam would be good (there are more introductory–level courses, but this is one of the few classes at Harvard that goes into this much depth). Really really would recommend any class with Professor Sagha.
Excellent course and excellent professor.
