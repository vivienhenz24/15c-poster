If you are at all interested in this course, please apply for this seminar. The course description does not do this course justice. Even if you are not sure whether or not you will enjoy this course, try to attend the first class. If you do choose to continue with this course, really make sure that you have time in your schedule. The course load is very heavy for a freshman seminar, and from day one, Professor Bsheer will tell you that it is not meant to be an easy course. However, the amount that you take away from this course is incredible. Do all of the readings, really try to understand the central argument, engage in class discusison, and you'll walk away having learned so much.
Be prepared to do the work for this class. The weekly readings will take a substantial amount of your time but they are so worth the phenomenal discussions that you will have in class if you truly engage with the text. Professor Bsheer is an excellent instructor and mentor who made this class truly transformative for me. I highly recommend this seminar as long as you are willing to devote time to the syllabus.
Incredible class just be prepared to work and not be like all the other freshmen seminars. Amazing class and professor Bsheer facilitates great discussion but yeah lots of reading for a freshmen seminar.
Take this course!! It is definitely challenging, but it is so rewarding. It allows you to change how you think about oil, international relations, and knowledge production. Such an incredible class and was definitely the highlight of my semester!
Be sure to take this class! It is definitely difficult, and sometimes you will feel like you are being treated less like a college freshman and more like a grad student, but you will never regret taking Oil and Empire. Not only is it an amazing class with interesting and engaging course material (be prepared to read (Prof Bsheer recommends skimming) 100–200 pages a week) but Prof Bsheer is an incredibly knowledgeable and approachable teacher who is interested in teaching the class for the right reasons. She will always be ready to change the topic and engage with what the class really wants to talk about, or even just talk about what's going on around school/in students' lives. Even if it can feel a little intimidating, going to Prof Bsheer's office hours is always a pleasure whether you're going to talk about your research paper or just how your life is going.

The research paper should be mentioned. At the end of the semester, a 15 page paper is due. However, the way the deadlines work is that most of it will be due in early November, meaning there is not much to do after besides adding the final section and revising. If left to the last day this is still a lot, but even spread out over a few days it is a very manageable amount of work. It definitely wasn't one of the things stressing me out the most during finals week. You will find that, by the end, you'll be doing the paper less because you have to in order to pass, and more because you want to show yourself and Prof Bsheer that you can do it well.

Our class this year became very close, which isn't something that I can promise. But I'll say that among all seminars, Prof Bsheer's is uniquely suited to building a community that goes beyond the classroom. If it is proof: when people asked what seminar I was in and I responded "Oil and Empire," they always seemed to know which one I was talking about, and had only ever heard good things!
