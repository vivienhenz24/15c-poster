Because nearly everyone who takes this class is going so for the requirement, I advise people not to be intimidated! Even if youve never coded before or are bad at math, Prof. Legewie and all other course staff are very understanding. Youre mainly responsible for understanding the topics conceptually and learning to use R but particularly once you learn the program, the class becomes quite easy. Go to lecture/section, look over the psets in advance, ask questions, and youll be golden!
A great class to take if you have little to no statistics background. Joscha and the TFs teach content in a digestible way. Also, the assignments are graded fairly and are evenly spread out throughout the semester.
If you have no idea how to code and are trying to learn without making it your life along with an easy QRD, take this class. Hours vary by when an assignment is due (usually its 3 hours a HW assignment) but the class overall is extremely doable and I reccomend it, especially if you are a humanities or social science person looking to get a QRD completed. Soroush is also an incredible TF and was extremely helpful during all of the assignments.
A great opportunity to work with quantitative methods of reasoning, especially if inexperienced in this regard.
Overall, not a bad way to fulfill your QRD. Probably the easiest introduction to R that you will find. There's a learning curve, but Joscha really works with you to help you understand the material. There are very few problem sets, and the course moves very slowly to make sure that you understand the material. If you're confused in class (or just don't go to class), PLEASE make sure you go to sectionmy TF (Charlotte) was great, and section is a great chance to actually learn the material and apply it. If you're a social science concentrator and you're really scared of coding, this is the class for you.

Make sure to start the PSETS early and ask for help to understand them better. You will have to do PSETs and write papers.
If you're taking it, it's likely a requirement, but don't be scared. The teaching staff is great and I learned a lot!! Don't let rough grades at first tear you down!!
This class is perfect for beginners in stats and R. You don't need any background for this course. I do think you should go to lecture and sections and try to pay attention so the material sticks, but the lecture slides were easy to follow. The section TFs were great at helping when coding got confusing, but there is plenty of support. I would definitely take this class if you need a Stats requirement and don't want to be super stressed the entire semester.
This is an excellent introduction to coding and R. The teaching staff is very patient and kind and they genuinely want their students to learn more about coding. If you put in enough effort, you will do well in the course.
If you are a sociology major, I would encourage you to take this course over Stat 100. It is definitely more tailored to the social sciences and from what I heard, also a bit more manageable. This course was small enough that you could get to know the professor 1–1 and get help that way. Additionally, my TF section leader Soroush was very kind and always eager to help whether that meant moving his availability around to help me. Seriously, he once met with me while he was at the airport about to go on vacation so that I could understand how to do my final project analysis. I came into this course with no coding background whatsoever, and the assignments in this course, while definitely a bit challenging, were doable. Yes, I would be confused during lecture, but attending section taught me whatever I didn't get during the lecture. That said, if you struggle with R like I did, try to block out time to do p–sets. I wish I had started earlier than when I usually did so that I could have attended office hours since some concepts required more time than I thought. The professor is also very kind and would slow down during lectures when he noticed students were grasping what he was teaching. This teaching team was very responsive to student needs and I appreciated that. I wouldn't call this course a gem but if you put in the effort, it can be. Do go to office hours and sections, especially if you have no background because without it, I do not see you being successful. The final paper, while intimidating, is broken down throughout the semester. For example, we turned in our abstracts in October, our code in November, and finally the project in December. The teaching team does their best to prepare students for this during class so it's not just ignored. The midterm was open–notes and you could use past p–sets so as long as you have a rough idea of the content until then, you should be good to go. Overall, I really enjoyed this course, even though I hate coding, as it was well organized. Once again, if you are a sociology major, I'd take this over any other options to fulfill the requirement.
