Unless the HW changes, it can be very repetitive, and the quizzes are unnecessarily challenging. You might well be confused about your grade until after the final. That being said, the material is super interesting and it's a great S&T course for non–stem people.
Two competing effects dominate my view of this class (1) the material is extremely interesting, (2) Professor Henrich has figured out how to effectively force you to engage with it in ways that sometimes get in the way of life and your other classes. I would be more resentful about (2) if (1) wasn't so true; at the end of the day, I think that having to repeatedly engage with information about human sexuality, power struggles, evolution, religion, and other key topics was quite valuable for me. However, it is worth knowing that you will have to do the reading on Perusall, an AI software that will auto–grade your comments (though generously) and that you will have to watch many flipped classroom lectures if Henrich doesn't change the way he teaches this class.
A second–order effect is that I did find my faith in humanity somewhat diminished after taking this course. On the other hand, I came away with quite a few facts about sex that my friends found interesting.
