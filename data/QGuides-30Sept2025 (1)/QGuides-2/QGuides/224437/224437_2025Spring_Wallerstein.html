Great class!! Definitely one of the more time intensive classes, but I think it is really rewarding. I really enjoyed being able to conduct my own research on a topic meaningful to me. Also, coming into this class with no qualitative methods background, I gained a solid understanding of qualitative methods.
This class is great! The readings are actually very interesting, and don't take a crazy amount of time out of your week. Definitely start doing research and collecting data early! Don't wait until last minute. Highly recommend this course!
This is such a wonderful junior tutorial!!! Joey is such an excellent instructor, providing feedback and insights into the field while also being very relatable and meeting everyone wherever they are coming from. I am so grateful to have had this experience and definitely did not have the dread that sometimes comes with required department classes. I would recommend future students think deeply about their term paper from the very beginning because you end up spending so much time on it that it is much more meaningful if you love what you're doing.
I would highly recommend this class. You learn so much about the world and how different social relationships exist in the world, as well as how they are connected to one another. Additionally, the material was very understandable and interesting. Lastly, Joey is a great professor and is super chill and does a great job of engaging you with the material, as well as always being available to be there for you if you need him.
As far as sociology concentration requirements go, this is a super fun one to fulfill, especially if you have a topic you enjoy. I would say to stay on top of the readings, and always remember you have less time than you think you do to wrap your head around the concepts and arguments you want to make, complete interviews, do an elaborate literature review, and write 25 pages. To that end, start early, and make life easier for yourself later. also, choose a topic you genuinely are interested in, because you will get to know it well throughout the semester. If you have a chance to take a Junior Tutorial with Joey, definitely do it. He is great, and if you need any accommodations of any kind, he will make sure you have what you need and will always be open to feedback if things need to change throughout the semester.
This class is awesome and really gets you to critically think about social ties in ways I had never previously thought. Be sure to spend time on the final paper throughout the course or the 25–page limit will really creep on you.
I highly recommend this course or Joey as a professor for junior tutorial. He is a great teacher and well–versed in sociological theory. Social networks and social network theory are going to serve you well in whatever you want to do from healthcare to education to law. The papers during the seminars leading up to the final paper are helpful.
Solid sociology junior tutorial. Professor Wallerstein is wonderful – would highly recommend any of his courses! This course felt kind of repetitive – you can only analyze social ties so much before it feels redundant
