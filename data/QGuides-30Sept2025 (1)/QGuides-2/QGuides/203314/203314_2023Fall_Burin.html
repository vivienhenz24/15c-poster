What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is a really fun course! It is a lot of work, and you are expected to come in to work on your projects outside of class, and to be honest thats the only way you can finish them anyways. SUPER hands on.
This class was fun and interesting, and left a lot of wiggle room to explore materials and methods that students found most interesting. I really liked the range of things that we were introduced to. It was a slightly unorganized class, just in that it did not strictly follow the syllabus and it was sometimes difficult to follow as things changed mid–semester, but overall I would recommend this class.
4 This course is not for everyone as it teaches art from a process over result perspective, focusing less on purpose or symbolic meaning but more on experimenting with materials, and tends to be pretty unstructured at times, not following topics on the syllabus. It takes more of a [d]uck around and find out approach for teaching that can be self–centered and fun at times, but also frustrating, especially when a lot of time is spent on projects which make me question "what is art." This course has some pro's: I learned a lot about process art and experimenting with materials, as well as getting out of the perfectionist mindset and instead appreciating "mistakes" and "destruction" as avenues for art to grow and explore. It also helped me to get a better feel for how AFVS exhibits work, how to install artwork in general, and the different resources the carpenter center offers. That being said, there are better courses in the AFVS department. With so much making and breaking, I too had a couple of breakdowns over this class.
The class is great if you need an easy course to bang out a requirement. Having the freedom too create whatever you want is fun, and you can't go wrong.
