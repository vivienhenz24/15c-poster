EVERYONE SHOULD TAKE THIS COURSE!!! no matter what your religious background (I'm an atheist) or your sexual identity, this class is a must. It's interesting, eye–opening, funny, bizarre, amazing. Just don't do what I did and wait until there were only three weeks left in the semester to start the papers. Or turn in the take–home exam late like I didoops.
TAKE THIS CLASS. Dunning is amazing. There are only 2 response papers throughout the whole sem, and a take home final which is just 2 more response papers. Lectures are amazing, readings can be tough but you can kinda pick and choose which weeks you wanna engage with.
Take this class! As a senior, this has been one of my favorite classes at Harvard––it's really reshaped my understanding of epistemological approaches to hermeneutics and theology and how it bears on modern conversations and politics around sex, gender, and sexuality. Professor Dunning aims to problematize the notion of calling something "Biblical," and he has done so wonderfully!
This course was taught by an amazing professor who was clearly just so knowledgeable and so personally invested in the subject matter. I loved the in class discussions and explanations of the readings. The readings were super difficult for undergraduate students outside the Religion concentration (like me), so I really appreciated Dunning's explanations of the readings in class. I also appreciated that female authors were included! It is really difficult to have this in ancient writings. However, this class is extremely difficult if you are not a religion concentrator. I would not take unless you are ready to grind.
TAKE THIS CLASS! Professor Dunning is fantastic and the workload is no more than 3 hours a week. You have two essays for the term and a take whole final exam. The grading was very fair and if you participate it should be more than fine. The best part of this class is being exposed to all different perspectives. I encourage you to enter into the class with an open mind and see how your ideas on sexuality and Bible change throughout the course.
