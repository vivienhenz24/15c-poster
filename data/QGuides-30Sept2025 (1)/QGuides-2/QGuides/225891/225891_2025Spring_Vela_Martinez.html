Very interesting material and a relatively low–stress class. The final project is a creative assignment
The texts that we engaged with in this course were incredibly complicated, but they allowed for great discussion and revealed the many complexities in our systems of gender.
The class was overall great. The TF and the professor are genuinely wonderful people with incredible knowledge on the topics discussed during the course. Although this course was presented as a graduate course, it didn't pose the challenges most grad courses entail. I can see this class being great for freshmen or sophomore students (especially in the section where there is a heavy emphasis on learning how to build arguments). If you are a senior, I would say this course might be a bit underwhelming. We didn't focus on analysis or theory very often. This course would greatly benefit if they didn't separate the grad and undergrad students as much. Additionally, the course isn't very organized, so if you're planning on taking this course, keep this detail in mind. The professor would often adjust the syllabus or make small adjustments to the plans.
Its a great class! Prof. Vela Martinez knows a lot about Latin American literature and film in general, so you learn a lot even about other topics. Its a fantastic course that really helps you dig into the idea of femininity and violence.
I had a great time in this class! Alejandra is always an amazing and thoughtful professor and the course material was very interesting. It was fun to read/watch about such a wide variety of women killers, and it's such a rich and nuanced topic. The reading load is definitely on the heavier side, but there are great films/books/stories to learn from in the syllabus.
Take it take it take it! Though the syllabus may seem a little intimidating, you learn so much and it's just so interesting! If you know Spanish, you should take at least one course with Vela Martnez.
This is a fantastic class! Dr. Vela–Martnez is a great professor; her lectures will teach you so much and discussions in this class were always incredibly interesting. The workload is not too difficult but you do have to read some novels and watch films, so you should be comfortable reading and listening to Spanish. Overall, getting to study 'why women kill' for a whole semester was such a unique opportunity, and the class really made me think more deeply about society's expectations for women.
