What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I wouldn't do a thesis again if I had a choice – it was very stressful to have that letter grade hinge on the opinions of two readers who I'd never met before (unless this has changed, my thesis was graded by my PI and two other readers who my PI/the department selected). From everyone I've talked to, publications matter a lot more than senior theses, and focusing on a publication would've been a better use of my time while also likely being less stressful. However, the grading is probably a little more generous than you might expect, though the scale is not standardized and some readers can be arbitrarily harsh. It's also clear that some readers don't really read the thesis, and several readers turned in ChatGPT–generated comments.

Also, let your advisor know that the Hoopes prize exists early on so they can make a decision on whether to nominate you without being rushed! It's their final decision on whether to nominate you, but a lot of PIs aren't aware that it exists.
Quit. Don't quit. Noodles.
Before doing a thesis, I would ask yourself if this lab experience/research is something you would have done anyway even without the end product of a thesis, if you think your lab is giving you the support/guidance you need to complete the thesis, and if you are ok submitting something that you aren't going to be 100% happy with, because you're never going to have the time to do everything you want to do for your thesis. If the answer to these questions is yes, then you should definitely do a thesis!
