What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Take this course!! Professor de la Fuente is a wonderful teacher and really makes this course great. There is a fair bit of reading each week (some weeks a book a week), but it is always manageable. I also know that if for some reason you are not able to finish the work for the week or you are struggling just in general, Professor de la Fuente is understanding and really cares for all of his students as people. He is always available to meet with students and even if you can't make it to his offices hours, he'll make an effort to schedule another time to meet. In terms of the actual material, while at times it can be tough, it is incredibly important and you will learn so much. It is an interesting class and approaches race and racism from a perspective that I had ever learned about them from. This class has helped me to understand the origins of both a lot more clearly and how to find the logical fallacies that come with their construction. I completely recommend you take this course!
I think this is a very interesting class. You will learn so much in this class because there are a lot of readings, and the discussions will prompt you to think in a very different way about race. Be prepared to do a lot of reading and writing. It is definitely a lot of work, but you will improve both your thinking and argumentation skills in this class.
As someone majoring in HisSci (hopefully!) I think this course was a great intro to what I am going to learn more about in future classes. Though the readings are a bit long, I enjoyed a majority of them!
I cannot emphasize enough how incredible this class is. Prof. de la Fuente is a fantastic seminar leader, and engages you and your classmates to intellectually interrogate the texts that you will read throughout the semester. The readings can be difficult, but they are consistently rewarding and thought–provoking. You will develop so many new skills through the semester, from being able to closely analyze a scientific text to tracing the historical evolution of a certain social concept like race or racism. Take this class!!! You will not regret it!
