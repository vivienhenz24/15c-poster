I TOOK THIS CLASS PASS/FAIL. IF YOU ARE PLANNING TO DO THAT, THIS INFO IS FOR YOU. IF NOT, IGNORE.

If you want this GenEd to be on par with the difficulty levels of other ones, pass/fail is pretty much a requirement. There's a couple key details to pulling this off, however.
1) Don't go to lecture. You will learn all the concepts in lab. Guest lectures are a lot of fun, but 4.5 hours a week of class time is a lot for pass/fail, so pick a few guest lectures you would want to attend and skip all the rest.
2) Don't study for exams. If you have some basic math background, and can remember a few concepts from lab, you will get around 70s on exams, which is plenty.
3) Minimal effort into the psets. They take a long time to do well. A long time that, presumably, you do not have at your disposal. Just answer 80% of the questions. AI is your friend.
4) Do good labs. You have to be there, so you might as well try. Learn what you can, and fill out the notebooks well. This will save you points.
5) Do a decent final project. This is worth a lot, so do put time into it. Only pick a non–pass/fail partner if you are willing to go hard on the project, or else it's simply unfair to them.
