okay!!! when i read the qguides, everyone was talking about how easy this course was as its main failing. i have spent a great deal of time teasing out what exactly about this course didn't sit right with me, and saying it was too easy isn't it; i'm always down for a nice chill, easy course. here's the thing: if you don't know a thing about positive psychology and wanna take an easy class, go for it, hang out for the vibes, learn some new things, pet professor langer's dog. if you DO know anything about positive psychology, you will be sorely disappointed and increasingly frustrated. professor langer categorically rejects stress. she firmly believes stress is always both avoidable and harmful, so all of her lessons boil down to "just think differently and you won't be stressed anymore and then everything will be fine." which, yes, reappraisal and mindfulness are both really amazing tools in positive psychology, but they are neither our most interesting tools nor are they universally effective in combating stress. by focusing solely on these methods, the course robs itself of the ability to meaningfully engage with important other coping factors such as meaning in life, religion, and problem versus emotion focused coping. by largely refusing to deal with or acknowledge stress, this class denies itself the opportunity to grapple with hope, courage, coping, resilience, and post–traumatic growth. the course flattens my beloved field of positive psychology down into a shallow, two–dimensional pantomime of itself that solely consists of rejecting, avoiding, or ignoring the difficulty we face in life instead of accepting it, grappling with it, and letting it change us.
