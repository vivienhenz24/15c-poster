I would highly recommend this course! Of course Andrew Berry is a great, very engaging lecturer; you really have to see his lectures in person to understand. I thought the exams and problem sets were fair, and the term paper is not bad as far as length and requirements and you choose your own topic. I think the fact that pretty much the whole class showed up for the optional last lecture, which was on stuff that we knew would not be tested, goes to show how much everyone enjoyed this class.
TAKE. THIS. CLASS. If you have even the slightest interest of how life on earth got to be how it is, and potentially how it is going to turn out, this class will be a great time, regardless if you are concentrating in OEB or not. If you have to take one OEB course, I would recommend this one.

Dr. Berry is an excellent instructor, and beyond being incredibly knowledgeable, he is just downright funny and engaging to learn from. I always looked forward to lecture, and though the exams aren't necessarily "easy," they are not impossible. I found them to be fair, not too difficult, and reflective of what we went over in class.
This class is a fair bit of work, but it's really interesting and you learn a lot. Andrew Berry gets to know (or already knows) most of his students and makes the lectures somewhat interactive, so it's a very fun environment. You get a really good understanding of how life arose, in many different aspects of that question.
***this course delivers on what it's meant to be, which is an introductory survey course to evolutionary biology. Andrew Berry makes lectures very fun and has great teaching analogies. exams were fair with p–sets designed to be exam prep/practice; make sure you understand the section readings well, as those can be the hardest questions on the exams if you tend to skip readings and/or aren't focused in section. (note: as an Evangelical Protestant Christian, appreciated Dr. Berry's emphasis that religion and science aren't mutually exclusive, although I do wish he defined 'Creationism' in more detail before discussing it in class, as the 'Creationism' we discussed in class is only one particular anti–science creationist framework albeit one held by many Christians at the popular level amidst the many well–argued scientists, theologians, and scientist–theologians who disagree in good ways about how to correlate Scripture and science.)
Best class I've taken at Harvard. It feels more like an *experience* than a mere class. IYKYK. Take it
Super fantastic course. It is evolutionary bio. Wish we could have learned more about behavioral evolution and sex as those were the coolest parts of the course. I'm not that much of a fan of archaeology and the only reason I didn't mind that module was the fact that AB is such a phenomenal teacher and human being. Love that guy.
Take OEB 53 for Dr. Berry, stay for the fascinating course content. Dr. Berry delivers the most engaging lectures of any Harvard professor, replete with hilarious analogies, entertaining lecture slides (impressive powerpoint skills!), and the occasional chalk–throwing and standing on students' desks to demonstrate the advantages of bipedalism. I genuinely looked forward to coming to class, even on Friday, so don't be scared away by the 3x a week lecture + section. Section was also incredibly well–run and involved a lot of scientific literature reading which was very interesting. The weekly readings and occasional PSets were manageable but the exams were difficult study in advance and attend the optional review sessions. Overall a phenomenal course, Dr. Berry is awesome and the material is fascinating I truly think everyone should learn about evolution even if you're not a life sciences concentrator.
Very fun and interesting lectures taught by the one and only ANDREW BERRY!!! Exams were fair but sometimes difficult (make sure you really pay attention during lecture and take good notes so studying in the future will be easier for you).
Andrew Berry is just awesome. Even though this is a hard class, he makes every lecture super engaging and entertaining. Because he was so enthusiastic for the subject matter, I became more interested in it and grew to appreciate many of the topics taught within the course. BE PREPARED TO WORKKKKKK FOR THOSE MIDTERMS. 3 midterms...just get ready. After not doing well on the first one, Andrew Berry helped me in understanding what I got wrong and made me feel better about the next 2 exams, and I ended up doing better on those. The 3 psets are mostly chill, and section really helps you in understanding the course material and section readings. Use the term paper to write about something you're really interested in, and you'll have an enjoyable end to the course!! Overall, I'd recommend for anyone considering this class!
The lectures were super engaging and the TFs were wonderful in helping us understand the material. The workload is very manageable, but you get a lot out of the course.

Great class! Three psets and three exams throughout the semester, with brief weekly readings for section. No final exam final paper instead, 10–15 pages of synthesis on a topic of your choice. Andrew Berry is highly entertaining as always and will get you excited about evolutionary biology even against your will. Really solid mid–level biology class for IB concentrators and others. Not too stressful in the week–to–week and lecture is always super fun.
If you're looking for a good OEB mid–level, take this one. It's not onerously hard by which I mean, if you study and pay attention, you will do well and is incredibly enjoyable during lecture. Andrew Berry is one of the most engaging professors I've ever had, and I don't say that just because I sat in the front row and he picked on me all the time. Whether or not you're fascinated by evolutionary biology, you will come out of this class feeling entertained, and that's what makes this class special.
