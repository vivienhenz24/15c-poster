This is an extremely relevant and timely course. You'll learn a ton about epigenetics but also just recent biological research and advances. There's a great mix of learning highly relevant content and larger, broadly–applicable skills (how to read a paper well, how to think about/construct experiments, focus on mechanism). And of course, Jason is an absolute legend in the field and an amazing professor with lots of really interesting biology takes that he invites you to disagree with and debate him on. I grew a lot from this course and would highly recommend it to everyone, regardless of whether you're actually interested in biology–related careers... and if you are interested in biology/research as a career, then you definitely need to take this course.
The people in this class were some of the smartest I've met at Harvard. The class material is very interesting and presented in a followable manner. The professor introduced a lot of novel models. You'll get a lot of practice thinking like a researcher/ thinking creatively. I'd recommend doing the homework on your own so you are prepared for the final.
Very good course, incredibly interesting material taught quite well. Jason was lovely and is a great teacher. Although it is slightly unclear the degree to which he will be teaching this next year, so keep that in mind.

We saw a lot of new research at the frontiers of the field, and Jason put together a very coherent course bringing us up to speed on epigenetics. Guest lectures were also quite interesting.

Computational section was a good idea, but felt a bit rudimentary. It's obviously kind of hard to get this just right when you have people coming in from all sorts of backgrounds, but it kind of felt more like office hours during a problem set than instruction in computational methods. Maybe this is the form that just makes the most sense for the class, but just something to be aware of.

All in all, a wonderful course that I'd recommend!
I found that this course picks up a lot at the end, and I found the last 3 weeks of the course especially difficult due to the back–to–back nature of problem sets, exams, and final projects.
THE BEST CLASS I HAVE TAKEN HERE!!! Jason is an incredible teacher! He facilitates discussion so well and his lectures are always super engaging. You spend a substational time with your classmates as the class meets three times a week, so you will bond with them quite a bit. I loved every single class and loved people there, and I strongly encourage everyone to take this class! Jason is a leading person in epigenetics, and learning from him is such a unique and special experience. The class has refined my understanding of science in so many ways, and I believe every biologist should take this class!
This class is AMAZING! This is (probably) the best class on epigenetics offered anywhere in the world. I would also say it is the best class I've taken at harvard so far. This class offers incredible breadth and depth in epigeneticsit is comprehensive in its coverage of fundamental biology, cutting edge techniques (a lot of sequencing), and unique experimental design. Moreover, we learn a bunch about modern day computational biology workflows through weekly computational sections.

Lastly, it is so cool that the class is taught by the scientist who actually kickstarted much of modern epigenetics research through his invention of ATAC–seq. Jason is so incredibly knowledgeable on the field that he is able to provide a question on ANY question asked in the class, even if it is tangentially related to the specific material on the slides. He is also super chill and awesome in general. Emily and Ena are also some of the funniest, kindest, caring, and most helpful TFs I've had in any class.
Great class if you want to learn about epigenetics. Jason is a wonderful and approachable professor, like most in the SCRB department (you should concentrate in HDRB!). To be honest sometimes it sounds like an ATAC–seq class, but Jason does a good job covering all the frontiers of epigenetics today. Also the computational component is really interesting if you haven't had broad exposure to that before – I didn't expect this, but I learned a lot about computational genomic and epigenomic analysis.
It's an interesting class with an interesting topic. The coding portions can be annoying and be prepared for a sudden increase in workload at the end of the semester.
Jason is a fantastic lecturer (truly) and the course content is super interesting, but the evaluative aspects of the course (i.e. psets, the final) need serious revamping. Also, you must have some experience with coding (specifically R) if you are going to succeed in this class.
This course is an excellent introduction to epigenetics. It incorporates a computational component that introduces basic analyses as well, though not with very much depth. There are typically two assignments due weekly, some individual work and some group work. They are all useful in learning how to apply information taught in class.
This is a great upper–level SCRB class! It's the least pre–med–heavy SCRB class I've taken, which I think was important to the community we established in the class. Jason is a great professor with a lot of enthusiasm for the material, but be ready for lectures to meander some (you'll want to take notes or have some other strategy to pay attention). Do not slack off on the final project, it's a very solid amount of work and you will regret leaving it for the last two days. In general, expect to need more than one day to do all of the psets.
