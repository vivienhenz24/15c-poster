Look for connections between theoretical concepts and real–world scenarios. The course often explores case studies and applications, so consider how the material relates to everyday life and societal contexts.
Mayron is absolutely amazing and very understanding. He makes this course what it is!
I highly recommend this course for anyone who is interested in clinical psychology, neuroscience, or just passionate about learning how to improve mental health. Mayron is such an engaging and kind professor who genuinely cares about his students. In this course, you will be able to learn about the importance of identifying the biological brain basis of different disorders and understanding the role the reward system plays in order to be better able to treat these disorders. We only meet once a week for 2 hours and each week you just have to complete a few short readings and write a 1–2 page reading reaction. The only other assignments are discussion leading once with a partner on the disorder you are interested in presenting on and a final project chapter that makes exam period so much less stressful because you write a section of the chapter with your partner every other week. By the time exams come around, you will already be done writing and will only have to add your infographics and bibliography. This is definitely my favorite psychology class that I have ever taken at Harvard and everyone in my class loved it too!
