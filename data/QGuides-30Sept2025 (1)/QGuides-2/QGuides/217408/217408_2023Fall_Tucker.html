Comments
Loved this class! You learn so much from students' presentations of macro papers and general class discussions! Also, with the paper you receive an incredible amount of feedback from Summers and Tucker during research presentations!
This class is wonderful. Class discussions are always interesting (the topics are a bit random sometimes, but that’s part of the fun), and it’s great to hear the professors’ takes on things going on in the news. It’s also a great opportunity to practice doing research related to macroeconomic policy and to get feedback from the knowledgeable course staff.
Take this course. One of the best courses I took during my time here. You will get to know Larry, who is amazing and genuinely interested in the students. You get world–class feedback on your papers
I would recommend this class! Getting to be mentored by two people with such deep experience in policymaking, in two different economies in fact, was a really valuable experience, and I gained so much insight into macroeconomics and how policymaking goes – it was really interesting to hear different anecdotes . That said, the class can be slightly disorganized at times as feedback may not come in super regularly and quickly, so it is really incumbent on you to be self–directed with the research – go to TF office hours, seek out Paul and Larry, etc. I came in with not a lot of deep knowledge in finance etc., so it was a little hard to follow class discussions at first, but it definitely gets better over time.
The course is a great opportunity to work closely with the teaching staff and other students taking the course. However, the course is very unstructured and not intended to teach you the tools that you need in order to write a successful economics research paper. The course also does not feel like a priority to the teaching staff, which is understandable, but comes across in the way that readings aren't set ahead of time and that expectations for assignments are unclear. Still, the discussions in class are very interesting, and its the kind of class I would recommend to someone who already feels comfortable in economics/applies math.
Take the course; beyond Professor Summers being a true titan of macroeconomics, he is truly an amazing teacher beyond his incredible resume. Very pedagogical, engaging, and, most of all, kind. He clearly loves teaching and his students and wants us to succeed. I can say the exact same of Professor Tucker. Their back and forth was both hilarious and illuminating.
This is the best experience I have had in the economics department at Harvard. Larry and Paul were amazing to have in a smaller–format course, and I always felt like I was learning something incredibly insightful from both of them (whether in meetings with them about our paper or while we were on a wild tangent in our class discussion).
This is one of the best classes offered at Harvard. Larry and Paul were both excellent. The only class I've taken at Harvard where we had 3 hour lectures and everyone paid close attention. Brain definitely got bigger.
If you are at all interested in honing econ research skills and learning about macro policy, you have to take this class. Every class, I took away a new idea or concept and by the end, had built a new framework mentally for thinking about macro policy issues. Profs. Summers and Tucker and TF Namrata were all exceptionally kind and available for support during the paper–writing process. Even though it was challenging at times, it was definitely worth it .
The course needs no introduction. Literally – just look at the Professors. By far the best experience I could ask for at Harvard, and one I will never forget
