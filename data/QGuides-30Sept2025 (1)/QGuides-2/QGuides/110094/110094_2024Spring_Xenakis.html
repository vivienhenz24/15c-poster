They constantly work to improve the course with student feedback and really care about us/how we're doing/if we're learning which is refreshing/so thoughtful. I struggled a lot in this course (like spent a LOT of time in office hours haha) but really loved how applicable the content is (psets are about biology/clinical trials/education) which made what we were learning feel relevant and tangible (and now I can read papers and actually understand the statistics part of everything). Strongly recommend for anyone working in a lab or interested in medicine/science as you will learn so much about stats/how to read and interpret them! It can be a struggle but office hours are amazing and really helpful/the class is so valuable.
A hard class to get grades up in by the seems of things but good to have.
Stat 102 is amazing. Julie and James are both so nice and supportive, and there are definitely a lot of resources offered to help you succeed.
A reasonable introductory course and good substitute for Stat 100 for students interested in the life sciences.

Pros:
– Many opportunities to improve grades (e.g. through participation, midterm corrections, etc.)
– The course goes through relatively interesting case studies that require you to actually interpret data results in relation to the real world and think through ethical/social considerations.
– Good opportunity to improve fluency in R

Cons:
– PSETs can be long and difficult (especially interpretation questions, where it's easy to lose points due to forgetting to mention a specific analytical aspect on the grading rubric); attendance at OHs is often mandatory to understand PSET questions.
– The exams are not difficult, but are quite long and stressful to take (you get 3 hours, which you may think is a lot of time the reality is that most students finish the exam just as the allotted time is up).
take it only if you want a challenging class

This class is a heavier workload than others but as a result, you will gain a lot of practice with skills that I found very useful and applicable. If you're interested in learning real applicable skills, this is a great class. The materials are also excellent and there are a lot of support resources!
Take this class!!! The course staff are probably the kindest I have come across. Each member goes above and beyond to ensure that each student fully understands the class materials, and the content of the class is incredibly interesting and informative in regards to real–world applications.
I learned some very specific statistical tests that are very common for data analysis in natural sciences, and a strong sense of how to organize the notes so I can refer to them whenever I need to do the analysis. However, I did not acquire the coding skill as I wished nor the general ability to learn about a new function from sketch. Nor am I confident after the course that I can start data analysis from the beginning. The course material is awesome. Yet, regrettably, there is simply not enough time to cover all of them over the course, and it does take a lot of self–study to understand the full picture of the statistical analysis. I would recommend taking this course if 1. you want to familiarize yourself with the functions and wants some real world examples to see how they are being applied (the course did really well on this) 2. You are very self–driven person and have a lot of opportunity to use the course material outside of class. You would not come out of class knowing how to do real–world analysis, but it will become much easier for you to overcome the initial barrier for doing one.

I would not recommend this course if you do not have some kind of experience with statistics and coding. I felt the course assumed that students had taken AP Stats since it used so much vocabulary, and there is really too much coding to learn if you have never used R before. While I can say that I did learn from this course, the sheer difficulty of it all has elicited in me a sense of regret. That being said, if you have a passion for medicine and want a challenge, the professors are kind and helpful, and this may be the class for you.
TAKE THIS CLASS!! James and Julie are two of the most amazing instructors I have ever had. The PSETs are long and difficult, so start early and GO TO OH. Office hours truly saved my life. I was in there every single week for multiple hours on end, and it was so incredibly helpful. James is the best; he truly cares about his students. He ensures that you are learning in a supported environment and is always there for you. Julie is so kind and skilled at explaining these difficult concepts in a digestible way. I could not have asked for better instructors.
If you give this class your best effort, go to office hours, get help on the problem sets and study consistently, you will not only learn statistical skills that will help you for the rest of your life, you will also learn how to code. I was worried about taking this class at first because I had zero computer science background. I actually had to do below–grade level computer science in high school and even then I struggled. But by the end of this class, I can write and debug my own code and successfully analyze/wrangle data! This course is definitely beginner–friendly. Of course there will be people in the class who have already done AP Stats or are already R experts and they will inevitably have an easier time, but this class is taught in a way that anybody can catch up. It requires a lot of effort but in my opinion it's worth it. Grading is very fair and Julie and James are so kind. They are great lecturers and are always willing to help you with anything you need. I would never have been successful in the class without Julie, James and the awesome TFs.
