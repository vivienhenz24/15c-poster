What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class has lots of readings, and you will get out of it what you put into it. The readings are interesting and important.
This is a greatest hits series on political thought today that gives you a whirlwind tour of some of the major thinkers in political philosophy since the Renaissance, which left me with plenty to think about. Landauer was an engaging lecturer who elucidated the content very well, although he was just visiting this semester. Approach the material with intention and interest to get the most out of it because it the writing, unsurprisingly, can be very tepid at times. I essentially just skimmed everything but it's feasible to say that one would've taken a lot more out of the discussion and concepts covered if all the readings were done (although that would be a time sink). While it's easy to get lost in the details and get overcome by the drudgery that unfortunately is present in a lot of political philosophy, it's not hard to see that the ideas discussed here are among the most impactful and subversive over the last few centuries and it's arguably a favor to yourself to get to understand them.
I loved this class. You read tons of interesting books, most that are foundational. You should read some of these if you study anything political/social related anyways. Landauer is a visiting prof, but he also had great lectures; always so witty and insightful, prompting discussion during lecture. Final exam was not super hard and essay prompts are actually interesting. The grading felt fair. I feel like I read many of the authors I'd read in social studies 10 (had i not dropped the class) but here I had like 2–3 lectures to discuss their works and went way deeper. The modern canon is so interesting and I am glad I took this class.
Any feedback on the course staff won't apply to you, as there will be a different professor next year. But I really enjoyed this classI came away feeling like I have a strong foundation in many works that I have wanted to read for a long time. I now think that all Harvard undergraduates should have to read Marx, Mill, and DuBois in order to graduate. (The other thinkers are all interesting too). This seems like a good class to take as an elective and/or in your senior year even if you're not studying Government or the humanities.
Great class if interested in this subject. However, recommend having experience in a more introductory (political) philosophy class first
This class is one of the harder gov courses (200–250 pages per week of difficult reading) but it is rewarding and you learn a lot about political theory. It also counts for the theory subfield, which is one of the harder subfields.
The readings take a LOT of time (>4 hours a week easy) if you're going to be doing them. If you're not going to be doing them, don't bother taking the course. Just keep up with that and you'll be fine. Final was easy when I took it, and 2 assignments throughout the semester that weren't too harshly graded.
This is a difficult class but worth it if you are able to put in the work. The readings are hard and super dense but if follow through with themes and continuously apply the debates/ideas to today and it will be so enriching!
