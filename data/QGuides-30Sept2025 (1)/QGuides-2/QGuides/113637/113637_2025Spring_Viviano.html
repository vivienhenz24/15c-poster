In the past, this course has received quite negative reviews, but please please realize that this course was completely revamped. The exams were all very fair as long as you started studying well in advance and did the practice tests. Also, the grading scheme being 20–20–20 percent for both midterms and the final made it so that no one test completely determined your grade. I recommend going to section and forming a study group. Either way, this class 100% exceeded my expectations. Do not be scared by previous year's reviews. This is a great course and you will do well if you put in the effort.

Definitely definitely definitely take this course! Initially, I found a lot of the course pre–requisites really scary because there's statistics, linear algebra, and multivariable calculus. However, a lot of the course material doesn't require extensive knowledge of these topics. As someone who was very curious how econometrics works from a statistical and theoretical perspective, I found this class very interesting. I had taken EC50 a year prior, so I was familiar with the R implementations for various causal inference observational studies and wanted to understand the math behind them. If you're more so interested in getting very comfortable with applying these techniques in R, then EC1123 would probably be better for you. This class goes in–depth into derivations and mathematical assumptions, which gets really interesting as you progress. Also, the material nicely builds on top of each other, and there are cool topics that the Professor will discuss without them being tested on the final, giving students an opportunity to just enjoy the material. If you are thinking about doing EC1126 vs STAT 111 and EC1123, I'd say that EC1126 does a nice job in providing all the relevant statistical insights that you need for econometrics with good pacing. The course textbook is really good, the slides are very insightful, and the sections have nice R code implementations. The homework is also very close to the lecture material, which contrasts other classes where you might need to go OH to OH to even understand what a problem is stating let alone how to approach it. Lastly, Professor Viviano is amazing and genuinely wants you to succeed. He designs assignments close to the lectures so that the focus is on understanding the core class material. The exams were all very fair, equally weighted, and you were allowed a cheat sheet, so there are definitely opportunities to succeed. TLDR: Take this class :)
