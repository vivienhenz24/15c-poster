What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Professor Estrin is such a wonderful leader in class and the seminars were always engaging for the entirety of the class time, despite it being a 2h45. I truly felt like I learned a lot from this course. It is definitely helpful to have background in art history and experience in higher level seminars before taking this, but Professor Estrin is very accessible and willing to help if you are ever confused at any point or need guidance on your paper.
If you are interested in more theoretical art history readings and how to apply those, you will LOVE this class. In general, if you're interested in art history, you will really enjoy this class, and learn a lot. It helped me to think differently when writing papers.
Prepare for the class knowing the general idea of every reading, it is a seminar so discussion component is a big part of it.
I would say that if their work is concerned in any way with visual experience, be it in history, philosophy, literature, religion, or any related discipline, this course will help them think through what it is to see, and what it is to make or portray things to be seen.
