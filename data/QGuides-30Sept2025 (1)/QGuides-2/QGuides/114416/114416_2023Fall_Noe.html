Mariana is a great teacher. It was not always easy for me to absorb philosophical material but during her lectures it felt like all I needed was to have enthusiasm and curiosity for the readings and I would leave lecture knowing what was going on. The class is designed excellently to teach philosophical writing. Personally, I finished the class feeling pretty ok about writing a Phil paper. If you are really interested in the subject, I think it would be hard for you not to enjoy the class.
This is an awesome course. After closely reading classic texts by Aristotle, Plato, Seneca and the like, I have a better sense of my own motivations, desires, and what defines a "good" life. I find myself referring back to these ancient schools of thought in my own everyday life. My philosophical thinking is deeper and much better informed. Professor Noe is lovely; an engaging lecturer, very approachable, and very funny. The essay topics were super interesting (e.g., should our decision making be motivated by pleasure?)––– although I wish the essays themselves were grades less on style and more on ideas. You should most definitely take the class. Trust me, you will enjoy it!

Take this class!!! Mariana is a lovely professor –– incredibly warm, sweet, and engaging. Lucy was also amazing, and gave very helpful feedback on papers. The workload is relatively lax –– short weekly homework assignments, a practice essay, two graded essays, and an exit interview. It's a wonderful introduction to philosophical writing and the students in the class ranged from first–timers (never taken philosophy before) to philosophy concentrators.

Also, the content takes you through extremely relevant ideologies, like virtue ethics and stoicism. I was surprised by how many of these ancient philosophies resonate today; I think it's useful to have this general knowledge to take through the rest of life.

Highly recommend this class –– I cannot think of a single shortcoming. It's intriguing and accessible with a chill workload and amazing teaching staff.
