TAKE THIS CLASS! Andrs is an absolute gem of a person, a very fair grader, gets constructive and thorough feedback back to you in a timely manner, always facilitates discussion and participation rather than simply lecturing at you, and is easily accessible outside of class. He truly cares about his students' growth and success, and it shows through the way he runs his class. If you have any interest in urban economics, take this class. Even if you don't have any interest in urban economics, take this class. The theories and topics discussed are very real–world applicable and interesting. Each class meeting will discuss a different academic paper; engage with each one fully to get the most out of the class, not only for writing your own research paper at the end, but for broader lessons to take with you beyond the class. For 970, your lecturer is arguably more important than the topic of the tutorial itself, and I promise you will not find a better one than Andrs. I have been waiting all semester to write this raving review. Words cannot express how much I recommend you choose Economics of Cities, and more importantly, any tutorial that Andrs is teaching. Again, TAKE THIS CLASS!!!
A fantastic class if you are interested in the topic. 970 teaches you how to write, but even more so, how to criticize economics papers and the larger field of academia.
This class was the highlight of my sophomore spring! Andres is an amazing professor and person. Even though there were times that I struggled in class, he was willing to constantly stay back after class or arrange times outside of class to help guide me. Writing the research paper was also super rewarding, it convinced me to write a thesis. The topics in this class are very interesting, even as someone with little urban planning/metrics/city knowledge, all the topics were incredibly meaningful!
