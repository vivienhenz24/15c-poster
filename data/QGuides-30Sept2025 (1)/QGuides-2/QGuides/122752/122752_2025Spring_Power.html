Patrick Power is the best teacher for this course! He is very patient, understanding and adjusts assignments with the pace of the class. He provides detailed feedback and ample time and assistance to complete assignments. I've always heard the horror stories of sophomore tutorial and the copious amounts of reading or writing but Patrick has made me excited to come to this class every single day. This has been one of my favorite classes I've taken at Harvard so far and I am in my senior spring. Try your best to get Patrick! On top of Patrick, the topic of the course is intriguing. It applies to everyday life and privileges we encounter everyday.
TAKE THIS CLASS. Patrick is the best Econ Tutorial teacher and is so passionate about Housing and Homelessness.
I took the Economics of Homelessness with Patrick Power and I strongly recommend you to take this class! Patrick leads sections with great care, ensuring everyone truly understands the material. He also assigns relevant and interesting coursework, so I truly enjoyed taking the course. He also takes the time to meet with you in office hours to make sure you are on track for the course. The hard part of the course lies in the PSets. The Office Hours are not that frequent and the staff are not always that helpful. I struggled through the PSets, but everything else in the course was absolutely great. Patrick was very fair in his assignments and workloads, and understands we all have very busy lives. Patrick is awesome and has the best 970 Section hands down!
It's honestly super interesting and can be raw at times, but I will say that his teaching is not for everyone. Most people had difficulty following along with some of the tangents, but he was still very nice and funny. I would have liked a stronger rubric for assignments as I found myself not understanding my grades on them most of the time.
The Homeless and Housing Tutorial is great. Patrick is very particular with what he wants but he is super understanding and will truly be as involved as you let him be in helping you with the writing assignments throughout the semester. I enjoyed the topic and very happy that I took this particular section and had a better experience than I was expecting.
If you're taking the sophomore tutorial and you have any interest in helping others and understanding the factors that lead to despair, take the "economics of housing and homelessness" section of 970. You will learn a really broad variety of topics surrounding the issue of homelessness, from the economic/policy side to reading books about the personal stories of people experiencing hard times. The assignments felt important and relevant to the real world and our instructor Patrick was fantastic
