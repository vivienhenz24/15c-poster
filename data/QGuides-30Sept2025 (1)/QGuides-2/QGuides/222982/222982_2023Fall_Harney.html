Highly recommend this class. Consistent course load and no exams. This class is pretty easy and you learn a lot about genetic analysis and a surprising amount about population genetics. It would be helpful if you had at least some exposure to coding before this class, but don't let that scare you away from this class. Your hand is held through a lot of the coding and anything done on your own is mostly done through Excel (experience in Excel will allow you to crush this class).
Theres a lot to learn but some of the class practicals can be tedious though you will come out of them a better writer and communicator of research
I'm a humanities student and took this course for science distribution, and I'm so glad I did! Definitely not an easy gem science divisional distribution especially if you're not in a STEM field, but made me remember what I love about life sciences and was a great opportunity to exercise those muscles.
Take this! Whether you are and HEB concentrator or not, you'll find this course fun, interesting, and engaging! Great for a research requirement if you are an HEB concentratior.
this is an amazing class! even if you aren't interested in ancient dna/population genetics you will learn a lot about how to write a scientific paper and use computational tools to analyze a genome, which can be relevant in many different scientific contexts. dr. harney is very responsive and accessible, which I appreciated a lot. my advice would be to start putting together your final report as the semester goes on to make exam period a little less stressful.
Take this class if you have any interest in a very guided introduction to computationally analyzing genomes! I had no coding background but Dr. Harney did an amazing job of prepping the course materials so that everyone could understand. She is also extremely accessible via email or office hours and is just an amazing and kind professor. She was so knowledgable since she works in the field and it was a joy to learn from her! The structure of the course was super interesting since you get assigned a human genome and then get to slowly uncover things about them through the practicals every week. Not only do you learn but you get to do the analyses yourself and actually discover information like ancestry, projected height, and disease risk.
Do not take this course if you do not want to code!! The learning curve is steep and be prepared for the practicals to take a while to finish. There are no exams in this course, but the practicals all add up to 40% of your grade, so doing well on them matters. Grading is a little harsh, so I recommend going to office hours before you turn in any practical.
I recommend this class! Dr. Harney is a great teacher and she explains things very clearly. I also learned a lot about different methods of genetic analysis.
As a brand new course offering, I thought the overall structure and organization of this class were quite good. Dr. Eadaoin Harney did an excellent job putting together the curriculum and assignments on such an innovative topic blending human biology and programming. I appreciated that the hands–on coding, lab work, and writing assignments directly aligned with and reinforced the overarching learning objectives for human genome research skills.

My main critique would be around the clarity and consistency of grading criteria on the practical lab write–ups and coding projects. Moving forward, I would love to see the course develop very detailed grading rubrics and benchmarks for each assignment. I would have benefitted from additional guidelines to better understand the expectations of my work.

Overall, I found this class stimulating as both a programming and genomics challenge! I would highly recommend future students comfortable with self–directed learning take this course to develop an invaluable skill set leveraging code to solve mysteries of the human genome.
Great class with a great professor. Take it if you want to learn a lot without being overwhelmed by the amount of the material. Practicals grading was a little harsh but it made me learn how to actually write a proper scientific report. Very excited to see how this course develops in the future.
