What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Genuinely transformative course. If you go through Harvard AFVS without taking this course you are doing yourself and your education a massive disservice. This is one of those courses that reminds you why you go to Harvard. Teaches you everything about the crazy process of filmmaking. Will be a pretty huge and sometimes unexpected time commitment that sneaks up on you but the work is fulfilling and fun. Everyone in your class will feel like family by the end. Robb, Joana, and Vera are all truly incredibly instructors that will help you grow as artists. This is also a class unlike anything you could take anywhere else in the world, the opportunity to shoot such an insane volume of 16mm film and edit by hand on a Steenbeck is not offered at any film school anywhere. Even if you don't have an interest in documentary filmmaking, please please please apply to take this course, I am mainly interested in fiction and feel that I gained so much from taking this.
If youre currently taking 50A, prepare yourself for the amount of work and commitment this project will come with. On a better note, this course was honestly one of my most favorite courses Ive ever taken in Harvard. Look forward to the spring :)
If you are interested in film, you will learn and gain the most valuable experience. Take this class if you want to become a better filmmaker where get to collaborate with different visions.
Youll take away as much if not more from the struggles than the successes. This is one of the most standout experiences Ive ever had and one of the hardest things Ive ever done. Its trial by fire and especially for concentrators it feels like a rite of passage.
If you want to make movies, this is the best college class you could possibly take. It's so far removed from the normal filmmaking process, which is why you learn so much about the craft. The professors are by far the most involved on campus. The experience of both shooting and editing on film is necessary for anyone interested in filmmaking. It's the most time–consuming class you'll ever take in the arts by far (expect quite a few all–nighters by the end of the semester). But the intensity of the course is its greatest strength, because it brings out the artistic voices of every student involved by the end of the year. Could not recommend more: Robb Moss, Joana Pimenta, and Vera (TF) are the best faculty around.
