TAKE THIS CLASS PLEASE. Even if you're not concentrating in English, even if you're not trying to be a poet, you're going to learn so much about the development of the black interior as well as poetry as a whole. it is so interesting and Prof. Smith is literally a bundle of joy. Loved the way she taught and she puts so much of herself into her lectures. There wasn't a day I didn't feel exposed to something new and even if I wasn't feeling school I would show up just to be in her presence. I grew and took so much away from this class and I felt challenged to grow as a writer and a person
Please, please take this class. Beyond obviously being an incredible poet, Professor Smith is one of the best lecturers I've ever had the privilege of taking a class from. Truly the greatest English course I've taken here!
TAKE IT, It's an amazing class; the content is accessible to engage with, and you will not be overwhelmed in the class with assignments.
Take this class if you want to read a lot of poetry and sit with artists and their work, and appreciate the tradition of lyric poetry and all of its power.
Take it. You get to listen to Tracy K. Smith talk about poetry every week, which is an immense privilege. The coolest thing about it is, as you discuss and dissect the world of the black lyric and all of the different poets who who were connected each other through their works, the black arts era, and more, the unspoken part is that Tracy is an active part of this history as well. But she isn't here to teach about herself, she's here to share her knowledge and her love for the art, and she clearly feels so much joy in doing so. Worth it!
If you're reading this, take this class. I'm serious. You don't need to have any poetic or critical background to succeed––you just need a genuine interest. It's not hard at all to stay on top of things, and I learned so much by taking this class.
