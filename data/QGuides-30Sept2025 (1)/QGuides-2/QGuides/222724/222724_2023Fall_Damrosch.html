TAKE THIS CLASS!!!!! If you are remotely interested in literature, you will love it. Professor Darmosch is by far the best professor I have ever had. He is brilliant, incredibly engaging, and has so much knowledge and experience on the subject. Do the readings, or as much of them as you can, as it completely changes your experience in the class. Even if you don't have time that week, try to read a couple of summaries and read the first and final chapters of the novels. I can't stress enough how good was this class. *
I would highly recommend this course if you love reading. You will have to read 100–200 pages a week (plus a discussion post). You get out of this class what you put in. There are no in–class quizzes or tests, so you could skip the readings (I suppose) and get through with very little work, but if you engage with the material, it can be an incredibly fulfilling and fun experience. The only assignment is one essay, so I was a bit stressed about the grading. Overall, I loved this course and I would recommend it highly to anyone who loves reading books!
THIS IS YOUR SIGN TO TAKE THIS CLASS!!! I'm confident that this class will likely be one of my favorites of my whole time here, as it was overall such a great experience and I learned so much from it. The syllabus was what initially drew me into signing up for this class, and after reading all the books I still stand by that fact, as I read intriguing novels that were often not what I was used to reading. Professor Damrosch really dissected each novel for us and I'm still amazed that he could pack that much analysis in a 2–hour class and completely redefine what I initially thought of each book, in some cases! He's an incredibly enthusiastic, engaging, and entertaining professor, who is also very supportive of his students and readily makes himself available to meet for office hours. Workload–wise, this class was light other than reading novels each week and a final paper at the end, which I loved because this allowed me to really focus on being able to dive into each novel without worrying about juggling other assignments simultaneously for the class, and I honestly think I learned and retained much more that way instead of having multiple assignments to do during the semester! Professor Damrosch also allowed us to get feedback on a draft of our final paper, which I feel like is rare for professors to do, which shows how much he is willing to help his students and really eased a lot of the stress in writing a final paper. He was also super receptive of student feedback on the readings for this course and is making some changes to the syllabus based on it, so I'm sure next semester will be even better in terms of the content! This was my first–ever CompLit class and it certainly won't be the last (seeing as this class helped solidify my decision to declare CompLit as my concentration), and I strongly recommend this class to anyone who is interested in learning more about literature!
This class is an excellent companion or introduction to the humanities. This felt like Hum10 but with a much more manageable workload. The novels were all very engaging and do not fear, the longer ones have excerpts taken out. The professor is brilliant and you will learn so much just from listening in class.
If you love literature and if you are open to reading a novel every week, this class is for you. You would need to be able to balance your time throughout the week to be able to finish your readings. If you're not so good with making a schedule, you might have a hard time completing the readings every week and come prepared for class. I personally am a slow reader and not the best at scheduling, so I had a tough time finishing the readings on time.
While it requires a significant amount of reading, the class proves to be highly gratifying. Professor Damrosch is a brilliant mind, and he will give you valuable insights into a broad range of literary works, making the learning experience truly enriching.
This is an amazing class and the selected readings will definitely be worth reading. however, the only grade in the class is your final paper which can be a little nerve–wracking.
The professor is incredibly open to speak with students about their work, so I found it helpful to go the teacher early to discuss essay topics. Additionally, the class truly becomes interesting if you do the readings (no matter how good you think you are at participating without reading), so start early on readings to gain a comprehensive understanding of them.
