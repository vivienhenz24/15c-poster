I think it's important to break down my evaluation of this course into two components. Firstly, the content of the course and what you will learn as a student and secondly, how the course is run and taught. From a content, perspective Ec1011B is really cool and exciting. There are some really important ideas and models in macroeconomics that are taught in this course and that are worthwhile to learn. However, from a teaching/administrative perspective, this is *possibly the worst course I have ever taken (yes, like ever, including before coming to Harvard)..* Saying this is actually so disappointing since I had so much fun at times during this course, but I think being truthful and giving honest feedback is an important first step to making this a better course. Firstly, the lack of resources/teaching support is pretty wild in this course. The answer keys will give maybe at most one line of explanation which is not particularly helpful. Nothing is really returned in a timely manner in this course (by the time the final came around, only 4 of the 8 problem sets had been returned to us). The most helpful resource was the derivation sessions which was held because of the wonderful CAs, but seriously the course needs to have more teaching support (and probably more than 1 grad student TF since poor Rachel had to everything as the only grad student TF). Also, just putting math on the board and not walking through derivations step by step is not very helpful, especially since this course doesn't really have a great textbook companion (which is a real shame since a well–written textbook would be very easy to write or find for this course). Secondly, the way exams were run this year was extremely frustrating. Professor Bilal totally changed the ways exams were run in this course in a way that was unprofessional, unnecessary, and without a clear pedagogical motivation. It was actually wild that they posted exams from past years that were *way easier* and totally unrepresentative of the actual material that would be covered on the exams. To make matters worse, there were no psets or extra practice materials that adequately prepared you for the way he wrote exams. On the final, his section of the exam was just a test of how fast you could do algebra and was not representative of any economics you might have learned throughout the year. The course didn't even tell us the structure of the final exam before we went into it, which is pretty wild and this is the first time I have ever been in a course like that. All we were told was that there would be some short answer and some long answer which is not particularly helpful especially since they changed the short answer and long answer formats from the previous year. I really don't like to have negative comments about people's teaching and historically I have followed a rule of not filling out the Q if I had something negative to say, but I feel like it would be a true disservice to not offer feedback for this course. Professor Bilal has an extraordinary gift for demeaning other people's work or making hard concepts feel trivial so if you're confused he does not really curate a welcoming learning environment. He clearly understands economics and math quite well, but being able to teach it well is a whole other skill and I think there should be some time given to developing this skill. Thirdly, another thing that was really frustrating about this course was thr way in which they witheld information until very late. Lecture recordings were only posted a week before exams so you could not regularly go back and rewatch lecture material. This was always just a very perplexing decision to me. Also, the answers to things would be posted weeks late and in one case, the 2024 final practice answers were posted the day before the final. The TLDR of this answer is that I am glad I took 1011B since I learned so many interesting things, but I am so sad there is not more efffort put into the teaching and administration of this course because it would make all the difference in a course like this. Finally, one last thing to note: a course structure where it is co–taught but the professors are really only involved with their section of their course is a bad way to each. It makes it feel like 2 entirely incongruent courses and would be so much better if the professors traded off (as was done in Ec10).
