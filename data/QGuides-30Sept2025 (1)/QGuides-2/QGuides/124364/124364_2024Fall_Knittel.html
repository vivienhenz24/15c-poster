This course is very heavy on coding websites. I thought the course would be an equal balance between data visualization and web dev, but turned out to be mostly coding, so something to keep in mind. The PSETs take a bit of time, but nothing unreasonable for a CS course. CSS/HTML/ JS background would definitely make your life a lot better if you're planning on taking this course.
For my CS secondary peeps like me, it is very manageable but take it with friends and a good final project group. you will not do well alone unless you have previous web design experience.
The class is time–consuming but the material is decently easy, so imo it doesn't feel like a heavy class (until the final project). It's all d3.js, so if you've got zero interest in that there's no reason to take it. I had no Javascript experience coming in and I think anyone with basic programming knowledge would be fine taking it.

The coding assignments are generally enjoyable and the instructions walk you through them. You learn d3 by doing the labs and homeworks and lecture is just broad design principles which felt like a waste of time many weeks. There's a kinda annoying amount of busywork (ex: Tableau. what was the point of Tableau?? also random sketching assignments or write–ups for design critiques). The course also felt disorganized and materials were sometimes published on canvas pretty last minute (plus I think they straight–up gave up on giving us reading quizzes, which was honestly great). Grading is inconsistent between TFs but you can just go to the head TF for regrades (I had to get I think 3 or 4 hws/labs regraded).

The final project schedule is not good. For some reason they expect you to have your project "almost finished" two and a half weeks before the actual due date to get full points on the prototype v2 milestone. We were also basically expected to come up with the idea for our projects the same day they introduced the project to us which led to our topic being boring because we just ran with the first thing I thought of. I guess my advice is to start thinking about the project early and come up with a clear and interesting vision so you actually enjoy working on it. You've also gotta be self–sufficient by the time you start working on the project because (at least for us) our project mentor was basically a nonentity and generally did not provide helpful answers to our questions.

Overall though I think the core material of the course is fun and it's a great option if you're looking for an easier CS course.
a few annoying busy–work assignments, but overall a very useful course. learned d3 and basics of frontend javascript. lots of hands–on exercises
