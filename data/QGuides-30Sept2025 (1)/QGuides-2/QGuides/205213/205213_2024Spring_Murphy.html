Generally a good, chill introduction to RL. No psets and only a final project, so you get what you want out of this class. I enjoyed the final project, and it was cool seeing what everyone in the class did. I liked the mixture of textbook and paper reading we did. I also enjoyed the guest presentations at the end of class. Keep on top of reading quizzes! My criticisms of this course are that the lectures could get a bit into the weeds of math and I was not the biggest fan of the in class discussions.
Susan is an amazing professor and this course is a wonderful way to learn about sequential decision making/reinforcement learning. The readings are well chosen. Susan makes the lectures insightful and digestible. I particularly like how Susan fosters discussion during the class meetings with small group and class wide discussion. It is helpful to hear the insights of others and think about interesting questions as a group. The project is a very substantive experience to explore a topic in reinforcement learning that you are interested in, and the course staff provide excellent feedback at various points in the process. I would strongly recommend taking this course if you have the chance and are interested in reinforcement learning on the graduate level.
Take this course! You will have a very nice survey of many areas of reinforcement learning. Make sure to give your best effort on the final project to get the most out of this class. Prof. Murphy is an amazing professor and a very kind human being. You can't go wrong.
Susan is such an incredibly interesting lecturer and is super enthusiastic about RL. It's a decently niche field but this was a wonderfully run course and not too time–consuming or difficult/tedious thanks to the use of what are essentially reading comprehension quizzes rather than psets. It's really a grad class in the fullest sense of the word that you can get by without too much effort if you want, or learn an incredible amount from a leading research if that's what you want instead. Final paper and poster presentation are actually quite a bit of work so get started early. Overall a very good class for requirements in a department full of otherwise incredibly time–consuming courses.
Susan is wonderful! This class is pretty difficult in terms of content, but in terms of workload it was only reading quizzes and the final project. As a result, this class is very much a class where one needs to be self–driven to learn. Though the format of this class makes it low stress, I do feel that having psets or a bit more structure would have been helpful for understanding big concepts in the class. I also did not particularly enjoy the flipped classroom setting since it felt that a lot of times, many people were confused during the discussion questions.
