TAKE IT!!! (if you love physics and/or CS)
This was solidly the best physics course I took at Harvard. Every problem is very clearly intended and curated to actually make you think about the underlying content, and all of the lectures are clean, easily understandable, and well–organized. The content is extremely interesting and makes you interested in pursuing physics in the future if only to learn more about each of the topics, and while the problem sets are hard they're generally pretty short. Overall, I'd say that this is one of the best freshmen seminars for those who are genuinely interested in doing real–world physics. Also, if you're taking Physics 143A, the second half of 143 is basically covered in the first 4 lectures of the seminar, which is pretty helpful.
Despite being a freshman seminar, this class serves as an incredible introductory course in quantum computing/information science. The readings and problems from Nielsen and Chuang, which is THE textbook on quantum information science, are both accessible but challenging, and will help reinforce everything that is covered in the in–class lectures. As this is a freshman seminar, there were other types of interesting readings such as investor presentations by quantum computing companies, leading to discussions about the industry as a whole and the "hype" surrounding the technology. The second half of the class introduced topics such as phase transitions and symmetry breaking, in order to build up the motivation for time crystals, giving us a taste for what the current research in the field looks like, and what the important questions are. Beyond the learning opportunities, we got to bond over pizza every week, and I am grateful for the incredible group of people I met. If you are at all interested in quantum computing, I urge you to consider this class.
This was a great seminar. Norm does a really good job covering high level topics without going into unnecessary detail that make the work too hard to understand. I would definitely recommend you have a solid background with linear algebra before taking the course, however I was fine taking 21B concurrently.
