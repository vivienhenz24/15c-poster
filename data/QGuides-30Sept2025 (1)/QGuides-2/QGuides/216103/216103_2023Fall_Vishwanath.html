This course is scientifically very accessible to everyone, which means that if you're like me and have a strong math and science background, you might wish it went more in–depth or gave more rigorous explanations. It gives an introduction to quantum mechanics, but it doesn't really teach more than surface–level knowledge. The demonstrations are really fun and interesting – on the first day we levitated superconductors – so that's one high point of it. The class is mainly lecture, not discussion. It's a really light work load – I basically did no work outside of class except for a couple readings and one big group presentation. Many weeks I had nothing to do for this class. Just be aware if you're taking this to balance out other classes that you think are going to be hard, because First Year Seminars are automatically pass–fail, this class will lock you in to taking all of your other classes for letter–grades.
