Comments
The class is proportionally rewarding to the amount of engagement one puts into it, but it really demands high engagement in order to make seminar interesting for oneself and for everyone else in the class. On top of that, it is just worth getting to know this period, as it is so wonderful and so rarely taught. If you are not affected by these poets at the end of the course, that would be a very sad thing.
I loved the new perspectives and detailed readings I gained during this seminar! Professor Teskey is super knowledgeable and kind professor, and I felt lucky to be able to hear the ways in which he thinks about poetry. As someone who has read a lot of contemporary poetry, I appreciated how this class was able to broaden my horizons. It is not too much work – all you have is a weekly discussion post + one final project, yet I also felt like I learned a lot.
Prof. Teskey leads this class on Metaphysical Poetry with force and style. Come, mainly for the poetry though. Each week, you will be blown away by the genius of each of the poets whose styles, idiosyncrasies, interests are more diverse than you might expect.
This class is amazing and Professor Teskey is so knowledgable!
This course is way more lecture–y than other seminars – Prof Teskey will spend much of the class talking. I really liked this because I learned a lot more than an average seminar, but something to be aware of if you like classes where you get to yap.
If you like poetry from this period, definitely take the class! Discussions are interesting and assignments are very manageable.
This was a really fantastic course. Professor Teskey is extremely knowledgeable and passionate about metaphysical poetry. He is very kind and very invested in his students. The workload is relatively light and graded leniently as well. For people who are interested in learning about the history of poetry, reading good poetry, or seeking to write more poetry (there are some creative assignments and your final project can be a poem!) this is a great course. It would be nice if the seminar could be a little more discussion oriented; it sometimes feels like a bit more like a lecture, especially when we are going over the backgrounds of the poets, but overall a very good experience. Professor Teskey is a great professor to learn from.
This is a good seminar without much work. The weekly readings and discussion posts are quite manageable, and some of the discussion post assignments allow you to write your own poems. This seminar was more lecture and less discussion than I expected, but I still enjoyed it. I still prefer more modern poetry, but found that my enjoyment for older poems increased throughout the semester.
Go into as many office hours as you can! Prof Teskey welcomes all his students with open arms and you'll learn so much chatting with him about the poems.
Prof. Teskey is a wonderful professor who deeply cares about both his students and the subject he is teaching. He adds witty commentary and helpful context about all the poets and their lives. He challenges students to engage with the course material and is very invested in including all the voices in the room. He also offers many opportunities for creative choices!
Super interesting class with a super low workload. Professor Teskey is great, and you can tell he's genuinely very knowledgable and very passionate about the subject he's teaching. The whole class is participation and completion–based except for the final project/paper which really isn't too bad if you know what you want to do it on early on. I had a great time in this class, as someone who's usually in STEM classes, and would definitely recommend it.
