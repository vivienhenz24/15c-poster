What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
An okay introduction to a set of wonderful seventeenth–century poems you likely won't encounter elsewhere at Harvard. Nonetheless, the course was a little underwhelming. Prof. Teskey takes a very hands–off approach in class (even though he might read many of the poems out loud) and doesn't spend much time analyzing them closely or giving anything beyond biographical context. The weekly posts are good, but you won't get any feedback until the final assignment. In all, this is a very light course, where you won't learn much more than general things about the poets on the syllabus, and which, should you want a little introduction to seventeenth–century poetry, could be a good fifth class.
Take this course! Professor Teskey is one of my favorite professors here, and the poems he selected and his wealth of knowledge made the course truly enriching. If you're turned off by the idea of writing poetry, don't be –– I was pleasantly surprised to find this very rewarding!
This class is a great opportunity to learn about metaphysical physical poetry in depth in a relatively welcoming, low stakes environment. Teskey is an incredibly engaging professor and speaker, and seminars always felt fulfilling. Sometimes the exact expectations regarding participation and assignments were pretty blurry, although this is a small price to pay for an otherwise engaging class.
Take this class. Teskey is wonderful, assignments straightforward, and discussions enlightening. ***
If youre looking to learn more about poetry, its structure, history and especially how to write it, I highly recommend taking this course! You will learn a lot – the discussion posts can be a pain sometimes but they will help you grow. The final project, which can be poetry or a paper, was a wonderful experience especially when you choose poetry because you can practically write about what you want and create work that you are really proud of.
The course load isn't too heavy and the authors are interesting, but if you are seeking a very rigorous engagement with authors you are not familiar with, as opposed to more summary–style learning, I would recommend a different seminar course.
