What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This is a really great course to learn about how to take innovative science from the lab and turn it into novel therapies for patients. It is taught by 2 extraordinary trailblazers, Doug Melton and Mark Fishman, and features lots of guest speakers who are pioneers in their respective fields. Amongst the science courses, SCRB 197/297 is a unique one in that it gives you an appreciation for what happens after something is discovered in the lab.
The course is definitely useful for anyone interested in developing therapeutics and biotechnology. It provides a range of insights into different therapeutic areas and modalities, as well as exposure to a number of interesting physicians and scientists in the Harvard ecosystem. The section component of the course is a bit disorganized, with area for improvement. The paper and presentation assignments can feel a bit repetitive, but provide an opportunity for you to dig into specific areas of interest with a decent amount of flexibility.
