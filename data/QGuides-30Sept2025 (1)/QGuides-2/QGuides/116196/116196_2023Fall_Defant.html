Comments
This class was very difficult, at least coming from someone who was not a 55er (like a little more of half of the class was). All in all though, I felt like I liked Colin's lecturing style when I understood it, and he brought a bit of informality to the class in terms of his energy that made the class more intimate (while also getting through a lot of material).

To give you some reference on the difficulty of the class, Curt McMullen (who was teaching a graduate complex analysis course in the room the class period before us) asked us if this was a graduate combinatorics class early on in the semester.

All in all, the office hours were helpful and the course staff and Colin made sure you knew the material if you asked for help. They were also generous with accepting unpenalized late work which was greatly appreciated by many of us.

Next iterations of the course are not guaranteed to be as difficult since the course content and structure changes so much from year to year, so if you are looking for math classes to take try sorting by instructor if Colin isn't teaching this class next fall.

Even without having to take exams, I feel like I have a really solid knowledge of algebraic combinatorics in general and other combinatorial objects based on how much I was thinking about them from week to week. Some problem set questions still have me a bit traumatized (the cute kind of trauma though). I also really liked the topics that involved algorithms and drawing pretty pictures. This is a very visual math course which I love.

TLDR:great and interesting class, difficult class, great staff, got something out of it.
If Colin is teaching it again, you'll have a good time.
Must take if Colin teaches it again!
