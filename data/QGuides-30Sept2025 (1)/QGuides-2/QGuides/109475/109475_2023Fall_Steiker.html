This class was fantastic!! I would highly recommend it to any student who is interested in law. Professor Steiker is an expert on the Death Penalty and we all learned so much from her this semester. Each seminar meeting, we engaged in a lively discussion of a specific aspect of the death penalty. The debates that we had were so engaging and often lasted up until the last minute of class. The readings are well–selected and very interesting, and I felt that the short homework assignments set me up very well for participating in discussions. Absolutely take this class, you will not regret it!
Take this class! It is fascinating, and the small size of the class makes it very easy to engage with other students and the professor. The readings are dense, but class discussions help to clarify the information.
TAKE THIS SEMINAR! Professor Steiker is amazing and you will learn a lot.
Professor Steiker is undoubtedly one of the most accomplished legal scholars in America today, and in this class, you will get an incredibly in–depth education of the philosophy arguments for and against the death penalty in America today. However, this class is more work than average for a First–Year Seminar, with around 100 pages of reading a week. I also would not recommend this class for people that are interested in the mere politics behind the death penalty, as this class largely explores philosophical arguments behind capital sentencing.
This freshman seminar is definitely worth taking! It takes some time to do the readings, but you gain a lot from said readings. If you're not into constitutional law or ethical debates, however, your experience with the class might be different.

Professor Steiker is a great instructor, that is an expert in the field. She is passionate about teaching and provides her students with many opportunities to learn more about the death penalty and partake in additional events outside of class. Her feedback is really helpful to improve your writing and if you are interested in more niche topics related to the main topic of the course, she will help you find appropriate resources.
It's a very interesting course if you're very into the material and enjoy/don't mind doing a lot of dense readings, including supreme court cases. It's a great class if you're very engaged with the course (very interesting topic and discussions), but I wouldn't recommend taking it unless you're 100% sure you want to. It's a lot of work (weekly readings anywhere from 60–100 pages and 3 essays) and it's easy to let it pile up at the end of the week because you're doing work for your other classes.
Take it if you have the chance. Professor Steiker was awesome and this was by far my favorite class. It won't be offered for a few years because of her schedule so don't take it not running for a few years as any sort of bad sign. If you're interested in law, criminal justice, or the death penalty specifically take this class and you will enjoy it.
Consider taking this classit's incredibly rewarding, and you will most likely be interested by the material throughout. However, you will also be perfectly fine without taking the course (I feel like there is always such a hype and "FOMO" around first–year seminars in the summer, but the truth is that you don't need to take one if you'd rather take another course).
