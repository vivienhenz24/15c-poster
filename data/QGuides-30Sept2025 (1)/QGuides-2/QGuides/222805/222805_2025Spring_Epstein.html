I would not recommend taking this course unless you're experienced in the subject matter. I was hoping to learn more about the war, which I was thoroughly uneducated on previously. However, the course is designed for students with an already–strong grasp on Russian and Ukrainian history, culture, and language. Additionally, assignment descriptions were vague and hard to follow, even when supplemented with office hours. Ultimately, you do learn about the war, but possibly at the cost of your GPA ––– given that assignments are graded harshly and with the goal of perfection, seemingly, in mind.
This is a fantastic course for anyone interested in exploring the war in Ukraine in depth. However, be prepared: its quite reading–intensive and requires a significant time commitment. The grading throughout the semester can feel tough – especially if youre used to consistently receiving A–/A grades at Harvard – but the detailed feedback is incredibly constructive and will genuinely help you improve as a writer. If you submit a good paper, you get a good grade. It is quite fair, IMO.

While mid–semester grades might be lower than you expect, the final grades are curved, so most students end up doing very well overall. Dont let early grades discourage you, just improve based on the feedback.

Sections are engaging and often the highlight of the course. Nancy, you are the best!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!

Professor Epstein is highly accessible and supportive, but also a demanding graderstrong work is rewarded, and weaker submissions receive appropriately lower marks. Unlike some other classes, this isnt one where everyone automatically gets a B or higher for assignments , BUT it does not mean you will not end up with a final grade of A if you improve and actually show that you care about the class.
This course is enriching because it offers a robust set of theoretical lenses with which to process and make sense of the war. However, it felt lacking in discussion of modern developments. Even in discussion of topics directly related to the the current state of the war, like sanctions and future prospects, readings were often from years back, towards the beginning of the war. Also, lectures sometimes felt unfocused, with the intended objectives/takeaways being less than obvious. Nonetheless, Nancy was a phenomenal and incredibly supportive TF.

The midterm also came with ambiguous instructions and had unreasonably high expectations for just an hour and 15 minutes. Feedback on the final essay outlines was unclear, and section discussions were, at times, difficult to sit through.
I don't think taking this class was worth it at all. Regret it pretty much every single day
This class was okay. There were definitely good parts. I enjoyed learning the theory and the readings were interesting. But the assignments are graded harshly and you are sometimes lost on assignments.
I would tell future students to take this class with caution. It is very difficult, so they should not expect to get good grades in this class. However, it is also deeply engaging and was a rewarding and formative experience.
