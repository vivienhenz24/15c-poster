Comments
To get the most out of this class, make it a priority. Really try to do all of the readings each week and give a lot to the two term assignments, because Dr. Clavey gives great feedback. Since you only have two term assignments (and the final), you'll have the time to focus on the readings.
Take this course if you plan on writing a theoretical or historical thesis. Whether or not you are or plan to be a Social Studies concentrator, this course provides you with all you need to know, all you didn't know you needed to know, and more. Charles is one of Harvard's best.

Some strengths of the course
– Thorough and nuanced overview of theoretical and historical methods and their application to potential theses
– Charles is easily one of the best instructors I've had. His command of material and ability to facilitate discussion, answer questions, and pose new ones were appreciated throughout the course
– Great conversation with peers
– Clear and useful assignment whose purpose was quite evident
– Charles often adapted to students' needs and methodological interests in a way that made the course feel responsive and tailored
– I appreciated the split between learning about different methodologies and then witnessing their application, usually in the same

Assignments are not too much work and are very useful.
Charles is an exceptional teachermaybe the best I've had at Harvard. He has an answer to basically every methodological question and, when he doesn't, he knows who to direct you to. He is brilliant, he takes a real interest in everyone's thesis projects, give super helpful feedback on assignments, and is extremely available for meetings to discuss thesis ideas/methods (which he encourages).

If you're considering a historical or theoretical thesis, I would strongly recommend taking this class.
