TAKE THIS COURSE! This course is great. It is as great as a language course could be. I am very surprised by how much I learnt and liked this course, but the work is not overwhelming. I really think this course is structured greatly, and both professors are so helpful. Professor Chen is so accommodating and one of the best professors I've met here. She is patient and has everything a teacher should have! I think if you are interested in learning and practicing Chinese, you should take this course!
If you're thinking about taking 140a, you definitely already know what's up with the Chinese department. He Lao Shi and Chen Lao Shi are both awesome and amazing teachers for this course. Definitely rigorous, but your Chinese will improve a lot, this was the first semester where I really felt like looking at news articles I could start understanding enough to fill in the rest of what I didn't know with context – pretty cool feeling. I'm planning to take 140b next semester but I know a lot of people pivot into other classes like 142a or 163, etc. so definitely recommend 140a regardless of what your overall goals are with Chinese.
Chinese 140a is, in my opinion, the PERFECT way to cap off traditional Chinese language study at Harvard. It teeters on literary Chinese, but not completely, and the readings and vocabulary become more and more true to the real world, authentic, and genuine (as Chen 老师 says, formal and conversational Chinese is no different from one another). The teaching staff is so amazing, and I genuinely look forward to each class. I don't think I've missed any class this semester thus far! A word of warning, however: this is now advanced Chinese, and the vocab and grammar just get harder and harder. At the same time, as students are more likely to be accustomed to this style of teaching, it is easy to just take the foot off the gas pedal. Keep looking for opportunities to practice outside of class! Engage fully in class! As this is most likely, a personal decision to continue taking Chinese, make the most off it.
A very good class with teachers whose motives are to introduce you to the topics and writing styles in chinese the most effective way possible. A very straightforward class and similar to other chinese classes.
Great teaching of actual material and practice speaking. However, particularly in essays and homeworks, you are not very incentivized to improve on your mistakes. Although the teachers attempt this by essay revisions, in reality you just accept their corrections and resubmit. Although logistically difficult, I wish teachers personalized feedback in terms of focusing on your mistakes in language and continuously helping you overcome them.
