What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This year's iteration of Math 232 (including Math 232a) was taught as complex geometry. This entire year long sequence has been the best part of my time at Harvard. Sometimes the class makes me feel like one should learn complex geometry before learning scheme theory. The intuition that this course gave is amazing. Everything feels so much more real now. Professor Popa is hands down the best instructor I have had in the math department during my time here. Excellent organization and boardwork, and injections of rich wisdom and insight. Don't get me wrong, whatever class Popa teaches will probably not be easy. But that's the point. Some classes if you really want to learn, the instructor and the class are just subpar. But if you really want to learn, Popa will make you learn. Fantastic class. Thank you Professor Popa.
The second half of this year–long course on complex algebraic geometry was just as legendary as the first. We started off with the language of line bundles and divisors, moved to the really monumental Kodaira vanishing and embedding theorems, did a lot of concrete stuff with curves and surfaces, moved on to deformation theory, and finally capped everything off with an amazing discussion of local systems and the classical Riemann–Hilbert correspondence.

The homeworks were a lot harder than in the first semester (just because the material got a lot more advanced and a lot more "standard grad alg geo Hartshorney knowledge" was implicitly required), but were still very instructive and complemented the lectures very well. Overall, if this version of the course is ever offered again, I would whole–heartedly recommend it!
The complex–analytical viewpoint is very interesting and you should definitely try to take this iteration of the class if Prof. Popa teaches it again.
