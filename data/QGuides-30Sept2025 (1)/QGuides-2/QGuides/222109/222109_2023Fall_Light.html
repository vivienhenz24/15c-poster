This class is amazing and a really excellent introduction to WGS courses and WGS faculty. Professor Light is amazing––she's slightly intimidating because she's incredible, but she's also an incredibly sweet woman who makes an effort to get to know her students and an excellent instructor. The reading materials for this course are really interesting and there's a lot of variety, which keeps everything interesting. A very woman– and queer–friendly space, which I really really appreciated coming into my first semester at college. Highly recommend!!!
Take this class if you are looking for a fun and interesting seminar on weddings and US history. This covers ALL of the bases and Prof Light is super passionate about the subject. The readings are dense, but interesting and you get to watch reality TV shows and modern info too! The assignments are manageable and discussions interesting. Would recommend
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! Profess Light is amazing and the material is absolutely mind blowing and you'll come out of this class with wisdom not just about weddings but also thoroughly applicable to other academic and personal settings.
This course is super fun and interesting. You learn a lot about weddings and the history of wedding traditions. This course is 3 hours though which can be incredibly tiresome.
Dr. Light is genuinely a joy to be around (you should go and visit her in the WGS offices in Boylston!). However, the class will be difficult to manage if you struggle with reading large amounts of writing and need to pull all–nighters for other classes. Also, that final paper will seem daunting, especially because it's finals season and you have a dozen other things to also focus on, but try to relax and just write. Even a little bit a day goes a long way.
TAKE. THIS. CLASS!!! I cannot recommend it enough. I learned so much about the intersections of gender, race, class, consumerism, and sexuality in this class. It covers a ton of content and is super insightful. The most difficult part is the readings. If you want to get the most out of this class you need to do the readings and prepare for discussions, and that is time–consuming. Most of the readings are alright, but some of them are very long or dense. Once you do the readings, though, the rest of the class is pretty easy. The discussions are largely student–led and you will learn a ton from your peers. There are very few assignments; outside of one assignment at the beginning of the semester, the midterm, and the final, it is just discussion boards. You only need to do 8 discussion boards for the class (though you may find yourself voluntarily doing more) and they are short, informal, and open–ended, so there is very little pressure associated with them. Professor Light is incredibly intelligent and sweet, and she also brings food for students every week! You will not regret taking this class, it is so interesting and it has changed the way I think about everything, I regularly find myself analyzing art with tools I gained in this class or bringing up insights from class in discussions with friends because it is just THAT relevant.
Participate! You really only get out of it what you put in, especially as far as utilizing the professor herself and understanding topics discussed.
