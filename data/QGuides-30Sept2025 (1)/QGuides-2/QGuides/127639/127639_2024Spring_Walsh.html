ES 227 is very difficult, but by the end you will be a changed engineer and have a serious project to show for all your hard work throughout the semester. It is 100% worth the effort and hard work. However, don't come into this class expecting to have an easy time or to be able to coast along off your groupmates' contributions–– everyone needs to put in the work to contribute to complete the assignments and the project throughout the semester. It was really great to be able to meet with the teaching staff as a team each week–– I felt like I got to know the professors + TFs individually, which is not often an experience you get in engineering courses. While the projects briefs given at the beginning of the semester may not match your individual interests, by the end of the semester you will become an expert in that niche sub–field. Also, you get to collaborate with clinicians who are actually working in the field, so you are treated as a real engineer, not a college student. Even though it was a difficult semester, I am very happy with my decision to take ES 227 over ES 96.
This class is so brutal. I easily spent 20+ hours every week, and 80+ the last week of the course. You are expected to put in a ridiculously high amount of work, and the frustrating part is that it often doesn't feel like the work is paying off, neither in concrete success nor in staff acknowledgement. Your experience likely depends a lot on the team/course staff you are assigned to, so my experience does not speak for everyone, but there is little doubt that this will be the most time–consuming and stressful class in your schedule. Expect to feel extremely discouraged and demoralized at times. You will learn a ton about medical device development and hands–on prototyping techniques, which feels rewarding and useful. I had a great experience working with my team and am proud of the progress we made and everything we learned, but this came at the cost of a lot of other things in my life and so I'm not sure it was worth it.
I entered this class with an open mind, truly wanting to love it. ES 227 is known to be difficult and time–consuming, but I was hoping that it would be worth the learning experience, as I had heard from some peers. However, I find myself more frustrated and disappointed at the end of this semester. While I am incredibly proud of the final product my team produced and had a positive experience with my team, our meetings with the teaching staff were, week after week, demoralizing. This is certainly not true of every teaching staff member (in fact, other teams in the class had a very good experience with their teaching staff), but my teams' meetings with our TF wore us down enough that by the end of the semester, it was getting difficult to believe in and be proud of our own work. Our TF seemed to have expectations on how the device should end up looking and working, and when we deviated from that in our own designs and thinking, we were put down; it felt as though we were never listened to during meetings, only bulldozed over. This environment became increasingly difficult to work under as the semester went on, and it was apparent that it took a toll on the entire team. Looking back, I truly did learn so much about engineering design and teamwork, but I'm not sure that it was worth constant exhaustion and anxiety; it's a decision that I believe future students must make for themselves, depending on the teaching staff.
I would advise future students of the actual time commitment required of this class in order to meet expectations, with the qualification that they will get a lot out of this course in terms of some technical but many non–technical skills.
Be ready to commit for spending time in this class, you need to be hands–on and on top of the class every week
This class is better than ES96, so if you have to take one, take this one. However, be prepared to put in the work. Do not procrastinate, and try to frontload any work on the project. The last few week can be super stressful if you do not keep up with the work.

I think this course was a ton of work but an amazing experience. It really does consume your whole life for the semester, but in the end I thought it was worth it 100%.
I do not regret taking this class, but it absorbed all of my time this semester. There were weeks where my entire team was at the SEC for entire days leaving early in the morning to return later that same morning. The project will not always work and will cause a huge amount of stress and sorrow, but it will be great preparation for your thesis and the project you will do will be significant. The feedback and guidance you get is very dependent on who is TFing the course and if you get a bad one, you will be miserable much of the semester. You will get very close to your team and the rest of the class, which will be a very good bonding experience albeit one based in exhaustion, stress, and often misery. Your social life will shrink and you will see your bed a lot less than is healthy due to the extreme workload, but at the end of it, you will have completed a huge, interesting, and significant project, and will be more than prepared for your thesis.
This course is extremely intense – it is not a regular course. Expect to put in two or three courses' worth of effort, especially during the last few weeks of the course when you're preparing for the final presentation and report. The course staff will do their best to keep you on track, but the expectations are extremely high. Get ahead on things as often as you can.
In exchange for all the work you will do, you will leave the course with a deep understanding of how to actually design and successfully implement a medical device, better than any other students at this school. No other course's hands–on component compares to the experience gained from the semester–long project involved with this course.
This class is awesome, but a LOT of work. If you're passionate about medical devices and go in with the expectation of doing a lot of work, I think you'll love this class. There's a lot of work that needs to be done each week for the assignments, and the pace of the class is very very fast. That said, I really enjoyed the class and think it will be the most useful class that I'd have taken in college. I also think that this course is great preparation for ES 100, as the pace and workload are much more representative of the work of a thesis as opposed to ES 96
