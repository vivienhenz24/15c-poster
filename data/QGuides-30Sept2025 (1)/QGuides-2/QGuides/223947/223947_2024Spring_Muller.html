TAKE THIS CLASS!!! Not only is it very easy workwise (only a reading response each week, an outline for the midterm, and a final essay of your choice on incarceration), but the classes (once a week) are discussion–based, so it's driven by student perspectives. The 2–hour meetings go by so fast with all of the great conversations. Christopher is a great mediator and guide in these class discussions and is so knowledgeable about the class. Definitely take it if you're considering, even if you don't know much about criminal justice (I didn't).
Take this class! If you have any interest in criminal justice reform, you will love this class.
Highly recommend! The workload was pretty light– weekly reading response and a final paper that builds throughout the semester. Professor Muller is very kind and understanding. Each class was strictly discussion, based on 3 questions he'd generate from the readings. He does read everyone's reading responses in detail and I walked away with new perspectives on incarceration. The readings were interesting and fairly reasonable in length, except for a few. I learned so much about the history of mass incarceration, its consequences, and paths to reform. I always looked forward to class and I hope he gets to teach it again next year.
If you have ANY remote interest in mass incarceration, race/class in America, inequality, prison abolition, punishment, or any related themes, THIS IS THE COURSE TO TAKE. It provides a robust introduction to America's mass incarceration crisis, basically exposing students to every influential scholar in the field along with many other helpful and eye–opening additions. This course is a treasure. The readings are very interesting and not too overwhelming if you have a couple of hours free each weekend to sit down and do them. The workload is super manageable. The assignments are relatively easy to fit into a busy schedule––and for the level of in–depth insight it provides on mass incarceration, enrolling in this course is an amazing investment of (not too much!) time. TAKE THIS COURSE!!!!! You won't regret it.
This is a great class if you really want to be able to walk away with knowledge. The assignments and class structure force you to learn. You learn a wide range of information about why we incarcerate so many people in America and what we could do about it. Each week, one must write a reading response, forcing one to do the reading. Then, the class is 90% regurgitating this reading response and hearing from everyone else what you missed in each reading. This results in everyone's very, very, strong understanding of the readings and facts. However, if one is interested more in analyzing such facts and having discussion and debate, this would be the wrong class. It is not geared toward analyzing or debating or even discussing, it is simply geared to providing everyone the most complete understanding of all of the readings assigned. I found that this method enabled me to walk away with a significant amount of ready–to–go knowledge, and I would now feel prepared for a more analytical and discussion oriented class. However, I was disappointed at the lack of analysis. Given that everyone had to do a reading response, one could have structured the class to be analytical and discussion oriented, instead of basically regurgitating reading responses within the actual class, too..
Professor Muller is a rockstar in the space and this class is absolutely worth taking. You'll learn more about the state of incarceration of America today than you would ever imagine and this will transform your views on imprisonment.
Absolutely take this class. Professor Muller is incredible: he knows everything there is to know about this topic, and is a skilled and kind discussion leader. The readings are manageable and consistently fascinating, and the assignments aren't too bad at all.
I would tell students to take this class but know that there can be quite a bit of reading some weeks. However, the readings are very interesting and the content of the course is extremely rich.
