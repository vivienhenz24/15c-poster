191 is super rewarding!! It's great in the fall especially –– the course staff are fantastic. I learned so much, but it is a lot of work. It's a lot of time in the lab and also out of the lab when papers are due. Also, make sure you take it with a lab partner you know you work well with, because you spend a lot of time together.
This is a great class, even if it is quite a lot of work. Be prepared that you will have to put in a lot of time outside of class during the paper writing weeks.

The class is very rewarding, it teaches you a lot about how actual scientific experiments work.
It's a great class to delve into experiments that are on the scale of actual research projects. It is a lot of work even outside the 8 hours of lab every week though.
This was a very informative class for me, especially in confirming that I wanted experiment to be a part of my future physics experience. Keep in mind that the professors listed in my.harvard are primarily there to help you with writing the papers and data analysis, while the lab staff (shoutout Joe and Jieping!) will be the main ones to help you through the actual experiments. While 4 hours on each of Tue and Thu seems like a lot on paper, it ends up being very self–paced and not rushed at all. Above all, communicate a lot with your lab partner(s), and start the papers early!
Take this course if you want to go in experimental physics. Take this class in Fall! Choose good lab partners. If you are reading this and did not think of a partner, then start searching now. The quality of lab partner/s significantly affect the quality of the experience. The class teaches you a lot. Easy and hard experiments are determined based on how hard the experimental part is, an easy experiment can give you a very hard time in your data analysis. Try taking experiments from different fields and try to do experiments you are unfamiliar with. It is a low risk environment to explore new things. Also listen to the seemingly random advice course staff gives. Kathryn teaches important stuff about electronics. Although not mentioned in the course description, familiarity with electronics and data analysis is extremely helpful. Senior fall is the ideal time take this class.
I think this is a class that every physics concentrator should take. It won't always be fun but if you want to go into experiment it will give you a sense of what it's like, and if you want to go into theory it will give you a sense of what the other side does.
It is a class like no other. You will work hard, but the rewards are huge. You probably have to take it, but I promise if you have a good attitude and really immerse in the experimentation, you will have a good time and learn A LOT. Take it in the fall if you can.
