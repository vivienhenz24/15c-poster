This class was a fulfilling challenge for me! I learned a lot and found joy in trying to solve hard problems with my classmates. Office hours were incredibly helpful in clarifying hard concepts that were presented in lecture.
I took this course without taking Chem 40. It is hard, but doable. Make sure to always go to office hours, utilize the amazing resources that is the TFs and Prof Nocera, and make sure to do understand all of the pset problems really well before each test. I made the mistake of not going through each pset problem fully myself before the first test, whereas I did do that for the second and third tests, and my exam grades reflected that.
Good inorganic chemistry class, reinforces what you learned in Chem 40 but with more depth and background. Dan is a very good lecturer
Chem 154 was super interesting. I always enjoyed lectures with Nocera (and particularly appreciated his tangents). I did take this class without taking Chem 40 previously, and that certainly made the material a bit more challenging, but the course definitely provided enough background that I never felt totally lost. I definitely recommend taking this class, though I'm not sure if I'd recommend taking it without previous inorganic chemistry experience.
I came for inorganic chemistry, I stayed for Dan
TAKE THIS CLASS! Prof. Nocera is truly one of a kind, and the class is great too! I learned a lot about what I did not know and took for granted in previous classes. Gives a wonderful foundation for other inorganic chemistry classes! Also, Sam and Brandon are some of the best TFs I have had!
Dan is a fun teacherhe tells many stories during class, and approaches class with a fun attitude. The TFs are what make this course truly special, however, and Sam and Brandon did a great job handling Dan, prompt grading, and making the problem sets fair. Chem 40 is not strictly required, but if you are trying to take 154 without taking chem 40, you should spend some time learning group theory and some of the material before the first and second examsthey were easy for anybody who had taken 40, but very challenging for those who had not.
Definitely take this class! The lectures are well–delivered (but take time to read up on some of the math outside of class), Dan has fun stories, and you'll gain an appreciation for parts of chemistry that you had just assumed prior to this class.
The difficulty of this class will heavily depend on your background. Any undergrad course in inorganic chemistry will make this class very interesting while still being very manageable. The course is constructed in a way that actually no prior experience is needed. However, many more hours will need to be put in to grasp the concepts and get practice on how to approach problems. Prof. Nocera is a fun lecturer and incredibly knowledgeable about anything connected to the course material, which makes the course so interesting.
extremely well–designed but also challenging course – highly recommend to anyone interested in inorganic/group theory/spectroscopy, or anyone doing research adjacent to these fields
The course material is great, but the lecture notes don't seem to really get into what you see on the psets which can be a little lengthy. However, office hours with Sam and Brandon are very, very helpful for learning the material, completing the psets, and preparing for the exams. The course has three midterms (no comprehensive final), all of which were fair and straightforward, but it was a little frustrating not having any practice midterms / expectations going into any of the exams (especially the first).
Pretty interesting content with relatively light workload. Finishing the problem sets took the most work, but there were only 5 graded problem sets this semester, and they were excellent preparation for the exams. While lecture wasn't sufficient to do the problem sets or exams, I found that additionally attending office hours was enough to learn the content in a reasonable amount of time.
Very useful course for a chemistry major, and well organized.
