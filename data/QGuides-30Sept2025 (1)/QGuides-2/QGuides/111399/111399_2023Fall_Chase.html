What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Take this course to meet Claire Chase in her element. Seriously. This course helped me understand what it means to meet a professor who is a leader in what they do, and who embodies that leadership with a passion that will perfuse the entire ensemble with the desire to find that same spirit. Prof. Chase will never doubt the ability of anyone in the ensemble, and work with them, uplift them, until they feel confident as a performer of New Music. It is rare to take a course with a professor who is so committed to teaching and performance and the same time. Do it to be inspired.
For the musician who wants to grow, expand their horizons and make new (and sometimes unexpected) connections, this course is a very special opportunity
This is one of the best classes I've taken at Harvard. It serves an important niche – courses in practical music–making are generally in short supply here, but Claire Chase is an incredible professor and and just the coolest person you can spend two hours a week with, and I really recommend taking whatever she offers in any given semester. The class will force you out of your comfort zone no matter what your musical background is, which I think is really important, and is also broadly accessible. It's not your traditional new music ensemble –– where you sit down and read knotty scores and count out quintuplets; the class is mostly an exploration of directed improvisation –– but I can't imagine that being a problem for a lot of people.
