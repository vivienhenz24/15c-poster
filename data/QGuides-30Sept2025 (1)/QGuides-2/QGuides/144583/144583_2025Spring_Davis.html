This is an incredible class. For starters, the teaching team is phenomenal. Chuck and Ryan are both amazing, so passionate about plants, care so much about the students, and are probably the best teaching team I have ever had at Harvard ( as a graduating senior). The trip to Miami is easily one of my favorite Harvard experiences and allows you to learn how to botanize in the field, and also allows you to get to know your peers. I loved everything about this class, from the trip to the lectures to lab. This class has been taught for a while, and you can definitely tell based on the awesome thought that has been put into the course content. It would be very helpful to either have OEB 53 Evolutionary Biology or LS1 b before you took this class ( some evolution terminology I only learned from those classes in depth). I took this class with OEB 52, and I would HIGHLY recommend taking both at the same time. I thought maybe I would be confused taking two plant classes at the same time, but they actually both helped inform each other! Truly a wonderful class that I couldn't recommend more.
Take this class if you like plants. The field trip is great and all but this class is amazing through and through. Prepare to commit to a serious workload. Chuck compares this class to a language course and he means it; the amount of memorization required to succeed in this course is immense. Be prepared to work hard, but also to take on of the best classes of your time in college.
