Since commitment to the spring component of this class is required in order to take the fall component (at least this year it was), I would recommend taking this course only if you have a strong interest in archaeology beyond fieldwork. Personally, I found the fieldwork from the fall really engaging, but the lab component in the spring was a bit more tedious (a lot of cataloguing). However, it was a great learning opportunity overall!
The fall portion of this class is fun since youre mostly outside in the field digging. The spring portion is extremely boring. Cataloguing is just a super dull time and it takes forever. Also the lectures are way longer than they need to be. It feels like the same content is covered 5 or 6 times throughout the course. Overall I would not recommend this class. Its only saving grace is that its a complete gem which requires almost no time during the week.
This class is great and I recommend you take it! Spending a quarter of your time digging in the dirt is a fun thing.
TAKE THIS CLASS if you are interested and passionate about archaeology. It is very unique and you learn sooooo much! The teachers are amazing too!
TAKE THIS COURSE!! There are so few times in your life that you will ever get an opportunity like this. It is a hands on environment that welcomes all comfort levels in anthro and archaeology. You get to know your classmates incredibly well throughout the year, making it a special learning environment. Trish and Diana are truly the best of Harvard and make this class feel more like a HYAP family.
Take this class! The class is super engaging and the professors and teaching staff are incredibly helpful. Whether or not you are an archaeology concentrator, this class is super engaging and you'll gain interesting information about 18th century artifacts.
