What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course is just incredible. If you're looking to get familiar with computational physics / quantum chemistry in any way, this is where you should get started. Course topics are very fascinating and the assignments are very focused on the practical application of the principles (you'll learn how to do all the code). The more background you have going into this course (QM, statmech, etc.) the more you'll get out of the lecture and theory.

Teaching staff is really smart and knowledgeable, as well as very good at debugging. However, the staff is super busy sometimes so help is definitely not guaranteed outside office hours.
This class provides an overview and foundations of fundamental computational techniques used to study and understand materials, and provides opportunities to apply the methods to understand them in practice and apply them to a problem of your choosing.
Overall, the class was great! Boris does a great job communicating a ton of cutting–edge ideas in lecture.

My main concern is support. There is no section, there was only one hour of office hours which were over zoom, and the Ed was not answered all that frequently.

If you are confident in your ability to solve problems yourself, you will really enjoy this class.
Both the instructor and TA are kind, understanding, and approachable people with genuine enthusiasm for their subject.
A survey course that requires lots of effort to get learning reward. The self–guided final project was a big highlight and a fantastic opportunity to learn organically with guidance along the way. Not particularly well organized. Best for students who are already researching with computational tools for physics and chemistry.
This is a really accessible and useful course for learning computational materials science and chemistry methods and learning how to apply them to research problems you may be interested in!
