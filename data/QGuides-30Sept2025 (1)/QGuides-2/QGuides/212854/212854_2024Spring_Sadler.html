This was the best GENED I've taken at Harvard BY A MILE. The course staff took an approach to science that inspired excitement every week. The PSETS took 2–3 hours each, and the final project at the end of the term was so much fun. GENED 1037 is one of those special classes where the work never felt like work. Phil, Marc, Max, and John poured hours of time into the class to make it as incredible as it was. Furthermore, our PSETS covered everything from physics to optics to chemistry to biology. . .I have never been passionate about biology, but after emerging from this class, I am sad that I have not studied it more. GENED 1037 was a game–changer in more ways than one. To prospective enrollees, best of luck in the lottery. . .this is a class that you have to take before you graduateregardless of what you study.
The psets often feel as though there are far too many reading questions, which don't contribute to understanding but take a while. Having to perform the experiments outside of classtime is annoying, as is both having a final and final project. Sections feel like a waste of time. Psets are poorly written with grammatical errors
This is the best class I have taken. It is a pset class, but you rarely spend many hours outside of class. Also, we ended up having no midterm, just psets, a final project (which was all done in class), and then a take–home, open book final... and you get to have BBQ with the amazing professor at his house. It is definitely the most fun and least stressful class I have taken, but it is still very rewarding, and you learn tons about experiments and scientists.
I would tell future students to remain in the course, despite the dense first few problem sets
This is a gem. It won't teach you all that much (except for some minor history facts about science that you will probably forget), and it's very similar to a high school course. That being said, you need to get rid of your gened science requirement somehow, and the practical approach of this course makes it a good candidate for that.
This is an excellent GenEd that values the importance of curiosity, the organic process of exploration and inquiry, and a foundational understanding of key scientific discoveries. Unlike the fast pace and endless cramming of information you experience in most STEM courses, this course is refreshing and allows you the space and encouragement to investigate something that captures your attention. It's liberating and reminded me as a STEM concentrator what I loved about science in the first place. Regardless of your concentration or background, the material is not technically very difficult. As long as you put in effort, keep a flexible mind, and engage with the material, this is a very fulfilling and supportive course. The instructors are very involved and dedicated to your learning and it was such a fun class to be a part of. Expect blowtorches, melting glass, and doing experiments outside which will certainly attract people to ask what you are doing (great opportunity to teach others what you're learning in class).
This course has A LOT of homework and reading for just a GENED. Although there is no midterm and the final is a take–home test, it is a high–time commitment and seems not worth it at times. The teaching staff is great, very happy and interactive people, just the work outside of the class makes it annoying at times.
You should take this –– it's the platonic ideal of a Gened
This class rocks!!! One of the best geneds at Harvard for sure. Take this class if you can!!!!

This course was a lot of fun! Some weeks are more time–consuming than others but besides that Phil was a great professor and it was cool
Great course to fulfill your science gened!! It is actually a little bit of work (psets and readings), but honestly I thought it was very worth. Max and Phil are the best
This class is really fun––great GenEd. The teaching team is awesome. You will need to do a PSet each week which can get a little tedious, but they are not hard, just a tad lengthy at times. You need to be in class to get materials for the experiments and PSets. I would definitely recommend this course––you will have fun and you will learn some cool stuff.
TAKE IT!!! This class is just like the Magic School bus. The professor teaches us a new experiment every week, and he even wears the appropriate ties (ex. with prisms). He is so enthusiastic about his job, and he cares a lot about his students. All the TFs are amazing as well, and they care about building relationships with all the students. The first reading might be long, BUT DO NOT DROP OUT. It is the only long reading, the rest are about 3–4 pages on average. The readings are fun and the problem sets are not very long. If you like drawing, you will also like this class very much. There is a lot of applied learning as well, and we build a lot of our experiments in class with the help of our professors. This class ended up being my favorite class of my Sophomore year, so if you are thinking about it, do yourself a favor and take it!

This is a great class! It's a lot of fun to do the experiments, even if sometimes it feels a little rote/childish. I think the main thing I took away from this class is that you can have fun and do cool experiments even without super fancy materials, so that was a lot of fun to experience again.

While I do not think it is too difficult to do well in this class, you will have to be somewhat diligent in completing the PSets, which can be tedious at times.
