Pretty good for an HSI gened. There's a decent amount of reading depending on what background you're coming from (>60 pages per week), but sometimes you can get by with just skimming / focusing on introductions, headings, and conclusions. Sections focus on discussing the readings, and lecture is more about covering historical events. This class is kind of like AP US History: Religion Edition, but still interesting because it generally assumes knowledge of US history and instead focuses on developments in religious communities over time and how those play into the larger history of the US. Will teach you to notice, understand, and analyze instances of religion in political and public speech, which is a valuable skill everyone should have. The course is very Christianity–centric because it focuses on the US, but there is also discussion of Jewish and (somewhat more rarely) Muslim communities. Assignments aren't too bad; there's one 400–500 word response every few weeks, one 1500–2000 word paper toward the end of the semester, and in–class handwritten midterm and final exams that are a combo of term definition and short answer questions.
