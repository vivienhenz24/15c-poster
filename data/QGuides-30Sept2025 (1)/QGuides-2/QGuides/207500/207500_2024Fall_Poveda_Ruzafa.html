It was overall just great and will impact my thinking in a permanent way (for the better) and I anticipate taking more courses in this vein, and also I anticipate that this will change the trajectory of some key decisions in my research program as well!

Take this class if you are looking for completely different math to anything you have seen!
This class is the easiest math class I've taken at Harvard. The only assignments were four problem sets, all of which were relatively short and easy, and all of which could be resubmitted with corrections. Alejandro is a lovely person and clearly enjoys teaching. The one drawback is that you cover very little material. I was hoping to gain a sense of what formal logic looks like, but we moved at a snail's pace and I felt I learned very little. Take if you're looking for a math gem; avoid if you want a rigorous introduction to formal logic.
Alejandro is goated. This was a huge learning curve as a philosophy concentrator with very little proof background (I took phil 4). Although I lost at some points, by the end of the class, I could grasp what was going on, which was crazy to me because I didn't think I'd be able to understand such a difficult topic. I made great friends in the class and would take it 10x over if Alejandro teaches it. I cannot emphasize enough how goated he is.

Considering his office hours were popping (and how amazing of a professor he is), math department, please get Alejandro an office in the Math Lounge.
This class was incredible! Alejandro was easily the best math instructor I have had at Harvard, so much so that he is making me ponder enrolling in math classes as electives. He generates lots of enthusiasm for the subject matter, gives very clear lectures that supplement the book nicely, and is super supportive and accessible beyond the lecture times. The content was so interesting, and gave me a lot to think about in terms of the logic that underpins math!
This course is very much a "get what you put in" kind of course. The content is interesting, but the mode of presentation can sometimes be overwhelming (a stream of continuous proofs with little break). However, Alejandro and the course staff are so excited to help you learn and improve, and the course materials and problems are well–made, so if you choose to engage you will have a much better time. I imagine that this course will continue to improve as Alejandro teaches it more in future years, and gains more practice with choosing which parts of the curriculum are worth providing depth to and which parts can be more hand–waived. At some points this course was frustrating, and at some points it was quite interesting. Given how passionate and energetic Alejandro is, I imagine the balance of those too will shift more toward interesting year on year.
Take this class, it was difficult but rewarding.
