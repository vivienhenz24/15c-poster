Not really an introductory class and the problem sets take long. Graduate students usually start the assignments late, so it is hard to work with them
Very comprehensive introductory course
This was an excellent introduction to the field of quantum computing. Building up quickly from fundamental concepts (qubits, density matrices, quantum operations), you cover a pretty solid range of topics –– entanglement, quantum algorithms, quantum error correction, hardware implementations, etc). I feel like it's a good enough introduction for me to be able to get a reasonable understanding of current research by reading on my own! Would recommend if you're interested in quantum computing
A wonderful course on quantum information theory. The material is amazing and super interesting. Highly recommend. The every–other–week pset gives you adequate time to devote to actually understand the material, and the lack of exams is very comforting. That's not to say that this class isn't difficult; it very much is. The psets are often quite time–consuming and require a genuine understanding of the material to solve, but they are written well and really make you think more about the content. Nazli and Andrew, the teaching staff, are also wonderful people and very knowledgeable. Lukin's lectures are also fairly understandable. All in all, a great course that you should definitely take if you are at all interested in quantum information theory or computing.
This class is really hard, but very interesting and completely worth it. Prof. Lukin is a great professor who is clearly very excited about the subject matter and happy to talk with students. Would recommend to anyone interesting in learning about the fundamentals of quantum info.
This course is great
If your research is in quantum information it is a comprehensive course to see how quantum computing works from the ground up. However, it isn't likely to directly help with your research. If you decide to take the class, definitely find a study group asap because working on your own is a bad idea for this course.

Take it with misha. The material is really interesting and he is a good teacher. During class he is really funny, and he is one of the most important people in the field so it is an amazing experience to take it under him. Regarding workload, I think it is very manageable. The homeworks were long, but never unreasonably hard (they were long but each subpart was reasonably easy). Overall, a good experience and you learn a really cool topic in my opinion.
