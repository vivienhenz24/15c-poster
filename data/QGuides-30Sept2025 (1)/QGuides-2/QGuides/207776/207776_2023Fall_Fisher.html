What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I would highly recommend this course to pre–meds or bio concentrators who have an interest in dermatology or medicine. Dr. Fisher is a great lecturer and has a wide range of experience in the field that allows him to provide feedback and content that covers all areas of the field. There are weekly presentations starting after the first few weeks of the course, and each student is assigned a topic every other week to give a 5–10 minute presentation on. These topics are very easy, and range from reading a wikipedia article to a research paper on a skin issue, but typically only took me an 1 to an 1 1/2 to complete.
I would tell them that not only is it incredibly interesting, but that the professor is amazing and incredibly knowledgeable. His passion for his work and for what he teaches makes a world of a difference and made me as a student excited in turn.
It's a really cute and amazing class. Dr. Fisher is such a great person.
I would tell future students that this freshmen seminar is very useful if you are interested in pre–med or specifically dermatology. You will meet many other pre–meds in the class and Dr. Fisher is very knowledgable. However, instead of directly teaching the class he mostly sits in while students give weekly presentations on different skin topics. I didn't find this instruction method the most effective or engaging and think a more interactive class would have been better.
Dr. Fisher is SO COOL! Casually dropping the biggest information bombs on the class about his world–renowned research. Such a cool experience!
Dr. Fisher is such a wonderful instructor, and always has thoughtful and extensive input as we complete presentations for the class. The workload is quite manageable, and I found myself enjoying everything I worked on!
