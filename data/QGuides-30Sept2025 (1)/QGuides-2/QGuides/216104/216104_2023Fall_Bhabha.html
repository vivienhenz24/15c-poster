What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Wonderful class! The class was composed of reading one book/week, then a class discussion on the book. We opened each class with a 10 minute presentation by one of the students, then transitioned into a class discussion. This class was very enjoyable and insightful, and I feel like it helped me learn how to read "better," in a more critical style. The discussions often transcended content in the books alone, which was nice and allowed us to connect the books to other intersecting topics.
If you're interested in learning about different people's cultures and the racial dynamics in America, this course is perfect for you. Most of the books covered relate to these topics! Also, this class is VERY centered on discussion. It's all about sharing your perspective, but taking in others as well. It felt very much like a highly–academic book group!
This class is fantastic!! It requires a lot of reading and some textual analysis, but overall, the depth of our class discussions was worth the outside work. Professor Bhabha creates a caring classroom atmosphere that discusses societal and racial issues with sensitivity and thoughtfulness. I highly recommend this course for all first–years looking for a meaningful seminar that pairs together iconic literature (novels by Toni Morrison, Virginia Woolf, James Baldwin to name a few) with conversations around facets of identity. This is a gentle and cozy class that hits at heavy topics in a purposeful way, and I cannot stress how grateful I am that I stuck with it!
It's a lot of reading, and the discussions are honestly quite surface–level. Instead of building off one another, we should just go in a circle and say something. We would talk about the same topics over and over again and by week 5, it's a bit boring. Also, it's sometimes hard to understand what Prof Bhabha is saying sometimes.
Come to class prepared with a lot of questions or comments about the reading. Sometimes, the text is indeed very difficult to get through and understand, but I guarantee you that doing your best to process will make class discussions so much more interesting and thought–provoking. You really do get out of this seminar what you put in.
