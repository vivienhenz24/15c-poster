What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Professor McCormick is an amazing guide as you navigate the intricacies of Late Antique and Early Medieval History. He begins the course with a history of the seminar method, and builds up the class as a community of researchers. The class really becomes an opportunity to build interdisciplinary skills, apply them to a topic of personal interest, and workshop a publication quality paper over the course of the semester. You will learn a great deal about the period, acquire new concrete skills in philology and archaeology, and have great fun along the way. I highly recommend the course to any graduate student with even a tangential interest in the period.
I would highly recommend the seminar. Professor McCormick paid equal attention to technical and professional skills, provided attentive feedback to student drafts, and successfully created a collegial and collaborative intellectual environment. The pacing and demands of the seminar provided the space for students to actually explore and focus on their research once topics were decided upon.
If you are interested in Late Antiquity, or in learning how to synthesize a variety of sources with care and discernment, or love Latin, this course is for you! I began the class unfamiliar with the time period and approaches under study and I leave it with a much greater understanding not just of sixth–century Italy but also of the relationship between philology and history, and the evolution of the Latin language.
I would highly recommend it to grad students with a background in Latin!
