Her lectures are always interesting and engaging, and she is constantly open to student feedback. The readings of this course are great –– even if you don't love every single one, the conversations about the readings will make it worthwhile. The class environment is also very supporting and welcoming, which just makes the class even better. At a school where there aren't very many environmental humanities classes, this course is a gift. I wish I could take it again.

I loved this class and definitely would recommend it to anyone in English, as well as anyone else who is thinking about taking an English class or who is interested in climate change.

Prof Dimick is a wonderful teacher and clearly put so much thought and love into this course! We covered many interesting questions about the genre of climate fiction, and read many different types of works from poetry to novel to short story. Note that this class is less a lecture course and more of a seminar/tutorial style, with many small group discussions and participation encouraged. If thats something youre interested in, overall a highly recommended class!
I'm a senior and this is my first English course at Harvard ––– I LOVED this course. Super non–stressful. The pace of reading was extremely manageable, and Professor Dimick is just about the most kind and welcoming course facilitator you could ask for. Our class cohort was made up of a lot of really wonderful people from a lot of different backgrounds, which made for really interesting in–class discussions. The reading choices weren't what you would expect for a "literature" class since climate change literature is all very modern and undefined, but that meant I was introduced to a lot of new and unconventional books that I wouldn't have been otherwise. If you take this course, make sure you conference early on with Professor Dimick about your essay ideas –– she helped expedite my planning process a lot and gave me a lot more confidence in what I was writing!
TAKE IT. This is not a class just for people who care intensely about the climate crisis (it's obviously something we should all bear in mind, but I don't spend everyday thinking about it the way some people do and I wouldn't want someone who feels similarly to be scared off from taking the course). Prof Dimick is wonderful and so enthusiastic about what she teaches and clearly knows a lot about the content. The readings are engaging and there's something for everyone's reading tastes. This is probably one of if not my favorite classes I've taken at Harvard and cannot recommend it enough.
