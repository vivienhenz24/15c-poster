This is a very good course. You continue to develop your Spanish skills in a good environment. Good next step from Spanish 20 and manageable.
This is not an extremely difficult course as long as you take the time to learn the grammar concepts as they are taught. The assignments are all very manageable and fairly graded, especially after taking Spanish 20.
It’s a great class if you’re looking to engage more deeply with Spanish and learn more about the culture in tandem. It really helps with creating a strong foundation in your conversational Spanish, as well as changes the way that you interact with Spanish materials and media.
If you just tested into this course, please take it! Do not be afraid of taking a language course that may seem above your level, because I promise you will receive so much support from the TF and your classmates that it is doable. The smaller sections allow you to really and truly converse in Spanish––not just basic statements, but actual conversations about the world.
This class was a great reintroduction to spanish after not taking any classes since high school. It was fast paced and I felt like I was always learning new things without being overwhelmed. I thought the 4 countries setup was great and not only expanded my language abilities but also my cultural knowledge. It is not too much work but is still very informative. The attendance policy is quite strict, but other than that, as long as you show up the class should not be difficult. It is structured pretty similarly to a high school class with more tests and smaller assignments so it’s more consistent work but also does not have a final or a midterm!
