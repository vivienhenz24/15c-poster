Highly recommend super engaging content, there is a lot of reading 5–6 science papers each week but thats how you learn
This class is very good. The workload is heavy week to week (ie: 5–7 primary literature research papers) and a potential pop quiz, so be aware that you need to set out ample time to read these papers and thoughtfully engage with them. The final is a really cool way to engage in any topic of your choice and I was very curious to dive into mine! As an undergraduate or graduate student, this class is worth it for the methodology and discussion on addiction.
This is a great neuro elective. There are weekly readings that are manageable (I don't like to read but I found these interesting and understandable and well explained in class).
It's a great course. Brian is an engaging lecturer when he goes on his introductory monologues at the start of class. If you do not have any prior coursework on addiction, your views on what it means for someone to have a substance use disorder are likely to be challenged in a meaningful way
The course was overall really great. Make sure you know the materials big ideas because the quiz questions can be a little difficult. Also if you need to hype yourself up to speak, come up with at least one thing to talk about per article because participation os half of your grade.
