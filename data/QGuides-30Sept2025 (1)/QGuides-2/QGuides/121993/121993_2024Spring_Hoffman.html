What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
One of the COOLEST classes I've taken. EVER.
This course can take up a lot of your time, but your time and efforts in this course are definitely rewarding to shape you as a young physicist. I recommend everyone taking this course to prepare to put solid 10~15 hours a week for this course outside the lab sessions, but I can guarantee that you will learn amazingly if you do so.
Physics 191 may seem hard to fit into your schedule because it takes so much time, but I think it is one of the most worthwhile classes in the department. You will be introduced to so many new ideas, and trying out experiments in a few different subfields can help inform what you might want to work on if you want to do a PhD in physics. Even if you've taken the most advanced grad classes, I still think there's something to learn for everyone.
This class can be very frustrating but also very rewarding. You will definitely learn a lot about doing experimental physics, writing papers, and teamwork. Make sure to pick your lab partner well! You will spend a lot of time with you lab partner (8 hours in lab + extra hours outside of class when you write outlines/papers). Lastly, the teaching staff is awesome –– especially Joe.
Go in with a partner who you can work well with, complements your skillset, and you enjoy talking to!
This class is definitely one of the best classes I have taken at Harvard. Throughout the semester, you will design and conduct three different experiments allowing you to explore various branches of physics. There is no better motivation to understand new physics than to see it (quite literally) with your own eyes. The lab staff is also immensely helpful in setting up the experiments so definitely do talk to them early on. You will require their help to get started, but once you get past that initial point you will have a lot of freedom in designing the experiment that you want to do, especially as you grow familiar with the lab equipment. Finally, while writing reports takes time, the benefits of learning a proper framework for doing so is incredibly helpful beyond this course and this is a great chance to practice it.
The course provides good help for improving scientific writing skills. Be careful when choosing experiments. Some of them are harder than the others. Make sure to leave enough time to write and polish the papers.
This class was a lot of work. At times, it didn't really feel like it, but looking back, I spent a huge amount of time on this course.
This is a great class for learning things you don't get anywhere else in the Harvard physics curriculum. You get to learn how to use lab equipment for a bunch of really neat experiments, and honestly it's pretty fun. I would strongly recommend it to other physics concentrators, although that's probably not that relevant since you have to take it anyways for honors. That said, even if you don't need this class, I think it teaches really valuable lab and writing skills.

Now, the warnings... You need a good lab partner, and not necessarily your best friend–––you need someone who will do work. Also, it is a big time commitment. Tu/Th, 1:30 to 5:30, but often you need to stay until 6 to finish a measurement, not to mention the writing and planning you need to do outside of class. Don't expect to get good grades on the first try, either; you will have to do revisions for all of your papers to fix even very minor things to get an A. Is this annoying? Yes. Does it take a lot of time? Sometimes. Is it reasonable? Yes–––it makes you a better writer. The point is, some weeks you will probably be spending around 20 hours working on this class. With an awesome lab partner, maybe less. With a crappy lab partner, definitely more.

Go in excited about the experiments. It's a really fun, really useful, and really unique class in the physics department. Good luck!
