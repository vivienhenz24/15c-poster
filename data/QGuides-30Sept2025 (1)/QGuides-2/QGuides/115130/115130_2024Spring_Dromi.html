I would recommend people see this class as an opportunity to familiarize themself with the department and acquaint themselves with traditional theory! Obviously if you are planning to take this course, you are already planning to be a Sociology concentrator –– complete the readings/discussion posts, attend section (which are even more important than lecture!), and ask for support from your TF (they are all veterans of this course!) Although the class work load is marketed as a time commitment, it is very manageable because the extensive readings are balanced by a relaxed assignment schedule. Only warning, this class also discusses race/racism, so if you're BIPOC it might feel already familiar/redundant!
AWESOME CLASS !!!!!!!!!!! can't recommend enough. sociological theory can sound daunting, but the engaging lecture and sections made it so fun, constantly relating these sometimes convoluted concepts to relevant, modern examples and media. Such a cool class, I enjoyed so much each week. Really enjoyed visits to museums etc. which happened almost every other week. readings cannnn be a little dense/long but overall not too bad.
Do the readings. I really liked this class, and section is a great way to engage with the course content and hear from different perspectives of students.
This class is wonderful! Teaches the fundamentals of sociology and focuses on the sociological cannon and how one progresses beyond that. The teaching staff is really kind and caring towards their students. The readings could be a bit difficult and hard to grasp but just give yourself time and look for external resources!
It's a solid course, 7/10. The readings can and will take longer than you expectthe material is dense, and careful, detailed reading is often important for understanding. If I could change one thing about how I approached the course, I would read everything twice instead of once. Readings are dense enough that it is possible to bounce off them completely and take basically nothing away, but they are all interesting enough to be worth putting in the effort to engage with seriously.
TAKE IT TAKE IT TAKE IT (and pray you get Zehra as your TF)
There are so many readings and the discussion posts are graded super strictly. I highly recommend you work closely with your TF and reach out to the professor with guidance on how to write a discussion post. The discussion posts are not for class participation, they are a big part of your class and grade, do not start them the day they are due, start them early as they take quite a bit of time and effort.

Overall an interesting course that teaches you a lot and the teaching team works hard to teach you well.
If you're concentrating in Sociology it's a requirement anyway––but my experience with Soc 97 was honestly a positive one. I really appreciated Shai's focus on media analysis. It felt kind of unconventional for a theory class, but as a media nerd like myself, I loved having the opportunity to apply what I was learning to pop culture (which I do in my spare time anyway)!!! We watched Black Mirror and The Devil Wears Prada in class. And I LOVEEEEE MY TF ZEHRA!!! SHE IS THE COOLEST. The majority of your real theory break–down happens in section, and I feel so lucky to have been in hers. She was great at simplifying concepts and super patient with all of our questions.
This course is really interesting and teaches you a lot about social theory. The assignments are very doable, and the teaching staff is amazing. Zehra was a great section leader, so if you get the chance to have her, do it. You will get to learn through different forms of media and have open discussions in class. The field trips are also very fun.
Honestly, I was surprised by how much I enjoyed this class. I think all of the teaching staff is great at helping break down difficult concepts
