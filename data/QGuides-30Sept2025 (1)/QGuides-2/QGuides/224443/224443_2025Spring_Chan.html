What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course was really a mixed bag for me. Samuel was a really nice professor and the feedback he gave me on every assignment was always careful, detailed, and helpful for the final paper. Also, the guest speakers he brought in were really powerful and informative; specifically, we focused a good amount on the Tibetan and Hong Kong diasporas, who are really good case studies for the themes we discussed. However, I think our discussions in class were always a bit lackluster; maybe it was the morning time slot of the class, but I don't think a lot of us were engaged in the readings or in the discussion itself. I also think the themes of the class kept reverting back to the questions/debates surrounding "transnationalism," which personally did not compel me. Sadly Samuel is leaving Harvard, but he was a genuinely nice scholar and professor, and I hope that future iterations of this seminar may work better for others.
