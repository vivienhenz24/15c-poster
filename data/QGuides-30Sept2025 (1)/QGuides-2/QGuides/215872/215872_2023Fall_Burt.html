Be prepared to read.

On good days, you will be assigned around 3–6 comic issues to read, each taking me about 15–20 minutes to read, and perhaps you will send a "Question of the Day," detailing a question you had about your readings to the professor and the TA, proving you did the readings.

On bad days, you will be assigned a novel to read in a single weekend, the longest being around 300 pages, and you will have to send a Question of the Day.

In this class, you may be asked to do a roleplaying session with a group, in which you act out as a character you design, and hope the person narrating your story is creative enough to keep it interesting. Also, if your group is small, and half your group is absent, it makes it hard to tell the story. Still, I heard great stories being told when we shared these DND–esque stories, and they seem genuinely fun!

Attendance was mandatory. Not fun. They will count how many of you are in class, so don't think you can get by by having someone send you the link to the attendance. If you get caught multiple times, you will get talked to by the professor.

Essays are not too difficult. Can be up to 1500 words if I recall correctly. Just basic interpretation of comics that we've read.

We were given the choice between a final exam that was supposed to be easy, and a final project that required you to read 5 stories of some superhero. I chose the latter, personally because I did not do most of the readings.

Lectures are not very substantial in terms of understanding the readings, but can be interesting to watch what happens that day in class. Never a dull moment there.

Take the class if you like superheroes (if you don't know anything about them, you'll be totally fine), and if you will be fine with the readings. You will likely end up with an A, A–, or B+, but you better work on those essays to make sure they get As or A–s because you only get 2 and they make up a large percentage of your grade. Risk it if you want.
