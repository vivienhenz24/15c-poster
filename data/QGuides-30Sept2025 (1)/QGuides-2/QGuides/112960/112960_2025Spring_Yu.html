I think that when you look at a Q guide review, you should gauge how much your background matches that of the people who write these. For reference, I went into this class not having taken very many CS classes (only AP CS and CS 50 online), but I had learned about functional programming with Haskell in high school. I have more background in pure math and math competitions, and I briefly did coding competitions in high school.

Honestly, I think the content presented in this class is very solid and gives you a good overview of different programming paradigms. I think OCaml really lends itself well to this, and I would agree that the course is more about programming than it is about OCaml. However, I think that the way the content is presented, in the textbook or the (absence of) lectures, makes it hard to fully recommend this class. The flipped classroom format doesn't really work for me, and I feel that more content could be covered if lectures were spent discussing new concepts. The textbook contains all of the material needed, but it can also be unnecessarily dense at times, with the professor adding his own anecdotes and extra–complicated words (and I mean truly words that have no application to computer science or anything else) that make the course feel a little slow.

I took this class to satisfy my Prog 2 requirement (since I didn't take CS 50). In hindsight, I think I had enough CS background to skip CS 51 and take CS 61 and something else like CS 109a. Still, I think this class is a solid introduction if you don't have experience from CS 50 or took CS 50 and want to see a little more CS before diving in.
