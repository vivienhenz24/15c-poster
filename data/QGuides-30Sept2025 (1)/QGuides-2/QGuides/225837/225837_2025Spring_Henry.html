This is the first and only class that I have taken at Harvard that not only interested me, but moved me, sometimes to tears. The contagious enthusiasm of Professor Henry combined with her original pedagogical innovations––interviewing her own father in front of the class according to the model we had learned right before––had me actually shed a tear earlier in the Spring. I think this class will forever remain one of the highlights in my many years at Harvard.
Take it! It's beautiful. A strong emphasis on method with rich thought–provoking readings and a professor who is thoughtful, warm, capable, and invested in your well–being. You won't be disappointed.
Dr Henry is a spectacular teacher who provides both practical and theoretical approaches to life stories and oral histories. An essential class for any humanities or social science students.
Come to the class with some idea of who you would like to speak with and questions you would ask.
Start transcribing early!
Take the class it is fun, engaging and you will learn a lot.
If you're planning to conduct interviews, take this course! It will prepare you both technically, ethically, and creatively to do the work with depth and confidence. It is one of the best methods courses I've taken.
This course is incredible, it offered practical tools while pushing my critical and theoretical thinking. I highly recommend it especially for working with stories which are often missing from formal archives.
If ethnography will be a key part of your dissertation study, this course will be an essential one to add to your toolbelt.
If you want to truly learn how to engage in qualitative research in ways that honor marginalized communities and amplify the voices of those within them –– PLEASE TAKE THIS COURSE!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!
This is a very useful course to address methodology critically and opening the ears to multiplicity of disciplines and approaches to oral story.
