What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This was a pretty horrible course overall. It was very messy, poorly planned, and overall a very flawed idea. This year instead of teaching normal ASL 6 they mixed the class with LING175 and made "Linguistics of Sign Languages" for both signing ASL students and non–signing linguistic students. the course never found its feet and seemed to be a sinking ship for the entire semester. I hope that they revert back to how ASL6 used to be taught, because this was a sad way to end the ASL program after 6 semesters. Kate Davidson was a particular low point of this course, as she frequently violated ASL ethics by doing things like simultaneous–communication (or sim–comm) regularly
Be aware that this was the first time this class was offered so the feedback you see will look very scary. This class was made for the linguistics nerds who are interested in sign language and have skills in sign language. For the advanced ASL students coming into this class, we were expecting something totally different and it feels like our skills didn't really get better. A lot of my development came from outside of class.
