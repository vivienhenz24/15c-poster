English 20 is a required course so you can't really get around this if you're an English concentrator, but it genuinely is very low stress! The readings were MUCH lighter than English 10 and the assignments were a lot more creative and engaging. Honestly, as long as you stay as engaged as possible throughout the semester, you'll find the workload to be super manageable and you might even have fun with the assignments! Definitely will not be a super stressful class to add to your schedule, just come prepared to listen, read between the lines, and learn a lot!
you get out what you put in. Professor Lynch is wonderful, the readings are outstanding, and the assignments are infrequent and engaging. despite being a required course for my concentration, this was one of my favorite courses this semester. I highly recommend.
English 20 is a decent class as far as common courses go. The class materials depend largely upon the individual professor, so there can be a lot of overlap between courses depending on who's teaching. The course is set up in a way that really does help to strengthen reading and formal writing, and I found the instruction especially helpful for the lyric form.
This course is an awkward attempt at exploring various literary forms and their significance throughout history. The big class size for this semester made it difficult to contribute to discussions, which the lectures unfortunately rely upon. I was really confused most of the time because I wasn't sure what I was supposed to be taking away when 90% of the class is just kids yapping. Also make sure you have experience with writing essays for the English department and close reading, reading/writing poetry, etc. The teaching staff is approachable and nice but they will not hold your hand throughout the assignments which make up most of your grade. I'd recommend you take Expos/Hum 10 and English 10 before taking this. In fact they favor Hum 10 kids and reference a lot of texts that some of the class hasn't read so I felt confused and left behind at times. Overall it's a requirement so only take it if you need to; I think the dept can do a better job at emphasizing what it is students need to take away as English concentrators.
TAKE THIS CLASS! If you like reading literature and writing about it, you will love this class. It will teach you new ways to look at works and help you to situate them in literary history. It's also a great class for improving essay–writing skills since you are pushed to experiment in your writing and write to the best of your abilities. Class discussions are really interesting, and the works we read are really cool. I loved this class and would highly recommend it. Professor Lynch is great.
Professor Lynch's English 20 is AMAZING!!! Please take it with her if you have the chance, you will fall even more in love with English. She is enthusiastic, knowledgable, thoughtful, passionate, and just the sweetest person alive. She was able to connect the course texts (which are distinct in content, time period, and form) in a fluid manner, and taught me so much about literary history. I still vividly remember something she said from the first lecture, that the literary past is always alive, and this opened my eyes to the numerous ways in which writers react/respond/intervene/borrow from the literary past. Prof. Lynch also offers amazing feedback, and is always available outside of class to talk further. I cannot recommend her course enough.
this is a solid course but the syllabus was a bit confusing and the theme was difficult to articulate. it was fruitful for building my english skillset, but sometimes i wasn't sure what the broader goal of the random texts we were reading each week was.
TAKE THIS CLASS WITH LYCNH! My favorite class of the semester by far. It's not too much work and the assignments are really fun/have a creative spin to it. Tf's are very supportive and helpful and prof. lynch tries to maker herself as accessible as possible during office hours etc. If you want to learn how to analyze works through form this is the class to take. I would recommend this course also to non–concentrators since I think it can teach you lots of analytical skills that are applicable to many other fields.

This is a really great introduction to the English department, and as traditional as this class is on some levels, you can really make of it what you want. The assignments are all great opportunities to explore, and Professor Lynch is so available and eager to talk in office hours. She is so helpful and enthusiastic about English, and it is contagious. The readings are not at all overwhelming, discussion posts are extremely manageable, and all the discussions are interesting and fun.
I did not really enjoy my experience with Professor Lynch and I think that it could be quite a good class with a different professor (as many English common courses are) and less in–lecture discussion. The readings were alright and the workload not too burdensome, but lecture itself was not engaging.
