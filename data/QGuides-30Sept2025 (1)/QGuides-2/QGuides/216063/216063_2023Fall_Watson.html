I think this is a good course. Professor Watson and Vanessa are deeply delightful people. I wouldn't take it as your first English course––not because it would be difficult––but because by fixating on the minutiae of language and the making of argument, we sometimes lost sight of why these texts are beautiful. I also wish Professor Watson would lecture for a little longer––the lectures were wonderful, and interesting.
This course has changed how I read, how I interact with texts, and how I form thoughts through written articulation. The books/poems we read were super cool!!! The standard for writing/essays is pretty high, but as long as you are organized and articulate well, it isn't terrible and, actually, very fulfilling.
I found the selection of texts seemed a little random. Also the focus of the course just did not sit right with me. It was supposedly on forms but then it was unclear in lecture etc how exactly that was tied in and that gave it an unfocused feel. I also felt the class was badly run. it was not clear what weeks we had a discussion post and what weeks we did not which made me do a lot more work than I needed to.

Would not recommend unless you have to take it.
I took this class as a senior CS student and loved it. I'm someone who loves to read (but doesn't always make time for it) and this class forces you to read something each week. The weekly discussion posts allow you to think a little more deeply about the text if you're not a talker. The assignments besides those are quite manageable: 2 essays, an imitation assignment (where you write two poems on the same subject in different forms and reflect on it) and an in class final. The in class final was honestly really fun as weird as that is to say. The time crunch forces you to run with whatever ideas pop into your head first and ~1.5 hrs later you've written 3 full essays.

As someone who hasn't always been convinced by substantiating claims about literature with super close reading (e.g., the alliteration here conveys the overwhelming sadness of the author, or the consonance here proves blah), this class helped convince me otherwise.

Downsides are its a decent amount of reading with a particular focus on the kind of British classical corpus (jane austen, medieval poems, beowulf), so you're getting what feels like a lot of a certain type of literature. Also, the class is broken down into lecture and subsequent discussion, and occasionally professor watson's lectures take up the vast majority of class, leaving you either to check out or no time to discuss things that excite you. His slides are occasionally very hard to read.
I strongly recommend this class if you want to engage with a breadth of English literary work and want to build up your skills in literary analysis/discussion.
