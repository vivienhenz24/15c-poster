It's not the most exciting class in the world, but that's just because learning about literary forms in general can be kind of dense. With that being said, Professor Pexa and Celine really go above and beyond to draw you into the class. I think the updated English 20 is a really fresh take on what I gather was a pretty traditional course. Also, try to keep up with the readings, but if you can't it's not really that big of a deal. Just make sure you've read enough of them that you have a text to pull from for the essays, which are really chill assignments. Basically, this class is what you make of it, especially since it's a required course for English concentrators/secondaries.
I enjoyed this course and Professor Pexa + TF Celine were amazing! Wish there was more structure to class discussions / variety in class activities. Also grading rubrics weren't very clear so be sure to go to office hours before each assignment to ensure you're on the right track. Sometimes grading feedback is vague and unhelpful, though I felt the amount of assignments was fair. Be sure to stay on top of the readings and if you're interested in indigenous literature, taking the course under Professor Pexa is great.
Pexa is a really kind and caring professor who is really down to earth! But I'm not sure if the lecture–discussion format worked for me. Granted, I think a good amount of people skimmed through the readings for this class which made discussion a bit hard, but sometimes discussion felt unfocused, which didn't help with how scattered the English 20 reading list is in general (even though I've gained a new appreciation for poetry).

Celine was a really great section leader. She provided a ton of feedback for our essays, and she also led section in a really structured way!
Take this class with Professor Pexa!! He is incredibly knowledgable and will care about your individual learning in a unique way. Class discussions were always interesting.
I'd encourage a faithful reading practice, and a commitment amongst other students to discuss the works earnestly each week
I think this class is too large to have good discussions, but overall, the quality will depend mostly on the professor. I didn't really like Professor Pexa; he didn't do enough research on the texts on the syllabus that he didn't choose, and he didn't get students to engage much in the discussion. Most people were doing other work on their computers. I wouldn't take this class unless you have to.
The syllabus changes every year.
I have very mixed feelings about this course. I took the class thinking I would pursue a secondary in English, as this class was a requirement for that, but it kind of discouraged me from doing so (it wasn't the only reason but it was one). The course in terms of workload is very accessible, sometimes a lot of reading needs to be done, but that's what you sign up for. There aren't a lot of assignments, and none are too difficult, so in terms of work overall, the course is fine. The reading list is nice as well, I enjoyed reading most of the books and class discussions were fruitful. The professor and TF were nice, and were very fair when it came to grading. My main gripe with the class is just the homogenous thinking of everyone else involved. I quickly found that it wasn't really the place to have controversial opinions or say anything separate from what the group thought. There were definitely stretches when I kept my mouth shut for entire classes because I didn't want to say what I really thought. It might've just been the way I saw it, but I'd say in general I didn't love taking the class, but it's fine enough if you're interested.
We didnt learn how to write as much as I thought we would. Maybe thats the point but I thought we would discuss form more and how to write a formal analysis in more depth.
Was so pleasantly surprised by this courseit's required for concentrators and I didn't think it would be that good given that it's mandatory, but I really enjoyed Professor Pexa's teaching and many of the texts in the course! Be prepared for some of the Western canon texts to be a slog if you're not really interested in them–––the highlight for me was definitely the Indigenous texts that Prof. Pexa brought into the course, along with the poetry anthologies. Would recommend highly!!
This course isn't a difficult one and the readings are very interesting. However, I felt that at times we honed into one concept in a reading too much and failed to explore the themes that were extremely relevant in the novels.
