What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Read the textbook carefully and ask questions about the reading – one of the great advantages of this class is that you have the author there to guide you through the topic at hand. Start the readings for the position paper as early as possible to have time to develop a compelling argument.
Very thought–provoking class with good readings and interesting lectures. It's not about how laws relating to particular matters of dispute changed over time (as I thought it was before I signed up) but more the change in the way law was conceptualized and developed over time, so I'd say it's much more of a history than a law course. The workload isn't too heavy and the final project was fun. Sometimes it felt like there were unnecessarily long discussions, though.
A very interesting course with a wide range, but your grade does depend on the final essay, which might create anxiety for some. Otherwise, light workload.
