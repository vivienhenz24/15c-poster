Attendance and class participation are also weighted heavily. Otherwise, this is a great space for anime lovers or those who are interested in learning more about the medium/watching lots of it across the semester.
While there is an additional section and screening time, you will come out of this class with an increased understanding. You'll learn how to engage with and think about media, with anime as a more of a case study. It's such a fun class and you get out of it slightly more than the work you put in (you always come away with something).
TAKE THIS CLASS. Definitely my favorite class I have taken at Harvard in terms of content. It is extremely interesting even if you do not actively watch anime. It teaches you a lot about film, media strategies, and Japanese culture.
This course is very good introduction to EAS or film study. If you are interested in any of these, this might be a good course to start.

Prof. Yodas Anime GenEd was extraordinary. I learned so much not just about anime, but about arts and culture as a whole, especially through the lens of how we consume entertainment. Prof. Yoda knows what shes talking about, and it shines through in every assignment and lecture she gives. The course materials are fun and engaging, the lectures and sections are great, the TFs are exceptional, and the class just goes above and beyond in all aspects. I especially loved the section component of the coursewe all got really close in section, and our TF (Mari) was an awesome alternate perspective on what Prof. Yoda taught in class, and just an incredible teacher, mentor, and collaborator overall. This class was so good it helped inspire me to study abroad in Tokyo this upcoming summer, and all the TFs have been so helpful in my preparations. To make a long story short, if you need an Aesthetics and Culture GenEd and are at all curious about anime (I came in with very little experience with the medium), take this class!!!

(+) class content is fun and readings are interesting
(+) time required outside of class is theoretically very low. professor will never ask students to know content that she had not covered in lecture. thus: just pay attention for the hour+fifteen min of lecture and you're set for all assignments + quizzes
––>if you want to be super prepared for assignments, just mark down which reading is attributed to which line of thought and CTRL+F for keywords when you're writing later
(+) professor and TF want you to succeed and will give heavy support for assignments + further direction. other than final assignments, extensions are pretty freely given
(+) prof + TF are cool yappers and are willing to geek out/entertain your anime love. or if you're not into anime they have deep thoughts on the intersections of media and the rest of the world
(+) readings are free access through the canvas page yay

(=) grading is done entirely by TFs, but the TFs give amazing feedback and are extremely qualified to comment critically. grading can be harsh (according to peers in course) but i write things the day of/the day before and have gotten As thus far.
(=) professor is open, kind and considers all questions in lecture. ALL QUESTIONS. even objectively stupid ones that were covered two slides prior. prof yoda considers every question in full

() readings are long. unwieldy and obsessed with covering complex topics in as little sentence count as possible
() course expectations and standards are a little unclear
() final assignment is very involved (due over reading period) AND there's a final exam (last day of class)
