Do you think the self exists? Take the course to find out. Super interesting and engaging. Prof. Nichols is great at explaining complex ideas. Not too much work if you stay on top of the writing assignments.
Such an intellectually stimulating class! It was very beginner–friendly, both in terms of content and in terms of discussion because Prof. Nichols always guided the class really well and allowed anyone to speak up and agree or object; this was my first philosophy class, so I was a little nervous about whether I would be able to keep up with the discussions. I definitely wasn't able to keep up with every conversation lol but hearing everyone's thoughts about the readings made me rethink the content and see them in a different light that I hadn't considered before. I do genuinely think this class has changed the way I view how I interact with everyday life (for example, when I process my decisions and think about how my body responds to my mind), and I'm excited to see more applications of the theories and philosophies in the future (and hopefully connect them back to what I've learned!).
Taking this class with Shaun was great; hes goated and it sucks he has to leave.
If you've ever wondered how the mind works, how we know the world around us is real, or what it means to think about things then take this course! It was guest taught by Dr. Shaun Nichols when I took it (Spring '24) so my experience is not necessarily reflective of how it will be next year, but I have heard excellent things about Dr. Chen's version as well
9/10

This is a fantastic class. You will need a few courses in philosophy before taking this one to not feel very lost. The material is NOT a breeze, but it is well worth it. There were some people for whom this was their first philosophy course, and they still pretty much thrived. Professor Nichols is a phenomenal teacher with a humorous side that makes classes fly by. Please note that this review is from a year in which there was a guest professor, so it may not reflect what the course will be like when you take it.

TLDR: If you're the person who won't stop asking "why?" take this course. Avoid if you don't like thinking hard.
I LOVED this course. There is so much about consciousness, the mind, and the mind–body connection that we don't know about. These are all concepts that I grappled with as a kid, and knowing they are genuinely debated concepts has been simply fascinating to discover. Being around such intelligent individuals has been eye–opening. There are so many connections to physics and neuroscience and I feel as though this course ties together so many various aspects of study. I recommend it 10000%.
