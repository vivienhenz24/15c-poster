What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

I cannot recommend this course with enough enthusiasm. Conceptually it can feel fairly solipsistic and egotistical, but in practice with the help of peers and Professor Blum's expertise the class becomes a unique way of distancing oneself from the concept and institution of Harvard to think critically about the ways in which the world of both Harvard alumni and non–affiliates think about the university. Moreover, the diversity of form, genre, and content in the syllabus makes for a very interesting way of experiencing Harvard in everything from films to archive trips to (of course) novels. Especially to upperclassmen who have spent some time inside the Harvard bubble, getting the chance to see that bubble from the outside looking in is an exciting opportunity to think about both Harvard and the American university more generally as both actual institutions and literary tools.
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! I had very slight reservations because I didn't want to seem too navel–gazing of an individual, "Harvard Novel et cetera," but this has been my absolute favorite class I have taken since coming to Harvard. The selection of texts are so interesting and I was so engaged throughout, and the research component also revolutionized the way I use archives and Houghton Library now. Prof. Blum is a phenomenal professor and brings in sociological and other lenses seamlessly into thinking about these texts. Coming to this class was the highlight of my week.
This class was fantastic, and I'm really glad that I took it! It's been so helpful to sit down and deconstruct the Harvard experience with my peers and with Prof. Blum, who's an incredible facilitator and extremely knowledgeable about the subject materials. The assignments were novel (haha) and challenging – I really liked being able to do archival work and take advantage of the resources that Harvard has to offer, and I feel like I've gained skills that will be useful the rest of my time here. There's also space to explore works of fiction that you're interested in, and there's an excellent balance between the structure of the syllabus and the freedom to incorporate personal experience into the class. It's an amazing class, with one of the best vibes I've ever had in a section!
Do the reading and show up ready to share your thoughts! The class is so much more interesting when everyone has an opinion on the novels we're reading.
I truly think this class changed how I view my time at Harvard, and it rekindled a gratitude and sense of appreciation. You really examine all facets of Harvard in the cultural imaginary, and its often directly relevant to your experiences and offers an opportunity to reflect and hear from others. The class is more casual, which I think helped us bond and become comfortable in discussions, and the syllabus included both novels and movies! Professor Blum is so sweet and just an incredibly professor in the way she facilitates discussions, expresses genuine care and interest for you and your interests, and is so thorough in her feedback. I highly, highly recommend this course!
Make sure to do the reading, and Professor Blum will do the rest. You'll see this set of four years in a new light, and you'll also get some great critical lenses, which I didn't necessarily expect from a class on this topic. Harvard rarely affords a genuine opportunity to reflect, so I highly recommend you take this one!
Take this class. Take any class you can with Beth Blum. She has completely transformed my college experience, and made it much more depthful and self–aware. This class especially has funny and insightful books on the syllabus, you will not regret joining it!
