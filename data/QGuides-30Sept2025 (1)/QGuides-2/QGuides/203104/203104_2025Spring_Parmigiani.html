Stat 117 was fantastic!!!! Definitely my favorite elective course I've taken. The discussions we had in class were always thoughtful, engaging, and nuanced, which were made possible because of our incredible professor! Dr. Parmigiani has put so much thought and care into the semi–flipped structure of the class, as well as the class notes and materials for each lesson, and his extensive experience in this field adds so much depth to our discussion. All the homeworks and projects contribute uniquely to our understanding of the material––the homeworks do an awesome job helping us to digest the material, and then the projects give us the opportunity to take our understanding and innovate on it even further. Overall, this class is very, very well–designed and has taught me so much about biostatistics as well as a general intuition around how to handle data and use it to the best of our ability!
Great course! Flipped classroom format, come ready to discuss. One of those classes where you get out what you put in, not too hard of a course but room for some really interesting explorations.
This was one of my favorite classes not just in the stat department, but in my time at Harvard. Giovanni is a great professor, and this course has been polished over the years he has taught it. The course is the right balance of being conceptually challenging while still a very reasonable workload, so that it will always feel engaging and interesting but never burdensome.

You do not need a bio background –– the first 3 lectures give you all the background you need. As someone with little bio background, I found the motivating examples and the bio intro to be some of the best parts of the course!
Prof Parmigiani ans Iris are both fantastic and so accessible, and this class felt so meaningful + really made me appreciate the great ways stat can be applied to cancer research! After spending so long doing the more lecture type / non–applied stat classes, I was a little jaded but the project based hands on nature of this class + the incredible professor and opportunities to learn from my classmates made me realize why I love stat again. Probably the only class I would take at 9am my senior spring!
You will learn so much about working with gene expression data, building classifiers, and modeling gene expression more generally. Looking back at the course, I'm so happy with all the new knowledge I'm taking away! That said, lectures can sometimes be confusing without a background in cancer research and bioinformatics methods. I found that I often struggled to fully grasp the concepts from the lecture notes + class discussion, but that I was able to make huge leaps in understanding with the help of the projects. Giovanni is a wonderful professor who is super excited to discuss the material and any ideas and questions you may have. It can just be a little tough to know where to start asking questions when so much of the content is so new (or at least for me it was).
Giovanni is very passionate! He is a good speaker and always kind and approachable, with a sense of humour. I think that because participation is voluntary and because there is not an active structure that makes it easy for people to talk in group settings in class, it ends up being Giovanni talking most of the time and maybe 5–8 of us chiming in at certain points, or when the wait for someone to speak up is too long. Nevertheless, the opportunities to present both your individual data projects and group projects are more than sufficient for you to hone your scientific communication skills and public speaking skills (and the chance to get "grilled" by Giovanni and the more engaged classmates). Also, shoutout to Iris for being an amazing TF since Stat 110 and for always being so on top of it and responsive, even when you're juggling thesis writing and many more commitments.
This is a very good course to take if you're even remotely considering a career in biostatistics and a good one even if you're not. Giovanni is such an amazing professor and all the assignments are very fair. Taking this course also helped with learning how to present my projects better and the course is really well formatted.
