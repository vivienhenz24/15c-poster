What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Lina is a great professor! She is very clear in her lectures and expectations; you will learn a lot from her classes regardless of what you actually take. For this class in particular, you learn necessary background information before reading research papers. In my opinion, the homework is not super challenging but very useful. The papers were drawn from and exposed me to a wide variety of topics. You gain the useful experience of giving a 1 hour presentation on a research paper. I recommend this class.
Really good, course very fast–paced. The first and second halves are very differentfirst half is pset based and second half is read–and–response based, with quite a lot of time needing to be allocated to readings, posting questions and comments, and writing reflection notes.
This class is great if you're really motivated to learn about control, and it's perfect for anybody doing research in control. However, this is a MATH class. The problem sets are all formal proofs (no coding), and the second half of the semester is reading papers in control theory (not control applications). You can do an implementation project at the end of the course, but for anyone really interested in engineering solutions for control, you might be better off in a different class.

For coursework, the class is split into two parts. For the first half of the semester, there is a PSet due every two weeks. Read the book religiously–––the lectures were copied straight from the book, and it was extremely helpful for problem sets. The problems aren't too bad if you give yourself enough time. Once problem sets stop and reading reflections begin, expect to spend a lot of time reading papers on linear control theory bounds and scratching your head wondering how useful the stuff really is. I thought it got better at the very end when we started looking at MPC and RL, and that stuff is really useful!
Interesting class! The first half of the course was lecture/pset format, and the second half was seminar/project format. I felt there was more busy work (reflection notes) in the second half which I did not prefer, but overall thought the course was well–taught and a great introduction to modern research in control. Lina is very helpful and willing to work with students! It's helpful to have some prior exposure to ML or RL to get the most out of the recent papers.
