10/10 class. But like any English seminar, the quality is determined by the people in the class. This class had such thoughtful + wonderful souls squished together like sardines in a tiny room, so it was awesome sauce. Also the books ruled + Jesse always rocks.
what an incredible privilege to take this class with Professor McCarthy. the environment and discussion are robust and genuinely get me thinking about how these texts relate to my life as well. Also approachable for non concentrators and does transform the way you think about texts that you may think are initially niche or inapplicable to your daily life.
TAKE THIS CLASS! These two authors spoke to each other beautifully but also so much to the moment we live in. Opens up questions about the work of literature + how to live without being too general or straying from close textual analysis. Loved my cohort, we were over capacity and everyone truly wanted to be there. McCarthy creates a great environment for discussion and is very kind, reasonable, and relaxed without sacrificing rigor.
TAKE THIS CLASS. This is absolutely the best class I've taken at Harvard. Three hours seems long on paper, but it flies by. Professor McCarthy is not only a genius but such a kind and enthusiastic seminar leader. And the kind of people this class attracts, who are willing to read hundreds of pages a week in search of insight into life itself, are some of the best people you will meet here. Every class made me feel so motivated both to read more and to live more, as well as to think more about both of those things. I will never forget what I learned about myself and the world in this class.
I would highly recommend this class. I had no background in English (or the humanities) at all, but saw the incredible q–reports from last year's class and decided to give it a go. I am so glad that I did. While the readings might seem long, they are (for the most part) really enjoyable books and are totally manageable given that you have an entire week to complete them. Also, there is very little class work outside of the readings, so the class really is manageable even with an otherwise busy course load. Truthfully, I would take this class even if the readings took me twenty hours a week (they don't). That's just how much I got out of it. This class changed my semester for the better by encouraging me to reflect on my own life and habits, inspired by really meaningful readings and class discussions. I am so glad that I took this class, and can not send out enough encouragement to any other potential enrollees!
Take this class!! This is such a special seminar with incredible, moving readings. Professor McCarthy creates a warm and fun class environment and discussions are genuinely interesting and productive. It is a lot of reading, so be prepared for that. Don't take the class if you want a gem, but do if you want a phenomenal English class.
This was an excellent course. Two very different writers led to such rich discussion. Jesse facilitated such an open and engaging environment that created a truly unique seminar.
I feel I have been fully convinced by the course's main conceit linking James and Baldwin. McCarthy's union of the two authors opened up historical and ideological linkages between vastly different (but essentially connected) time periods and American sensibilities. I learned so much about what these authors think it means to be a man, to be queer, to be American, to be successful, to be a good person.
