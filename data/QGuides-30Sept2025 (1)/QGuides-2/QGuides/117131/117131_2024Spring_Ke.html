Though the course content is not always the most engaging, this course does a great job of teaching about its core topic, which is conducting time series analyses. There is a strong emphasis on applying concepts through code.
This iteration of Stat 131 was a very dumbed down version of the course offered two years ago. No more statistical finance and HMMs were covered only very briefly. Instead, a lot of basic theory and remembering the output of R code. Tracy also rushes through all of the slides, and the mandatory quizzes were the only thing that made me go to class. After taking 242 last semester as well, all I can say is that the statistics department really needs to improve its time series courses.
This course was a lot less workload than I expected so if you're looking for a lighter statistics course I would definitely recommend it! I will say I'm not sure how useful it would be to real world analysis, but you do get a broad overview of many time series models and techniques. The quizzes are kind of a pain, but do help you to stay on top of the material.
If you can avoid taking this class, I would not take it. The workload itself is not significant for a stats class, but the lectures are confusing and I don't feel like I gained much from the class.
I really enjoyed this class! Tracy was really an amazing instructor as well, always active on Ed to clarify questions and generally being very accommodating of students –– modifying pset deadlines, moving class on the eclipse day, etc.
This class does a good job covering time series and very common modeling. The best parts of this class are (1) Tracy's communication, she is very clear with what expectations are, and (2) the final project because it nicely applies many concepts we cover. The weekly quizzes might seem intimidating, but they were not that big of a stressor. The slides are very well done even though it might seem overwhelming during lecture; when reviewing them for exams, I could finally appreciate the logical flow and see how the details fit with big picture ideas.
Mid. Interesting course but too much time spent on the same material, felt redundant. Hard to follow lecture and not much extremely the course .
While time series is a very important type of data to be able to analyze, and while I understand it's generally uncommon for time series classes to even be offered at the undergraduate level, I don't think STAT 131 was taught or implemented the most effectively. I am a grad student, and I took my first time series class as an elective during undergrad. The time series class I took at my undergrad institution was significantly more applied compared to STAT 131, which I know is comparatively less theory heavy relative to STAT 242. However, in my opinion, I think STAT 131 should focus more on learning how to analyze time series in R (practical skills) and focus less on the math and theory behind the concepts. I feel like STAT 131 was taught like a math class with no bigger picture perspective. For instance, while studying for this class each week, I had to ask ChatGPT to explain to me all the core concepts that were briefly name–dropped during lecture because we only learned the math and not the qualitative foundational ideas behind these core concepts. We learned so much math and so many proofs, but it wasn't even clearly explained in class what the concepts we're trying to define mathematically even mean on a higher level. My other major complaint about this class is that I think we were overworked. There was a quiz every week, a homework every week, a midterm, a final, and a final project. I have mixed feelings about this because I think all of these components were very important––I learned a lot from each quiz, homework, exam, and the project. However, the workload was certainly quite heavy, and perhaps if more of the math/theory is removed from the class, the quizzes, homeworks, exams, and project will become a bit easier.
Please take it if you are interested in time–series. Professor Ke is the best.
This class is great! Very organized lecture slides, regular quizzes to test comprehension of the materials, fair exams, and a fun final project. Professor Ke is also excellent and helped us a lot on our final project (she met with every group twice before the deadline).
Great course to learn about time series analysis. Provides both strong theory and strong practice, which can be a rarity in the stats department. Good amount of learning for the time needed.
Tracy is incredibly nice and a fantastic lecturer. The course material is interesting and fundamental.
