This course is a great introduction to the major policies that have shaped U.S. environmental regulation. From air quality standards to power plant emissions regulations, I got a good sense of the most important environmental laws on the books, as well as the current landscape of environmental law and the opportunities/vulnerabilities the field faces in the near future. Professor Jenks is very knowledgeable and friendly, and she also has some great connections –– especially if you're considering a career in the environmental sector, the guest speakers she brings in are incredibly helpful and give a unique peek behind the curtain. Fair warning that the course is very concrete: it's satisfying not too up–in–the–air, but if you're not into the specifics of regulatory frameworks and the alphabet soup of policy acronyms, this course might seem dry at times. Overall I'd recommend it.
If you are an environmentalist, this is a "must–take" class! Easily the most practical knowledge I have gotten wrt EJ and policy–making, which I think can be said regardless of your academic background
TAKE THIS CLASS! Prof Jenks realllllyyyyyyy knows her stuff and I was a little hesitant coming into the class given it was new but very quickly it became a highlight of my week. You really get into the specifics about certain rules and policies on the federal level, and Prof Jenks does a great job clarifying these sometimes really confusing histories and developments.

It gets a little bit into the weeds of policy stuff that could def sometimes get a little abstracted/boring, but honestly its super important stuff to know, and I am surprised it took so long for this class to develop in a concentration literally called Environmental Science and Public Policy. As someone hoping to go into environmental policy or law, this will probably be one of the most foundational courses I have taken at Harvard that will actually have a direct positive impact on my career. Also attracts really great classmates that raise really good and interesting points/questions.

And all of this with a pretty manageable workload. WHAT IS NOT TO LOVE!

It's a great introduction to Environmental Policy in the United States and I learned alot. The weekly readings may seem a lot, but they are interesting pieces and the reflection is a low stakes way of helping you think through the readings and concepts presented. Take it if the topic is of interest to you!

Take this course to learn a lot about environmental policy!
