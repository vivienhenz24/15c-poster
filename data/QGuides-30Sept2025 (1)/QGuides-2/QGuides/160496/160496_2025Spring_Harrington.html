Pretty interesting class and somewhat well structured, but assignments are graded super strictly and arbitrarily. On the blog posts, you're given feedback at the halfway point, but the feedback makes it seem as though they were looking for one particular thing all along when they told you at the beginning of the class that the blog posts are designed to be flexible and for you to write about whatever content feels meaningful to you. Same goes for the final essay; they frame it as if you can be flexible and do what you want with it, then their comments make very clear that you had to do very specific things in your essay in order to do well. Because there's only a few assignments, there's not really a buffer. For the final essay in particular, there wasn't a set rubric and their grading seemed very arbitrary. Don't get me wrong this is a decent class with super interesting content at times, and decently well taught, but keep in mind it can bite you in the foot very easily if you care about your grade. (It's the type of teacher who will tell you great job and then give you a B+).

This was a pretty interesting class. The content was engaging, and the assignments felt fair. However, I did not like the course structure or grading approach. I think this class would work much better as a seminar. It was difficult to facilitate meaningful discussions in a large lecture setting. We were supposed to have small group discussions to help address this, but they rarely happened and eventually stopped altogether toward the end of the semester. This led to certain students dominating class conversations (i.e., non–stop talking) while others struggled to participate. 3–4 people regularly took over the discussion, making it hard for anyone else to contribute. We often ran out of time and were not able to fully cover the material or reflect on what we had just learned. Staff did not attempt to correct any of this. It also would have been better to use a poll or form to choose a date for the movie night. I was uncomfortable when the professor questioned students in front of the entire class about why they could not attend. The section head did a great job of leading our preliminary group presentation discussion, but it became clear that they had some favorites. Didn't really get to hear much from them in lecture as the prof had a bad habit of cutting her and the students off.

The grading system did not leave much room for improvement. We received feedback on our blog posts at mid–semester, and although I applied that feedback and was told my work had improved, my final grade stayed the same. The staff cited issues with earlier posts. If they expected us to revise previous blogs, that should have been clearly communicated. It felt unfair to be penalized after making changes based on their guidance. Course materials, assignments, and expectations were often vague or clarified only after delays. Overall, this was an interesting class with strong potential, but it would benefit from a more focused structure and clearer communication. Some thoughtful restructuring (involving slimming down the class size) could make a big difference.
