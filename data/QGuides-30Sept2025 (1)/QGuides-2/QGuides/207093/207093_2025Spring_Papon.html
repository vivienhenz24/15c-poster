*** Great class. Professor Smith and Garfinkel genuinely care for you to succeed as data scientists.

You will write about 2 small papers (2 pages and then 5 pages if I recall correctly) at the beginning and the end of the course and a poster at the end of the year, in which you essentially predict the future of data science based on recent events in the news submitted by peers throughout the semester (one of the papers also shares this prompt). You will make comments on readings before class, but these are quite manageable and do not take up much of your time. I did most of these a few hours before class and did fine.

As for the majority of your assignments, the class is half split between individual and group work. The individual work is mostly writing participation reports, in which you write 2 page explanations on 3 ways you participated in class. These can be as simple as answering the professors questions or making comment threads on the reading assignments. Some miscellaneous tasks include getting a certification in ethics of human research, discussing ethics sections in technical papers, and creating a comic strip (these can be AI generated, if that gives you an indicator of the difficulty of the class).

The rest of the work is group work. Id assume this classs course content changes from year from year to reflect the changing landscape of the field of data science, but this should serve as a good survey of what you might expect. We worked on implementing and showcasing differential privacy via Python (very surface level, so dont worry if youre not good with stats or math), image recognition projects, and a deepfake project.

All of the Python needed for these assignments can probably be done with CS32 experience. CS109a experience is a definitely enough. CS50 + some individual Python experience works as well.

The only issue I have for this class is that sometimes the rubrics/instructions can be vague. At times, instructions for what was or wasnt allowed during group work projects were unclear, but this was such a minor setback that was quickly resolved by just asking the professors that it was essentially insignificant.

You will pass this class. And in fact, Id be surprised if anyone got lower than a B–. Most people probably ended up with A or A–. If you put in just a tiny bit of work, you will do just fine. Again, these professors are awesome at what they do and I am so grateful to have taken a class like this as an undergrad. One of the best classes Ive taken at Harvard.
