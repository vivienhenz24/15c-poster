What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
You should definitely take this seminar if you're interested in social movements/education. I can't say enough great things about Dr. Simon– she is so kind, considerate, and caring. Exactly the type of community I needed my first semester at Harvard.
Take this class! Nicole is so wonderful and does her best to set you up well for your first semester of college by introducing you to resources you may not have known about (such as the Learning Lab) and facilitating connections between you and your classmates. The class covers three movements (desegregation, DREAMers, and gun violence prevention), and features several activist/historian guest speakers as well as a field trip to East Boston High School. While the readings are definitely comprehensive, Nicole tries to format them in a way where you can listen/read while doing other things, so it doesn't feel like as much work. It is clear how much she cares about us as people and wants to make sure we are successful. The class is super discussion–based and I got to know and love everyone else in our section. Radical Actors was always the highlight of my week and the semester flew by so fast :)
Please please take this class! This class changed me for the better in terms of how I understand American social movements, how to think critically, and how to develop a project /learning tool to encourage students. From the guest speakers to the readings/podcasts to the field trips, I enjoyed every aspect of this class! Highly recommend!
Take this class! Nicole is extremely kind and caring. I learned so much about activism, especially regarding integration in Boston. I feel more connected to Harvard's resources after taking this class. There are many thoughtful discussions and remarkable guest speakers with experience in activism.
TAKE THIS SEMINAR!! It was so great to have time to eat lunch with classmates and talk about the material discussed or life in general before we actually got into the groove of things. If you've been involved in activism as a student in anyway, this seminar is such a nice way to be informed on work that you've done in the past or are currently doing.
You must take this seminar! Each week, you have a few readings or materials to listen to, which are not time–consuming. At the end, you have a project that you work on during the semester, but it's not overwhelming because different parts of the project are due at different times, and Nicole guides you through the project process. This seminar is such a welcoming and inviting space, even if you're not great at raising your hand or discussion. It's not strictly discussion–based, which is great because you get to do different activities with your classmates, go on field trips, and hear from guest speakers. A great plus is also that you get lunch from the Quincy Dining hall for each class, which is really good! I enjoyed this seminar so much; it's one of the best ones out there!
To really enjoy the content and the people in this class, it truly made the experience wonderful.
