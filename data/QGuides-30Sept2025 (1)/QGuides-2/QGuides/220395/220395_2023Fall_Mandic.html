The content this course covers is very interesting, the lectures are captivating, and the section leaders are approachable and excited to answer any questions you may have. The issue I had with this class was how difficult the weekly assigned readings were. They assigned around 100+ pages of readings each week and they were all very lengthy and dense. I found myself spending around 4–6 hours per week reading and rereading the texts to try and gain an understanding of the material before the daily in–class quizzes. The quizzes were usually 3–5 multiple choice questions which meant if you answered one question incorrectly you would earn a B, C, or D for the day which would have a big impact on your overall grade. While I loved learning about the sociology concepts, this course made me so stressed out between the readings and quizzes. Also, I received no feedback for my final research paper which was very frustrating as I fulfilled every requirement but was graded pretty harshly without explanation. I would not recommend this course to a first year student still adjusting to the college workload because I realized that I should not have taken SOCIOL 1000 my first semester of college alongside two pset classes and Expos.
