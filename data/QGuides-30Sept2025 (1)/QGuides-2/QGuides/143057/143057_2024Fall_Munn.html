What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course is excellent preparation for future medical practitioners or researchers, particularly those interested in oncology or cancer research. Guest lectures allow students to learn about a range of diseases and approaches.
I highly recommend taking this class if you are working with cancer. Although the title is Tumor Microenvironment, it provides a broad overview of every subfield of cancer, including mechanobiology, tumor metabolism, drug delivery, immune checkpoints, vascularization, pediatric cancer, omics, and computational tools. It also covers how these approaches disentangle the heterogeneity within the tumor microenvironment.

Each subfield is taught by a leading pioneer in the field, or even the founder of that subfield, offering fantastic insights into their personal perspectives and expertise. The course has only one final assignment: writing a grant proposal (on a topic of your choice) similar to an NIH grant. You will be paired with a post–doc, professor, or clinician who currently works on the related topic you choose to write about.
If you are remotely interested in the topics discussed in this class, this course is exactly the type of course you want to take in graduate study. It offers a somewhat low–commitment way to learn from the very top experts in their fields and also develop critical scientific skills in the written proposal with the valuable support from a 1 on 1 mentor (the proposal is the only grade). I was unsure about taking the class at first because of the commute to MIT from the Harvard Medical Campus, but after the first class, I knew it'd be worth it for me.
