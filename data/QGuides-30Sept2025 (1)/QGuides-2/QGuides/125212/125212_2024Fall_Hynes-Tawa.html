Make sure you have time to put in the work for this course and you'll be rewarded. Very useful if you're already involved in something music–adjacent, like singing in a choir, playing in a band, and even dancing.
assignments are graded too harshlyno drops, no late days, and the rubric is unclear. The assignments are quite long–winded and repetitive, and are quite superficial as well (busy work, not insightful). Even if you are a concentrator, its best to avoid this class and just do Music 150 directly for the theory requirement. You wont really learn much and its probably more efficient to just self study the content.
This is a good class to take if you're looking for an introduction to the nuts and bolts of how and why music works the way it does. The material covered all the different chords, how and when to use them; cadences; and musical forms, among other things. Dr. Hynes–Tawa was overall a pretty good lecturer; just be ready to hear a lot of Beatles and Japanese/Korean pop as in class examples, haha. Fabian was a friendly TF, and I could tell that he wanted us to understand the material (and was pretty generous with late work). He was occasionally a tough grader, but not terribly so. I will say that there was sometimes a conflict between what were being taught in lecture and section, mostly due to a difference in musical backgrounds in the instructors (classical vs. jazz), and Fabian occasionally seemed skeptical of the utility of some of the things we went over (Nashville notation, Sacred Harp music). I'm really not sure why there are two sections a week scheduled for this class; I don't really think we had to cover so much to warrant an extra class meeting, and some sections seemed kind of filler–y. The DAW assignments were kind of cool, but since we weren't really constrained by following the "rules" we had learned in class, I'm not super sure what the point of them were. The regular assignments were good at testing your understanding of the course content, and didn't really take super long.
Don't take it. That being said, the professors and TFs were incredible. I don't mean to make it sound like they were the root of the problem, because they were not. It was the curriculum, the disorganization, and the structure of the course that caused the issue.
As someone with many years of classical background, I found this course really fun. That being said, I did not find the lectures to be very good. My TF, Stephan, made this class as good as it was. He was extremely engaging and was able to teach to students of all levels in my section. If you are interested in music theory, definitely worth taking.
Great class, lecture kind of pointless, but section is great. Stephen is awesome.
I think this class is pretty good to gain some knowledge about topics that you may have not fully covered while learning an instrument. You can definitely learn a lot if you dig into the material and interact with professor.
This class is somewhat demanding but grading is fair and the content is well structured and well taught overall.
This is a well taught class for what it is. As a musician passionate about pop music and not really classical, I was not fond of many of the topics that were covered, but I think that all topics were covered very well, so that may be more of a testament to my own preferences.
Sections are the best part of this class. Take notes and go to your section leader's office hours every week to go over the assignments.
