Just do the readings
This class is absolutely life–changing – TAKE IT! This course was hands down one of the best classes I will likely ever take at Harvard. The combination of the passionate, kind professor with the captivating course content and curated students made learning about human rights life–changing in every aspect. Now, while I am considering a career in human rights, I know that I can apply similar principles of meticulously measured empathy and survivor–centered tactics to any work of public service I may pursue. This course will teach you how to think critically about dense legal texts for the first time, which is extremely useful for the remainder of your undergraduate and post–graduate experience (especially if you want to pursue pre–law!). Susan is one of the kindest, most accomplished individuals you will ever meet and it will be a privilege to learn from her each week. The readings are dense at first, but if you take the time to read them, you will leave the class feeling well–read and accomplished. Please please please apply to this class!
TAKE IT! Susan is great and provides so much support, and is generally a great role model. You can learn so much from her experiences and the experiences of the guests she brings in. I loved every seminar – the readings give you a wide variety of perspectives on different topics within human rights law/advocacy and genuinely make you question your preconceptions. 100% a class that you get out as much/more than you put in. You get to know one of the most incredible HLS professors and talk about some of the most incredible topics. Absolutely take it.
TAKE THIS SEMINAR!! Susan is a fantastic professor, super involved and passionate. The readings are slightly long, especially if you have a lot of other reading–heavy classes. However, they are all generally interesting and educational. I will probably go back to some of them which I found particularly interesting. Overall, this seminar is exactly what a freshman seminar should be: low–stakes, yet still extremely engaging and informative.
Do the readings. They are kind of long (or more) but they are super interesting. Beyond that, participate in class. It makes the whole experience all the better.
This course was amazing. To begin with, the topics discussed in class were highly engaging and insightful as they encouraged difficult conversations that are not often held in the human rights sphere. Professor Farbstein has become a role model for each of the students that have received the opportunity of having her as a professor. She conducts the classroom in a way that highlights the voices and perspectives of every student, ensuring that every individual is recognized. The readings and assignments all are given with a purpose and have been very meaningful in teaching the class about the aspects of human rights that are often overlooked. Overall, I would take this class again if I could and I highly encourage anyone who is considering to take it, even if your passions do not align with the human rights sector, there are many universal lessons taught in this course.

You will learn so much and have a deeper understanding of advocacy methods, learning how to center survivors in your work, and the importance of community lawyering.
