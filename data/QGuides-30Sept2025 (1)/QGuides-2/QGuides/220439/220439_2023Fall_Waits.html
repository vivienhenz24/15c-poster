What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Would fully recommend this course to anyone looking for a small, discussion–based, seminar. The professor, Hannah Waits, is excellent — super engaging in lecture, incredibly supportive and responsive in every respect, facilitates great discussions, and provides awesome feedback. Discussions in this class were absolutely amazing, as well. In this course, I learned a lot not only about the content — imperial conquests in the Americas — but I learned ways to look at these events. Through the varied forms of course readings — memoirs, poetry, historical secondary texts, and images, at times — I learned that there is such deep understanding that can be taken from these, and the ways each can complement each other to form an more complete understanding. I also have been inspired to pursue a Hist and Lit concentration, from the amazing discussions, people, and professor of this seminar!
This is a wonderful course! If you've never taken a Hist and Lit class, this is the perfect introduction to the department. Hannah is one of the best professors I've had at Harvard. The course has a consistent workload: two papers, a public engagement project, weekly response papers, and a final project (broken down into smaller assignments). However, I never felt overwhelmed in the class and the material was so interesting that I didn't mind spending a long time on the assignments. I enjoyed learning about imperialism in its historical context, as well as looking at modern–day iterations of imperialism, such as international aid and adoption. I also appreciated that Hannah gave us multiple options for the public engagement project and the final paper. If you don't want to write a traditional paper for the final, you don't have to!
take this course, you will not regret it
Absolutely loved this course. Please take if you are at all interested in: exploring Hist–Lit, learning about imperialism not taught in high school history, or even just for fun. Engaging discussions. It's hard to find courses like this one, in which you get so much out of, leaving the end of the semester with a more developed, refined interest in history of the Americas. Eye opening and provides historical context about many modern–day issues related to imperialism & will definitely guide my understanding going forward. I am inspired and looking for further EMR/Hist–Lit/Hist courses related to this area.
