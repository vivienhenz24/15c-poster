I think this class is great only if you're super interested in translation, comp lit, or poetry. If you're not interested in any, you'll find the class a bit abstract and vague. I personally loved this class and learned so much from it, but I had mixed feelings in that there was a lot of poetry I had to read and I wasn't honestly expecting that. I don't mind poetry, but when I have to read Oberiu absurdist pieces right after doing two problem sets when my brain is already fried, it can get kind of annoying, especially when you're someone who likes to get the right answer and find the ultimate meaning. I think the ultimate point of this class is that you'll never get that singular message from any work of literature, and that's okay (though you may or may not be satisfied with that answer, that deceptively simple message opens up a lot of room for further complexity and a diversity of interpretations). I really loved the creative aspect of this class and I got to use a lot of interesting theories, ideas, and concepts, and apply them to an assortment of things I got to write. I think you definitely get what you put into this class: this class has definitely showed me that I am still interested in literature (though not that much in poetry) and it has even convinced some of my peers to pursue comp lit or translation studies. The readings were a lot more than I expected, and although you could technically get away without doing them, you'll do yourself an injustice. The films, poems, novels that we read/watch are amazing and will introduce some good ideas. The professor is awesome and super passionate.

Sometimes this class goes into very abstract concepts (like Benjamin's "messianic language") and it might feel like you're losing touch with reality, but it's kind of a nice break from the problem–solution oriented courses. However for some people it could be very unpleasant and intangible. So it really dependents on your interests and what you want (ideally you would only apply for this seminar if you really wanted to be in it anyways).
