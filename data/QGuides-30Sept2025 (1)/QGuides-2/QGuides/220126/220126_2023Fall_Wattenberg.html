I liked how creative this course was in the midst of all my other STEM classes. This class is not easy though as it requires a lot of time especially if you are not familiar with p5.js. Make sure to start assignments early or else you will be spending 10+ hours the day before making your visualization. p5.js can be really annoying to work with and you might spend hours trying to debug/implement the smallest thing. Lectures are super interesting since you just spend the entire time looking at different art pieces. Grading sometimes felt a bit critical and comments from critique can sometimes be nit–picky but I think overall it's fine as long as you make good revisions. Overall, the people in the class are very nice and lovely and I enjoyed looking at all the cool pieces everyone made.
Take if you like code, art, or code and art, or spending time with cool people, and being a bit creative. Delightful course!
This class is AMAZING! This is one of very few cs courses that focuses on helping you tangible skills and allowing you to express your creativity. If you are at all interested in either art + cs or data visualizations + cs, this course is for you! You get to code data visualizations and art pieces which makes the work feel enjoyable and like a break from your other work. Martin is a great professor and there is so much to learn from him.
Best class I've taken at Harvard as a CS concentrator. Martin and the course staff are amazing, really combining art, creativity, data, and code in such an intuitive and fun manner. If you are excited about creating cool things every week (from abstract clocks to visualizing complex data in unique formats) that blend art with novel insights and topics (truly anything you care about), this class is for you.

Only warning is that it is more of a consistent workload than other classes – feels like a marathon at times, with the same level of work expected every week throughout the semester.
You don't need any CS background for this course beyond MAYBE CS50. The "hard" part is not coding, but coming up with cool and creative ideas for your artpieces. I put hard in quotes because it's time consuming but still very FUN. You get to explore your personal interests in depth, and the feedback on your pieces is so good that it's hard not to improve week–to–week.
This was my favorite course I have taken at Harvard. It teaches you a lot of useful skills and not to mention is just really fun. Each assignment makes you think outside the box and brings out your creativity and artistic ability. The coding is not too challenging, and the TFs and professor do a good job of teaching and making themselves available to you if you need help. I loved the in–class critiques because you got to see everyone else's project and give and receive good feedback. I highly highly recommend taking this class if you get the chance.
Definitely a very fun and enjoyable class but can sometimes be stressful if you do not plan your time wisely. Please start your final project early, it was definitely a grind to finish it.
This is the best CS class I've taken. Ever. Maybe even the best class period. The course staff is incredibly delightful and insightful, and you learn a lot about fundamentals in art/design/digital media. The assignments are also fun to work on, and they are one of the few things I look forward to each week! I have never been so motivated to work on class assignments, and its a great creative outlet. Expect to put in a fair bit of work (since you're creating ~one new piece every week), but it is incredibly rewarding. Take the class!
Take this course!! Its so fun and really gets your creative juices flowing. Martin is such an awesome, fun, understanding teacher and the TFs are great as well!
This course is amazing and is definitely one of my favorite courses at Harvard. The course staff is fantasticTFs provide great support and Martin is unbelievably knowledgeable, creative, and encouraging. Lectures go through concepts that are relevant to the weekly sketches, but they can lack a little bit of engagement at some points. Class critiques are so much fun, where you get to see what other students have come up with and receive great feedback on your work. In general, the course is very flexible and you get what you put in in terms of assignments.
CS 73 is without question the best class I've taken in my three years at Harvard. As a non–CS major, my coding and graphic design skills improved immensely and I had a ton of fun along the way. What you get out of this class, however, is completely dependent upon what you put in. The weekly assignments are open ended projects where you have the freedom to choose what you want to work on within certain guidelines. As a result, it is easy to spend four hours or twenty hours on the class each week, depending on how much time you decide to put into your work. Martin is an amazing instructor who gives fascinating and engaging lectures. The course TFs (Cynthia and Trevor) are also wonderful and there are tons of opportunities to engage with your peers through the weekly design critique. I cannot recommend this course highly enough!
This course took a lot more time than I was expecting, but each project/assignment pushed my thinking and creativity in regard to data visualizations. Each project is very open–ended, which can make it challenging at times, but I felt as though the class taught me so many valuable lessons in ethics and the principles of representing data.
TAKE THIS CLASS. Hands down one of my favorites at Harvard ever. Reads like a studio art class with weekly sketches and critiques during lecture time. If youre like me and have minimal coding experience, the learning curve for getting used to OpenProcessing and p5.js (the language used in this class) might be a little steep, but the course staff is helpful and lenient regarding the actual sophistication of the code. Its so fun to see everyones weekly sketches and watch them slowly improve with each assignment. No exams is always a plus the final project is an expanded sketch that you have the opportunity to present drafts of in critique and get feedback on. Having a class guided by creativity and design was refreshing at a place like Harvard.
I loved this class!!! It is an awesome opportunity to combine art and code into one medium. The class meets twice a week with one lecture and one critique class, so the schedule is pretty chill. Usually each week there is a creative assignment which can take time depending on your experience level. Personally, I am more on the art–side and only took CS50 (two years ago), so the assignments were challenging and time–consuming. Some assignments I spent 10+ hours, but I really enjoyed the work in designing visualizations. More CS experience would be super helpful and would make the workload lighter. You will love this class as long as you love art/visualizing data or have CS experience
One of the best classes I have taken at Harvard. Manageable time commitment, so fun, and super helpful for practicing practical coding.

One of the best CS courses I've tkaen at harvard. do it. Prof Wattenberg is the BEST.
If you get in, take this course! You will learn from each assignment and be proud of the works you do each week. Every week's lecture is extremely interesting as well. The time requirement is flex, depending on how much time you choose to put in, but it becomes very tempting to spend hours upon hours making a better and better product. Do take!
As a senior CS concentrator this was a great class to take towards the end of my time here. I felt that having a strong programming background let me focus more on the creative output in the weekly compositions and less on the implementation details, and that it provided a whole new angle on programming through generative art that I had never thought of before. It was very fulfilling to create these compositions week in and week out and receive a ton of feedback in critiques and grading, although I definitely fell into the habit of pulling an all–nighter every Monday to finish (or start in some cases) the weekly composition which was pretty rough. The only work is the weekly compositions (and the final project), but if you want to create good quality compositions then expect to put in a fair bit of time on those.
I learned so much about data visualization and design. It was awesome to be exposed to so many different pieces and to gain confidence in the creation of my own visualizations as well
