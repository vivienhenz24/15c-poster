loved this course!! it touched on such important topics & perspectives in the history of reproductive politics, a lot of history you think youd come in knowing, but what you learn from the course material shocks you. the professor is so sweet and attentive to the subject matter.
The idea behind this class is really great. Reproductive technology is super interesting and the students who enrolled in the class were all incredibly passionate and interested in the subject. The readings engaged with technology from birth control to IVF to population control, all of which were really fascinating. Professor Suri is really great one on one but in the larger class she focused more on facilitating discussion than really leading it. It would have been nice to have a slightly more lecture component to the course, or some bullet points or a hand out to guide things and make sure we hit main points. Having computers out in class also meant that a lot of people were zoning out and the discussion never felt like it had everyone locked in. The assignments were fun and creative, although it would have been nice to have slightly more scaffolding and a clearer communication of expectations, especially for the final project. Overall, Professor Suri was really knowledgeable and the class was very interesting.
If you are at all interested in reproductive technologies, histories, ethics, or medicine this course is a fantastic way to discuss those topics.
Professor Suri, while perfectly qualified, seemed to insist that we not learn anything from her. She ran every class by asking the same reading comprehension questions while saying as little as possible. Because Professor Suri never lectured or gave any context on the readings, I walked out of each class knowing as much as when I walked in. The understructured discussion made it difficult to participate and allowed certain students to dominate the classroom. The points students made were obviously directed at maintaining a high participation grade, and it felt like no one was really listening to each other because we weren't learning anything. I was expecting this class to be a highlight of my college experience and was extremely let down. I'm sure that Professor Suri will be a great teacher one day, but she will have to start teaching!
This is a wonderful course! 100 – 150 pages of reading per week, and the more of the readings you do, the more you'll get out of this course! Grading and flexibility on assignemnts are generous and much appreciated! The readings cover a broad scope, are challenging, and exciting to read!
VERY interesting course, and would highly recommend if you're at all interested in reproductive justice, OBGYN, pro–choice movements, birthing practices, or history of eugenics. The readings are not too bad (some are a little long but tbh most people were skimming them), and the assignments + discussions are interesting and worth it. You will get from this course what you put in, so I definitely recommend really trying to think about the readings deeply and engaging with them. One note: If you're very interested in talking about abortion or birth control in terms of *contemporary* debates, this is not necessarily what you're looking for––we only spend two weeks on abortion and all of these topics are very much approached through the lenses of capitalism, colonialism, etc. However, doing that is quite interesting, and Professor Suri is so kind and interesting to talk to as well!
This is a great course! Dr. Suri is a super kind and generous instructor and the dicussions were engaging. The assignment load was super manageable. There is quite a bit of reading but the readings were all very interesting.
If this course is offered again, definitely take it! Amazing readings and. you get to discuss niche and very interesting topics including history of abortion restrictions, mass IUD campaigns, IVF, and more generally a history of reproductive justice. There are some readings which take a global approach which is an added plus. Most of the readings are very accessible. Reading is slightly on the heavy side for some weeks but overall is very manageable.
The readings can be lengthy but are all extremely interesting. You leave the class with new perspectives that are delightfully applicable to more of your life than you would think. Dr. Suri is incredible and a masterful guider of conversations. She is very approachable and gives good feedback for project ideas, she makes an active effort to check in with everyone which is always appreciated. Take the class!
