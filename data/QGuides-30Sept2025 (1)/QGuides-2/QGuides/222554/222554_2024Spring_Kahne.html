Take this class! I had a wonderful semester with Sien, Dan, and Franky, as well as with all of my peers. This class is very well orchestrated and a wonderful crash course in what you need to know about research, reading scientific articles, and thinking curiously!
If you think you are interested in science and research but don't have much experience, take this class! Instructors are outstanding, and you will learn a lot from them.
This class is a very good introduction to the world of research and scientific papers. The work itself isn't difficult, but you get out what you put in. I would recommend it to those who are brand new to research, as it is an excellent way to be introduced to scientists and the work that they do. It makes research less intimidating, and if they don't change it, it also offers a summer program, FUEL, that teaches many chemistry and biotech lab techniques, as well as scientific poster/presentation skills. Also, the teaching team is fantastic!
TAKE THIS COURSE! Not only will you have some of the best mentors available for first–years, but you'll have a much easier time getting summer lab opportunities.
Future Students: TAKE THIS SEMINAR! Dan and Siene are so great and if you do not have research experience, everyone is on the same playing field so you will not feel excluded in any way. This course is very relaxing and you will have very cool guest speakers come in who have done amazing things. In fact, you can talk to them right after they give their presentations! I highly recommend this seminar for anyone who is hesitant about research and wants to obtain the confidence to do further research in their desired field.
Take this seminar! Dan, Sien, and Dilek are amazing and do a great job of teaching this course. The workload is very light and in class, you basically just talk about the weekly readings and discuss/debate what we're learning that day. It is an amazing environment to be in and a nice break from all the pset classes, plus you end up really close to a bunch of people in the seminar because of the small class size.
Embrace the chalk talk and practice it more than once or twice beforehand, also be open to have your views changed and don't be afraid to speak up
I highly recommend taking the seminar, the environment is so welcoming and open to different kinds of people and gives insight into what doing scientific research entails. There are weekly readings, but they are very manageable and don't cause too much stress to complete in time. If you are curious about whether or not you want to do research in the future and want to learn in a low–stakes environment, I recommend taking this seminar. You'll have the opportunity to freely learn more about research, the process/mindset of being a researcher, and overall the types of research that can be done here on campus or elsewhere. The seminar is very engaging and worth participating in, and the final project for the course is very doable and allows you to practice interpreting information and relaying that info to others.
If you're interested in research, apply to this seminar! ESPECIALLY if you have never done research in a formal setting before. Everyone in this seminar is so nice and approachable (shoutout to Prof. Dilek). I can't think of a better environment in Harvard where you're free to ask any question you have in your mind about research. Make the most out of the guests that present their projects– they're nice people and you should definitely reach out to them!
TAKE THIS CLASS! It is truly a gem.
Although this class was taught for the first time, I thought it was extremely well–structured and highly fulfilling. The teaching team was great, and all of them endorsed open discussion. I highly recommend taking this class if you are unsure on how to start with research and want initial exposure!
This course was very well structured and a great introduction into the world of science research. Dan and Sien are some of the BEST instructors at Harvard and really prompt you to think (instead of just telling you what to know). We got to speak to some amazing researchers, one of the most notable being a researcher who is about to change the worlds energy consumption as we know it. We additionally discussed the ethics of research and even had a great class on gender bias in the world of STEM. 10/10 course, I would recommend it to anyone looking to learn more about doing and performing research!!!
Really interesting professors, listened to a lot of really interesting Harvard faculty talk. Dan and Sien really want to help you find ways to pursue your passions and are very knowledgeable in doing so. Great, interesting course with little outside work
