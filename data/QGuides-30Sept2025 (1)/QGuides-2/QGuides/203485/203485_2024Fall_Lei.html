Take this course! Professor Lei is amazing and is so well educated. You get to hear from a lot of guest speakers who are renowned in their fields and have a lot to offer. There is minimal work; just the typical reading memos for a sociology class. I learned a ton, the readings are digestible, and section was great. Easy and enjoyable class!
Professor Lei is a great instructor, and she is very understanding to her students. She wants nothing but the best for any student she has, and I could tell that from the jump. The assignments are very manageable week to week; the discussion posts are not bad at all, and the readings are pretty interesting. Some of the readings are extremely long, and they can be a hassle if they are assigned for a singular day between lectures (i.e., Wednesday between Tuesday and Thursday classes). If you are not interested in law, this class will not be a relatively good time filler/requirement filler for you, as understanding the readings is needed for the midterms and the final assignment. Participation (both in lecture and section) is also a huge element of this course, and it is the largest component of the grade. I love talking, so this helped me, but keep this in mind if you do not.

There were some perceived issues with the grading of the exams this semester, which is handled by the TF. It definitely seemed a lot harsher compared to previous years' Q–guides (although I am a first–year, so I may just be bad at interpreting things). From my vantage point, I had lost points on midterms for responses that had value in them, and I was actually able to earn points back after discussing an instance of this with the TF. I do not think she was actively trying to hurt people's scores, but I did notice a harsh undertone in the amount of points taken off when looking at the mistake. Overall, I recommend this class. I would just remember you may need to speak up for yourself at times and participate in lecture to score well.
Very interesting and easy class for anyone interested in the law. Professor Lei brings a background (former Taiwanese judge) that allows her to approach the law from a lot of different perspectives. The class can move slowly at times and two midterms and a final isn't ideal (although they are very very manageable if you study) but overall a worthwhile experience for Socio concentrators or pre–law people.

Professor Lei is a great teacher; she is so kind and welcoming (knows everyone's names!) and prepares thoroughly for each class. I looked forward to lecture every week, and section too as Bernadette was a really thoughtful TF who cultivated a friendly and supportive atmosphere in section.

Would definitely recommend this course, especially for pre–law students! This course helped me to understand law from a societal lens, which is a crucial foundation to have for a comprehensive picture about law from various perspectives. The readings are especially interesting and incorporate many real–world examples of law in application. The course has a broad, interdisciplinary curriculum, going over not only social theories and legal structures but also social inequalities, dispute processes, technology, and the legal profession too! Workload is not too much, and as long as you take time to fully understand the readings and lectures, you will get good grades on the mid–terms! Professor Lei is also incredibly kind and knowledgeable, and guest lectures were wonderful too. I would definitely recommend taking this course for any social science students!
