As Americans, we usually here a very one–sided story about Iran, the Islamic Revolution, and even Islam in general. This class was wonderful in that it gave us context into why events like the Islamic Revolution occurred, which really humanized Iranians as people just like you and me. I believe this is especially important in a class on the Middle East, as people in the region are regularly dehumanized and belittled in our American political discourse.
Enrolling in the History of Modern Iran and Turkey course was a profoundly enriching experience that provided me with a comprehensive understanding of the historical trajectories of both nations. The course structure allowed for an in–depth exploration of political, cultural, and socio–economic developments, with the instructor's engaging teaching style making the lectures both informative and thought–provoking. An admirable aspect of the course was its commitment to offering cross–cultural perspectives, fostering a nuanced comprehension of how historical events shaped Iran and Turkey differently. The comparative analysis not only deepened my understanding of the region's complexities but also highlighted the relevance of historical events to current affairs.
