This class is one of the gened gems that you are looking for – especially if you want to learn more about the intersection of different forms of text with their importance – how they are transmitted through history, the way books came about, how publications became the norm in modern day publishing industry, and doing so with the focus of a text. It's just so thought–provoking, intellectually revitalizing, and engaging. Definitely recommend!

the teachers are super nice and knowledgable about the field and honestly the TFs are soo nice and helpful! Super interactive and interesting class and really enjoyed the visits to the museums and especially the one to the Pussey library (super interesting)!
I'd say definitely take it if you get in! As far as the HSI requirement goes, this is a really interesting and fun class to fulfill it with. I loved the field trips –– the ones to the archives and preservation center were big highlights of my semester. The professors are also clearly very passionate about what they're teaching, and it's a well–structured class.

I knew before going in that I was interested in the topic, but the class does a great job of introducing and synthesizing different perspectives and approaches to textual transmission. Pick a text you're interested in for the project, since you have to work on it for the whole semester –– I really enjoyed working on mine, but the assignments can take a surprising amount of time. Minor cons: a few of the lectures are a bit boring and disconnected from the general flow of the class, and if the other people at your table aren't engaging then the in–class exercises aren't super enjoyable. But the final presentation week was a great end to the semester –– everyone's projects were so different and so interesting. Overall it's a very solid GENED!

Definitely a fun class. Every day it was super enjoyable and enjoyed section just as much.
This course is wonderfully taught and structured. The tag team approach from Professor Blair and Professor Whittington allows for equal insight into the literary and historical dimensions of textual preservation and transmission. The weekly assignments build very nicely into the final paper. By the time you get to the end of the semester, you are an expert in the text that you are going to write about, and not only does this lessen the load of the final paper, it makes the capstone projects and presentation a truly meaningful experience. If you are at all interested in history and literature, this is the class to take. There is so much diversity of topics and materials explored (from Sappho to Dickinson, Montaigne to Shakespeare) that you will always find something to love in the weekly readings (which are nonzero but completely manageable). Grading is fair and TF's are as passionate about the material as the professors.
I really like the topics covered in the class and the activities and places we go to every week!
This is a really good course as far as GenEds go. The professors clearly care a lot about the material, and put a lot of effort into making the course run smoothly. The TFs this year (especially Manny) were also amazing. I found grading to be reasonable and very fair. The course itself covers the history of textual creation, transmission, and preservation over thousands of years, so certain portions of the course are naturally much more interesting than others. I'd definitely recommend having at least some interest in the material if you choose to enroll, since the frequent field trips and in–depth discussion sections might start to feel tedious if you aren't engaged.
In terms of assignments, most weeks involved submitting a short response paper which built to the final project. Some of these felt a lot like busy work (which was especially inconvenient given that they were due on Fridays at midnight). That said, final project is extremely open–ended, so you could get a lot out of it if you choose to work on something meaningful to you.

The weekly assignments are fun and really help with the final! Great opportunity to do a semester–long research project on a topic of your choice while still fulfilling a GenEd requirement.
Wonderful class! The history of texts was really interesting to learn, especially in relation to how those texts are engaged with in the modern era and how the stories of the texts are relevant to modern questions of preservation and conservation. They build up to the final project so working on that is also not very difficult because a good portion of the work has already been done. The field trips were really cool and a great opportunity to go to so many different parts of Harvard that I had never been to before. All in all a great class, I'd take it again.
