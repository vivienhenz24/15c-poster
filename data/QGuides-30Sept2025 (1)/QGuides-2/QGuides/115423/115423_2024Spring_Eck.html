Diana Eck is truly a gift to the world and it breaks my heart this was her last semester teaching the class <3
I wish you could take this! This is one of the most impactful classes I have ever taken at Harvard. Professor Eck is not just a giant in her field and a spectacular scholar, but a truly kind and genuine person who I will always be grateful to have learned from. This class was simply incomparable.
This class stands out as one of the most disappointing experiences I've had at Harvard. While the emphasis on personal and religious exchanges among students during class and section sessions initially seemed promising, the disconnect between these discussions and the essay assignments is glaringly problematic. Despite the engaging class dynamic, the essay prompts are excessively ambiguous, and the grading criteria provided bear little resemblance to the actual grading standards. Unbeknownst to students, the expectation seems to be to craft serious philosophical papers referencing academic sources that are never explored in class. This incongruity between the lighthearted nature of the class discussions and the rigorous demands of the essay assignments is deeply frustrating. For instance, in one section session, we found ourselves engaging in fortune–telling activities, further underscoring the disparity between the course's tone and its academic expectations. Overall, the lack of clarity in expectations, coupled with the harsh grading, renders this class profoundly unsatisfactory. I advise against taking this classs unless one is specifically seeking this unusual combination of elements. It certainly ranks as the most disappointing class I've taken at Harvard.
