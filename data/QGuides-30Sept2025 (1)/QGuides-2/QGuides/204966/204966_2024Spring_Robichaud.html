Take this class!!!
I'm not sure if they're offering this course again but if they are and you're interested in how truth is made, why it can feel like people no longer accept facts, whether people really disbelieve science, and whether misinformation is a problem (and you need a Ethics/Civics gened), then definitely take it. Total gem in the way where you can still learn a lot without spending too much time on it. Only assignments are relatively light (but super interesting!) readings and a takehome midterm and final that I found pretty fun.

I came into this class extremely skeptical of the idea of post–truth, disinformation, etc., but now I'm more skeptical of the B.S. parts and less skeptical of the serious issues (in my view lol). And there is a lot of BS: at times the lectures can feel a bit theatrical. But those ones are at least still entertaining. The readings also give a lot of different perspectives, so you can really critically examine the issues. The only annoying part is the last few weeks of the class on intellectual virtues. I hope they get rid of that next time.
i think this course represents the core ethos of GENED –– it teaches us about pertinent issues in the world around us and importantly confronts us with introspection into our own thought habits. i feel like this is one of the few classes where my academic commitment translates into personal development. especially in a semester rife with controversies about speech and disagreements, i have learnt to listen not to advocate or speak, but listen to really understand how to disagree more respectfully.
Excellent class! Chris is one of the best lecturers and teachers I've ever had.
This course was AMAZING!! If it gets offered again in the future, please take it (but Chris implied that he may be taking a break from teaching this particular course for some time). This course succeeds on so many fronts and I think this iteration matched exactly what expectations of a GENED should be –– the workload is low and never suffocating, but you will learn a ton if you put in the engagement. Chris's brilliant philosophical insights never seem to cease, and if you pay attention in lecture you will come out the other end of this course a better person with a significantly better understanding of the world and some of the problems we face in fake news, conspiracy theories, and getting people to pursue the truth (on both sides of the political aisle). Also, go to office hours!!

Great Gened! Low workload, you really just have to go to lectures. Personally I didn't find the material super interesting, but if you are interested in post–truth era problems I'd highly recommend.
This course is definitely worth taking! I found myself having a greater appreciation of the world around me after taking this class, perhaps in spite of the post–truth era it describes in its first third.
AMAZING COURSE!!! I learned so much about the role of truth in society and politics, and even more about myself. You will come out a changed person, older, sadder, wiser, and also a better thinker and knowledge seeker. Not too much work; readings can be long but very interesting, and midterm and final projects are fun activities.
Amazing class. Highly recommend it, and I hope it is offered again soon. Professor Robichaud was not only an amazing lecturer but also did a fantastic job facilitating discussion in class. The atmosphere in the classroom was something I hadn't really experienced in most of my other classes––just about everyone actually wanted to and was willing to discuss the course content as a class. The course was not only a good dive into the post–truth era but also served as a basic introduction to philosophy, which I personally enjoyed. Since 80% of the course's grade was the midterm/final those two assignments were a bit stressful, but other than that I really, really enjoyed this course.
