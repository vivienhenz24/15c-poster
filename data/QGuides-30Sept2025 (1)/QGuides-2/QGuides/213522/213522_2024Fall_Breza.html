Incredibly well done course with amazing teaching, great structure, fair work and grading, and insightful assignments. Definitely take this course for all the reasons listed before!
It is important to stay on top of assignments and studying for exams because there is a lot of content that you essentially have to understand but also memorize. Also, each assignment holds a lot of weight and the best way to be successful is either going to section or reviewing section notes. Emily and Yifan are super responsive to any concerns so stay in contact with them if you have any questions.
So much to say about this course! First, I'll say that Emily and Yifan are both incredibly kind and were super accommodating throughout the semester, they also were both great instructors. Psets were appropriately challenging and felt very relevant to lecture while also prepping for the midterm and final which I also found to be very fair. The content was genuinely so interesting, and Emily generates a lot of enthusiasm for the subject matter. The only thing to be wary of is that the course does have a 10 page paper in addition to a final, which I wish had been administered before reading period, but I overall had a great experience in this course
I think take this class with some pre–caution. It's not bad course, but because it is such a small class, there is not much support. The Pset's can be difficult because of wording, so I would advise going to office hours and finding a pset buddy. She is really interested in the content, and you can tell, so if you really want to learn, do not hold back from taking this class. Exams are not that bad and graded pretty fairly. If you can spend time and effort in this class, you will do good!
There's not many courses that feature emerging markets heavily, and as someone who is interested in the space, I still found it quite useful. Professor Breza covers a lot of products, and I enjoyed the case studies
The content of this course is super interesting – you learn a lot about the current state of economics research, and although the focus is on developing countries, it's good information to have as someone living in a developed country as well. The workload also wasn't too bad – problem sets were a manageable length and reading responses are pretty interesting. My only complaint is that homework was sometimes worded poorly, feedback is slow and sometimes not clear as to why points were deducted, but the course staff are usually pretty flexible when any problems come up.
The stata required for this class is easy to catch onto. You should take it if you're interested even if you don't have much experience in that regard. There's a lot of support in this class, and the section leader, Yifan, is literally the GOAT.
I would highly recommend this class! The coursework is manageable alongside other classes, and the information is really interesting. We learned a lot about how economics applies to small businesses in these countries, which provides an important perspective that other Harvard classes dont offer. Its interesting to see how fintech and venture capital are being creative in developing countries to formulate solutions to many of the issues they face.
Excellent course –– covered broad ranging topics and very recent field research. Emily and Yifan both are great teachers, very kind, and understanding. Highly recommend as an ec elective!
This class was one of the most impactful classes out of all that I've done in economics, and Prof Breza's lectures are great.
This course deals with how financial markets affect countries without robust financial systems. The lectures are very dense (just words on sides basically) but many of the topics covered are interesting. Everything is graded fairly thought the final was a little harder than I thought.
This class had a good balance of assignments as an elective class. I am a STEM concentrator who had only taken ec 10a and 10b (and ec 50 but that's not needed) before this class and I would say that is really all of the preparation you need. The concepts are interesting and the class goes through following the results of influential studies which was an interesting method. It is lecture based but it was a small lecture, and not recorded so you need to go to class. The reading response assignments and homeworks were all very manageable and well spread out. For us, the final and final paper were due two weeks apart thanks to the final being on December 20, but I could see those two adding a lot of work at the end if they were closer. Other than that, I felt this class as a good balance of fair expectations, interesting learning, and time balance throughout the semester. I highly recommend taking if you are interested in the topic of finance of developing countries at all because it is a really interesting perspective on where our world is going with the implementation of technology to improve lives in developing countries.
Although this course was quite difficult for those who did not have any background knowledge in economics and statistics, it was quite rewarding. For those interested in learning about developing economies this course is for you.
This class is a solid Econ elective. The lectures aren't always super engaging but if you choose to opt in and engage, the content can be interesting. Professor Breza is very thoughtful and super helpful at office hours.
