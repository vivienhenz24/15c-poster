What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I would recommend this course to any student of architecture and/or architectural history even if your primary area of interest or focus does not specifically concern the architecture of twentieth–century Europe and America.
Don't be too concerned if you can't answer all of Patricio's questions in class (some for them are intentionally polemical).
This course was excellent. The professor is passionate, funny, and engaged. If you want to examine history and theory of architecture in this specific era, I highly encourage you to enroll.
Have a general idea of all readings prior to class, it is a seminar so discussion is a very important part of it.
