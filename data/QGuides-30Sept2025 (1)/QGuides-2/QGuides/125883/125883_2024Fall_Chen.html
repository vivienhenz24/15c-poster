What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is a great class if you are interested in cosmology.
Solid course that was elevated by the presence of Xingang, who is without a doubt one of the best professors in the astronomy department here. Take anything he teaches!!
A must–take for astrophysics concentrators! There's a disparity in difficulty between the lectures and the homeworks/exams (the latter being easier), which creates a wonderful opportunity to learn those topics that you've always been enchanted by but thought you weren't smart enough to study (the big bang, inflation, dark energy, etc.). Professor Chen's lectures are thoughtful and crafted with care, but you do most of your learning by reading the lecture notes. Our TFs put a ton of work into typing up the lectures and detailing every equation and concept you need to know. Don't simply skate by, though, because you'll get a lot out of this class if you're fully engaged.
This is an excellent course taught/supported by a great lecturer and great TFs! The course content is conceptually challenging but (like any astro class) extremely interesting, and the psets are very reasonable. If you are an astro concentrator or even a STEM person looking for a good elective, this'll be right up your alley. Cosmology is a deeply interesting subject (with a lot of open questions), and with this course you are really able to immerse yourself in all that we do not yet know. As an astrophysics concentrator doing cosmology research, I found that this class not only greatly enhanced the concepts central to my research, but also reaffirmed my love for cosmology!
This was a fascinating course. Although I hardly internalized anything we talked about, the content is so niche and you won't learn about this stuff elsewhere. One downside of this course is that its almost entirely a math lecture. Every class is an expansion of math/physics from the week before, and it can be difficult to pay attention always/keep up because it's relatively mundane. However, once we tie the math back to the physical picture, it is a lot easier to make sense of. The first PSET was a bit of a jumpscare, but the future PSETs have more to do with lecture. The teaching staff provides very extensive and helpful lecture notes that will guide you through the PSETs, and the textbook often covers a lot of the same questions in different fonts. Also, the TFs are wonderful! Please go to to their office hours. And Xingang is so knowledgeable and kind! Midterm/final matched PSET difficulty level/content.
