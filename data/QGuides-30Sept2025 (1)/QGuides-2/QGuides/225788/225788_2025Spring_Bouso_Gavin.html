PLEASE TAKE THIS CLASS!!! Aitor is such an amazing teacher and person who not only cares about you as a student, but as a human being!! He is genuinely concerned with your wellness and your ability to engage with the course materials. This course was genuinely so fun and I learned more than I have in many courses here. Even if you have no background in Latinx studies, he does a great job introducing you to core concepts and making sure you are able to participate. The best part of this class is that it can also be used in your personal life –– showing you different ways to engage with art for the purposes of healing, both yourself and your community.
It is such a great class! Aitor and everyone in this class was super open to learning and discussing, it was my favorite class this semester!
Take the class!! Youll learn so much Latinx theory, which is incredibly rare at Harvard, through a very accessible approach. Aitor is an amazing instructor and will make sure youre successful in the course but will also push you to challenge yourself.
Do the readings with ample time so you can really connect with the material. Some weeks I would skim and wouldn't be able to contribute to the discussion in class as much as I would've liked. Professor Bouso–Gavin is a very student–oriented professor and is there for you, so make sure to verbalize and communicate your difficulties (which is something I failed to do) so the class can be as enjoyable as possible.
Just because something is not "traditional" does not necessarily mean that it is bad. Spirituality can teach us so much about ourselves and the world that people should be open–minded. That said, please don't co–opt peoples ways of being and livelihood for academic aesthetics. It's not cool or fun but extremely disrespectful and harmful. While we learn we have to credit those that are stewards of the knowledge systems that exists and have existed.
This is an amazing course that provides a safe and engaging space to discuss crucial issues affecting Latinx communities. It was incredibly impactful learning about how my own ways of thinking were entrenched in colonial frameworks and studying different approaches to healing colonial wounds and centering the mind–body–spirit. I cannot recommend the course enough!
Aitor is such a wonderful professor, the class is so good, the readings are doable every week, and the knowledge is invaluable!
