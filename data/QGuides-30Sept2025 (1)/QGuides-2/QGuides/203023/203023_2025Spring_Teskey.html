Extremely easy class; all it requires is reading one book of Paradise Lost per week. A couple short, easy papers.
The lectures are very weak. Teskey clearly does not prepare prior to lectures and, while an expert on the topic, what that leads to is lengthy nearly–meaningless meandering with very little purpose or information transmitted. Manan is a great TF, though, and the book itself is very good.
Some of the theological concepts presented in this class may appear intimidating––as can Milton's language. But Dr. Teskey makes this course highly accessible. You will move through the coursework at a reasonable pace that allows you to appreciate Milton more deeply.
If you love language, take this course. If you love theology, take this course. It is not too much work and you get a ton out of it.
Take this class. Gordon Teskey is an unbelievable professor, the lectures were works of art. I cannot recommend Paradise Lost enough. This is a class on the greatest poem to ever come out of the west, taught by one of the greatest professors in the English department. You would miss some thing of inordinate value by not taking this class.

My only warning would be that Professor Teskey and John Milton are not theologically consistent with the Orthodox Christian understanding of the Bible or the world. Nevertheless, still a class worth taking.
I really liked this class! Teskey can get a little repetitive, but by the end you feel like you really understand his ideas. Milton's poetry can be hard at first but is truly so beautiful and often prescient. A passage anticipating anti–Natalism, for instance, felt timely.
This class was a highlight of college. Relaxed environment to think deeply about questions about humanity, fate, justice. Plus read great poetry.
This is an amazing class! As a stem concentrator, this was my first english class, and I'm so glad I went out of my comfort zone here. The class has a low workload outside of reading a chapter of Paradise Lost each week, which gets significantly easier as you get accustomed to Milton's poetry. And the memorization ended up being really fun, too. I really enjoyed the content of the epic itself and also now feel much better equipped to try out tough texts on my own.
This is an incredible course! Professor Teskey is wonderful and so knowledgeable. Take it!
I loved this course! Would definitely recommend. Professor Teskey is a preeminent scholar of Milton and his expertise on and love for Paradise Lost is clear. This is a text that begins popping up everywhere you reading has commenced and you begin to see how foundational the story of Adam and Eve, especially as recounted in Paradise Lost, is to western society. A very worthwhile class!
This is a foundational class in Paradise Lost. If you have any interest in the poem I highly recommend taking it, as it is a very expansive and detailed look at Milton and his work.
Take it! Amazing lectures and fascinating discussions and just an amazing book everyone should read.
