This is a great class if you like information–heavy, fun fact–oriented teaching (which I do). Lots of interesting stuff in there and it really gets packed in––I think I took like two to three pages of typed notes every day, just trying to hold on to all the interesting information going by. Not a ton of discussion which you might've hoped to see in such a small class, but there is designated time for it, and assignments felt relatively purposeful and creative.
Do the readings! So many are fun with or without context and seeing them how they would have been seen when published is a joy to explore.
doable class, standard history class! my first hsitory class and i enjoyed it. interesting lectures
I would definitely recommend this course! Flynn Cratty gives good lectures and is a great professor overall. The workload is light, yet I still feel like I learned a lot about British history. There is one in class midterm, an in–person final, and a final group project (which ended up being pretty fun to work on).
This is an exceptional course at Harvard. It was my first one in the history department, and I found it to be well run and fascinating. Cratty is a fantastic lecturer, one of the best I have had at Harvard. The work for this course is reasonable but still informative, and I leave feeling like I have a much better understanding of an indispensable period of history. An irreplaceable course!
