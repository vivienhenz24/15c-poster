The readings can be dense at times, but they are all super interesting and completely worth it. Learned something new every class. Truly cannot recommend this class enough!!
This class is so interesting and it was one of those classes that really changed my worldview! Professor Ternullo is so great, and I learned so much from her, both about place and about how to write a good research paper. There is a lot of reading, but I found it really interesting and it was all relevant to our class discussions. Take this class, you won't regret it!!
This is a great seminar! Professor Ternullo is extremely good at facilitating conversation. The topics of this seminar have something for everyone, and the readings are super interesting!
This class is fantastic and the readings prompt very interesting class discussions. I really enjoyed learning about this topic and the readings built upon a lot of ideas I have always wondered about. Loved this class!
Take the class!!! This is a great 94 and while difficult, I found it extremely rewarding. If you put in the work, by doing the readings and engaging in discussion, you will learn so much about the history of the United States in relation to inequalities. This class really gave me the details to issues that I have always known about but not necessarily studied. If you are even slightly interested in how place can impact people and vice versa, I think you will enjoy the majority of this class. Also, start the final project immediately after the midterm. You will need more time than you think.
This was definitely my favorite class of the semester and also my favorite Gov class I've ever taken! While my other Gov courses have also been interesting, it was easy to zone out a lot of times, but Stephanie made this class soo engaging and my head was in the game for the whole two hours every week. All of the readings were so fascinating and probably some of the most insightful and interesting content I've engaged with during my time at Harvard. You really get what you put in. If you do all of the readings, seminars are so fun to participate in, and Stephanie is also super purposeful about incorporating everyone's personal experiences, which I thought was really relevant and a good way to emphasize the subjectivities that come with studying politics and place.
This class introduced many significant and essential subjects such as red–lining, gentrification, and how politics affect place and how place affects politics. If you're really interested in urban, suburban, and rural studies and the intersectionality of this with race, this is the class to take. The readings were a lot compared to other courses I've taken; sometimes, we wouldn't be able to discuss all of them during section together each week.
TAKE IT! so interesting and professor ternullo is AMAZING, so knowledgeable & passionate about the material, just be aware that the reading load is REALLY hefty so prepare accordingly
Ternullo is a LOVELY professor with tons of infectious passion, knowledge, & enthusiasm for the subject material. Really enjoyed leveraging interview methods for the first time for my research paper (which can be a heavy lift, so I recommend getting as much of the substantive analysis/results done over Thanksgiving break as possible). Learned lots about segregation, concentrated advantage/disadvantage, the intersection of partisanship and place, and tons of other topics highly relevant to every other area of political science in America
This is a great course, and certainly worth taking for all those interested in political behavior or American politics – the only asterisk is keep in mind that this course has a higher workload, so avoid taking this at the same time as other high workload courses
