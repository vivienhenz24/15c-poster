What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Overview

I enjoyed French 50, though I personally preferred French 40 (which focused on films). This course explores language and rights, and the shift in topic made it feel a bit more intense.

Structure & Workload

Small class size (14 students) = highly interactive discussions
No exams, just 5 short vocab quizzes (lowest dropped)
3 main writing assignments—each with a pre–write, in–class draft, revision, and oral activity
Final project: create a manifesto on a social issue of your choice
Dossiers are back (thanks to Dr. Turman), which makes the class feel familiar if you’ve taken French 20

What to Expect

Tight deadlines: pre–writes and Dossiers are due before in–class drafts
Feedback comes within a week, and you’ll have another week for revision + an oral submission
Quizzes often fall between drafts and revisions, so time management is key

Final Thoughts

This class is more demanding than earlier French courses, especially with back–to–back deadlines. Balancing it with heavy STEM classes (like orgo and stats) was tough, but I gained confidence in both my writing and speaking skills—and that made it worth it.
This is a course where you get out what you put in. Definitely spend the time working through your assignments and taking the feedback into account.
