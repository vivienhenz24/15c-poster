This class has long homework assignments but they are doable if you work with others and start early.
Very nice course with focus on thermodynamics and material science aspects of battery technology
Xin Li is very passionate about batteries and is so knowledgable in the topic because his lab focuses on solid state batteries. I learned a lot in this class about batteries, their common problems, comparisons between different types of batteries, their varying applications, why patents are important, etc. I found it very very interesting as someone who is interested in energy and engineering. It is a cool introductory class to a large industry and is approachable to undergrads.
Amazing class, I highly recommend it. The exams are quite manageable but there is always a tricky question.
If you're passionate about energy transition and want to actually take a class with some substance – go for it!
Take this class even if you do not know anything about electrochemistry!
Take this class to learn about batteries and materials. Chill class as long as you keep up with assignments and don't wait until last day to do them.
A do–able course for undergrads, if not the most engaging course you'll ever take. If you want to learn about batteries and electrochemistry, it's not a bad option. Professor Li knows what he's talking about and his lectures are pretty good. If you're not interested in battery or energy technology, it can be hard to stay motivated; some self–motivation is required if you're an undergrad because there are fewer, more heavily weighted assignments making it easy to fall behind on material if you skip lectures or zone out.
This class is for students who have some or no experience with batteries and interested in studying more about this field. The homework might take some time to complete and it is helpful for understanding the lecture material.
Take this class! Prof. Li is an excellent professor, and this course is very accessible for undergraduates who are interested in energy and energy materials but have a difficult time finding udnergrad–level courses suitable for the subject matter. Nothing is too difficult, but a lot of the theoretical bases of battery systems are covered in the course.
