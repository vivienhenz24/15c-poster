Take any class Elena Glassman ever teaches. Period. End of discussion. Not only is she the undeniable GOAT of HCI, but most importantly she treats her students as colleagues and encourages ideas and preconceptions to be challenged and questioned while keeping an open mind. If you're someone who has a strong background in AI/ML, UI/UX, and HCI (which includes experience reading conference papers), there is absolutely not a single reason to not take this class. If you're an undergrad, the final project is going to give you a lot more freedom than you're likely used to, and you're expected to manage your progress completely outside of class over the course of the semester, but as long as you've got a solid project group you'll be all set. I know everyone says this about every class, but you'll honestly get out of this course what you put into it. Do the readings every night, and be active on Perusall. Participate in class discussions (at least within breakout groups if you're not comfortable sharing your thoughts in front of a class), and you will find yourself gleaning so many more insights from the papers than otherwise possible. I don't know what else to say––Elena is awesome, Catherine is the best TF––just take this class!

Absolutely FANTASTIC class. Papers are extremely well–chosen and engaging, and Perusall discussions break the mold of other courses by sparking genuinely interesting conversations and providing valuable design insights. Elena is a marvelous professor and really helped guide in–class discussions without commandeering them entirely, leaving students (everyone does at least one presentation on one of the papers assigned during the semester) to initiate and fuel discussion, interpretation, and critique of the papers and studies assigned. The course project can seem daunting, but Elena and Catherine provide extremely helpful feedback along the way via multiple optional (and one mandatory) checkpoints, and are always willing to discuss and provide feedback on ideas or concerns. Would recommend this class a thousand times, could not have asked for a more engaging and thought provoking experience.
This is an incredible course and you should absolutely take it. I have learned so much not just about HCI, but about the future of technology and modern life. Each week we would have really interesting discussions in class and I learned as much from these as from the readings. Professor Glassman is an incredible professor who is deeply engaged in the course and so knowledgeable.
