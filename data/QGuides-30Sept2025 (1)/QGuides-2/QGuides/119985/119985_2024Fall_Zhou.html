Even with a basic quantitative backgroundincluding linear algebra, derivatives, partial derivatives, and probabilitiesyou might still find this course challenging. However, if you are serious about gaining a deeper, more theoretical (mathematical) understanding of regression, I would highly recommend it.
If youre interested in learning about linear/logistic regression, this is your course. Prof. Zhou walks through these concepts pretty thoroughly and is happy to stop class if a concept needs further explanation.
Took it to fulfill applied math sociology. Wouldn't take it otherwise. The class is entirely 1st year PhDs apart from a handful of AM undergrads, and the lecture component is boring while section is unnecessary. The problem sets are very difficult to code, while the class is not a coding class.
Coming into this class, I didn't expect to get much out of it having taken prior computational / stats (at the 110 level) classes. But ultimately I found the class quite useful – learning R is great, and I felt like I learned much more about concepts like confidence intervals and statistical significance. The final project was actually really neat – this was my first time really writing up a formal research report and it felt very accessible given the methods we learn in the class. Psets are very reasonable, as are exams! In general if you're looking for something that's a step up from undergrad sociology quant classes, this was a great fit for me.
I took this class to satisfy the Applied Math sociology track, just to qualify my answer here. Every assignment in this class was more than fair: psets did not deviate from the content of the lectures (and were a fair bit less theoretical than the lectures actually), the midterm was open–note, and the final project was scaffolded and supported as much or as little as you want. The teaching staff (Prof Zhou and Jen–Chen, our TF) are both extremely competent and kind, and I felt like they were very invested in my success in the class, but at the same time not overbearing. This is the natural option for AM–sociology concentrators (obviously orders of magnitude less grueling than Stat 111, e.g.)
Aa lot of time devoted to linear regressions in this class. At first, I was skeptical, but after seeing it pop up again and again in papers I read outside of this class, I realized how useful it is to have a solid understanding of linear regression which is the workhorse of social science.

Xiang goes into a lot of the math intricacies, but you don't have to completely understand the deep math stuff to do well on the assignments and final which is very practical–oriented. Pretty easy if you have a good math background, harder if you don't and are more sociology based.
I definitely recommend this course! I feel that I learned a lot and feel much more comfortable with using RStudio and being a competent consumer of quantitative research. I especially better understand Linear, Multiple, and Logit Regression and can interpret coefficients and create calculations. If you are hoping to learn more about these concepts (and some other concepts like KOB Decomposition) and like to code or want to learn to code, I recommend this class. Xiang is a caring professor who goes above and beyond to support his students. This was definitely a time consuming course between the DataCamp, ProblemSets, Final Research Project, preparing for the exam, and more, but I feel that it was worth it and I learned a lot.
If you're an AM/sociology concentrator, 100% recommend taking this class!
I took this course to fulfill my application requirement for AM/SOC and would recommend it! The lectures are really interesting and the workload is not too bad with DataCamp assignments due at the beginning of the semester and then 6 psets due every week at the end of the semester. However, I will say the midterm was difficult and was graded pretty difficultly.
