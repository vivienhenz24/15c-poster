Regardless of how much you think you wrongly placed in Math Ma and should probably take a more advanced class, take it! This school has such a learning curve and being able to fill in the gaps in your learning with a class like this is extremely important. Additionally, the TFs and CAs are honestly so amazing!
If you don’t feel confident in your algebra/precalc abilities then taking Ma is a good idea. Highly recommend going to the MQC just to work with other students and CA’s, and it’s a great opportunity to make some friends/establish a work group in the class since the MQC is truly and experience. There’s a lot of integrated practice in the curriculum with workshops and simply the use of repetition to learn topics. Your learning experience can definitely differ based on the TF you have, but most of the time you’ll still get useful something out of the class. The class can get tedious at times with 3 psets a week, and meeting essential 4 times a week (3 classes, 1 workshop), but it’s nothing overly complicated.
– You have to learn the concepts yourself because section is not helpful. I recommend watching the Organic Chem Tutor and Khan Academy before or after section. Their videos will help you get the basics down.
– PSETs are complex so go to MQC or Office Hours for help.
– Worksheets are more helpful for the exams than the PSETs and Practice Exams. You'll see problems from the worksheets on the exam than from the actual practice exam that you're given.
– If you have major imposter syndrome, it is not you. It is the way the course is taught.
