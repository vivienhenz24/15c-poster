What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is an incredible class! Teju is awesome and very personable. One of my favorite classes I've ever taken at Harvard.
This has been one of my favorite classes at Harvard (and I have taken a lot of classes here). The structure of the class is very simple: you begin by reading and discussing published short stories for the first few weeks, before moving into discussing only student work. Everybody writes 2 stories, a short one and a longer one. But what makes the class so great is the depth with which you discuss each piece of writing. Teju gets you to intensely analyze everything that the author of each work does, and in doing so, you gain a very strong understanding of how to be a successful writer. Even when the work we were discussing wasn't my own, I felt that the lessons Teju and my classmates were conveying via their feedback/analysis to be very helpful to my writing. This was my first writing workshop, and I'm sorry I didn't take it sooner.
I would recommend any student from any background at Harvard to take a workshop course like this. Teju is a very talented writer and provides excellent feedback on your work and creates a great community of readers and writers in his workshops.
Prof Cole is a great professor and they do not need to be worried about embarassment or anything like that when they share their stories with him.
Familiarize yourself with Teju's work before the class! He really loves a certain kind of strangeness and has very strong ideas about student work. Take the class if that's what you want!
Prof. Cole has a unique perspective on writing, and his feedback is always interesting to hear. An issue I had was that he did not provide any written feedback, just verbal.
I was absolutely terrified before every class, but it definitely made me a better writer. Teju is very cool. My classmates were even cooler.
