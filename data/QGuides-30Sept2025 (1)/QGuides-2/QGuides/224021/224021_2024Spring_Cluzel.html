TAKE THIS CLASS. You will learn so much and think of living systems differently (the physics perspective is one that rarely anyone thinks of; I think you would be hard pressed to find a member of the MCB faculty who knows what an autocorrelation function is or who could interpret a power spectrum!)

You should be prepared to have your mind blown –– if you are an MCB concentrator, expect to have your head hurt after ever class because of the difficulty of the concepts. But midterms are given orally (with questions provided beforehand) so there is literally no stress there. And then the final is given on paper but very fair exam. Cluzel is also very fun. Would recommend without reservation.
One of the few classes here where I felt intellectually engaged and focused during the entire class. Small class and active style of learning fostered a good community, and in fact I wish there had been *more* psets so that we could've met up more and forged a stronger community. The professor is dedicated to his teaching and his research and is great to talk to.
This is a fantastic course! Philippe pushes us to engage with the advanced material, and we arrive at some really beautiful derivations throughout the course. The course also felt fairly interdisciplinary (connections to molecular biology, engineering, AI, computer science) while being deeply grounded in systems biology. Throughout the course, we had a really close knit community, and the vibe is very collaborative overall, which makes challenging problems and concepts so much more approachable.
Great course! I feel that I got more out of this class than what you see on the surface. Professor Cluzel is fantastic.
