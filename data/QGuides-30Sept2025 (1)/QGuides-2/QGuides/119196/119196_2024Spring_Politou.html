This was a difficult class but I had a better experience in this class than in Math 1B. I liked the teaching format a lot better and I think the material was more digestible. I would recommend this class as it was quite interesting and definitely has applications in many fields.
Be prepared to review lots of course material
Course can be very enjoyable if you are in a section with TFs, CAs, and peers that you vibe with! Ask questions, go to MQC, and meet with your TF outside of class if they offer! Eva (TF now but soon to be course head) is absolutely the most amazing and caring person I have met at the college so far. She has great plans for improving the course as well.
Your experience is ultimately determined by the TF and the people in your section! Going to math class honestly is something I look forward to everyday because I get to work with my friends! HOWEVER, psets and exams are pretty difficult, especially the exams. Ask for help, go to mqc, office hours, reach out to your TF. My TF was really nice, and was always willing to take out time to give me individual review lessons (which was really nice I LOVED). Try hard and know that you have to commit lots of time, but if you are prepared, just take this class and have fun!
As someone who took the 22 series in the fall semester, do yourself a favor and take the 21 series! There is so much more support and the small class format allowed me to know my instructor, classmates, and the content in a way I never stood a chance to in Math 22. The content is super interesting and presented in a very explainable way. If you don't understand something, there is the MQC and super accessible instructor office hours, so you can always get more support. The grading scheme is quite generous, so you will be fine! The workshops were often boring and useless, but I think they are changing the structure next semester.
I think this has been my favorite class at Harvard so far. So well thought and it builds on each topic extremely intuitively! Three psets per week seemed tiring but they're short enough you can finish each in an hour or so each, so not too bad!
Great class!! Loved the teaching style and all the support available. I previously took a multivariable calc class, but I learned so much more in Math 21A & truly gained a better understanding of what I was learning. Eva was also such a great TF!! Explained everything so well & was so nice
