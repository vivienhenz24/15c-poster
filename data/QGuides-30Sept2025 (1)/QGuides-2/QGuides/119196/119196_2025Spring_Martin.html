I think 21a is such a well–taught class, and you really come away with a deep understanding of what you're learning. You don't really have to do too much outside of class to grasp the material, and there are SO many resources to help you succeed (MQC is literally the best, and there are so many practice tests, etc. to do). If you're debating the 22 and 21 series like I was, having done both, I would strongly recommend 21 as I think the structure and resources is much better designed to help you succeed.
Go to OH and MQC for your psets––you can basically knock your psets out in a little over an hour if you go, plus you'll get a better understanding of the topic.
Make extra to check your psets with a peer or at MQC, little errors accumulate in this class. Also! Make a little sheet so you understand the different types of integrals, it's super critical that you are able to switch around between the theorems.
The teaching staff are super nice, but know that the quality of section varies based on TF (Amadeus is amazing!!).
3 psets a week are usually pretty manageable if you leave ~30–90 minutes for each of them. I found the grading quite generous. Yes, you do need to get the correct answers and explanations, but there are many opportunities to recover from a poor performance, like on a few (or many) psets or a midterm. Amadeus was a wonderful preceptor––he was super accessible and approachable, and made each class engaging (although we often went over a couple minutes).

Having taken Math22a previously, this course is much more structured so it forces you to stay on track. Keep in mind that the math in Math21a is not rigorous per say. I missed the mathematical rigor from 22, but I feel that I retained much more content from 21 because of its root in application.
Go to office hours. Find ones you can go to every week and stick to it. Also find a good group that you can work through psets with and study together. Do not fall behind or you will really struggle to catch up!
Stay on track and seek help early
Math 21a is my favorite math course I have ever taken! I learned so much about thinking and reasoning in 3–D space, and believe that the concepts covered in this course are applicable to many fields of study. The workload is manageable, and I found it helpful to study and do p–sets with classmates. I highly recommend Math 21a!
It's definitely not as bad as people say. That being said make sure you actually understand why things work the way they do. The concepts build gradually on top of each other, so if you have that understanding makes the class very doable, if you don't I can see how it can be very hard to stay on top of things. The 3 PSets can be a lot because there is always something to do for the class, but they also make sure that you stay engaged with the content. Before exams, take all of the practice exams and go step by step through the solutions to make sure you understand what you are doing right and what you are doing wrong. Don't let negative comments about this class discourage you from taking it, I wish I had taken it earlier and had the potential to take further maths courses. Amadeus is also amazing! Very understanding and helpful course had and section leader who always has the best interest of his students in mind.
As an athlete, the midterms were the toughest partthere just wasnt much time to study for them. That said, they were super generous in letting your final exam grade replace one of your midterm grades, and since we had all of reading period to prepare, that helped a lot.

Be aware that exams can be scheduled as late as 7 PM, which isnt ideal. Also, the workload is heavy as youll be doing three problem sets a week, and the AI assignments can be a bit annoying. Still, if you stay on top of things and actually study, youll be fine. Go through the worksheets and problem setsthey provide tons of materials.

Also, the practice tests are very similar to the real exams and do more than enough to prepare you if you do all of them in full.
Definitely take this class if you are looking for a good multi variable calculus class. For me, making sure to complete the worksheets regularly helped. Make sure you actually get the basics right and are not just going pset to pset. That makes it really smooth. It is easy to forget the older content as you move further into the course. So, having mind maps of how all of the concepts tie together might be a good idea.
Take it if you need. It's actually kind of fun so it's not bad even when it gets hard.
21A is honestly so organized and there is so much support!!! Amadeus is amazing and wonderful teacher. He will always be there to support you, and I honestly really grew in this class. I also just completely fell in love with math thanks to this class and this is my second math class at Harvard by the way.
This course is typically taken as a concentration requirement so there is no avoiding it for most people. I would recommend to not take 2 other pset courses with this one or else your workload throughout the week will be quite hefty. Though in my opinion much less content and time spent on homework then 1b, so if you felt comfortable with 1b, this course should be no problem. The practice exams are just as hard or easier than the actual tests, so make sure you understand those before the exams. Midterm 2 particularly was very hard for most students. STUDY STUDY STUDY and you will be fine. Also attend class, profs and tfs are great and they help make this very visual math course understandable to people who arent very visual math people.
This class is definitely challenging, but also really rewarding if you stay on top of the material. The key is to not fall behindthings build on each other quickly, so going to section (mandatory attendence), doing the problem sets on time, and actually practicing the concepts regularly makes a huge difference. Make sure you start the practice exams early on. Dont be afraid to ask for help, go to office hours and study groups are super helpful, especially when the concepts start to get more abstract. MQC is almost every day and dont just go before the PSET is due its often packed then. Also, try to focus on understanding why things work, not just how to plug into formulas. Its a lot of work, but by the end, youll be surprised how much your thinking and intuition have grown.
