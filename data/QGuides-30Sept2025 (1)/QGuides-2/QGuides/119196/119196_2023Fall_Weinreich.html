This course is not the end of the world. If you have taken Calc II/BC Calc and feel comfortable with that material, you can succeed. Success in this course is often a matter of discipline – doing problem sets early, going to office hours/MQC when needed, and studying early for exams.
The 3 p sets in this class are a real pain, but I totally loved the content. I would say the only thing to note about this class is: you must must must go to the MQC and build it into your schedule as a habit super early in the semester. This will save grades, save time. and save stress so please do that.
This is a pretty standard math class. I felt the class focused more on giving students a conceptual understanding of multivariable calculus concepts, which I liked, instead of having a basic understanding of concepts and then making you spend all your time doing stupidly tedious computations. So a lot more focus on understanding things, setting problems up, and applying knowledge rather than calculus humdrum.

Before taking this class, I "took" a multivariable calculus second semester senior year. I think this experienced helped me to some extent, especially with more basic concepts like double/triple integrals and partial derivatives, but I didn't really understand things like Flux or surface integrals or the integral theorems, so 21A was still beneficial and I learned a lot

I think 3 P–sets a week is manageable if you manage your time well. Start the day it is assigned if you can or at least look at the problems so you know where you will need help. Work through what you can before going to MQC because I didn't find it very conducive to working. Ultimately, it might seem like a lot but its still less time P–setting per week than some of the other math classes

Also one thing that was super helpful for me was looking back through notes after class everyday or the day after. Just 15–20 mins will help you get a better understanding of the stuff
Take this class!! Especially if you have Max as your TF, it will be incredible. What a great teacher. Strongly recommend taking it. Really fun. Favorite course this semester.
How well you understand will definitely depend on your TFso go to office hours (not MQC since only CAs run that) to get explanation of the materials from other TFs (Fanhui is the best!!!). Math 21a has A LOT of office hours, which is super helpful. Study off of of practice exam solutionsthat's where I learned most of the materials. They're pretty thoroughly explained, and for this class, understanding comes from application to problems because it's very hard to link everything together purely conceptually. Also, know that everything you learn are technical tools and shortcuts ultimately to solve flux integrals. The lecture didn't often give an overarching tie among all the things we were learning, so always ask TFs at office hours what they see as the broadest concept that ties together whatever you've learned so far. MYY
Good course, 3 psets a week keeps you on track (probably same amount/less as the 1 22a pset). Recommend paying attention during section and switch TF's if you don't like their teaching style
Try not to leave every pset to the night before it is due, get them done when you have the time.
