This class is such a great way to explore your research interests and test the limits of your writing. My tips would be:

1)Start early. It feels like the paper deadline is far away but especially because of the way assignments are set up, the draft deadline does sneak up on you, so even in the beginning when it feels like you're working on non–essay things, begin brainstorming on your own if you're someone like me who struggles with picking a topic.

2)Don't be afraid to explore the archives. Look things up on the finding aid, talk to librarians, and reach out to people who have resources you're interested in to get the full experience of archival research.

3)Take advantage of the meetings your professor offers. Go to as many office hours as you can, and have an open mind towards to constructive feedback you will receive that can only help improve the quality of your paper.

and most importantly: HAVE FUN! This class is such an awesome opportunity to dig into your prospectus research question or anything completely different that you'd like to explore. Don't limit yourself and actually put in the time to produce something you're proud of.
Histsci concentrators, you will absolutely love this class! I really enjoyed how the course was divided up into smaller sections (mine had just 4 total students), as this allows you to engage more deeply with the material and also receive more individualized guidance on your paper. The 25–page paper is certainly daunting, but it turned out to be a very rewarding challenge. I hope you enjoy this class as much as I did!
It was fun to choose a topic that interested me, I just wish I did a better job keeping up throughout the semester.
This is a great course! Though a 25 page paper is daunting, I think if you take it seriously, you could get some really great material for your senior thesis.

Overall a nice class! I really appreciated the small size and felt that it allowed me to get personalized, helpful feedback on my research.

I found the previous sophomore tutorial (HS97) to be quite unuseful. It left me wishing for a more comprehensive overview of the historiography and fundamentals of history and science. While HS98 did not fill this gap, I much preferred it to the HS 97 tutorial. The class taught me the basics of writing a research paper, and all the many steps that come with not only writing but also researching and picking viable and unique research topics. I liked the small class structure of the course, as it made me feel like I got to know both my classmates and my TF better. Simon is awesome!
Junior tutorial is fantastic!! There are so many support structures in place to make sure you're not banging your head against the wall trying to write 25 pages in the span of two days at the very end of the course. Simon Torracinta is also fantastic if you get him as a section head super kind, super knowledgeable, and overall just fantastic at offering feedback and support. I had a great time in junior tutorial!
Each week, when you turn in and receive an assignment, make sure to go over the edits and implement it into a working draft. While I definitely had enough to turn in before Thanksgiving break for a rough draft, I wish I had more material so I could have more substantial feedback to work off of when writing my final draft.
Stay on track with the incremental assignments as you approach the bulk of the research essay process and you will be set up for success! The heavy hours of work pick up in the final month of classes. If you touch the project every day, you will not be overwhelmed with the workload. For that reason, try to add something to your project every day, every other day, a few times a week, whatever goal will motivate you. It will help you out in the end! Ultimately, the research paper is a heavy workload but one that brings lots of pride and satisfaction.
