– GREAT CLASS TAEKU IS AWESOME!
– this class is really chill, you don't have to do the readings if you don't want to after week 3, but the READINGS ARE SUPER INTERESTING STILL
– as a non gov major, this class has made me better understand why someone would want to concentrate in gov, and it has made me understand why an Asian American political collective IS important despite the Asian diaspora being soooo diverse.
– Taeku is kind and frequently brings pastries to class, he lets us watch interesting documentaries (like Jeremy Linsanity)
– The projects and papers are genuinely interesting because YOU CHOOSE what you want to write about and you can take it somewhere that you actually care about. they feel actionable and purposeful!
This course is excellent, strongly recommend to take. You will learn tons about the background behind the status of Asians in America and engagement with the political system. A lot of these issues are backwards facing, looking at history, but much of it is very applicable in our present moment, whether that be questions of voting access or affirmative action. You will also get the chance to put your knowledge into practical usage, drafting strategic policy memos for political action. Super useful and interesting stuff! It's not incredibly easy, but it's not overwhelmingly hard either –– definitely a Goldilocks gem!
100% TAKE THIS CLASS. This has been the best class I've taken at Harvard and fulfills both gov concentration/secondary requirements. Professor Lee is extremely knowledgeable about the subject, meticulously crafting reach class to target a separate issue about Asian American politics and creating a friendly atmosphere that can't be beaten. He's so kind to his students and really tries to get to know everyone through OH visits or during class. Regarding grading, he is very fair, giving you constructive feedback on what you could potentially improve on and takes time into writing his feedback.
An incredible class; definitely take it if you are at least somewhat interested. Not a very difficult class in terms of assignments. Prof. Lee is an incredible teacher and really appreciate his mix of lecturing and discussion facilitation. Low–stakes learning environment and super interesting the mix of sociological and political phenomena discussed.
This course had the necessary balance of quantitative and qualitative discourse surrounding Asian American political studies that was very instructive and refreshing. I'm so glad that we have an Asian American politics class finally being taught and it's Professor Lee who is teaching it. He is extremely understanding, courteous, and all–around amazing instructor.
TAKE THIS CLASS YOU WON'T REGRET IT!! 10/10 class, 10/10 experience!!

This class is super accessible for all studentsregardless of your background. Taeku spends the first few weeks building a foundation in both political science and ethnic studies, then pivots in later weeks into more nuanced and deeper topics. Moreover, the assignments are catered towards student interests. For our two papers, we chose a congressional district to collect data on and analyze, enabling us to explore our individual interests; for me personally, I chose my hometown's congressional district, ultimately furthering my understanding of my upbringing and positionality as an Asian American. Lastly, Taeku fosters an amazing space for learning and communityI genuinely look forward to every Tuesday morning, and feel that I have built a strong connection with each of my classmates.

If you're at all interested in Ethnic Studies, this class is 100% for you! Even if you're not, this course provides fundamental representation in a field where Asian Americans are historically underrepresented, and I wish that everyone could learn the contents of this course. My only qualm is that I so badly wish there were more classes like this. It's disappointing that this might have been my one and only Asian American politics course in my undergrad years.
Take it
