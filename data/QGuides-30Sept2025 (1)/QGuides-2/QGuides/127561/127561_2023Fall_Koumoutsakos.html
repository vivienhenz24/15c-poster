Petros is a great lecturer! I think this class looks pretty daunting with all of the prerequisites it demands, but the main two that are non–negotiable seem to be statistics and Python. I have a decent background in both, so this class was never too confusing, but both skills are certainly tested throughout the course. Overall, I was scared to take this class as an undergrad, but in hindsight I would say this course was my favorite of the semester. It is certainly not easy, but the course support is great and my computational skills have genuinely improved.
Course is rather difficult if you haven't taken STAT111. The lectures move really fast and are hard to follow. Course is mostly problem set based so you can get a decent grade going to office hours, but the exams are difficult, though it is nice that you only have to take the midterm OR the final!
Very good class. Material is taught in a rewarding manner.
Be prepared to dedicate 1.5 times more effort to this lecture compared to a normal STEM lecture.

Excellent material, but the course is not well organized at all. Problem sets are interesting, and you learn a lot of applicable stuff. Highly recommend for AB/SM!

Tough but interesting class that covers a lot of topics
If you have a solid understanding of the prerequisites, and the time to commit, TAKE THIS CLASS!!! It really is a game–changer for the way you code, the way you approach statistics, and the way you see research in the world. You get the best of both worlds as far as being able to code and do math and discuss higher level topics, while also being able to read research papers and stay in touch with the historical and current literature and sentiments (through the reading homeworks). If youre a grad student, I would be surprised if you didnt take the course. If youre an undergrad student, this could be a life–changing course. Its so much better than any undergrad course in similar topics.
If you are firmly set on a path in academia, this course could be a fit for you, given its intensive focus on Computational Chemistry, Physics, and Biology and broad applications. However, for those not specifically targeting academic careers, I would earnestly recommend exploring Dr. Weiwei Pan's course or other course offerings instead, which may be much better suited for those looking to enter the industry and has been highly regarded by previous students for its practical and industry–relevant approach.
Don't be scared off by the reports of how much work the class is. It's a moderate workload and the grading scheme is very reasonable (assuming they continue to give a homework drop and an exam drop). The material is super interesting and well taught, definitely worth the time you put into it.
Take this class if you are at all interested in stats/programming. At the beginning of the semester they make it clear that is is a demanding course with involved material, but it proved to be very approachable.
Half of this class was just about promoting professors own research and software; it was quite inappropriate to use a required degree class for self–promotion. The midterm exam was inadequate. We got a practice exam with 3 short problems and then the real exam was double the length, so it was testing the ability to write fast rather than a proper understanding of the material. And when the mean of the midterm was 50, Petros dared to say in class that this was the students fault because we searched for homework answers online and used ChatGPT so we werent actually learning. Quite a pathetic teaching technique.

New topics were poorly motivated and the lecture part of the course was absolutely useless and unintuitive to follow. What a shame that this class is no longer taught by Weiwei because my friends were actually raving about how good and useful of a class it used to be.
There are probably better courses that cover the same material. The professor is very disorganised. However he is nice and wants you to learn and so does the teaching staff. Before taking this course I heard a lot of discouragement that the course is difficult. I wouldnt let that scare you away. The actually material is actually not difficult especially if youve taken a couple statics related courses. What is difficult is understanding what the material is, what youre supposed to know, and where the relevant information to learn it is.
