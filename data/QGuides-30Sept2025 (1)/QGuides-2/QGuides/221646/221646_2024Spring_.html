Take this class! Stat physics is such a beautiful subfield that yields really powerful results about important problems in statistics/ML, and Subha's lecturing is engaging and super instructive. The class is definitely hard though, and a lot of the concepts require a few rereads before they even start to make some sense (let alone getting solid intuition). I'd definitely recommend a strong probability background (212 concurrently is probably enough) and math background
Subha does an excellent job explaining the intuitions of the statistical physics method. Personally specking, the material is hard for me, who have not learned statistical physics before and had no physics background. Physics background is definitely not required but it may help give some intuitions. The main thing is the course project and homework grading is lenient. There are only 3–4 homeworks.
one of the hardest courses conceptually that I've taken at Harvard, but cool content!
