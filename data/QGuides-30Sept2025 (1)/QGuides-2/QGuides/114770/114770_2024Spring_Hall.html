This is a terrific class. Essentially a history class with a focus obviously on political history. If Western European History is interesting to you, this is the class to take. Professor Hall is a very good lecturer and Ben the TF does a fantastic job. My favorite class at Harvard thus far. On the practical side, though, readings can definitely pile up and be a lot. Two essays and a final are difficult and the grading can be difficult, but overall it is very fair.
Take this course! Not only will you learn about thousands of years of European history, but you will also be challenged to use a comparative approach that can be applied more broadly.
The class overall covers a very interesting question in political analysis, which is democratization and why some countries successfully pull it off while others fail. The readings can be very dense, but the lectures and lectures slides coupled with the weekly sections make it manageable. Ben was a great TF.
This class is great as long as you utilize the sections to ask questions
Good course; keep up with readings and you'll be fine
A good interesting class to take even if you dont have big history or government background.
The essays were pretty tough, and the section made it even tougher. The TF likes to cold call and is a very harsh grader, no As are given on the first paper, so it is extremely tough to get an A– or higher in the class. The material is pretty interesting, especially how Professor Hall manages to cover 500+ years of history in 1 semester and I do feel like I learned a lot, but try to take this class when you have an easier classload.
highly recommend this course to everyone!!! especially for those who want to have a deeper understanding of the formation of the modern politics and of Europe. very deep discussion with a very wide range of topics. the lectures, readings and sections are all super thought–provoking. Professor Hall is such a marvelous scholar and brilliant professor~
TAKE THIS CLASS IF YOU ARE INTERESTED IN EUROPEAN POLITICS OR HISTORY!!!!!!! I cannot recommend it enough. Professor Hall is a fantastic lecturer––the way he explains the material is both incredibly straightforward and satisfying in how all the pieces fit together. The parallels among Britain, France, Germany, and Italy are fascinating, and I enjoyed the dives into the history of each country (especially the weeks on Germany and Italy). The essay assignments from this course were definitely on the more difficult end––hopefully they raise the allotted time from 7 to 10 days––but you can plenty of support, and you will do great if you are familiar with the readings. This class is incredibly rewarding when you invest the time and effort into it. I recommend it 10000000%!!
Great course. A lot of history. Some readings can be dense but overall its pretty reasonable
Take this course! Hall is legit and definitely definitely take it if Ben is the TF
Do the readings early, and do all of the readings (to the best of your ability). You WILL suffer on the midterm(s) if you don't. Besides that the class is quite chill and lecture is interesting. I wasn't personally that interested in Europe and just wanted to broaden my knowledge, so the content was less stimulating (just not my area of interest..). But a highlight of the course are the people – both Professor Hall and Ben (if he's still TFing!) are wonderful, wonderful people. They're both super interesting and have lots of fun stories.

Ben is an excellent TF (I can't stress this enough – best gov TF I've ever had, maybe even best TF I've had period) and was extremely responsive and accommodating with scheduling office hours; you can tell that he actually cares about the students. He's a great resource for the class and is also just a great guy.
This is a great class! It provides a strong historical overview of democratization that is very applicable to a variety of time periods
This is an excellent class. Prof Hall and Ben are both incredibly passionate about teaching and you learn A LOT about modern European history and the political developments that led to democracy. It is not one of those classes where you study something for the semester and forget it after. The intuitions you develop stay with you and help you think about modern day politics too. Honestly, it's just sobering how much you learn about European political history. Very very interesting stuff. Also, the class only has 2 midterm essays and one final essay, so you're worried about random weekly reflection papers and stuff. Ben did an awesome job in section breaking down the complex readings and making sure we got all the takeaways instead of worrying about random details.
This is a great course, yes it has a lot of readings but for the most part if you skim and come to class with maybe 2–3 major points or even a quote from the reading you are going to fine. I never did the readings in this course and was still able to use the powerpoints to point me to the weeks readings and information that I needed to write the papers. Professor is super understanding and not really super hard on students especially if you commnicate and build a relationship with him. The TF Ben is so funny, tries to make section easier for students, and he also just so understanding and helpful. He is always trying to make himself available to students and is really working to be a support in this course.

Reading can be a lot though and you do have to do (at least some of) it for the papers. Ben the TF is also amazing and makes section not suck.
