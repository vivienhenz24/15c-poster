What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Dr. Whitham is a brilliant, accessible, friendly, and kind teacher. He is all that is good about academia, and is very aware about what can be dry, monotonous, and perverse about academic orthodoxy. As such, he provides materials that are genuinely interesting and worldview–changing. The discussions can sometimes stall, but you would be very lucky to take a course with Dr. Whitham––I think I can confidently say wherever you fall on the political spectrum (especially as it relates to revolutionary politics) you will emerge from this course with a more nuanced, sophisticated, and changed–for–the–better outlook on politics and history.
I would certainly recommend this tutorial. Dr Whitham is an excellent teacher, and the class content is so interesting with really well chosen readings. Whitham also provides so much support throughout the research process, which was especially helpful as I had not written a history paper in quite some time, and never one with such a focus on primary source research. As I changed my topic a million times, he met with me and offered me really helpful guidance and feedback.
I learned so much about how to think about the world, how different ideologies live, die, and change, and how to think about my own political beliefs. Revolutionary politics is so interesting, and you will definitely leave this class having changed or having a more nuanced understanding about your own place in history. I highly recommend this tutorial, Dr. Whitham is the best ever.
Take it! Dr Whitham is a great teacher and so knowledgeable and passionate about the material (go to his office hours as much as you can!), and you'll learn a ton. Do the readings: they're super interesting and if you're good at skimming at all they're much more doable than Social Studies 10 readings.
This was my favorite class of the semester! I looked forward to it each week. So many of the readings have really stuck with me and I'll probably be thinking about them for a long time. I feel like a lot of the classes I've taken at Harvard have been interesting, but stop short of *really* challenging your beliefs. This class, on the other hand, really pushed me to think about things completely differently. I highly recommend!
