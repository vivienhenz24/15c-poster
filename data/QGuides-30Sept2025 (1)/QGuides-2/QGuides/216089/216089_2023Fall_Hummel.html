You can learn a lot– it depends on how much effort you put in
This is a class that if you’re not someone who understands things like game theory or if you’ve never taken something remotely close to it is going to be difficult. The professor is super rude to students who don’t seem to catch onto the content throughout the semester and she always seems annoyed to be helping that group of students. Juan was an amazing TF and super understanding. But do not waste your time with this class because she’s not interested in teaching students who need extra support.
Great course if you are interested in looking at game theory models in a simpler way and seeing how they can be applied to politics
Professor Hummel is an extremely eloquent lecturer. She is also very supportive and patient in answering questions or responding to thoughts, very approachable. The course is not math–heavy at all, just occasionally some use of probability. The course load is not too high, and the assignments are creative in the sense that it helps you link what you learn with the real–world. there are no exams!
This is a great course for people with little–to–no background of game theory to learn the basics in a fun and applicable environment.
Gem! Absolutely loved this class, definitely one of my favorites. There are only 4 essays and a final paper, around 12–15 days given in between each assignment. The lectures are great, slightly difficult to follow with no background in game theory or any theoretics in Calculus. Professor Hummel is a great professor and is always available and helpful during office hours. No exams/psets which was definitely a course strength.
This is a class that provides a strong, and moderately paced introduction to strategic models/game theory. Every assignment was more than doable, and grading (at least with Juan) was very generous. I felt that I could learn a lot in this class without having to worry about my grade. There is more math than I expected, but it is 100% doable (just basic algebra) so don't let it scare you. Frankly, the class is very interesting if you invest into it. Professor Hummel's slides are clear, and she is a great lecturer – the class also has fun videos/simulations, which were always a delight. Definitely would recommend taking this class, especially if game theory in politics is something that piques your interest.
This class has a very manageable workload but is also very interesting in that you learn a lot about various game theory models. I liked the direct application to different political scenarios. This class doesn't assume any background, so I thought the pacing and difficulty of assignments was just right. Professor Hummel is very kind and Juan gives a lot of good feedback/advice for how to approach assignments. Grading is very chill and stress–free!
If you are interested in Gov, or political economy in particular, this class is for you. Prof. Hummel is one of my favorite professors I've had at Harvard. This class is a great, math minimal introduction to political economy/game theory/strategic models. The workload is manageable and you get the opportunity to be creative and dive into your own interests on assignments.
