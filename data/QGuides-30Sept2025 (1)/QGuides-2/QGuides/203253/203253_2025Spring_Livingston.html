Best language class for those with learning disabilities or best class for that period. very accommodating. VERY NICE PEOPLE.
Definitely picked up from Latin I, but as long as you had a solid foundation and keep up with vocab and new concepts, it is a very fun and engaging class. The teaching staff genuinely wants to see you succeed and is very willing to help you and maximize your experience. Come to class prepared because it is very interactive and you are expected to know that week's concepts before class on Monday so that you can actively practice it. No shame (most of the time) if you don't know the concepts but it isn't the most fun having to be talked through questions you find out later on are fairly trivial in front of all your peers. Nonetheless, it's all part of the learning process, and you get to learn a lot about ancient greek gods and roman history. Super cool!
Awesome community. Ivy is a great teacher and the class moves at a very manageable pace.
This class is such an amazing experience. The work is not bad, and you learn so much about Latin. Ivy is an amazing professor, and she is so kind. This is the smallest class I have been a part of, and the environment is genuinely so much fun. And the TF (Hannah... idk if she will be the TF again next semester) was the best... so fun and caring. I definitely recommend this course.
This class is very manageable, but stay on top of the vocabulary, and don't fall behind on grammar concepts.
