What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This course gave me an incredibly good understanding of the concepts behind a wide range of optics phenomena. It is the most *applied* physics class I have been in, and focuses heavily on the how and why more than the equations or math. TFs are great as well, Nicholas especially helped immensely with conceptual questions.
This class was definitely an experience as an undergraduate. On one hand, the content is unique; Professor Capasso (if he's teaching it) is a juggernaut in the field and you'll learn concepts in the latter third of the class that don't have textbooks written on them yet. So, from a pure novelty perspective, I think it is worth considering. On the other hand, I didn't think Capasso was a very effective lecturer; he is someone who is highly intelligent, but not very aware of the level at which everyone else is at (especially undergrads) and either doesn't know how to communicate to us or simply doesn't care to do so effectively (I personally think it is the former). The bulk of my learning in this class came from the TFs, Nicholas and Andrea (these two really made the class for me and if at least one of them is teaching the class, I think you'll have a fine time), and doing the homework assignments and exams.

I will say that you should take my words a healthy amount of salt: this is a graduate level course and my only real preparation for the class was Physics 15B and Physics 143A and I've never taken an optics course of any kind in my life. I believe I got the majority of the content I was supposed to from the class, but I definitely felt out of my depth half the time. I highly recommend that you become very comfortable with E&M before taking this course (especially E&M in dielectric materials) and give yourself at least a little exposure to ray optics.
Don't recommend. Although the TAs were wonderful, the professor's lectures were incomprehensible, particularly in the second half of the course.
While this course is interesting, the lectures are not very effective. You get out of it what you put into it. The sections are the best part of the course.
This course is very time consuming and challenging, but you will learn a lot. The lectures and sections cover a lot of content. The teaching fellows, Andrea and Nick, are amazing guys and very willing to help. I would recommend this course to people who want to learn about the latest advances in optics and photonics, but be warned it is quite a bit of work.
Great course for anyone doing research in optics, or who just wants to understand the physics behind a lot of advanced technology that leverages these phenomena
