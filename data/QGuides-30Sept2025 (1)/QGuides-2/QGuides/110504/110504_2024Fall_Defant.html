Colin Defant is a very good instructor and structured this course extremely well. He's great at explaining things clearly. Colin's also understanding and looks after his students. Would definitely recommend you take this course or any course taught by Colin in the future!
main issue: Feedback. going into our last class, one of our CAs only just graded their second problem of the term and we all only had one pset graded with about three days left until our final project was due. I mentioned this during OH and most students agree, most of us were penalized for subjective judgments of our writing styles, and the resulting comments were very unhelpful in both their content and extremely late timing. I understand this is a graduate course with higher expectations of independent learning and people are busy. But providing timely and substantive feedback is a core responsibility and if one has too many of their own other commitments to do this, it really hurts student learning.
Really solid class! We don't have a ton of combinatorics here so it was cool to get to know the subject better. Colin is great. It was chill too. Only 3 psets and a fun final project!
This course was certainly a "for fun" course: 3 psets, a final project, on a very niche subject where Professor is really good. With this stuff, I sometimes ask myself why I'm doing this, but that's all pure math really; however here especially, sometimes certain combinatorial `things' feel like a ton of definitions are disguising a very simple structure. Sure, by extending the definitions you sometimes get interesting, new, structures, but missing one definition in these sorts of things can confuse a lecture because everything builds. But Colin was AWESOMe w examples and clarifying, which made it way easier to follow even with patchy understanding. Overall big fan, I think it's just generally hard to motivate super fringe, new math over traditional combinatorics, but papers can be written!
