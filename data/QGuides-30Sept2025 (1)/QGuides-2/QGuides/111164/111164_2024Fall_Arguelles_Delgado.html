Course background: Two 1hr 15min lectures per week, one 1.5hr non–mandatory section per week, one PSET per week (average of 10 hours per week), two midterm exams, one final exam.

My background: A mechanics and waves class in 9th grade, AP Physics C Mechanics in 11th grade (got a 5), BC Calculus in 11th grade, concurrently taking Math 21A

My recommendation: This class is not for the faint hearted. Despite taking two years of physics in high school, I had a difficult time in this class and devoted most of my beyond–class time and resources to doing well in it (much more so than any other class). The PSET problems are just really hard and take that long to solve (often with the assistance of CAs during office hours who tell you whether you are right or wrong).

If you are a non–physics concentrator, take this class only if you felt really good about physics in high school/previous settings. If you struggled to a non–significant degree then, you will probably find the same in this class as well. If you are only taking the course to fill a course requirement (and you are not that interested in physics), I would say there is nothing wrong with lightening your load and taking Physics 12 or a joint bio or chem physics class.
This course is not hard, and you can definitely self teach yourself eveything. Don't rely on the lectures too much, but to do all the PSETs, readings, attend discussions, etc. If you put in the effort, you can do well in this course! Not too much of a heavy workload.
