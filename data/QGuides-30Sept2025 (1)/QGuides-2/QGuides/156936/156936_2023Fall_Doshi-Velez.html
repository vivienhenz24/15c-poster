Note that you need some ML background at the level of CS 181 or 182 at least. It helps to have background into whatever the topic is for that particular semester, but CS 181/182 are a must for sure. Finale is very knowledgeable and approachable. She has great insights to offer. This is the type of course where you get out what you put in. If you skim the readings and don't discuss in class, your experience will be subpar. If you engage more, you will learn more. The semester long project can be a grind at times but it is a worthwhile experience as with any semester long projects. Overall is a solid class, definitely not a gem but not too intense. Great elective choice.
This is a good class that does not require much prerequisites for a grad–level computer science course. There are only 3 homework assignments in the course that all could be completed in a short amount of time (less than 5 hours). The big component of this course is the research project, which you should start early and make sure you're continually making significant progress across the semester. Specifically, make sure you're obtaining results during each checkpoint, and choose a topic that is actually feasible to execute on.
It was a great class and I learned a lot! I found working on the final project to be difficult, and required a ton of time, but it may be because I had less experience going in.
The papers are all on a very specific topic, which at times can feel repetitive but truly give you a great understanding on the different methods for IRL. The project can be long and difficult at times depending on what topic you choose, but Finale makes sure to check in with you and provides feedback at every checkpoint assignment, which is useful for generally staying on track. Please start early!
IRL is a relatively new area of research, and you'll be able to learn about all the state of the art methods in this class very exciting!
If you are interested in research and solving inverse decision–making problems, this course is great. The focus is almost exclusively on the research as the lectures are mainly for presenting and discussing papers and the coursework is a self–cirected project related to the class topics. Finale is also a great teacher!
