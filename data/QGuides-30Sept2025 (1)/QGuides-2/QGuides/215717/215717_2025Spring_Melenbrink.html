DEFINITELY TEKE PS70. You will probably need help, don't be scared to ask the course staff or fellow students. They are always ready and willing to help!
PLEASE DEAR STUDENT TAKE THIS CLASS. If you want an answer to the question "what were your top 5 most favorite classes you took throughout all college?" this should be top 3, if not the first. This class is MIT's equivalent of "how to build almost everything", and you're not missing out by not taking the MIT version: this is seriously one of the best courses offered here.

My background: CS/Econ. Almost no 'building–hardware' experience but wanted to explore that.

You get to work with a small group of people, which I think really adds to the vibe. The staff and teaching fellows are super fun to hang around and are very easy to ask questions to. What's kind of funny is that the lab is in the science center (SC 102) and visitors will come by to look in at the work you're doing. Feels like you're on display, which some may not like, but I actually loved it because it felt cool to show off what you've been working on!

[What I Learned]
The course site essentially describes all of this, but I'll put it here:
– Microcontroller programming
– CNC Milling (wood, acrylic, foam)
– Laser Cutting (wood, acrylic)
– Metalwork
– Circuitry fundamentals (capacitors, resistors, breadboards)
– Inputs/outputs you can attach to the circuit (servos and other actuators, stepper/DC motors, Piezo buzzers, RFID scanners, light displays, and so much more)
– Circuit board fabrication (PCBs)
– Radio communication (wired, wireless, Bluetooth)

[Typical Week]
– You'll be given a project to work on Tuesday, then have until the following Thursday to complete it.
– If you don't complete it to the level you desire, you're free to redo it or continue on it until the end of the semester, but I advise just moving on and focusing on the next weekly task, then go back to that one at the end, that way you're not feeling lagged behind by any means.
– The workload each week is not the same. Some projects will take longer. Of all the weeks, I think the heaviest weeks are: kinetic sculpture, radio communication, and machine building (robot machine). Those weeks take about 10 hours.

Again, PLEASE TAKE THIS COURSE IF YOU WANNA DO HANDS ON WORK. This stuff is so fun. I know I say 10 hours for the worst weeks but honestly it does not even feel like 10 weeks, it's more like "an hour of work here" and "two hours of work there", what's nice is you can basically come in any time with a buddy and they have so many office hours it's quite ridiculous, there's never a shortage of that. You'll do awesome!!

This is a truly, truly special and remarkable class. It is not easy by any stretch of the imagination, but it is the most rewarding class that I have taken over 4 years at Harvard. The opportunity to use these really high tech machines and have expert guidance while using them is incredible, and you can make some really amazing projects with them. It is a lot of work, but it is well worth it, and it is fun work where you are using your hands and messing around with laser cutters, not doing a problem set. You also get out of it what you put into it. Often, the expectations that the teaching staff has for you is much lower than the expectations that you have for yourself, which drives you to put in more time than strictly necessary. The class also meets you where you are at – from no experience to almost a professional, you will learn a ton from this class.
