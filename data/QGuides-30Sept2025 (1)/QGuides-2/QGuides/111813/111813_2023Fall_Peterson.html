This is the response you want to read. Professor Peterson may seem intimidating but he's a nice dude, and although you might not get to know him personally without going to office hours, or running into him before class he is always a joy regardless. He keeps his politics and opinions out of class, and takes a view of American Government in well, 'a new perspective' like the name of the class. But to get to the point, this class is great if you are: starting your government concentration, unsure on majoring in government, want to take a class that is very entry level to government, and also looking for a class to fill your schedule that doesn't take too much time! There is a one prompt short answer 5 min question at the start of every class, but they are also relatively the course material from the videos so don't worry too much, and it is easy to catch up if you get sick and miss a class.

The readings are very inaccessible due to costs, so I recommend finding a way to get them through library reserves or speaking to the Professor or TF, I got one of my books from requesting it from an inter–library loan which is another option. I try not to spend money on books so that is my advice!
