This is a fun and rewarding experience for serious musicians or those who want to keep playing in a smaller group. The chamber coaches are fantastic, I loved Daniel Chong and his feedback helped me and my group improve.
I'd encourage anyone unsure about whether this class is right for them to take it. I'd been scared off of applying for Music 189 for a few semesters by the claims of how it was really only for conservatory musicians, how you needed to have a pre–formed group to even be considered for a spot, how it's a scary environment, etc. This was really not my experience –– the coaching sessions were terrific, I learned a lot, I was given an environment to work on chamber music which I wouldn't have been able to construct for myself, and I got course credit for something I love doing. I submitted a pretty pathetic application to the class and was still admitted (in what I think of as a fairly impacted instrument), so I'd encourage anyone considering whether an application is worth it to just put in.

I found that Music 189 is not the most rewarding social environment for people outside the NEC/HRO circle. The environment definitely feels very conservatory–oriented; there's a lot of focus on the nitty–gritty phrase level elements of music present in the whole–class coachings, which generally feel like a room full of people who would rather be anywhere else. No one seems excited to engage in the content of the material; I don't think anyone was ever encouraged to actively participate in any whole–class setting where they were not performing. This is fine if you believe that watching masterclasses as a pure spectator does enhance your pedagogical experience, but I was a little disappointed by this; generally, I have grown to appreciate that (especially at the undergraduate level) the music department at Harvard does not resemble a conservatory, and in other courses there is more of a focus on big ideas, history, theory, critical analysis, etc. With that being said, there are a lot of musicians who want to play their standard repertoire without being pushed too much on what it means, and this class works very well in that regard; and even for people who come from a different approach or background, I think this class is well worth taking.
