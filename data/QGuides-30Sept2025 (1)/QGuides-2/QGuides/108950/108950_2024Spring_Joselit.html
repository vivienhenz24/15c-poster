This is a super fun and interesting course that isn't a lot of work week to week. It provides a broad yet thorough overview of modern art in an engaging way. The lectures were generally very fun. The exams require a lot of memorization and are a bit unwieldy, but overall this is a good course.
Of the three GENED courses I have taken in my first year here, this was the most interesting and comprehensive. I, like many, came into the course with a surface–level knowledge of art history and did not appreciate most of the modern or contemporary works I had seen in museums. However, the course taught me to think critically and develop new interpretations and appreciations for art, supported by the required memorization and varied readings. Take it if you have any interest in art. Be prepared for the amount of work and be communicative with the TF in discussing readings if you're having trouble getting through them. The required memorization can seem like a lot, but if you get ahead of it, exams are instantly made simpler for you. There is obviously an infinite amount to learn about the art beyond the titles and dates of the works, including how they are made and what the intention of the artist was in making them, which the course does a great job of teaching between the three different professors.

I had always enjoyed going to museums, but I started going to the Harvard Art Museums for brief visits for fun this semester, and am eager to explore more museums in the Boston area next semester.
