Comments
take this class even if you're not an anthro concentrator but interested in the subject! it was very fulfilling and a great change of pace from stem classes; also very thorough and a great not–introductory course
If you have the chance, take this class. The readings are a little difficult at times and the essay assignments can feel daunting, but it is so worth it. Fierman oozes intelligence and leads the seminar in such an accessible way. Though the assigned readings are hard, she discusses them and their takeaways before the class talks about them. She also returns the papers really quickly and offers very good feedback. Like most seminars, the amount that people are engaged is very important, but if you put in even a little effort, you'll get a lot out.
Take the course if it and the syllabus seem interesting. It's basically more advanced book club (in the best possible way).
TAKE THIS CLASS –– if you're interested in any of the social sciences, you have so so much to learn from this class. Especially if you're an anthro major, you really should take this class. You'll learn about key theorists and see connections woven throughout all of the texts.
Take this class! The readings in the syllabus might appear daunting and possibly even obscure (both of which are not untrue), but under Prof. Fierman's guidance, they really come alive. I feel like I have learnt so much, in a way that was both unified (in terms of theories and concepts and how different readings built on each other) but also incredible diverse (in terms of the geographical range of places that we covered over the course). And you will surely find so much to think of and with, that speak directly to contemporary issues, no matter what your background is!
