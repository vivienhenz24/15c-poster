Comments
Especially emerging from college as Harvard graduates, this class orients you to how society favors some and disadvantages others; an important thing to keep in mind while entering the workforce as the heavily favored. It offers opportunity for self reflection, helping to determine exactly which type of things matter to you in your career, the manner in which you behave on a day to day basis, and what is important to you in life. If you read and discuss with the intent to apply these ideas to your own life, you can benefit from a lot more than just what the syllabus advertises.
It is an enjoyable class, but it is definitely what you make it. even if you're the obly one with a certain view point, bring it up as it can spark intellectual debates that others may not have considered.
This class has a lot of reading that you have to do in order to participate in class (most of your grade) and the readings are quite dense/difficult. The class is largely spent just summarizing readings. It isn't a super difficult class, but just be prepared to explore the readings during class. Other than that, the essays aren't too bad and Emma is really understanding about deadlines/assignments.
This is an incredibly interesting course taught by a fantastic professor. It is obvious that Emma cares deeply about the material and the students, and she facilitates engaging discussion about the readings. The work load is not too bad and you get a lot out of this course. I had never taken a political theory class but loved this one!
A seminar so really depends on the students in the class
