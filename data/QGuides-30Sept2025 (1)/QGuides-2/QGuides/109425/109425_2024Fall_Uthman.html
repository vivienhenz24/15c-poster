Put in the work!
Definitely less stressful than A series, end of semester does pick up a bit. You are still expected to work daily, so just be prepared for that.
Ozzy is very knowledgable on the subject of Arabic. It’s very impressive and appreciated by his students. He also was generally encouraging to students trying to learn the language. But I have to say that many things about this class were disappointing and did not meet expectations. First off, Ozzy needed to reflect more on his classroom management techniques. The rules of Arabic classes are very strict, especially on the syllabus. Some of these rules are puzzling and too harsh such as having absolutely zero tolerance for late homework, which is inflexible and not realistic for students taking full course loads. Of course because of this, students appreciate it when instructors are more lenient from time to time. But the key is consistency. There were moments that rules were applied inconsistently it seemed depending on the instructor’s mood, where a bad mood meant students would be treated more harshly. That led to a stressful and unstable learning environment at times.

Second, towards the end of the semester, it was very unclear what was happening and what students were supposed to be doing. We did not have any information about the final exams, for example, which was very odd as even the TF was not informed of what was happening. There was also a mismatch between exams and what was covered in class at times. For example, we learn much more fusha than dialect in class, and yet tests are filled with dialect. The final exams were also very long and difficult and the coursework (along with lack of final exam preparation) did not help students to meet the challenge of the exams. In the future, Arabic courses should throughly prepare students for exams each had every time, even if that means using material outside of Al Kitaab.

The TF Zaina however did try to have students read and listen to Arabic and engage in active learning and it was much appreciated, especially since there wasn’t not much speaking practice in the course otherwise. But all in all, the course and the syllabus, with its very strict and harsh policies, failed to create an environment where students want to be and actively engage in. Hopefully Arabic instructors can reflect on student feedback and create a better classroom and learning experience.
