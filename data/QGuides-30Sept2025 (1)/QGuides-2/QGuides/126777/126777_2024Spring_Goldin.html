Don't fall behind. Meet with Claudia and Larry. Get support from your TF. And choose a project that is interesting, but also in scope for the course. I went into this course with high expectations, but was left a little unimpressed. Professors Goldin and Katz are both so well–renowned in their field and it was such a privilege to spend some time hearing their thoughts about issues in the education system. However, they were very hard to reach and connect withto be expected of a giant lecture hall, but disappointing in a smaller seminar class. The problem is in part due to the robotic nature of lecturemany of the readings were regurgitated in lecture, and there was no discussion between students. At the end of a 90 minute lecture of materials, there was some time allotted for questions to the professors, but this was the extent of an open dialogue. Additionally, there is little to no help available during the research and writing process of the final paper. The comments left on assignments were informative, but hard to follow up on as there is typically a long wait to see the professors for office hours. The most in–person feedback I received was during my 15–minutes of Fame presentation, which felt particularly harsh in front of a classroom full of people, while trying to show my peers the work I had done thus far on my research. Many of us have written maybe one other empirical paper in our lives, but still need much guidance on the basics, such as coding in Stata or R. If taking this class, I would highly recommend getting started early and finding ways of getting help elsewhere, like coding office hours provided by the Ec Department.
A transformative experience. I recommend that you take this course in the strongest terms possible.
This course was absolutely phenomenal and top–tier. This was easily the best course I have taken at Harvard so far. I would certainly recommend having a strong foundation in Economics prior to taking this course, especially ECON 1123. The Professors are so incredibly caring and provided such in–depth feedback throughout the semesters on our papers, while being very accessible during office hours to discuss things 1–on–1. It truly is a once–in–a–lifetime opportunity to learn under Professor Katz and Goldin. The best part of the course was they were always giving us feedback, whether in discussions in class, during our presentations, or on our papers. While it was slightly intimidating at first, you can tell that they were truly doing it out of a willingness to ensure that we were reaching our best as students. I walked away from this course genuinely thinking about pursuing an Economics PhD and learned a lot about myself.
