This is a lovely junior seminar, especially if you dont have particular aspirations in energy/more traditional ESPP fields (or even if you do!) and just want to talk about the crossdisciplinary study of wetlands as critical and deeply interesting ecosystems! Jackie is a delightful professor and the form of this class cultivates community very easily in my experience.
Take this class!! Jackie is an amazing professor who meets every student where they're at, and you will learn so much without feeling overwhelmed by an impossible workload. The readings and assignments are manageable, interesting and intentional so it doesn't feel like you're doing busy work. Jackie is also very caring, funny, and makes the classroom environment feel warm and welcoming. If you're considering taking this class, do it!!
This course was consistently the highlight of my week!

The title "global wetlands" is a tad misleading, as the course didn't have a super international scope (in terms of studying specific wetlands abroad). But, it did focus on international policies, which I LOVED
I would tell future students that if you decide to take this class, you will learn the most out of the class if you actively engage in class discussions. I really do think the class is extremely related to what is occurring in the climate and environmental spaces today, so just engaging with each discussion allows you to learn so much on top of doing all the readings.
Good class! The workload is manageable, and the professor is very approachable and reachable outside of class. You will only get out of it what you put in to the class, as it is mostly discussion based.
This is a great course to get a general overview of the biology, policy, and social issues regarding global wetlands! The course instructor, Jackie, is so enthusiastic and kind and creates a great atmosphere in class. The assignments and readings are not rigorous but is required to actively participate in discussion during class. The final project is very open–ended which allows you to pick a topic you are truly interested in!
This course was great because of the different ways it incorporated science, policy, and technology from a variety of different fields. Jackie was a terrific instructor who helped guide us through the more complicated technical portions, and took the time to help those who had less experience or familiarity. Overall, it was an eye–opening course which really helped to expand my idea of how important and what a wetland could be.
I would tell future students to TAKE this course. Jackie is wonderful and so passionate. The assignments for the course are all very logical, and Jackie ensures you are equipped with the right resources to succeed. You will learn a shocking amount about wetlands, but it is important to go into the class caring about them or being intrigued otherwise this course will feel like forever.
