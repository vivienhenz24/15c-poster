Hajnalka is more organized/structured, Richard is more entertaining as an instructor
dont be lazy with it. do the little work weekly so you dont struggle with the content before exams.
I think Hajnalka Ji will get too much criticism here she knows an incredible amount about the language and cares about the class. However, I do think that this class is truly for heritage speakers, and should not be taken by people without any spoken hindi experience. There is very little emphasis placed on spoken hindi (<5m a day, and that is generous) and no time at all spent on learning vocab. While these are things you can technically study outside of class, it is hard to do so with a Harvard schedule. If you are not a heritage speaker, unless you are willing to put in extra time (on top of the already arduous workbook + Urdu + weekly assignment schedule) you should reconsider.
the tfs are great, richard ji is great and honestly the goat. i think hajnalka ji is a nice woman and definitely super academically competent, but she may be suited for a different role. she does not seem to enjoy teaching at this basic undergrad level, and it has not been a super enjoyable experience under her. while she is a kind professor, she puts up with a lot from the department and other obligations that give her a short fuse sometimes. i think she will be happier focusing more on her persian and urdu studies as opposed to teaching elementary hindi. i dont think she's a reason to not take the course, but few teachers generate such enthusiasm and learning that richard ji does, which makes him a reason to enroll in this course even as an elective! overall its an intense course for sure, but the grades should work themselves out. i hope it can be improved in future years, and i love this department, but more resources must be given to all teaching faculty since they are all overextended and underpaid.
you get out of this class what you put in. its easy to bs assignments and come out having learned nothing. do the work and it'll be so rewarding.
This semester of Hindi/Urdu was definitely more challenging than the last. There were weeks with a lot of assignments, including Urdu workbook questions, Urdu worksheets, and Hindi workbook assignments. The Urdu component of the course was especially challenging and felt quite rushed. The curriculum, although it teaches a lot, can be quite overwhelming so make sure to stay on top of assignments weekly!! Seton is an amazing section leader, though I don't think she is teaching next year. Hajnolka is also a great instructor; at times her teaching is more lecture–style which can be monotonous but if you pay attention, it will teach a lot. My best piece of advice is to complete workbook assignments as they come – that will save you so much time and you'll take a lot more from the class!
fun class, just a huge time commitment and lots of busy work, but thats just language classes in general anyways
This class was a lot different than Hindi 101a. I, like many others, was under the impression that we would be taught by Richard ji all year, so adjusting to the teaching style of Hajnalka Ji this semester was definitely a challenge. I enjoy both professors, but Hajnalka Ji is much less engaging and entertaining overall. Additionally, significantly fewer verbal questions were asked to each student in the class this semester, despite similar class numbers to 101a. Hajnalka's style is to teach from powerpoints, so in contrast with Richard Ji, it's a lot more difficult to sit and learn by diffusion instead, you'll have to treat this like any other high school/intro college language course, which is unfortunate because that's part of what made Hindi 101 special with Richard Ji. However, this class is overall good and not very difficult; I just wish the department would have been more transparent in what was going to happen with class sections during/after winter break.
