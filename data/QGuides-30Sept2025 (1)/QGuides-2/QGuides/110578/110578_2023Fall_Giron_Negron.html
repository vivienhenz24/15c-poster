This is a language class that meets three times a week in a small format, but there was hardly any student participation. Some of the readings were interesting, but they are pretty hard to get through since you aren't really tested on any of the material. No one in our class really knew each other, and I don't even think the professor or the TF knew any of our names.
If you really like Spanish and would love to work more deeply with the famous Spanish Golden Age literature that is out there, this class is perfect for you. You will read lots of amazing works by famous authors like Miguel de Cervantes, Garcilaso, and Quevedo. The work is also very doable. For the majority of the semester, you get to enjoy reading for reading. The professor, Luis, will be able to show you so much, and you will learn lots for sure. You will think about the literature in ways you have not considered before. These eye–opening revelations are mindblowing. The rest of the work load is writing 2 essays during the semester plus a final exam with more essay questions. This class is very manageable overall and has a very friendly learning environment.
