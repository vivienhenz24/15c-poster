If you have any interest in thermo I would definitely take it!! I loved this class and Professor Martin is a great lecturer
I'm sure much easier workload–wise than other thermo, but you have to like the seminar–style format, which can be hit or miss. Professor also is particular about certain things but is passionate about the material.
This course is probably one of the most accessible engineering courses at Harvard because of the workload and lecture style. The focus is on thermodynamic theories, and the math component is minor. Weekly assignments tend to be on the shorter side and don't essentially require anything outside the class. Professor Martin is great, but his lectures sometimes feel a bit dry, and it's relatively easy to get lost. I would recommend taking this course only if you clearly know why you want to learn thermodynamics and have some engineering background. Otherwise, you would not learn anything substantial.
Scot is a great professor who clearly cares a lot about students not only succeeding in his class but also coming away with a deep understanding of the material. Lecture was engaging and section was very useful for understanding how to problem–solve on the HWs. The hardest part was honestly keeping up with the discussion posts, but they're not time–intensive, it can just be hard to come up with a question for every reading. The environment and structure of the class was very welcoming though, and Scot made it a place for us to feel comfortable engaging with the material in ways beyond problem sets. The presentations were fun. to make and the final project was something to be really proud of. Amazing class.
respectfully this class kinda sucked but would take it again for an easy A. if scots teaching it, prepare to have ur name butchered and to be confused and/or bored in class.
This is a really cool course, and if you are lucky enough to get in through the lottery, it will be a great schedule addition. Took because it was a requirement but found myself deeply engaged in thermodynamics, and the framing of the course through case studies, examples, and real–life contexts makes all of the topics feel relevant and useful. Scot takes care to keep things in scope and I really appreciated him as an engaged, exciting lecturer. Professor Martin is also really accessible for office hours and questions on class topics, has a real passion for making students understand the content, and gives really constructive feedback generally. If you're willing and motivated to do the readings, you can get a lot out of this class.
This is a great class for an accessible introduction to thermodynamics. Note that the final project is extensive so it is important to plan ahead and be willing to put in the many hours needed to complete it. Section was option but attending regularly makes the homework and the midterm much easier.
This is the easier version of Thermo. There are some challenging psets in the middle which you may want to start early. Don't underestimate the midterm or the final. Sadly, you will not learn about problem solving techniques as easily in this class compared to the others, but you can get the principles. Scot is engaging, but a little too much. Don't take this class if you can be "on" for every class.
