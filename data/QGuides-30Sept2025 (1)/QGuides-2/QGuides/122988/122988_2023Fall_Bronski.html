This is a great course and I loved the material! Take it!
It's an amazing class. The course has light reading and the reading is interesting but not even super necessary for class discussions because they are really open and unstructured. The topics are really interesting and class is always fun when professor plays a bunch of songs and brings in artifacts. Assignments are interesting and very easy to manage!
Take this class if you're interested in activism and history. It's super interesting to learn about the politics that is often undiscussed. The workload is very manageable. It's about one discussion post, a few readings to skim, and three papers throughout the semester. The grading and support makes it all worth it!
The class is simple and serves as a decent intro to social movements and activism history. Liked the addition of popular media. Personally feel like there were too many (unnecessary) readings. Simple assignments. You get what you put in!
I cannot recommend this class highly enough! There is a lot of reading, but Michael invests a ton of time into the class and the discussions so it is really worth the time and energy that you put into this. Does not require a WGS background, course is accessible to anyone but you should be willing to speak up in class (at least for the seminar version of the course).
This class is amazing!!! This is one of my favorite classes I have taken at Harvard. Professor Bronski is an amazing and supportive professor and really cares about his students. Also, the topics that we cover in the class are extremely applicable to real life issues and I found myself leaving the class with a lot of insight and knowledge. This is a must take class!
This course provides a great foundational understanding of 60s/70s social movements. I learned a lot about key figures and concepts that I didn't previously know about. I'm appreciative of the way the course has given me a deeper understanding of today's social movements. We talked a lot about levers of power and how to affect tangible change, which I found interesting.

You dont need to be a WGS major to take this course (Id never taken a WGS class before, and neither had many others in the class). I really recommend the course if youre looking to understand Black Power/feminist movements/gay liberation both historically and today. Professor Bronski is a tremendous resource and has an incredible amount of knowledge.
