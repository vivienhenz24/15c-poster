enjoyable course that has a lot of cool lectures and enjoyable section. however, you get out of this class what you put into it
I'm incredibly grateful to have been exposed to the artists included in this course. My understanding of what it means to be a self–actualized musician has become a much more spiritual sense in me, rather than a want to adhere to a specific genre type. Despite the excellent curriculum and access to artists discussed, I recommend this course with reservation as the feedback on assignments was incredibly unsatisfactory (as of the end of the semester, all major projects – worth 75% of the course grade – and half of the weekly assignments have no grade or feedback). Moreover, the structure of the course is a very paltry from of a seminar; most of the guest Q&A time involves the professors talking to the artist at the front of the classroom, sometimes only allowing a few students to ask questions within the last hour of class. Alternatively, Yosvanny and Vijay are incredibly knowledgeable about the subject matter, as evident in their ability to ask questions that provide interesting answers, but the structure of the lessons and the classroom itself can leave you feeling pretty removed from the conversation between the three of them (Vijay, Yosvanny, and the guest artist). Additionally, it was clear that there was a lack of consistent, organized communication between the professors and the TF. Notably, a major project deadline was created without anyone in the course knowing AND without the assignment being listed on Canvas; we only found out from an offhand comment made by one of the professors in class, less than a week before the deadline. Additionally, sections towards the end became less structured and a bit pointless. Ultimately, for myself, the course was transformative by its material, but had myriad issues with organization and structure.
This is a really interesting course. My conception of what music is expanded, and I gained so many insights about genre and other musical topics from our discussions. Professors Terry and Iyer are the best, and you'd be lucky to learn from them.
Please take this course. If you are interested in music at all, please take this course.

There will be music that is challenging to most ears but if you focus on learning from the creative process rather than the content of each artist you will get a lot from the course.
