This class has very little structure. The syllabus changed midway through the year (10 weekly assignments ended after only 5 assignments, effectively doubling the weight of each already finished assignment). In my opinion a GenEd shouldn't have both a capstone and a 3 hour final. The capstone length also wasn't released until 3 days before it was due (after classes ended) and the capstone ended up being 3–4 thousand words. Furthermore for the final there was next to no resources offered for preparation. An interesting but ultimately very frustrating course.
I loved this class and got a lot out of it! Especially as someone who is interested in psychology, but had never taken a psychology–related course, I found a lot of the psych–related content to be very fascinating. I feel like I can see the concepts I learn about in my daily life and that this course had made me better at decision–making. The guest speakers were also phenomenal. However, this class has a pretty heavy workload for a gened, so be aware of that. We had both a ~4,000 word essay and a 3–hour final exam, and we also had an in–class midterm, which meant that this isn't the type of gened where you can blow it off. But if you're interested in the course content, it's a great class to take.
I'd really recommend this. Incredibly enjoyable and interesting course –– think of it as something like "applied statistics" with a bunch of other cool perspectives from guest lecturers dropped in. Its going to be very easy for you if you have a stat 110 background because that stuff if the hardest part of the course. Otherwise you're going to learn about biases you are probably susceptible to, and even if you aren't, the vast majority of people are, making it useful info for any student of life. The work is very light too, and every part of it helps you to grasp the concepts better. Oh, and Prof Pinker is a treat –– such a lovely guy, and with such great lectures and guest speakers. So glad I took it –– I was skeptical at first! 10/10.

READ THIS! I came into this class, hesitant, because there was not a Q report....and took it regardless. Interesting decision on my part. READ THIS to actually understand what this class is like, coming from a Freshman Gov/Psych concentrator. The topics in the course are interesting. What makes it extremely difficult is in the first half of the class, you learn an entirely new concept every single lecture. Topics range from probability to game theory to biases. While the topics are interesting, I barely had a background in any of the subject matters and found myself extremely confused every single lecture during this 5 week stretch when you learn SO MANY TOPICS (and then you learn nothing new after Spring Break so that is an interesting setup). Also, during this period when I was very confused, my TF graded my assignments super harshly and my grade saw the consequences, even though the weekly assignments were 2 points. Midway through the class, Pinker decided to COMPLETELY change the syllabus, and ultimately those 2 point assignments were extremely important for my grade. By the time the midterm came around, I studied for at least 12 hours and did well. By well, I mean I got an A, which was originally a 71% uncurved. But, I saw people in tears after that midterm and heard Harvard students call it the "hardest test they have ever taken". Second half of class is all guest lectures so that is nice. Capstone project was fine – don't have my grade back yet so not totally sure. Have not taken the final yet but anticipate it being very challenging. More than half of my grade is still up in the air so I really cannot comment on that, but I know it is going to be an extremely hard A. This class was not easy. Not a gem. None the less, I enjoyed the topics. Pinker does not cater to students who are unfamiliar with the topics, and be ready to teach yourself EVERYTHING. I have mixed feelings, but I hope this helped you understand what the class is like.
