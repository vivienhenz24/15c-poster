best class i've taken, kristina and tf tejas are mind–blowing! that said, the more prepared you are, the better you will feel in class, so either take the reqs beforehand (lin alg, coding) or lock in beforehand and learn it the best you can. very much the get–out what you put in vibe
This is a really great course and Dr. Penikis is amazing and cares about her students. If you have taken and felt comfortable with all the prereqs, you may find the class easy. It is very much a survey course and does not provide a truly rigorous treatment of the material (ie. breadth over depth). The homeworks are usually short and really engaging. Expect lots to change about the course from year to year as it is a new course.
Definitely take this course if you are looking for an introductory neuroscience course. It goes over many topics that are relevant to the field of neuroscience, and you will also get some coding experience from taking this course. The course was very enjoyable, and the professor and TF were very helpful. Having a set due every other week helped make this class less of a burden and more fun. Totally would recommend.
Highly recommend this course. Even if you have minimal coding experience, Dr. Penikis and Tejas are able to help you, and the PSETs are geared towards a lower level of understanding of the code and a greater level of understanding of what goes on behind the code. Very supportive overall
This is a fantastic course to provide a broad range of persepctives on computational neuroscience at an accessible and approachable depth. I think the course is extremely reasonable, but you should be prepared to go to every office hours if you want to truly understand the material. I think it is a great course if you are CS and want to learn the applications of the material in a super cool way, or see how another field has contributed to CS. I think it is great if you are neuro or bio and want to learn how quantitative mechanisms have informed neuro as a field. Really valuable!

Kristina is super supportive and accessible to help you through. She is very fair and transparent with grading so that you actively see how your choices affect your grade. Tejas is one of the best TFs I have ever had at Harvard; he is beyond knowledgable and has helped so much in walking through not only what we do on the psets, but WHY, and how to make my code clean! He steps through everything very slowly and nonjudgementally without just giving you the answers. Knowing not that much coding background, it is super helpful way to practice your scientific coding skills with gentle scaffolding!

Overall, I would highly recommend the course, but only if you will actually care about the course content, because it is super interesting and can change the way you think.
Kristina is a great teacher! So nice and very flexible with deadlines. She also makes very good and detailed learning resources for the class. The homework assignments are fun to do because they apply cool topics., and she gives really good guidance for the final project. Definitely take!
This class gives you a great tool set for how to approach comp neuro and the topics it encompasses. It will also teach you some mathematical techniques used in comp neuro as well.
