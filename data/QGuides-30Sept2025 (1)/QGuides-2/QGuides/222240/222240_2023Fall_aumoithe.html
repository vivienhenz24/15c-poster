You should definitely take this course if you are interested in understanding the contemporary healthcare situation in the US. It challenges you to grapple with this history and how we think about history unfolding. I cannot recommend more highly!
This is a great course, but be prepared to put in a lot of time. Heavy weekly reading load, and you must do the reading to participate in class discussion. Readings tend to be the type of material I expected to find in a government class rather than history. You'll also be required to facilitate class discussion 2–3 times by submitting reading questions ahead of time. You will get out what you put in. I learned SO MUCH in this class, and Prof. Aumoithe is incredibly passionate and engaging, truly a great professor. He made me feel very comfortable in class and office hours, and encourages out of the box thinking. He is incredibly knowledgeable on this topic.
Great teacher, very kind and interesting course but a ridiculous amount of time and effort will be spent on this class which makes the positives not worth it in my opinion
Cool class –– a great intro to seminar style History courses at Harvard. Prof. Aumoithe is very knowledgable and approachable, and the conversations we had were quite good because the class was small (approx 9 people). However, I'd caution that the workload can be a bit misleading, as the final paper, final op–ed, and wikipedia pages (ie the three real projects/papers) are all due at once during reading period, so the writing and grading aspect can be quite stressful. Secondly, the course is much more about general ideas than specific healthcare policies, so if you want to get into the weeds of comparative policy, then this course might not be ideal. Nevertheless, this is a truly very interesting course, and falls at a satisfying intersection between Gov and History.
I highly recommend this course but there is a very heavy workload. Take advantage of office hours and extensions if necessary. Also Professor George Aumoithe is great and refreshing. Thoroughly enjoyed his instruction.
If you are good at doing independent work then do it. Don't take it if you are not previously interested in American Healthcare or Welfare. Assignments are vague and are able to be taken in a direction that you decide on. Not that much engagement with the teacher.
