Take this if you're at all interested in thinking about what is just, and what society ought to look like in the future. Adaner is great, one of the best professors I've had thus far.
A truly amazing class, one of if not the best classes I've taken at this school. It is structured so intentionally with interesting readings and amazing conversations that always felt like they ended too early. Classes are structured around number premise summaries of the arguments of the texts we read which makes for deep and meaningful engagement with the ideas presented. There is such a intellectual diversity in the texts that I also really appreciated, forced me to really clarify my intellectual and moral convictions. Adaner is such an amazing professorone of the best I've had herehis feedback is thorough, he is kind and flexible, and he does so much to foster curiosity in and outside of the classroom. It really feels like Adaner is invested in and curious about his students intellectual development in a way I have never experienced from a teacher. This class was one that I was always excited for throughout the week, and I really enjoyed the conversations we'd have after class during coffee chats. The only thing is that there aren't really any assignments related to the final paper so the potential to procrastinate your paper till the end of the semester is presentgood be helpful to set up some intermediate deadlines with him for feedback and accountability. Definitely wish I went to more office hours. Going to miss this class!
I have taken three junior tutorials and Usmani's tutorial is the best social studies class I have taken. His teaching method should be mandatory for all social studies professor he is outstanding. I could not recommend this class enough.
Some of the best discussions in any course I've taken at Harvard. Offers a deep, theoretical, and imperative exploration of capitalism, inequality, and social change that will challenge the preconceived notions of anyone who takes it .
Take this class!! I definitely found it extremely challenging and it teaches you a brand new style of writing and analyzing, but you walk about having learned so much more about how to approach and add to an argument. There was definitely a steep learning curve in terms of the writing and it was one of the most challenging courses I've taken, but I definitely grew a ton from the experience. Adaner's great and it's clear he cares very deeply about the course and every student.
