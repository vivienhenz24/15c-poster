Not all that much not to like! You get to watch some really great American films some are better than others, but all are fun. And the workload is very reasonable: discussion posts, in–class midterm, final paper... and that's it. A really excellent way to fulfill the A&C GENED req!

I do wish the course would dedicate more time on film analysis techniques. The moments in lecture where Rentschler is going through the films shot by shot and directly commenting on them are truly great. Unfortunately this is only around 25% of lecture. The rest is spent with Rentschler offering his glean on a specific theme in American political history. Unfortunately, his arguments are often repetitive and self–evident, dressed up as being some profound interpretation of American history. And he is very verbose.

Anyway, it's still a great course. But lectures would be a lot better if Rentschler threw the pretentiousness aside and just focused on the films themselves, and perhaps their immediate contexts.

I am SO glad that I took this course. I truly had an amazing experience. The workload was present, but manageable (weekly 200–300 responses), the only criticism was the amount of readings for some weeks. Usually they were okay, but especially on weeks where you had to watch two films, we're talking about almost 100 pages of reading which can be quite backbreaking on occasion. The films were so touching, and truly thought out. It made me have an incredibly different outlook on the idea of the American Dream and how American history has shaped this narrative. Furthermore, I feel like I know so much more about films, the canon and the movies that laid the groundwork for what we see now. The lectures were incredibly engaging. I don't think I have even had a single conversation with him, and his office hours are usually zoom and not in person. The section felt more of like the class than the class itself. Overall, I definitely recommend, especially if you're a film aficionado and are interested in seeing the entertainment that brewed alongside US history.
