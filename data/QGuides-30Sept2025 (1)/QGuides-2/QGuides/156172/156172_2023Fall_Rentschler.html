What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Though the topic of the course sounds very specific, and indeed it is, the way it's taught makes the Frankfurt School broadly applicable rather than just being a review of the school of thought itself. This course also explores various perspectives on the Frankfurt School and its theories on mass culture and critique which I found really helpful.
Take this with a grain of salt, but definitely try to learn skills in philosophy/theory reading. Frankfurt School can be a bit confusing, and the class is very fast–paced, making it hard to learn these skills during the semester (at least if you do not want this class to take up your entire week). That being said, I think everyone would benefit from learning this content.
Definitely do the required readings, discussions would be hard without it. The class will take up a good amount of time, so try to balance your schedule. The projects are great though, and allow you to explore the Frankfurt School in your own life. Rentschler is a great professor, will guide you and provide you great feedback on your work
