GENED 1120 is an interestingly structured course. It attempts to give a very broad overview of different topics in political economy, ranging from history to theory to current events, while not containing any particular depth. The week–to–week workload is very reasonable, composed of about 90 minutes of asynchronous lecture videos (watch on 2x speed!) and perhaps an hour of reading material depending on your reading speed and attention span. There will be a weekly reading quiz each lecture, but this is not worth a substantial part of your grade. As long as you do the panopto lecture videos, you will understand most of the course concepts, and the reading can supplement this if you have the time to, but the class should not require more than 3 hours most weeks. Make sure that you participate every week in section, as participation is a decent part of your grade and requires engagement in section in addition to full attendance. Policy memos comprise 40% of your grade and are fairly graded as long as you follow the requirements and look at some of the provided samples. The midterm is hand–written in class and there is a lot of time pressure, and it is generally harshly graded, so make sure that you cover all the points the questions ask for and manage your time well. The final exam is take home but is also challenging, requiring long essay responses, and I assume that it will be harshly graded. However, the overall grade distribution in this class is reasonable and as long as you put in a reasonable amount of effort you should get an A or A–, so I would not recommend taking this class Pass/Fail unless you legitimately have no time to dedicate to this class.
