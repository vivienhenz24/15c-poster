Great preparation for writing an empirical thesis in Social Studies. Matt knows everything there is to know under the sun about the science of research, and he's a great resource to go to.
This course teaches you a lot about research methods, but it doesn't have a topic so you don't feel as though you are learning something very subject–oriented. If you are thinking about doing a non–theoretical thesis, take this course! Matt is fantastic.
This is truly one of those courses where you get as much as you put in. Matt is amazing and always wanting to help while the assignments will really prepare you for the next stage of your thesis. You get a lot of freedom to do what you want with your topic. But you should be prepared to be on top of your work and give your thesis the care it truly deserves, because independence can sometimes turn into laziness (speaking from personal experience). Don't treat it as a requirement, treat it as a great opportunity to understand your topic and think critically about the method you want to pursue!
Matt is such an incredible teacher and person. SS–50 is an objectively tougher course to generate interest for, but he is really the person for the job. He goes above and beyond and makes it enjoyable! You are in good hands if Matt is your teacher.
Social studies students, take this course as your methodologies course!!! Matt is a fantastic teacher (one of the best you'll ever have) and you will leave this course excited for your thesis. I recommend taking the class in the spring, as it lines up well with research grants.
I was first skeptical before the semester began, but this class has singularly been the most important class in helping me construct my thesis. Matt is absolutely wonderful, the class exposes you to both methodological and real thesis concerns (eg. grants, reaching out to faculty advisors) and guides you on how to navigate them. Particularly useful for Social Studies students, but also for other social science department students.
TAKE THIS COURSE. Social Studies 50 was the most helpful class that I have taken in regards to my thesis. The class spends each week going over a different type of methodological approach (which I came in knowing very little about) all while drafting helpful parts of your thesis. Matt is the best, very accommodating, and gives wonderfully helpful feedback on assignments. The sections were optional, but I always wanted to go to them because they helped my work so much. This class was better than I could have imagined, and I recommend it to everyone in Social Studies.
Take this class! Matt is so great and the class is not only interesting, but really useful for thinking about your thesis before senior year. Taking it in the spring will set you up well for summer, but taking it in the fall will set you up for grant applications at the beginning of spring semester. If you are really unsure about what you are writing about, it might be worth it to take it earlier, so you have more time. The readings are not much at all and the assignments build on each other nicely, so by finals period, you really don't have too much to do.
This class was incredibly helpful in helping me build confidence in myself that writing a thesis would be possible. I recommend taking it in the Spring, closer to when you know your prospective thesis topic.
If you are a jr. planning to write a thesis take this course!!!! Matt is the absolute BEST! He provides such great feedback and guidance throughout the process and is just simply such a kind human. This course gives you a very comprhensive look at a variety of research methods. The assignments force you to think critically about your thesis and you walk away with a solid research plan going into the summer. I reccomed taking this course in the fall versus the spring because it prepares you for the summer/ next fall a bit better. Additionally, Matt provides a lot of extra opportunites to help with grant writing and workshops to work through the methods that are super helpful and fun!
