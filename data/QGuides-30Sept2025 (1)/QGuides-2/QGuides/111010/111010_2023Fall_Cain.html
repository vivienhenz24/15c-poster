This course is a fantastic introduction to college–level Maths if you completed a regular international highschool degree (British–/German A–levels). Might be very challenging at the beginning as getting adapted to the new teaching style, especially when English is not your first language but it's a great opportunity to make friends and struggle together with others. I didn't enjoy the MQC, so did P–sets in study groups but many really enjoyed the experience there as well.
Don't be intimidated take this course, there's a lot of support. Try not to miss section, because every section is a new topic and it becomes easy to fall behind. Go to OH and MQC for psets, and start studying multiple days, even a week or more in advance for midterms and final.
Take this class! Very well structured and if you redo the worksheets before the exam you will be totally fine. PSets do not take too long most of the time and a lot of help is provided for them.
the worksheets are most similar to exams!
Avoid this course if you can. It is incredibly challenging for no reason. Difficulty is subjective, but if you took AP calc AB or BC in high school, you'll likely struggle in the course. Not everyone, but many students can get the pests by attending the MQC. But even the MQC office hours and ARC are not good resources because there are not enough CAs for the number of people you need help with. You'll sometimes have to wait 10 minutes to get in one question, which sometimes will need to be answered or answered with a question. If you believe you can get away with taking a higher math course, do it. If you are not strong in calc, I'd recommend taking 1a. This course is NOT worth the time and hassle. This course was not required for my concentration, and I took it because I loved math in highschool. I highly regret it.
This class is great if you enjoy classes. Three psets a week can be a lot but if you're doing them with people and going to the mqc it can be a lot more manageable.
Take this class very seriously, and it will reward you! Many will be tempted drop the class before they have given it a fair chance. The office hours are your best resource and doing the PSETs early is your best friend!
