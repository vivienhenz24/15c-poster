This course is great for anyone who is going into any science or science–adjacent field, but be warned it is a lot of work! If you want to be successful, you must be prepared to set aside hours a day for PSETs and more hours for MQC/office hours. However, I made more close friends through Math 1b than any other class, and I am glad to have taken it.
This class is super nice for making friends, and forming a community around math!!
You don't necessarily need Calc BC, as I never took it in high school, but I do strongly recommend Calc AB. Three PSETS a week that are doable if you go to either MQC or office hours. Personally MQC worked more for me because office hours has people from multiple math courses while MQC is strictly a room full of people who are in math 1b and probably have the same exact questions as you, so you'll have CAs and peers to check your answers or help you find them. I also recommend going to MQC on Monday/Wednesday night vs Tuesday/Thursday because it's usually less crowded. Erica is a great TF that is very friendly and helpful, making the section a positive environment. Edwin is an amazing CA who is very passionate about math and happy to help people during MQC.
Math 1b is hard. It's a lot of work. But I promise you, it is not as bad as people make it out to seem. Before enrolling in math 1b, everyone told me not to take it, but I found it interesting that most people who gave these negative opinions about the class were people who never took it. It's notorious for being really difficult, but I personally believe it's not that difficult. Or I guess I would say it's not too difficult compared to other math classes, 1a and 21a both have 3 psets a week and are the same amount of work. Math 19a and 22a have one pset a week, but their psets are long and hard, so they're probably going to take you just as long to complete as the 3 1b psets. Anyways, if you were placed in 1b, I highly recommend you take it. I was placed in 1b and people told me to skip to 21a, which they thought was "easier", but the 21a people were complaining more than the 1b people this semester. Math 1b truly is not bad at all, as long as you put in the effort. GO TO MQC for pset help. The CAs are SO HELPFUL!!! I personally didn't go TF Office Hours a lot, but the TFs are also there to help you, so go to office hours and see whether they're more helpful for you than MQC. I personally went to MQC every other night. I met some of my closest friends at MQC and had a pset group. MQC was definitely an integral part of my semester. If you took AP Calc AB and not BC in high school like I did, you'll find yourself learning a lot of new material. You might find it a little difficult to adjust your learning to how math classes work at Harvard, but you will for sure get the hang of it. Some of my friends took BC in high school and they knew the material vaguely but the way the concepts are taught here is very different, so if you're not too confident about your knowledge on BC content and want a refresher before jumping into multivariable calculus then 1b is a good choice. You'll still learn a lot, but it wouldn't be as hard as trying to learn something completely new. Anyways, I highly recommend this course. You should definitely take it if you are placed in it. And I promise you it's not as bad as people say, and it does get a lot better. The first midterm was a little rough for me, but the second midterm was better, and I think I did really well on the final! It's definitely a learning curve, but I feel like I learned a lot and will probably take more application–based math classes next fall.
Most importantly, learn to make friends and be generous with your time to others when it comes to explaining things and not being shy to ask someone to compare work at a random office hours/MQC session. There are always other students who are the same
My best advice would be to attend MQC to ask questions about the homework assignments if ever having trouble.
