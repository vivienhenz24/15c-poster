This course is great to learn or brush up on Calculus 2. I felt supported throughout the semester because there were tons of resources. Assignments were graded fairly.
You probably have to take this class, but I promise it's not that bad. Once you get to the last unit you'll be chilling. I took Math1b in the fall and dropped it, and took it again in the spring. I strongly prefer the spring version of this class – the spring p–sets are shorter and overall feels like a better vibe, but maybe that's because Amadeus was leading it this time around and he's awesome. This was my first math class at harvard and my background in high school calculus was very helpful. I'd recommend forming a p–set group early on and getting comfortable asking lots of questions in section. Don't be scared of asking questions!!! Also, shop around with the sections because some section heads are wayyyy better than others, which I found out a little too late in the game. Overall, I got rid of a lot of my math anxiety in this class, so I feel really grateful. I don't think I got an A but I'm still leaving the class pretty proud of what I've learned.
1b is amazing, if Amadeus is in charge!
If you need to brush up on integrations, series, or differential equations, this class is perfect for you. With Amadeus's lesson plans and my TF, I found it easy to grasp the what they were trying to teach.
Make sure to spend time on every problem set to understand the concept and not know how to do it as understanding will help in moments when you may have forgotten how to approach the problem. Also make time for the class as 3 problem sets a week can be heavy if paired with other problem set courses as well. Other than that, the class if very manageable and is interesting!
As long as you pay attention in class and go to office hours or MQC, you will do well in this class. The PSets can be long at times but overall are very helpful for internalizing the material. The exams can be difficult but the study materials provided are a good indicator of the type of questions that will be on them. I definitely recommend having a good relationship with your TF and CAs as they will help you if you have any questions.
