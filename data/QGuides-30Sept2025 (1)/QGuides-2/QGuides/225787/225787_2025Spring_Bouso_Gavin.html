Take this course! I cannot recommend it enough. Professor Bouso–Gavin is a wonderful instructor and takes the time to get to know you so that you can best succeed in the course. Given the small class size Professor Bouso–Gavin does a good job of cultivating a community within the course. I made so many new friends taking this class. The course content and discussions are enriching and help you get a grasp of both foundational texts in this underdeveloped field of study as well as new scholarship, visual arts, and literature that all work to give you a good understanding of Afro–Latinx studies past, present, and possible futures.
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! Processor is so amazing and I learned so much :) He genuinely cares about his students and about the content hes teaching about.
Bouso–Gavns Afro–Latinx Studies class has broadened my perspective on the lived experiences of different individuals within our society. It introduced me to works of visual art, spoken performance, and literary writing by marginalized individuals that explored concepts related to race, class, gender, and sexuality. As a STEM–major, I had never explored these themes in an academic setting, but found the discussions that Bouso–Gavn facilitated to be fruitful and productive. My experience in his class has certainly enriched my Harvard experience.

Overall, I had a great time in the class and really enjoyed the readings/conversations. The class is easy to follow along with and theres so much to discuss/ learn.
