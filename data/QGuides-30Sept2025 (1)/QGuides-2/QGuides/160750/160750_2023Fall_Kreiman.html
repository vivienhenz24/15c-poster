Good class.
There's three things you should know about this course: 1) very interesting material but will lean into CS way more than you expect 2) the workload is quite light 3) despite a light workload, the course is fairly difficult. First, you move beyond neurobiology into CNNs pretty quickly. If you have any interest at all in machine learning this is a fantastic primer and viewing ML models through the lens of neuroscience is fascinating. Second, expect to do one reading response a week, which is not especially time–consuming. However, your reading response needs to have good ideas which means you should have a somewhat solid neuro/computational background. If you write things that don't make sense you will lose a lot of marks, and the papers can get quite technical. So don't expect an easy course, but do expect a course without a lot of work.
