I have nothing but positive things to say about Phil 188a! Professor Matherne is such a lovely instructor, and her passion for the material comes across in each section. Furthermore, this class fills an exciting new place in Harvard's department, and I think that it should be held as the model for a "Philosophy of Literature" discussion–based course; the theme for each week and selection of accompanying philosophers makes a lot of sense for a course of this style, motivating the conversation and allowing us to cover many topics without the risk of being shallow. Furthermore, Professor Matherne's assignments (as in every course she teaches) are wonderful, proving thorough direction with specific but exciting prompts; even more than this, Professor Matherne leaves detailed feedback on assignments, and she makes herself available to help through the writing process. I have nothing but high praise for Phil 188a and Professor Matherne, but, if you decide to take this class, prepare yourself for a *lot* of reading each week!
This is a wonderful course. Professor Matherne knows how to run a stellar seminar. The readings are really well paired, and make for fascinating discussion and she prepares a worksheet for every discussion based on your posts so that there's a guide to the discussion if we need, which really helped to make discussion focused and productive (which sometimes can not be the case in a seminar)
Take any and every class you can with Prof. Matherne, but especially this one! It challenges your notion of what philosophy is supposed to look like and who is supposed to write it. Beware that it is a very reading–intensive course, but the readings mostly Austen novels are very accessible and enjoyable.
This is one of the best classes I've taken here. Professor Matherne is one of the most brilliant professors I've ever had she knows the material inside out, both on the literature and the philosophy side. Even if you don't enjoy reading Austen, it's so worth taking the class to study Austen's technique, the philosophers she s in discussion with, and honestly even just to take a class with Matherne. I can't recommend enough!
I would recommend that anyone interested in Philosophy or Austen or just literature take this class. It's incredibly interesting and fun, and also really fruitful in terms of understanding Austen better and being exposed to different types of philosophical texts. You do read quite a lot but it's manageable and definitely worth it.
This course is a lot of reading every week, so be prepared! Other than that not many assignments really unlike 3 papers, which are not so bad. Prof Matherne emphasizes discussion and understanding the material – participate in class and do the readings and this class has been one of my favorites – you get what you take out of it!
Probably the best, most interesting course I've taken at Harvard. Professor Matherne is I think the best lecturer in the philosophy department – she can explain complicated philosophical texts and synthesize/organize ideas like no one else. Not only is the course super fun because you're reading Jane Austen (duh), but it's actually helped me think a ton about important ethical issue (what I owe other people/friends, what kind of disposition I should adopt towards hardship, etc.). Could not recommend this more highly.
This is an amazing class, and Prof. Matherne is an amazing professor. It does take a significant amount of commitment to read all six of Austen's novels over the course of the semester, with an additional 30–50 pages of philosophy on top of that, but if you are interested in learning more about Austen or Romantic literature in general, it is worth the work. The essays are very approachable, and Prof. Matherne is always very helpful in guiding you through the writing process so long as you are proactive enough to ask for help in an appropriate amount of time.

P.S. A pro tip would be to read a few of the novels before the semester so that you can cut back on the stress of keeping up.
Matherne is great!
