Grading is a little crazy at first, but they eased up towards the end of the semester. Has gemmy aspects but isn't really a gem because you should actually show up to class and section if you want to do well on the homework. We were exposed to some truly beautiful and moving pieces of art and this will be one of the few classes that I will actually remember in twenty years time. As a whole...what did I get out of it? I came in a novice and left pretentious. It was exactly what I wanted.
Such a wonderful class! Professor Abbate is an amazing lecturer and you're not expected to have a music background. I learned so much. The assignments are not too difficult or long but still very impactful and also even fun sometimes! It doesn't feel like there is any busy work in this class. Would highly recommend!
There needs to be separate sections for people that know nothing about opera and those that are more experienced. I went into the class knowing nothing about the topic, which the class encourages, but there sections seemed to cater to those that already knew about opera or music theory. Granted, you did not need to know about either or those things to do well in the course, but it made sections difficult to sit through.
** 100% take this class. Only section is mandatory but lectures are actually engaging and interesting. Assignments are light and graded generously. I took this class with friends, we'd to section go on a Thursday and listen to some opera, then chat about it a bit. We had to go to Boston to see an opera at one point. Super cool. I thought opera was a bit ridiculous before taking this class, now have a decent appreciation for it, and understanding of it. Love this class.
I would like to tell future students to really lean into the assignments, especially the listening ones. Depending on your TF, you may get significant hints on how to complete them during section. Don't hold back; if the music makes you feel a certain way, write that. Strong reactions are favored over moderate reactions.
Nice class and fairly interesting. I was worried for my grade for a good bit since the weekly assignments were kind of hard(especially since I know literally nothing about music) but the grading lightened up at the end of the semester. It doesn't hurt to take, but be slightly prepared to be scared for a bit. Also all the section leaders and professors have a serious passion for opera, and don't necessarily appreciate other's frivolous treatment of it(or the class), which is valid.
I would recommend this course to anyone who likes classical music and is interested in learning about opera. If you don't like classical music, this class may not be amazing. There is a lot of opera and it can be a bit intimidating. I absolutely loved this course. I got exposed to so many different operas and this changed the way I will listen to opera for the rest of my life for sure.
Whether you study Music or not, this class is truly a gem. As a Music concentrator who previously thought she knew a lot about Opera (I was wrong), I learned *so* much in this class. Professor Abbate is just wonderful and the TFs are super friendly, approachable, and helpful. The assignments are always manageable, readings arent too long, and its not too difficult to get a high grade as long as you put in some effort. Truly the definition of you get what you give – lots of people take this class for an easy A and dont pay any attention in lectures, dont contribute in section, etc but if you put in the effort, you will learn so much and have a great time doing it. And, to top it off, theres no final project or exam, so it will make your life easier when finals season rolls around!
Take this course for a relaxed introduction to opera. It's a great way for non–music students to fulfill the arts and humanities requirement and learn a lot!
Take this class!!! It's pretty gemmy, but way more importantly than that, it's incredibly interesting. It's rare to find a class that gives you such a comprehensive introduction to an art form (let alone one that gives you free opera tickets). I learned something new in every lecture, section, and reading. Opera often has a reputation of being inaccessible, and I definitely thought as much before taking this class. Music 20 gives you a really solid base of understanding of both the opera itself and its history. I now feel like I can attend an opera and properly understand it, and more importantly, I'm so excited to see more operas on my own. Take this class, and you'll find that you too will start to go out of your way to attend operas!
Great survey course! Very easy, very gemmy. If you're a serious musician, the class will be valuable in pure exposure, but don't expect to be learning great details. After all, it is a survey course.
Take this class! The content and lectures are fascinating, and even if you don't start out interested in opera, you'll gain (at least) an appreciation for the art. You technically don't need any music background to take this class, but keep in mind that most of the class probably has at least a bit of experience in music, so it'll take some extra work.

Music 20 is a really fun and engaging course, and it's also not that stressful. I kenw nothing about opera, even though some students in the class did, and I took away a lot of things and really enjoyed the course. Your grade is pretty TF dependent; from speaking to students in different sections, assignments are graded pretty tough (maybe average of 22/24 on the portfolio essays?). You should also reach out to your TF and go over your ideas because they tend to nitpick; even though the portfolio essays are supposed to be open to your thoughts, if you don't say some things that they are looking for, they will take points off. Still, pretty chill and fun course overall that I recommend.
This is a really great way to fulfill your Arts & Humanities requirement. You really don't need much music background. I have extensive music background and it was still a fun class to take, so don't let the "no background needed" scare you away either! The workload is really light. You don't really have to do the listening and reading assignments to do well but it helps to be able to participate in section and get background for the listening responses. You should attend lecture though. The writing assignments are low stress and some of them actually fun.
