This is a really awesome course if you are interested in ballet, classical music, or art history! You learn a lot about how ballet began and has transformed into what it is today. Daria is very passionate about the material and there are opportunities to attend the Boston Ballet through this class. A fun chance to learn about ballet's history!
I really enjoyed this course! The professor and TF were such incredible people and all the discussions and readings and excursions were so insightful. Id recommend if you enjoy the art of ballet and are curious about how modern ballet came to be.
Dancers and non–dancers alike could really benefit from learning about ballet in such detail and with so much passion. Professor Khitrova shares both her knowledge and love for ballet in a variety of ways –– through performances, through critics' writings, through watching Boston Ballet, and through the archives of ballet sources –– so there is some way for anyone to connect with the topic. Not only do I have so much more respect and love for the Art, I also really appreciated the skills I was given to watch a ballet on my own and understand what is being communicated.
As a graduating senior I can honestly say that this has been one of my favorite courses at Harvard. Professor Khitrova really brought the material to life and I learned so so much. I wasn't a ballet or dance person, but I really feel like I discovered something new and exciting here. Definitely take this course.
This course is super interesting with fun field trips, passionate professors, and very minimal work. There were two assignments for the entire semester and I had a great time completing both of them, especially my final creative project. If you have any background in classical ballet you should definitely take this course–Daria and Maria are both awesome!
This is an amazing class! If you're a lover of ballet this course is perfect for you. Professor Khitrova is an incredibly knowledgeable, witty, and clever lecturer with a huge passion for the topic, and that really shows through every single one of her lectures. This course will take you through all the major ballet developments, you will get to see some amazing shows (recorded and live!), and the workload is very reasonable! I also loved the creative final project option.
If you are interested in learning more about ballet history, don't hesitate to enroll in this course! It is a great opportunity to put ballet in context with broader art history and see how this beautiful art form has evolved over time.
This is a great course if you're interested in ballet, you will learn a lot of history and the intricacies and nuance of ballet (on a global, socio–political scale) that you won't get from going to a show. There are also great opportunities for creative projects!
This course will mark your academic adventure at Harvard! Be ready to plunge headlong into a wonderful world of ballet, where you will learn a lot about history, aesthetics, and politics. There are so many surprises in store for you! Take it and you'll have one of the most beautiful intellectual experiences in your life!
This course is so great in teaching about the history of ballet. I've always appreciated ballet, but now I can say I appreciate the art so much more after taking this course with Professor Khitrova. The visits to Houghton Library and the Harvard Art Museums were also really informative in looking at primary sources to study ballet.
Take this class! It is amazing and the teaching staff is wonderful. You learn so much about ballet (ie. history, visual culture, etc.) and they are so flexible/willing to work with students to see them succeed.
