Go to classes and engage in discussions. Think thoroughly on daily assignments, they're fun and helpful. Take the readings seriously. No pressure and just enjoy everything!
Before taking this class, I had always thought of health economics as a primarily empirical field, focused on applying microeconometric tools to health–related questions. This course completely changed that impression. It offers a rigorous, theory–driven framework that helped me see health economics as a coherent and scientific discipline. I was constantly challenged and inspired by the material. I often stayed after class to ask questions, and the conversationswhether in person or over emailwith Professor Cutler were incredibly helpful. Listening to other studentsmany of whom are PhD candidatesdebate and dissect the material during lectures was also one of the most intellectually stimulating aspects of the course. Professor Cutler is not only a world–class economist but also a generous and thoughtful teacher who truly cares about student learning. Even as an undergraduate, I felt welcomed in this PhD–level class. If youre willing to put in the effort, this course will not only expand your academic toolkit but also fundamentally change how you think about economics. I couldnt recommend it more.
