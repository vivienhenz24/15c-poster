What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

This is a great class whether or not you really already have data to use, as long as you are excited to learn about the applications of statistics to ecology. Even if you have no experience in R you will be able to succeed in this course.
If you are doing a senior thesis or are an early grad student in bio, this class is INCREDIBLY helpful in teaching you about (the basics of) all the statistical tests and analyses you could possibly need. Also, Ben does a fantastic job of going through the best scientific practices for conducting research from the very beginning before you even have a research question, through the tiniest details like bringing pencils instead of pens and crossing your 0's and 7's, all the way to writing your final proposals/papers. The one thing to keep in mind is that the class is going to be WAAAY easier if you have already started working on your research/thesis so that you have at least an idea of what your results *might* look like. If you don't have a project already, it's still very doable and you can still get a lot out of the class, but maybe not as much as you would if you already were working on something.
It's a great class and a must–take if you're interested in doing research into ecology/biology. It's super helpful for experimental design, set up, thinking about your questions, hypotheses, etc. Also it's great for learning the statistics. However, the statistics can become quite hard at some point and some familiarity with statistics might be helpful. Furthermore, this class is a LOT of work. Especially around midterms and finals. Ben is also a great teacher. He's very kind, knowledgeable and always happy to help. truly an amazing class.
You will gain a different perspective with which to approach your work, however you define it.
This course is very effective at teaching you what you need to know in order to properly use statistical tools and r. It is going to be difficult. But not so difficult that it is impossible. They are there to help you, so use the help. If you have a project you are passionate about, this class will help you apply concepts to your project.
TAKE THIS CLASS! It will help so much with your thesis. It will help so much with learning how to be a researcher. Ben and Calvin are amazing. Literally my favorite teachers here on campus.
Take it if you struggle with coding or statistical analysis! If you have data and dont know what to do with it... this is the course for you!
TAKE THIS CLASS! It is very helpful to have at least some background in ecology & research before you take this, and to have access to some sort of dataset (or an idea for a dataset that you could simulate). Ben is wonderful and provides a ton of support. Psets are very reasonable, as was midterm exam. This class got me through the analysis portion of my thesis.
Unbelievably useful and well taught course. Calvin and Ben are both fantastic actual teachers (not something that can always be said at Harvard), and the information from the class is stuff you will use for the rest of your career if you do anything in ecology (or really any science with statistics)
Superb course. An excellent exposition on statistics as they pertain SPECIFICALLY to ecology. This is very well geared to anyone hoping to perform research after undergrad. Take this course if you have the opportunity to, just know you will get the most out of it if you have conducted research over the summer and have data to work with for the independent investigation.
I would say to take this course if you are working with an ecological dataset. It is almost as if the lectures and weekly sections are a personal workshop where you can always be working on your broader interests. It is an invaluable class!
There's a fair bit of work, but you really learn a lot of useful skills in this course!

This is a great introduction to statistic methods relevant to ecology fields. You will be exposed and challenged to practice different statistical approaches using diverse data sets. While doing so, you will also have freedom to develop your own scientific questions of interest and pursue them as a class project.
