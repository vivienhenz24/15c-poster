Most students this year agreed that the readings and assessments can take up a lot of time â€” much more than previous Latin classes I've taken here. I did appreciate how all students were treated as if we were experts on the material and had important contributions even if I was sometimes very lost in our discussions. If you're required as a languages and literatures track Classics concentrator to take this course, or if you're looking to improve your translation and sight–reading abilities quickly, then this is the course for you, but if you want something less time–consuming and are not feeling prepared for this course, take it the next time it is offered. I'm really glad I took the course when I did and had a good experience with it, but it's good to keep your other commitments and workload in mind.
This class might be useful if you want to survey a selection of different works, but it is very reading intensive.
