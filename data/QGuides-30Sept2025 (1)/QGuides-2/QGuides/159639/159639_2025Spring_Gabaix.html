Ludwig is a fantastic professor – very thoughtful, kind, organized. He explains things very clearly, though I recommend re–reading lecture notes to keep up with all the new topics.
Cool stuff, tough exams
Excellent class. The first half is more of a classic overview of real business cycles and New Keynesian models. The second half is more scattered topics based on Professor Gabaix's research, which were all interesting as well. My one concern is that the class moved a little bit too slowly at times, and we covered less topics than I would like. For example, I would've liked a more in–depth treatment of behavioral macro but we had run out of time. This is probably more attributable to lots of questions being asked rather than a fault of the professors. I would've also liked some discussion of heterogeneous agent models in the first half, but I understand the time constraints. All in all, it was a great way to cap off the first year macro sequence.
I guess this is compulsory for the graduate students, but if you are an undergraduate thinking about taking this class, I would recommend it if you are interested in understanding what graduate macro theory is like. The math is harder than undergrad macro so you might have to spend more time, but the concepts you learn are really interesting and it's very cool to see the models from undergrad macro like the NK model more fully fleshed out. Opens up a lot of exciting research fields, and I felt more comfortable reading macro research/theory papers after taking this class.
Ludwig is one of the best instructors in the whole department!
