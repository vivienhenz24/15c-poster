It is an unmatched opportunity to hear about the world–class physics research at Harvard, and I was surprised by how enthusiastic they were to talk with us. Also, it is very low commitment because there are only 3 short papers about anything you want and a final presentation.

My #1 recommendation in the department.
THIS IS A GREAT COURSE. Professor Samuel is such an amazing human being and very smart. It was interesting to hear him ask insightful questions during talks. The course format involves meeting twice a week: visiting a research lab on Mondays, usually in LISE, Lyman, Jefferson, Northwest, or sometimes Mallinckrodt, and attending a talk on Tuesdays. There is free dinner in Lyman before the talk with the professor, where they discuss their research and career journey, and it has always been fun attending the dinners.

Besides attendance, we had to write three response papers. These papers are about what piqued our interest from the talks and research lab visits. Writing the response papers might seem intimidating at first, but it is actually not bad and can be interesting if you ask the right question. There was also a final presentation at the end about one of the response papers. It was not a lot of work, just a brief 10–minute summary of the response paper.

Professor Samuel and Armaan have been amazing, and they are so nice and understanding. You will also learn a lot about the research happening in Harvard labs from the visits and talks.
This class is a great opportunity to learn more about physics, and expose yourself to research options.
This class is so, so fun!!!! I loved the lab tours and the talks––this class helped me realize I wanted to go to grad school and it gave me great opportunity to really engage with current research. I took this class having done some research and physics courses already and that gave me the perfect background to meaningfully engage with the talks. The assignments are not overwhelming, there's lots of opportunity for feedback. They really help you learn to think critically about frontiers in research by asking questions that sometimes people don't know the answers to.
I had an amazing experience in this class. It's a great way to learn more about what research looks like on campus, and Professor Samuel and Armaan are the best. Great class taught by some of the kindest and most understanding people you will meet at Harvard. I highly recommend it!
