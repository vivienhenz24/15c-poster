What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This was by far the best course of the entire program. I learned more in this class than the entire first year econometrics series
A great class for seeing how econometrics is actually used in research. The methods covered are modern and framed in the context of real–world applications, especially financial research. The course emphasizes when and why to use different tools, highlighting how messy applied work can be.

That said, much of the content builds on graduate–level econometrics, so to get the most out of the class, you'll want at least a strong undergraduate metrics background. The assignments are not designed to teach the techniques from scratch. Rather, you're expected to apply them in open–ended, research–like settings: tasks like reproducing tables from papers using public datasets and libraries, writing referee reports, and completing a final paper or presentation.

Even if your grad–level econometrics is rusty, the structure of the course still allows you to engage and succeed. Even if half the lectures are over your head, you'll still learn a lot and be able to complete the assignments.

The workload was reasonable overall, but Assignment 3 (constructing equity factors using WRDS) was challenging, requiring a WRDS account and working with SAS code. Overall, the TA and professors were responsive and helpful, so definitely reach out if you get stuck or if the instructions aren't clear.
