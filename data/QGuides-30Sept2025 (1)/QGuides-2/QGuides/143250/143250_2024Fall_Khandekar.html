TAKE THIS CLASS OH MY GOD. one of the best, potentially the single best classes i've taken at harvard. narayan is amazing, as are the rest of the conservators and scientists that lead various components of the class. LOTS of detailed feedback on assignments, which is super helpful as the kinds of papers you write for this class are a bit different from those in other art history classes. narayan is clearly very passionate about the topics he teaches and about teaching his students the skill of looking closely at an object and thinking incredibly deeply about its creation and past. this class is amazing. take it take it take it
This is one of the best classes offered at Harvard! You receive hands on experience with witness conservation and have access to the conservators for projects they have worked on. Everyone is super responsive and create a wonderful environment that generates curiosity and learning.
HAA 206 will be a really interesting class if you enjoy the Harvard Art Museums space and really want to get down into the research that the conservators and scientists are pursuing. It is one of the leading facilities in the technical art history field! That being said, if your interests are very specific for art history, some of the objects may not be very interesting which can be difficult when you have to spend so many weeks with them. The conservators and scientists are all very kind and helpful, though Dr. Khandekar can be very exacting about the quality of work you produce. Relatively chill week–to–week but prepare to put in a lot of work for the written reports.
This course is pretty interesting. I think it's a good way to fulfill the distribution requirement. It will be taught be a different prof next year (Georgina) who I think will also be really good. The assignments can be pretty annoying since we had to give a long presentation and then write a paper that was the exact same thing as the presentation, but we did the paper individually rather than with our presentation groups. That was weird but not such a big deal. Overall, I think it's a pretty easy class that I learned a lot from which is pretty ideal in my opinion.
Take it!
The instructor team is incredibly supportive in accommodating the learning objectives of each individual, regardless of their background. Even if you, like me, have little understanding of the subject, you can learn so much through this object–based, lab–setting learning process, guided by experts in the field. The course also offers a rare opportunity in presenting the behind–the–scenes efforts involved in bringing artwork from storage to display. Strongly recommended.
I would highly recommend this class! Dr. Khandekar is AWESOME and the material is just facinating. You get to look at so many different pieces in the museum collection. The primary assignments are pretty much all group projects and reports on the group projects, so be warned if that isn't your thing. Quite worth it as an elective. It really was so cool to take a class at the art museum!!
