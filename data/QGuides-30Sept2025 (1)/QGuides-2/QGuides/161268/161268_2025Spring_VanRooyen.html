BEST CLASS I'VE TAKEN AT HARVARD. Take it, or regret not taking it. There is no other class like it. If you have even a remote interest in humanitarianism, TAKE IT.
Make sure to ask questions during class.
Cons:
This class has a lot of potential, especially in terms of content, but it falls short due to the teaching style and assessment methods. There is a lot of busy work for a class that did not even fill half of its seats this semester. Each week included a short response, a "muddiest point" question that was never discussed in section, and a quiz that was often outdated or overly confusing. The midterm involved both a paper and a group presentation. The paper felt unnecessary, and there was very little time to complete the midterm. Most of us had to work on it over spring break. The staff focused too much on presentation style instead of the substance of the content. Their insistence on using Chicago–style citations was absolutely ridiculous. It often seemed like no effort had been made to update the course materials or lecture slides for the current semester, which was frustrating. Staff were often just as confused or unaware of what was going on as us. There was also no time for in–class discussion, since the professor lectured straight through the two–hour block. During the simulation, we met a number of people working in the field, but I did not come away with much knowledge about humanitarian work itself. The experience was mostly sitting around in the woods and getting bitten by bugs. Grading was extremely inconsistent and unclear. It felt subjective and unpredictable, and grades took a long time to be released. This made it hard to know how we were doing in the class. Staff did not take any meaningful steps to address concerns and instead offered vague or placatory responses that felt more frustrating than helpful. I also wish there had been a more technical or skill–based component to the course. Overall, I would not recommend this class. With the right structure, it could be really engaging and inspiring, but the current format does not support that. The lack of interest this semester reflects those issues, and I do not expect that to change unless the course is redesigned.

Pros:
I appreciated that the instructors acknowledged how much the humanitarian field was shifting this semester and adjusted the midterm to reflect that. The simulation, while flawed, was an interesting concept. The best part of the course was hearing from the teaching team. They each brought different experiences, perspectives, and roles from the humanitarian sector. Those personal insights were genuinely interesting, and I enjoyed learning from them.
This course is one of the best at Harvard! The faculty are so amazing and they show us so many different pathways to do this important work. As long as you are genuinely interested and engaged, the work is super manageable and it is just a great way to learn and explore this field. I 100% recommend this class.
I would like to tell future students to TAKE THIS CLASS!!! This course was genuinely one of the best courses I have taken at Harvard. It was truly life–changing and the teaching staff is second to none. You absolutely will not regret it, and you will feel so much more connected to both local and global communities.

Take this class!!!!
I would 100% recommend taking this class. It is definitely one of my favorite classes I've taken at Harvard. Dr. VanRooyen is an incredibly engaging lecturer and one of the kindest and most knowledgeable people on this subject matterit is a privilege to get to learn from him. The teaching staff was also so sweet and incredible to haveI feel like we all became friends by the end of the class (which is pretty rare). The case studies we go through are super interesting and the material feels highly relevant today (in a time when funding cuts to the humanitarian situation pose serious challenges). The assignments were very manageable and I liked the group work because it took stress out of completing a large research project. Lastly, the humanitarian simulation was truly a once–in–a–lifetime experience and I learned so much about myself through doing it.
This is a great course to explore humanitarianism. If youre interested in a heavier introduction with more technical aspects, consider petitioning for the graduate school course. As an undergraduate, make use of the teaching staffs connections to talk to professionals in the field and potentially getting involved in research. Use midterm and final assignments as opportunities to dive deeper into specific issues and make sure to coordinate well and cohesively with your classmates. Also, try to get the teaching staff to talk as much as possible about their experiences in the field, they have so much to talk about. While the simulation was enjoyable, finding ways to either experience the aspects of it that align with your own interests or talking to the other volunteers when possible will make for a really great experience to talk to experts working in the humanitarian space. I somewhat wish I had taken this earlier during my time at Harvard because this would have been a great introduction to the field and to the people at Harvard who could have helped me shape my later coursework and thesis into more of a humanitarian endeavor.
AMAZING CLASS!!! This class was such a unique environment where you are surrounded by people from all walks of life who share a common interest in humanitarian aid and learning about the strenghts/weaknesses of these systems. Dr. VanRooyen, Matt Jelacic, and Dr. Michelle Gonnet Gonzalez showed genuine curiosity for our interests and goals, and made sure to format the class in a way that fulfilled the whole class's interests. They are extremely well connected in the humanitarian field and care about their students well–being first and foremost. I took this class because I knew I wanted to work in the humanitarian sector and this sentiment was very apparent throughout the rest of my classmates. Short responses and readings were very manageable. The group projects were great. I appreciated having the opportunity to get to know my classmates better outside of class and all of the presentations are amazing since they are all on different world issues. My only complaint was that it was difficult to prepare for the in–class quizzes since the slides were not regularly posted and they really push for discussion over note–taking in class. I also would've appreciated small–group brainstorming sessions throughout lectures since this class attracts such varying perspectives and background education that you learn a lot very quickly from discussion with your peers. This was by far my favorite class that I have taken for Harvard and it's the first time I felt a class was actively preparing me for my career aspirations. I couldn't recommend this class more!
