It's a lot of fun and I learned a lot!
TAKE THIS CLASS!! I constantly found myself having "that's the coolest thing I've eve heard" moments in this class. Does any of this sound interesting to you?
– how to fabricate electrical circuits at the scale of nanometers using patterned light–sensitive chemicals
– how to deposit something ATOMIC LAYER BY ATOMIC LAYER. Like. Atoms. ATOMS!!!
– the intersection of physics and chemistry with applications in EVERYTHING. Neuroengineering, sustainability, immunoengineering, and quantum physics were just a few of the final project ideas
THEN YOU SHOULD TAKE THIS CLASS. You will walk away from the course with a firm practical and theoretical grasp of major microfabrication techniques, such as photolithography, physical & chemical vapor deposition, atomic layer deposition, reactive ion etching, and more.

Kiyoul is the sweetest professor I have ever had. You will meet AMAZING people, even for Harvard, and Kiyoul and the teaching staff put so much effort into building a small community amongst the class, whether it's taking us out for dinner after labs or bringing us Tatte during class. The TFs are the true GOATs, going into the lab on weekends for 8 hours at a time (unpaid) just because they want us to have a good lab experience.

Don't be intimidated by the 4 hour lab it's usually 50% sitting around and doing your PSets since many of the processes only require 1–2 people (you can of course still watch if you're interested). Take 277 if you can the only difference is a final project!
this class has to be the most chill, gem, grad class. kiyoul is such a supportive and friendly professor. he is so thoughtful in curating the course material to cater to our interests and learning and is soo approachable outside class time to discuss class content or anything related to subjects of his interest. the labs are incredibly fun, and you can truly see how the concepts you learn in class translate into a device you fabricate on your own. definitely one of the highlights of my semester
The CNS microfabrication clean room is an incredible resource that Harvard students are very lucky to have access to through ES 277. Kiyoul is an exceptionally kind and personable professor who places great emphasis on fostering academic relationships between ES 277 students that will last beyond the semester. Though the in–class/in–lab experience of ES 277 can be intimidating at times, the Kiyoul, the teaching staff, and the CNS staff are very supportive and will help you with anything. ES 277 requires more in–class work than the typical class, but it is made up for by a comparatively low homework load.

I highly recommend this class to people who plan to work in microfabrication or immediately adjacent fields (e.g. circuit design, or photonics). If you are signing up for this class just because it seems cool or will look good on your resume, please consider leaving the spot for a student whose work will more directly benefit from their involvement.
