I do not recommend this class unless you are genuinely really into Borges' literature and that's it. If you're taking this class to practice speaking Spanish in a seminar format like as in other upper–level Spanish class, then don't. If you're taking this class as a non–native Spanish speaker to improve your reading skills, this probably won't help either. We had the option of reading everything in Spanish or English and they were still quite confusing in English, and some were almost unitelligible in Spanish. If you're a Comp Lit concentrator who actually puts effort into all of your readings, then you might actually like this class. But otherwise you just sit there for 3 hrs per week and listen to rambling about Buenos Aires. The Friday 3–6:15pm was brutal (2 hr lecture followed by a really awkward 1 hr section), but also kind of great because you only have to be there once a week and get it over with, and don't really have to spend time on this class outside of that time block. That also makes it quite easy to do well in this class. So if you want an easy class and are okay with wasting your time, then take it for sure. But if you actually want to learn and improve your Spanish, and you don't care all that much that it's about Borges, then don't take this.
TAKE THIS CLASS!!!! Mariano is a fantastic professor, the content is super interesting, and Borges is a truly fascinating author. I really cannot recommend it enough.
This course: Good reading and Borges is very cool, but the course itself may be too much lecturing. It is a small class size so it feels like the class does not take advantage of the potential for more breakout discussion etc.
Mariano and Anita are both incredibly kind, knowledgable, and understanding individuals who want all students of the course to love Borges and understand his work. Many of the readings are incredibly difficult to understand (in Spanish AND English) until the class discussions where the genius behind them is revealed and can be fully grasped. You can get away with shoddier writing and reading skills in Spanish, but you must be able to understand Spnaish auditorily, as Mariano and Anita seldom speak in English.
Take this course if you are interested in Spanish Literature, it was provide you an introduction to one of the greatest literary minds of World Literature and the Latin American position in World Literature.

Outside of the excellent discussions he leads, he is genuinely a very funny and approachable person. Every class feels very comfortable, welcoming, and fun.

Seems intimidating, based on the readings and the length of the class. Readings are always short and manageable, don't worry about getting them done. English translations are also offered, which is helpful if you're not a native speaker and pressed for time. The class itself CAN BE BORING – Mariano repeats himself a lot, and if you tune out, you really can start snoozing. I recommend that you TUNE IN! remain engaged! Mariano is a phenomenal professor with great answers to your questions, and his enthusiasm is infectious. It's tough to be in a 2 hr class on a Friday evening, but it was totally worth it. The midterm and final are very, very easy, especially if you pay attention / take notes in class.
Marino is the greatest professor at least in Romance languages but probably all of the humanities. Toma su curso y tmalo en serio
I would strongly recommend this course for those interested in opening their minds to complexity and different ways of seeing the world. I was familiar with Borges' reputation prior, and had attempted to read some of his works in Spanish, but I feel this course really brought to life how versatile and profound his work can be and how it transforms the reader in the process. Borges is the creator of the stories, of course, but Prof. Siskind brought him to life for us – he really shines through in this course, both for his enthusiasm for the subject matter, his approachability, and expertise in Borges and literature more broadly – great course!
