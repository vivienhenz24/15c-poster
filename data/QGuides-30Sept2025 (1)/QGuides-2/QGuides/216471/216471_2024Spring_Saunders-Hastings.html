This class was incredible. I did not think I would enjoy political theory, but this course proved me wrong! We discussed so many important, contemporary issues from a theoretical perspective, from abortion rights, to climate change, to prison reform and more. Professor S–H did a wonderful job teaching the class and leading engaging discussions, which was especially admirable considering we didn't have sections. She will be missed! But if next year's syllabus is anything like this year's, I would encourage all to take it.

With no prior experience with philosophy, I found this course pretty manageable. The instructor does a good job of providing guiding questions for readings. I thought it was extremely useful to understand the readings. That said, the course did focus a lot on the readings. The reading assignments consisted of about 1–2 hours of reading but was nothing terrible. The professor talks about the readings and asks questions throughout the lecture about then. I would highly encourage students to do the readings because you will be lost without doing them. I really did like the discussions that we had in–class. It can get confusing sometimes but slowly you start to understand things. Overall, I think it's a good course if you're considering taking a philosophy or government class. I liked taking a new philosophical approach to government.
***Definitely take this class if you're interested in learning more about the ethical sides of public policy, need to fill that requirement for the public policy track, or just need an easy gov elective. The class is very low commitment but I still felt like I learned a lot – only a midterm, final, and one assignment which you can do in groups which doesn't take too long. Readings are a bit dense but still interesting – overall highly recommend, although if you're super interested in ethics or political theory generally this might be a bit too basic.
ESH is a visiting prof so I'm not sure she's teaching it again, which is a shame, because this class was great! The workload is very manageable, and I really looked forward going to class to have interesting discussions on different topics in political theory. Although this class is lecture–based, lectures were very interactive and we were able to discuss our opinions freely. For the exam, ESH tells you all the topics that could show up, you can be prepared. This class is a great way to fulfill the theory requirement, or if you are just interested in political theory in general.
This is an excellent class that enjoins you to consider a variety of ethical issues across a bunch of public policy fields. It's more of a survey in nature, but all of the readings are at a level that is very accessible to folks from any background and experience. Yet it also covers a lot of the philosophy hits, for example, Mill and Bentham.
