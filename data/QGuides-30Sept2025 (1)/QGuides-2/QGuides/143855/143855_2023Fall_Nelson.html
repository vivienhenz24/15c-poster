What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Lectures and reading material are great –– but don't stress too much about grades, methods of assessment are challenging and imperfect
The first half of this course focuses on crystal lattices, phonons, and band structure, which I found pretty understandable and well–taught. But I had no idea what was going on during the second half. This might've been because I had forgotten a lot of E&M, but I also think the course tries to cover too much material without giving you time to understand it. David Nelson is an engaging lecturer, but his lectures don't always align with the textbooks and he doesn't post notes. The PSET problems don't really align with lectures either, and exams required too much mathematical derivation in a short amount of the time. Chenyuan is a very generous grader––as a result, I feel like I winged my way through exams and PSETs without understanding much, if anything at all. Overall, there's a lot of useful material in this course if you want to do condensed matter research, but with David teaching, the course isn't good for actually understanding the material.
Strong mathematical ability is essential for this course. Be careful about it
This class is extremely good for anyone looking to introduce themselves to solid state physics, while gaining an in–depth understanding.
I think the course is hard, and sometimes due to its structure is unnecessarily stressful. However, it is informative and I think if you are willing to take it and not be too worried about your grades, it is a helpful course to have taken if you are interested in solid state.
