If you are one of these individuals, maybe taking the course pass/fail would be a good idea! Otherwise, as long as you put in the work for this course, do the readings, attend lectures enough to do well on the assignments, and participate in sections, just take this course for a letter grade. I would recommend this class. I guess you don't really need to go to lectures or do the readings, but I would really recommend putting in effort to learn what this class has to teach. Professor Davis is a really smart lecturer and there is a LOT of material that she covers every lecture–––although not all of the material is relevant for quizzes/essays, following along will teach you a lot about global trade. The first half of the course (the history of trade in East Asia) is REALLY interesting, but by the second half when we started talking about WTO dispute procedures it got a bit boring. I'd still recommend, though. You can get an A if you put in the work and take advantage of your TF/office hours.

This class fulfilled two requirements for me and I still regret taking it. There were definitely some big upsides though. Professor Davis and the rest of the course staff are incredibly lovely. Trade is a super important topic and it was great to get an introduction to it as well as to East Asian history. That being said, Davis is a very dry lecturer. By the end of the semester I was one of a handful of students who still attended the lectures and I honestly don't blame others for not showing up. The class was additionally really strangely structured, being neither chronological nor going by theme. It felt like we were haphazardly hopping from topic to topic such that one got the sense that the class was simultaneously doing too much and not enough. Overall not the worst class in the world, not a super heavy workload, nice teaching staff, but really nothing to be impressed by–– could be done so much better.
