What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Suitable for grad/mature students who do not need a lot of guidance throughout the semester and have the discipline to stay on top of readings. Prof Unger is keen to discuss during the class, which makes it interactive and engaging.
This class is pretty cool! It presents a new alternative to our current economy structure that isn't something I've read before. It's a bit of a self learning class but if you read the book, "The Knowledge Economy" by Unger and go to section, you should understand everything quite well!
I don't believe this course is worthwhile. The droning nature of Unger's presentations failed to foster consistent engagement during lecture periods. The course also suffered as it failed to provide an objective overview of contemporary political–economic theory; starting the course with this basis could have made Unger's ideas more compelling. Assignment expectations are broad and sometimes unclear, and Unger often repeated himself during or across lectures in a way that made me tune out. Unger is also not the most responsive to questions. Ultimately, other courses might better serve students wishing to learn about political economy.
