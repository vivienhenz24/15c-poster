What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
The classes and reading list felt disconnected at times, so make sure you thoroughly understand the readings and ask the professor if in doubt. He is happy to help, but you'll be on your own if you don't as slides were not sufficiently detailed to help with some questions. I think the class could really benefit from slides being made available in advance, since the calendar seemed to not line up with the reading list, so it was difficult to prepare sufficiently for class. Interesting material covered, but I didn't always feel as though the class supported my learning.
I totally recommend this class. Even if you are not completely interested in trade, this course will give you a good set of tools to tackle questions in different fields. Additionally, since trade is permeating through different topics (like growth, contract theory, inequality, labor economics, etc.) I think is a good instance to know where potential avenues for collaboration are.
A good crash course to get you up to speed on all things trade, though with a heavier emphasis on theory than empirics.
