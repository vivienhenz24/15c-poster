The course was really loosely structured. I think that Prof Koellner has a brilliant mind about the area, but without a good structure, we didn't have any depth. A lot of the class discussion devolved into bringing up hypotheticals about the dangers of AI. I didn't feel like there was much substantive discussion.

I enjoyed the final project component and I think Prof Koellner was very available to give feedback and provide his experienced insight.
This was one of the worst pedagogical experiences I've ever had. The course had no syllabus and no assignments so weekly discussions were often unfounded and hand–wavy. This class only met once a week, and yet at least 3 meetings were canceled and others pushed to Zoom. I honestly felt cheated out of what could've been a cool learning experience.
This class requires extremely little work outside of lecture. It talks about cybersecurity and requires very little prior knowledge. I'd recommend it if you want to take an easy and interesting class!
This class is super interesting! There is so much to learn from the lectures in this class, which will change the way you view the infrastructure and technology around you. Additionally, the discussion component of the course allows you to learn from your peers and hear diverse perspectives on topics like privacy and AI. Finally, the course allows you to explore a topic of interest with a research paper, giving you the time and flexibility to learn a lot about a topic you care about.
This class felt a bit disorganized, but overall the seminars had enjoyable discussions and they were quite relaxed. There isn't a lot of guidance on the final paper and its quite open–endedthis is something that I appreciated but every student might not.
