It was so cool to learn about something completely new. The professor is awesome and has such an amazing set of experiences he draws from. Totally take this class.
As a graduating senior, this was a very refreshing and straight up fun course. It was the first class I took where every person was fully willing to participate, even if they looked 'stupid' or silly. This is largely helped by the fact that Pete is a very warm and welcoming instructor. His passion for theater and these techniques is admirable and make the course what it is.

This class has minimal out–of–class work, which makes it great if you're taking p–set classes. However, do not take it for granted – this means you have to show up and actively participate in class. Overall, I really recommend this course!
Embodied learning, creative play, and cultural understanding are all critical aspects to developing as a well–rounded scholar. It balanced my academic experience at Harvard to grow and learn from a course that was not scriptocentric for a change.
A fantastic course that I looked forward to every week. It helped my movement abilities and therefore artistry.
This class is awesome and pushed you out of your comfort zone in unconventional ways. It is so much fun. You wouldn't understand just how much fun it is unless you took the class. We learn tai chi, yoga, cartwheels, headstands as well as more technical theater strategies like character archetypes, mask work, and grid work. Professor Simpson cares more about effort and attitude than he does about your actual execution of the physical work (as in... you don't actually have to execute a full–blown headstand in order to do well in the class). He also ensures a comfortable and safe environment conducive to this type of learning.

Take this if you want to become a better actor or director because it will help hone your eye for body language and teach you new ideas about how to use movement to tell a story. Take this if you don't want that and you just want to be more in tune with your own body and have fun. This is an excellent class if you want something less reading/writing heavy, or as a 5th if you're feeling especially gungho one semester. Pete, the professor, is the longest–running blue man group member, and he is just such a cool guy and you cannot help but be inspired by his passion for the work and inspired by his attitude when it comes to theater and different approaches to storytelling.
