What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class was very fun! I loved everything that we learned about and it helped me develop new interests and really see how much chemistry is in our real life.
If you come from a non–chemistry or non–material science background and need to work with any kind of organic material, take this class! Joanna is an expert in material science and knows everything about organic materials. She covers a broad range of topics, including material structure, properties, andmost importantlytheir applications. These applications span various fields such as biology, medicine, the oil industry, environmental science, and more.

The small class size allows Joanna to tailor the content to each students prior knowledge and interests. Joanna and my TF (Reena) are incredibly welcoming and always available to help you succeed!
Take a different course instead, or take this when Reena is not the TF. Professor Joanna is very passionate about her job and is relatively good at teaching. The class structure doesnt make much sense though – you spent the first few weeks cramming so much material and then the last few weeks youre going over all the nature papers the teaching staff has published. The quizzes are too long and stressful for anyones good and It feels like Reena rolled a dice every week to determine how harshly she wanted to grade the problem sets.

Also, sat in the course with people that had multiple chemistry degrees and random undergrads and somehow everyone gets curved the same way. Wouldnt take.
I would like to say that it takes ~8 hours per week to do problem sets and prepare for quizzes.
