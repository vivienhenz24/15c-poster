This class is extremely redundant with BCMP301qc and will build upon the concepts introduced in that course but may not provide much new content or understanding.
Form a good pset group quickly! The psets are looking for relatively specific answers, so go over your answers with a fine–toothed comb.
If you are someone interested in drug development and chemical biology, this is a must take. It features a set of phenomenal outside speakers, a wide array of diverse and interesting topics, and plenty of depth into each of the topics from a scientific lens. I took this class as an undergrad, so if you are an undergrad who has taken some chemical biology/biochemistry and organic chemistry (and some biology), you should be good to go to understand (and get a lot out of) the class. Psets take about 4 hours once every 2 weeks, so it isn't much work. However, grading is fairly arbitrary and takes a ton of time (at the time of the last class, only 2/6 psets had been graded). You can randomly get points off on the questions for no reason if you do not include some very specific things in your answer that they are looking for, but I think grading should work out in the end for everyone. Journal club is also very low pressure and very fun. Take the class!
