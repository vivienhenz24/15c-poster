This class is just absolutely wonderful, and while the workload is a little intense at times doing the readings well and deeply engaging with them before class is worth it to be able to get the most out of the daily discussions! This is the most work out of the CS 270 series courses, but it also covers the broadest range of topics, and tackles some really important issues in tech design. This class is called Design, Technology, AND Social Impact, not FOR Social Impact––we tackle some of the important questions around design in class but don't always come up with the answers. Get started on your final project sooner rather than later, and don't stress too much about the milestones––they're for your own pacing more than anything else.

Take this class.

The class sharpened my ability to engage with research papers critically and provided a framework to assess the effectiveness and impact of socio–technical projects. CS 276 is, by and large, an exercise in making meaning. You will make meaning through dialog with your classmates in what may be the world's most discussion–centric engineering course. You will make meaning through social engagement with the stakeholders of the things that you engineer. You will make meaning through introspective reflection on what personal values and motivations have led you down the path of engineering in the first place. You will make meaning by generating a design that is tailor–made to fit the needs of humans you care about. The class will challenge your long–held views and primary assumptions about the practice of engineering. It will probably be more existentially challenging than any engineering course you've taken before. But that is only all the more reason you should take it, and become a better engineer for it. If you care about the social impact of your work, or are looking for direction in how to perform more socially–conscious engineering, then this is the course for you.
