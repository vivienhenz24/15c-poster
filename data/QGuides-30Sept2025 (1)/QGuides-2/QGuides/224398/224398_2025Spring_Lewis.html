What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I loved the micro–history approach of this class. The readings were highly interesting and engaging and the exercises we did in section reinforced our understanding of the material. Niko is also an amazing TF and very helpful and available. However, the work does build up at one point we had an essay every other week and an entire book. One week was annotated bibliography, a historiographical essay, a primary source essay, a final paper draft. Some of the assignments felt a bit like teaching you the methodical approaches so if you already had background, it can feel a bit pointless. I did love reading all my peers final essays though they were so amazing and I genuinely loved it.
If you have any interest in microhistory as a discipline, this sophomore tutorial is a great choice. Professor Lewis' syllabus gives you exposure to a fascinating variety of microhistories that open your eyes to the myriad applications of this alternative approach to the retelling of history.
This course will improve your writing (I didn't really believe the students at first who had taken this in the past) but I can say with full confidence that your writing will improve. You will be humbled beyond belief but it will help you gain an awareness of the type of writer you are, what your pitfalls are, and where your strong suits lie. You may even find a topic or area of study you are interested in thesising in too!
