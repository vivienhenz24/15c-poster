If you haven't taken math classes (aside from statistics) since high school, this course will be very difficult unless you do a heavy review of matrices and calculus.
Greg does an excellent job, really really phenomenal and stand–alone quality across the many courses I've taken at a variety of institutions. He's got his finger on the pulse of what students are capable of, how to teach complicated material, and how to make it immediately applicable to research (with problem sets that imitate what you might write in research papers) while building foundational intuition about econometrics. If I manage to become a professor someday, Greg's approach to this course is my playbook, I've seen nobody do it better. What a joy, honestly, this course was to take.
Take it! It's really useful
Great class! would recommend for applied policy researchers who don't to be stuck in theory–world all day
This course is BY FAR one of the most helpful economics courses at Harvard. Coming from a math/econ undergrad who took the undergraduate analog (Econ 1126), I felt that I walked away knowing a lot of theory without much practice, such as how to implement panel regressions, binary outcome models, etc. In contrast, a considerable emphasis in this course is applying challenging theory to data sets. You will get a chance to not only learn the theory, but then turn around and code everything as the problem sets are very applied. This class deepened my research skills and understanding of econometrics considerably.
Greg is a really kind and organized instructor. This course provides a lot of information and the psets feel constant, but I feel like I learned things and received resources / notes that will help me in the future.
I would highly recommend this class for anyone (including undergraduates) with the right prerequisites (classes in probability, inference, linear algebra)! The course has a good mixture of mathematical derivations and empirical applications, and you will learn a ton of useful skills through the problem sets. Dr. Bruich is a great lecturer and his course materials are great foundations to econometric studies.
1) professor bruich is the best. it is not only clear that he cares about his students, but he is a very engaging and talented lecturer and you will learn a lot from him. the class is designed for you to leave with more knowledge than you came, not just to memorize it for a short time and forget it shortly thereafter. if you are looking for econometric training, I cannot recommend it more
