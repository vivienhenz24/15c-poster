Comments
This is a very good class to take if you are at all interested in the climate crisis and want a slightly more technical look into the causes and effects of it than you could get in a course like confronting climate change. It is very approachable and a relatively light workload so long as you go into it with an open mind.
This course is a great introduction to a wide range of climate related concepts. I would prepare for some chemistry and physics, but overall, you will leave this class knowing so much more about earth processes and climate change.
This class is a very good introduction to earth science. The assignments are a reasonable difficulty, the workload is very tolerable, the midterms are an appropriate difficulty, and the course doesn't assume many prerequisites. Overall, this class should be useful and manageable to a wide variety of students.
Take time to study the lecture notes before the beginning of the next class and take advantage of office hours if you don't understand something. It can be very useful in passing the mini quizzes.
This course covers a lot of content in a way that is generally thorough but also accessible to those without extensive background in environmental sciences, and I feel I have a much better understanding of fluid Earth processes as a result. At times, the presentation of concepts in lecture is unclear, but the handwritten notes that Professor Linz provides are helpful to review, especially in preparation for the midterms. I highly recommend attending Professor Linz's office hours with specific conceptual questions. The problem sets and labs are reasonable.
This class is made solely by your TF; if you have a good TF you will have a great experience, but if you do not have a good TF you will probably not find this class very rewarding.
Take this course if you are looking for an accessible and broad introduction to climate science, Dr. Linz really cares about teaching and that care is reflected in the high quality teaching materials
Overall the course specifically the psets and midterms was more difficult than expected
This course was really well taught and the TFs were incredible but I would not say it is an introduction course. If you do not have a background in science then you will likely struggle through this class and it will require much more work and time. The coursework is really interesting if you find yourself wondering about the history of geological earth and how these systems are perpetuating climate change and global warming. I recommend for STEM majors but otherwise would offer hesitations.
