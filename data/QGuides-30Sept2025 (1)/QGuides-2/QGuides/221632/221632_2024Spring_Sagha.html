Take this course if you are interested in learning more about the history of the Middle East and the context through which current events are occurring. Professor Sagha does a great job of not just sticking with Iraq and Afghanistan, but understanding the broader architecture of the Middle East (including Iran and the Gulf states). At the same time, one should come in aware that there might be a slight ideological bias; however, it is a perspective that many people have, and certainly should be considered and studied.
This class is easy. I would recommend taking it for your divisional distribution if you are at all interested in learning about the middle east.
This was an extremely interesting class. As a math concentrator with no other Government or Middle Eastern Studies coursework at Harvard, this class has been an incredibly enriching and accessible introduction to those worlds. There is a lot of critical engagement with the two case studies and discussion of the broader Middle Eastern security architecture, so the class is in ways limited to just Iraq or Afghanistan. Prof. Sagha has been fantastic through the semester and puts a lot of effort to engage students with the material. Strongly recommend!
