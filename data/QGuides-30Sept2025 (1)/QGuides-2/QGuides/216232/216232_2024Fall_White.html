The syllabus for this course was awesome, and super thoughtful. If you want to read Graham Greene one week, James Bond another, and watch Homeland the next, this is the course for you. The discussions were really interesting and we learned to apply a similar set of skills and lenses to espionage fiction from different periods. Super engaging course, and Duncan White is a fantastic professor. The classes just got more and more exciting and engaging as we moved through the term and closer to the present day, and the essay assignments were clearly explained and provided a number of opportunities to improve over the course of the term and refine the examination and analysis skills that Duncan seeks to teach. He grades them very fairly. Because of the way the course introduces and then runs with the same genre expectations and tropes, you will learn to be a much more careful reader and consumer of espionage fiction and popular culture. I had a great time taking this course and highly recommend it!
Professor White is an excellent lecturer and facilitator of discussions, the readings and film selections are fascinating, and the papers are creative and interesting. This course is rigorous and teaches lots of new skills without being overwhelming. This is a great course.
Prof. Duncans Espionage is a one–of–a–kind class, and I wholeheartedly recommend it to anyone. This is not an easy class (be ready, at times, to read a book a week), but every moment is worthwhile.

First and foremost, Prof. Duncan is a tremendous teacher. His feedback is always on–point, and I could feel myself actively improving with each essay. Discussions in class are extraordinary, and though we cover some difficult and controversial topics, Prof. Duncan ensures his students feel comfortable sharing and contributing. Moreover, hes a great mentor, and guided me through a lot during my first semester of college.

And the class is, for lack of a better phrase, plain fun. We read and watch James Bond, Jason Bourne, John Le Carre, Sherlock Holmes, and a whole lot more. Critically, we learn how to analyze these works with a scholarly lens, and this class took my literary and historical skills to the next level.

In short, this class is not a gem, but it was worth it in every sense (even though Im not a Hist–Lit major, and dont plan to be). I grew as a student and a scholar, and I loved every step of the way. Thank you, Prof. Duncan!

And to anyone even remotely considering Espionage TAKE IT!!!
This class was incredible! We had so much fun in discussion and it completely changed the way I view spy literature and film. Duncan is a great classroom presence and really helps keep everyone involved for the entire 2 hours of the seminar. Highly recommend!
This course is really incredible. At first I wanted to drop it because there is A LOT of reading, but the course matter is so interesting and the discussions are incredible in class. Duncan is a GREAT professor who empowers his students to develop their analytical and writing skills each class.
I loved this class!!! It's super interesting and a great balance of sources that are incredibly fun interesting to watch/read but also provoke great discussions
This class is super engaging! Great way to improve writing skills while learning more about a really fun and interesting topic
TAKE THIS CLASS! This is one of the best classes I've taken at Harvard, and if you are lucky enough to get in, it is an absolute must take! Duncan is an amazing professor, and the material covered in the class is so engaging and interesting. It is certainly a class where you need to keep up with the material (you can 100% tell when kids don't do the reading / watch the film) but the work is 1000% worth it, and outside of the weekly reading / film there are only 4 shorter papers
Do you like movies? Take this class. Do you like spies? Take this class. Do you like having fun conversations with people that actually care? Take this class. I don't care what year or concentration or GPA you have. I am walking away with no regrets. I saved this for my senior year as an engineering student and it was worth the wait.
