This is a great course. It is extremely easy and at the same times it's great to have an opportunity to learn from Summers and Liebman. Make the best use of it and make sure to meet with the professors outside of the class, because they can give you a lot of valuable advice and support. Some of the stories the professors tell in class about their experience in the government are incredible, and the joint lectures are very entertaining. I would say that the material can be sometimes boring if you are not a hardcore Econ bro (turns out I am not) and the course being so easy adds to it, but it still really solidified my understanding of real–world macro policies even if it came with the realization that Econ doesn't actually excite me much. The TFs very really helpful and easy to approach. Overall, great course, and I really enjoyed taking it.
Lectures are super interesting even if attendance not required
This is a really singular class because of the instructors' depth of knowledge about economics and about the political process of policymaking. All of the war stories from serving in presidential administrations were fascinating to hear. This added texture beyond what one gets from journal articles and textbooks alone.
** This class is life–changing. One of the best classes I've taken. I truly understood how economists conduct macroeconomic policy after this. Larry and Jeff are wonderful. Get to know them and your fellow classmates.
Liebman and Summers are great professors with extensive experience in government. The videos they post on Canvas are helpful in situating students in the lectures, so watch those in advance if you can. Try not to skip sections unless you have to. Although you can get the materials and selections online afterward, you will still miss a lot if you don't do those problems yourself.
If you are going to take this class, you need to do the readings. It features lots of debates on complex economic topics, and if you don't do the readings, you will be lost. The sections feel a bit detached from the actual content, too, since they focus on how to do economic textbook practice problems but don't recap lecture effectively.
This class if a survey of the key economic topics in the U.S. The debates between Professors Liebman and Summers are fun, although know that Professor Liebman leads most classes alone. The exams are consistent with past exams (which they release) and the questions that you will work on in section. The readings are not essential. Highly recommend.
Ec 1420 is hands down one of the best courses I have taken at Harvard. It was the perfect blend of policy and economic models. Unlike in the Ec 1011 series I did not drown in tedious and uninformative algebra. I left the course with a more solid conceptual understanding of economics – and in particular, how economics is practiced in the US. The Larry–Jeff combination is fantastic – I learned a lot from both instructors, and their strengths are complementary. They have so much practical experience working in the Clinton / Obama administrations and you really get firsthand exposure to how economic decisions are made in presidential administrations. I think this is one of the rare classes at Harvard which really succeeds in dramatically changing and expanding your understanding of the world. Without a doubt, the course is worth taking.

My one caveat is that the course isn't really that challenging – you can think of it more as a sort of Ted talk–esque ambiance where you come and soak in the knowledge from world–class instructors (which is great!). But don't expect to gain much from the assignments (which are minimal), with the exception of the policy memo, or exams.
Take this course before you graduate! The professors are some of the best you will ever have in undergrad. This is a great way to apply all youve learned as an Econ major. Even if you arent an Econ major you should take this course anyways. Great information for anyone remotely interested in the economy.
This class is pretty high level and some of the topics went over my head as an undergrad, but you can prep well for the assignments to do well in the class.
Summers is really great, and I really enjoyed this class. Difficulty = moderate, probably equivalent to the summers Gened if that is a meaningful reference point. But the material is interesting, lecture is always engaging. Very little week to week work, which is great. 3 crunch points –– policy memo, midterm, final –– where you have to do a lot of work to do well, otherwise, very relaxed and I dare say enjoyable. If you need an Econ elective or AM/ec one like me, this is a no brainer in my view.
THIS was the class that I was looking for when Harvard promised me a transformative education. Probably the best class I've taken at Harvard. I honestly wish I could re–take this class. Liebman and Summers did a fantastic job of bringing the real world into the classroom, and while the lectures required some background in economics, it was so worth it. Liebman is one of the most caring and compelling lecturers, and he really cares about his students. Him and Summers brought their long, storied backgrounds in real world economic policy to the classroom. Readings were very interesting; they were mostly old WSJ articles written by them or other contemporary economists, which I thought was amazing as it really brought the real world to the classroom.
Take this course if you're interested in learning about the wide variety of challenges and methods in American economic public policy today. You will leave with a much better grasp on the nature of these issues in the public policy sphere. Lecture is not always the most laser focused, since professor Liebman will likely just monologue and go on tangents when he lectures, and Summers will just talk over Liebman when they debate. Still, these professors are at the forefront in their fields and have worked directly in major administrations, so it is very helpful to get their insight and learn about being a policymaker.

The exams are not super difficult, just do the past midterms/finals, they will be very very similar to what you see. The policy memo is not horrible either, as long as you do it in advance and follow all the instructions. And the policy simulation is actually pretty fun. Overall, I enjoyed this course.
Seems like the course will be changing its instructor lineup for upcoming years, but this is a great course to take for anyone interested in Economics. It's a weekly session for Larry Summers' and Jeff Liebman to have their sage words wash over you and for you to become enlightened. This class is generally pretty interesting and eye–opening at times, just the occasional stories that get told, and their closeness to political power makes for an interesting course. Nowhere else on Earth can you take a class with such easy access to some of the leading thinkers in Economics other than at Harvard. This is the course to take. This is what Harvard's tuition is for.
I think that this is a very good class – think about it like an extension of Ec 10B, but certainly quite a bit of work. For less stress, I would advise to take it Pass/Fail. The exams cover nothing in lecture, and the outside material can sometimes be really challenging to understand.
Great course, those with genuine curiosity will do well and enjoy it
