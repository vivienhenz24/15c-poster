OVERALL THOUGHTS

I truly loved this class. I looked forward to lectures every week. I took it first semester of my first year as someone who applied to Harvard as a psych major, so I may be biased because I was already a huge fan of psychology. But this course solidified for me that I want to concentrate in psychology. PLEASE try it out; do NOT be discouraged to take it because of the rigid grade breakdown.

ADVICE

1. Please read the textbook and take thoughtful notes.
And try your best not to fall behind on this.

I mean, you can fall behind and still do well, but itll definitely be a pain to be drowning in hundreds of pages of reading the week before an exam worth 50% of your grade. I speak from personal experience.
Also, quite a few questions on the exams (Id say about 20%) are content exclusively from the textbook. Id recommend knowing all the diagrams, lists, and vocab words from each assigned chapter. Thats really all you need memorized from the readings to do well in the class.

2. Do the Book Club – (1) its interesting/fun; (2) it cushions your grade for not that much work

3. Study as much as you can for exams, and then some.
Seriously.
If you dont do well on exam 1, its hard to bounce back. Im glad I listened to the Q and was very careful about preparation for the midterm and final and finished with an A in the class.

4. FYI, grades are curved. For the first exam, a 43/50 was the cutoff for the A range. For the second, it was a 44/50.
