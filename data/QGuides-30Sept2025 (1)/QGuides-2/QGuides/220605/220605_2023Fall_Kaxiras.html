Overall this is a solid course –– well–organized and fairly interesting. It provides a good foundation in python, and is easily understandable for those who don't have a coding background coming in. Note that while the class does teach you python, there's definitely an emphasis on math/ science applications –– it's not just a "coding" course. This class was probably more work than I expected. It's not high stress because there are no exams, but the psets are time consuming and the midterm project is long and falls at a time when you'll have a lot going on.
This is a great course if you are a physics concentrator. It has a low–time commitment but everything you do is very applicable to other courses and especially to research. I wish I had taken it earlier and I'm happy I took this instead of CS 50.
definitely teaches you computational physics and leaves you with concrete code to use. not good if you’re looking for a theory based coding course but good if you need python experience for your research or a starting point to gain some fluency in the language. go to section !!! and make good pset buddies, you’ll need them for the midterm and final.
