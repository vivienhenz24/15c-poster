This is a great course and integrates a lot of different ways of examining the period.
This is a terrific class on postwar Germany in the context of the global Cold War. It will help you understand how contemporary Germany and European politics. The reading and class discussions are fascinating. I highly recommend it!
Take this class. If you are at all interested in Germany, Professor Spreen is a font of knowledge from which you can draw forth the most esoteric and obscure of facts. The lecture component of the class can at times see disorganized but in retrospect, there is a clear method to the madness of the course. The section component is truly phenomenal as it forces you to critically engage with the course material and really think for yourself.
This is a fascinating course not only for its insight into Germany but as a lens into 20th century history (decolonization, Cold War, etc.). The professor is genuinely excited about the subject and teaches with enthusiasm. Assignments are very manageable.
I CANNOT RECOMMEND THIS COURSE ENOUGH!!!! The readings and lectures are downright fascinating (I looked forward to the lectures and enjoyed the readings), and you will cover so many facets of German history post–1945, from the post–Holocaust process of "coming to terms with the past" to role of gay spies in East Germany. I highly recommend this course even if you have never taken a history course or know much about Germany. The course is welcoming, discussion–based, and reasonable in its workload. As long as you attend class and stay familiar with the readings, you will do well on the in–class quizzes (which are meant to be low–key) and the midterm and the final. The final paper also gives you tons of leeway to choose a topic interesting to you. Professor Spreen is not only an expert on this stuff; he is also an incredibly kind and understanding professor, which hasn't always been my experience at Harvard. The same is true of Kabl––the TF––who not only brought up fascinating points beyond what we covered in lecture, but also just radiated compassion and warmth. I loved this class and wish I could take it for the first time again.
This is a great class for history and non–history concentrators alike. Professor Spreen and Kabl are a great duo who really helped to create a fun and intellectually stimulating learning environment. I particularly enjoyed the discussions and debates we were able to have in class, alongside the wide variety of historical material we consulted. If you have any interest in German or Cold War history, take this course!
