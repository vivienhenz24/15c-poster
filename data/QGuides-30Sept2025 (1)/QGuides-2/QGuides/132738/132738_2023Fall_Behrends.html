If you are interested in philosophy, take this class. I understand that this is an intro course, and a lot of people were disappointed by the use of premise conclusion form to present arguments / Prof Behrends's teaching style. I can empathize that this method is not something many people are used to, but breaking arguments down into their core parts is just an inevitable part of philosophical inquiry. I personally think that this class (and its material) is very well formulated. The content is great, and I think Prof Behrends does a great job presenting these complex ideas in approachable and engaging ways. If you are looking for a 'classical' introduction to philosophy or ethics at Harvard, this is the best class.
Though I believe this course was a good introduction to ethics, it was not exactly what I expected going into the course. I had believed there would be a larger applied component where we analyzed real life incidents from a morality perspective, but it was more theoretical than I would've hoped. Going into this course, go to office hours and engage in section because course staff actually want you to succeed. Lectures were slightly monotone, but they do make the abstract ideas in the texts much easier to digest. I would not say this course is the best introduction to philosophy, but it is a very comprehensive overview of ethics.
This is certainly not an easy A, and you will not understand why you get the grades you get.
Highly recommend if you're looking for an introduction to ethics. Behrends is a fantastic lecturer and professor. The assignments are really good–– they make you think–– and the exams are totally fair.
