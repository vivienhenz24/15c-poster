Take this class!! This is an excellent class for fulfilling the divisional distribution requirement. You do not need to take a intro sociology course to succeed in this class.

Dr. Dromi's lectures were pretty straightforward, and he would present a video(s) after reviewing the concepts to demonstrate how they apply in popular media forms. One time, we watched "Black Mirror". The readings can be somewhat time–consuming, but as long as you get the main ideas/concepts from them, you can get away pulling off a 100 on the short quizzes at the beginning of the week. They are multiple choice by the way. The midterm and final paper assignments were not bad at all either. Just make sure you schedule an appointment with a Sociology Teaching Fellow or head to the Writing Center early on so that you get a headstart on the paper as they can be itme–consuming.
This was probably my favorite course I've taken so far. The class is great, the professor and TFs are great, and you will learn so much. I went in without having ever taken a Sociology course before, but I still never really felt like I was behind anyone. The readings were long and due before the Monday of every week, but honestly I appreciated getting to absorb the week's material for myself and start on the same page with everyone else before we discussed in class; class itself was organized by heavy theoretical discussion Mondays and more pop culture/media application Wednesdays, which I liked because it made acquisition of the course material extremely intuitive and engaging; in class, it felt that everyone's verbal contributions were valued, considered, and incorporated into the lesson. Also, every week people's photo posts on the discussion board were added to the class presentation, which was super cool! I felt that I learned more about the school and world around me in just this class than I have in entire years of schooling.
