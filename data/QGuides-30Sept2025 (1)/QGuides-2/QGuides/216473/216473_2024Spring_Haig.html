Comments
This is a wonderful course to take for humanities concentrators. Dr. Haig is very patient and will explain the more biology heavy concepts while also making space for you to share your knowledge of the humanities. I never felt intimidated after the first class because Dr. Haig creates such a welcoming environment where everyone's opinion is valid.
I highly recommend this class. Professor Haig is so knowledgeable and kind and this is such a fun learning process.
Professor Haig is wonderful. He facilitates conversation well, and considers all students' point of view. Discussions are open and not at all judgemental; people approach problems from multiple angles and consider new points of view. In fact, my favourite classes were the ones in which people presented opposing viewpoints. The book is interesting – regardless of whether you are a "science" person or a "humanities" person you will leave the course having learnt not just new concepts but new ways of thinking. Professor Haig also suggests additional readings to answer questions posed in the weekly responses which are always insightful. The course is manageable in terms of work. You get out of the weekly responses what you put in – there are no other assignments, which might stress out some people who wish to take the course. Feedback on responses is comprehensive. Definitely recommend for science distribution or for upper level IB.
A very interesting class! Do not let Professor Haig scare you away! ***
Take this class! You will learn so much from Prof Haig's thoughts and comments. He provides very insightful feedback on all responses, and though discussions you'll explore everything from molecular biology to moral philosophy. A great seminar style class!
This class is unlike any course I've taken at Harvard, andI'm very glad I took it. Even though I can imagine that the style of teaching is not for everyone, I really appreciated the structure: reading a book chapter, sending questions to the professor, receiving responses, and then getting together with the class to discuss, which was the best part. The class made me a more curious person and someone who was better able to ask important and difficult questions. The material in this course technically focuses on philosophy and evolutionary biology, but you do not need any background in either of those subjects. Haig appreciates all points of view and values all comments, and he is a wonderful professor and person. He has fascinating insight and has thought very deeply about matters that I had not ever considered, and his friendliness and enthusiasm about what he studies is contagious. Overall, I'd recommend this course to anyone who has even a small interest in what the book discusses.
