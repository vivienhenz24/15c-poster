This class has very interesting material, and Hoffman is a nice professor. However, the Psets were often difficult to understand and the work is not small. That being said, the only math/ stats you really need to know is taking expectation(a little) bayes rule and LOTP.
Interesting course – if you like math and understanding human behavior, consider taking it! Only thing is that lectures were quite hard to follow and the board was tough to read so often felt quite lost. Having said that, going to OH would solve all these problems. The workload is a little sneaky – weekly PSETs and prompts and had to go to OH for most of them
Hoffman is very lenient with extensions. The final is open book and open internet.
If you are not someone who is okay with disorganization and self teaching a class this one is not for you. It nearly impossible to do well when the lecturer's handwriting is illegible, there are no solutions posted for psets/prompts, no feedback given on assignments, and on the rare occasion answer keys are posted they are incomplete with WRONG answers (studying for the final was insane). I will say however the TF was amazing and the lecturer was a smart and interesting person, but on the academic front this class is not worth it.
Interesting class, but the lectures are chaotic and the psets are boring. Game theory is fun and useful, but I think there are classes that can teach it better.
Grading for this course was Prompts, Problem Sets and Exams.

Prompts: very enjoyable, a lot of the answers were in the book hidden game, some of the prompts though I didn't understand their purpose, like the American Revolution one. Expect to skim about 4–6 papers a week, this only takes a few hours though.

Problem sets: These fluctuated a lot. Some very easy, some crazily difficult. Inconsistency was difficult. TF Ivan's office hours were great though!! But he is only one man, and the course should have had 2 Ivan's in my opinion.

Exams: super–duper chill, very fair, just covering basic knowledge. I would bet almost everyone got above a 90.

So in conclusion, this course is a fair amount of work during the semester, but chill coming up to exams!

Also, you can do all of the prompts and psets in pairs of 2 and submit the same thing which is nice! take the class with a buddy! (or meet one!)

Final comment is that lectures were sometimes disorienting, but Prof Hoffman was very happy to explain things after class.

Also class basically had unlimited extensions. This flexibility was extremely helpful. Very grateful!
I really enjoyed this class! Would definitely recommend reviewing Bayes' theorem before taking this class.
The handwriting of professors is terrible. No slides available, and no online recordings. But the pset is doable within a group.
You definitely need to be pretty comfortable with Bayes rule to do well in the class. The psets are not always easy, and the math is usually not explained in class, so you often have to figure it out in office hours. This year there was only one TA who was hosting all the office hours and grading all the assignments, so understandably it took a bit of time to get them back each time, which was not ideal.

The class is overall a little bit less organized than others in the econ department, but the topics are very interesting and a good blend of conceptual and mathematical ideas.
Ok. Heres the deal. The psets really werent that bad, and the material is interesting, but this course was just not taught well. Also, I found it very difficult to prepare for the final; the provided study materials were littered with errors, and the grading is unpredictable.
