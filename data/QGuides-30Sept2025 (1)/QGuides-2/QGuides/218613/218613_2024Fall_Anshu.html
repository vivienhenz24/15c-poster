Youre graded on a R+/R/R–/L/N system. It always seems a bit unclear on how you achieve any one of these grades. What constitutes an R/R–/L seems to be, at least from the perspective of a student, more of a vibe thing more than anything (although Im not sure if the TFs have a strict grading system to follow or not). I believe the class could be a bit more explicit in when a reduction proof needs a correction or runtime proofs alongside them. Some of the PSETs were quite tedious for no apparent reason, especially for problems where you resolve SAT clauses.

Regardless, I think this class is very nicely structured. Youll want to form a nice PSET group or attend office hours. ChatGPT can help here, but youll need to keep in mind that CS1200 has certain definitions that ChatGPT wont pick up. For example, ChatGPT may recognize that NP_{search} problems must be verifiable in polynomial time, but CS1200 needs you to recognize that the outputs of said problems must be of polynomial length. But, given enough context, you can maybe slide by with it. Good source of insight in guiding hints, though. Take this class, youll be okay, just dont forget to stay on top of things.
