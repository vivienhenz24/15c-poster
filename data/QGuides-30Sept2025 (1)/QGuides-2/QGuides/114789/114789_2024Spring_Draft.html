This is definitely one of the best intermediate neuro options!! Nice medium class size and so great to have section be a hands–on lab and still only be 75 minutes! Getting to patch clamp neurons is definitely a rare opportunity at this level so it's a worthwhile class to experience. Just make sure that you don't fall behind, because there's actually an impressive amount of content. The first lecture is basically a review of Neuro 80 but from there it goes so much further into detail than what you've probably learned about cellular neuro so far.
definitely take this course!! Dr. Quast and Dr. Draft are incredibly accessible and want you to succeed. course content was really interesting if you are into neuro and the more complicated working of neurons. psets are very manageable–– esp if you start after class and even so if you start like a day or two before. exams are very reasonable.
This class is a great way to complete the advanced neuro requirement. You learn a lot, and the labs are super fun. Assignments and exams are fair and are heavily based on lectures and psets. The teaching staff is so friendly. Ryan and Katie are so knowledgeable. Definitely go to their OHs!
Neuro115 is a great class, the lab component is even great and fun! I will say the only issue I felt I encountered was that the midterm and final exam were much more difficult than the concepts taught in PSETs, but maybe that's the whole point of them to test your understanding on a further level. Great course overall besides that!
If you liked the first half of neuro80 and are looking for a foundational neuro class I would definitely recommend neuro115! It's definitely a bit more challenging but there are a lot of opportunities to do well in the course. Katie and Ryan are also very kind and always willing to go over concepts in office hours. Exams can be a bit hard so I would recommend reviewing your notes early as things pick up pretty quick. Overall I really enjoyed this class!

Labs are two students per TF, and you get to use real electrophysiology equipment. I feel like there are a lot of translatable skills if you want to go into neuroscience research.
