Highly recommend this course!! Bruno is an awesome professor, he is so knowledgeable about this topic and so interested in discussing it. The course was great, super flexible in that we discussed things that we were interested in and guided the focus. It is everything a freshman seminar should be!
This is an awesome class. If you're looking for a fun and engaging first–year seminar with a pretty light workload, this is the one. I came into this course with no prior knowledge on urban studies, and left with a strong foundation in that field as well as a new lens with which to examine to world around me. Bruno is a fantastic professor, and truly one of the coolest people I've met, you would be so lucky to take a class with him!
If you are a student who wants to learn about the history of urban planning in a laid–back environment, this course is for you! Bruno really loves what he is teaching, and it shows through!! Keep in mind, the class focuses on looking at things from the past and how the future was predicted back then, so if you are looking for an in depth course on how the cities of today are designed, this may not be it for you. That said, it was a great addition to my year, and I quite enjoyed the discussions we had in class. It's not a ton of work either. Highly recommend!
Amazing class. Bruno is a great teacher who is incredibly enthusiastic about the course assignments. Course material covers a broad range of topics, ensuring you never get bored
This class is great if you love talking about cities and visions of the future. This class is mostly discussion with little structure. If you are really interested about the topics, it is great. If you aren't super interested in the topics, it can be a long 2 hours.
This is one of the best seminars you will take, without question. If you are interested in politics, issues of race/class/ethnicity, and the historical development of cities/nations, this class really is for you. Bruno is a great guy and an excellent instructor, and clearly loves what he is doing; he also is deeply philosophical, so if you seek a philosophical take on the history of our cities, this class will be great for you as well.

The assignments are not cumbersome at all, and as it is a seminar, this course really is what you put into it. Do try to put in a good amount of effort into this class, though, because you will get a lot out of it.
I would recommend taking this course if you are interested in Hist/Lit or Sociology, but do not go into it as an "urbanist" expecting to revel in the boons of bike lanes. You wil find that Bruno is a top tier professor and a true gem of the college, as well as a very interesting person to speak with on issues around Cambridge!
Take this seminar! Bruno is so enthusiastic about this topic and does his best to generate this level of enthusiasm among students too.
