Just get your Linear algebra together. Not computation but understand the theorem well. One good example is spectral theorem. You will never hear about this but once you start the projections onto the Hilbert spaces or function spaces using the orthogonal basis of sinusoids, it will be super helpful.
156 is amazing!!!! Flavio and Claudio were superb instructors. I know you may be nervous, but you are about to have your mind blownnnnnn by the world of Fourier. I truly fell in love with this part of EE and feel more clarity about my future. :) Start PSETs early, go to office hours, and ask questions! <3
Out of the core EE classes, ES 156 is definitely the most math–heavy and arguably the most conceptually challenging. You fly through a ton of content, but it all comes together during the course, so it wasn't as bad as it could have been.
This is an incredible course! Professor Calmon is knowledgeable and friendly, super passionate about signal processing! The problem sets are very intentional, and I felt like all the problems I did gave me much better understanding of the course material than I would have had from the lectures alone. Final is take home which made it much less pressure. This class was a fair bit of work but really one of the best courses offered in SEAS. Highly recommend as an elective for a SEAS/stem student.

If you're taking this class for an EE requirement, TAKE AM 104 FIRST! Made this class WAY easier for me than it was for everyone else, having already seen Fourier Analysis (Transforms, Series, Convolutions) and being able to use complex analysis to solve these integrals meant that I could do the problem sets on my own with little trouble.
Be ready to learn a lot on your own. Office hours are limited and they dont respond to questions on slack. Good luck
Take this course. Flavio is an incredible lecturer and generates so much enthusiasm for the course content. The overall environment is crazy supportive.

If you're an applied math concentrator looking for an elective... consider this one. It's very math–y and so, so cool.
This class is great, but you do have to put in consistent effort. Flavio is amazing and very supportive, but be sure that you go through all of the lecture notes and content to gain a conceptual understanding of the ideas. I think this is a great class and you will learn a lot! The grading scheme is also decently generous, with an optional final project that can boost your grade significantly.

In terms of some cons, the teaching staff was not as robust as it could have been. Only having 2 TFs with limited time made it difficult to gain feedback on assignments in a fast manner and get the necessary help to learn. Definitely would have learned better having more staff and office hours. It was also difficult to study for exams without solutions to the practice exams. I think having the solutions to some of these problems would have helped immensely.
This is definitely not an easy course, but a fascinating one! Prof. Flavio du Calmon is a great professor that loves engaging with students. I would highly recommend getting some PSET buddies.
This course is so interesting. Flavio and the teaching staff are so passionate and clearly extremely knowledgeable in the field. the content itself is very interesting, but it can be quite convoluted. It is very math heavy and the class is very fast paced. There are few office hours, which is difficult considering how hard and long the psets are. Just start early and READ THE TEXTBOOK or find some other alternate to support you in this class because there are many confusing topics. I highly recommend taking ES 155 first.
Content is LIFE CHANGING and fundamental. I believe it will make me RICH and SUCCESSFUL one day.
If you intent to do anything related to signal processing or Fourier transforms, this class is essential. Flavio is a great teacher, and you will gain a deep understanding about the topics. There are a lot of problem sets, but if you understand the course materials, these shouldn't be too bad.
