This is one of the best classes I have taken at Harvard, and I would highly highly recommend it. I think psych classes at this school are generally wonderful but occasionally too intuitive/I don't feel as challenged. This course challenged the way I think (while also not being difficult grade wise) and covers a wide range of topics that I had not thought deeply about. I was genuinely excited to attend lecture each week which presented incredibly interesting material and was also made engaging with games and fun experiments. I think everyone would benefit from taking this class, and as someone who struggles to make decisions, it provided me with a lot of comfort and approaches that I integrate into my daily life. My only critique is that I think section was unnecessary, but aside from that, I really have no complaints with the class.
This is the best class I took at Harvard. The professor is EXCELLENT. His lectures are engaging and inspiring. He knows a lot about the topic and is very organized, his lectures are structured and very interesting and interactive. The topics are great – it covers everything about decision–making. This class really changed my way of thinking. I would STRONGLY recommend that anyone who is interested in decision–making takes this course. Professor Ullman is the best, his class is more complete and deep than many HKS and graduate courses I took about decision–making. The course work is easy and the final project is really fun because you can choose what you want to do (movie, song, paper, etc.). This class is just one of the most inspiring I ever took and Prof. Ullman one of the best and smartest people I have met.
TAKE THIS CLASS! Professor Ullman is one of a kind. The kind of class I will remember when I think back on my time at Harvard. The concepts are so interesting but what's even better is how he approaches teaching them. A super interactive class that I honestly was excited to attend every time. An amazing course!
READ THIS BEFORE ENROLLING IN THIS COURSE. When I first came to Harvard, I would never expect that I would end up having to endure a course like this one. I started out in the course eager to learn about decision–making in a psych elective that seemed to have great reviews. After having taken it, I could not believe it is an actual course taught at Harvard given how much of an embarrassment it is. The slides and lectures are often unstructured, with the slides themselves filled with jokes from start to finish that meddle any possible material we might be able to pick up, and though Im sure he means well, the professors eclectic nature often overwhelms the class and prevents students from gaining anything actually useful.

I am disappointed that I took this as one of my 32 courses at Harvard, and it felt like a waste of tuition and time. Many of the concepts taught in class are self–explanatory and were not taught as well as I had hoped. The weekly assignments were alright, given they were discussion posts, but regarding other graded aspects of the course, we were only provided with 8 practice questions (!) for a final exam that is worth 40% (!) of your grade, with 37 questions total. Many of the questions reminded me of brain rot found online, with jokes written in some answer choices and questions themselves that distracted you from the fact that your grade in the course relied heavily on this test. At times, class gave the same effect as doomscrolling on Tik Tok through random tangents and jokes. Then, for the "final thing," what is veiled as easier given the assignment is so loose and has no rubric becomes a nightmare for that exact reason. People can do interpretive dance, theater, podcasts, whatever their heart desires, but we had no rubric to go off of for our work. The only rubric provided was one of four emojis (!) from a death skull emoji to a blushing smiley face with a couple of sentences describing each level.

If youd like to get started on bettering your decision–making journey, start by heavily reconsidering your decision to take this course. Im surprised it is even offered and seriously listed as one out of the otherwise stellar choices of electives in the psychology department. I hope they make many improvements for next year if it is offered again.
