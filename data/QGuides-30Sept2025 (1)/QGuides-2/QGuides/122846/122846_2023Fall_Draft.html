What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I learned how to problem–solve and how to run experiments more independently and efficiently. This course just teaches you how to be a scientist and you can apply these skills to a life of science or life in general. However, it should be noted that your experience is heavily dependent on your lab director/principal investigator or mentor. Try to find a mentor/PI BEFORE applying for this course so that you don't get paired with some random PI who might ruin your experience.
Budget the amount of time you spend at lab a week wisely. Don't devote all your free blocks to it, as you won't have any time to do work! Remember that you'll have to budget transportation time (especially if its at MGH or Harvard hospital). Its okay if you only do 10 hours a week (that is still alot of time!)
Make sure you have a good relationship with your P.I. and mentor. Otherwise, it's a good litmus test to see if you want to thesis or pursue research post–grad and an overall fun experience!
This is a research for credit course so it is highly dependent on what project you choose to pursue and your PI. I cannot say enough good things about my PI, Dr. Hecht. She goes above and beyond to make me feel included in the lab and ensure I feel like my workload is manageable. Be prepared to spend 15 hours a week working on your research as is required. Do not try to take this as a back burner or you will be stressed come time to write your thesis.
Please make sure that you choose an advisor who is willing to mentor you and dedicate time to helping you grow. Otherwise, do not enroll.
You should take the course, especially if you want to dedicate more time to research!
The "course" is determined by your PI/Thesis advisor, so it'll look differently for each person. Please be sure to have clear expectations about what the grading expectations are or you can get pretty screwed over/stressed out!
Start working on your thesis chapters early. If you manage to compile all of your citations for the intro before reading period, then you will be in a great spot, but if you could also have it written before reading period, then you'll be exactly where you want to be
Stay on top of everything!
