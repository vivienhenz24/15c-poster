Everyone should read Shakespeare and this class is a fantastic way to do it. I could listen to Professor Teskey talk for hours (he's super insightful about the plays and the history surrounding them), but we actually spent the bulk of the seminar acting out scenes and discussing them, which was super fun and surprisingly instructive. The workload is perfect, keeping it low–stress while giving you the chance to reflect on the plays. Definitely take it if you can get a spot!
I took this class senior spring (while writing a thesis), so I didn't mind the rather relaxed nature of the class. In a different semester, however, I would have been a bit more disappointed.
In–class discussions were underwhelming. We spent a lot more time acting out scenes rather than analyzing them. Discussions of plays often focused on literary history rather than literary analysis; the analysis we did perform was quite general (e.g., list the themes of the play). (I did enjoy learning about Shakespeare's life an times, though.)
I was also sad not to write a final paper. The final project was too easy to feel satisfied.

Professor Teskey is quite knowledgeable and impressive. I just he could have spent more time lecturing!
This will be the most interesting English class you ever take!
An excellent course. Take it! Prof. Teskey is a warm, entertaining, and intelligent teacher. It's a whirlwind course since you're devouring plays at breakneck speed, but the difficulty of the assignments are very reasonable. I looked forward to taking this class every week!
This course was a great opportunity to read Shakespeare's works. I felt like I was engaged with the material, but the discussion environment was casual and assignments/contributing/reading was never stressful. The one thing I wish I knew going in was the acting componentwe were expected to act scenes out a few times throughout the semester. We didn't have to memeorize lines or anything like that, but 50% of class time is spent acting/discussing how acting makes us view the play in a different way.
This course is a very solid way to get your pre 1700 rec out of the way *** and reading Shakespeare of course was wonderful
GORDON IS AWESOME AND THIS CLASS IS SO MUCH FUN!!!
If you love Shakespeare, take this class! Professor Gordon Teskey is a wonderful professor and does well to curate a love for Shakespeare's works. It's a great way to read any plays you haven't before and share your thoughts about Shakespeare in a low–stakes setting. It's especially fun because there are opportunities to act out scenes from the plays and the class gets really close to each other through this opportunity! I took this as an elective and I am very glad I did!
The acting was a lot of fun. I loved doing it. Note that the deadline for discussion posts is 9am on Monday. Also, this is one of those English courses with general appeal, so some of the kids in the class are not trained in English, which sometimes makes discussion in seminar a little more lackluster as an English concentrator (although I am of course happy that they can enjoy the class still!)
Genuinely enjoyed this class a lot!! It was so neat reading these plays and noticing common threads and themes which connected Shakespeare's early artistic project. Really enjoyed Prof Teskey's teaching style, and I really enjoyed being with people in the class. We mostly acted out the scenes in comparison to actually discussing the plays themselves; although I really enjoyed the former (I never expected to have this much fun with theatre performing), I wish we had more space to discuss these plays more deeply. The workload is really balanced; you read one play a week + discussion posts, and the final project was very fun and light.
This class is a gem. 12/10 would recommend. The course is so easy, you just do a weekly discussion post. The plays are great, (maybe Love's Labour's Lost is the one meh one) but it's so fun to act out scenes with no stakes. It's super engaging to watch your classmates enact the death of Richard II, or to goof around as the comedic clown characters, but all in all this is my favorite course at Harvard. Professor Teskey is a gem of a human being and not only is he super knowledgeable and insightful about Shakespeare/plays, he is super open to hearing your ideas. I'm a bio concentrator and took this course for fun and lowkey makes me want to be an English major now.
