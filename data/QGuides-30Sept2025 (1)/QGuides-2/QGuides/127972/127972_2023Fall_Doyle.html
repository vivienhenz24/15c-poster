What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
It's a really good class if you don't have a very good background in physics. If you do, then you probably will be bored.
This seminar was amazing. Course load was very easy which is good for a seminar. This allowed to explore only for the love of physics. Professor Doyle is very passionate and knowledgeable in Physics so take the most out of discussions. Final project was very enjoyable.
No matter what your background in physics is, this seminar has something to offer for you. Professor Doyle's teaching style takes a little getting used to at first, but you'll definitely come to appreciate his philosophy on education (especially when you realize how nice having no homework in this class is). The final project isn't worth stressing over, and the lab visits at the end of the semester are absolutely the coolest thing ever. Doyle knows so much about physics, and you'll definitely get a lot out of this class if you participate and ask him a lot of questions. For the amount of work this class has (or lack thereof), this class is an absolute gem for a more physics–y seminar and the breadth of topics covered are sure to give you (at the very least) a good pedagogy of what there is to learn in physics beyond mechanics and E&M.
This class is worth taking if you find the content interesting and would like a kind of crash course in a wide variety of physics––there's very little work involved, so it can be taken in combination with some much harder physics classes (or classes in other subjects) without draining much of your time. I came in perhaps expecting slightly more of a comprehensive survey of physics than I got, but Professor Doyle makes it pretty clear that the aim of the seminar is to familiarize you with some general concepts in physics so that when you actually learn them in higher classes, they're already partially embedded.
