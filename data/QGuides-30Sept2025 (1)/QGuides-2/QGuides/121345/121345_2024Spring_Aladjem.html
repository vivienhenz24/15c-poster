What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I recommend taking this junior tutorial! You will get to have a lot of smart, interesting conversations about issues that are very relevant to the real world. You are taking a social theoretical approach to the law, which is NOT the same as studying law (but you probably expected this given that this is a Social Studies tutorial). You'll read about jurisprudence and court cases, yes, but you will also discuss them in relation to broader social issues and thinkers that you've came across in 10A and 10B. The assignments (2 major papers) can be a bit taxing, but you are free to choose your own topics. I would've appreciated slightly clearer criteria about the structure and grading expectations of the papers. However, Terry does hold two workshops on discussing paper topics, and his feedback is always very detailed and smart. Overall, I think it's a very good tutorial.
This is a good course. Terry is a great facilitator. The first few theoretical weeks can be a little rough, but the second half of the course especially is great.
Terry is a wonderful person and so is his class! The course covers a great selection of readings and topics, and I felt that I got a good sense of the issues presented, both in their historical context, theoretical grounding, and contemporary applicability.
You should 100% take this class. Professor Aladjem is an amazing and accomplished professor that truly cares about your learning and growth. It will be difficult at times but the content is beyond interesting and the conversations are informative and amazing!
Terry is an amazing teacher and super super kind. He is so interested in his students and so encouraging. His feedback on essays is extremely thorough and he returns them incredibly (!!) quickly. The readings he selects are fantastic and he gives really helpful guidance on how to approach the readings. If you're interested in law, philosophy, political theory or anything remotely related to (any one of) those topics, this class would be worth taking. Strongly recommend.

Don't be dissuaded by the midterm essay + final essay. While most other tutorials only have one final essay, they usually have weekly or biweekly assignments in addition to that. It all averages out to a roughly similar workload, and I actually preferred having two opportunities to write an essay and receive feedback.
