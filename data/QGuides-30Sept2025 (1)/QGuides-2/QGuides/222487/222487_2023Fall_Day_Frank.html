Morgan is great, and this class is well–worth taking. The environment is pretty great, especially for introverts. Morgan himself is pretty introverted, so I think he does a great job making the space accessible, but sometimes it borders on low–energy. Still, the readings are great.
The professor is extremely thoughtful and accommodating. He tries extremely hard to tailor the class to the needs and interests of his students. Do the readings! They're manageable, fun (relatively speaking) and allow you to get the most out of this course. Also, stay on top of your written assignments. There are only three in the semester, but they'll take some effort if you want to do well. Professor is a fair grader â€” not lenient.
Great course! Definitely recommend if you are interested in learning more about the history of journalism in the U.S. You'll be exposed to a lot of seminal works and rich discussions in class. Morgan is a phenomenal TF who leads discussions well and is always available for extra help/support. Something to keep in mind –– the course is history–focused, so you won't spend too much time on the implications of modern journalism specifically. The course load is very reasonable and was never overwhelming.
One of my most meaningful classes at Harvard. I've had some of my most thoughtful, fun, and engaging discussions through this class. Some fun perks are that there's a trip to Houghton, some movies, and cool sources, but honestly the most memorable days of class were the more seemingly boring ones where we just sat around at the table and had a discussion for the whole time. Readings aren't difficult, there are only two essays and a final paper (and you get to pick which weeks you want to schedule those 2 papers), and meets once a week, so super easy to manage. Beyond that, it's enjoyable and Morgan makes the class very open and exciting. I highly recommend.
The class is reading–intensive, and helps you learn the importance of primary and secondary sources and how to evaluate those sources in relation to one another. Overall, the course material is engaging, as well as the class discussions, and Professor Day Frank is enthusiastic about the material, as well as eager to teach it.
