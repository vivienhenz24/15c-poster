I cannot recommend this class enough. You will grow as a writer, reader, and thinker. You will leave each class wanting to write for hours and every single conversation we have is one that alters my perspective on storytelling and narrative. Damina created a wonderful and caring class environment that valued the perspective of every single student, and I looked forward to every single class. Additionally, the readings are incredible and you will walk out of the class wanting to read more than you ever did before. Without a doubt, this is my favorite class I've taken at Harvard.
I would highly recommend this course to anyone who is interested in anthropology, especially as your first introduction into the subject. Professor Damina is one of the most caring and understanding professors I have come across. She genuinely exudes so much love for her students and the subject, which fostered a strong sense of community in the class. The readings are all interesting, though sometimes long, but you come away with knowledge of many different perspectives of stories and story–telling. The writing exercises have helped me to get back into the joy of creative writing, and it is something I will take with me even after this class is over.
