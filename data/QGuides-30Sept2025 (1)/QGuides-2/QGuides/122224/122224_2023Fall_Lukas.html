I went to every single lecture and actually paid attention most of the time because I just found myself thoroughly engaged with and entertained by each lecture, and I really felt like not a single second was wasted.
2. You will use the material you learn in this class both as soon as you learn it, and for the rest of your life. The course is essentially split into two halves; the first covers psychoactive drugs that are often used recreationally (alcohol, marihuana, caffeine, over–the–counter meds, etc.) and the second covers the pharmaceutical management of various psychopathological disorders (depression, bipolar, schizophrenia, ADHD, etc.) in both adolescents and adults. I find myself thinking about the material I learned at least like once a week, either when talking about recreational drug use with friends or when thinking about the way we manage mental illness (which definitely impacts a LOT of harvard students). This class just teaches you valuable life skills and you'll appreciate Lukas and the course all the more for it.
3. The quizzes/tests are very reasonable. This course doesn't take much of your time outside of class, and every assignment is given only because it enhances your understanding of the material.

– interesting material

Cons:
– Section is boring and not helpful

– the exams are hard! the quizzes and midterm are online, all multiple choice. the final is in person, multiple choice and short answer. there are a lot of questions about super specific things like drug names, mechanisms, etc and it's a lot to memorize. plus, i don't know how many people cheat on the online assessments but i'm hoping that doesn't screw the curve.

So it just depends what you are looking for.
