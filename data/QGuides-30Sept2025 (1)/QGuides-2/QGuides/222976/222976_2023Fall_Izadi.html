This was such a great course with an unique approach to understanding the histories of two communities! I thoroughly enjoyed learning from Mandy and with our classmates!
This is a wonderful class and would HIGHLY recommend. The course material is well presented and discussed, and Mandy creates a safe, dynamic class environment. This is the class to take if you're interested at all in the course topic and wish to learn from one of the best at Harvard
Take this class!! Professor Izadi is intelligent, attentive, and passionate about the subject. You will learn so much, whether it is about Black history, Native history, or the discipline of history more broadly. Professor Izadi also does her best to make the course engaging, from taking us on Indigenous walking tours to seeing a movie in theatres to bringing in speakers. You will enjoy the readings, discussions, and new perspectives you are exposed to. You will also grow in your skills as a writer because of Professor Izadi's EXTREMELY extensive feedback (more feedback than I have ever received in a course, which I really really appreciated). It is very obvious how much Professor Izadi cares about the subject, the course, and her students, and this care makes this course so valuable.
This course proved to be profoundly inspiring and transformative. Upon entering the class, I held the belief that my understanding of U.S. History was above average; however, this perception swiftly evolved through the impactful and substantive discussions that transpired during class sessions. Without having to complete in–depth readings, you will leave every class wondering why certain systems were established and continue to function today. Though not all inquiries may find explicit answers, the course's inherent value lies in the process of unlearning prioritized narratives. Take this course when you get the chance!
Definitely take this class. Professor Izadi is amazing and fosters such a open environment for such important dialogue.
This class beautifully wove together histories that are rarely paired, while exploring the ways they stand in tension! And Professor Izadi is incredible.
