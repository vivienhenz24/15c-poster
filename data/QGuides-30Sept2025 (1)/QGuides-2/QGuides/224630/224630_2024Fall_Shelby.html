What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
FYS 32R was by far my favorite class of my fall semester. The course draws strength from the amazing set of material that we were able to get through over the course of the semester. The demand of the weekly reading pays off during our weekly discussions, when Professor Shelby addresses key philosophical, political, and literary elements of the autobiography that shine it in a new light. During our weekly class meeting, I found a completely new understanding of key African American historical figures that has shaped how I consider political resistance and the state of racism in our current day society.

This course redefined how I think about African American political resistance and the role of literary media like the autobiography in advancing movements. If anyone is at all interested in learning more about traditions of political movements that trace back to resistance against America's most foundational system of oppression, this is the course to take.

Professor Shelby is what made this course so impactful. His knowledge of the content (as a leading scholar particularly in the work of Angela Davis, Martin Luther King Jr., and Richard Wright) is invaluable. Whether it be this class or others he teaches, I recommend everyone try to take a class of his if he is teaching.
Do not procrastinate on the readings! You will read about 200 pages a week, so give yourself ample time to read and absorb the information. Professor Shelby is great and you will take so much away from the course if you make an effort to complete the assigned readings.
I loved this class, but you need to do the readings. It can feel like a lot but I promise they are WORTH IT. I liked using physical books, Hollis, and audio–books, whatever works for you do that just please please do the readings.
This is an excellent class, and I highly recommend it. You have to be willing to read a lot, but Professor Shelby is excellent and you'll learn tons through discussions.
Great course if you enjoy reading! If you have any aversion to non–fiction autobiographical, reading, DO NOT TAKE THIS COURSE. However, if you enjoy the course theme, this will be an enjoyable class! The reading is about a book a week, with two response papers and a 8 page essay. Pretty mangeagable for a first year seminar. Professor Shelby is very understanding and dynamic!
Be prepared to read a book a week. It may not seem like a lot, but it is A LOT. Each student has to prepare a presentation on one book, and I was glad that I did mine early because the last few weeks, I was struggling to finish the books. If you have to read a lot for other classes, I would say to skip this class. Although, you do learn a lot about a variety of authors. Overall, this class was good, but it was definitely one of my hardest classes which is interesting because this class is a seminar.
