Stick with this course! Although the math may be a bit hard to begin with, the TAs are great and practice (Psets especially) make perfect. Interesting concepts like dollar dominance, China and Ukraine. Also, the policy debate at the end was more interesting than expected!
Take this course! Professor Rogoff is super nice and at the top of the field, so you're getting the best info on a really cool and important topic. The course is designed to make sure you do well, so even though the models can seem a bit difficult, take the time to actually get acquainted with them and the course will get a lot more intuitive. Easily one of the best and most interesting econ courses at Harvard
This class teaches you a lot of interesting and important concepts in economics (and their real world applications/examples). Sometimes there are some concepts that may be difficult to understand, but the TFs were super helpful in answering any questions!
Take this class!! Many students dropped the class at the start of the semester because it can appear very math–heavy at first. However, the problem sets are completion–based, and the exams are quite fair when it comes to the math. The second half of the course is definitely more geared towards policy too. I came in wanting to improve my intuition in macroeconomics and learn about macro beyond the closed–economy scenarios from my intermediate macro classes, and I definitely did that through this class. The papers were particularly interesting. It might initially feel daunting to write the group papers, but Prof. Rogoff meets with each group outside of class and provides sufficient guidance and support to write a strong paper. Prof. Rogoff also invited us to his house, which I thought was nice – he's an interesting guy! Both Jieying and Anhua (the TFs) were great too.
You should take the class if you wish to understand the current dominant themes in macroeconomics largely at a theoretical level without much mathematics. Its a good course for someone who intends to understand the global themes.
Not super challenging, so don't let the syllabus scare you. International finance sounds cool in principle, but the cool parts are a small portion of the class (e.g. speculative attacks). Much of the course is kind of dry.
