A year ago I was where you are now, looking at Spring classes and wondering if this is worth it. I saw a student say this class was a once–in–a–lifetime experience. I thought that was a bit dramatic. However, now that I have taken this course I can say it was definitely a once–in–a–lifetime experience. You don't even need to be a STEM major to do well in this class and take home something meaningful. The free trip to Panama was a no–brainer, but I also made connections with people that I never would've met otherwise. It was so cool and we're all so close now. Definitely take this class.
Very good course. Not too difficult if you keep on top of studying the new content every week, but it's easy to fall behind and cramming is basically impossible since it asks you to know a lot. There is very little required work outside of class every week, so use that time to consistently review content. Cassandra and Bronwyn are great – don't be afraid to email them your questions and especially head to their office hours before exams.

The trip was super fun –– very unique for Harvard classes. This is not your typical beach bum vacation, though –– if you don't enjoy science and labwork, you won't enjoy the trip or get nearly as much out of it as you should. The trip is to collect animals to study in lab, so it is rewarding if you're passionate about the subject matter and like applying what we learn to real specimens, but not if you're looking for a free beach vacation. That being said, we got 2 nights to explore the town/city, but otherwise you can't leave the base at night.

Overall it's a very fun and rewarding class, but they make you work for it. Don't do it if you're only loosely interested in it or because you want a free trip –– do it because you're actually interested.
TAKE THIS CLASS! This class is an amazing opportunity to learn so much about different invertebrates and of course the field trip to observe invertebrate diversity in Panama is incredible. The workload is very low for most of the semester (no psets!) but there is a lot of content –– you'll want to keep up with it because the midterm and final do require knowing a lot of detail about the different animal groups. The course was just taught by Dr. Extavour this semester, so there was a lot of focus on developmental biology, but generally it is co–taught by Dr. Extavour and Dr. Giribet, so the course may have been different this year than it is other years.
take this class!
In this course, you are expected to learn deeply about a wide variety of animal diversity, but the hands–on labs and accompanying field trip make this diversity memorable and understandable. The two exams (the mid–term and the final) are fair but robust tests of the material covered in the course.
