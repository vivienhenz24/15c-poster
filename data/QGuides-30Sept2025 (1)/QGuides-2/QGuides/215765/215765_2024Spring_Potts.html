This course really makes you think about religion and forgiveness as a societal concept. I didn't expect to think so deeply about the American punitive criminal justice system as I did. GREAT class. Take it! Take anything with Matthew Potts.
This is a pretty manageable class, but the reading can be intense. The professor and TFs are very accessible and understanding.
Try to stay on top of the readings. PREPARE for the final paper, or else it will come back to bite you, as it is currently doing to me right now.
Professor Potts is an incredible lecturer, and his great reputation meant that the class was filled with so many interesting, thoughtful students from a variety of religious traditions. The class was less pointed than I expected it to be – I found it to be more of a survey class than a class moving toward a specific thesis, although Professor Potts definitely has one.
