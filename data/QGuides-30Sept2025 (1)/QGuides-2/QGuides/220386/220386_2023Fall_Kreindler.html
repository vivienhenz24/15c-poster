It's a good ec elective, not that much work, but not mind–blowingly interesting either. I'm a senior and I took it as a theory elective, and I will say that this is one of the chiller theory electives. There are 4 psets, and they're definitely doable as long as you go to office hours / check answers with peers. It's not a super hard class, but there are times when the theoretical models don't make any sense. Karthik's sections were amazing for ironing out any tough concepts, since he was amazing at giving concise explanations. Professor Kreindler is super kind and empathetic too, so he is a great person to learn from as well. However, lectures feel like they dwell too much on the econometrics of the papers, which can feel like a drag at times. If you need a theory elective, take this one, but if you're looking to just take this for fun, this may not scratch that much of an itch for you.
