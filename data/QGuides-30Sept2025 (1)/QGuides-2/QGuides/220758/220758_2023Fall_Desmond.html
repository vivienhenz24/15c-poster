TAKE THIS COURSE!! It's a great way to fulfill the SEAS requirement, and the course content is fascinating. From the course's beginning, Dr. Desmond sought student feedback on the direction that the course and assessments should take. In addition, the range of assessment types (weekly responses on readings, mid–term essay, and final exam) allowed students with different strengths to excel. Near the end of the semester, there is an opportunity for extra credit, and you get to explore a topic of your choosing as it relates to human evolution. The class visit to the Peabody Museum was a unique opportunity to interact with the artifacts that we learned about weekly. Visits from current students, lab/field researchers, and faculty also helped contextualize the everyday workings of the field of human evolutionary biology.
