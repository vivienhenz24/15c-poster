Really great course. Great professor and great TF. Background in evolutionary biology is not necessary but could be very helpful, especially if you have prior questions coming into this class. Course material is generally great, makes you think through things, especially in relation to your final project.
Take it!!!! Jack and Katie are so passionate about the topic and sooooo helpful
Absolutely loved this class! Great class for early in your HEB career/if you arent in HEB. If you are in HEB, a lot of the content will probably be very familiar, but Dr. Desmond does a great job of bringing up new/different perspectives on these classic topics in HEB, plus referencing ongoing research, outstanding questions, and how these topics fit into wider conversations (a sociology–like perspective tends to come in). The semester–long research project is super doable and interesting, never stressed me out because of the scaffolded assignments, and they give great/specific feedback. Would recommend for a low–stress, interesting class with an open ended research project. One thing to note is there is essentially no neuro content in this class. With a general focus of cognition, you kind of go through a bunch of general topics in HEB.
*** The lectures are interesting and there's no exam so it's very stress–free learning. Your only assignment the entire semester is a 2500–word research paper you write during the semester, but there is a lot of support and feedback along the way. Really interesting topic, very light workload, and great professor. Dr. Desmond is super nice and always stays after class to talk. Take this class if you want to have a nice, light, interesting course!
Super interesting interdisciplinary class on human cognition – would highly recommend. Professor Desmond is great and the capstone paper is fun to write/not too hard overall
This class is so chill. It previously was a discussion–based class but so many students enrolled that it became a lecture based–class. The lectures are engaging, but there is no work that applies the course content, so little incentive to engage. I do wish the class was still discussion–based because it would've been more engaging. There is only one assignment throughout the course where you choose a cognitive trait of your choice and write about it. Dr. Desmond is the coolest!
This is a fantastic course with very clear and reasonable expectations. Every lecture is fascinating and interactive and the professor is a great presenter.
