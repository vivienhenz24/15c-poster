What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course has potential to be very fascinating for anyone interested in aerospace; however, it was somewhat disorganized this past semester because it was Clarke's first time teaching it, and there were only 6 students in the class. It was hard to be engaged in lecture when there were so few students, and the topics week to week often jumped around with no clear thread between them. We were given an assignment to create two lecture–style presentations throughout the semester to teach our classmates about an aerospace topic, but the guidelines for these were really unclear (basically nonexistent). If the course material was cleaned up and organized for future semesters, I think this would be a great course because Clarke has a lot of experience and clearly knows what he's talking about, but that didn't happen this semester.
David Clarke was wonderful to have in class. His knowledge in the field of materials science is unparalleled, and in such a small course it was a great opportunity to learn from the best. Take this class if you are interested in aerospace, power generation, etc. , as it shows how materials science is truly the bedrock of modern technology.
