TAKE THIS CLASS. Simon is an amazing teacher who is nice and only wants the best for his students. Lectures are engaging and assignments aren't bad at all. Weekly discussion posts and 3 unit reflections across the entire semester which wasn't bad at all, and a final at the end. If you are a hist sci concentrater – make this your course. I took three hist sci courses this semester and this clears any of them because of how good simon is and how well built the class is. I wish other hist sci classes were structured this way
Was a STEM major before this course, but after taking it I became a HistSci major. Torracinta's a great instructor! Love his presentations and engagement with the topic –– rich with content but easy to comprehend as you piece together history through the lens of the course. Readings are 50–100 pages a week and the unit reflections are the main tests of your comprehension as you move through the units. It's definitely course that you get more out of the more you put into it, and I'd recommend even if you aren't quite sold on history of science.
This was one of my favorite classes I took at Harvard. Simon does an amazing job covering so much content in such a short amount of time, and the section component was a great way to solidify the concepts we learned in class. This was a great class for senior spring, and I felt like a learned a lot about the history of scientific advancement and capitalism.
