Definitely take this class if you can. Greg is a fantastic professor, always makes class enjoyable, and leaves great feedback on papers. You also get to visit many different museums throughout the semester, which is a ton of fun.
I would highly recommend taking this class. Dr. Given is a wonderful professor, and so understanding of students and their needs. His feedback is always given warmly yet is helpful throughout the revising and editing process, The readings are quite difficult and took a lot of work, but I always felt supported through them and rewarded as long as I put in a good amount of effort. I had never had experience with art history or repatriated art, but really enjoyed the content of this class. It was also so cool to see the real–life applicability of what we learned as we visited Harvard's museums and libraries as well as the MFA. Overall a really well done Expos course and one I'd definitely recommend!
This is not the easiest Expos, but the topic is super interesting! Plus, you get to go to museums and libraries with the class and explore your own interests as well. Take it if you care about art and provenance, it is SO worth it.
If you are humanities student, this is an absolute must take course for Expos! And if you are not a humanities student, this course may change your mind.
I recommend this class for anyone interested in either museums or discussions around cultural restitution! Though there was a decent amount of assignments each week, I found that they all were both impactful and easy to understand and would often come back up later in the course for other assignments. I really enjoyed my time in this class and because we didn't have many students, I found that I ended up getting closer to my peers throughout the course of the semester.
Expos is never fun, but I think this course is as good as it gets. Greg gives awesome feedback and always makes himself available to answer questions. He is very passionate and friendly and classes are enjoyable. Also many classes take place in different museums which makes class very interesting. I would recommend this expos as content is interesting and feedback is good.
