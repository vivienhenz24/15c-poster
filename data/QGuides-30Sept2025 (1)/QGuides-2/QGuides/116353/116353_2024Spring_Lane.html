Take this class! I had a wonderful time with both Dr. Lane and the students in this class. It was greatly informative and was a wonderful deep–dive into my writing.
RANK THIS EXPOS (OR ANY OTHER ONE WITH DR LANE) FIRST!!! It was as fun as Expos can be, and Dr. Lane is a wonderful instructor. She is super friendly, makes class interesting, and is very available for advice in writing your papers. She is also a fair grader who understands the work you put into your paper. Expos really is what you put into it. If you take the time to really work on your paper over the course of a week, go to the Writing Center, check in with Dr. Lane at office hours, you will do well on your paper. Conversely, if you wait until 8 pm the night it's due to start, yeah things aren't going to go as well.

On weeks where there were no papers due, I maybe spent 2 hours maximum on Expos – Dr. Lane is aware that we're all really stressed and busy so she doesn't load us down with work outside of papers, which is nice. Also, you get to watch movies instead of do homework! Of course, when papers were due that sucked up my entire week, like at least 20 hours if not 30. Overall, I had a lot of fun in this class (which I wasn't expecting for Expos). The discussions are very casual and interesting, and most of the kids in my section (including me) are STEM kids, so the atmosphere was quite chill and friendly. I highly recommend taking any Expos with Dr. Lane!
This is a great EXPOS course, especially if you're interested in technology. Isabel (Dr. Lane) is incredibly kind and supportive, class discussions were generally interesting and fun, and the course itself has an engaging array of readings and assignments. The workload is very reasonable (and the last "essay" isn't an essay!); it's not "gemmy" in that you will have to (and should) give a good effort to produce quality work, but the standards and expectations are fair.
Take this. I expected to dread Expos class every week, but this class was quite fun; Dr. Lane is an awesome teacher and essay editor.
Dr. Lane is one of the kindest professors out there. I found our discussions and readings super interesting. I think that the grading is a little harsh, but that may be expected across the expos courses. Choose the expos that you're most interested in because you will enjoy writing those papers most!
It kind of speaks for itself but this course definitely helped me improve as a writer. All three major assignments, the analysis paper, the research paper, and the capstone project are all distinct in their structures but are connected in some way, allowing you to take skills learned from one of them and carry them onto the next.
The course is great. Something to note is that the way it is graded is a little ambiguous. She grades on a 4 point scale which is scary, but the grading is fair. The class doesn't focus on some stuff that I would have liked. For example we read Frankenstein (the coolest thing we read in the course) but we don't focus on any historical context in which it was written because the essay about it has to focus only on the text itself which was mildly disappointing. It's fun overall though and I'd recommend.
