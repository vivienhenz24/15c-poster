Should be your #1 lottery option, this is by far the most interesting course that I have taken. The readings are quite short and to the point, the discussions are always interesting, and I felt like I learned something after every class. Professor is the sweetest but also wants to help you grow as a writer and gives really good feedback on drafts before you submit the final paper.
This class is amazing! Dr. Herron is great and the course materials are very interesting!
Take this class! I have heard so many stories of bad expos classes. Dr. Herron is a fantastic instructor, and the discussions and readings are very engaging. Expos may have been my favorite class of the semester (whereas it is many people's least favorite class of college).
Dr. Herron subscribes to a very leftist interpretation of society and the economy, but he invites open debate and allows for academic freedom. This was one of the few academic spaces I had this semester where I never felt the need to self censor.
Do the readings well! A large chunk of your essays will be derived from ideas/evidence from the readings so doing them well the first time will be beneficial for you. Dr. Herron is also such a down to earth, approachable professor and will do his best to help you throughout your writing process so don't be shy in reaching out for any questions or help.
I would strongly recommend this class. I believe the curriculum is well organized and I enjoyed my topic!
I would very strongly recommend ranking this expos first. The readings are very manageable, the course material was interesting (at least to me), and Herron is great. His makes his views clear, but encourages discussion and disagreement. He gives a lot of helpful feedback at the draft stage, and I thought his grading was fair.
Rank this class 1!
The readings in this class are extremely interesting and will make you reflect deeply on social class and inequality in the US. Professor Herron is a very supportive and understanding instructor and will push you to really think through your ideas as you develop your arguments.
