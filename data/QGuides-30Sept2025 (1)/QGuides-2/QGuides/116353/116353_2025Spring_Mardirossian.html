This class is tedious at times, but ultimately really helps your writing and gives you skills that you will need and use throughout your other classes
If you think you might be interested in the topic, it's worth considering this course because Expos is a class you have to take, it's worth looking for one that you feel like you would enjoy. Taleen is an excellent preceptor, offering thorough feedback in conferences.

However, something I did not really consider when ranking my Expos choices that I think makes much more of an impact on your experience than the topic is the type of essays you will be asked to write. If you really want to write an op ed, for example, or like the idea of getting to do a research paper on a topic you chose, Tongue Tied is a great choice. However, for me personally, I wish I'd gotten the opportunity to explore different kinds of essays that I know were covered in other Expos courses. Because the course really is more about the writing than the topic, I think that focusing on what kinds of writing you will be asked to do is more important at the end of the day.
Taleen is the sweetest TF. The assignments are fair. The first one is the most tedious with a lot of readings, but when you do the research paper and oped with any topic of your choice (I chose religion) it is genuinely fun. Pick something you're passionate about! It's very open.
Take this course! Taleen is such a sweet and helpful preceptor who provides plenty of feedback on the essays. You might not receive an A in the class, but the criticism is mostly reasonable. There are weekly readings and two main essays plus a capstone project.
Taleen is an amazing person, but this class was hard for me in general. It is definitely up there in terms of expos sections.
