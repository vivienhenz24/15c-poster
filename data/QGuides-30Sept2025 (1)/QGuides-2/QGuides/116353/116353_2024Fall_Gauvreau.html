Be prepared to analyzea lot. A lot of the readings in this class can get to be very confusing and really needs time to dissect. All of the reading content is really interesting though, so take this course if you are willing to put in a lot of work and analyze some really cool forms of media. If your semester is very work–heavy and your interests lie in other fields, keep in mind that you will be writing a lot in this course, more than some other EXPOS options.
Highly recommend this Expos 20 class! Professor Gauvreau has high standards for his students, but his standards are thoroughly reasonable and makes you want to challenge yourself without it feeling overwhelming. Hes extremely chill and wants to see his students succeed and hear their thoughts, which makes the class more enjoyable. The discussions can be a bit repetitive, but if you enjoy reading and discussing books youll enjoy it. Highly recommend!
It is a great course if you are interested in the content, but it can be a bit tedious/difficult if you are not.
I suggest taking this course because the frequent in–class discussions that involved reviewing sample papers were especially useful in identifying aspects of my own writing that needed improvement. The instructor would also be more than willing to explain the errors in my submissions, using thorough questioning techniques, and I greatly appreciate the emphasis on seamlessly connecting evidence with reasoning that was evident throughout the course structure. The individual conferences with the instructor were especially beneficial to my grasp of the qualities of academic writing, and these conversations often resulted in me adjusting my thinking regarding a specific aspect or aspects of my argument.
I don't regret taking this class if you are interested in the theme of originality and inspiration. The first paper was somewhat narrow, requiring comparisons between Hamlet and specific movie adaptations, but there was more choice with the second and the third was almost completely open–ended. The timing of drafting felt kind of tight, but I think that had more to do with general Expos standards than Joseph himself. Joseph is kind, clearly interested in what he studies, and gives detailed feedback at each stage of the process to support you in your writing.
This class was one that changed my life. As a first–year, this writing course guided me on how to read between the lines, extrapolate based on evidence, and how to write an engaging paper. I would 100% recommend this course, and the course materials are not boring nor lengthy.
