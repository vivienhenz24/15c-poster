If you need to fulfill your Expos requirement, take this class to do so!! This course has taught me so much, not only in the realm of academic writing, but also knowledge of feminist theory. Readings are incredibly captivating and the fictional pieces are absolute page–turners, while the more theory–heavy papers you will read are intellectually stimulating. I love how class sessions are structured as a seminar with open and encouraged discussion, and I feel that Dr. Gold has created such a safe and welcoming space where we can be confident and vulnerable when sharing our views and experiences. Every class, I just feel compelled to contribute to the discussion and invigorated by my peers' inputs, much of which has challenged the way I consider and understand feminism. 10/10 RECOMMEND!!
This expos seemed solidly middle–of–the–pack to me. The assignments are great (the third assignment is a multimedia project instead of an essay which is way better) and it's not a big time commitment, but if you've taken a class on feminism before you might not encounter new theory/have your ideas challenged very much.
Take this class!!! Dr. Gold is an amazing professor who cares so much about her job and her students' success. She provides amazing feedback on your writing, is willing to help you brainstorm ideas if you're not quite sure where to go with your essay, and does not give as much busy work as other expos classes do. Everyone should take this class as it teaches you more about feminism and how to be a better feminist.
I really liked this class. As far as Expos goes, the workload was super reasonable and manageable (only two major papers, a shorter two paragraph assignment, and a capstone project). If you are genuinely interested in any aspect of feminism or learning more about feminism, I don't think you can go wrong by taking this class. One thing to keep in mind is that at the end of the day, it is a writing class, so while you do get to explore the content of the course themes a little bit, the depth with which you do that in class isn't great, which I'd assume is true of most Expos classes. It really is more about the technicalities of writing. That said, the Expos class you choose is still important because you have to write about the course topic! So follow your heart, but this one's not too bad, I promise ;)
Take this class even if you're a non–woman, a non–feminist, a person who knows nothing or agrees with nothing that feminism says. Take this class if you are great at writing, okay at writing, or if English isn't even your first language. Take this class if you like being heard and guided by one of the most skilled and knowledgable professors in this school. This class is perfectly crafted. All the materials/ readings provided are super targeted both in style and topics––nothing feels repetitive or redundant. Yet, you always have enough resources/instructions for anything you might do in this class. Dr. Gold spends SO MUCH TIME giving excellent, in–depth feedback. She will make entire videos, leave comments, hold draft conferences AND office hours to make sure nobody is left stranded with an assignment. That being said, her teaching skills are so admirable that she manages to provide this support without EVER giving people too much help or a lazy way out. She is able to guide and motivate without doing the work for other people. I am a better writer and a better feminist after this class.
