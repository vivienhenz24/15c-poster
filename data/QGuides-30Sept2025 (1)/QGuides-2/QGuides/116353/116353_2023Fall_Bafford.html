What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
It is a great class. It is very challenging at first if you are an international student who hasn't had so much experience with reading lengthy and difficult articles, book chapters that are specifically related to the field of study. I think people with interest in sociology, history and possibly philosophy would benefit mot from the class, the readings and the focus on writing. However, even as a more science oriented person, this class is extremely useful to grasp the influence of psychology, behavioral sciences and their influence in the questions of causality for example.
Do no take this course unless you have an interest in anthropology or African religion. I thought it was going to be about ghosts, witches, vampires etc. It sort of was, but it was actually about people who write about those things. The workload varies every week. There are constant readings, but the writing portion comes and goes in steady waves and I was usually prepared ahead of time knowing which weeks would be easy and which would be more of a struggle.
I think this was a fun course to get the expos requirement out of the way. There are definitely some interesting ideas and worldviews you get to explore and it's a good introduction to this specific field of cultural anthropology. Overall, take a course you are interested in, but this course is not that much work especially if you like the topic.
