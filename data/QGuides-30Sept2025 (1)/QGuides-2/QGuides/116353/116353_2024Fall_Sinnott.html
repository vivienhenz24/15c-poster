What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is a solid expos choice and the conversations are really interesting. The work load is manageable although the first unit was challenging to me. As someone who wasn't used to college level reading (content and amount), it was definitely a steeper learning curve. Past that though, the work is not at all overwhelming. Prof. Sinnott is also the sweetest and is always there to help when you need which is great! Take advantage of office hours, even if she doesn't have too much advice, talking things out definitely helps make you more comfortable and confident.
I believe every student should know that Professor Sinnott is a wonderful individual who has great enthusiasm for the class and shows great care for her students! I will admit, entering Expos and this course, I struggled a lot with my writing and the lengthier readings; depending on your background, this class will be a good amount of work as you dive into more philosophical territories and argumentative writing. However! I find that Professor Sinnott is not only very understanding but also highly encouraging and patient (trust me!). While the amount of material and new expectations may be a big adjustment (as Expos may be in general), I highly suggest this course for individuals who want to improve their writing while diving into the complexities of what defines free speech in both digital and real–world contexts.
Prepare for class. You shouldn't feel like you have to do an intensive read for all the texts, but your participation in the class is very useful in building the ideas you'll carry forward into your writing. I think participating in this class is very important, not just because there's a grade incentive for doing so but because it is enjoyable and it sculpts your writing.
I really recommend Dr. Sinnott's course, especially if you think law school is of interest. She is a great professor and the course material is relevant and interesting.
THIS CLASS IS AMAZING!!! I thoroughly enjoyed this course and the materials provided, although I am very interested in government/legal theory. If you have no interest in concepts of American free speech or legal debate, this class may not be for you (although there was a decent amount of STEM concentrators in the class). Dr. Sinnott is hands down one of the nicest instructors I have ever encountered, and you can tell she wants the best for her students. Her feedback is stellar for the most part; it improves as she gets to know your writing, so that is something to note for the first essay. A lot of my peers and upperclassmen have expressed this worry or hatred for expos, so I was very worried about what the grading/work would look like going in. I can say that while Dr. Sinnott is an honest grader, she is not unfair, and the work she assigned was relevant to the course without being too long or difficult. The readings in the first unit are a little dense, but she gives ample discussion time in class, where I learned a lot from my classmates' perspectives on legal doctrine. This class disproved my expectations for expos in the best way, and I am so grateful I got to take it. I could not recommend it enough.
