Very applicable knowledge for when you choose to enter the workforce. Professor Meyer will try to help, although can be hard to meet/work with at time. Very knowledgeable though about the topic, you will definitely come away with a new perspective on work environments.
I would HIGHLY recommend this course to my peers. I entered the course with no experience writing expos–style papers and no knowledge of sociology. Throughout the semester, I learned a substantial amount of the sociology of work (a topic that I knew I was vaguely interested in) and my writing significantly improved. The assignments were sparse but meaningful, the class was not difficult, and the topic was engaging and applicable to the real world. I would highly recommend this to anyone that is interested in how society functions (and why) as well as STEM concentrators that do not want to analyze literature. At the same time, taking this course if you don't care about the content is a recipe for disaster.
In the course itself, the feedback was always meaningful and relevant in a manner that significantly improved my writing. There was also always time for office hours or emailed questions. The sections would occasionally feel very long, especially towards the end of the course, however I believe that this feeling is a result of the expos model and not the course itself. The class readings were also INCREDIBLY reasonable. They are short, relevant, and assigned before any work is due. This is not true of many other expos classes. Finally, the class deadlines were clearly communicated and reasonable. There was always time to write, edit, meet with the professor, and procrastinate while still turning everything in on time.
Make the most of the zooms you get with the teacher, those are the most helpful.
Rank it at the top of your list if you can. Expository writing is mostly to help you learn the basics of college writing, but taking a course on a topic that's interesting and relevant to most students will help you in that process, and you will have learned so much more about labor and the theories behind it (including some Marx). The workload is still light enough that taking a class dealing with complicated theory won't make you fall behind with anything else you're taking. Just make sure to put in the work on pre–essay assignments, think deeply about what you want to write, and remember that writing is rewriting! You will do great in this course.
I this course is good. Dr. Meyer is great and understanding. She wants you to really understand the content and also makes the amount of reading you need to do minimal. There are only three essays which are not hard to write and the response papers are typically just finding 1–2 pieces of evidence and explaining how it relates to your topic. In comparison to other expos classes where you need to write one page response papers. Good expos class.
It is an interesting course for social studies and STEM concentrators alike. The readings are relevant to the topic work and its connections to the different aspects of human lives. They were mostly engaging and reasonable in length. Since Expos is a mandatory course, you should approach the subject with an open mind and try to get the most out of it. Writing can be challenging, and I am not particularly fond of it, yet this course has been manageable. I even enjoyed writing the research paper, the last of the three assigned essays.
Not that bad for an expos class.
I really enjoyed this expos! Workload is completely manageable and the instructor assigns everything for a really clear purpose. I would totally recommened this class to anyone that wants to talk about the aspects of the workforce. The readings were (shockingly) super interesting, and some of our class discussions were great.
