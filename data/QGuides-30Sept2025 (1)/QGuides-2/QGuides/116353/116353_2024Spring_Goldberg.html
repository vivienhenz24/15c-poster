TAKE THIS EXPOS!!! Dr. Goldberg is amazing at giving feedback on essays. The course material is fascinating, particularly analyzing very complex novels like Passing or films like Lady of a Portrait on Fire! For the last essay, you have a lot of liberty in choosing the piece of media you want to analyze as long as it is approved and it is analyzed on the lens of norm–breaking.
This class is great! It has super interesting readings and the professor is so kind and patient with the students + makes the class very fun and conversational. Overall, superb class and you learn so much in a very relaxed environment
Worth taking; please go to office hours to talk through paper
The readings were interesting and reasonable (moderate or average workload compared to other expos courses). We had a lot of freedom with the essays and did not have to adhere to a specific prompt which made it much easier and more interesting to write about complex ideas which is required in expos. I enjoyed this class which says a lot given that it's expos. This expos was good for me specifically because I prefer to write about books specifically and am not great at the creative elements or alternate final projects (podcasts) of other expos courses.

I do recommend this class if you are interested in the readings because it really matters a lot what the readings are, especially as you will be pouring hours into thinking and writing about them. The grading was not too harsh and so I think this class is accessible for a wide range of writing abilities and levels of preparation, but I also didn't ever feel that it was too easy. I felt like I learned a lot in this class.
