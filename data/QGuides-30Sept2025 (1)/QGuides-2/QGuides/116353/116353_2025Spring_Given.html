Tough class, but super important! Dont worry, youll look back and say it was a great course.
Professor Given is an amazing instructor who truly cares about helping students grow as writers. The assignments help you learn different ways of researching and writing, and I found that the readings and discussions we had in class expanded my worldview. I loved how I also got to interact more with the wider Harvard community through this course. Our trips to the Harvard Art Museum, Peabody Museum, and Houghton Library showed me a side of the University that I don't think I would have known about otherwise. Additionally, the resources I learned about for archival research through the specific assignments of this course will help me in different research projects I later take on during my time on campus.
While this is not necessarily an easy A course, it is very possible to do well if you are willing to put in the work. My biggest piece of advice is to take your time really reading Gregs feedback on drafts/assignments and to come to your one–on–ones with him prepared. These one–on–ones are super short, but I was able to clear up a lot in the 10–20 minutes allotted and always came out feeling a lot more clear about the direction of my assignments. Take his feedback into account and dont be afraid to radically change the direction of your papers between the draft and the final. Do the readings before the class! Not only will this benefit your participation grade, but it will make the class a lot more enjoyable as you will be able to engage with your peers. It will also give you a strong base to work off of when creating your paper, and you will not have to scramble when the time to write arrives.
A bit more readings than other Expos classes, but theyre really interesting. Great feedback on papers.
Please take this class! Dr. Given is quite literally the most dedicated professor I have met in my year here with how much thought and time he puts into selecting the most aweing course readings and giving feedback/scheduling draft conferences. This course is doable for anyone to any background or interest with museums or not coming from a STEM major. I really enjoyed my time going to different museums with the class and choosing my own research project for unit two. I would highly recommend doing the readings even if they are long because they will most certainly come back in your unit 1 paper and significantly reduce your work by then. Make sure to take your commonplace book seriously since it does help you reference back. The unit 2 paper and capstone were all extremely doable and fun. Ultimately, as long as you really do the readings and contribute to discussions, this class would become an amazing experience looking back at all the interesting content you've covered and the growth you'd gain in writing and articulating. Never be afraid to speak up or share half–formed thoughts because the instructor will easily rephrase your statement into something more cohesive and representative of what you're trying to convey.

Honestly couldn't have imagined a better option for an expos that is rewarding and fun! Definitely takes work, but nothing more than what any other expos would.

Suppose you're interested in how museums acquire their objects and the ethical considerations in the process. In that case, this course is exceptional at presenting a wide range of viewpoints regarding these current debates and asking you to analyze them. The class discussions and writing projects were fulfilling, albeit challenging, considering the anthropological lens you have to approach them with. I think this course wasn't as easy as I thought it was going to be going into it –– the grading on the first essay made me really nervous, so definitely be ready to work for your A. Dr. Given is a great writer and teacher, and don't be afraid to ask for clarification about his feedback since he's very willing to talk about it!
