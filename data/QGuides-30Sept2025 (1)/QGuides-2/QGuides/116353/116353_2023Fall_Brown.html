Dr. Brown is an amazing professor who is really interesting and insightful. There usually is not a lot of work during the weeks in the unit before the paper, but when the paper deadline approaches it generally increases. Overall, one of the best Expos courses, Dr. Brown is a fair grader and will never be too harsh on your work.
A SUPERB expos course. Some people take this section of expos because they hear it's super easy or whatever. But for me, the fewer page counts for this course ( 3–4 pages for the first 2, 5+ pages for the final) actually made it harder. I learned how to write concisely and with command, whereas I feel like I got away with a lot of stuff in high school. Collier really cares about his students and also gives amazing essay feedback. I really, truly appreciated this class. It's perfect for inter–disciplinary students too (humanities + sciences)!
In my opinion, this is the best Expos class there is. Collier is great, you often get out of class early, the papers are interesting and graded fairly, and creativity is emphasized. Definitely pref this class if you are interested in global political, economic, or social issues. This is one of my favorite classes and balances out the rest of my PSet–heavy schedule well. I always enjoy doing homework for this class moreso than some of my others.
Dr. Brown is very nice and understanding, he provides great clarification during one on one meetings and great feedback during those times. I would recommend scheduling office hours before you start writing so you can make sure you are writing the right topic and going in the right direction before submitting a draft you have to rewrite later. This will save time!
This Expos section is enjoyable, and low–stress. Professor Brown provides very good feedback for essays as well.
1. use the conferences for the essays wisely; take up Professor Brown's offers on revising of your thesis & topic sentences
2. Professor Brown's feedback is super tailored & will guide you towards a higher grade if you choose to implement them seriously
TAKE THIS CLASS. It's a very accessible expos with a really understanding Professor who is on your side & does not overload you with work. However, what you get out of the class is what you put into it, and be prepared to work if you want a good grade. The topic is very interesting and it makes you reconsider your perceptions of value and waste. Definitely my favourite class this semester.
This is an excellent class. Some may characterize it as an "easier" expos, but arguably it's only easier in the sense that you will spend less time doing readings in this class than in others. There is still a strong analysis and writing component that is likely as difficult as most other Expos. Collier is an amazing instructor and he's very kind and sweet. His feedback on essays is extremely helpful. Make sure you take it seriously. Your thesis is going to play a huge role in the grade you get on your essays, so make sure you put a lot of thought into crafting a strong one. Non essay weeks are relatively light (just a couple of readings), but essay weeks can get very heavy, especially if writing isn't your strong suit. I spent about 15–30 hours on each of the three essays. The topic is also pretty interesting, as you explore the concept of a "wasteland" to its fullest extent, going far beyond what you might characterize as a wasteland. Good class I highly recommend it.
