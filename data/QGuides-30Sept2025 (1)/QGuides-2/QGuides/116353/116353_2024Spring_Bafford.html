Professor Bafford is an excellent writer and is very passionate about the topics in the class. Do all of the readings and engage as much as you can, but don't expect extensive discussions about the course materials in section. You will have to do a lot of the reflecting and thinking about the course content on your own, or with your classmates outside of class. Meet with Professor Bafford one–on–one outside of class as much as you can to get good grades on your papers and listen to his feedback, which is typically excellent and will make your writing better.

Definitely one of the easier and more interesting expos. If you like learning about religion, witchcraft, and conspiracists and having philosophical discussions using anthropological evidence, this is good for you. The first two essays are on witchcraft with a heavy focus on Africa and the last essay is a topic of your choice relating to some kind of belief system. There are lots of long readings but you don't have to do any of them except for the ones that you write essays on (Horton, Tambiah, Evans–Pritchard). Sometimes you don't get to the readings at all so as long as you have a general gist of them you'll be fine. Doug is super flexible with deadlines (like TOO flexible) so it can be hard to submit things on time but for your own sake, do them on time.
This is a very good expos class.
