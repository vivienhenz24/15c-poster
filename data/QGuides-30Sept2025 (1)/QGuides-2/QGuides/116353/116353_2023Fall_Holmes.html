Great Expos, but you should only take it if you are really interested in climate/climate fiction. You will spend a lot of time with the subject so it should ideally be something you're passionate about and want to learn more about. The workload is generally pretty manageable, but your time invested in this class will shoot up when it's time to write your unit papers. Unit 1 is 4–6 pages, unit 2 is 6–8, and the final is 10–12 pages, so the difficulty will definitely ramp up over time. Eliza is an absolute sweetheart and gives really helpful feedback on your drafts and revisions, as well as during office hours.
Oh my god! I loved this course so much – it was my favourite course from fall semester because the topics were just so interesting. Professor Holmes is such a lovely and wonderful preceptor and person to work with – she makes us all feel so welcome and safe in the writing environment within the classroom. Climate fictions as a course is also so insightful with all of the variety of sources that we are able to get to look at, and the questions we ultimately get to answer regarding cli–fi as art, and the responsibilities of the artist or viewer to inspire such provoking thought, is so profound to think about. Take this course if you love getting to explore the forests of Princess Mononoke as a film representative of climate art, or getting to analyze poetry set in a post–apocalyptic future where the world is ruled by octopi! So worth it!
Please take this course! I typically despite writing essays, but Eliza and her assignments made writing not only tolerable but also enjoyable! All 3 major writing assignments offer great freedom in the texts you choose and argument you present; as a result, you feel much more engaged when writing. As for Eliza, she is the greatest strength of this course. Eliza is a passionate, understanding, and encouraging professor. She clearly loves what she does and encourages meaningful consideration and conversation of the course material. Eliza is also incredibly understanding; she knows students (especially those new to college) are only human. Finally, the feedback she gives on assignments is incredibly helpful and has helped me become a much stronger writer.
This is a great expos! There is a fair, but not unreasonable, amount of readings, and they were all very interesting and taught me a lot about climate fictions – we even watched Princess Mononoke. Eliza is an amazing preceptor – genuinely passionate about helping every student – and draft conferences and office hours were very helpful during the paper–writing process. You'll learn to read a lot of works as climate works throughout the course, and this definitely helped improve my writing ability.
This class was great!! Eliza is a wonderful teacher and gives really helpful feedback during draft conferences. She is very considerate and understands if students are shy/would rather participate in other ways (she doesn't cold call.) Time commitment was minimal except before essay drafts/final essays were due. Definitely spread out your work when working on the essays and keep in mind your thesis and essay structure are super important for your final grade.
This is a wonderful Expos class to take, especially if you are passionate about the climate crisis and want to read works that are very climate–oriented. Ms. Holmes is a kind and down–to–earth, I personally felt very supported throughout the class. I recommend signing up for office hours if you do take this class, because just talking through your ideas/concerns about writing with Ms. Holmes is very useful.

Fair warning, sometimes there is a decent amount of reading to do per class, depending on the books/art planned for a unit, but nothing excessive in my opinion. At most I think I read 60 pages of a novel or 100 pages of a graphic novel, and even then that was during the longer break between classes ( Wednesday to next Monday) I personally really enjoyed the variety of readings/art we analyzed.

In general, I think Expos is a class that you get back as much as you give to the class, so if you're really dedicated or interested in a topic, you'll grow more.
If I were to envision what I wanted my EXPOS experience to be like, it would have been something like this. Eliza is an amazing instructor that provides specific feedback; the course requires a reasonable amount of work, but it really helps you grow as a writer.
An interesting class for sure, but a thing to note is that the topic is climate fictions, not climate change directly, so the objective of the class is not about the issues with climate change. Also, make sure to give yourself enough time for the revisions as they often take longer than the initial draft. Also, Eliza is super enthusiastic and brings such a positive mood to the class. Last note: grading is fair but can seem hard at times.
