Taleen is awesome, super cool and a great person to learn from. She really cares about what she is teaching and it makes even boring readings interesting. This class definitly had a good amount of readings, some of which were super interesting and some of which were really boring and hard to read, so keep that in mind.
Tongue–Tied is a very manageable class with interesting readings. It attracts very interesting and diverse people as well which is fun. Taleen made an effort to make this class more engaging than a typical Expos 20 class might be (writing an op–ed for our final, going to the Learning Lab at the Bok Center for class, etc).
Take this expos course! It is by far the best expos course out there, trust. I heard lots of other freshmen who hated their expos. For me, it was one of the most fun courses of the semester (and I am not a huge fan of writing/reading). The topic is very interesting and you will find the readings engaging and sometimes even entertaining. Assignments are not too long: there is a short writing assignments for every sections (really no big deal), in addition to the three main unit assignments. The first two unit assignments are the comparative essay and the research essay, like most expos courses, but the third one consists in turning your research essay into an op–ed of 750 words! But the main reason why you should take this course is Taleen, the instructor. She is just an amazing teacher, one of the best I have ever had. Her feedback is so valuable, and she is a pretty fair grader (she will acknowledge the effort you put in your essay in the final grade). Her classes are so interesting and she is so good at making everyone feel welcomed and respected. It is no surprise that her sections soon became one of my (and lots of my classmates') favorite moments of the week. Also, she will bring you to several different visits, for example one was at the Arts Museum. Another one was at the Bok Center, where each of us filmed the trailer (yes, the trailer!) for our research essay. It was a conciseness exercise in the view of our final project (op–ed). It was one of the most amazing experiences since I have been here. Please make yourself a favor and take this course!!!
Expos 20 can be challenging, but choosing a class with a topic you genuinely enjoy makes all the difference. Since Expos involves a lot of reading and writing, ranking classes with topics that interest you is essential. For me, I believe feeling personally connected to my class's topic was a major reason why this course became one of the highlights of my semester.
Take advantage of the writing resources and learn how you teacher teaches writing to apply them into your papers. Go to writing center and office hours when you need more essay feedback.
HIGHLY RECOMMEND THIS CLASS!! Taleen is the nicest person in the world and a phenomenal teacher too. The class itself allows you to write about a wide range of topics related to language, so if you are interested at all by language, identity, sociology, politics, etc., this class is definitely for you. The workload is not too bad at all, especially for an expos. Taleen truly makes the class incredibly engaging and informative!!
I LOVE THIS COURSE SO MUCH. Taleen is the absolute best and makes writing so approachable and understandable. She is super friendly and creates a chill, open environment where everyone is comfortable sharing their opinions and thoughts. Since she used to teach Expos 10, she knows that students struggle with writing so she provides very detailed feedback and even gives you templates and worksheets for brainstorming. She offers office hours by request, and extra office hours before large essays are due. She also conferences with each student after you turn in a draft. This expos specifically has 2 large essays and an op–ed based on the 2nd essay (research paper). Pretty much only the large essays are graded but there are usually reading notes or some small assignments due each class. The readings were super interesting, especially if you come from diverse cultural and language backgrounds. I loved getting to know everyone in the class and even becoming friends with them, which I think is extremely rare for an Expos class. She allows technology and doesn't make you handwrite everything which is a blessing. Taleen is an extremely fair grader and is relatively chill about it. She doesn't accept late work, but she will still provide extensions if you are sick. Honestly, just show up excited for class and open to discussing the subject matter of the day and you will have a great time. I truly don't have enough words to express how much I love this class and the professor. YOU DEFINITELY NEED TO TAKE IT!!!
If you're interested in language and are curious about the way we think about it, this course is absolutely for you. Taking it alongside LING 101 or another linguistics class makes it even better, as the blend of the scientific and semantic approaches can get you thinking about language in a whole new way. Don't procrastinate assignments or treat first drafts lightly––without adequate time, this course will catch up with you. Taleen is a phenomenal professor and clearly cares deeply about what she teaches, which makes the class highly engaging and fun.
