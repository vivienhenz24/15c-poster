What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Well–paced class with pretty interesting material and relatively light workload. If you enjoy doing close–textual analysis of literature, perhaps this isn't the class for you (with the exception of the first paper, which is an analysis of Frankenstein, the majority of classroom discussions revolve more around discussing AI, reflecting on technology, etc.).

Upsides: 2 papers and a final capstone podcast, Prof Lane is lovely and a fair grader who gives extensive feedback on your drafts, lots of optionality with picking what you want to write about for your second paper (research paper that only broadly needs to be about technology). Homework assignments are pretty few in number and easy to get done.
Downsides: Like with all Expos courses, the discussion periods — especially when they're centred around "writing" — can really drag on. You don't get so much time to write your drafts/put together your revisions (perhaps 1.5 weeks for each), so be prepared to put in the hours during those weeks.
Great course, awesome instructor. Lots of freedom to do what you want for assignments. More difficult than I expected
Take this class if you are interested in technology, luddism, and reading frankenstein. It is a niche topic, but provides a lot of freedom in assignments to do your own thing. Seek feedback and be proactive about completely assignments. Put in a lot of time and effort to papers in order to get the results you want.
I think you should take this course. Dr. Lane is very friendly, and all essays and final podcast were relatively open–ended so you could discuss anything you want.
For any STEM kid this is probably the best EXPOS 20 topic to choose from. The class was enjoyable mainly due to how enthusiastic Dr. Lane was about the topic and the work we turn in. You will probably have a harder second to last month than your peers, but the final capstone will give you more head room to work on other finals. So overall, if you think you'd like an EXPOS 20 class where all you do is talk about any kind of technology than this is an amazing course, if you hate talking about technology don't take this topic.
Even if the course material isn't 100% your favorite interest (it wasn't for me, at least), I'd still recommend this class. Isabel is a great teacher who's incredibly accommodating and accessible, which makes the course pretty enjoyable and very far from the time–suck that other Expos courses can be. You definitely get out what you put into the course, but honestly it's a pretty nice gem of an Expos course. Pretty easy readings, straightforward essays, a really nice capstone project (I took this course first semester of freshman year, and when you have a ton of finals you're stressed about, having a podcast which takes like 2–3 hours max to do is a nice break from studying), and overall a good environment. Overall a solid pick.
Talk to Dr. Lane more. She is very knowledgable and can give good advise on your essay. Just that you need to be proactive. Peer review (not the in class one) is also very important. Your peers who work on the same assignment can oftentimes give feedback that you might not have thought of.
