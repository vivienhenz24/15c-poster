Take it!!! If you have even a little interest in romantic comedy, or are just a plain old sentimentalist – take it. You will not regret this class, Dr. Napier is a wonderful wonderful preceptor.
Dr. Napier is a great professor! He gives a lot of really helpful feedback, and is always open to discussing ideas with students in office hours. What you put into the course is what you will get out of it, so one of the best things that you can do is meaningfully engage with the material (do the readings, watch the films, read the sample essays, etc) and make an effort to go to office hours at least twice before your final revision is due. I genuinely enjoyed this Expos topic, and would highly recommend rating it as one of your top choices!
This course was definitely on the more demanding side. Dr. Napier is very specific in his requirements but a fair marker and was very supportive throughout. Although the course could be extremely stressful sometimes, it also helps you significantly improve your writing skills. It is certainly a challenge, but a very worthwhile one as Dr. Napier really makes sure that you are prepared for college–level writing by the end.
Expos is difficult but try to focus more on improving your writing and the rest should follow relatively smoothly
Good expos class to take. At first I wasnt really vibing with it because of the book we first read – Twelfth Night. Felt like a waste to take a romantic comedy class and just read another Shakespeare book. However, as the semester went on I enjoyed the class a bit more since we got to watch films. Though, I do wish the films we watched were a bit more diverse. They were all old black and white Hollywood films. A lot of assignments did feel like busy work but its understandable since its Expos – its not like theres much to do outside of deadlines. Napier was always available for office hours and always gave productive feedback. I wasnt really vibing with him at first but hes honestly just a nice guy. Recommend the class for sure, I think its a bit more interesting than some of the other Expos classes.
This is a better–than–average expos with less work than most others. You will have a fair amount of autonomy in what you write about and how to write it, not much work, and nice grading on top of that. It's not amazing, and it's expos, but it probably won't be your worst course of the semester – which is very good for an expos.
This was one of the expos classes I feel was the least stressful. The topics were very interesting and he gives you as many extensions as you need (a big bonus!!!). Grading wise I never really understood how I got the grades I did because the comments didn't really align with the grade but he really aids in helping you improve.
I would highly recommend this course! Dr. Napier is an amazing teacher who truly cares about his students. The texts in this class are super interesting, and I learned so many deep and interesting things about romantic comedy through this course. I also gained a really strong understanding of the fundamentals of college writing and a good process that I plan to utilize in future courses.
The professor is great, the films/books are good, one of the better expos and probably right in the middle for workload.
