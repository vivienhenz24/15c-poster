GEM of an Expos. Keating is such an awesome and caring professor, the work really is not too bad, and compared with other Expos classes I've heard about, this has been among the best ones. I'm not even really a sports fan, but it was interesting and my class had a nice sense of community.
This class was just a bunch of bullshitting. I wish I picked a more intellectually interesting Expos. The issue isn’t that the topic of sports and politics wasn’t interesting. It is just that Keating is very hand–wavy and speaks in very fluffy terms that don’t mean much. I don’t think I learned anything that made me a much better writer. A lot of the assignments also felt like busy work.

I took this class thinking that it would be a very easy A without much work. I’m not sure what my grade is, but I think I probably ended with an A or A–, but the work involved was a little more than I expected. I do think that the time I spent in the class was a waste though, and wished I took a more intellectually stimulating class, or at least a class whose preceptor was more thought–provoking. That way the time I spent on the class wouldn’t feel as wasted.

In general, Keating’s feedback is also sometimes pretty vague. For example, he would say “you can try this” or “you can incorporate this,” but wouldn’t necessarily make clear what the problem with the current essay is. However, Keating is a very good person and cares a lot about you as a student. Whenever I had any questions about his feedback I would email him and he would respond with very thorough responses.
Keating is an incredible professor. He's enthusiastic about the (very interesting) course content and gives excellent feedback for future essays. He is a fair grader and he genuinely cares about each student's voice. Many people despise Expos and go into it with a negative mindset, but if you're lucky enough to take More Than a Game, you'll likely love the class.
TAKE THIS CLASS! More Than a Game was my favorite class I've taken at Harvard. Keating is the best professor: he's willing to take time out of his day to help provide feedback / improve your writing, he's funny, and breaks down each essay before we start it so it's less daunting to write 8–10 pages of content. Even if you're not super interested in sports, this class delves into the racial, gender, and societal parts of the athletic world. It's truly eye–opening and your peers are going to be amazing.
This class is great. If you are someone shy who does not usually engage, then this course will most definitely help. It sometimes feels very difficult to give your opinion due to fear of being wrong, especially at a school like this where you feel like everyone knows better. However, in this course everyone is engaged and close enough to make you feel comfortable sharing your thoughts with the rest of the class and asking questions when needed.
Its a fun class if you make it fun. The more work you put into the none graded assignments the easier the essays will end up being.
Keating is a super kind teacher, and he makes sure to provide fairly relevant and interesting sources in the sports/politics sphere.
This class was amazing and More Than A Game has the best teacher
