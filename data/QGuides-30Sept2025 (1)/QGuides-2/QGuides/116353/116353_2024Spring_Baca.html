HIGHLY RECOMMEND THIS EXPOS CLASS! Dr. B is seriously one of the best teachers I've ever had and honestly even had me excited about waking up for 9am Expos. She really does care about every student and facilitated a really nice + supportive class environment. I felt connected to my whole class by the end of the semester and honestly miss itwe definitely had inside jokes, but also really good conversations. I think that Dr. B works to make sure everyone feels heard in the class, which can lead to some precocious people dominating the conversation unfortunately, but especially if you are on the quieter side, I encourage you to speak up as much as you can. I definitely don't think that this is an easy A ExposDr. B is very knowledgable, in a way that definitely makes her picky about argumentation and structure. Still, I really think I learned a lot from this class. The material is really interesting and our readings covered a range of fields (social science, medicine, psychology, etc) which was cool. I think students who were already good at writing definitely thrived in this class, while as someone with a relatively untraditional background, I struggled a bit, so I cannot recommend starting EARLY and going to Dr. B with questions enough. She is very supportive and helpful, but deadlines creep up fast. Still, assignment prompts are pretty flexible and I really felt like Dr. B cared about each of us developing as a writer. Of all my friends, I think I had one of the best experiences in Expos. I will say that the Expos writing style (super short paragraphs), which is taught across the different courses, is a bit peculiar, so watch out for that. But TLDR Highly recommend!!!!!
I highly recommend this class to fill the Expos 20 requirement. There is a lot of structure and direction, and all of the material is very engaging. The lessons all build towards the final essaysthere doesn't seem to be any busywork. I would recommend taking draft conferences very seriously, as this is the best avenue to get feedback on your writing and improve your assignments before they get graded.
This course shares the same harsh grading as a lot of other Expos classes. It gets better throughout the semester but it still isn't a class where you would get an A if you're the average writer. Also, the Toys topic is going to feel repetitive at times where even though you have freedom in how to approach the source, a lot of people will stick to the stereotypical topics aka racism, feminism, inclusivity, etc ...
I would tell future students to be open to asking questions about their essays and to start their essays early. Being open to your preceptor's and peers' suggestions is important for learning how to be a better writer and their suggestions can only fully sink in if you give yourself plenty of time from first draft to final draft to reflect on what they've said.
Super interesting content and lets you relive your childhood! Doesn't involve much dense reading like other Expos classes.
This is a great expos! Dr. B is extremely fun to talk to and engages the class effectively. She also provides constructive feedback on essays consistently, and the format through which she provides feedback is useful.
Dont be afraid to ask your instructor questions about your writing.
Toys' stories with Professor Baca is the best Expos you can take. Dr. Baca makes the course material super engaging through the discussions we have and her feedback on essays is very reasonable. The first two essays are written on the work of famous American psychologists and their opinion's on play. The second essay allows you to compare the physcologists ideas to the TV Show Sesame Street. The final essay is the most rigorous but engagning because you get the opportunity to write on any object of play of your choice and its broader social implications. In taking any Expos, the most important skill is to start writing drafts as soon as possible. This allows you to have the time to acess your professor, the writing center, and develop your essays to become the best products possible. Expos may be a grind but the course is kind of rewarding in the end. Its easy to make a lot of good friends in section, especially with Dr. Baca leading it.
Expos 20: Toys' Stories was one of the highlights of my freshman year. The skills you develop in Expos apply to all disciplines, and Dr. B. is a really great instructor. I strongly recommend this course if you are at all interested in the source material.
