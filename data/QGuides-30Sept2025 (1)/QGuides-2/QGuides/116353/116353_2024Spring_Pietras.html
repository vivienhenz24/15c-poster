This class really helped develop my ability to write longer, more cohesive essays that went beyond what was expected of me in high school. I did not go to a very good high school, but this class was manageable and really helped me develop my writing skills.
really good course!!! i think this is one of the more interesting expos topics (queer coming of age stories) and i genuinely looked forward to class (most of the time...) course material was very engaging and i loved how class structure depended on partner/group discussion. the small class size made it so that it was easy to have productive discussions as a group where everyone said their piece and contributed. the participation part i guess can be daunting but everyone in section was invested in the topic so it made for a good community/environment during discussions. now for cons! i did feel for a lot of the papers that i couldn't argue what i really wanted to because the draft conferences provided no clear feedback on how to revise. a lot of the time i came away from them switching my argument entirely to fit what the preceptor wanted me to argue but i guess that's expos! overall, this is a good expos topic (even with the vague feedback on papers). and the class did help me improve my writing skills and understand the expectations of writing at harvard.
The class is difficult and asks a lot out of you, but is very ruffling when you can express your argument in a way that flows and grows in complexity. Choose a topic that you will be interested and engaged with because you will spend a lot of time on the material. Dr. Pietras Queer Coming of Age Stories was great!
As far as expos classes go, this one was pretty good. I didn't love everything about this class, but most of the things that I didn't like would be true of any expos class. The readings and movies that we watched were all really interesting and relevant to the subject. I thought we had some really engaging and nuanced conversations about queerness, which was great! I also got really lucky in the sense that all of the other students in my class were wonderful and we all bonded really well, which helped the class be much more enjoyable, as well. The feedback that Dr. Pietras gave was really helpful and in–depth and the essays that are assigned are not too bad (especially given what I've heard about some other expos classes)
The content is interesting, but be mindful that the feedback you receive isnt always helpful and essays may be graded more harshly than you expect.
