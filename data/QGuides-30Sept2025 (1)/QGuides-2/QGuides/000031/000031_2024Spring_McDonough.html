PHIL 31 is an amazing introductory to philosophy course. Do not be intimidated by the assignments if you have not taken a philosophy course before. Lecture is extremely helpful in understanding complicated readings.
This class has a lot of information packed into each class each week. It moves quickly but the readings are especially interesting. Professors McDonough is a terrific lecturer and conveys a sincere interest in the students.
Take this class!! Professor McDonough and Finlay were both fantastic and enthusiastic about the subject matter. Lectures are really well structured. Each lecture revolves around a big question (e.g. "Good Without God?" and "May We Believe in Miracles?") and is informed by both historical and contemporary perspectives. It is a class where you get out what you put in–––I would recommend doing all of the readings, attending OHs, and engaging during lecture. If you do this, you'll walk away feeling really fulfilled. It was my first philosophy class and I really couldn't recommend it more.

As for assignments/exams, you have to write four short essays (you have ten opportunities). They are not that intense and it provides you with the opportunity to engage with the supplementary readings. There is also a midterm and a final exam–––both not that bad either, though their structure is a little weird (MCQs are 60% of both exams).

You'll leave with more questions than you came in with and no "right" answers, but that's philosophy (and life)...

Take it if you are at all interested in religious thought. I thoroughly enjoyed the course and feel like it educated me, truly educated me, on a variety of religious thinkers in a very low–stakes environment.

This of course extends to the world after Harvard as well.

You don't need to be religious to be fascinated by everything presented in this course. TAKE THIS COURSE. Jeffrey is so engaging and so helpful and kind. Office hours are so interesting and although lecture is not necessary, it is so fun to attend!

9/10 for me, 6/10 for any random student

This is a great introduction to philosophy of religion. Since it is chronological, you get to see how things progress over time, which adds to the experience. Professor McDonough is a delightful lecturer who made this class a highlight of my week. The lecture is so productive because there are ample opportunities to ask questions and learn from each other. The assignments are fun and respectful of your time.

The only reason I would not recommend this to everyone is that the subject could be boring if you don't like philosophy or religion. The readings are a bit long, so if you found them uninteresting, I can imagine it being a slog. They are all similar in genre but with widely differing perspectives and writing styles.

TLDR: Open a random reading in the course. If you enjoy it, you should take the class!
