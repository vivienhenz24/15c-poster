This class is interesting but it's much more conceptual than I expected. I am more of a fan of learning nitty gritty details and this class targets larger concepts more. It also has much more math and physics aspects than I was expecting so be prepared for that. Over all a good higher–level bio class especially if you're into ecology or environmental science.
Great class, not too difficult. Prepare well for the final.
This is a plant physiology class. Highly recommend to take – workload is manageable, psets are difficult but there are loads of office hours. In class activities are loads of fun and engaging.
This class is fairly unstructured and focuses way more on how plants function than the expected "how plants function with the environment." The class is long with a 1.5 hour lecture that goes incredibly fast with little ability to keep up with plus an hour of an activity. If you don't have a solid background in chemistry, physics, or plant biology, I'd be wary walking into the class. The PSETs were long and difficult, only getting easier by the end. The discussion posts weren't make clearly, the readings were hard to find, and the topics were very random. I just didn't take much away from it and I wouldn't recommend it.
I have never taken a more disorganized class at Harvard. The professor prepares inadequate slides, homework grading seems to be completely random, and TFs don't know what's happening overall. 2.45 hour lecture is divided in half, and the second half is useless, boring in class activities. The only good part is that Missy is teaching it and you get to meet her. Otherwise an awful, scary experience in terms of your grades.
One thing to note is that this course is 100% a plant physiology course. It does an excellent job of exposing students to plant processes and how environmental factors dictate features of plant physiology in a very theoretical way. However, if students go into this course expecting a more "these are some ecosystems and these are the plants you find there" kind of class, or a climate change–focused class (e.g. if you are an ESPP concentrator) this course may not be exactly what you are looking for.
I am a humanities major and took this class and had a great time (although idk if Im expecting an A lol but in my opinion thats fine). If I could go back in time Id still take it even though it was difficult at times to understand the math – I left with a lot of cool knowledge and also some sweet friends!
If you are interested in understanding how plants work and why it matters in the face of global climate change, this is an amazing class to take!
This is a great class to truly learn about how plants respond to climate change and the relationship between plants, light, water, and temperature. Although some of the material is challenging, it is very worth it to understand how climate change will impact plants in the future. Missy is a great instructor, and if you ask for help she will always respond and explain in an engaging way.
TFs are amazing!! Sophie, Lucy, and Zhe are all so ready to help students and communicate concepts so clearly. I would say some LS background would definitely be helpful; I didn't have any coming in and struggled a little with some of the concepts. Psets are challenging but doable if you give yourself a few days and go to office hours. Course title/description is a bit misleading as it is definitely more focused on plant physiology than plants' relationship to climate, and lecture material can be a little dry at times.
