This class is really well taught, and both professors are very kind and knowledgeable in the field. The discussions in the class were also really good. This was the class that made me reflect on that and realize that this is the first class I have taken at Harvard that made it feel like a HARVARD class, where the professors are both soo knowledgeable, the readings are so insightful, your classmates are very good at articulating their words. I learned so much in this class and the feedbacks on papers, especially the second paper. The professors are both the kindest people you will meet and always open to meeting in office hours. They know how to raise discussion questions, how to direct you toward innovation, and how to guide you. They are both amazing. The papers were fun to write. You focus on a topic you are passionate abouton an intervention you are passionate about. I WOULD HIGHLY RECOMMEND.
I deeply encourage students from all backgrounds and concentrations to enroll in Stigma! I valued my experience interrogating disease through the lens of how stigma and culture impact patient lives. I specifically learned multiple frameworks by which stigmas on various diseases operate and exacerbate patient illness experiences, realities, and adversities. I am especially grateful for the intervention–based focus of the course: we not only learned how disease stigmas historically influence the social world, but what we can productively do about these stigmas in the contemporary. This is an intellectually and civically driven skill necessary for students from all Departments, concentrations, and pre–professional backgrounds.
If you want/ need to take a social science and have an interest in health...TAKE THIS CLASS! It is so interesting and brings in so many perspectives about topics that are embedded into society (sexuality, weight, illness, etc.). The readings and discussions are all incredibly engaging, and the professors are also super nice and supportive. The assignments build up on each other in a way that leaves finals period/ reading period not too stressful for the class, but they will also ensure that you have a final assignment to be proud of. Additionally, the main focus of the class is to choose a stigmatized condition and develop an intervention for. This gives you so much freedom to explore any conditon you would like, and the process of desigining and presenting the intervention is incredibly thought–provoking. There is almost nothing bad I could say about this course and it may be my favorite course I have taken at Harvard so far.
Be prepared for good conversations!
