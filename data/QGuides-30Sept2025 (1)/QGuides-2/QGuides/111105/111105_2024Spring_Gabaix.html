this class can feel pretty unstructured because there isn't much of a textbook component, so i was basically learning things from the slides. it's really helpful to have stat110 knowledge, and also to have strong economic intuition. i didn't have some of the prereqs so maybe struggled a bit more than some of the upperclassmen students here. it's somewhat a useful class if you're into finance/hedge funds/quant, but more theoretical than applicable. teaching staff could be more thorough/accessible imo
Take it!! A great class for anyone interesting in trading. You go over how to identify mispricings for different stocks, bonds, derivatives. I feel much more prepared for in internship in trading after taking this class. Also, I was worried about the workload based on previous Q–reports but this class is NOT hard. 9 psets total and you get 3 drops. Psets can be done in a day. Tests are pretty straightforward as long as you study. All around good class!
It's a good course for people going to S&T or banking. Be prepared to get into some models, the math isn't too bad, nothing really beyond a FOC or partial derivative, but lots of understanding how models like CAPM work that might not be super intuitive right off the bat. Psets are graded on a check basis and you only need to do 6 of the 9, and they do get easier as the class moves on so I'd recommend doing the latter ones. The exams can be tricky with true and false style questions a la 21B that tend to be trick questions sometimes, but the rest of the exam is normally similar to the practice exams. Sections are offered, though I never went and the attendance wasn't graded. We did learning catalytics in the course, through I'm still not sure why because we weren't graded on attendance, but were told to not "look too hard into why." If you plan on working in finance, take the course, it won't be the most interesting course you take at Harvard but it's good to know and a good refresher for taking things like the SIE exam in the future.
Great class to take if you are interested in a quantitative dive into the economy and assets.
The exams are super important since they constitute a large amount of your grade. Expect the averages to be decently high as well.
This course is a good intro to finance for someone without prior experience. Just know that it's pretty math heavy, and it doesn't get into the institutional details of finance.
this is a really well structured and taught class! a good combination of models/math and theory/intuition. the concepts are very relevant for quant finance roles, not so much for traditional pure finance
Solid course, be ready for a more mathy econ elective rather than qualitative facts about capital markets. If you are a mathy person, there is a lot of handwaving and details that are swept under the rug (may or may not be for you). Exams are fair, not too difficult and similar to what you would expect of a pset.
This wasn't a bad course. It was never stressful, annoying, or time–consuming. And Professor Gabaix was great and did his best to make the class interesting. That said, unlike math, physics, and other economics courses I have taken at Harvard, this class is not interesting in its own right. If you need to have a basic understanding of finance and don't have the motivation to self–study, this is a really fun, light, engaging way to learn.

I also regret not taking the graduate version instead. While I learned things, I don't feel like I learned anything I couldn't have learned in a few hours of Internet research. The lack of mathematical rigor was disappointing. Maybe this is just me, but I expect Harvard to teach me things in a way that isn't just providing a foundation for investment banking jobs I want something that has at least a bit of theory, not just a class where every problem set has made–up numbers and repetitive calculations. If you want a job in finance (and not like an interesting one like one where you get paid too much to spend 100 hours a week working in Excel and PowerPoint), take this class. If not, learn this content elsewhere and take interesting classes that make you actually think.
An amazing class! Xavier is a great professor great lecturer, class content (slides) are great. Very good broad overview of capital markets. Definitely attend lecture since Xavier provides further insight beyond what is written on the slides, though the core material is definitely learnable from just the slides (and the internet). I believe having some strong econ background (1010,1011) is very helpful since you'll be able to understand and appreciate the nuances of the topics and lectures better. Class itself is not difficult.
Great class. Should 100% take if interested in finance post grad
I enjoyed this class. The content was pretty straight forward, the psets and exams weren't too challenging, and learning catalytics exercises in class were good for reinforcing knowledge.

The class is great and the material is awesome in terms od preparation for future financial jobs. There is also a lot of discussion about the intuition behind all the formal concepts discussed in lecture and how it played out in past financial events. The book is also very well–written and the lectures slides are concise and easy to study from.
My only concern was that Gabaixs lectures failed to tell the story of the topic at handhe was very good at explaining a single slide (or even a section of the presentation) but did not manage to communicate the big ahas from every lecture effectively.
