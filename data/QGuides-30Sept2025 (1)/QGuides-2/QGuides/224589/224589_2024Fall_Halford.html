Sarah is an incredibly clear instructor who is invested in her students' success. She facilitates a class environment where students feel comfortable taking risks and asking questions. She puts a lot of thought into readings, so they are actually applicable to projects. She is one of the best professors I've had at Harvard. Take her class!
If you are reading this, you are taking a junior tutorial because you have to, but if you can rank them, I would definitely put Professor Halford high on your ranking. She is such an intelligent woman who showed genuine interest in every single persons project and interests. She was great at keeping discussions going and challenging what you had said without making it sound condescending or like she was doubting your intelligence. Her feedback on assignments was so well done and quickly returned, and I feel like I learned so much in this course through my research.
take this course!!!! this has to be one of the best junior tutorial. Professor Halford is incredible and takes you through every step of the journey.
This class confirmed to me that I really enjoy qualitative research, especially digitally! It provided a space for me to confront the many, many challenges associated with the qualitative process (esp. online), most of which I honestly hadn't anticipated. Dr Halford is very intentional, thoughtful, and thorough about the feedback she provides, and is a great source of wisdom for the qualitative research process. She is really good about encouraging an open environment of mutual respect in class. She capitalized on our small class size by checking in on each one of us every week, which made me feel like I was being held accountable for my project progress, but in a measured and low–pressure way. Overall, I'm happy I took this class: Dr Halford is a great teacher, and this semester brought a lot of nuance to my decision–making process re: my thesis methods this coming year.
