This class is great!! Very thought provoking and stretches your thinking about art history into the realm of witchcraft. Will make you draw connections you haven't before and learn more about witches through the lens of art history. Would definitely recommend!!
If you have interest in the time period or witch trials, the course is really interesting and Koerner and Pereda are really interesting to hear from on the topics. Communication about assignments wasn't great, but assignments are pretty open ended so you have to be driven enough to come up with something you're passionate about. Readings are long and it's much more effective to try to skim readings or get the general idea without closely all the way through.
Phenomenal art history class. The Professors are brilliant and very excited about the course content and engaging with students ideas about such. The readings can be long and arduous, but worth it. Midterm and final are challenging but rewarding. Overall, great course.
If you're interested in studying the art history of witchcraft, then this class will be fun. Due to the class's disorganization I felt like my own interest is what was driving me through it. If it is taught again I'd hope it was slightly more polished.
Professor Koerner and Professor Pereda are two of the kindest, most caring educators I have met at Harvard. They are so supportive of their students and truly invest in their success. This seminar was a great introduction to witchcraft and promoted interesting discussion centered around the images and readings. I also learned so much about how to write humanities / art history research papers, which was so helpful and inspiring. Take this class if you can!!! I absolutely loved this class and the professors
