Love this class. It is different and fun.
GEM!!! As far as first year seminars go, this has got to be up there with the best of them. Ann does a great job at teaching about food, a key topic in today's world, from a scientific lens, in a stress–free way that is understandable. The course really challenges you to think of food holistically and from many schools of thought that value science as the key player. The assignments are fun and relaxed, and the final group project (building an operational farm) was definitely a highlight of my semester. The small class also allows you to meet some great people with similar interests. BONUS OF THIS CLASS: SNACK TIMEEE!
Take Roots & Routes! Going into this class, my attitude was something along the lines of “I’m going to really enjoy this class, and possibly find my calling in it.” Still not sure about the second half, but I was certainly right that I’d enjoy it. I learned a lot about agri science and systems in a setting that I wouldn’t otherwise have had the ability to. I’d had some spark of interest in agriculture and food prior to this class, but it was instrumental in building out that interest to something much more real. Partly because of 52Q, I applied to a food justice internship for the spring–fingers crossed for that!

I think that Ann did a great job of broadening its scope beyond hard science; the majority of the readings were very accessible and many addressed indigenous food ethics & practices, cultural significance of food, and narratives about the science we were studying during class. Of course, I learned a ton about plant and food science as well. It's a pretty chill seminar as far as workload goes; roughly 2 hours of reading per week until week 10, with two very short essays during that time as well, plus one 8–ish–hour group final project.

And, as a bonus, Ann brings a themed snack every week, which is especially helpful because it’s a 9am (or at least was this year) but you don’t have to eat breakfast before :)
I highly recommend taking this seminar. It is very low stakes and you learn a lot, changing your perspective on the food you eat at all time. It truly is the easiest and most fun I had the entire semester.
Make sure you are very passionate about farming.
If you have any interest in sustainability of our food systems this would be a good class to take
