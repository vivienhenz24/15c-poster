The course is not super organized but Im hoping that will improve. Dr Vega–Duran is a great teacher and her lectures are very engaging. Shes also always willing to answer questions from students and really makes an effort to get to know the class.
Great course. Readings are a moderate difficulty and you will grow as a reader and writer throughout the semester. Material is very interesting and would recommend for any Spanish secondary student. You learn a lot about history and literature.

Week 8–9 are tough because you have to work through two novels at the same time. But other than that assignments are very manageable and you get a lot out of them.
Professor Vega's class is a must! Not just this class in particular, but any classes she teaches! She has such a clear passion for whatever topic she teaches that you can't help but share her enthusiasm! I've taken Spanish for years now, both at the high school and college level, and love seeing professors so kind and passionate like her. She is also very willing to meet with you at any given point, even just to chat! Take advantage of this opportunity to improve your Spanish and get to know faculty! If you feel like you are not understanding something or simply want to improve, she will make herself available at almost any time of the day to make sure you can get the help you need! Truly there isn't a kinder Spanish professor you can have!
Not to mention, while the readings can be long and hard, they are enjoyable & get easier as the class goes on!

The content she teaches is phenomenal, and she does an excellent job picking the coursework for her students to enjoy. She is engaging and sweet and VERY SMART! I could not recommend this class more. This is an incredible course taught by one of the most engaging and kind–hearted professors at Harvard. It is challenging especially as someone who does not speak Spanish fluently, but the teaching staff meets you where you are and encourages participation regardless. I felt that reading and engaging with texts instead of learning grammar improved my speaking skills further. I recommend this course if you like history, if you are interested in Spain, or if you have an appreciation for culture / cultural identity in general. The texts we studied were impressively broad and clearly a ton of thought has gone into the materials. It's not so easy but if you do the work every week you will do well. They want you to succeed. Great course.
This is a fairly lecture–heavy class; you will not have the same opportunities to practice your speaking as much as lower level courses.
This course requires Full Fluency in Spanish. If you need to translate every few words when reading a book for example, this course is not for you and the assignments will be tedious to complete. The course is conducted in Spanish and you should feel comfortable presenting in Spanish in front of the class. Furthermore, you will have to write (several compositions, journal entry) which require you to have mastery of Spanish grammar and of contents discussed in class. If you doubt your Spanish skills, this may not be the right class for you.
Take this class! The materials are interesting and Professor Vega–Durn gives great lectures! Section with Maria was also great, they both care a lot about the course and are so helpful and supportive! The format of the course is good with opportunities to practice speaking, which is less common at the 70 level. Do note that the course follows contemporary Spanish history from select political/cultural moments, as opposed to a more sweeping review of the history of Spain (we started in the early 19th century). The assignments offered good opportunities for writing practice and the exam was very fair, this course was amazing overall
If you are looking for a Spanish class that is not a lot of work and you are interested in art, literature, and different perspectives on history then you should definitely take this class.
This is a course that I would highly highly recommend students to take. There's such an organic mix of materials – novels, films, documentaries, paintings etc – that together contribute to the bigger discussion of what it means to be Spanish. The professor also has such an acute awareness of the zeitgeist and tension behind each historical period, thus naturally pulling out important questions like how should a country view its history/collective memory from the texts we read. The professor and TF are both very approachable and they genuinely want to support your learning. Every week, you will write a journal diary on the content discussed during the week. The prompts are free, and although may sound arduous, really help improve your Spanish writing skills and have you constantly reflect on what's going on in class.
