Take! This! Course! Professor Lynch is such an incredible professor, the readings are amazing, and the classroom activities are so much fun. You'll get the opportunity to meet students from Cambridge Public Latin Schools to the organizer of the Prison Book Program. It teaches you perspectives on literacy that you would have never considered before. Genuinely one of my favorite classes in the school.
I would so recommend this course! The readings were well–selected and engaging, and our course discussions were always very lively. It's rare in the English department that you get to learn about reading itself, along with the ways reading is inevitably bound up with politics. The assignment structure was very flexible which I appreciated. The community service component was new this year, so I think that could have been a bit more orderly but it was still a good experience.
This class deals with the sociology of the very concept of reading, but it is an English class and so does so in a way that attends to the aesthetic and humanistic qualities of texts. Would recommend.
I learned so much from this course and loved that I was able to tie in my personal experience with literacy. Much of the discussions built off of our experiences (which were at times heartbreaking) with education, assimilation, and falling in love with reading. This will forever be one of my top 3 favorite courses at Harvard. I think everyone should take this course, even if you aren't an English concentrator.
YOU NEED TO TAKE THIS COURSE!!! PLEASE TAKE THIS COURSE!! PLEASE PLEASE PLEASE I cannot tell you how much I loved this course ; it is the best course I've taken at Harvard.
This was an excellent course with phenomenal readings and a great flow in the curriculum. Professor Lynch generated so much enthusiasm for the topic and we had incredibly insightful discussions and enriching engagement components in this class. She is dedicated to her students, cares about what we want, and really wants us to be better writers and thinkers!
Professor Lynch is an absolute gem –– I took this class as a non–English concentrator because I was intrigued by the Mindich component and because I had seen Lynch give a stellar lecture on Virginia Woolf when I took Hum10. The Mindich part didn't quite live up to the hype (although visits to the Prison Book Program and Houghton Library were super interesting) but everything else about the class was stellar. The discussions on this topic will be top notch (assuming you get a decent group of students, which is likely) and Lynch does a great job of coming up with interesting writing prompts to get you thinking about the text. The final literacy memoir allowed me to think deeply about the way in which my family fostered reading as a value, which I think a lot of Harvard students have some variant of but rarely get to think about and cherish in a structured way. TAKE TAKE TAKE
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! Prof. Lynch is the best and sweetest professor I've ever had. The workload is very doable and the service component is an emotionally–meaningful, but not onerous, compliment to the course. If I could give it three thumbs up I would.
