This class is unnecessarily difficult. There is so much reading. I thought the subject was interesting and most of the discussion was interesting. Quinn is nice and engaging, but I find that he has a rigid view of things and was not always as open minded as he wanted it to seem. Some of the reading was amazing, so if you take the class, really do it. It is, however way to much, so people in the class often did not. I would probably have taken a different class, but the subject is interesting.
This is a great class—a true deep dive into the intersection of philosophy, ethics, and art. As someone with no formal philosophy background (and you definitely don't need any coming in), the formal methods of reasoning in the readings and class really stretched me as a student but were extremely rewarding to work on and think about. The readings are definitely not always the easiest to understand but Quinn helped make everything much clearer in class and we had some super cool conversations.

The teaching of this class is great—Quinn is one of the most dedicated, knowledgable, and friendly professors out there and is extremely chill and accommodating. At the same time, I felt like he cared a lot about each of our thoughts and was dedicated to helping us improve our understanding of philosophy, from (extremely!) thorough gradings to cool conversations. ESPECIALLY if you are on the more shy side, I encourage you to speak up as much as you can in class (it's ok to get things "wrong"!!) and go to Quinn's office hours! He has really interesting insight and it honestly just feels good to express your beliefs, and the class starts feeling a little lopsided when only a few people dominate the conversation. Start your assignments early so you have time to chat about them with him. If you are thinking about taking this freshman seminar, I 100% encourage it, but definitely make sure to review the syllabus to ensure that you are interested in the readings—I admittedly didn't look too closely and had a different idea of the class than what it actually ended up being so some weeks felt drier to me because of that. This course is a lot less work than a lot of first–year seminars so a great way to get acclimated to Harvard—you get as much out of it as you put in! Lookng back, I wish I had engaged with the course even more because the ideas are all so interesting!
This seminar course is a great introduction to (the) philosophy (of art). Quinn is a really nice person and a great instructor who gives excellent feedback on assignments. The workload is manageable and reasonable, readings were extremely interesting, ranging from highly accessible to somewhat dense (ease–of–reading–wise), and events outside of class like Game Night were fun.
The course was a very comprehensive exploration into questions of ethics in art. Not just "is this piece of art moral or immoral" and not just questions about cancel culture––we learned about different philosophical definitions of beauty, the moral importance of art, games as art, etc. I always looked forward to class and thought the discussions were fascinating! Quinn is a wonderful professor who clearly really enjoys teaching and really enjoys this particular subject, and his enthusiasm was contagious. We had a couple of meetings outside of class (watching Hamilton, going to the MFA, playing board games) that were worthwhile and helped show the real–world implications of our discussions.

I originally thought this class would be more of an art class, and it turned out to be much more about ethics. So for the first few meetings and readings, I was a little disappointed. I thought I didn't really like philosophy, but I figured I would stick with the class anyway. That was a great decision! I thought that each class was better than the last, and we read so many enlightening readings that genuinely changed my perspective on art and beauty. The pass/fail nature of the class means that you get out exactly what you put in, so I never felt stressed about readings or papers or assignments, but by the end of the course I prioritized this class over other, graded ones because I really loved this work! Take this class!!
Take this course. Quinn is an incredible teacher and person, I can't recommend this course enough. The readings are engaging, and even though it's a long class, he keeps it interesting and engaging.
