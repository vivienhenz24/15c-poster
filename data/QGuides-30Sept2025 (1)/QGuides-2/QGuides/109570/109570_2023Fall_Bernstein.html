What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Professor is amazing but the readings are dense
pros:
– great variety of topics in religion and a focus in the beginning especially on theoretical frameworks to understand religions
– many readings that allowed me to branch off in interests
– professor Bernstein + our TF Majid Dohan are great people to talk to one–on–one with interests on the course.

cons:
– week to week, readings sometimes felt a little dense and tough until they were broken down later in the class.
– lectures sometimes left me still unsure of some of the main points of core theorists, especially towards the beginning half of the course. Secularization in particular was a bit confusing, and required me to re–read and re–engage with the material a couple times before I could confidently apply it in the later parts of the course and in my essays.
– while I appreciated the open–endedness of the assignment prompts for the final research paper, I wished we had more guidance on example prompts or even just the type of paper and scope of argumentation that was desired from the first two smaller papers.
– while this may not be a fault of the course heads themselves, encouraging student participation and motivation during discussion–related aspects of the course was tough. It was unclear to me whether this was out of lack of understanding the material, lack of comfort in the discussion space, or lack of piqued interest.
This is a great class to take if you have prior experience in either anthropology, social theory, religion, or a similar subject. The class has both undergraduates and graduate students, and I think that undergraduates who had some prior experience with the types of material got the most out of the class. The discussions were amazing and I learned a lot from both Professor Bernstein and my peers.
