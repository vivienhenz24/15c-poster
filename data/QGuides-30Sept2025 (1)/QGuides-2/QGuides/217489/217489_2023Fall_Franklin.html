Take this course! You learn so much about the immune system and new research in the field of immunology. The final project is also so enjoyable and a fun way to test your knowledge on a subject that interested you in the course.
This is a great class! It definitely introduces you to the immune system from the very basics to some advanced topics. The material is not too stressful, and it gives you very foundational knowledge that any biologist should have!
SCRB 177 is a good course, but definitely has a high learning curve. The first few lessons can feel very overwhelming if you have no background in immunology because a lot of information is given to you very quickly. The course gets easier when you start to internalize the basic information about immune cells, but the opportunity doesn't come for that until the first "problem set" really. Overall, the class is quite low–stress, since everything is take–home and there are no exams. The course was challenging, but not stress–inducing, which was a nice change of pace. The Science Communication project was also quite fun and it's nice to have a real final product you can be proud of after the course!
Take this class if you want a low stress SCRB class. The teaching staff is amazing and the workload is very low – only 3 psets, some readings for friday sections, and a project at the end. Very very manageable. Lectures can get monotonous over time and you definitely go way too into depth into the immune system, but overall a solid class. I did fall asleep in a lot of lectures though... its hard to stay engaged.
Highly highly recommend this class — it’s low–stress and a great way to learn more about immunology, which is a complex field but Prof. Franklin makes it much simpler in a really impressive way. There are no exams, just three psets and a final project. The psets were deceptively simple at times and/or had some ambiguous wording, so I recommend going to office hours for all of them just to make sure you’re on the right track with answers.
Take this class! It is a great way to learn interesting information about the immune system without the stress of having to memorize specific information. I appreciated the insight I gained into immunity and specific diseases. Ruth is an amazing lecturer who knows the answer to basically any question asked. I recommend this class with enthusiasm. The Psets are very fair. They’re open book, so if you can read the lecture slides and understand the information on them, as well as draw conclusions from data, you’ll do well. A great upper scrb class to take.
Making your own notes after the lecture is very helpful for the "PSETs".

This class is a great overview of the foundation of immunology. The lectures are very clear and the professor explains the concepts very well.
SCRB177 is a great class if you want to walk with a comprehensive understanding of the immune system and some of its really cool roles in both human health and disease. The teaching staff are very supportive and you can really tell that Dr. Franklin is passionate about the subject. I also really appreciated her willingness to answer questions and give feedback. I think that when it comes to upper level SCRB classes, the professor definitely makes or breaks the classroom experiences and in this case, I definitely felt that I enjoyed the topic of the class way more because I could tell the staff were enthusiastic about the topic. I also think that the work is definitely fair and manageable.
Take this class if you can; it is an absolute blast (the content is so interesting) and the teaching team is phenomenal. Prof Franklin, Alex, and Patricia are always available to answer questions and provide guidance; they really made the class with their invaluable expertise and experience.
