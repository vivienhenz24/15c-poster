Puskar is great!
The material in this class can be very difficult, especially if you don't have previous experience with tensors and have to build intuition for it during the class. However, Puskar is a wonderful instructor and he makes this class definitely worth taking. He is extremely patient, kind, encouraging, and enthusiastic in his interactions with students. No doubt my favorite lecturer at Harvard!
Cool class that gives glimpses of physics.
Loved this class; interesting content and reasonable assignments (4–5 psets + final project). Definitely recommend to fulfill geometry requirement!
Puskar is the best !!!! I am not sure if he will teach this class again, but if he is take it. The class is low–stress in the best way possible where you can really learn a ton. As a physics major, this was a really cool intro to GR from a mathematically rigorous perspective. The connections to physics are endless and certainly do not end at GR, learning differential geometry in this way is crucial for understanding all kinds of theoretical stuff. Highlights included the geometric proofs of Noether's theorems and of course the derivations of the Einstein equations. Puskar did a great job of motivating concepts and proofs with geometric intuition that made abstract concepts pretty intuitive. I did not take 136 and got by fine, but would definitely recommend taking it before if possible. This class is like a substantially lighter version of 230b.
I really enjoyed Math 166. The course material was super interesting and the workload was very manageable! The lectures were always clear and engaging. Puskar is a great lecturer, and also extremely friendly, understanding, and funny! He is always eager to answer questions during or after class (or at office hours), and makes you excited about differential geometry and its beautiful applications.
TAKE ITTTTT.
