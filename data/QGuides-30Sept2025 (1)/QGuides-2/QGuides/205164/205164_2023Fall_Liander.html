The cooking is absolutely phenomenal I love it.
This is a fun class. You learn a lot about Spain's culture, food, and history. Days in the kitchen are the best, and the prep/homework for those is light, but that kind of gets offset by the amount of writing you have to do. It feels like a lot of essays, but they are all fairly short and a good balance of creative and analytical. It's not hard, but it is work that is probably pretty reasonable as long as you don't save it all for the last minute.
TAKE THIS CLASS! It is unbelievably fun. Prior to the course, I knew nothing about cooking. Now, I am eager to try out the recipes we prepared in class with my mother back home. What's more, my Spanish–speaking abilities have grown by leaps and bounds. A nice medley of written excerpts, movies, and paintings propelled the curriculum forward. Now, I find myself on Google Flights looking for cheap tickets to Madrid, because my excitement about going to Spain has been amplified by a magnitude of 10. Profesora Liander is the best!
the cooking classes are the best! Keep an eye out for assignments all over the course site and be prepared to ask a lot of questions about what is due and when. There is a written test at the end of the class, but it is not too bad! hold on to your favorite recipe, have a 1–2 sentence level grasp of the different things you read over the course of the semester, and learn as much of the food vocabulary the class collects each week and you will be set.
This class is so much fun! You get to cook in class and learn about so many interesting recipes. The workload is manageable but the written assignment at the end of the semester is challenging and requires studying in order to do well. Overall, I really enjoyed taking this class!
This is a great course! Great opportunity to practice speaking skills in a fun way. Take it if you're interested in learning about the history of Spanish cuisine or even Spain in general. The cooking sections are very fun (about 4 throughout the semester)
Really wonderful class! Work is on the higher end, but you won't have to worry about grades if you attend class and do all the work on time. You learn all about history and culture of Spain and Spanish cuisine, and you also get to cook every other week!
This is a great Spanish class to take if you are interested in learning more about Spanish cuisine and how its evolved throughout history. The cooking classes are really fun and you get to make some delicious stuff like Spanish hot chocolate and fried eggplant. Homework is very minimal, and the essays are pretty easy to write the night before. If you're looking, however, to significantly improve your Spanish language skills, I would recommend taking a more advanced Spanish class instead.
