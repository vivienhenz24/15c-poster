Great class that takes you on a trip to different parts of the world every week. Be aware that this course is taught by a roster of guest lecturers who cover different topics each week. Some guest lecturers gave one of the most engaging and inspiring lecturers I have seen at Harvard. Some guest lectures were one of the least effective teachers I have and will ever encounter. The great variance on the calibre of teaching was frustrating at times, but I am very glad I took this class.
Exams are really long and you have to memorize A LOT. Lectures were interesting because the way the course is structured, guest lecturers teach about their "thing."
If you do the readings and come to all the sections and take notes you'll be perfectly fine.
Please take this class! The vast range of knowledge you will acquire from each professor and your section TFs is invaluable. Every week is like a tour in an art museum!
Took this course as basically an elective, and really enjoyed it! You do learn about the landmarks themselves, who built them, how they were built, etc., but really the focus of the course is on viewing society/culture through architecture. It's an interesting lens, and the guest speakers do a great job of connecting ideas and topics that I never would have thought of.
Assignments are very chill, and a fun change of pace from the other psets I was doing.
The tests were probably my least favorite part of the course. They require a good amount of memorization to do very well, and you're expected to write a lot very quickly during the exams.
Also, the instructors allowed thesis–writing seniors to skip the midterm, which was very kind of them.
Midterm and finals are HARD. Be prepared to write as fast as possible. But the week–to–week content and topics are quite simple.
This class is great! I found the varying lecturers to be very engaging and interesting. it is a great way to experience an art history class at Harvard!
I really enjoyed learning about architecture around the world! The class taught me to see the influences of some very famous architectural names that I had always heard but not understood. I do wish that the lecture were recorded, since it can be easy to fall behind and there's no way to catch up to study for the exams.
It's fine. Some units are interesting, some are very boring. You don't end up talking about the actual architecture that much which was the biggest peeve I had with the class, I was hoping to learn more about the sites and their personal histories, or the histories of architectural movements, as opposed to several meandering lectures about abstract symbolism.
This class is rewarding and offers exposure to a wide variety of architectural landmarks from around the world. Ive learned not only about architecture, but also about global history and culture through the course. While some lecturers are more engaging than others, be prepared to memorize substantial amounts of informationmuch of which may not appear directly on exams. Still, mastering that volume of information is challenging and deeply satisfying.
This course is incredibly interesting, but quality of the week greatly depends on who is lecturing. Sometimes you get a professor that can't lecture, and it's really difficult to follow along, never mind have to compare things later on. Despite this, I highly recommend going to lecture and not just searching up information, because you'll know what they want you to focus on during comparisons for the midterm and final. Grading for the two papers is really opaque and there aren't many comments. I don't read the assigned readings and I don't think you need to, but you should if you find them interesting and they'll probably be pointing you to what the test will want you to focus on.
This is a good class. The guest lecture format keeps it interesting and the amount of work is reasonable. The midterm was on housing day which sucked. Both the midterm and final are reasonably difficult so be sure to do plenty of review.
I really enjoyed HAA11 as an easy way to get a perspective into the architecture concentration and learn about many famous locations. Felt like a museum tour each class with professors that are leading their fields spending their time with you for a week at a time. Section was great as the graduate TFs are passionate and knowledgable about the topics and stitch them all together. Cool Course!
Good class to take if you need to add something to a busy schedule. You can get away with skipping lecture, but the only downside is that the midterm + final are all about material covered in lecture (that is not really gone over in section). It's a chill class but just very boring.
A low stakes class, but the exams are quite hard and intense to prepare for! A lot of memorization.
Loved this class, it was a great introduction to architecture as someone doing STEM. The rotating lecturers were very enjoyable, I really appreciated having experts in each landmark explaining the logic behind each building.
The two short paper assignments were fun because you got to draw blueprints of buildings, but otherwise, I did not find the material that engaging or interesting. Definitely depends on the person, but this class showed me architecture is not one of my passions, of if learning about various historical buildings and the intricacies of them, then its for you. Be prepared to sit through lectures of professors speaking for the entire class with little detail in slide,s but if you're fine with that, then class would suit you. Exams are really just rote learning so be prepared for that.
