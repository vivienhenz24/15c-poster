Assignments are pretty chill and teach you to read architectural drawings a bit. I took this class as a STEM major with no prior art experience. It's an excellent elective and is low–stakes especially if you are a senior and are eligible for the midterm drop. This class is truly a you–only–get–what–you–give–– you can sit on your ass in lecture and not pay attention and just do the readings glancingly and lose out on a semester's worth of tuition and time while still getting a passing grade, or you can sit down and properly listen to the resident experts that come every week and pick up on a field you had no prior appreciation of.

Regarding logistics, I'd recommend that you just spend a week in advance of the final or midterm memorizing the buildings from the study slides. While I'm sort of mixed on the heavy memorization component in the final exam, I will say that the workload is low (2–5 hours a week) and thus the need for memorization does not cause an undue burden if you plan your flashcards/study schedule appropriately
