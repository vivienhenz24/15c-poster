lecture is ok, kind of boring most of the time because he just summarizes the texts (with easy reading quizzes each class). section is where the real discussion happens. readings vary in length from week to week. some readings are way longer than others (most was 100 pages), but seth makes it clear that some parts of the readings are optional. essays did not take me that long to write and the prompt felt manageable and broad enough for you to explore a topic of your liking.
Loved this class; in–class polls can be annoying but essays are not a lot of work.
If you're really into moral/ethical philosophy, I think you will absolutely love this class. If you're only kind of interested like I was/am, you'll have a good time, but things can get a little boring. The class is VERY formulaic week to week: you read a few texts, Seth explains them in lecture, and you discuss them in section. There is very little throughout the semester to break up this routine, which can make the whole course just blend into one big blob of philosophy. That being said, I still give this class a very positive review. Seth is very intentional with what/how he teaches, and it's obvious that one of his goals is for each of his students to leave his class as better people. He's also just a cool dude, and makes the whole class a super chill vibe.

I also really like how major assignments are structured. You have to write two ~1500 word essays which are based more on your own thinking and perspective than a standard research paper. They were overall really enjoyable to write, and the grading is pretty friendly if you do a good job.

Great class! If you're looking for a fun elective which is easy, but not a joke, and you want to learn something along the way, this is an excellent choice.
Take this class. It has the ability to teach you more than you could ever imagine. It is not crazy demanding, and the work you put in benefits your life greatly.
