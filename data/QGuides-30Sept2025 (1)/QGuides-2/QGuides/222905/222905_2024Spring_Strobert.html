What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Take this class if you already have some experience in art making / drawing / painting. The first half of the course you work on a project with steps that get revealed each week (multiverse idea) as a class, so everyone ends up with a project that is based on one theme. Then, the final project is an opportunity to explore anything you like. Take this class if you want studio opportunities and a chance to spend Harvard budget on supplies (~$200). We didn't really learn much about the concept of multiverse but rather the prof had us come up with our own definitions of it. If you feel limited by very structured courses and like looser guidance this course may be for you.
The structure of the class is unconventional, and expectations are not clearly communicated
This class works very well for students who have a strong sense of independence and creative freedom. I was able to pursue an interest that I would have never pursued otherwise. I allowed myself more creative freedom than I've ever had because we were allowed to explore literally anything. I loved that all resources and materials were provided for us.
I think it's a great class to take if you're hoping to get a better understanding of how to spend time as an artist, and how to find and build meaning and energy in your practice.
This class was amazing. Kianja and Exyl are the best, and they provided immense feedback and support to really explore any project or medium you really wanted to. They also had amazing insights about your work and art in general. However, the class did lack structure: it felt like the initial project dragged on for longer than needed (where weeks kept getting added to it), whereas the final project seemed quite rushed. It also felt hard to access Kianja and Exyl outside of class. You definitely could email them, but I didn't really see them around the studio outside of class time, and I wish that weren't the case. I would still highly recommend this class, and I really enjoyed the focus on process and all of the work that was produced. I also felt like discussions in this class were very thought–provoking, and I enjoyed the great variety of mediums and work that we ended up producing as a class.
