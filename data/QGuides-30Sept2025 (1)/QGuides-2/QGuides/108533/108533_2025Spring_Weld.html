If you're a non–history concentrator looking for a history course where you'll learn a lot, take this one! Great professor and interesting material that is criminally undertaught in the U.S. The structure of sections and the final assignment also offer a great glimpse at how historians think about the world.
Take it, Having an understanding of how various external factors have shaped regions such as Africa, Latin America, and the Arab World is crucial when conversing about almost any global issue.
Take this course, you will not regret it!
READ
Content is extremely broad in its time and content, so its natural that you gloss over some of the material. Having entered knowing very little about Latin America's history, the class was very accessible and was a lot of fun. The readings are a bit excessive at times, but keep up with the primary sources for sure.

Tip:
Make flashcards for the primary sources to prepare for the exams. This gives you evidence you can easily deploy and helps with the author matching part.
If you have any interest in Latam history, take the course! Professor Weld's lecture are consistently engaging.
The class is incredibly rewarding and the material that you learn is awesome. I think everyone moderately interested in Latin America, particularly in relation to the United States, should take this class. It breaks a lot of the preconceived notions of the United States and its influence in the region, but also teaches you a lot about the movements from a Latin American perspective. The class is very hard, and you have to go to every single lecture to do good in the exams since the readings are certainly not enough. But overall, you learn a lot and it's very rewarding.
This class has to take priority over your other classes if you're shooting for an A/A–. If you can balance it out with a course that you are pretty confident you can do well in with less time, I'd recommend that. Do every reading and really try to grasp the central argument or value it plays in the historical context. This class is a constant grind in my opinion. Still very grateful for the experience of taking this course, but would love to see some of the aforementioned suggestions implemented.
Kind of an APUSH–style course but for Latin America: the level is not super difficult maybe because the class has a lot of non–history/social science concentrators and a lot of freshmen. There are a ton of readings each week but in practice you just need to read the primary sources and go to lecture if you don't have a lot of time.
This class is fantastic! It gives a great overview of the region and is very much connected to the present as you are able to draw lines from earlier historical processes. While there are quite a bit of readings, each reading was carefully selected and I enjoyed every single one. Definitely take this class, you will not regret it!

There is also so much room for creativity with your research topic!
I recommend this course with enthusiasm. The work is completely manageable, the professor and teaching fellow are wonderful people to be around, and it is such a joy to be in the class and to absorb so much information throughout the lecture. It makes full use of its hour of instruction and always left me feeling more knowledgeable and content with my enrollment in the class.
