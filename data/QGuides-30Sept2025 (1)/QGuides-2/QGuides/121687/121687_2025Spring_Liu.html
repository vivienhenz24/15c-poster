If you're interested in organometallics, DO TAKE!! Very clear expectations about course goals, logistics, and grading were set from the start, which made this course an even better experience. Richard is an amazing instructor: He is very clear in his teaching, ample examples in class are provided including the more interesting/challenging questions at the end of each lecture that keep us thinking beyond the basic content. Weekly quizzes are very helpful in keeping us on track and made our lives 10x easier when it came to studying for the final exam. Overall, very enriching course and highly recommend.
A natural continuation of undergraduate organic chemistry courses into the more advanced and modern context of organometallic reactions. Essential content for any synthetic organic chemist. I recommend taking both 153 and 105 for the most robust organic chemistry understanding the department can offer. Richard is an excellent lecturer and the course is well structured to teach efficiently even without problem sets.
First half of the course covers the basic and pre–requisite information needed to understand organometallic chemistry. Second half covers more reactions, catalytic cycles, and mechanisms.
This class is GOATED. It was my favorite class that I took this semester, no contest about it. Here are some of the highlights of the class:

1. The Teaching Staff is awesome. Richard is an amazing professor– he is incredibly smart, very friendly, easily accessible outside of class, and welcomes input. You will learn a lot in this class from him. The Head TF, Paul, was also super knowledgeable and a very chill and approachable guy, so it made seeking clarification accessible and easy.
2. The class curve is very generous. We had extra credit on nearly all of the in–class quizzes, and the grading was quite chill on all of the assignments overall. If you do every assignment and just a little bit of review each week before the quizzes (not more than 3 –4 hours a week), you should easily be in the A range.
3. You cover a lot of very interesting material, but in an intuitive and low–pressure environment. I took this class concurrently with Chem 40 (which is usually a prereq for this class) and I wish I had just used this class to fulfill my Inorganic requirement. The first 1/4 of the class is a speedrun of Chem 40 content going slightly more in depth into everything; the next 2/4 is an overview of all the elementary steps of organometallic chemistry followed by an in–depth exploration of the mechanisms of each; and finally, the last 1/4 is applying this new knowledge to learn new reactions. You will learn a lot in this class, but it will not be stressful because of the above reasons.

TL;DR: Overall, this is an awesome class that I would highly recommend anyone studying Chemistry take when it is available. It is everything you could want in a class– interesting material, awesome professor and teaching staff, not too much work per week (3–4 hours), and a generous curve. Definitely take Chem 153 if you're considering it!!!

Take this class!! It's a wonderful way to develop a better grasp of the field of organometallic chemistry. If in organic chemistry you ever looked at a reaction and wondered what the heck is that metal over the arrow and how does it make the reaction happen, take this class. It really helps bridge the gap between organic chemistry and the use of metals in current synthetic methodologies.
