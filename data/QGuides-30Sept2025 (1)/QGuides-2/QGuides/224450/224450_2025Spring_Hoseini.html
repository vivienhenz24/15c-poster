What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
(for non AFVS students) Take this class if you can. I am an econ and computer science major but I have always had an artistic itch in my brain that I needed scratched. This class met once a week and the homework was always to prep materials for a painting session next week or finish a painting you weren't able to finish in class. It's very chill and during class we always talked shortly at the start then began getting into painting on our own with earpods in. Definitely a great break from math and structured academia where you can enjoy creativity, perception, and the other side of you brain (all areas that are rarely touched if you are a hands on econ/CS/math person). I wasn't the only one. One of our best painters was Pre Med Track. Definitely the best and most rewarding way to take a class if you are a creative person and dont want to take a lame ahh gened and write a meaningless 10 paper paper. Atleast here, you actually train an untouched side of your brain, have fun, low stakes/workload, AND have artwork to take home at the end of the semester. I reccomend going to UPS and shipping your paintings buy paying for the fragile packing service (somewhat expensive but worth it if you made paintings you love).
TAKE IT!!! Prof is super weird but in the best way. You will become a much better artist. I had the best time.
If you are looking to enhance your still life painting skills I would highly recommend this course. Its not terribly difficult and not too much is required outside of class time. If you would like to challenge your artistic lens and develop your own style, this is not the course for you. This is a good course for really any level!
This was truly a very helpful course, particularly for those with less experience (or at least less recent experience) with painting because it encouraged developing style and focused on breaking down how to make paintings that are more than than recreations of what you see. There was lots of in–class time to paint and consistent advice/feedback from the professor and TF, and critiques were long but helpful for learning about what others take away from your work.
This class was incredible. You can go from knowing nothing to creating something you are really proud of!
