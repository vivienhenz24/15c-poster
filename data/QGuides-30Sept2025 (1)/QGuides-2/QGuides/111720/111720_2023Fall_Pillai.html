I left the class with a much greater understanding of the immune system in broad strokes that were relevant for my work in public health. Take it
Take this class!! The content is amazing, difficulty isn't too much and Shiv is great! You will not regret taking this class and will leave with a great knowledge base. You don't even need to be planning to study biology at an advanced level (I am not).
Take this course!! It is excellent, organized very well and Dr. Pillai is a very knowledgeable and kind professor who will definitely expand your understanding and appreciation of immunology. I took this course and without a doubt, it is one of my favorite courses I have ever taken!
Lectures can be disorganized, and most of the learning happens in section, but it's a light workload and interesting material
This class is an amazing class if you have any interest at all in immunology. You learn so much and Shiv is an amazing professor.
This course is what I expected for an introduction to immunology. The topics are quite challenging and some level of memorization is required. The lectures can be sometimes disorganized as the drawings/handwriting are illegible, but the content is reinforced in section (which in my opinion is the best part of this course). Having some graduate students in the course can be daunting sometimes, but the workload (3 psets and the HMX course) is manageable. While PSETs are relatively simple, the responses and grading are very nitpicky. The textbook is way too thorough for the course and should not be the main source of the content as you will only be tested on lecture content.
Definitely stick with it! There are a lot of terms to memorize, but the class is incredible, interesting, and my favorite biology class I've taken so far. TAs and Dr. Pillai are so willing to help and the lectures are so well done and interesting.
I really enjoyed this course. Coming from someone who didn't really like LS1B, this class is great. It does require a bit of memorization, but if you're premed I think this would be a great class to take just to get an introduction about diseases and how your body deals with it. I'm sure it'll also be great for med school. Also Dr. Pillai is a great professor, sometimes very messy with his lectures, but they're understandable.
Two main points:

1. This is my favorite class ever.

2. This is NOT a gem, but if you do the work you will get a good grade!

The course does have its flaws and lectures can be hard to follow at times. There are lots and lots of details and it takes WORK to memorize all of them. I was studying for this class weekly it was maybe 40% less weekly studying than LS2, for reference. Don't let the "3 psets" fool you into thinking it's an easy class; you have to be disciplined and study because there is just too much information to memorize at the last minute. Don't worry about reading the textbook, just make sure you stay on top of HMX and are paying attention in lecture/studying your lecture notes weekly because if you zone out you miss a lot, and most of the time lecture slides are not at all helpful. So if you miss something you have to ask a friend or go back and rewatch the lecture.

However, lectures are really interesting and engaging. Dr. Pillai is a great speaker. Sometimes he goes a bit fast and you will inevitably miss important points. But your section leaders have your back, so pay attention in section too and you'll be fine! I heard there's a giant curve but this is being written before final grades are out so I don't know if that's real.
It is a good class to give you more detail about the mechanisms behind immunologic processes.
This class is beautifully taught and makes you really appreciate the many moving parts of our immune systems. Despite the varying degrees of expertise in the class, the course makes sure everyone has a lot to learn while keeping it manageable and understandable, even for undergrads. I really liked the way this class made me think about molecular biology and the logic and mechanics of every pathway instead of focusing on memorization. I especially loved the poems at the end of each lecture! Overall extremely happy I took this class!
