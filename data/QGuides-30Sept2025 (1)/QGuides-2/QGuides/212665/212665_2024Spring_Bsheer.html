If you are interested in taking a history course at Harvard, TAKE THIS ONE. It is super useful/topical knowledge to understand modern Middle Eastern history and the lectures each feel like a really interesting podcast. Professor Bsheer is an incredible teacher and inspiration person; she is invested in her students, so I would definitely stop by one of her office hours to chat with her. Could not recommend this course more highly.
This course is a great introduction to modern middle eastern history and sets up a lot of concepts (like Orientalism and the Politics of Knowledge Production) that are important to learn if you want to understand the complex history of the region. This course made me a better writer and taught me never to take history at face value. I felt comfortable expressing my opinions in class even in this current charged situation.
Take this if you are really into Middle Eastern history and already have a good background. Do not take it if you are a government student interested in learning about the region for geopolitical reasons, you will find it confusing, overly difficult, and boring.
It is a good course with good readings, a knowledgeable professor, and thorough lectures. Sections were the highlight because the TF facilitated discussion very well and the readings were rich. However, I think the course topic often lost some of its nuance, especially during weeks where topics that are still politically controversial were covered. I think most students came in with a pretty defined understanding of the Middle East in relation to the West, wherein the all–powerful and ill–intentioned West is oppressing the innocent and impotent Middle East. I think this perspective, which takes away agency from Middle Easterns, both the ones that work for and against the good of the people of the region, was reinforced in this class. It forgets a) that the people of the region aren't mere passive victims, many of them have agency to fight or continue/increase oppression and b) it forgets the contingency of history – the West will not forever be some political/military oppressive superpower, and so when eventually the balance of power shifts, we will be left shocked when some other region takes power and starts oppressing others – thus we have to learn history differently and more objectively so that we don't get caught like this.
Professor Bsheer is an incredible professor. This class is an amazing introduction/enhancement for your knowledge about the Middle East.
A good course. Bsheer is lovely but clearly supports certain things. She is very aware and smart. Take advantage! Midterm sucks, very hard and under extreme time constraints. Keep track of all your notes and do the readings
This class is good, but if you do not have a lot of middle eastern history background I would just be aware they may assume you know some. The course is also pretty scattered historically.
Take this class! Professor Bsheer is an amazing lecturer and really walks you through the history of a very complex region. I cannot recommend it enough!
Fascinating and important course but quite dense. I would not recommend taking this if you havent taken History at Harvard before. Sometimes its unclear how deep your understanding needs to be.
Bsheer is a great lecturer and the course is quite good. I wish there was more of a focus on late 20th–21st century history, as the course doesn't address situations in Iraq, Syria, Yemen, etc as thoroughly as I expected, but I suppose there's only so much space in a semester and overall the class is well–structured, the themes are clear, and most major content is covered. Not a toxic/competitive class environment and a lot of people are there because they're interested and want to genuinely learn about the issues at hand!
Please go out of your own way to make a connection with Professor Bsheer. She has a wonderfully intimidating lecturing style, but she is the most down–to–earth, kind, and widely knowledgable teacher that will leve you more enriched after any conversation you should have with her.
