What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Animation is so many things and there are so many different ways to animate. Don't compare yourself to others in the class and focus on developing your style and what feels best for you. And even if you are taking this class just to experiment make sure first and foremost you have fun and learn what you can!
I think this course is a pretty good overall introduction to animation. I found it to be more of a very broad overview as opposed to learning all the details, which is fine, just don’t expect to become extremely well–versed in any one animation technique. The workload is very doable and the teaching staff are often available to help out, especially for the final project.
Be prepared to do a lot of work. Also, there is a lot of introduction to new computer programs and animation techniques. It can feel like a lot, but if you put in the effort to learn the various forms of animation you will find much success in your work.
**Take this class!! It's very fun and engaging and you'll learn so much about animation. Beware, it's a pretty time consuming class (had to spend a few nights in the studio working on my final project) and you'll definitely have to put in quite a bit of effort. But it's so worth it once you see your projects come to life. If you have any interest in animation, I definitely recommend as a broad intro into the practice.
I loved loved loved this course. If you’re looking to explore different mediums for telling stories and exploring ideas, this is the class for you! However, one thing I will say is that this class is not about character design or 3d animation, so don’t expect that.

Ruth and Exyl are absolutely amazing, and their enthusiasm for animation is contagious. I loved the weekly(ish) screenings, as they were really helpful and relevant to each other weekly assignments.

I would say that the workload is honestly not that bad, as long as you manage your time effectively and use class time well. I spent around ~4 hrs per week outside of class during most of the semester, and then around 8 in the last few weeks.
Amazing course! Ruth and EXYL were amazing, and really took all the time to make sure we had all the resources we needed to succeed in the class. Ruth would come in on weekends sometimes to help, which made me feel really supported as a student. Gets time consuming especially if you're taking this in the fall, around Thanksgiving, because that's when the project is due. Everything takes longer than you think it will. But effort is worth it –– as EXYL says, "Animation is a medium that rewards hard work."
This is an incredible class! If you have any interest in anything artistic, this class is a great way to fuel that excitement. The assignments take a while because animation is a long process, but if you love the work you're doing, then you'll have so much fun making everything and will feel really proud of yourself in the end. Ruth is hilarious and gives great advice. The TFs are also really engaged and thoughtful in the work we do.
