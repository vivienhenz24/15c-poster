Comments
If you're a grad student thinking of working in or near the elections space, this is a great course to take. You'll read key pieces of the literature, and get the seeed of a paper that you can work on moving forwards.
Excellent class. Steve is great at facilitating discussion and diving very deeply into the papers. Also takes time every class to check in on people's research, give them opportunities to get feedback.
Ansolobehere is great, you should definitely take this if you are an undergrad who is interested in the more mathematical side of gov and want to be in an environment where everyone is very willing to share their ideas. As an undergrad I actually like being with the grad students and hearing about the things they were interested in. This class is a really great foundation to so many topics related to elections, and I came away from it with a really well–rounded and informed grasp on the big ideas about elections and voting out there in the academic world. (Really great class if you are planning on writting a thesis)
An interesting dive into the elections and electoral politics literature. Love that the assignment throughout the course is a real research paper with real data on your topic of interest that builds upon the topics learned in class. Prof. Ansolabehere is an engaging lecturer whose enthusiasm for the topic(s) makes the class fun and interesting, and keeps you motivated to learn. He will also help you find data for your class paper/project and/or point you in the right direction. Previous political science coursework is helpful but not necessary as many of the foundational works are covered. Knowledge of statistics is needed in order to do your own data analysis for the project. Would definitely recommend this course!
