This is a good class to get introduced to environmental engineering in a low stakes environment! No exams and psets very doable. Unfortunately lectures can be boring but overall an okay course.
I took this class as an elective (not a concentrator) because I wanted to be more informed about climate issues, and I thought the class was okay for that... it ended up being a lot more time spent trying to understand python than trying to understand the environment lol. The teaching staff is very helpful though and the workload is not too bad. And the guest lectures are pretty cool. It's a good broad survey course, just doesn't go super in–depth into anything.
Bryan and Steve genuinely care so much about their students and want them to enjoy the class and take as much as possible out of the class. The course is manageable, as there are no exams and you never get that deep or technical into any one topic, but you still seem to learn quite a bit through the labs and problem sets. Additionally, the final project was a fun opportunity to explore an air quality issue that interested you.
Wonderful class. Wofsy is a legend of the field and the class spans a wide range of environmental topics. Bryan is a good lecturer.
Take this class if you have any interest in the environment! It serves as a great survey course for a bunch of different important topics. Lectures and guest lectures are engaging and you definitely get what you want out of the course if you put in the time. The coding component is not something to be wary of: I entered the course without any coding experience I was able to do fine on the assignments and learn a little bit of Python. Support is great and office hours are super helpful when it comes to coding questions or completing PSets. Workload is super manageable and labs are interesting but not too intensive.
Take it if you have any interest in the topic! Im not concentrating in a STEM field and I hadnt taken any college physics or chem classes but I still found it manageable and interesting. This is a hands–on and assignment/ project–based class so its not very stressful.
You will love the section TF's. They are dope and there to just help you get through. Brian is a tough man but he's sweet and cares a lot for his students on the inside. Steve is the best, genuinely. He is what makes this course amazing. The course perfectly lets you familiarize yourself with the basics of environmental engineering, nailing the main concepts. Highly recommend.
Overall this was an okay introduction to environmental engineering. You mostly get a feel for the field and the people you'll be spending time with since ESE is such a small concentration. Get ready for a very very limited overview of ESE and some interesting labs, some interesting P–sets, but overall a very light load class with not a lot of interesting lectures.
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! It is a fantastic way to learn about our climate and environment and the natural and human causes of climate change. The lectures are super engaging and the teaching team is fantastic. The highlight is the lab components and final project. The coding component is not taught very well; however, office hours will get you through.
Take PS11 concurrently!
This course is a great introduction to the ESE concentration, at times lecture can be confusing but you don't have to always make sense of it all until the PSet which you will need to go to office hours and clarify what you learned. There is no tests which makes the learning much more meaningful in my opinion and the staff really want you to succeed in the course and love ESE. The labs are also very fun and captivating and don't always take much time. In terms of choosing between the R and Python sections, choose R!! As someone who knows a little bit of Python and nothing of R, I am really glad that I was able to learn R.
This class is really fun and not too much work. I will say that it is a bit difficult being a beginner at coding but office hours are super helpful. I reccomend keeping track of your work well because sometimes tasks piled up (lab report, final project midpoint and pset at the same time) so be careful to not slack off too much on weeks when there is less work.
This is a course intended for first–year students and is very approachable for anyone with any background. That being said, the course is (extremely) easy for anyone with even a mere science background. PSETs won't take more than 30 minutes, labs also won't take more than 30 minutes. It is still a great course for learning fundamentals of environmental science and engineering!
