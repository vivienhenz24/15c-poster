What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If you're a PhD student in Biostatistics, this is a great class to be taking alongside Inference I because it's not nearly as much work in comparison.

If you _really_ want to learn Computer Science, I feel like there must be a more rigorous / theoretical course somewhere that you can take, perhaps in the CS department.

Georg is a very nice instructor, love him to bits. At the same time, I thought the presentation of material in class was very disjointed, so I feel you're almost better off writing down the main topics that class will go over and finding a quiet two–hours here and there by yourself and reading about them in–depth instead if you want a very clear, theoretically rigorous treatment.
This is an interesting course that covers a wide variety of topics. I think if you are looking for a deep–dive into any of the particular topics, additional coursework may be needed, but this is a great overview. A background/familiarity with essential computer science topics is very beneficial.
I highly recommend this class if you're looking for an instructor who genuinely cares about teaching and engages directly with students. Instead of merely reading from slides prepared by others, Professor Hahn actively explains concepts, making the material accessible and interesting. In this class, you'll enjoy learning about data structures and algorithms. Professor Hahn is an expert in algorithms and NP–hard problems, and he shares his knowledge with passion and enthusiasm. Join this class for a truly enriching educational experience!
