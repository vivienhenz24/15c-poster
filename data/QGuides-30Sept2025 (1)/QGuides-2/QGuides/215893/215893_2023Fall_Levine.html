First off, this class is amazing and also a gem. You will have to put in a bit of work (maybe an hour or two) a week outside of class. Take this class if you need the Gen Ed req. out of the way and want a fun interactive course.

The class is centered around the final project where you present as another person in your section – forget the acting techniques, how to be Marlon Brando, or method acting stuff. I remember reading the q guide last year about this course and a comment said that "this class was more of a social psych class than anything" which now I could not agree more with. For the first two weeks, you learn various acting methods and watch classic films trying to analyze the methods at play, which I LOVED. After you draw the sticks in section, you then learn theory on social presentation (Plato's allegory of the cave, Aristotle etc) and dive into complex social problems and their intersection with acting (race–based casting etc). At some points, the discussions felt a little forced along the lines of "let's have a meaningful progressive conversation just for the sake of it, to feel good about ourselves, and most importantly to sound modern and smart".

As you can tell, I took this class hoping to be Marlon Brando and did not achieve that (I learned to be someone else though!). However, I am so glad that I took this class. The community you gain from participating in this class is exceptional. I've met some great friends from section and now we have an amazing bonding experience (learning to act like someone else in section and portray that) that we can continue to talk about for years to come. I am so glad I took this class!
