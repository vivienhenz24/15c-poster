This class has some really interesting content, and its probably one of the most relevant courses that you can take if youre planning on going deep into renewables and the energy/power industry. However, in exchange for that educational value, be prepared to deal with a bit of awkward course pacing and some logistics quirks. Definitely go to section and either the TFs or professors office hours, and a strong piece of advice: while there is no official course textbook listed, The Physics of Energy (by Jaffe) is basically such a book, so start reading it as soon as it goes on library reserve and itll help tremendously. Do those tips, and otherwise the course experience is generally great, including the professors value–packed lectures.
Relatively difficult course material (depending on your background) and problem sets. However, if there is an interest in thoroughly understanding energy science (i.e. solar cells, fission, fusion, & various battery designs) down to the atomic level, this course is excellent!
Very interesting applications of technologies developed with the help of concepts taught in this class.
It is a great class, I just wished professor Hau went a bit easier on the physics and math part and focused more on conceptual understandings.
***Take this class! Prof. Hau is an absolutely wonderful instructor – she provides the perfect balance of theory and application to physics and creates a stimulating, yet approachable, learning environment. Psets are every two weeks and are easy to complete if you attend office hours. No exams are a perk, and the final project is very flexible and engaging. Overall 10/10 – the best physics course I have taken yet!
