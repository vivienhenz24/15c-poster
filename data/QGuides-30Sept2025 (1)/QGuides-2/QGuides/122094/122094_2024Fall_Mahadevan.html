This is class has interesting material and is well taught but is fairly challenging and requires a decent amount of time to complete the problem sets.
AM104 is a solid class. The material is actually quite interesting if you enjoy math. However, there's no doubt that it's challenging, and the class is incredibly fast–paced. Maha is a good lecturer but, frankly, I sometimes found his lectures a little bit hard to follow. They are very conceptual, which is great if you respond well to that, but they tend to overcomplicate what you actually need to know for evaluation purposes. Most of the PSETs are fairly reasonable, but definitely have a good study group. 2 off–weeks were built in which is nice. However, the last PSET of the semester was 3–4x as long as every other homework and was assigned over Thanksgiving Break, which I did not appreciate. I found that completely unreasonable. Otherwise, PSETs were what you would expect. Midterm was hard but graded generously. The take–home final was quite hard as well – make sure you give yourself a LOT of time to work and, frankly, try to shore up any concepts you're uneasy on beforehand because those questions are super brutal. Overall, if you enjoy math this class is interesting, but make sure that you have the time to dedicate to the very hard subject material. The first few weeks are deceptively easy. It gets harder.
