Great class if you are interested in the law! Prof. Holland and the teaching fellows are great and are super available to help with your learning. Prof. Holland will often open lecture to discussion, so if you are the type who likes to share your opinion in front of the class, I would recommend this course. The memo grading felt pretty easy, but the midterm was a little challenging & takes up a big portion of your grade, so make sure to prepare for that by doing the readings and knowing the concepts.

Because this is a survey class, the content jumps around a bit from week to week. This means that you don't really get a "deep dive" into any one topic, so I wouldn't recommend taking the class if you are excited for any one course module. A broad interest and willingness to learn about new areas of the law will help in this course.

He also takes a genuine in his students and gives tailored advice! Professor Holland is also so nice and smart! Def go to her OH! If you're interested in law or going to law school, I would say this class is a must–take! I feel I learned a lot more about law school and the purpose of studying/practicing law.
TAKE THIS CLASS! Prof Holland and Uma (head TF) are the best. This class is definitely not a gem, but totally doable. You will learn so much about the law and how it can reify inequality, and it'll make you think twice about how the US does things compared to other countries. We talk about police brutality, drug laws, immigration, gender violence, AI, housing, and a bunch of other relevant policy and legal areas. This class was truly amazing––take it!

I came into this class with a vague understanding of how the law interacts with social issues such as drug and decriminalization laws in Latin America or housing laws. While the readings were long, I was able to get away with not having to read all of it and instead fill the gaps in my knowledge through lectures and section. I think this class helped me clarify that I'm not too passionate about law but that wasn't because of any flaws with the class. I did appreciate the class trip we took to observe a law classroom. At the very end of the course we also had a movie festival where students submitted video and song clips from media to observe the topics from class in the world. Overall, I really enjoyed the class and I would recommend it to anyone who cares about learning more about social inequality.
