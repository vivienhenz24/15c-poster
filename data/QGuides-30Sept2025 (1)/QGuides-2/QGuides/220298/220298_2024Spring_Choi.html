TAKE THIS CLASS!!!!!! The work is manageable but thought–provoking, introspective, and truly unique. Professor Choi is a gem of a professor and she truly wishes you to succeed. This course gave me the vocabulary to talk about Asian and Asian American issues, as well as being exposed to materials/discussions that helped me to articulate feelings I've had as an Asian American immigrant but couldn't locate prior to taking this class. Even if you aren't of Asian American heritage or of immigrant backgrounds, there is something for everyone to take away from this class. This class touches on issues like feminism, social constructs, modern issues, and history that will enhance your learning experience in your other courses as well.
Take this class if you are ready to deep dive into Asian literature! Although it is a survey class, and many weeks you will read excerpts rather than the whole work, it is still quite intensive! The workload and time commitment is certainly manageable, but because the class meets once a week for nearly 3 hours, our conversations can get really deep and still require ample effort/preparation. Nonetheless, it is structured so some weeks will be heavier than others, so if you need a break, you will get one some weeks. If you are passionate about Asian/Pacific/Asian American history and Asian/American women's history, this class will be very enjoyable and fruitful for you. It is a great way to gain exposure to more writers, learn about history/imperialism/misogyny/modernization, and build up your back pocket of works you are able to reference. Professor Choi is very knowledgable on the topic and has high expectations for you, even though she will adjust the course to help you succeed! Even though it is a chill and understanding class (as many WGS classes are), Prof. Choi still makes discussions very deep and you will have to read actively! There are not many assessments, which I really enjoyed, besides the big final project, which can be as inventive as you like. I don't think you'll regret taking this class, but it's also not a must–take! Very useful nonetheless to expose urself to more authors
TAKE THIS CLASS!!!!! This is an ABSOLUTE GEM!!! Professor Choi is the sweetest and most considerate prof I have ever met. The class provides a low–stakes completely stress–free environment in which you can interact with works by Asian and Asian American women authors that many people do not know of. It has completely changed the way in which I view race and gender especially in regards to literary works and was such a valuable class for my personal identity exploration as well. I cannot stress how good this class was and highly recommend it to anyone, no matter your experience level with the course topics. You will not regret it at all.
I absolutely recommend this course to anyone and everyone, especially to female Asian and Asian American students! You will learn and discover so much about female Asian and Asian American literature and yourself. The connections you make between society, traditional cultural norms, and your personal experience are foundational for contemplation and reflection about your life. Many of the struggles and challenges that you have faced are likely tied to the societal themes that are covered in this course. You will also learn about East Asian history in class, which provides context to the readings. The way the course integrates history, literature, film, guest speakers, and a creative final project is truly one of the best liberal arts experiences you can get from a course at Harvard.

Professor Choi has structured the course effectively to where the readings and assignments are a manageable workload each week. She has put an immense amount of work into curating the readings and assignments each week so that the students will get the most out of them. The short quizzes about the readings are not stressful at all, which further supports the students to truly enjoy each reading for what it is. Professor Choi is the most supportive instructor, always prioritizing her students and making time for them during office hours to ensure that they succeed throughout the semester. As students, we could not ask for a greater course or instructor.
The course itself is a must–experience if you're at all interested in EAS, WGS, or the literary studies. It feels almost like an amalgamation of sociological analysis, critical reading, and historical contextualization. The most wonderful part, though, was definitely the way that we were all invited to integrate our personal experiences into our discussions. I've been able to learn so much from my peers (and Prof. Choi, of course) with their vast and comprehensive insights, and it was so cool hearing from people with such different disciplinary and cultural backgrounds.
I think everyone should take this class, especially if you identify as an Asian American female student. Prof. Choi is one of the kindest human beings and makes you feel incredibly seen, heard, and valued. Your ideas get expanded and challenged by your peers, and the classroom community is absolutely unmatched, as everyone is fully bought in and engaged. Even if you're not a WGS concentrator, I highly recommend taking this class. It was not only intellectually so fulfilling to read all these texts, but it also broadened my artistic horizons and made me feel like I was capable of literary analysis and of being a humanities student.
I would say my experience with this class had its ups and downs. The readings for this class are absolutely phenomenal if you're interested in learning more about East Asian literature. We read so many different people and some of the stories are incredible and I know I will be thinking about them for a long time to come. I also learned a lot about East Asian history and culture through this class. That said, I think my experiences during class had their ups and downs.

Discussion wasn't always the most interesting. I think that sometimes the discussion questions provided didn't lend themselves to digging deep into the texts and finding interesting insights. Instead, they often focused on recapping the plots of the texts we had read. I think that this issue could be overcome if the people in the class came to class ready to take the discussion questions into deeper/ more interesting territories, but that wasn't always the case this semester. Additionally, sometimes it felt interesting discussions would get cut off for the sake of getting through all of the discussion questions. I think that the weeks where we discussed Asian American issues were much better but that some of the weeks where we were discussing Asian texts were not as engaging.

Another thing to flag is that this class has many assignments. They are usually small and easy to complete, but you really need to be on top of them. Professor Choi puts a big emphasis on turning things in on time, but if you talk to her she is extremely kind and understanding and will accommodate you.
Take this class! Prof. Choi is the warmest and kindest professor, and this class was very intellectually rewarding! The readings were carefully selected and included a wide range of works from the pre–modern to the modern era; the pieces were all written by Asian Women. Taking this class was one of the best decisions I made, and it gave me a broader understanding of history, culture, and my own identity.
Take this class! Professor Choi is a wonderful person who cares deeply about her students. Some weeks are particularly more intense with the readings, but still very doable.
TAKE THIS CLASS IF YOU CAN! An absolutely amazing class with wonderful readings. Very well structured and not stressful.
No other course I've encountered has such a thoughtful, well–curated syllabi of works that go from the pre–modern era to today...I came into this class knowing two authors and came away with so many novelists and poets whose works I will continue reading after this class. Come into this with an open heart and be as earnest as Prof. Choi generously is and you will have a great time.
Enjoy Professor Choi! She is by far the most wonderful human and professor on campus (coming from someone who's gotten to learn from her for two semesters). If she had more classes for me to take, I would. Make sure to utilize office hours because she cares so much about helping you! And please do all the readings thoroughly for your own sakeshe never makes this stressful, but you will get so much out of the texts we read, especially with the historical context Professor Choi always makes sure to provide. She is a gem on this campus and this class is a must–take before you graduate if you're an Asian female!!!!!
