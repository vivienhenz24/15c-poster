What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Take the class if you have less workload.
Ken is a wonderful professor that cares deeply about his students. There is so much to learn, both from the class materials and from Ken, if interested in the subject matter. It also gives you a chance to explore your learning in the context of a country that you enjoy, and the final project is split up into multiple parts that make the work not too daunting. Absolutely take this class! Best professor on campus :)
I would not recommend this class unless you have a light schedule otherwise. Ken is extremely smart, so you learn a lot, and solid waste is a very important and applicable topic, but the execution of the class is much less than ideal. Assignments are often too ambiguous for what the grading wants, and feedback doesn't provide much clarity for what you could have done better. Doing poorly on pop quizzes is pretty much inevitable even if you do the readings in detail and study before class because you don't get much time to do them and they are extremely specific (fortunately they don't count for too much of the final grade). The final project is manageable given the work you do throughout the semester but the individual parts are a lot of work given the expected turnaround. Overall, it is possible to do well in the class but you have to put in a lot of time and work, so this class will have to take the front seat if you want to do well.
Be ready to work
To be direct, this course is difficult. If you want to have an A, you will have to put in a lot of work with outlining, editing, and attending office hours to discuss and refine your ideas. This is a course where it really is best to start the assignments the day that they're assigned, because waiting until the last minute will have a negative impact on your ability to perform well. However, that being said, Professor Thomas is understanding, fair, and accommodating as needed. It is hard work, but if you plan well, always ask questions and prepare yourself to expect to put a lot of work in heading into the course, then it is quite feasible to have a positive outcome grade wise. Grades aside, it still is informative regardless. I still am glad that I took this course.
The course material and structure is good, systematic and covers a lot of useful topics. It gives a good base to understand the issue. However, in terms of workload, 2 assignments per week may become an irritant, in addition to the possibility of quizzes every week. Also, grad students may feel a bit out of place because of the methodology being a bit inflexible – keep this in mind as a trade off between the very useful material and topic and this concern.
