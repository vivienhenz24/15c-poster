You should take this class if you're interested in the subject material. If you already know a lot about the continent though, this may not be the course for you because it is a survey of the continent's comparative politics, not in depth on particular regions or countries. You should know that there are three 5 page papers throughout the semester so the writing is more spread out than in other GOV 94s.
Really great class! I knew very little about Africa going in, and was a great way to learn a ton about a bunch of different countries. I liked getting to choose my own country to research and become an expert on. Readings were great, discussions were interesting. Would really recommend this class to anyone, but particularly gov concentrators looking for a great gov94 about IR (which there are pretty few of)
If you want a Gov 94 on a unique topic, take this course! There aren’t many courses on Africa in the Gov department and this one is a great way to learn about a wide swath of the world you might otherwise never touch. There’s a decent amount of reading, but it’s mostly interesting, and so are the class discussions. I will say that the course readings could use an update, since a lot of them are around 20 years old and it’s not always clear whether the arguments made in them are still relevant today. While this course is heavy on writing, it’s fairly spread out, with a 5–page paper about every month and then a 10–page final paper. I thought 25 pages on one country was a little bit too much (you pick a country to write about for the semester), so maybe diversifying the prompts a bit would help with that. Overall, though, I definitely felt like I learned a lot and I really enjoyed the weekly discussions.
Tons of papers but they aren't very hard
I cannot recommend this course enough for anyone interested in IR or Comp gov, or for anyone who wants to have a much better understanding of African history, the problems the continent faces today, and some of the solutions and reasons for hope in the face of those issues. Dr. Rhodes was a wonderful professor, and he managed/led discussions masterfully. The semester–long case study project was incredibly manageable and gives you an opportunity to take a deep dive into one country. The content covers basic history of pre–colonial, colonial, and post–independence Africa, with the weight on the latter of the three, and it covers issues from coups to economic development to identity politics, and coming out of this course I feel like I have learned SO MUCH about the continent and especially my specific case study. On paper, the readings may look like a lot, but they are very manageable and if you ever feel that you don't understand one of them, your classmates will be so into the class that it won't be noticeable that you aren't talking for that section of the class. Meanwhile, I never felt like any of the readings being assigned were unnecessary or a waste of time to read, and everything in the course is wonderfully crafted! If you have any interest in this topic PLEASE TAKE THIS COURSE!!! I wish there were more like it, and I am incredibly sad that the semester is over
