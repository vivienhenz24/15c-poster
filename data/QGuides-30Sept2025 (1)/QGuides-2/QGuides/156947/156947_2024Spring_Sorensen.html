This class is so fun and sweet and opened a window into a side of science I never really considered. The teaching staff was so supportive and kind and the workload was not too intense which was nice. You get to do a lot of cool experiments, like make bread, brew mead, make yogurt, and more! Class is very hands on and the small size really means that you'll get to know your classmates well. Come with a cool idea, and you will have a great time with the final project as well. I would also recommend taking this class if you are writing a thesis! The balance of work pairs nicely; when you are stressed about your thesis, this class is a bit lighter, and when you are done with your thesis the work ramps up a bit more. I would really recommend taking this class you get to learn so much about the foods and drinks you interact with on a daily basis and it was really eye–opening.
*** This is a great class and I would highly recommend it to anyone. After this course you will be able to ferment a lot of different kinds of food, and the class overall is a great bonding experience especially if you're a graduating senior.
TAKE IT TAKE IT TAKE IT. If you get into this class, you are very lucky. An really great experience that added so much to my semester.
As a social science concentrator (and a senior), this was easily one of the best (if not the best) courses I have taken at Harvard! After slogging through many writing–intensive courses for my concentration requirements, this class reminded me why I love science.

The teaching staff does an excellent job of actively engaging students in the labwork while also teaching you about fermentation. The course is truly a diamond in every sense of the word –– not overly difficult, very engaging, AND you learn something!
Super fun class! Lots of hands–on exploration and Pia is a wonderful, enthusiastic professor!
This is an amazing class! Try and take it before you graduate. You learn so much, and Pia is such a kind and enthusiastic teacher.
TAKE THIS CLASS. Probably the best class I've taken at Harvard. Because it is so small, you grow really close with the teaching staff and they are AMAZING. It was really fun to learn about fermentation and explore a project of my own interest in this field. The teaching staff is super nice and receptive to all feedback. 11/10 recommend.
my god, this was such a wonderful course. Prof. Pia's enthusiasm for the material is infectious, and she and head TF Alex were so great to work with and knowledgeable about the material. The small class size means that it was really easy to get familiar with everyone, and Pia really encouraged open discussions and exploration. Class is 2x a week, no additional lab or discussion sections. The workload was practically nonexistent outside of class other than a few short readings, a short midterm project, and the final project (which is when you might really have to invest time into the class / come to the lab during non–class hours, based on what question you want to study). I ended up doing a solo project while most people worked in groups, which Pia and Alex were super supportive of, and helped me with planning the components and making sure that they were feasible to complete within the allotted timeframe. They absolutely encouraged students to dig into what interested them. The classes were also filled with lots of delicious fermentation experiments, many of which are very reproducible at home! but maybe not in a student dorm lol. The field trips to breweries, labs, etc. were also super interesting! I was also thesising during the semester, and this was an absolutely great class to balance with that workload and other more time–consuming stem classes. Overall, absolute gem for anybody and everybody, and I can't recommend this course enough, even just to hang out with/talk to Pia and Alex on a regular basis!
This course is truly so much fun. If you're interested in learning more about fermentation and are happy to get your hands dirty with in–class experiments, this is the class to take!
