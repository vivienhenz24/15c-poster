Comments
This will stand as one of my favorite courses at Harvard. The materials are entertaining, so much so that I never minded doing my homework for the course. Most of all, Professor Biel does an amazing job of keeping up the discussion in class when students hit a lull in the conversation. He is open to all sorts of discussions on the material and his comments on our write–ups were useful and kind! Highly, highly recommend taking this course.
Best class I've taken in my life, without a doubt. Please take it regardless of concentration, you will not regret it. I really hope it is offered in the future. The films and readings are fascinating, Professor Biel is the best ever and cares about his students, and this course will make a permanent impact on you, even if you're not a hist–lit concentrator or humanities student.
If you're looking to explore Hist/Lit, fulfilling a requirement, need a light class, definitely take this class. There are ten films, and homework is a small essay each week. You choose six of the ten to do, so you could go four weeks in a row literally just watching a movie each week and nothing else. It's really not bad. Professor Biel is definitely not a super easy grader though, so don't expect an easy A. He's a really sweet guy, but no matter how interested he may be in the film, he can be veryyyy quiet and monotone. The class is very much what you make of it. You can stare at your computer and not participate each week, or you can give your energy and thoughts and help to stimulate some discussion. If you feel like you can bring a fun energy to the class, take it. Definitely expect a really cool opportunity to discuss films with your peers and with an interested professor.
TAKE THIS CLASS
Must–take. Hands down one of the best classes I've taken here. The workload is manageable and gives you the flexibility to make some weeks lighter than others. You will be introduced to excellent new films, and course discussions are enriching and welcoming. Very little background of film analysis is needed for this course – as long as you have an open mind and a desire to watch several great films and engage with them. You will learn a bunch about the films and the historical context in which they were made, and you will be introduced to some of the all–time greatest ones. Not a gem, but assignments are a very fair length. 5/5 class.
Take this class if interested in movies & recent–ish US history. Professor Biel is a great facilitator and there are a variety of movies on the syllabus which makes class fun. Be sure that you have time though since there are 6 short assignments with a quick turnaround.
TAKE THIS CLASS! good movies, manageable workload (you only have 6 short essays and a final paper), great professor—what else can I say?
Wonderful course if you have a passion for film. Biel is a film nerd in the most wonderful way and it really helps to make for an exciting and interesting learning environment. The films do range from snoozers (sorry Wanda) to really fascinating (The Conversation), but I truly love the range of genres and scales we touch on in this class. Discussions fly by very quickly, so don't be scared of the two hour time slot.
