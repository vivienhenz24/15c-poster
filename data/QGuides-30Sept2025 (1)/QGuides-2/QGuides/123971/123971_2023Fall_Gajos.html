I have mixed feelings on this class. I feel like I learned a lot about the design process, including many considerations to make when designing a product that I would not have previously considered. Most of the lectures are quite engaging (the guest lectures not as much), and you gain the opportunity to practice different design techniques in class. I also really liked my section. Priyanka was a thoughtful and caring TF.

On the downside, the constant project work can feel like a drag. It is a lot of busywork at times, and because there is a deadline every week (except for Thanksgiving break) it can feel nonstop. Additionally, two out of the three projects you are assigned to a team, and who you are on a team with can make or break the experience. For the third project, seek out other highly motivated students from your section. It is better to work on an idea that is not your top choice with people who actually care, as opposed to working on an idea that is more aligned with your interests but then have to deal with unresponsive teammates. I would advise not taking this class with other heavy CS classes. I took it with CS61 and CS120, and while the work in this class did not take as long most weeks (except for the final project), it felt much more draining.

Overall, I think you can get a lot out of this course, but you need to make the time for it and be intentional about the work you are completing and who you are working with.

This class teaches structure/theory while allowing you to apply what you learn to projects.

This course is excellent for an engaging introduction to the design process. Projects are relevant and interesting, and bolster fundamental design considerations, processes, and Figma skills. However, this course does at times feel like a lot of busywork (each project report easily went over 30–40 pages), and it seems like the course rewards being able to spew word vomit, including from the TFs. This meant that sometimes we would get a lot of feedback (in word count) on certain milestones, but after reading it back, we'd realize that we either didn't understand it (it was just a bunch of big words thrown together), it was inaccurate to what we'd done, or it was not applicable to the scope of our project. Despite this, I think that the grading generally reflected effort, and was fairly forgiving. Definitely one of the best CS courses I've taken (though the bar is low & this class is just so different). Don't expect to take this as a super low–effort class, as I think each week was a fair amount of work (writing, user testing, group work).
