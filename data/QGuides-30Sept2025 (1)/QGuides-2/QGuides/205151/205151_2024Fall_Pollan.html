Having spent most of my life in journalism, I was uncertain about how I would fare in a personal essay writing course. All of those hesitations melted away during our first courses. I couldn't ask for a better teacher than Michael in creating an welcoming environment where everyone felt comfortable opening up about their most vulnerable experiences. Essay by essay, I could feel myself taking in the constructive and honest feedback from my classmates and Michael, and it was so heartwarming to see my writing improve over the course. In addition to the essays themselves, I really learned the different contortions that a personal essay can take to serve different purposes from the guest lectures and readings. I will never forget our guest lecture with Terry Tempest Williams –– it was probably the most emotional I've ever left a class at Harvard. The readings and lectures gave me creative freedom in experimenting with different forms of the personal essay that I would never have thought of otherwise.

I would 10000% recommend this course to anyone regardless of personal essay writing experience. You will leave it with a better understanding of yourself and how your life experiences fit in with your worldview –– with the best professor and classmates to help you along the way. :)
Best course I've taken at Harvard. Take it. No questions, no fuss.
This was an amazing course. Professor Pollan is a master. The texts he chooses are brilliant and interestingthey will definitely be the highlight of your homework each week. Our class was around fifteen people, and the best thing about it was how much everyone buys into the course & invests in their own learning and that of their peersyou will get a lot of feedback and it will definitely push you. Professor Pollan is not someone to mince words. HIs feedback is clear and precise and it will get to the crux of the flaws in your writing. The work I produced in this class is some of the work I'm most proud of throughout all of my time at Harvard. Creative nonfiction will not only push your writing skills but also push you to interrogate your values and what is meaningful to you.
If you care about writing, this is the best course you can take at Harvard. Michael is an amazing and generous editor (puts in an enormous amount of time and effort towards giving us edits and comments). His feedback was always on point and helped improve my writing every single time. The readings are chosen carefully and will impart key lessons beneficial to every aspiring writer. You will learn how to establish and sustain a writing practice, how to edit and take on board editing, how to develop a voice on the page, etc. It was incredibly valuable and while it does demand a fair bit of work, it is every bit worth the effort. Don't think twice, apply for this class!
