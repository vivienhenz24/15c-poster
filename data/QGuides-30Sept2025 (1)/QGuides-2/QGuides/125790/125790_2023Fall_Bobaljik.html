I highly recommend this class! The content is really interesting and Bobaljik is a fantastic lecturer – super engaging, always willing to answer questions, and makes pretty complicated concepts make sense fairly quickly. I don't know any languages other than English and a little bit of Spanish, and I am always concerned that that will be a problem in linguistics classes, but you definitely don't need to be multilingual for this course. However, unless you have some previous experience with linguistics, you should definitely take one of the introductory linguistics classes first. If you're doing a linguistics concentration or secondary, or even just as an elective, this course is really great. It's definitely not easy; the PSets are challenging. But if you go to Office Hours and work together with other students on the PSets, you'll be fine. It's a reasonable step up in difficulty from Ling 101. Take this class!
This is an interesting class with an instructor who is very knowledgeable and passionate about the course content. Problem set grading can be pretty nitpicky, and the course units can feel a little disconnected from each other at times, but overall I thought it was a good learning experience.
Take this course! Professor Bobaljik is really passionate about this topic, and it's well–paced and well–structured.
This class is very interesting but it is graded extremely harshly which makes it difficult to focus on learning the material since accuracy is so important. Little points are taken off for things that aren't asked about in the questions, the lectures are hard to follow, and basically everything you need to get a very high grade on to get a good grade in the course. There's a very small margin of error and the cutoff for an A is pretty high, and participation points are surprisingly difficult to get. Definitely only take if you have a solid linguistic background, and even then, make sure you're willing to work really hard the entire time and score well on every single assignment.
Do not take this class.
This course is very difficult. However the reward for gaining a true understanding of content is huge. If you have an interest in morphology and are willing to work hard (go to office hours, reach out to peers for support) you should take it.
One of the best courses in the ling department. Professor Bobaljik is an amazing lecturer and has so much enthusiasm for the subject. The problem sets and the take–home final are challenging but fair.
Take this class! Even if you aren't a linguistics major, this is my favourite linguistics course I've taken so far, so it'd be a great introduction not only to morphology but the department as a whole.
Very manageable class with mostly interesting content and plenty of space to shape lectures and discussion to topics you might be interested in. Grading can be nitpicky sometimes due to important notation that can be easy to miss on occasion, but otherwise lectures/section set up nicely for problem sets to seem mostly straightforward.
Bobaljik was a great professor, and you can really see his passion while teaching. Morphology was a fun course, but there are some difficult topics. For ling concentrators, you this is not a required class, but I would still recommend as it gives you a solid foundation in tree diagrams. PSETs are worth a significant part of your grade, so I would recommend going to office hours or working with others.
This class is really fun if you like linguistics at all. Professor Bobaljik is really engaging and obviously cares about the material. It is really fun to participate in class as well, and I say that as someone who usually gets very anxious and uncomfortable participating. If you are nervous about the required participation, Professor Bobaljik makes it less scary. He gives positive feedback on your answer even if it's not quite right, and encourages participation for things like examples of English words that have some characteristic, which may feel easier to answer than a question about linguistic theory.

I didn't really make friends in this class, but I recommend you do for checking psets over. Even though the psets are easy, some small things are sometimes tricky, like confusing instructions or conflicting approaches.

I really loved this class though. It was the highlight of my semester. Lecture was exciting and the readings were interesting. There was never any busywork either– all assignments had a clear purpose and all readings had important information in every paragraph. The process project is extremely fun, so have a blast with it.

I didn't give this class all the effort I wish I could have, because of other hard classes and a personally difficult semester, but I still got a lot out of it. In some ways, having this class during such a hard semester in my personal life was amazing because it felt entirely like fun, even though it was something technically productive to work on. It's also a really nice reminder about how cool humans are in general. Language is neat because people are neat. I think if you like linguistics as much as I do, which I am guessing you do if you are a concentrator or taking a secondary in it, this class is the bee's knees.

Professor Bobaljik is a really good professor. I recommend taking advantage of office hours to get the most out of the class. If you are confused about topics or particular examples, he will spend a lot of time explaining it and even follow up with more information.

Honestly, this class nearly convinced me to become a linguistics concentrator. 10/10 experience.
