What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

This is truly an amazing course that I would recommend to any STEM student or student interested in learning about how different pathogens may infect our brains and cause neurological diseases. It sets a good foundation for thinking about neuroscience in the context of clinical settings and public health measures and efforts. There is a lot of information that gets taught during lectures so my recommendation is to keep a working document of notes throughout so that you dont have to review lectures in entirety after (which eats up a lot of time during exams season). Overall, the exams are pretty challenging, but if you dedicate the time towards preparing early you should be fine. Laura and Helena have crafted this course so beautifully and manage it so well. It is clearly well structured, and because of that, you are able to learn so much about the topics at hand. I never thought I would be interested in neuroscience research, but after taking this class, I am seriously considering it as a potential field to go into. It takes a lot of work, but you get so much out of the class.
This course is fantastic and super fun. The exams are fair, the weekly assignments take more work that I'd originally expected but were interesting and useful. The lectures are super engaging and the small course size really helped us all get to know each other. I loved this class!
This was a great course! Highly recommend if you want to learn more about how organisms can invade the nervous system. Assignments are doable, instructions are clear, constant workload – do study hard for the midterms. Helena was a great TF and Laura was an awesome teacher!
Take this class! Laura is a fantastic lecturer. Her lecturers are always a joy to sit through. The topics the course covers are super interesting and cover a wide range making them relevant if you are doing any neuroscience research. The class has improved my scientific literacy through the weekly journal article assignments. The workload is manageable and light compared to other neuro classes with psets. However, make sure to study in advance for exams because the course covers a lot of material in a short amount of time and the exams can get pretty specific. Helena is also great and super knowledgeable. If you have the space and are looking for a way to fulfill the advanced neuro requirement, this is a great class to take.
This is a great course, and you will learn a lot about neuroimmunology and different infectious diseases. Professor Magnotti is great and includes many examples in her lectures that are engaging and helped me absorb all of the content for the two admittedly very detail–oriented exams. In terms of upper level neuro electives, this is a great option, especially because of the small class size and interesting content.
If you like immunology, you will like this class. We learn about interesting diseases in each lecture and the exam is similar to this format: a 3–year–old boy went swimming with his family in (blank location), and a few days later he started getting headaches and then seizures, (Some more context). What disease does he have? What is the pathogen that causes it? Then some questions about pathogenesis. Very pre–med core.
I would highly recommend this class! It's not a "gem" –– you definitely have to do work, but in my opinion it'll be worth your time. You learn from every assignment and every lecture and this class develops your skills as a scientific communicator in addition to teaching you about lots of very interesting diseases. You do need to study regularly to keep up with all the course material (there's a lot!), you need to read the weekly paper and submit your write–up and you need to do a ~1hr presentation with a partner. You should also be coming to every lecture or else you'll definitely be confused. This is the most interesting class I've taken at Harvard though and I think for any pre–med student, this will give you valuable information about certain diseases that will make medical school a lot easier. If you work hard and do your best you'll be fine.
