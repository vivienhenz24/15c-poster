This class is super chill and covers interesting material! Because it was so chill, I feel like I didn't get that much out of it, but I think it could be a good learning experience if you invested more time / tried a bit harder than me lol. I still don't know what my grade for the final project is, but everything else was graded fairly/leniently. The implementation exercises were informative and at just the right difficulty level (maybe slightly too easy). Melissa is an alright lecturernot the worst and not the best.
I took this class as undergrad concentrating in CS. Would highly, highly recommend to anyone interested in machine learning. It is so much better than many ML courses at Harvard in that it actually helps you truly understand what DL is, where it came from and where it's going, and what made all the big advancements so big. Having done this course I feel much more confident in my understanding of ML.
A lot of the course is self–studying. During lectures, the professor will talk through the basic concepts, models, and applications thoroughly but this is most likely not enough for understanding the breadth and depth of deep learning. Instead you learn from reading online materials yourself, doing the implementation exercises, and doing the final project. So this course feels more like a surface introduction, plus an opportunity to apply deep learning firsthand and access resources/connection from top researchers in the field (the professor and her teaching assistants)
A good survey course of a lot of new AI methods for non–standard data formats. While the lectures weren't always the most engaging (I think you could learn a lot of the same content on your own time), it was still good to hear the content in class. Prof. Dell is also very knowledgeable about many of the newest evolutions, so lectures are a great chance to ask questions.
Great class if you're interested in Deep Learning. You can tell Melissa is on or at least very close to the cutting edge of what's going on and probably knows more about the subject than some old profs. in the CS department (I am a CS concentrator). The lecture materials are fanatic summaries for various topics in Deep Learning. The workload is fairly light and lets you focus on a project of your choice which is nice.
I strongly recommend this class, because it is of high academic quality, it is thorough and up–to–date, and it has helped me learn
Absolutely fantastic and informative homework with an opportunity to apply your skills to solve a real world problem in the final project, fantastic for a resume.
This class is a great way to learn about the more practical applications of ML concepts. Especially if you're coming from a more CS–heavy/theory background, you'll probably enjoy that this class has a lot of actual coding in it. The end of semester project is a great opportunity to work on a pipeline from beginning to end while also learning about an interesting dataset.
For now the most value added in the course are the implementation exercises and the final project. If only interested in getting a quick brush of how Deep Learning can be used in social science the recordings are a very good substitute.
Excellent course that introduces you to deep learning methods and facilitates understanding of how to apply them to unstructured data in social science research.
Enjoyable class; reasonable workload and very relevant/modern topics!
This is an excellent introduction to deep learning for social science applications; Prof. Dell does a really good job of covering large swathes of content. You don't need much background to take this course and it can be very helpful for research.
This is a really great class, Melissa is extremely knowledgeable about deep learning and you'll probably take away more from learning from her than any other class offered in the CS/Stats department. It's a very applied class and super helpful for anyone interested in doing research!
If you can overcome the lower teaching quality, the content itself is incredibly interesting.
