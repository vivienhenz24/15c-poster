This is the most relevant and applicable class I've taken in graduate school. Electron microscopy can be quite daunting and intimidating, but the instructors really guide you through how to use the microscopes and how to obtain the best images possible. They're patient and very knowledgable.
Lab sections are very useful especially if you know your research relies heavily on imaging
Get the textbook. Read the textbook.
If you plan to use TEM or SEM in your research, definitely take this class. Even if you don't plan to use TEM/SEM, it's a cool experience to see matter at Angstrom–level resolution, which is basically impossible by any other means.
This class is valuable for learning about electron microscopy. The lectures are important to review for the midterm but the majority of learning occurs during lab sessions. Labs are very informative and cover a wide range of techniques. However, there is generally little guidance about how to write up the final project.
This course is amazing, definitely take it! It will greatly help improve both your understanding of advanced electron microscopy, and your skills for using these tools for research. I can't think of any other class that will help you both knowledge wise as well as practically for your research.
