I took this class under Paul Bamberg, so the following only applies if he is an instructor. Interesting course. The material itself is not entirely inaccessible (I had not previously taken a high level proof–based math class and was fine, 22a only), but outside the proofs, Paul's "Workshop" problems are unintuitive and confusing. The textbook (Luenberger) has no solution guide, which made self–studying class material impossible (one has to adopt Paul's methodology and really live inside his solution strategies). If you are willing to memorize a couple of proofs, learn to pattern match a few odd problem–solving techniques, this class is manageable. Will say––though it only met on Fridays my year, the required outside lecture recordings (about 5 hours a week before class) proved challenging to get through, even on double speed. The recording is not all that great and Paul Bamberg is...Paul Bamberg. Super silly and well–meaning. Definitely shop if you are considering––first two psets are not bad.

Don't take this class if Paul is teaching it. If you want to learn functional analysis, you can read Luenberger on your own or wait until after next year (when he's no longer teaching the course). This class is flipped–classroom, and so you end up having a homework, two assigned lectures, plus a three hour class every week. This predictably leads to everyone skipping the recorded lectures. The problems in the class tend to be word problems, making it very different from other math courses, and it can be very hard to think about any applications of the class material. Homework problems can be substituted by asking questions to Bing AI weirdly enough. Just a strange class all around.

Paul himself seems like a nice guy, but unfortunately I can't recommend taking this specific course with him teaching it. Do look at other classes he teaches though!
