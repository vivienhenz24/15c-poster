What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
The best course I've ever taken in terms of contemporary philosophy, and very likely my favorite class I've ever taken at Harvard in general. The only time I can remember being explicitly upset that a class met only once a week. Professor Kopec facilitated extremely fun and enlightening discussion, while ensuring every course reading and topic was tailored to our interests. I'm sad it's over.
Dr. Kopec is a great professor/discussion leader. He gives good feedback and is very helpful with guiding you on your final paper. He is also very understanding with respect to occasional absences or slightly late submissions. The discussions we had during class were generally really intellectually productive, and I personally learned a lot from the readings and from the way Kopec led the discussions. The main component of the course is the final paper (it is worth 50% of your grade) but you have a lot of time upfront to begin working on it.
However, as a side note: Based on the title of the tutorial, "Normative Philosophy of Computing", I was expecting the content to be much more theoretical and abstract, and possibly quantitatively involved–––if you are expecting the same, prepare to be disappointed. The material covered in the readings focuses on specific technological developments like VR and social media with a highly applied/concrete lens. It was still interesting, but not exactly what I originally was expecting.
If you like computing and philosophy, you should take this class. Although I think it'll be a gened in the future.
