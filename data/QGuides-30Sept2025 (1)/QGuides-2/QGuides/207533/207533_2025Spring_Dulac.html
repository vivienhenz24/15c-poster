MCB 125 is a class that made a comeback for the Spring 2025 term following it being last offered two years ago in Spring 2023. The structure of the class is very forgiving, with Problem Sets accounting for 75% of your overall grade while Participation counts for the other 25%. As a result, this means that there are no other assignments, exams, or projects within the course.

The benefit of this structure is that MCB 125 is relatively easy to balance alongside your other classes, even if taken as a fifth course. There are only 6 Problem Sets throughout the semester, with them being spread out every other week. To be completely honest, most of us dont even start the Problem Sets until the night before its due, meaning that we go through ~12 hours of misery in that moment before returning to a non–chalant, calm schedule again. However, if you would like help, the Office Hours for this class are the biggest highlight, with many of the TFs being extremely sweet and very accessible for questions.

However, I think the biggest downside of this course relates back to the lecture component, which was often very unengaging. Although Professor Dulac is without a doubt extremely qualified to lecture on the content, her lectures are sometimes a little boring, which sometimes leads to students becoming very bored in class – occasionally even falling asleep. Combined with the fact that attendance is not accounted for within the course, this has resulted in student attendance dwindling as the semester progressed, with only 1/3 to 1/2 of the students attending lecture on a regular basis.

The one warning I will provide future enrollees is that the grade cut–offs are not predetermined or announced to the class ahead of time. As of right now, the averages on PSETs have included the following: 96% (PSET 1), 95% (PSET 2), 96% (PSET 3), 97% (PSET 4), and unknown for PSET 5/6 since it is due during reading period. This means that all students have been doing very well in the course, which has made many of us anxious on how final letter grades will be determined. At this point in time, it is unclear if there will be an unreasonably high cutoff, which is still TBD. As a result, I would highly advise any prospective enrollees to check with former students in the coming semesters on a more accurate perspective on how finalized grading is determined.

As a whole, given how MCB 125 comes with exams or projects outside of the six PSETs spread out across the semester, this makes it a relatively rewarding and relaxing class. Despite the lack of engagement, I think that MCB 125 remains a strong pick for anyone looking for a great class to balance alongside a busy semester.
