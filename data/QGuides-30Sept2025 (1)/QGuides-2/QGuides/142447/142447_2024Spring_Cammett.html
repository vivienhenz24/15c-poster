This packs SO much interesting content into a single semester. I went in knowing close to nothing about the Middle East, and I came out with a much better understanding of the region and feel more equipped to engage with contemporary issues as well. Content–wise, you will not regret taking this course, especially if you engage with it (go to lectures and do the readings – seriously). Readings can be a little long and dense though, but I found that a good strategy was to go to lecture first and then do the readings before sectionthat way, I already knew some stuff in the readings and could skim over it more quickly. Assignments are not super straightforward, because they try to frame it with scenarios, like "you're an analyst in this region and yada yada", but if you ignore the fluff and just write them like a normal analytical/argumentative essay, then you'll be fine.
Decent course that will definitely teach you some new things about the region and/or help you think about it in a new way. Going in, I felt I knew more about the Middle East than the average Harvard student, but I still came away from it with some useful knowledge. Lectures are chill and informative –– you probably don't even have to take notes, but they are still worth attending, as Prof. Cammett has a lot of good insights. She is super nice and very approachable, and you can learn a lot from her if you make the effort to connect/go to office hours.

As other people have said, this course is a fair bit harder than the average GENED and is closer in difficulty to a moderately easy Gov class. Make sure you at least look at the readings, because this will help you on the assignments. As for the essays themselves –– the grading is very inflexible and is basically dependent on how well you followed the prompt. This can be annoying, as you may lose points for something minor even though your argument was good.

GENED sections are GENED sections. No one except me did the readings, so the classes felt a bit awkward. However, they were good to clarify some of the content.

The final assignment is VERY dull and quite a bit of work. ~15 page annotated bibliography. Did not enjoy.

Overall, the course has some issues but I still think it's worth taking if you are interested in the region.

The assignments are comprehendible and relatively easy, and Professor Cammett is wonderful!
