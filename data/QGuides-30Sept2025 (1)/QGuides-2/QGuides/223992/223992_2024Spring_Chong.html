Fantastic class – covers interesting content while being super chill. Stephen Chong just lecturing about interesting things in various fields he's studied is pretty great, and the problem sets are all really light. This was the first time it was taught, so it was naturally a little bit underdeveloped, but it was still a lot of fun and I enjoyed it a lot. Take it!
This is a great class, especially for this year having been its first. Some of the homeworks were difficult to install, but the staff is very understanding. The final project is very open–ended, so it's important to have some direction or ask the course staff for ideas and feedback. Highly recommend to anyone, even those who are not as good at the theoretical/proofy parts of CS.
This class is super awesome!!!! You get to learn a lot and also dive into a project of your choice at the end! It's fairly chill but you still get to learn a ton!
If you enjoyed CS152, take it! If you haven't taken CS152, don't.
This semester was the first time that this course was offered, and Chong did a great job with it lectures were consistently engaging, and even though some problem sets had some small hiccups, they were interesting and built on what we learned in class. I would highly recommend taking this course if you're looking for something interesting to fulfill your formal reasoning requirement!
This is a really fun class, and Prof. Chong is a great professor. The topics are a bit niche but very interesting!
As a senior interviewing for new grad job positions with a focus on security, I wish I was able to take this course sooner. This course was a fairly quick paced survey of many security topics which aren't covered in any other Harvard courses.
This was the first time the course is being taught. Steve is an excellent lecturer and great professor who is very in tune with the needs of his students.

Two important things to note: (1) if you are not interested in formal methods or computer security don't take this class. If you are not interested in security don't take this class. For the systems / security bros, this is not 263, this is formal methods. (2) this is a grad–level course, everything is pointing you toward research. I see no reason why undergrads would take this if they are not interested in research.
Take the class! Steve is a great professor, and the class will only get better as the course goes through more iterations. This is Steve's research area, so he has a lot of personal background and interest in the topic as well.
This class is really great if you are interested in the material. Even if you have every little background, the course is set up to allow you to explore the topic area very effectively. As someone with no prior background, I would definitely recommend this course.
