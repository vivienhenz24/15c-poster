What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Do not take this class unless you are very interested in plant developmental biology. It's a very deep dive into the field and you are expected to memorize a lot of terminology and intricate details about every process. The midterms are not forgiving and the final paper is quite difficult. The professor has a hard time relating to the students in terms of the difficulty of the material. It's also hard to know what is going to be on the exam and what's not.
Very cool, chill class, and you learn a lot about plant development. Natural progression from OEB 52 (biology of plants) and more sophisticated as well. Workload is minimal since the only things this course grades on are participation, two exams, and a final paper. Not necessarily an easy course, however; the exams were challenging and the grading for this class is a bit ambiguous. Nonetheless enjoyable.
Start chipping away at your paper early, as well as the exams. Also, ask plenty of questions and be thorough with your studying
I would highly recommend this course, especially if you really enjoyed OEB 52! Dr. Kramer is an amazing professor and cares so deeply about each of her students. The assignments include two exams (one on the first half of material, another on the second half) and a final paper. While the course covers a lot of content and different genetic pathways, the exams are fair and reflective of the core concepts. Definitely take this course if you are interested in plant biology and want a more in–depth understanding of plant development!
