Professor Chang is simply a phenomenal teacher. I found myself looking forward to his lectures every single week –– he is extremely knowledgeable, charismatic, and genuinely cares about our success. He also encouraged us to raise (and try to seek answers) to raise important questions about our ways of being and the society, which I think is instrumental to meaningful scholarship and for humanity, in general.
This was an amazing class, easily the best I've taken at Harvard so far. The content is so interesting (readings included), and Prof. Chang is very enthusiastic and knowledgeable. As a Korean–American, this class did so much to help me learn more about Korean history and current events.
This was a great course I learned a lot and thought Dr. Chang was amazing. I would caveat that the assignments (3 page single spaced response papers) were a bit of a slog at times compared to a typical response paper, but this class ultimately wasn't too bad.
I highly encourage anyone remotely interested in modern Korean history to take this course!
best class i've ever taken so far!!! professor chang is the most effective (and funniest) lecturer, and i learned so many meaningful things about korea's political, economic, and social landscape.
prof chang is leaving, but if other iterations of this course happen with any other professor, take it! sociology/culture and particularly that of modern korea is really informed by korean history so the beginning part of the course is a history class essentially and the second half is sociology and understanding the trends of certain social patterns in korea (which is all extremely fascinating!!)
This is one of the best courses I've taken in my time here at Harvard. As someone with no knowledge of Korea, I emerged from this class knowing more about the peninsula and the 4000 years of history and culture than most people have. Professor Chang is an incredible instructor who truly wants his students to learn and become more knowledgeable about Korea –– a place that he is so passionate about. I am grateful that I had the chance to take this course during Professor Chang's last time teaching it, because I am not sure that anyone else could have done as good of a job conveying personal and historical insights into such a unique place.
The best class I've taken at harvard.
