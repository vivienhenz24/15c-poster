What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Professor Ghosh's wealth of knowledge often creates very interesting realisations both about the content of the class and about general methodology or reflections about the study of history itself. This is a class that benefits you as much as you put into it –– and the readings are engaging enough to warrant that effort. If you're not willing to participate in discussions, or struggle with a reading–intensive class, I would say take a lecture class from Professor Ghosh instead rather than choose a seminar course.
If you have an interest in Chinese history, history, and economic history, this is a great class to take! You don't need to know any Chinese (reading and speaking Chinese is not required). The class is structured like a traditional history seminar. Each session is a discussion where groups of students present on readings for that week. The readings can be hard to digest especially since a lot of the theories and ideas are very difficult to understand as an undergraduate, but Professor Ghosh is great at breaking down concepts. Don't get intimidated by the graduate students in the class. They offer so much knowledge, and honestly, you will learn so much!! Although the class was challenging, it was challenging in a good way. You will truly learn how different facades and aspects within Chinese history (and cultural theory) and economics helped China develop into an economic superpower (or attempt to explain how China developed so fast). The readings you will read are very diverse in language (you'll get a few math/quantitative readings), time period, and authors (historians, sociologists, economists). Truly recommend this course!
This course is fairly challenging. I enjoyed it because it really deepened my understanding of the Chinese economy, but I felt out of my depth at times. I feel like I could have gotten more out of the course if I had more bandwidth for it.
Take the class! I think it's a really fun experience, and Professor Ghosh is a very kind and knowledgeable instructor. If you're at all interested in learning more about how the Chinese economy has developed into the modern day, and how different political/government influences impacted these shifts, take the class. The readings are a bit hefty at times, so it can be a bit rough for STEM people (like me), especially if you want to be able to contribute during class. Nevertheless, the class is really enriching, and a great experience. I RECOMMEND!
If you are concentrating in history and want to learn about twentieth century Chinese economics (a dense topic) than look no further. If you do take the course, I recommend reading up on Chinese history after the Qing dynasty.
This was a really great course. Professor Ghosh is phenomenally smart and knowledgeable and is very good at leading discussions. The assignments were not too overbearing, but there was a lot of reading each week which was challenging to complete. Regardless, I learned a lot in this course, and it was certainly the most deep history course I have taken so far at Harvard. I would recommend it to anybody interested in China or economic growth.
