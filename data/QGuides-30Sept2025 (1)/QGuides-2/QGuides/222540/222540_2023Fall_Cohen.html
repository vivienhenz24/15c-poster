It is a steep class in terms of time commitment, initial understanding and truly grasping taught concepts. Very much a class for those very adept in the physical sciences. Course becomes easier as semester finishes. Combination of three midterms, psets, labs and final project requires lots of time.
An incredible class that provides a great intro to quantum and stat mech in the context of chemistry, lots of supportive staff and fun but challenging material!
Chem 10 is a good class that gives you a relatively comprehensive background in quantum mech and some other foundational chemistry topics (MO theory); you can mostly afford to leave problem sets to the last minute, although the time that you save on psets is usually added back in section and lab. Adam is a great lecturer; the course certainly veers more on the physics side of things than chemistry, but background above the AP level in either is unnecessary.
I absolutely recommend taking this class! It is a wonderful course with wonderful teachers. The course staff are very supportive and Adam is an amazing lecturer. The content is very interesting and helps you gain a deeper understanding of chemistry. The assignments are not super time consuming and help you better understand the lecture material. Although it may be tough at first, you will eventually get it so don't give up on the course!
TAKE THIS CLASS! You will learn so much about how the world works and will be given a solid foundation for understanding phenomena in all branches of the sciences, not just chemistry. Adam is a fantastic and engaging lecturer and the attention you get from section leaders and TFs in this class is truly invaluable. Problem sets can be tricky but never feel like theyre impossible, especially not with the insane amount of help available to you. This course gives you the same rigor as a lot of hard intro courses, but with a much kinder class culture. Definitely have comfort with calculus and maybe basic linear algebra for this classmath background was arguably more important than chem background for this course.
If you've taken AP Chem––TAKE THIS COURSE!!!! This class takes everything that you took for granted in your high school chemistry course and proves it ALL. Answering questions like "how do conductors conduct electricity," "why do molecules have color," or "why does Gibbs free energy have to be less than zero,for a reaction to be spontaneous," this course was extremely rewarding. Professor Adam is an excellent lecturer, and if you ever have any questions he will make sure to make it crystal clear. All the teaching staff for this course were wonderful, and this truly was one of (if not the best) course I've taken.

Even if you're not going into chemistry, though you still have a good chemistry background, you should still take this course since it explains so much about the natural world and gives insights on current research related to the course material.
THIS WAS AN AMAZING CHEMISTRY CLASS. TAKE THIS COURSE IF YOU CAN. Adam Cohen is an AMAZING lecturer. The content is incredibly informative and rigorous. If you're curious about the mathematical models that all of chemistry derives from, or you feel like you're just not convinced about how chemistry works yet, you should take this class. It's intimidating at the start, but it's very, very rewarding by the end. I had very little chemistry background, below the level of AP chem and AP physics, before enrolling; now I feel like I can hold my own in a discussion with chem concentrators. After taking it, I feel prepared to take orgo (Chem 20) as well as both quantum mechanics (Phys 143/Chem 160) and statistical mechanics (Phys 181/Chem 161/MCB 199).

If I had a few words to describe this class, it would be "difficult, but you're prepared." It was definitely out of my comfort zone, but it was never unmanageable or overwhelming. It is going to be intimidating at the start. The first section and first pset will be a challenge. Don't be scared – it gets much more manageable the longer you spend. Do go to office hours and don't be afraid of asking questions in lecture. It is absolutely encouraged, even expected, to ask questions – it's not seen as disruptive at all, and there are no stupid questions.

The course staff are amazing and very supportive (Lu, the chem department preceptor, is really nice!) and since the student population ended up being pretty small this year, we formed an actual community by the end. The teaching staff really got to know us and always took the time to answer our questions; they devoted so much extra time to this course, including one who designed and 3D printed a spectrometer for us to assemble(!!) You should definitely go to office hours even if you don't have a specific question – Adam is an awesome professor, hearing other people's questions can also be very informative, and you can always chat about research or about life in general.

Reasons you shouldn't take this class: you haven't taken calculus yet, you hate math, you have a really heavy workload outside of this class, or you don't want to put a lot of effort into physical chemistry for some reason or another.

Reasons you should take this class: you're curious and you want an amazing physical chem experience!
TAKE THIS CLASS!! This is probably the best and most rigorous introduction to chemistry you will be able to find. Though you will not talk about atoms until a few weeks in the semester, you will leave this class with a solid understanding of most phenomena that are otherwise just explained in a hand–wavy manner. You will have solid understanding of quantum mechanics and thermodynamics.

This class is certainly more work than other alternatives, but it is absolutely worth it. Due to the small course size, there is always course staff there to help you and the course staff is really nice and helpful. Especially Adam is an awesome lecturer and able to explain complicated topics in an easily understandable manner. Finally you will get to work on your own final project, in which you can learn more about a certain topic of your choice.

If you do not have a solid background in calculus or have never coded before, be prepared though that this course will require some additional work of you.
This course is a great alternative to general chemistry, for those who intend to major in anything chemistry related. If youre looking for a rigorous approach to quantum, I would recommend that you take Phys 143a instead, especially if youve already taken linear algebra (physics background is less relevant). However, this class is perfect if youd just like to understand the applications of quantum or stat mech to chemistry, which is heavily emphasized in lecture, but youre not expected to memorize all of them for the exam. Having seen the concepts before in general chemistry and having something to connect them to will make this class easier, but everything is taught from scratch, and the ample TF office hours and percent of the grade that they can help you get a 100% in makes this course extremely approachable to succeed in. In particular, I loved the final project, which allowed me to gain exposure to directing my own research in a controlled environment. The only downside is lab, which does e 3 hours each week, and can sometimes be frustrating in terms of getting usable results (the DIY spectrometer) or uneducational (like some of the labs from a kit), but I learned a lot from the MATLAB labs, and feel ready to do scientific computing in a real research context after taking the course, without having seen the language or coded much before.
This class is a wonderful introduction to quantum chemistry! Take the class if you want to learn about Schrodinger's equation and molecular orbital theory. Lu and Adam are fantastic, as is the rest of the teaching team.
