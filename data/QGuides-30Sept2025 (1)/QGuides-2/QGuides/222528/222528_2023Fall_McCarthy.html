There was a discussion at the beginning of this course on the ethics of autofiction. Of course, we were talking about our writing, but we were really doing much more than that, addressing a profound ethical issue with which many of us were about to be confronted. I came away from our discussion with a better understanding of myself, my peers, and the literary landscape we inhabit together. Professor McCarthy handled the discussion brilliantly, making it clear that we were free to submit whatever we wanted to him while still hearing our arguments for and against writing about the self in a collegiate setting. This is unlike any other creative writing seminar at this college in terms of the amazing feedback you will get, the wonderful community you will create, and the writing you will eventually produce.
