TAKE THIS CLASS! Dayron and Leah are the BEST and cultivated such a safe, warm environment for the class. We had two field trips on Harvard's dime (to see Odyssey and Night Side Songs), both of which included dinner :) This class didn't have too much work, but I still felt challenged to think in new ways.
TAKE THIS CLASS. DAYRON AND LEAH, THE INSTRUCTORS, ARE ABSOLUTELY INSPIRING AND INCREDIBLE AND WONDERFUL AND SO KNOWLEDGEABLE AND NICE AND WONDERFUL. TAKE THIS COURSE. YOU WILL NOT REGRET IT. even if you aren't super into theater and decide to take this course, try to lean into it otherwise you're wasting their money.
This class is truly what it says it isa deep dive into the intersections of theater and democracy. It is almost all conversation though, in a circle for two hours, which can feel a bit repetitive every week, but the content of those discussions are incredibly rich. The insights peers provide are so interesting to hear, and always leave my head spinning in the best possible way. It mentally and emotionally stimulates me constantly, but people don't really take notes so its hard to remember it all afterward. Dayron and Leah are great and so encouraging! Their presence is a radiant one in the classroom, and they are supportive. I LOVED watching performances with the class! It was my favorite part, to actually see theater happen in front of our eyes after speaking about it. It grounded the course more for me.
Great class! It's discussion–based, which makes it a lot more fun than a typical lecture class. The discussions can get personal sometimes for me, that made the class more interesting, but if that makes you uncomfortable, just be aware of that.

The profs are super chill and understanding, and want to help you learn. I have no theater (or even humanities) background and it wasn't a problem at all. At the end of the day, you get out what you put in. If you make an effort to engage and participate, you'll get much more out of this class than if you don't. I feel like I gained exposure to a lot of new ideas from this class, and that's largely because I engaged actively in the discussions.

For my fellow busy people, do not fret. The workload outside the classroom is incredibly reasonable, and most of the intellectual work occurs inside the classroom. As long as you commit to being present and focused in class (very important), even with relatively little work outside of class, you can learn a lot!
TAKE THIS COURSE! You will learn so much from Dayron and Leah and the class community is lovely!
You will get as much out of this class as you buy in! You have the opportunity to become really close (intellectually and personally) with your classmates. This class has fantastic opportunities to hear about the impact of theater from people who have so much experience/expertise in the field. I imagine discussion will change from year–to–year with the changing political climate, but it will always be pertinent and meaningful.
