What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

This class is the argument for the necessity of social perspectives in the entwined worlds of science and policy. It covers methods of information control and production, training of scientific actors and objects, and more. The reading material is comprehensive, therefore extensive. You can get out of this class what you put in, but you will mostly learn from the diversity of peers present in the class (often across many different Harvard Schools). I highly recommend at least one course in your life with Prof. Jasanoff, for her way of teaching is unique, and provides an insight into the necessary rhetoric on committees and in decision boards about the future of science.
Great class, great professor, fun and interesting and great way to delve into a topic related to the course that you may want to use for further research.
Sheila wields a crucial perspective on STS, which makes the course near mandatory for those working in the discipline. The class is broad and welcoming enough though for many students in the sciences and humanities to find it both highly useful and approachable.
Budget time throughout the week for your readings and take notes! It is a lot to read but giving yourself time to digest, reflect, and relate readings to each other will go a long way.
Jasanoff is a talented lecturer and frequently drops pearls of wisdom which are incredibly insightful. However, the "discussion" part of this class is a little bit of an overstatement –– it's more like a one–on–one answer and response to Jasanoff herself. A lot of people resorted to meeting before class to actually go through and understand the material, since class time generally skipped over that basic understanding point (which is difficult with such complex readings). I would recommend this class if you're interested in the subject, willing to do a lot of reading, and don't necessarily need to talk about those readings thoroughly in order to understand.
This is a fantastic course if you want to deepen your knowledge of STS concepts! If you are new(er) to STS, I would strongly recommend you first take one of Prof. Jasanoff's other courses, which also provide an introduction to core STS concepts but in a way that is more grounded in concrete case studies (and perhaps more accessible for new STS students).

The seminar component of the course is truly its strong suit. Students from all across the university (FAS, HKS, the Divinity School, etc.) bring their unique perspectives and experiences to comment on each week's themes. By the semester midpoint, you'll start seeing STS concepts all around you, which brings the themes of the course to life.
