What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

The class is great! If you keep up with all of the assignments and vocabulary, you'll get a lot out of the class, both in terms of learning Hebrew and enjoyment.
Ran is the best
Modern Hebrew is a really fun language to learn, and I'd definitely recommend this course. I think this was Ran's first time teaching the first semester of beginner Hebrew, so some things will be fine–tuned in years to come, but there are a lot of great components to the course already (notably, the usage of songs in the class is really cool). As with any language, you will have to put in some work to command the material you learn to the level you want. My main criticism of the course is that a few things are not taught comprehensively (I have the most complaints by far about how verb conjugation is taught), but over time you will learn what you need to know, and Ran is always there to answer your questions!
Take this class! Although it is pretty challenging just by the nature of Hebrew as someone who didn't speak it at all and a native English speaker, Ran made it super approachable and fun! There is a decent amount of homework, but it is understandable in its use––it always related to and reenforced the class material. It is super easy to get feedback and extra help from Ran or the TFs, they really went above and beyond to help. You also get to learn some good music, and the readings in class are great at showing how much we learn very quickly. Overall, this class is great, you learn so much in such a short time!
This is evidence of how the language programmes at Harvard are one of its greatest strengths –– this class beats any other sort of self–directed language learning like youtube or duolingo etc by miles because of the community it forges, as well as the systemic way we are introduced to the language. I also think that the instructors have very positive teaching philosophies that makes the challenge of learning a language actually enjoyable
This class is a great introduction to a really cool language. Just remember that it is a language class, so be prepared for a lot of work. There are daily assignments that take a good chunk of time to complete in addition to weekly assignments that also are not quick. The professor is great, but he places a lot of emphasis on memorization and being able to hand–translate sentences into English, which ends up being a large part of the daily homework. You'll be able to write quite a bit of Hebrew by the end of the first semester, but there is little emphasis on speaking or constructing sentences in Hebrew from scratch. Aside from homework, there is a lot of music in the class and the instructor brings a lot of enthusiasm and energy to the classroom.
