Background: My parents spoke Chinese but much more English at home. I took Chinese for 4 years in high school but this in a non–rigorous environment. I took this class for a language requirement.

Thoughts: Overall, I was less prepared than other students going into BX. Some of your peers will already know a good chunk of the material, whereas others will be learning a ton. The class is somewhat challenging but not terrible. Grades are quite fair but the teachers expect more than they do in BX. A sample of the difference might be: In BX, you translate: "The teacher tells his students to work hard because success needs practice," whereas in 123, you might translate: "Despite more than five years of PHD research in educational theory, the teacher lacks basic teaching materials for his students to succeed."

Using simple ideas to represent complex ideas in 123 is usually not enough.

You should take this if you want to improve your Chinese. If you don't need to take it and don't want to learn Chinese, then I wouldn't take it.
This course is absolutely amazing. Du Laoshi is a wonderful teacher along with the others (this year we had Huang Laoshi, Xiong Laoshi and Lu Laoshi), and the teachers are very passionate about helping students learn the course content. I feel like my Chinese reading and writing/typing abilities have massive improved this semester. The smaller class size also is great for building community: some of my best friends were made in this class. One of the only detracts is that it meets really often (every day), so you'd better understand how the class will work with your schedule. Otherwise, amazing course, 1000% recommend.

This course definitely improved my Chinese! I think it's mostly just from being in an environment everyday where you're forced to speak in Chinese for an hour and then the discipline for learning new words. I will say it gets kind of repetitive sometimes because lecture and section are basically the same thing and you definitely benefit from being in a section that is smaller because you get more speaking time, you're more engaged, and also there's just more attention devoted to you. Not too much work and grading is super chill!
Such a fun class. It's rare that I ever have so much fun while learning so much.
