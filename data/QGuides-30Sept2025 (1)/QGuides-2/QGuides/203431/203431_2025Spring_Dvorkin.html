Take it! Super engaging lectures, really cool professor opening to explaining anything in the field.
Cora is a very engaging lecturer, though the lectures can be a little disorganized. Get started early on the final project, and get as much feedback from Cora and the TF as possible (they want to help!).
Physics 212 is one of the most useful classes I've taken. It didn't just teach us theory; it also taught us how to apply what we know in research projects. There are also pretty cool statistics and their applications in cosmology. The first half of the semester involved weekly PSets which were pretty chill. I've definitely had harder PSet classes before. However, I think the main challenge of this course is the final project at the end. You have to put a lot of work to make something worth sharing in the span of 4–5 weeks. Cora and Prish were extremely helpful though! You basically get feedback from experts in the field which pushes you to grow a lot as a researcher. Overall this class was very fulfilling and it's one of those classes I would regret not taking.
You should only take this class if youre especially interested in cosmology and have quite a good background in coding, and some statistics since the class moves pretty quickly and assumes quite a lot of prior understanding (GR, particle physics). Also half of your grade is the final project which is a big mystery. There is very little guidance and most people work by themselves (which I do not recommend you to do since the project can be quite complex). Also the grading of the project is kind of up in the air. You dont know what they can deduct points for and how many. Even though you present in the last week of class and theres plenty of time to get your grade and some feedback on the project, you dont. Coras teaching is quite chaotic as well, lacking a storyline or flow, skipping steps and getting distracted by other thoughts (she says something then says to forget it, starts to explain something and randomly jumps to explaining something else etc.). For me it was difficult to follow. Also as much as youd like to leave anonymous mid–semester feedback the class is quite small so it gets less anonymous. She brought some of the reviews up in class and discussed them, which I believed was directed towards a few students who gave some of the negative feedback (probably including myself). I didnt appreciate that.
