If you are interested in learning about conventional energy sources (coal, oil, gas, unconventionals, and standard renewables), this class is truly excellent. Prof. Shaw is genuinely so knowledgeable and even though making it to the 9am classes are sometimes tough, the content is genuinely so interesting. Prof. Shaw is the most down to earth, kind, and committed professor I have ever met at Harvard and it really comes through in how he handles the course, students, and the field trips. Speaking of, the field trip(s), especially the long one, was genuinely such a highlight. Although having to wake up super early was a pain in the a**, the trip itself was super informative and honestly a once in a lifetime experience. By the end of the course, I learned so much and truly understand energy systems now!

Grading wise, I think the exams are very doable IF you know how to study. I was actually a government major taking this class, and so my approach (and the approach I recommend) is to use Anki and make dozens of flashcards for each lecture and just drill them the exams are much more focused on information recall than on problem solving, so I'd honestly treat it like a history/bio class when it comes to studying. Also, please study chemical formulas for the exams!

The labs were a bit annoying at times. The GOCAD labs especially were really tough, and honestly kind of frustrating, but that's just part of the process. I'd recommend having some lab buddies and just grinding them all out together. Especially the labs towards the end of the course are actually not that much work and are honestly pretty interesting!

Overall, must take for anyone who cares about energy. Prof & class materials: 10/10. Labs: 7/10. Field trips & overall vibes: 11/10. Must take!

This is an extremely good course for several reasons. This was a huge breath of fresh air for me after having taken some classes where the TF's are confused and irritable and the professor is nowhere to be found. Second, the course material is extremely well thought out and presented. Professor Shaw's lectures are concise, rehearsed, and overall quite impressive. The corresponding powerpoint slides are almost all graphs and diagrams, and that proves that everything he says is backed by scientific research. No smoke and mirrors with this guy. In addition, the labs and other assignments are all very well related to the central topics, so it creates a great experience of learning about something in lecture and then immediately applying it in a real–life lab scenario. And on top of that, the topics and field trip are absolutely essential knowledge for anyone even considering working in the environmental field. This completely changed my understanding of our Earth and its resources. Very highly recommend.

As a mechanical engineer, this is one of my favorite courses that I have taken at Harvard. Shaw gives extremely fair considerations of all energy resources, students who enter the class with the belief that renewables such as wind and solar will meet all of our energy demands will quickly be struck with the harsh reality that opposes this notion. It was interesting to hear about energy topics through both the policy and geological perspective, as my experience was mainly through engineering. If you pay attention in lab you can learn some useful technical skills, but the quality of your lab is highly dependent upon how good your TF is. TLDR; take this class if you want to understand the state of our energy infrastructure.
