This class is very interesting, and you will be challenged by the contents. However, it is always easy to manage the workload, and to ask for help when you need it. This class is also a good way to introduce yourself broadly to biophysics, whether you want to pursue a career in it or just think it seems cool.
If you are looking for an easy class to fulfill SEAS technical requirements, this is a winner. I did not learn much – entirely my fault because of the lack of time I invested – but if you need to check the box and focus on your research this is one of the best. Max is a great professor and very engaged with students, and I would love to learn about a subject I have more research interest in from him.
Good course for those looking for a survey of quantitative methods in biology.
it's a solid course to take for those in the bioengineering program without other applicable options within seas. though you will get out what you put into it. max really cares and is super approachable
This is an enjoyable class. That said you get out what you put in. You can definitely skate by and still do well in the class but there are a lot of opportunities to actually engage with the material and learn a great deal. I'd recommend going to office hours especially for the psets early in the course.
This course gives a good overview of the topics you should know in single molecule biophysics and at times was extremely helpful. For example, you will be taught the general principles of TIRF, confocal, SMLM, cryo–EM etc. You will be able to speak intelligently to someone else about these topics, but don't expect to be able to solve many problems yourself from this course. I think this course is difficult to digest. Without a dedicated photonics/optics or electrodynamics course it was hard to follow. That being said, it was not entirely ineffective. I did learn a lot, but I think the potential for this course will be best if you have a pretty good physics background. Workload is totally manageable, well planned, and reasonable. I would recommend, but only if you think you'd find it interesting.
Great class if you're looking to dive into the theory of single molecule techniques
The course load is fair and the topics of lecture are very useful for students who are interested in the principles of optical imaging and different biological imaging techniques of fluorescence microscopy (SMLM, STED, etc.)
This class was super fun and interesting! It wasn't a huge amount of work and you get a lot out of the class. The guest lectures in the second half of the semester were fantastic, and Max and Daphne are both amazing.
If you enjoy a more math heavy class, this is a good class for you. Be prepared to give presentations and write a lot.
