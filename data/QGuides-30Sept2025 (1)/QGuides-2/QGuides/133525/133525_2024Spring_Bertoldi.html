ES 128 is hard. Really hard. But it's super rewarding and very well–taught, and was one of my favorite engineering courses I've taken. Katia is a fantastic lecturer, and most of the teaching staff is very helpful and accessible when it comes to assignments. The problem sets were very fair–– 3 problem each week (1 abaqus, 1 matlab, 1 paper), and the material built upon itself each week, so it was really cool to see my understanding of the material expand as I did the problem sets each week. The exams (1 in person midterm, 1 take home 2 day midterm) were both fair. The one part of the class that I disliked was the final project component –– we were expected to create update presentations 3 times during the semester and present them during class (on top of the already difficult psets we were completing), and it felt like a waste of time to sit and listen to everyone's updates.

If you do not have a strong background in Matlab, do NOT take this course. Matlab is significantly used every week for the problem sets; this course should not be your first exposure to using Matlab. Take ES 159 first to get more Matlab experience before taking 128.
This course is extremely difficult. The homework assignments are very dense, and if you fall behind on MATLAB, you will need to fix your code before you can run the next week's code. I would only recommend taking this course if you are comfortable with MATLAB and Python. However, the skills you leave this course with are priceless, but they do come at the cost of long nights and stressful weeks if you struggle with coding. This class is difficult but can be rewarding depending on what you put into it. (AND YES, it is a 20 hour a week course)
Matlab assignments will take up a lot of time. This class overall will take up lots of time with homeworks and checkins
Dont take this class with 227. This class takes a ton of time. Attempt the psets before going to office hours and be prepared to leave feeling dumb if your solution doesnt work. Also all teaching staff has different methods for debugging abaqus, which gets confusing
This class gets a lot of recommendations, which is why I took it, but I would recommend being more wary of the hype. Overall, it is pretty well taught, especially for Harvard Engineering, but if you're really trying to get your bang for your buck with your classes at Harvard, I would recommend taking a different course. I did like the class, but I do not feel as if I learned as much as I could have and I still need more practice with FEA programs like Abaqus before I get comfortable.

Regarding specifically the coursework, it is not particularly demanding, but watch out for the first MATLAB problem set question. You hit the ground running with this assignment, and if you do not have experience with MATLAB or a similar language (I only had previous experience in Python, and I did just fine), then you will face a very harsh learning curve. After this assignment, though, things calm down considerably. Additionally, going to OH for the Abaqus problems is almost entirely necessary because the online videos are such low quality, and Abaqus is so particular that you cannot miss a single step without crashing the program.
This class is very rewarding but pretty difficult. Personally, I really enjoyed the class and felt like a learned a lot of really valuable skills. Problem Sets are challenging and will really make you understand the concepts you are working with, and the project will make you expand those concepts into a real world problem. The course staff were great; easily accessible, helpful, and enthusiastic. I'd recommend taking this class, but keep in mind that it will be a pretty big time commitment
