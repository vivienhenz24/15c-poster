Comments
If you are very interested in the interactions between slavery and the law I highly recommend this course. While the course description may not say this, this is primarily a class about slavery so just know that going in. If you are willing to put in the work, this class can be very enlightening and a positive experience. Professor Gordon Reed is obviously very knowledgeable and passionate about the subject. However, this class demands that you be a self starter. The entire grade is based on the final exam, so you must not get behind or cram.
The lectures are engaging and students get to voice their opinions freely and without any pressure. There is a large amount of reading though, and 100% of your grade is based on an open–book final exam in which you consult all of the semester's readings. Be prepared for a huge time commitment, as the readings are very important to class discussions and the final. You are guaranteed to learn a lot and for undergraduates curious about law school, this will give you a cool experience!
The course requires lots of reading and the professor likes to make the class very–discussion based. At times, the class can feel like it a bit unorganized but the professor is very knowledgable.
Take this course if you're truly interested in American legal history! Professor Gordon–Reed is a great lecturer, and it is pretty stress–free throughout the semester. You really learn a lot about the central themes and conflicts of early American history. However, a few people did drop the class, as the final is supposed to be difficult and accounts for 100% of your grade.
While this class does not have any assignments except for the final, this is not a class that you can coast through without actively engaging and trying to get the most out of the readings that you can. Interesting class but beware the 8 hour take home final with two essays.
Absolutely take this class if you are interested in the American Revolution and the history of the United States before the Civil War. On the other hand, be prepared to face a final take–home exam that will account for 100% of your grade. Attend the lectures – which are highly engaging – and do the assigned readings.
It's a lot of reading and the incentive to do so is not super strong given the lack of assignments/time devoted to covering them in class. Be engaged in class and the class will actually be quite interesting, Professor Gordon–Reed is a good lecturer/facilitator of discussions. The discussions make up the majority of class–time, final exam at the end is take–home essays so make sure you're good with that (only assignment of the year).
