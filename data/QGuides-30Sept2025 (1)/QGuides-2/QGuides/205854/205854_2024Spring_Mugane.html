It's a language; you get what you put into learning. But have fun and make friends with the people you're learning with. And try to speak with native speakers often; it helps a lot.
This class will teach you a lot about Igbo culture in a fun, interactive way. It'll get you out of your comfort zone, but you'll feel amazing and prideful afterwards. Try your best and study outside of class if you want to truly retain the language. It can be difficult, but the professor will help you each step of the way.
This class largely depends on the professor, so sometimes that Qguides will be good or bad. I would make sure that you are surrounded by a professor that supports and encourages academic growth. Professor Attamah was great at this and work incredibly hard throughout the semester.
Amarachi was a great teacher! I would definitely recommend taking it if she is teaching it, although I'm not sure if it will be her next semester. She is very passionate about teaching us how to speak the language, which is very important. We also had some group assignments, which fostered friendships among our classmates. If she's not teaching the class , then I'd say to give it a shot and feel out the new teacher. Honestly, regardless of who the teacher is, it shouldn't be hard to get an A in the class.
