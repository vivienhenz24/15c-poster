Comments
I truly came away with an appreciate of the subtleties involved in translation. Previously, I had never thought about translation, but there are many different strategies. You can shoot for an exact translation of each word, or you can translate the meaning across languages, or you can do a mix of both, or something else entirely. It's truly an art. I also feel like my Spanish speaking abilities developed, even though that may not be the aim of the course. The professor is fantastic! He's super fun and engaging and really tries to get to know individual students! He really generates participation amongst students.
I would absolutely recommend taking this class. Daniel is a great professor and makes you really excited to go to class every week. Although the class is two hours straight, they go by really quickly due to the heavily interactive component of the course.
Best class ever: low stress, little work outside of class, engaging and fun class time, and you learn a lot. Daniel is so funny, knowledgeable, and wants the best for everyone. Take it if you have the chance because it was my favorite all–around class experience at Harvard (as a senior).
Take this class! It is very enjoyable and a light addition to any course load.
This class is AWESOME! Daniel is soo nice and his passion for translation is contagious. The class is a safe space to practice your spanish and gain a new perspective on language. Overall super manageable workload with only two in class translation exercises. A great course for anyone who enjoys Spanish!
If you can take this class, you absolutely should! Daniel is wonderful and creates the perfect environment for this "taller" (workshop/seminar). I always looked forward to coming to class and found every one to be engaging and fun! Daniel somehow makes the littlest things interesting when translating. We might spend a few minutes discussing a single preposition or punctuation mark, and yet he makes that fun. Also, there is essentially no work that you need to do outside of class except for some readings that are very manageable, so it's super easy to do well in this course without spending too much time on it. I took this class to work on my Spanish skills, and it certainly helped improve my Spanish abilities, but beyond that it also gave me a newfound appreciation for translation in general.
10/10 I highly recommend everyone who needs to take a Spanish course for secondary/citation/concentration to take this course. Daniel is amazing and the two hours fly by. This course offers a really unique way of learning Spanish through translation that you can't get elsewhere. I will say that because it's a translation workshop you don't get the same "immersive" learning experience. Regardless, this was one of my favorite courses I've taken at Harvard so far and any course with Daniel is a good time and great learning experience.
