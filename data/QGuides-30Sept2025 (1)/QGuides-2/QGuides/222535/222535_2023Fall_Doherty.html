Maggie is a wonderful, generous, encouraging, helpful, insightful, attentive, hard–working teacher (she gave very helpful feedback on all the assignments of all of the members) and this workshop is a gem, and not in the usual sense of the term used at Harvard. I spent more time writing in this course than anything, because I learned so much, from my workshop–mates, the readings, etc. Honestly, you will get feedback on your writing that helps you see your own writing (and stuff in general) with a bigger perspective. I recommend this course if you have any interest at all in creative nonfiction.
Take this class! You come away with a real sense that you've improved as a writer (which is honestly a hard thing to say in a single semester). The readings are really phenomenal and Maggie makes sure that you come away from each class knowing what the "takeaway" or the portable skill is from each reading, so that you are reading for the sake of improving your writing rather than just reading. I would say the breadth of the class is quite wide, which can be advantageous for someone looking for a survey class or daunting for someone who needs more "prompting" to guide their writing.
Buy–into the class. Do the readings and participate fully in workshops, and you will get so much out of the class. By doing so, you will learn so much about each other and generate a very strong community.
