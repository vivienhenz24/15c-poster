To future students of Africa in the Twentieth Century: Culture, Representation, and New Diasporas. This class offers an incredible opportunity to explore Africas history, culture, and global connections in a deeply engaging and interactive way. Come with an open mind, ready to challenge stereotypes, learn from diverse perspectives, and engage in meaningful discussions.
The well–structured syllabus, thought–provoking assignments, and moderated presentations provide a solid foundation for critical thinking and collaborative learning. This course not only broadens your knowledge of African experiences but also encourages personal growth by reshaping how you understand history, culture, and representation in a global context.
Dear future students,

PLEASE TAKE THIS CLASS! If you see a course taught by Prof. Ogene, you sign up for it. This was hands down my favorite class I've taken here. From PhD candidates in African Studies and Religion to MBA students, and STEM/Humanities undergraduates, everyone brought their unique perspectives to the table and it made for productive, nuanced conversations.
