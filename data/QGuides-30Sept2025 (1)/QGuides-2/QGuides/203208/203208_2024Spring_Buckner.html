First of all, I don't think that this course should be offered to undergraduates. At the very least, I think it should be marked primarily for graduate students. The undergraduate neuroscience concentration curriculum at Harvard is largely focused on cell–biology, and the undergraduate psychology concentration curriculum at Harvard is largely focused on functional research methods to study the brain and behavior. This course finds itself in an odd niche between those two fields by focusing on the distributed circuits and anatomical connections in the brain, and so the usual prerequisites of those two concentrations (PSY 1, Neuro 80, PSY 14, etc.) do not prepare you well for this class. Additionally, having never taken any sort of macroneuroanatomy course I felt disoriented with respect to the geography of the brain and I was never really able to take my understanding of these abstract circuits and connect them to the physical regions of the brain that were being described; my understanding of the material felt very schematic.

The readings in this course are very technical and difficult to understand. The professor once told the class not to feel concerned if we felt like 80–90% of the content of the readings went over our heads on the first read, and I certainly felt like I barely grasped 10% of the readings at best. The papers are deceivingly difficult to write as well, given my difficulties even understanding the material, let alone working with it. If the subject matter of this course calls to you and you just have to take it, it might be worth considering, but otherwise I think this course is just too technical for most undergraduates to succeed in this class.
