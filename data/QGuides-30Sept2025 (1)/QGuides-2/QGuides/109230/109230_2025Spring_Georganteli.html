Fabulous class. Incredible professor. Lot's of old coins, so it's great if you're a numismatic. Dumbarton Oaks trip is spectacular. You don't want to miss out on this class.
Take this class!!! It is exactly what you'd imagine a Harvard class would be. You get to directly interact with objects from centuries ago –– treasures from the Harvard Art Museums collection that few people will ever see. Professor Georganteli is one of the kindest, most engaging professors I've ever had, and that's reason alone to take this class, even if you're not interested in the Crusades. And if that's not reason enough, you get to go to DC to visit Dumbarton Oaks, which is so fun and enriching.
This class is a once in a lifetime opportunity! You will get to handle real artifacts/artwork related to the Crusades (especially coins) in every class. I have truly never had a class like it before and probably never will since. We even got to take an incredible field trip to DC where we stayed at Dunbarton Oaks, Harvard's state–of–the–art research center for Byzantine history. Plus, we got to handle incredible manuscripts at the Fine Arts Library and even had an optional, hands–on workshop where we got to mint coins with designs from the Crusades period.

Also, Prof. Georganteli is wonderful! She has a reputation for being one of the kindest, most encouraging professors at Harvard, and it is well–deserved! She really goes out of her way to get to know each of her students as individuals, she cares about your successes in and out of class, and she is incredibly warm and gracious if you need help with anything. She supports her students and is happy to share resources and suggest possible opportunities. She even serves hot chocolate in office hours! She is also a great lecturer; she's very talented at presenting complex topics in an understandable way and keeping students engaged while she does so.

A few notes: there is a lot of reading (that doesn't always get discussed in class), and the class tends to use more European sources than Islamic ones, but even with these caveats, it is definitely worth it!
This course is an easy recommendation. Take it. The opportunity to engage with so many works from the collection of the Harvard Art Museums is one that you don't want to miss. You may think that the Crusades are a distinct phenomenon, but after this course you will never see them the same and you will see their reception and impacts on the millennium since they began. Take this course, talk to Prof. Georganteli, really buy into it. You won't regret it.
This class is incredible and I would recommend anybody takes it. This is one of those classes where you might not think about the Crusades every day now, but as you take this class you will notice how their history is woven into the history of Europe, the Middle East, and Christianity/Islam as a whole. You will spend a lot of time learning to analyze different artifacts and art pieces, and you should get started on writing your final paper as soon as you can. TAKE THIS CLASS.
