If you're interested in economics, take this seminar.
Like any seminar, only take it if it's something you're genuinely interested in. There are a decent amount of readings, and you will get so much more out of it if you genuinely pay attention to them. It's helpful to have some understanding of economics and economic theory–– there are two weeks on Marx, and he loves to yap. Overall, it's a wonderful seminar. Professor Marglin brought us apples every week from his orchard, and has tons of cool stories that he shared with us! He's a great professor and an even better person. Whether you're a capitalist, a socialist, a communist, or have no idea, I urge you to take this seminar!!
This is an interesting class if you want to learn about socialism in a very broad sense –– its history, original philosophical basis, small scale movements, socialism and technology, etc. Ultimately, it is prompting us to think about the prospects of socialism. Prof. Marglin is also a very kind and accessible professor! I would say that this class is heavier in economics and philosophy than I had expected. If you are more intrigued by the politics of it, or want to delve deeper in one particular aspect or case, then I'd look at other selections. This course also has a heavier workload concerning the weekly reading responses, a midterm paper, and a term research paper. Personally, I wish there had been more feedback given all the writings we did, and more unpacking of the reading content.
For anyone interested in doing a first dive into socialism, I would highly recommend this class. Professor Marglin is an accomplished economist, who, while being tenured, remains attentive to students. You will read about misconceptions about socialism, competing ideas on socialism's tendency to tend towards authoritarianism, what socialism looks like in practice, existing issues in capitalism, and by the end of the course, be able to take these ideas into account to build a coherent perspective on the subject. I enjoyed the class! :)
Take the class, you gain a better perspective on some of your views about class and wealth. Professor Marglin is great and lenient. Just make sure to do your work and readings.
The readings are mind–blowing and boundary–shattering, from Harvard's only tenured radical economist. Prof. Marglin's wisdom and ability to ask meaningful questions in an unbiased way will get the engaged discussion participator to doubt fundamental assumptions made from their beliefs from either side. Completely worth taking this course. Every week is a cathartic experience.
Seminars (this one, at least) can be a really great way to really get to know a topic and this course is one where you really got out what you put in. Marglin is incredibly knowledgeable and pushes people to think hard about their assertions in a way where it really is a fun quest for truth. Do note though that seminars aren't necessary, and although it's fun, it is a time commitment, so just know that going in.
