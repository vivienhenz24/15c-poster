This course forced me to correct histories I had been taught in my household. I feel it is impossible to leave Penslar's mentorship without an appreciation for nuance in these conversations. He is a phenomenal lecturer, an accessible professor, and a mentor who is committed to your academic growth.
It was a shockingly smooth ride. I learned an enormous amount and I never felt a need to self–censor, nor did I feel as though I was in an echo chamber with myself.
Definitely take this class. Penslar is an amazing professor and presents the material in an extremely unbiased way. You really will learn a lot and (at least for me) realize that it is not nearly as simple as either side would like you to think it is. In my experience, people were very open and the class itself was very respectful, even though we were talking about extremely contentious issues. Definitely not a gem, but totally doable.
This is not an easy class, but it is certainly a good one to take if you want to know more about Israel/Palestine, Zionism, and the wider Arab–Israeli Conflict. Professor Penslar is a judicious scholar and does an excellent job of presenting a narrative that was fair to Israel while also being inclusive of Palestine. As someone who is pro–Palestine, I rarely found myself agreeing with everything he said, but I never felt he presented facts out of context nor did I feel he pushed a narrative. If you find yourself disagreeing with Israel's policies or think of yourself as someone who knows enough about the Arab–Israeli conflict, you should take this class. It could do with more people who have pro–Palestinian perspectives who are willing to learn about Israel from a scholarly perspective, disagree respectfully, and emerge better–equipped to speak about the subject.

Logistically, the class has a lot of readings, but most of them are interesting. The assignments themselves are not over–bearing, it is really the readings that take up the lion's share of your time each week.
This is a superb course. Penslar has clearly thought carefully about every aspect the lectures, the selection of readings, the exam prompts, etc. He touches on obviously extremely sensitive material with nuance and wit.

I would encourage anyone with any interest in the conflict to take the course, regardless of your political orientation. Penslar says it from the start: he is pro nuance. That doesn't mean he isn't open to critique to the contrary, he very much welcomes it so I think anyone could learn a great deal from engaging. Also, this *is* a history course. We don't reach the 21st century until the last two weeks of the course. I think that's great, but don't expect this to be all analysis about the ongoing situation.
This class is one of my favorite courses I have taken at Harvard. I came into this class with very minimal knowledge on Israel/Palestine, and I now feel I have a firm grasp on many key historical and cultural aspects of the conflict. Everyone enters the class with varying levels of knowledge and different viewpoints, but I found it totally possible to follow along and keep up, thanks in large part to the discussions and lectures that were firmly rooted in historical argumentation, rather than opinion or speculation. I would absolutely recommend this class to anyone at all interested in history, current events, and really any type of academic discourse. This course gave me so much hope I was initially worried that sections or lecture would become contentious given the topic. However, everyone was incredibly respectful, even when they disagreed. I am so grateful to have had the chance to think deeply about this topic through an academic framework.
This is a great class. If you are interested in the topic, I highly, highly recommend it. Professor Penslar is a wealth of knowledge and brought in fascinating and renowned guest lecturers. The readings are all very interesting. I learned so much, and I am so grateful to have had the opportunity to take the class. The class does require work, but a very reasonable amount, and each assignment felt like a valuable way to be spending my time (not busy work). I will say that the course veers on the more progressive side, but that's not necessarily a bad thing –– just something to keep in mind. As someone who leans progressive myself, I would have liked to read more nuanced perspectives to have been prevented to challenge my own views, but I still learned so much.
This is a GREAT course–––it is exactly what you are signing up for: a very in–depth exploration of Israeli and Palestinian history. If you are looking to commit to learning more about this topic (which means doing the readings!!!), then this course will serve you well. If you want to just gain some talking points or have someone hold your hand while you memorize every fact about I/P there is, then you will NOT get that. This is an *academic history* course, so be prepared to really think and discuss about what historical sources mean. However, if you do ~most~ of the reading and pay attention in lecture, you will come away with much deeper knowledge on I/P, even if it is a topic you already are familiar with.
I came into the course knowing extremely little about this geopolitical issue but Penslar makes himself so available to students through office hours and actively encourages people less familiar with the topic to feel comfortable asking questions in lecture. He's funny and personable –– go to office hours! The material itself was just excellent with a mixture of primary sources, secondary literature, and movies and documentaries. If you have Kabl as a TF you're golden. My advice is to not be shy and you will learn a lot!
You must absolutely take this class. Without question. There is so much rhetoric about Israel/Palestine on this campusyou may even participate in itbut how much do you know about the other side, or even about your own? Taking this class inspired me deeplyPenslar teaches without dogma, with grace and skepticism, and in a spirit of good–faith inquiry. In a climate where this issue is so emotionally charged and socially/politically relevant, an introduction of this high quality is indispensable.
If you're really interested in the topic, this course is fascinating, nuanced, and in–depth. However, it's a lot of work, so I would not recommend taking it simply out of curiosity or as an electiveit's likely to be your hardest class, in terms of workload and grading, so make sure it's worth that investment.
Take this class! Professor Penslar is great and you will genuinely learn so much. Take it even if you're not a history concentrator, it's for everyone!
