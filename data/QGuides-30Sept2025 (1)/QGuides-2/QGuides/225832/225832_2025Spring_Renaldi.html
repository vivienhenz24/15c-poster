Take this class!!! Richard and Claude are amazing and you will learn so much about photography. Learning your way around a camera is so fun and you will improve a ton as a photographer. But even beyond that this class will make you just an all around more visually aware and observant person!
Take this class if you are genuinely interested in learning more about photography as an art, and with purpose. It is a time–consuming class, and requires lots of independent work doing photography during the week, editing the photos, and selecting the best ones, and doing so every week. It has the potential of being incredibly rewarding (which was the case for me, I walk away with strong technical foundations, a greater understanding of this art, and also a keen interest in pursuing my work in the future), but I would not take it if you don't have the time or desire to truly immerse yourself (otherwise it might backfire and make you not like photography). I took it just because I was curious and wanting something different in my schedule, and I walk away with a new hobby and passion that'll last me forever, so I am very grateful, and if that seems to be the case for you, then I would highly highly recommend it. Plus, Richard and Claude are amazing, they are available and helpful, and built a tight–knit class and welcoming learning environment.
Cool class to get into photography, but the lectures can be a bit tedious.
I learned so much not just about the logistics of taking a good photo but also, theoretically and artistically, what a good photo makes. Richard and Claude show you practically every part of the process, and I now have a new hobby that I will take with me outside the classroom! Many other students also were talking about getting personal cameras a testament to how much this course shaped us! This was one of the few classes I took this semester that I was genuinely excited to go to every day (even though the hours are long).
