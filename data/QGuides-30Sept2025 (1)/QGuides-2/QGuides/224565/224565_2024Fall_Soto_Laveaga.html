What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class is absolutely necessary for understanding the connections between development, underdevelopment, economics and science in the 20th century. I knew little about the making of "modernization" and how the mid 20th century relates to today, but this class provided the historical pathways of development from its colonial roots to the present. The historical approach served as a useful paradigm for open communication between classmates, who included students from the College, FAS, the Kennedy and Design Schools. Prof. Soto–Laveaga's style of opening the floor for discussion is unparalleled, and I have rarely been in a classroom with such lively communication.
This class is amazing! If you're at all interested in development you should take this class. You may come out slightly jaded about development, though. The readings are excellent, though there are a lot, and (at least this semester) people did them and brought many interesting thoughts to class. I really appreciated the class environment of this class as well. There were so many smart people with a lot of previously knowledge really dedicated to actually doing the readings and they were all so nice!
I highly recommend this course, regardless if you are a History of Science student, College student, or HKS student (like me!) The course is very engaging and allows us to "think" deeply about how we should approach the international development in 21st century based on 20th century experiences. While the readings may appear heavy to some, all the readings are highly relevant, connected, and interesting. There is no better way to explore the history of international development than this module!
