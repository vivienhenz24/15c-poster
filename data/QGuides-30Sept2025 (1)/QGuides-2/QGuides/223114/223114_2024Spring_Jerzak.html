The topic of this class interested me at first, but I regret taking it in place of a different Gov research or methods class. The main unfortunate aspect of the class was that lectures often felt disorganized and the instruction was unclear. While lectures would largely consist of walkthroughs of code and implementation, they were delivered without much forethought and often the lecturer himself was confused on the content. As a result, I often left class without a solid understanding of how to implement the week's lessons.
Another unfortunate aspect of the class that's out of the teacher's control is the fact that many students entered this class with completely different levels of familiarity with R and webcrawling. As a result, lectures had to accommodate a wide range of experience levels, which often meant that no one learned anything at all.
In the end, I have a slightly better understanding of webcrawling/webscraping than I did when I entered the course, but I believe that I could have learned much of this content online. The class could have been taught better if lectures were better prepared and thought–out beforehand, so that the professor could coherently spell out the key packages and methods we are supposed to learn.
Really great way to learn big data methods like web scraping, crawling, working with geospatial data, working with APIs. Connors a great professor who is super understand and wants this course to work for you and your goals.
Even wondered how to actually make the data you analyze? Take this course if you're interested in web scraping, web crawling, APIs, SQL, and how to use your computer to make cool datasets! Connor's an amazing instructor, and will help you as much as you ask for it. He thinks very quickly and broadly, which can be a bit overwhelming at times, but that just made me all the more excited to keep learning from him. Some background in programming or R will be helpful to keep up, but not necessary.
Be careful of classes with visiting professors... it was extremely unclear who this class was designed for, and as a result students had insanely varying skill levels that made pedagogy nearly impossible in any form.
Take it if you have some background in programming and want an overview of different scraping and data processing techniques. The instructor is great, the content is interesting and you get out of it how ever much you put in. Definitely worth your time!
This one of the rare courses at Harvard (particularly in the Gov department) where I've learned real, tangible skills. It could be organized a bit better (but that's natural for a course being taught for the first time) and taught in more of a "code–along" way instead of just scrolling through pre–written code, but with the recorded lectures it got easier to understand as the semester went on. The problem sets could also use more scaffolding and office hour work sessions, but Connor was super helpful thoughout the semester. If this course is offered at Harvard again (I really hope it is because this is one of the most critical things to learn if one wants to pursue quantitative gov academia or thesis research) I hope it can be organized slightly better. That said, even in its current form, I still learned a LOT this semester and count it among the most useful courses I've taken at Harvard. The course atmosphere was also nice.
