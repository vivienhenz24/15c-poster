What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
While this class has several expectations and assignments to juggle, I enjoyed the course overall and it provides a comprehensive foundation into Comparative Literature. The readings were very interesting even if difficult to understand at times, and the assignments could be challenging but in a fulfilling way.
This course is great and I wish it could be offered to non–CompLit concentrators/secondaries, since I learned so much about what comp lit really is as a field from CL97 than other CL classes I took previously. There is a lot of close–readings and theoretical discussions, as well as essays. I personally found the essays to be quite harshly graded on small details. The final month of the class was filled with presentations, meetings, abstracts, drafts, rewrites, and essays, which was very stressful at an already stressful time of the semester. It is a really cool class nonetheless, as you get to work on whatever you want for your final project and present it as if at a conference. Students in this class definitely treat CL97 with different levels of seriousness, though, which can make discussions and group presentations less engaging and informative.
This is a great course from which I learned a lot. Depending on who teaches the tutorial, it can be a lot of work. We had a rewrite, a final, and a personal essay all due within 2 days of finals period, as well as a class during reading period and a non–optional exit interview after last day of finals.
This class was an amazing introduction to the important questions Comp Lit scholars are debating about literature as well as foundational theory. Class discussions were always very interesting, and I loved the range of books and scholars we read.
Be prepared to read a lot, often a novel a week paired with a novel's worth of literary theory. You should be very interested in studying literature to take this. That said, Professor Lienau is one of the most intelligent professors at Harvard and incredibly accessible.
This course is a must take for all CompLit students. Professor Lienau and Shaowen were an amazing duo because they really took the time to get to know each student. Expect to be treated as an academic equal in the classroom as they'll take the points you bring up and your research interests very seriously. It can at times feel a little intense but it is a good introduction to the field as a whole.
This is a fantastic class. I took it for the secondary and ended up changing to a double. Really make the most of the teaching team and assignments because they teach you sooooo much.
This is a good class, and you get out of it what you put into it so I would encourage you to pay attention to all of the readings and come prepared to the discussions.
