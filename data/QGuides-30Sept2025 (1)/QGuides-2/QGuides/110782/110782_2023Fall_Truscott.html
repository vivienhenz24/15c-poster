What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If you are interested in studying Japanese, I can't recommend this class highly enough (especially with Professor Truscott)! Learning classical will massively benefit your language abilities and give you the ability to experience a new world of literature for yourself.
I would recommend this class if you want to learn more about the language and nuances of classical Japanese – while the class structure is not too harsh in grading (a lot of it is participation, and quizzes can also be retaken), but it is still not easy and still requires quite a lot of work because there are a whole lot of grammar rules to familiarize yourself wit. In some ways it felt almost like learning a completely new language because the grammar rules are quite different from modern day Japanese. I did come in thinking that this would be a literary class, and while there is some of that when you talk about interpretation or techniques, it is mostly a translation/ grammar class, so be prepared for it to be more on the technical side. Danica is really kind and really open to feedback, and I really enjoyed the class!
If you want to read Classical Japanese, take this class! It is tedious sometimes, the exams are hard, but overall it's just so valuable to be able to read traditional poetry etc.! Though you shouldn't expect to be able to read it fluently, we always worked with a dictionary/vocab list and grammar annotations. Danica is such a kind instructor and made classes really enjoyable. Work load wasn't bad, but you'll have to study a lot for the tests.
You will learn a lot if you spend enough time on the subject.
This course is fulfilling for any student of Japanese, especially those who enjoy Japanese literature.
