Certain lecturers are far, far better than others.
I absolutely LOVED this class. My tutorial leader was incredible, and the lectures were consistently engaging. Coming from Hum10, I was excited for another reading–oriented class, and my experience exceeded all expectations. The course is very much one of those experiences where you get out what you put in. You will not enjoy or appreciate the class if you do not read and approach material with an open and simultaneously critical mind. You will rarely get the experience to sit down in such small, focused groups to really discuss so–called great works. The class demands attention and rewards diligence.

Now, there are two main caveats. First, your experience will be heavily determined by your tutorial leader: I happened to be a good match to mine but that is not the most common outcome. Second, many people struggle with the "why" of reading the often outdated thoughts of rich white men. To some degree, that is what the class is. Professors will make the case to you over and over again as to why the readings matter. However, you should work towards your "why." You might enjoy certain thinkers, looking at the evolution of thought, or maybe really don't get it until Marx starts to put the pieces into action. Whatever the case, there can be something to gain from the course for everyone, you just have to consider what that is for you.

This class is not the reason you should concentrate in Social Studies, and it is also not the reason you shouldn't concentrate in Social Studies. It is without a doubt, however, a compelling way to become a more conscientious reader, writer, and thinker. Take this class!!!
