I would not recommend this course unless you have to take it for Social Studies. Too much material is squeezed into too little of a time period. Lectures range from good to bad, mostly bad. There's absolutely no reason to have 1 two hour lecture a week, and the class would greatly benefit from having two hour long lectures instead. Section is engaging, but way too long. It is exhausting, and not because it's so rigorous, but because it is too long. There's not enough structure given to Tutorials and they can range wildly in quality. The most important thing, however, is just the sheer amount of material covered. Reading a book a week works when it's Hum10, and you're working with literature. You can understand Frankenstein, mostly, in a week. You cannot understand Hegel in a week. It means that you come out with a very surface level understanding of most thinkers, except for those you choose to write about. Even then, you read so little of their theory that, save three or four thinkers, you feel as though you're not even engaging with them as thinkers. The environment of the course is also incredibly toxic, with just how much pressure they put upon you to concentrate in Social Studies. Every lecture includes 5–10 minutes about why you should concentrate in social studies, and you face actual legitimate pressure to concentrate, which is kind of unacceptable in my opinion. I often legitimately felt ostracized as someone who decided half way through that I wasn't going to concentrate. This course has the opportunity to be an incredible course, but is not there yet.
