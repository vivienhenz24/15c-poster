Take this class!! Amie and Maddie are amazing; you'll learn a lot of readily applicable stuff, like how to design experiments. I took this as a non–bio concentrator and it was a blast. The take–home exams are initially difficult to adjust to; I recommend going to more office hours than you think to really strive for high accuracy on them, but all are doable and there's office hours almost everyday. Plan for it to be not necessarily hard, but taking up a substantial part of your week (things due Monday, Wednesday, Friday). Definitely recommend getting the same lab group as your friends (!!!!); it makes the course exponentially more enjoyable!
Start assessments, assignments, and anything they give you early! The pre–lecture notes are also gold for the exams. Nothing should be memorized thought, seriously. Use that time for other things.
SCRB50 is literally my favorite class I have taken at Harvard thus far. It's not super easy, but it's miles better than SCRB10. It's 4 assessments, a final lab report, a final research proposal, and weekly deliverables for the final papers sprinkled in as well as weekly pre–lecture videos/ pre–lab quizzes. Mondays are lecture, Wednesday you do some real world application to what you learn, and Friday is optional. Lab is actually relevant to what you learn in lecture and you learn all kinds of lab techniques. There are SO many opportunities to get help in the course, especially on the assessments, for such a small class, although do not expect to get assessment answers at these office hours. Work with your classmates as much as you can, collaboration and communication is key.

Great class! Not super easy, but totally manageable and way better than MCB60. You will get good practice with scientific writing. You get to do a 10 week long regeneration experiment with planaria. There are 4 assessments that are 75% a take home pset and 25% a timed test portion that builds off the take home portion. It is generally pretty low stress, and if you put in the effort, then it will be a great experience. Love Amie and Maddie!!
SCRB 50 is a great class. If you took SCRB 10, the content and question style is similar, though there is a wider variety of lecturers and a greater focus on breaking into small groups. Amie, who does most of the lectures, is incredibly engaging and encourages us to ask and answer questions. Rather than psets or in–class exams, there are four collaborative take–home assessments (with a small timed individual portion). They are very similar to SCRB 10 psets and definitely require office hours. The assessments are not easy and require significant time and collaboration. However, I really enjoyed them and the collaborative aspect brought me closer to my classmates, so I much preferred them to in–class exams. The lab component is really fun. Rather than several separate experiments, we worked on a longer project over the whole semester to knock down a gene in planaria and observe changes (in my case by doing in–situ hybridization). It was a lot of fun and a great application of what we learned in class. However, I would avoid a 6–9 pm lab section if you can, as I was always exhausted by lab time which made me enjoy it less.
Definitely recommend! I come from a weak biology background and still managed to do well
As someone concentrating in HDRB, though this course is required, I really enjoyed getting to learn a lot of this material. This class was as a great introduction to essential lab techniques and core concepts crucial for anyone stepping into the science field and engaging with scientific literature. The course staff is incredibly supportive and genuinely invested in seeing you succeed. The lab component also offers great hands–on experience with various techniques, which is particularly beneficial if you're considering a future in research. Personally, I found this class to be very enjoyable and enriching. If you're interested on exploring biology and gaining practical skills for your scientific journey, SCRB50 is definitely worth considering whether you're HDRB or not.

SCRB 50 was so refreshing after SCRB 10. The class also teaches experimental design with a microbiology focus, and the class is structured into lectures, application sessions, and skill–building sessions. There are 4 exams throughout the semester, but they are so doable with attending office hours.
Amie is an excellent lecturer. Nuria is the sweetest lab section TF. Celine and Kaylee are also great TFs that are very knowledgable about their specific fields. Please use teaching staff as resources when writing your research proposal!

FAVORITE CLASS AT HARVARD. It is not an easy class, but if you really put in the work (do the assignments on time, attend office hours, and participate in class) you will do amazing.
