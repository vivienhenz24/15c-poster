TAKE THIS SEMINAR, MAKE TAKING IT YOUR FIRST PRIORITY FRESHMAN FALL, YOU WON'T REGRET IT. This class was the absolute academic highlight of my freshman fall. Staying two nights at the forest once a month was such a cool and unique experience, and I bonded so much with everyone else in the class. There is a lot being thrown at you on the class days, but is balanced out nicely by how much fun you are having. Dave is an excellent teacher and truly like a father figure to everyone in the class. He makes the experience so special. You also get to have only 3 classes the rest of the month, since this class doesn't meet regularly, which takes a HUGE load off academically and is a nice way to get adjusted. The homework is minimal. There is reading to do before each weekend. The only submitted work (aside from the final paper) is to submit a short writeup answering a couple questions, due 1 week after each trip. These are very low–stakes, chill assignments that don't take long, and you don't have any submitted homework at all the rest of the month. The final paper was legit but manageable and we had a lot of time to work on it. The forest was absolutely beautiful, the stars were incredible, and it was so cool to get to go around and explore nature – great way to escape the Cambridge bubble and get outside. The accomodations are truly outstanding as well – you live in a huge house with tons of beds, tons of space, nice bathrooms and showers, an active kitchen, and tons and tons of free food and drink that is purchased for you to snack on. You really feel like it is your home and you have total free reign to live in the space. You also learn a lot over the course of this class! Getting off campus 1 weekend per month is really not a huge commitment and is honestly really good for you – I looked forward to those weekends more than anything. It felt like an escape from the stress of Harvard life and into a whole separate world. In conclusion – this is literally, like, the best class ever conceived. It is such a cool, unique, interactive learning experience that you will never get again in your time at Harvard. You only have one semester where you can try to take it – don't miss it for the world!
TAKE THIS CLASS! It is an outstanding way to find community and friends in your first year at Harvard. You will also learn so many things beyond the book about nature and the environment. The hands on learning and real world applications of this course are unparalleled.
I'm planning on concentrating in Environmental Science and Public Policy. I took this course because of the course format, which is up to four–weekend trips to the Harvard Forest. We are also so much cooler than Tree. Instead of hugging trees, we identify forest species and how we can take action to conserve them. This is an incredibly cool course if you're interested in environmental science or anything relating to the trees and life that surround you.

One brief note: while the coursework itself is very light, the weekend trips may cause stress for the weeks before and after the trip. You will not have a lot of time to complete your other obligations. I highly recommend you plan your week ahead and manage all your commitments during forest weeks. Other than that, it's an incredible class!
This class was amazing. I highly recommend it! The professor is very intelligent and kind. The experience is once in a lifetime. I had so much fun, learned a lot, met incredible people, and explored one of my interests. The coursework was interesting without being too overwhelming. The hands on experience was amazing. I loved hearing from many experts in this field. If you are interested in the environment and global change, you should take this class!
TAKE THIS CLASS (AND RANK IT #1 IF YOU ACTUALLY WANT IT)This class truly changed the way I interacted with nature, caused me to be more introspective and appreciative, and helped me interact with new people. Being put in a group of 10 other people and getting to share this amazing experience with them really allows you to bond with and appreciate people of different backgrounds that you likely wouldn't do otherwise. This class has made me more appreciative of both nature and people.

I think a really great strength of this course is how it utilizes an outdoor classroom. It doesn't even feel like a traditional class most of the time because instead of being cooped up in a stuffy classroom, we're in the forest, hiking, exploring, and identifying the processes and organisms we talk and read about. The opportunity to spend a weekend in a house, eating great food, with cool and interesting people, and explore the forest is amazing!

Also, no background is necessary. Sure an environmental or climate background could be helpful (most students had this background), but I was one of the few with little to no background and I was fine!

Keep in mind that you will have to give up one weekend per month, so if you have extracurriculars where you need the weekends this might not be for you. But, if you have a few weekends to spare, you won't be disappointed!
This class is such a unique opportunity. If you are interested in spending time outdoors and talking about global change (climate change, invasive species, deforestation, etc.) then this is the perfect Freshman Seminar for you. One thing to be aware of is that the weekend experiences are very socially intense: the class does everything together and if you are an introvert like me, you might find yourself wishing you had time alone. I think I would have enjoyed the weekend trips more if I had been more deliberate about finding time for sleep/homework/breaks from people, so that is something you might want to think about as you go into the course.
