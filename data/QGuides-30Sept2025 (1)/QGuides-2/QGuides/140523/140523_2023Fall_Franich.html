Comments
If you're interested in exploring the intersection of linguistics with a sociological phenomenon, this is the perfect class for you. Even if you have minimal prior research experience (as I did), the teaching team will offer as much guidance as you need to be successful.
Take it to balance out a busy semester
This really is a fantastic class. It's minimal work, but facilitates really engaging and thought–provoking discussions. The final project is super lenient and the teaching staff is really receptive to students' ideas.
Sociolinguistics is a super cool field, and this class is awesome! You learn so much about so many different interesting dimensions of speech and identity, and it's really discussion–centric, we read 2–3 different sociolinguistics papers each week. Prof. Franich and the TFs are all really great. The semester–long project can be somewhat time–consuming (start early!!!!), especially when it comes to doing the data analysis because there's hours of recorded interviews to go through, but it's a very interesting process and overall a very enjoyable class.
This is the best linguistics course I've taken! It's much more accessible than a lot of the other 100s level courses, and doesn't require anything more than an interest in the subject. Prof Franich is amazing, and the class always went by quickly. The final project allows for a lot of freedom, and since it's something you work on all semester it doesn't feel overwhelming to finish during finals period. I'm so glad the linguistics department finally has a sociolinguistics class and I hope it stays around for many years! Take this class!!!
