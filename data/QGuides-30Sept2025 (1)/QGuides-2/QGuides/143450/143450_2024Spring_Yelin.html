Let me preface this by saying I generally like statistical mechanics. You learn some really cool concepts in this class; I came away with a much deeper and more thorough understanding of how energy works in physics and chemistry, which I feel is often handwaved in lower physics. That said, this class was a bit disorganized. Professor Yelin's lectures are enthusiastic but very hard to follow; the PSETs mostly relied on algebra tricks instead of building physical intuition, so I felt like I wasn't learning much from the time I spent. (Even some of the TFs were surprised at times by the questions that were on the PSETs, as if they weren't sure what was being covered in the class.) The take–home midterm was hard but fair. Most of your grade is made up of PSETs with an additional boost from completion–based quizzes, so it's not impossible to do well, but I do feel like my grade doesn't really reflect my proficiency. Going to section or office hours to build conceptual understanding wasn't really necessary for me to do well on the PSETs, but I had such a headache every week over the PSET (math isn't my strongest suit, plus my general courseload was pretty heavy) that I never had time to go to section or office hours, so as a result I'm exiting this class with what I feel are deficits in my conceptual understanding. I hope next year's class is a little more conceptual and includes a little more application.
Be careful about the professor.
