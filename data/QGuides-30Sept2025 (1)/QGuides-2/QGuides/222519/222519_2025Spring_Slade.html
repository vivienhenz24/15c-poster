This was a great class! I definitely appreciated that this class was offered, it fills the gap between wet lab heavy BioE courses and mech. I also appreciated that there was a final project as not a lot of BioE have that. The course itself was very well structured, and the staff are so knowledgeable, helpful, and kind. Patrick brings so much enthusiasm for the material and really makes the classroom feel like a safe learning space. Highly recommend!
This class is great. Professor Slade is awesome, he's so kind and always willing to take feedback. Sometimes the course can feel like a lot, with a bunch of assignments all due in a week, but the assignments are not super hard if you go to Office Hours. Overall great course, would recommend.
Patrick is an great teacher who gives engaging lectures.
This is a great class. It can be quite time consuming but the course content is interesting and the final project is fun
This is a great course. It's a really good opportunity to learn about the field of biomechanics, with Prof Patrick Slade, who's a fun, down to earth, and really invested in everyone's learning. It also has a good opportunity to experiment and build something of your own, fairly rudimentary but nonetheless a good way to do some actual building. It's less on the hardware and more on understanding human motion and human augmentation and touches on exoskeletons but not necessarily in the sci–fi way (the field is not that evolved yet). Regardless, it is a bit intense on the workload, but manageable, and a good experience. Great to do with friends, but also great to do solo and learn from others.
This class is probably the best BioE elective. Professor Slade is so enthusiastic, and very good at explaining difficult topics. You learn a lot in the first half, and focus more on the projects in the latter half. There is only one test (a two–day midterm) which is nice, but there can be a lot of busywork. Overall, it is a very fun course and the teaching staff is lovely! Would heavily recommend.
