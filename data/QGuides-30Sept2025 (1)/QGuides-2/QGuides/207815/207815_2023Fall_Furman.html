100% apply for this seminar, this was my favorite class this semester. I thought one book a week was going to be a ton of reading, but found them very stimulating and interesting. The seminar is also a low–stress environment and is chill with the workload as there are no major papers. I enjoyed class discussions mainly the fact that the class is split between people on the right and left, generating much more debate than what you would usually get. It's an opportunity to learn about topics that really matter in today's world and discuss them with one of the world's leading economists. Highly recommend.
This class is mainly a discussion–based class on various economic and social issues, and Jason has selected many interesting and thought–provoking readings to help guide our discussions. Jason is very enthusiastic and an expert on these topics, and he provides very insightful comments and helps guide our discussions. This course will give you a fundamental understanding of many of these issues and various perspectives towards them. Don't expect to find a definitive answer to them, though, do expect to challenge your prior assumptions about these issues and think critically. The op–eds, letters to the editor, and class discussions are very lively. Highly recommend this course to anyone interested in learning more about economics.
It's a good course! You don't necessarily have to do the readings, but I'd recommend doing most of them since they're interesting and make discussing the books (which is what the course is all about) much more fun. I'd also recommend not feeling intimidated to talk with Jason and reach out to set up meetings. It's a great opportunity to get to hear from and talk with an expert in public policy. It's not too much work at all. A book a week can seem like a lot, but they went quick for me and you really don't have to write much at all.
The course provided an excellent grounding in economics, facilitated by thorough readings. Furthermore, the horizon–expanding insights gained from vibrant class discussions with both the professor and classmates significantly enriched the overall experience.
This seminar is a great opportunity to engage with a wide variety of economic issues! Jason really cares about picking readings that are interesting and relevant. He is also very accessible and a wonderful person to learn from. A book per week is a lot of reading but it's worth it to read the books and keep up so that you can engage more meaningfully in discussions. I think the seminar discussion setting can be a little intimidating at first but once you get to know your classmates, it really gets easier. Overall, I enjoyed my time in this seminar!
