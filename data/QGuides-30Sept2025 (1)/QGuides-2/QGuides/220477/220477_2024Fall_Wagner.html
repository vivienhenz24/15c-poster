TAKE THIS CLASS!!!! Dr. Wagner is an excellent instructor and you learn so much from the readings and the discussion. This was hands down my favorite class of the semester and my favorite course I've taken in social studies.
This class is my favorite class Ive taken at Harvard. Rosie is a phenomenal instructor, wonderful to have discussions with, and such a nice person. You will get out of the course what you put in, but it is definitely worth every ounce of work to give it your all. Rosie is fair and will push you just the right amount, but youll never feel super concerned about your grade, which leaves all the mental energy to learning and growing. Wonderful junior tutorial.
This was a great junior tutorial for everyone who is interested in social movements and activism. I would say that it was slightly less theoretical than other tutorials, but instead you get to learn a more about various movements and ongoing issues in the world and there is still a good amount of theory either explicitly or that comes through in the other readings. Rosie is a great tutorial leader and such a good teacher, so I would highly recommend taking this class with her. You can tell that she really enjoys teaching this class, and it makes for a great classroom atmosphere.
Take this class! Dr. Wagner is so bright, engages with their students' thoughts and work in such a deliberate way, and facilitates class meetings that are engaging and generative. The course takes you through philosophical complications and conceptions of "solidarity," as well as several compelling case studies of solidarity in action. If you're looking to cover the kinds of topics you might be talking about with your friends in organizing spaces that few Harvard classes give their due academic consideration, this is the course for you. As a class, we discussed mutual aid, the role of love and friendship in organizing, Land Back, the Black Panthers and the Rainbow Coalition, disability justice, and much more. For me, this course was a grab bag of theories and real–world movements I care about and/or wanted to learn about in more depth, so it was a really wonderful academic experience.
Rosie is a wonderful instructor and very supportive. She facilitates great discussions. A lot of topics covered in the course, but the readings are great. Definitely go to office hours and talk about your projects multiple times over the semester to keep yourself on track.
Great course! Especially, if you are struggling to understand why are you learning social theory.
