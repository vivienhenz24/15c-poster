Great class. Dr. Baker is really engaging and really nice. Material is interesting ––– cool crossover between the sciences and the humanities. Really good option for humanities kids looking to fulfill the social science divisional distribution.
Take this class if you are a scientist and would like to think about communication and societal perceptions of science. It is not too much work, essays are interesting to write and the discussions are very engaging and constructive.
it's fine. the professor is cool and some of the information is interesting. you spend a lot of time doing group discussions so maybe take it with friends.
Erik is such a caring lecturer, and makes himself readily available to meet outside of class! I especially appreciated the (prompt) flexibility of all three essay assignments. I feel like I learned a great deal about science in popular culture from this class, though I wish people were more engaged during seminar (which only met once a week). Overall, I enjoyed this class, and would recommend to future students (esp if this becomes a smaller seminar–style class)!
Professor Baker is so sweet and knowledgeable. I really enjoyed this class and his enthusiasm for it. The assignments were very fairly graded and the readings often were interesting. This class makes me want to take more histsci seminars
I loved this class! The topics are incredibly interesting. It really made me view popular culture through a different lens. TAKE THIS CLASS!!!!!!!!
Take it!*
If you want to take a laidback history of science course, or if you're considering history of science as a concentration, this class is super fun and engaging! I'd recommend it, especially if you loved watching silly Billy Nye science–type videos growing up. If you're not as interested in popular science, or even the history of science and all its complexities, I would suggest digging deeper into the course material because it can be quite repetitive. Overall, amazing class and super dope professor who (you could tell) loves the class and the student discussions!
