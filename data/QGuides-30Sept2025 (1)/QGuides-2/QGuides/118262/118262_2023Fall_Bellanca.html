I would say take this class with reservations. I regret not taking Expos 20 and getting the requirement over with in one semester. Expos 10 is helpful in that it gives a lot of feedback on your essays, but I think with the writing center and expos 20 having one pre draft conference, you should be fine in Expos 20.
If you are on the boarder of Expos 10 or Expos 20, take Expos 10. I was worried I should not "waste" a semester taking Expos twice, especially seeing I am not concentrating in anything too writing related. However, I really feel like I gained a lot from this class. The discussions are interesting and the course is not too intense. You get a good group of people to work with and get to become a better writer.
If you need a more gradual push into college writing, this is a helpful class to take before EXPOS 20.
Pat is an amazing professor and you should take expos with her! She is so engaged and positive and makes it fun to write and read, even if you don't typically like to do those things! She is not a harsh grader and really works with you to make you the best writer you can be! She is also very understanding and tries to be as flexible as she can be!
If you know you are a good writer go straight to Expos 20.
Pat is a great preceptor. Highly recommend taking expos 10 with her.
If you are a bit unsure on your strengths as a writer, you need to take this course. Expos studio 10 will give you the tools to write clearly and effectively.
