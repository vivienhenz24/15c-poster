I highly recommend taking expos studio 10 if you are recommended for it as it helps prepare you to write papers not only in standard expos 20 but GENED classes and other classes at Harvard. If you take expos studio 10 I HIGHLY RECOMMEND taking it with Taleen Mardirossian, she is a PHENOMENAL professor who takes time to teach you how to think critically when writing a paper but she also takes time to check–in how you are doing in other classes and just your general well–being. I never fear asking any "stupid" questions in this class but most importantly Taleen is able to facilitate a fun classroom environment where you are able to become close with all your classmates by the end of the semester. I am truly sad that I won't have her again, but am grateful for the impact she has left in my educational career.
I loved this course and I always looked forward to going to my classes. It doesn’t eat that much of your time and it definitely helped me build a strong base for my essays. If you are not confident in your writing, there are many very interesting aspects you’ll learn about here that will certainly improve your writing, analyzing skills and confidence overall. It’s the end of the term and I never once regretted enrolling in this class. Take what I say with a grain of salt though. Everyone’s experience depends a lot on the professor they have. Please please do your research, I know there are some professors out there that make expos 10 look like a non enjoyable class. Taleen is a gem of a professor and person. I recommend her full heartedly and I can say she is genuine in helping all of us have a rewarding experience. The feedback she gives is amazing and helpful, she brings up amazing points in class that left me speechless so many times. I say expos is what you make of it but to me it was one of my favorite classes with one of the best professors out there. 10/10 recommend.
Start directly at Expos 20 if you can manage it. The Expos 10 class is fun and the pace is very relaxed, but you do not learn a lot of things and the topics are quite boring.
Take this class if you are not 100% sure of your writing skills. This class is fun and the workload is very much doable. Taleen is the best!! Do your readings and come prepared. Use the draft and conference as an opportunity to get comments and enhance your draft as you can.
If you are worried about taking Expos 20, but find that the year–long course of Expos 10 would put you back, do not worry about that. Moreover, if you find an Expos20 course you find interesting but are not confident that you would be able to get everything you wanted out of the class, consider taking Expos 10 as you will have the opportunity to switch to that Expos 20 in the spring after the fall semester.
If you feel any hesitation about taking Expos 20 or feel like your writing can use some improvement, take Expos 10. I didn't have experience writing long papers in high school, and Expos 10 allows you to focus on different steps of the writing process that will help you improve your writing.
TAKE IT! Taking Expos 10 instead of Expos 20 was the greatest decision ever. It is very chill, and you sure don't want to be too stressed in your first semester. Taleen is the greatest person I have learned from so far. She has an immense passion for the work she does, and it is quite undeniable that it also motivates other students to be better in the course.
Expos 10 is good for learning basic writing skills, but I recommend taking 20 if you're on the fence. The work in 10 can be tedious and may seem kind of strange, and the units aren't incredibly interesting material–wise. Take a 20 course in something you like –– writing would be more enjoyable.
