As a non–WGS concentrator, I would *highly* recommend taking this course. Professor Albert has a unique grading system where students grade themselves based on their accomplishment of self–established semester goals. Not only was the subject matter engaging across a variety of topics, but I felt like I was constantly looking to improve my writing/analytical abilities given the grading structure. Kendra is also a wonderful, kind, and extremely knowledgeable professor to work with; even if it's not this course, I highly recommend taking a course of theirs when you get the chance!
One of the best classes you'll take at Harvard. It isn't easy, but the students are given so much control over their learning that it makes it all worth it. And Kendra is simply phenomenal.
Take this class! It is so incredibly well organized, thanks to our professor Kendra, from the weekly readings that are relevant/succinct/deeply engaging to the discussions during class that push us to consider broader implications/cross–work connections. Kendra does an amazing job making the class environment warm and welcoming, and their vast knowledge really helps discussions, office hours, and feedback insightful in a "blow–your–mind" way. I loved the structure of the class and how manageable it was during my senior spring, a combination of Kendra's pedagogical approach, the thoughtful readings, and assignments that actually feel constructive to your understanding of the material. I was actually excited to do all the readings and the assignments in this class, which is not something I can say about other classes I've taken at Harvard, which maybe goes to show just how enjoyable Kendra is able to make the very complex topic of technology, gender, and the theoretical/practical implications of them.

Take this class if you love engaging deeply with theory, drawing parallels between gender/technology and literally everything from labor to war, and actually learning about what is practically being done (aka "so–i've–learned–all–this–theory–what–do–i–do–now). Take this class if the thought of reading about tomatoes, short monarchs, or guns for girls interests you as much as reading about transhumanism, data violence, and representation/inclusion discourse. And definitely take this class if you're doing CS/going into tech (the readings are incredibly relevant to your future work), if you've never taken a WGS class before (I'm a newcomer), or if you just want to make concrete all the thoughts you've been having about the role of technology in your life (you'll do it from so many different angles and on a range from total theory to total application). And if Kendra is still teaching this class, definitely take it. They definitely make it a life–changing experience!
This is an absolutely brilliant class. Take this class if you've never taken anything in WGS (trust me), take this class if you don't know a single thing about technology, take this class if you want to remember what makes education worthwhile and rewarding.
