This course was a lot of fun Professor Capdevila–Werning is an expert in the subject, and it really shows. Its just about the perfect amount of work (although I took it for my junior tutorial req, so the intensity was scaled up a bit towards the end of the course), and class discussions were always engaging.
One of the coolest classes I have taken. Highly recommend
The readings and papers were challenging but I learned so much. I genuinely loved being in class and I feel like I look at buildings completely differently after not having a background in philosophy before that. So fascinating!
Topics are very interesting. Teaching staff is nice. You will do a lot of the learning on your own through reading the texts, which are difficult. Class is mostly for discussion and you rarely go over topics. Personally I would say if you are not a phil major with a decent number of other phil courses under your belt, be careful because this class definitely can feel like at times it is self–learning the material.
Amazing class. If you are really into architecture and philosophy both, you will love this class, if only one you might struggle with it. it is not an easy class but if you put in the work and try to understand the readings and engage in seminar you will come out thinking about architecture in a totally different way. Remei and Tristan are both great techers. Highly recommend.
Hard class, readings are monitored, long lectures, unclear feedback.
If you're genuinely interested in the content, then it's worth it to suffer through the annoying parts of class (presentations, dense readings that you have to leave public comments on). Be prepared to read difficult things and summarize them.
This course is great. Remei is incredible in explaining philosophical concepts and Tristan is a great TF who knows a lot about architecture. Discuss with them the papers in advance and they are useful in helping you organize your ideas for the papers. The class presentations are totally feasible and there is not much stress involved. Overall, the class is great and you get to learn a lot about philosophy and architecture. Another plus is the field trips, guest speakers and out–of–classroom classes going to archives, libraries, etc. It's also a good course to fulfill the Philosophy Junior Tutorial requirement.
