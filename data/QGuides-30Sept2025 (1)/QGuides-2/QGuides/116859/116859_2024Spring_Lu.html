Overall: ES 150 is an introduction to probability and statistics. The mathematics involved is quite simple (some simple integration and matrix multiplication, no proofs required), especially if you have seen combinatorics before. Even if you have not, you will quickly gain an intuition for it.

Professor: Lu Yue is an impressive lecturer, he lectures from memory without notes. He is quite thorough in explaining ideas and is very receptive to questions. He also has a great voice.

Textbooks: Not particularly necessary.
D. P. Bertsekas and J. N. Tsitsiklis, Introduction to Probability, 2nd Edition,
Athena Scientific, July 2008.

Topics covered:
Probability basics, discrete uniform probability law, sets, probability properties; conditional probability, total probability formula; Bayes Theorem, false positive/negative; event independence; counting, permutations, combinations, binomial coefficients, binomial distribution; discrete random variables (RV), probability mass function (PMF), Bernoulli/Binomial/Poisson/Geometric RVs; expectation and properties, Law Of The Unconscious Statistician (LOTUS), variance and properties; joint PMF, conditional PMF, linearity of expectation; independent RVs, covariance and correlation; binary hypothesis testing, probability of false alarm/miss, maximum likelihood (ML) decision rule, maximum a posteriori probability (MAP) decision rule, likelihood ratio test (LRT); continuous random variables, cumulative distribution function (CDF), probability density function (PDF), uniform/exponential/normal RVs; derived distributions; law of large numbers (LLN), central limit theorem (CLT); finite state Markov chains, Markov property, matrix formulation, path probabilities, recurrent/transient states; Markov chain steady state distributions, balance equation.

Course structure notes:
Weekly psets and fortnighty python computing labs. Two in–class open–book midterms and final. Lectures center around examples/procedures more than complex mathematics.

Absolutely take this class. It is the most well–taught STEM class I've taken at Harvard as a CS concentrator. It covers the same foundational material (and CS concentration requirement) as STAT 110 without as much pain. This is largely due to Professor Lu's excellent teaching and the very organized class structure. Lectures are no–fluff, clear, informative, and informal enough that everyone is comfortable asking questions.

The content itself is extremely applicable to any field (CS, any engineering application, reading papers, poker / gambling, finance). Every lecture, you walk out knowing something new, useful, and understand how you can use it in the real world. That's not something that happens much in most Harvard CS courses.

PSETs and labs are challenging, but very doable. They feel like puzzles in the optimal level of difficulty. Exams open note, very fair, and their content is clearly communicated before hand. The course notes on Overleaf are very comprehensive and a great way to review.

Lecture is the highlight of this course. I wish Professor Lu taught more courses. I found myself thinking, "wow if he taught some of my other courses, they would be so much better!"
