Take this class if you’re interested in an overview of Nietzsche’s philosophy, or just want to get a taste for the study of philosophy in general. The readings can be difficult to get through but rewarding nonetheless.
Take this course if you are interested in philosophy. If not, it's not worth taking this class only because you're somewhat interested in Nietzsche. His writing is difficult to understand and the readings are heavy each week (almost an entire book each week). Phil 3 or GENED 1025 could be better alternatives if you are interested in an intro–level philosophy course. However, Nietzsche (regardless of whether you like/agree with his philosophy or not) is an important figure in the field. So if you want a solid background in his works, this course is great. Also, don't take this course if you're just looking for an easy class since this class won't be nearly as beneficial or fulfilling if you don't do the readings.
Professor Risse is amazing, and the knowledge you will gain about Nietzsche and his writings is fascinating. The course load is very light, and as long as you do the readings, you will learn a lot from this class. Excellent first–year seminar!
Make sure you really are interested in Nietzsche, as that's what you'll be spending a lot of time reading and talking about. I personally got kind of bored of him. His writing style is very difficult, which didn't make the reading experience fun. But in–class discussions were fun! Prof. Risse is very knowledgeable and will challenge you. If you're hoping to learn about Nietzsche very deeply then this course is for you.
I would think carefully before taking this seminar. Yes, it isn't much work, but Nietzsche is a very difficult author and reading ten Nietzsche books over the semester will not be fun unless you really love him. I would only take this class if you know you really love Nietzsche. Otherwise, I think you are wasting your time.
Take this class as a good low stakes intro to philosophy (really leverage the pass fail aspects, you could spend 30mins a week or 10hrs a week on this class and still pass it) or as a way to get exposure to Nietzsche. Really try your best to do some of the readings because Nietzsche's works are fascinating but all in all it is just a good class to take to lighten your course load (as it should! its just a FYS).
Only having weekly writing logs is so clutch.
I encourage anyone who has never taken philosophy to try it!!
