What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If you care at all about poetry, do not take this class. It is the least rigorous, most confusing way to spend your time I can imagine. Even if you put in the effort, it likely won't pay off. But if you are looking to zone out and chill, it's not a bad idea
If you can take the class with Reginald Dwyane Betts, do it. He's funny asl. He's like Kanye's like–able long lost brother ( I mean this in a good way, Dwayne is very cool). Course load was easy and he didn't make his class toxic by making it the first priority and workload on everybody's to do list. Dwayne was a busy man (and so were we as students ) so it was like a well constructed and beneficial and chill poetry club that met once a week. We just talked about poems, hip hop, the 90's, basketball, and anything in between. The class itself felt long but in terms of hours a week this is the lowest time commitment you can go. 2:45 hours a week and thats it point blank period. No section. No nothing. Maybe write a few poems on the weekend, but thats extremely slight if you ask me. He wants so succeed and will give you a good grade as long as you show up and add to discussion. As long as you aint visibly taking naps and snoring in class while drooling on the professors bookbag, you will get a good grade, so def take if you like poems and chill workloads.
Only take this class for entertainment. If you're seriously looking to learn more about poetry and have a chance to get feedback on your work, you won't get what you're seeking here.
Dwayne is a bit unorganized and I feel that we did not have much time to workshop our poems and get feedback unless we signed up for additional time through office hours. I also wish that he paid more attention to each student he didn't really learn our names until the end of the semester and there were a few students he would mistake for each other/not remember from previous classes. I also wish we had learned more about the craft of a poem I think learning about literary devices (which we would spend at least an hour each class doing) is not as helpful as actually having to write poems in different forms/employ those devices in our own writing. However, I learned a lot about modern poetry and about what makes a good poem, and alternatively, what makes a bad poem. It's also always very helpful to be working with a practicing artist to see how they approach their craft and learn from their story.
Be excited, though I heard this won't run again :(
