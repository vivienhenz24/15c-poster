Note that the topic changes each year, but this year's (Fall 2023) was Program Synthesis. Normally you need CS 152 to take this class, but we were able to get through this year without it. Each class is a student presentation of a paper to read for that class. So, a lot of the learning will be done by discussing with and listening to your peers rather than a traditional lecture format. Nada is truly a gem and is very knowledgeable about the field of PL (programming languages) in general. A great component is the final project which I think is a great venue to get a good practical experience. Highly recommend this course, it's a great elective
Nada is an incredible, compassionate, and knowledgeable instructor. I strongly recommend CS252R.
Professor Amin facilitated the course really well and allowed each students' individual interests and strengths to show through. I learned a lot from other students in the class and was consistently amazed by their expertise in certain areas. The students selected many of the readings for the class, which meant that the student presentations were often enthusiastic and enjoyable. Professor Amin was also very flexible and accommodating, making it a low stress learning environment where students felt like they could experiment with new ideas.
shoutout Nada, what an absolute pleasure to work with and learn from
The class is very enjoyable and insightful. The multiple invited talks allow students to get first–hand insights on current program synthesis methods. For this class, you will get as much as you put in, so always start your homework early and read all recommended papers thoroughly.
This was a great experience as an undergraduate for a first introduction into graduate level courses. The main component of the course was taking turns presenting papers of personal interest in the subject area. I felt like it was very hit or miss with each presentation, as some papers were just really difficult or hard to turn into a short presentation.

The final project was great. A fairly open ended exploration into the subject area but with feedback from Prof Amin available whenever necessary.
Very good course, this semester was focused on program synthesis. Great for meeting new people and getting exposed to new research
