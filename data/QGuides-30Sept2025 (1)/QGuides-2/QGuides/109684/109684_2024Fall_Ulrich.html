What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class is fabulous! Patrick is an amazing professor who teaches effectively and is also very understanding and helpful. This is one of the first classes where I feel like I genuinely learned the materials and am walking away with new skills and knowledge that has changed my perspective on aquatic environmental engineering. This class is challenging, but very doable and Patrick is there to help every step of the way.
This is easily my favorite engineering class I've taken at Harvard so far. It truly feels like an environmental engineering class, rather than just an environmental science or engineering course. The material is challenging but rewarding. You get to apply fluid dynamics, mass balances, energy dynamics, thermodynamics, and more to fascinating water–quality problems. The course also aligns with current standard environmental methods, and legal frameworks are introduced as useful references. The lab component is stellar; it really makes you think critically about water and how its managed. I also appreciated the community engagement aspectvisiting the Alewife Brook as an example of a stormwater basin was genuinely inspiring. Patrick is an incredibly kind and talented lecturer who is also very accessible to discuss the class with outside class time too. People make effort to show up to class for good reason and my classmates were also a wonderful supportive resource too. Take this class for good vibes and to learn tangible skills relevant to water engineering!!!
TAKE THIS CLASS! Hands down the best ESE class I have taken at Harvard. Patrick is an incredible teacher, lecturer, advisor, and overall resource. He communicates so effectively and strikes a perfect balance of holistic knowledge and background and actually introducing you to the skills you need to solve problems (problem sets but ALSO real world engineering problems). More mathematically challening than other ESE courses, but the first one where I actually got to design something and felt like I was actually learning how to engineer. And also incredibly fun. I loved this class.
It would be hard to overstate how much I would recommend this course to any ESE concentrator! Prof. Ulrich's passion for the subject matter shone through in every aspect of the course and really helped bring the material to life – lectures were always highly engaging and the content represented a perfect mix between engineering theory, applied case studies, and explorations of real–world environmental engineering methods/regulations. Furthermore, labs were great fun (especially when field sampling was involved), and the final project was certainly a course highlight as well, serving as an enjoyable and highly applied/field–oriented capstone to the content. The workload was also quite manageable throughout, and while problem sets could be challenging at times, the assignments did genuinely help to increase learning/understanding – my only recommendation here would be to take very careful notes in lecture, as the density of content can be rather high and details are important. Overall, though, the course was simply a great experience from start to finish – do try and take it if you can.
