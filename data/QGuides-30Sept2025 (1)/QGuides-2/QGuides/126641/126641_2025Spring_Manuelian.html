The exams really do require you to memorize an enormous amount of content, but it can be crammed pretty easily within a couple days. That's what I did at least and it worked out pretty well, but I also am good at memorizing things so mileage may vary. Honestly, the content is really cool and it will make you really want to visit Egypt and appreciate the insanely long and rich history of this civilization. I had a pretty positive experience and would take it again. The weekly Perusall readings and 2–sentence lecture responses sound a lot more annoying than they actually are. The workload really isn't so bad outside of preparing for the midterm and final exams.

TLDR: It's an interesting course with a manageable workload, but does require a fair bit of memorization for exams. Very nice and enthusiastic course staff, cool museum components, and a unique offering in an otherwise bland GENED category

PLEASE PLEASE PLEASE TAKE THIS COURSE!! This course changed my life. I never knew I would be so fascinated with Egypt, but lo and behold, here I am. Grace, Nisha, and Jake are fantastic, and collectively, they possess a wealth of knowledge about Egyptology as well. Despite there being some weekly assignments, they are still so incredibly interesting. This has been, by far, the best class I have ever taken at Harvard, and it has me questioning whether I want to pursue Egyptology. I'm not even a history concentrator, but this class has me second–guessing my concentration and secondary. Please take this class. I couldn't recommend it enough.
