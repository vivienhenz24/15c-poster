This was a really interesting class – if you're into sports, this class introduces a lot of concepts in the world of sports statistics. It is a lot more theoretical than applied however, and you definitely need some statistical background in order to take it (STAT 110, 111, and also 139 would be helpful). The class goes through a lot of different sports (soccer, basketball, football, baseball, fishing, chess, rugby, etc.), and the final project is pretty open ended and allows you to pick whatever you're interested in looking at. Glickman (with his publication on GLICKO) is pretty prominent in the field so its amazing to learn from him.
Great Stats class. Mark is a great lecturer and the materials were all good. PSETs were graded quite harshly (especially for interpretation questions), so just be aware of that. But overall really fun and you learn a lot about GLMs in the process.
This class is great! The topics (mainly team and player ratings and strength/ability estimations) are interesting and well–presented, the course staff is helpful, the problem sets are very instructive and challenging enough without being overwhelming, and Prof. Glickman clearly has a passion for and (very) deep knowledge of the material. Any complaints or hurdles associated with this course are few and will likely be resolved, as this is its first iteration in some years and some initial difficulties were to be expected. Definitely take if youre interested in the area, whether career–wise or just as a side interest!
Great course to learn but frustrating with the grading.

The grading is on the harsher side with many small point deductions that add up.

Unfortunately, I think a lot of time is spent covering simple examples –– which should be left to section. The course could have covered many more topics which would have made the course way better. However, currently the course only covered 80% of the planned topics, all of which revolve around rating system. As a student I would prefer the course to cover non rating system topics in sports analytics as well.

The direction for the final paper is not very clear –– leading to points deduction for misinterpretation of the final assignment. The coursestaff should give a concrete example to align expectation.
This class was fun and interesting! The work is perhaps not what one may expect when they hear "sports analytics", as we focused more on comparing team strengths based on results rather than things like biometric data or play–by–play analysis. However, the lectures are engaging and the content is well–explained and well–motivated
Really good Stat course, just make sure to go to OH! The homework can be tricky.
I loved this course and it makes me want to pursue a career in sports analytics. The class was much harder than the last Q guide suggested. The professor only had Zoom office hours which made it hard to get to know him. The psets were very difficult.
On the easier side of Stats class, very good teaching.
This is course is very heavily on statistical methods to analyze game outcomes (e.g. scores, win/loss) It is a not a course for player fatigue analysis, team analytics, or application in sports like VAR if that is what you are looking for
Mark is such a great lecturer and made this one of the best courses I've taken at Harvard. You can ask any question and he will take the time to make sure you understand, even if it is about basic concepts. The homeworks were a good mix of theory and applications, and the teaching staff offered lots of office hours to ask questions (necessary because the homeworks were pretty hard). I would highly recommend this course, just know you'll be dedicating a good amount of time to homework every 2 weeks.
Take it if you like sports.
This is a great course, especially as an upperclassman statistics concentrator. 110, 111, and 139 make the mechanical component of the material very digestible, but it is definitely possible to do the course without all three of these. Mark is a great lecturer, Peter is a great TF, and the material is just in general interesting (even if you're not the most interested in sports). Assignments can be difficult because of the computation, but overall not too stressful.
