Especially if you're in the history department, try to take at least one course with her before you graduate. It is really the kind of course that Harvard should be all about.
Great class! Lengthy readings though they are very interesting.
Professor Miles is the best. She is so passionate and knowledgable about the course. She is so incredibly wholesome! Saffron was also amazing. Saffron always made the best baked goods for our course. it felt like being at homes because of the comfortable vibes. The assignments are very manageable!
Really amazing class with great people and prof, but definitely very humanities and you really need to love primary source work and the time period to get something out of the class. If that sounds like you, then you'll love it.
This was an incredibly rewarding course. Professor Tiya Miles and Saffron are as kind as they are knowledgeable and passionate about histories of American slavery. The readings were eye–opening, and our visits to the Schlesinger Library to handle archival documents were unlike anything Ive got to experience in an undergraduate course. One of the best parts of this class was meeting the Harriet Jacobs Legacy Committee and learning about Cambridges ties to abolitionism. For the highest–quality intellectual and personal journey, I recommend this course wholeheartedly.
This course is amazing and very informative. The reading load is very heavy, though. The thought diaries are low stakes, but still matter for your grade. If you are not ready to read novels weekly, don't take the course. But also, do not let the amount of reading deter you from the amazing works we will uncover. It truly is a privilege to have such knowledge at our fingertips.
