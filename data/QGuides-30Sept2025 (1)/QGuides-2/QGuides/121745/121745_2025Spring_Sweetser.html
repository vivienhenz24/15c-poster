For any individuals who want to pursue a role within the broader medical field, but have never had the opportunity to work in a clinical setting, this class provides an incredible opportunity to understand a variety of genetic diseases through the patient's lens.
Very nice class, great professor who cares about your success in the classroom and beyond. Dr. Sweetser even connected me with one of his colleagues who works in a field that I am interested in.
–
This class is great! There is a different genetic disease as the topic each week, and each session includes a clinical laboratory researcher, a doctor with a specialty in treating this specific disease, and 85% also include a patient presentation. Having multiple different perspectives is a great way to learn about these diseases, and the patient presentation in particular is really the highlight of the course. Dr. Sweetser is very understand that we are busy students (there's a good mix of undergrads, grads/PhDs, and students in professional programs at MGH like the dental program), and so the only assignments are to read a weekly paper, and once during the semester, you will present the paper to the class in a "journal club." I would definitely recommend this course to other undergrads, especially SCRB concentrators!
This class, like many at Harvard, will give you as much as you put into it. You can put in the minimal effort and not read the articles or play games during class, but you are only cheating yourself. This is a truly incredible class, and I would urge everyone who has the chance to take it to truly really in this course. Even if you dont do the readings every week, try to pay attention during class.
This is a class worth giving up your Friday afternoon for! All of the patients are so interesting to learn from and the presenters are engaging and provide excellent background to understand the disease discussed each week. For somebody pre–med, it's great to have all parts combined here: an introduction to the illness, the scientific background and current research, and patient experience. I think it's a great to see the application of all the science and research we spend so much time learning about
Please take this class! It is basically a survey of a whole bunch of genetic diseases per class. A PhD, MD, and patient (best part) come in each time and talk about their experiences working with the condition and what it might mean for their practice/their life. It's a delight to hear from all of these experts, and the course organizer gets fresh fruit and snacks every class. I've learned so much about how genetic diseases affect people's lives in ways you've never thought of before. There is basically only one assignment per semester, and it is a group journal club presentation. If you put the effort in and are proactive about group work, you'll do great! The classes are at a lecture hall in MGH, and the course pays for your Ubers there (not on the way back)
You will LOVE it. Dont let the fact that its at the Simches center scare you! Its always worth it and is the highlight of my week. Youll meet so many amazing people, and learn in a way that you likely wont in any of your other Harvard courses. If I could erase my memory and take this class over again, I would, because the experience was just incredible.
TAKE THIS CLASS. This class is truly one–of–a–kind and will expand your knowledge of genetic diseases a great deal. You will learn about all of the great experimental strategies that clinical researchers are deploying to address conditions like Huntington's Disease, X–linked adenoleukodystrophy, and Alzheimer's. As an undergraduate, getting to attend a class at MGH and hear from clinicians and scientists was a great treat. I would strongly encourage undergrads who did well in LS1B (and enjoyed it!) to take this class.

This is a fantastic class. Each week we focus on a specific genetic disease and get to hear from researchers and physicians studying that disease, as well as a patient with the disease. It was a unique class experience that I highly recommend. The workload is pretty light (a few readings each week, a journal club presentation, and weekly feedback forms), and the assigned papers help you engage with the content. Some of the readings were hard to follow when reading them before class, but once you actually learned about the disease in class, everything fit together.
A well structured course and I enjoyed it so much!
Future students should know that this class offers a clear and insightful introduction to how genetics is applied in clinical practice. The faculty speakers and Dr. Sweetser break down complex topics into understandable parts, making it an excellent course for anyone interested in the intersection of genetics and medicine. Be prepared to engage with both scientific and clinical perspectivesit's a rewarding experience.
