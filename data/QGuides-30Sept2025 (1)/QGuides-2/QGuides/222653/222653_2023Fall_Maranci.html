Take this class!
Take this class if you can! It is rare to take a class with such a supportive, kind, and sharp professor. Professor Maranci provides cookies and tea each class and provides fantastic support for assignments. The readings and images are incredibly interesting, and the course is overall a great way to learn about Armenian textile culture and also how the field of art history looks regarding objects that are typically understudied.
I absolutely loved this course. Christina is an amazing professor. She is super knowledgable its insane, and a giant in her field yet so humble, kind, warm and considerate. The course material is super balanced so that most of the semester you are doing readings (which are so reasonable length–wise), a presentation of one of the readings with one other person, a final presentation and the final paper. I recommend start early with the final paper which is what I did and it wasnt stressful at all. Christina brings cookies and has tea every class which is actually the best. I looked forward to every class and time flies when youre talking about meaningful Armenian liturgical textiles even if it doesnt seem that way at first !

For context, i am a Neuro major (never had taken a history or art history class before) with a history secondary.
This is a great class for anyone even remotely interested in Armenian textiles. It's a great way to learn about a new topic in a relatively low–stress environment.
