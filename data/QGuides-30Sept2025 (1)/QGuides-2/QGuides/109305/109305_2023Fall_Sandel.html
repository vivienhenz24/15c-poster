Absolutely take this class. it gives you an intimate environment to discuss some of the most pressing questions facing the world at the moment. Professor Sandel is fantastic at facilitating discussion and asking questions to truly get to the root of our arguments. Each class is an opportunity to talk about a new challenge and how humans should attempt to face it. The paper at the end is a great way to explore your personal passion in relation to tech and ethics.
Absolutely take this course if you can. A wonderful intersection between technology and the sciences and philosophy and the humanities. Professor Sandel did a phenomenal job of facilitating insightful, robust discussions among students from diverse backgrounds and areas of study. The curation of readings for this course was also well done; everything was fascinating and challenging, and the final paper is tough but gives you a lot of leeway to dive deep into an area of interest. Even if you find yourself having little connection to AI, you’ll walk away with more interest than ever and a critical understanding of how technology is actively shaping the way we live as humans. Seriously, this course has positively changed my relationship with my electronic devices and also with my family. It’s a class I’ll always look back to from my Harvard career and would 110% take it again.
Any opportunity to learn from and engage with Prof. Sandel is a fantastic opportunity. He is a masterful facilitator of conversation and a renowned author and speaker, all while being very approachable. AI is an important and increasingly relevant subject; this course will present you with cutting–edge AI and tech applications and ask: is it worth it? Are we on the right track?
It is quite informal (only one graded assignment), which can be positive or negative (you need to motivate yourself to do the readings so that you'll be prepared come finals). I don't have anything negative to say. It is a great addition to a busy schedule; something not so heavy, but still enriching.
If you enjoy thoughtful discussion on technology ethics, this is the class for you! Expect to participate frequently and to have your ideas tested and challenged. You should also expect some ambiguity regarding your actual grade in the course.
This is a great course. The readings aren't difficult and the assignments are relatively easy. The discussion was extremely interesting. You have to be willing to speak in class in order to get anything out of this course – don't take it unless you are willing to participate. Everyone in class came prepared and engaged, which added to the experience. Highly recommend!
This is one of the BEST courses I have taken at Harvard. Sandel is an amazing professor, and the way he facilitates discussion is unmatched by any other professor. The readings are fun and light– many consist of videos and articles. The discussion component is so interesting because Sandel really finds a way to ignite discussion and get people who have diametrically opposed opinions (based on their discussion posts) to interact with eachother. I looked forward to this course each week and strongly recommend.
If this seminar is offered again, I would highly recommend taking it. The subject matter is extremely interesting and timely. Additionally, Sandel is an excellent facilitator of discussion and knows just when to ask a question that flips your whole perception of the topic. Perfect seminar for anyone interested in tech or philosophy, and the workload is manageable.
