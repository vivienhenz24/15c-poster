What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I would recommend that anyone who has completed Gov 1430/1433 take this course. I found it to be an extremely laid–back, fun way to round out the Tech Science curriculum.

In this class, you complete a research project of your own choosing. Meetings are every two weeks for the sole purpose of receiving/providing feedback. The teaching staff is really invested in your success and will help you gain access to any tools that you may need to produce a great project. Since my thesis is tech science–related, I was able to work on a chapter of it for this class, which was amazing.

Overall, this class is whatever you want it to be. For me, it was very low–stress and allowed me to pursue a project that I was genuinely interested in. It was also fun to contribute to my classmates' phenomenal projects over the course of the semester. 10/10!
Take it and the entire tech science track — it will be your best experience on campus!
A lot of great feedback from Leonie, and getting the chance to give feedback on other students' projects was a valuable experience. Switched to a biweekly meeting schedule with 1–1 discussion, which was good.
Absolutely take this class
If you're taking this, it's probably to finish your tech science program –– even so, I would definitely recommend! Make sure to pick a topic that will sustain your interest for a full semester and try to set aside time each week to work on the project, since it can be easy to lose track of time.
Take this class. It is extremely rewarding to work on your own project and see it culminate to a whole paper. The class will take self–discipline and drive to stay on top of your work, but what doesn't? There is always help from peers and the instructors and you can usually organize your work from week to week, having lighter workloads when you get extremely busy.
