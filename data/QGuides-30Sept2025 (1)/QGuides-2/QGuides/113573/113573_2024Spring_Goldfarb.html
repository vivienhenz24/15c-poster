Great course, shame that Goldfarb's retiring.
Took it because Goldfarb was lecturing (marginally also because it was on Wittgenstein). Truly a legend when it comes to Wittgenstein. Shame he has retired.
This course is amazing, Goldfarb is amazing, a real professional who knows a massive amount about the subject. At the same time, he is a whimsical, personable man – in this course, you'll get deep readings of the private language argument with a side course of gossip about Quine. With Goldfarb retiring, though, you will never get to take this course. Tough luck.
I took away profound insights about the nature of logic, language, and thinking. My views were challenged, and despite having no background in philosophy, I am grateful for having challenged myself and taken this really valuable and interesting course. I find myself thinking about it even now that classes are done relating things I learned to my everyday life, etc.
This course is fantastic! It's organized, the assignments are helpful for understanding the material, and Wittgenstein is a fascinating philosopher. You should take it.
