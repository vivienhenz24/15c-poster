THIS IS A GEM! For any history concentrator or anyone interested in the history of colonial Latin America, this course is excellent. Do not hesitate to take this course because you will not regret it.
good class to take in regard to assignments but not the most interesting material
A comprehensive and engaging survey of Colonial Latin American history. The course does a particularly good job of situating events in Latin America in a broader hemispheric and global context. Very interesting primary sources, which are discussed in each class meeting.
This is a course about colonial Latin America, but it's more so about how historians work and how to challenge traditional narratives about the period. For instance, you won't learn much about what Columbus did, but you will learn a ton about the epistemological debates about whether Columbus–type figures should even be centered at all. In this sense, I felt I learned a lot about how to think about colonial Latin America but didn't learn a ton of traditional facts about the era (you won't really learn about any kings, big dates/events, etc.). The course is structured thematically instead of chronologically or geographically, and Prof. Herzog brings in a lot of European history as a means to understand the period (which is interesting, since Prof. Herzog is also a scholar of Europe). The assignments are really good because they allow you to go through the historical process and aren't lengthy for the sake of more work. Kevin is one of the best TFs I've ever had and gives a lot of helpful feedback.
This course was really interesting and facilitated thoughtful discussions that challenged my assumptions and way of thinking. However, the course was not very focused on describing historical events and was much more thematic in its approach to colonial Latin America. I came out of it knowing a lot about general things that were going on and different legal matters but I can't really describe many political events that occurred for example. I don't think that's a bad thing but it is something to know if you are hoping to get more chronological and narrative information out of the course.
professor Herzog is great! engaging lectures, manageable readings and papers. Talking through all of it in section was also very helpful and fun
The course is very chill, and you really do learn a lot about the region of Latin America! Several assignments, but they're all short in length. Additionally, they all teach you different ways that historians write and study the subject, so the class is also useful in helping you decide if you want to major in history.
Great class for any student interested in the history of colonial Latin America, regardless of concentration. There is definitely no preparation or background necessary for this course. The assignments are manageable (weekly readings and 4, 3–page papers) and the course materials are very interesting (lectures, museum visits, readings). It is different from other history courses on the region in that it does not focus on the history within national borders, which is an interesting approach that not many courses take. Overall, a well–structured, interesting, and unique course offered by the history department!
If you want to begin to understand the current situation of many Latin American countries today, by looking at some of the events of the colonial times and the motivations of the institutions and the individuals back then, you might start putting the pieces together and see that the history of Colonial Latin America deeply affects the present
Interesting course –– lectures can be a bit dry, but the amount of actual reading + assignments are very reasonable. However, you will only learn if you engage with the readings and section, so if you are not interested in the material itself, then this course may not be worth your time. Kevin (the TF) was absolutely wonderful and Prof. Herzog is very knowledgable, so I'd definitely recommend seeking them out.
super fun, very different in terms of what you'll be learning (more Euro–centric than a lot of colonial LA classes you'll take otherwise), but really interesting in terms of the angles taken. Professor Herzog makes an effort to teach about the intricacies of daily life and events from the perspective of regular people with different roles in colonial society.
