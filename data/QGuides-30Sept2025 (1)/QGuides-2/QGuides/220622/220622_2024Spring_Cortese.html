I highly recommend Music 107 to all members of HRO. The assignments outside of scheduled rehearsals are very reasonable in scope and truly add to the experience of performing the music. It is an excellent way to gain a deeper understanding of the composer and music we play while also earning credit for HRO.
If you are in HRO, you should take this.
Take the course! You get 2 free credits as a GPA booster to your transcript, can add it to a music secondary or major, do pretty much the same amount of work you would do in Music 10, and get to learn more about the pieces you are playing. Win–win for everyone! Just need to turn in two 300 word prompts per semester and show up consistently to rehearsals.
Have fun and do the readings for the course! The workload is super manageable, and with the two assignments a semester, you really have time to invest in the content of the course. If you enjoy learning about music history, this is definitely the course I would take over Music10, as you get some credit and learn more about the pieces.
HRO is always going to be a great experience!
Take this class!! Definitely the best orchestra on campus and Fed and Yonah are incredible. Administratively, it is quite disorganized, but basically its a really good orchestra.
If you're doing HRO, you might as well take this class!
I highly recommend taking HRO for a letter grade if you're committed– it's a great class.
TAKE 107 IF YOU'RE IN HRO!!!!! The amount of work is really light since the main work is practicing and playing the music, which you're doing already. The only difference between just being in HRO and being in 107 is that in 107 you read like 20 pages and write like 500 words per concert, and the exercise is really rewarding. I didn't think I would learn nearly as much as I did from it going in, and I'm really glad I did it. Having more knowledge about the historical background of a piece makes me see it more fully, and in different ways than I do when I play it without any further knowledge. This class is also great because if you take it for two semesters you can just drop another class and still be on track to graduate. This can be really handy to have in your back pocket for when you need it – I dropped math on the very last day I could spring semester, and didn't add any classes, and it was fine because I had four credits from 107. As a result I was more able to take advantage of the musical opportunities in the spring, instead of falling really behind in all my classes due to tour, for example.
Great course
If you are in HRO, enroll. There is no reason not to. The assignments are very manageable.
If you are invested in HRO and can make it to rehearsal, take this course. Just know that enrolling in Music 107 means you won't really be able to skip rehearsals. The extra readings may be a little annoying to do the week they are do, but it's worth it imo. If you don't want to be forced to go to very rehearsal and just want to vibe in HRO, I wouldn't recommend taking this course.
If you're in HRO, you should be taking it as a class. I think Music 107 is worth it over Music 10 for anybody who has just a little more spare time to devote to readings, since they make you appreciate and enjoy the repertoire more.
