What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I overall enjoyed this course, but sometimes seminars can be a little stuffy.
You get what you put into the class.
While the class is clearly taught by extremely knowledgeable and renowned professors, it felt incohesive and unstructured at times, and the connections to "equality" during some of the sessions were relatively unclear. In this iteration, there were students from all sorts of schools and backgrounds, some with clearly extensive experience in philosophy, and others for whom I assume this was their first philosophy class. This meant that the level of the class was more that of an introductory philosophy class than of a graduate level class. The directions about the 25–page final paper were pretty unclear, and so is the grading of the class. That being said, the class is very light in terms of workload during the semester (1 hour weekly max) but the final paper can take a while.
The profs are famous but lectures were hard to follow.
