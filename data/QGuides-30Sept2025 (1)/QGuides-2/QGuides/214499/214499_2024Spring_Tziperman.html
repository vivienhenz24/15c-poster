Very interesting class about climate change, learned the most about it as a non–science concentrator than any other source by far. Easy class if you put in work during class too
Surprisingly tough and hard assignments but pretty fun course.
This is a great class! You learn about the science fundamentals behind so many areas of climate change and are able to visualize them through the coding problems. I feel like this was one of the rare classes where 90% of students were paying complete attention during the class, because everyone's really interested in the subject and the lecturing is super effective. Would recommend for anyone who wants to learn about the science behind climate change! Could potentially be a great QRD requirement for humanities concentrators!
An interesting class as someone with no prior background in climate science. I will say, I intended to take this class as a chill elective, but there are weekly coding/writing assignments which aren't too bad but can feel very repetitive. The TFs are very helpful and responsive, which was great.
Eli is a great lecturer and super kind. I think having a fairly strong background in python is necessary for this course but overall you learn a lot about how Earth's climate is changing. Also having one three hour class per week with no section makes meeting pretty chill. Go to office hours for help on homework.
I truly really respected and appreciated the lens that this course took to evaluate climate change. Often when you go into environmental courses, especially one such as this which is an overview of many particular tangible problems that climate change brings you expect it all to be a rant even if subliminal. Nonetheless, I did not feel this at any one moment in this course and Eli was great at making sure that everything said, done and studied was purely from a scientific perspective and all based on evidence. If there is one takeaway from this course is that it is important to be as critical of the climate alarmists and of the denialists that have in common the attempt to skew information rather than use facts. The assignments got me to practice my python and were tough but interesting to mix python skills with knowledge on environmental topics. They are very doable and there are multiple office hours with great TFs that you can attend.
Take this course!! The teaching staff is amazing and so helpful every week –– you learn so much about climate change in such a short amount of time.
Take this class. It is very chill. Each week, it is a 1 page response and a jupyter notebook to complete due Wednesday, the latter of which is worked on significantly in class the week before. I would work on it in class, not touch it until Tuesday night at their homework help sessions, and be fine.
This class is a bit difficult without any Python experience but not impossible. I do think the class is worth taking as we proved not only that climate change is occurring but really did go through and prove what it is affecting and what it is not while supporting it with real data. In addition, we did analyze current journalism around climate change and learned how to read it critically. Despite the class being a challenge, I don't regret taking it and the work is manageable especially with office hours. Eli is an amazing lecturer and teaches the material in a really digestible way. The TFs were also really helpful for helping us understand the material from a conceptual standpoint.
This is one of the best courses I have taken at Harvard. If you have any interest in climate change, this course is a great overview of the issues involved that does a great job of balancing technical and intuitive understandings of the mechanisms involved. Don't be afraid of the 3 hour class lengths as the combination of lecture and lab components makes this fly by.
