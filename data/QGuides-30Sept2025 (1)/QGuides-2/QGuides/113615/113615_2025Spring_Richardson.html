This course is excellent. Some PhD theory courses have the feel of "more than you can handle, 80% material mastery is good benchmark," but not so with this course. You really do have a chance to learn everything, and you have the materials and guidance to understand it all. Bravo to Chris and Sam for an excellently designed and equally well–delivered course on game theory and equilibrium analysis. The lectures are engaging, sections are also very useful, problem sets are fun, and the supplemental materials (Chris had shared draft textbook chapters that were just fantastic) bring you the rest of the way. I recommend the course very highly, you will enjoy yourself and find challenges that are approachable, encouraging, and fulfilling to tackle.
I really enjoyed this class, and I found it useful as a more "metrics" focused person. It exposed me to game theory and economic modeling more generally. If you already have experience with game theory, it might be a bit too slow or too easy, but I found it to be an approachable, manageable course for an introduction to game theory. Notes are amazing, lectures are engaging, and problem sets are not too long. They are weekly psets though, so it was a busy semester.
