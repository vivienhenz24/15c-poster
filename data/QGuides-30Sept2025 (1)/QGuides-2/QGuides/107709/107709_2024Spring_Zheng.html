Comments
This is a great course if you plan to use crystallography for your research. The workload is honestly a bit much for the content you need to know (weekly blog posts and replies, case study presentations, make–up blog posts if you miss a class, final project, take–home final exam), but it is not difficult.
Necessary and helpful if you plan on being a DIY user in the crystal lab. Not sure why you would want to take this course otherwise.
This is a good class if you are interested in crystallography or plan to use it in your research. The lab sessions were very helpful, and I enjoyed reading the various texts posted. The blog posts are a bit annoying to do. The take home exam is very long (10k words), and you are given such a short time to complete it. Grading in the class is quite arbitrary/not transparent, but your final grade will ultimately be fine. Shao is very passionate about this course and crystallography, and he knows so much about it. Lectures a bit hard to follow if you do not have prior experience.
It is worth to take the time and get familiarized with the softwares/commands used in the lab.
Shao is really excited about the class and very enthusiastic as a teacher. The lab section of the class is probably most useful, since the actual practical part of practical crystallography is taught and practiced in lab section. The case studies and final project were rather time consuming, but still useful for learning. If anything, the final was very long (recommended length for all the prompts was around 10,000 words) and was tough to do in such little time.
get ready to write posts, and feedback weekly. There are a ton of reading so make sure you have time to read. Also, don't expect much feedback from the teaching staff when it comes to your posts for grading. There are three of them.
