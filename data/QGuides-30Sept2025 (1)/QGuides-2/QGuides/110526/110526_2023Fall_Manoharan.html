To clarify (e.g. for future waiver applications): at least in 2023, the part of the syllabus referring to 'phase transitions and critical points' included the renormalisation group and the epsilon expansion.

Vinny is a fantastic lecturer who not only explains course content clearly and engagingly, but who also tends to teach as much of the broader motivation and historical context as possible. You'll come out of the course with a deep understanding of what entropy is (spoiler: it's not disorder) and how to resolve associated apparent paradoxes, and also with an idea as to the diversity of applications of statistical mechanics in current research. I'll prewarn that the final section of the course gets a bit pacy, and from certain conversations, I've heard it's important to choose final projects carefully.
