One of the most formative courses I've taken in my four years on this campus. Goldin is truly one of the best Economics teachers on this planet and whereas most Econ professors to that pedigree choose to teach grad students, she truly cares about her students and it shows in her interactions and feedback she provides to your papers.

If this is offered, 100% take it. Having a professor like Goldin advising an original piece of work you create is an opportunity that only being a student at Harvard can provide. This courses deepened my interest in family policies and labor economics, and the ideas have continued to inspire me beyond the classroom. I often find myself reflecting on questions like what we could learn from the demise of switchboard operators or how advancements such as egg freezing or expanded paid childcare could reshape female mobility and career advancement.
This course is amazing! Professor Goldin has a seemingly infinite bank of knowledge on the subject matter and you can feel it during her lectures.
