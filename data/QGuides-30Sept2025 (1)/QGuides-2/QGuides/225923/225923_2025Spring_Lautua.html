TAKE THIS COURSE!!! This was truly a special course where students build a real community under the guidance and expertise of Professor Lautua, who is extremely passionate for what she teaches and a very engaging professor. She is accessible outside of the course for conversations, reading tips, and further feedback about assignments. The reading materials are so interesting and the conversations during seminar even more so!
Take this course! It is an amazing way to learn more about Pacific indigenous cultures, traditions, and how they have been impacted by colonization and climate change. The discussions are so genuine and meaningful.
Professor Lautua is the best in the business and Harvard is incredibly lucky to have her for this time. She does a fantastic job cultivating a classroom environment that is very communal. I'm neither Indigenous or a Pacific Islander, but it was no matter to Professor Lautua – herself and the class are extremely welcoming of everyone. This was one of the best courses I took at Harvard. The spiritual teachings I learned caused me to reflect upon my own beliefs; resulting in immense personal and spiritual growth. Can't say enough good things about Professor Lautua! Mahalo.

TAKE THIS CLASS. It will help you look at healing and health differently!
This course was amazing and I'd highly recommend for anyone interested in the Pacific! Professor Lautua does a great job sharing and engaging the class in both discussions and activities!
This class will do so much for you, not only academically, but also encourage personal growth. I feel like many of the readings had me think about how I would intentionally like to engage with the Western medical system. The various texts talking about Pacific Indigenous approaches to well–being demonstrated care that recognizes the humanity and dignity of a person. It was particularly the section on motherhood that caused me to reflect on how my mother was treated in American hospitals and how I would like to be treated when the time comes for me to have babies.
