What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This is always the case for language courses: there is a very direct relationship between how much time you put into a class and how much you get out of it.
This class was a great continuation of the first course. Definitely more difficult, but not overwhelmingly so. This class gets much more into the less concrete aspects of Ancient Greek language. After this class, you'll have most of the tools necessary in order to read texts like the New Testament (which is why I took this class) and many famous portions of Classical literature.
If you took Greek 1 and enjoyed it, take Greek 2. It is harder, but by the end your progress with the language is very apparent.
Greek 2 covers a lot of ground compared to Greek 1 and is probably where we take on the most number of new concepts. It is a challenge but a completely and I repeat, completely worthwhile one. Some classes let you cruise through without pushing you but Greek 2 will demand consistency and self–motivation, two very important things in life. The classes are also very well structured and paced such that it never feels overwhelming even if it can sometimes be tough. You will have all the support you need along the way so don't be afraid to try it!
Memorize principal parts!!!
You will be expected to do most of the reading outside of class, and you will probably have to go to office hours if you have a lot of questions on the text since not a lot of the class is dedicated to reading.
