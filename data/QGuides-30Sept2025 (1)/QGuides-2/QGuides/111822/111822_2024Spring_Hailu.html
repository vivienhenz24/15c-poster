SO many strengths. The professor, the organization and structure, the textbook, the community, everything about this course is a strength. If you ever have Girma as a professor, go to his office hours! His lectures moved very fast, and sometimes the way he says "you should know this" or "this is very easy" might make you feel stupid in lecture, but then in office hours he genuinely takes the time to explain all of those things and when he says "this is very easy" he actually checks in to make sure everyone is actually understanding it. He goes above and beyond to make sure everyone is understanding what they're confused about, and additionally teaches important lessons about problem solving and such that just carry on into any pset class.
Girma is good instructor and very enthusiastic. It was clear after all.
Physics 153 is a great introduction/intermediate course in E&M that follows a satisfying, self–contained, and logical structure, moving from the bread and butter of electrostatics/magnetostatics to electrodynamics, then wrapping up with EM waves/radiation and potential formulation + relativity. A huge highlight of this class is its alignment with Griffiths, which is really a fantastic textbook that explains concepts/math/intuition so, so well (Griffiths is likely my favorite physics textbook in my studies thus far). Girma is a really enthusiastic teacher as well who really cares about the subject. The problem sets and take–home midterms/final were challenging but always to a very reasonable extent, and I particularly am a big fan of the take–home exam structure as I believe many of the E&M concepts/problems that require a strong understanding of the material should require deep thought and are thus unsuitable for problem–solving under a time constraint. Would recommend you give this class a shot!
Girma is awesome. Take this class with him if you can. But other than that, it's pretty standard material (this class is taught straight out of Griffiths), and you have to know it. The PSets were pretty easy, but having two take–home midterms and a take–home final (all 48 hours) was too much.
153 is very a great class with interesting material! Certainly worth taking, especially to satisfy your E&M or meet prerequisites. The teaching staff was incredibly kind and did a wonderful job .

Multivariable and differential equations are absolutely the most important prereqs, and you will not succeed without being familiar with both. 15b is not strictly essential if you have this math background, but it's surely more work since less is familiar.
Girma is a great professor, I would highly recommend taking this course with him!

I think everyone should take this class as undergraduate as a supplement or substitute for 15b. It really does make you appreciate and understand E&M more.
