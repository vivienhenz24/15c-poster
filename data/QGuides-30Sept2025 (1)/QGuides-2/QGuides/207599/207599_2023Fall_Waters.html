What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Incredible class with a one of a kind professor. I highly recommend it, without reservation.
This class is excellent for getting a sense of big questions in the field of migration studies. There are lots of readings and the class discussions focus on the readings. Prof. Waters is very flexible and willing to focus on areas of interest of the class. Knowing where to read deeply and where to focus just on key takeaways from readings is important for this class – which Prof. Waters talked about in the class.

I really enjoyed class discussions and felt like the class was flexible enough to focus on areas of interest for me while giving a good survey of the field of migration studies.
This class will give you an in–depth overview of the sociology of immigration. You will learn about immigration policy, theories of integration/assimilation and its discontents, how legal status affects millions of people in the US, how natives react to immigration, and even about new and important topics such as refugees and climate change migrants.
Know going in that you do not have to read every word of every book and article assigned in order to do well! Just skim enough to get the gist and to have a few points to make in class. Then focus more of your attention on the pieces that really interest you and that are relevant for your own research interests.
