What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is a fantastic class! Ann and Hannah are great teachers and lead fascinating class discussions. The class attracted a group from a range of disciplinary backgrounds, and one of the highlights was learning from my fellow students about their projects and research interests. I also really appreciated the scaffolded approach that the class took to the final project: we submitted the essay in stages, and this process led me to produce a final paper that I think reflects some of my best work. All in all, I loved this seminar and I highly recommend it!
Class will offer interesting exposure to early modern history, and related methods of history of science and book history. Excellent and engaged professors who lead it!
Professors Blair and Marcus are the dream team. The projects they cultivated and the conversations they started will resound with me throughout my career as a historian
Hannah and Anne are both great and the course is frankly a lot of fun. It will provide a lot of good material to "think with" regardless of one's research focus.
An engaging and fascinating dive into writing histories of material objects. Site visits to a wide range of institutions holding rare and important materials are coupled with a great set of readings that discuss the nature of these institutions. I recommend this course with the utmost enthusiasm.
