What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I learned methods of nonverbal communication. This course completely changed my understanding and appreciation of classical music.
This class definitely has a reputation, and you should definitely ask around before you take it. Fed is certainly a unique personality, pedagogically speaking, in the context of Harvard teaching staff –– he can be tough on people, but he's never wrong. For people with a thick skin and a desire to improve, this is a very good thing; for people who want positive feedback or to have a friendly classroom environment, this is not. I was gratified to be personally challenged and fulfilled by the class, but I know that a lot of people have been hurt or bothered in prior years by the course. I do think that everyone with a passing understanding of classical music and a good brain for structure and sound would benefit from the class; no matter your personal idiosyncrasies, there are some practical aspects of how to get certain sounds out of an ensemble that Fed is able to impart. If you want this –– and you can take some very tough personal criticism with a smile –– I'd definitely recommend this class, and I regret that I only was able to take it once.
Fantastic introductory course for total beginners to conducting who have a solid background in music on some instrument. You will have to put in time outside of class to score study and (for me as a total beginner) practise gestures in front of a mirror. I put in the time to do this and feel like my efforts were rewarded. Support is also excellent (this might have been partially due to the unusually small class size) – Fed and Yonah are both available for one–to–one sessions to help outside of class, and I scheduled several of these appointments throughout the semester to work on my baton technique. Worth taking!!
