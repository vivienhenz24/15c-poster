It sparked a passion for advocating for equitable healthcare, fundamentally changing my perspective and commitment to social justice. It did sometimes cover topics that I felt were quite obvious, but I felt like the point was to go into depth as to why these social beliefs or inequalities were common in society. This is a very engaging course that covers some very interesting topics. However, it is clear that the exam writers do not have much experience crafting exams. Including True/False and multiple–choice questions in an exam to evaluate a complex subject like sociology is very tricky. This was apparent in the exams, where some questions were ambiguous or debatable in terms of their correct answers. While the course content is excellent, the exams are significantly flawed, and, given that these exams contribute to 40% of our final grade, this issue was a pretty serious one to me.
Not sure if this is advertised as an intro to sociology course but I would not consider it to be that. This is more a class on the social determinants of health, and how we can explain and start to understand health outcomes and disparities. I took this course for my GHHP secondary and would HIGHLY recommend to anyone interested in health, the medical field, or health inequalities. The assignments are chill, but doing the readings will help you answer exam questions because they are free responses. You can skate by not doing the readings though.
