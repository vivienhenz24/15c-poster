Tanya is as amazing as you would expect a professor of a class about education to be. She does a great job blending lecture and discussion, discussing both theoretical and practical viewpoints, plus is very understanding, cares a lot about her students, and is often available outside of class.
I loved this class. Super interesting and applicable content. Very nice class environment too.
It's a great class. Sit next to someone you like though. No offense to the person I sat next to, but we didn't really vibe and out conversations were stale. Sitting next to a different person towards the end, I realized how much more fruitful discussions could be when you sit near someone you vibe with.
Definitely take this class if you are interested in education. My class had people from a lot of different concentrations, and related the different units to their own experiences with education, and you learn about cool studies to figure out how students learn best. I think the best part is the policy proposals, that you do the second week of each topic. The work is really manageable, and there is a lot of class wide discussions that honestly made a 2 hour class go by so fast. Professor Levari also gives really helpful feedback on the policy proposals.
Fun class that covers an interesting and important topic. Especially great if you want to be a teacher or have any interest in education. You will learn strategies on how to become a better teacher and student, as well more about the education gap problem. Assignments are fair (weekly reading and response paper) and feedback is given so that you can improve your writing for the next week. Small class size is good for discussion and doing a poster presentation about our research papers with the class was a fun way to end the semester while also practicing a new skill.
Take this course! It's very well–paced, and Tanya is great at stimulating in–depth discussions every week. The workload is pretty manageable –– just be proactive and don't procrastinate the papers.
